Margaritas as a bartender would make it

---

## Assistant

A classic margarita, as made by a bartender, typically consists of the following ingredients:

- 2 ounces (60 milliliters) of tequila
- 1 ounce (30 milliliters) of triple sec or orange liqueur (such as Cointreau)
- 1 ounce (30 milliliters) of freshly squeezed lime juice
- Ice

To prepare it, you would typically:

1. Rim a margarita glass with salt by moistening the rim with a lime wedge and dipping it in salt.
2. Fill a shaker with ice.
3. Add the tequila, triple sec, and lime juice to the shaker.
4. Shake vigorously until well chilled.
5. Strain the mixture into the salt-rimmed glass filled with fresh ice.

You can also adjust the proportions of the ingredients to suit your taste, such as making it stronger or sweeter by varying the amounts of tequila or triple sec. Some variations include adding simple syrup for sweetness or using different flavors of liqueur for a unique twist on the classic margarita.

How does Airbnb usually handle house rule violations? What about complaints about cameras?

---

## Assistant

Airbnb’s process is fairly consistent:

**House-rule violations**  
- **Report & evidence:** Hosts (or guests) submit a complaint through the Resolution Center or customer-support chat, usually with photos, messages, or other proof.  
- **Mediation first:** Airbnb encourages both sides to talk in the message thread. Support agents will review the listing’s stated rules, the evidence, and the reservation details.  
- **Possible outcomes:**  
  * Warning to the guest and notation on their account.  
  * Partial or full charge against the guest’s security deposit (if one is set) or a Host Damage Protection claim.  
  * Cancellation of remaining nights or removal from the platform for repeat or serious breaches.  
- Airbnb rarely acts on vague claims; hosts need documentation.

**Complaints about cameras**  
- **Strict disclosure rule:** Indoor cameras or any device that can capture private areas (bedrooms, bathrooms) are completely banned. Outdoor/entry cameras or noise monitors are allowed only if clearly disclosed in the listing and positioned for common-area monitoring.  
- **Handling:** If a guest reports an undisclosed or intrusive camera, Airbnb typically suspends or delists the property while investigating. Verified violations can lead to full refunds, rebooking assistance, and permanent removal of the listing.

In short: Airbnb weighs written house rules and proof for general violations, but hidden or undisclosed cameras trigger immediate, much harsher action.

## Assistant

The best way to slice an orange for garnish depends on the look you want, but here are the most common techniques:

1. **Orange Wheels**: 
   - Cut the orange crosswise into thin, even slices. 
   - These are perfect for garnishing drinks or plating dishes. 

2. **Orange Wedges**:
   - Cut the orange in half from top to bottom.
   - Place the halves cut side down and slice them into quarters or eighths, depending on the desired size.
   - These are great for drinks or as a side garnish on plates.

3. **Twists**:
   - Cut a thin slice from the orange, removing the peel and pith in one piece while leaving a bit of the flesh.
   - Twist the peel around a straw or a chopstick to create a curly garnish.

4. **Zest**:
   - Use a zester or a fine grater to remove just the colored part of the peel, avoiding the white pith.
   - Zest is ideal for cocktails or to sprinkle over dishes.

Each method gives a different aesthetic and flavor contribution to your dish or drink.

## Assistant

Mr. White — **you can ask for a rental now**, but **GEICO may refuse to direct-bill/authorize it until they accept liability on the other driver’s policy**.

### Practical answer

Because you were **parked** and both of you have GEICO:

1. **Call the third-party GEICO adjuster and say:**
   > “My vehicle was parked and unoccupied. I filed a third-party property damage claim against your insured. I need rental authorization or written confirmation that GEICO will reimburse reasonable rental/loss-of-use costs from the date my car became unusable.”

2. **Ask directly:**
   - “Has liability been accepted?”
   - “Are you authorizing Enterprise direct-bill?”
   - “What daily rental rate/class are you approving?”
   - “From what date will rental/loss-of-use be covered?”
   - “If you need the other driver’s statement, what evidence can I upload now to speed this up?”

3. **Upload everything immediately:**
   - Photos of your parked car damage
   - Photos of where it was parked
   - Other driver’s insurance/driver/license plate info
   - Police report or incident number, if any
   - Any text/admission/witness/security footage
   - Tow/storage/repair estimate, if available

### Do you have to wait?

For **GEICO to pay directly**, probably **yes, unless liability is already clear**. GEICO says its liability examiner commonly contacts involved parties, takes statements, reviews photos/police reports, then determines cause. citeturn366148view0

For **you to physically get a rental**, **no**. You can rent one yourself now, but keep it reasonable because reimbursement depends on GEICO later accepting liability and agreeing the cost/time was reasonable. California loss-of-use damages are generally based on the reasonable cost to rent a similar vehicle for the time reasonably needed to repair or replace the car. citeturn176728search0

### Your best move

If you need a car today:

- **First choice:** get GEICO to authorize Enterprise/direct billing under the third-party claim.
- **Second choice:** use your own GEICO rental reimbursement coverage, if you have it, while GEICO sorts liability internally.
- **Third choice:** pay out of pocket for a normal comparable rental, then submit receipts as third-party **loss of use/rental reimbursement**.

GEICO’s rental reimbursement page is mainly about **optional first-party coverage** on your own policy; it says that coverage can have daily/per-claim limits, Enterprise direct billing, and exclusions for gas, mileage, extra rental coverage, and security deposits. citeturn890933view0 For a **third-party claim**, the issue is the other driver’s liability coverage and your loss-of-use damages.

### Key leverage point

In California, once **liability and damages are reasonably clear**, an auto insurer cannot push a third-party claimant to use their own policy just to avoid paying under the at-fault driver’s policy. citeturn772027view0 If they deny, delay, or dispute liability/damages, they have to put that in writing; California claim regulations also require a fair, objective investigation. citeturn356777view0

So the line to use is:

> “Since my car was parked and unoccupied, please confirm whether liability is accepted. If not, please tell me exactly what information is still needed and whether GEICO will reimburse reasonable rental/loss-of-use from today once liability is accepted.”

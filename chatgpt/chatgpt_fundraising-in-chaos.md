## Assistant

Mr. White, your reaction is justified. This is a bad working environment, a bad sleep environment, and a bad sensory environment. You are trying to fundraise from a cramped, dirty, cat-disrupted studio where you do not feel ownership or control. That would make most people feel trapped and irritable.

## Right now: get through the fundraising call

Do **not** try to fix the apartment before the call. You need a temporary clean zone.

### 20-minute call setup

1. **Move the call away from the mess**
   - Use a plain wall, curtain, or tight crop.
   - Blur background if needed.
   - Laptop/phone camera angled slightly down.
   - Put a lamp in front of you, not behind you.

2. **Kill the sensory triggers**
   - TV off.
   - Cats out of the room if possible, even temporarily.
   - Litter boxes visually blocked with a chair, sheet, laundry basket, or box.
   - Cat food moved out of frame.

3. **Create a 3-foot clean perimeter**
   - Clear only the desk/table and the camera background.
   - Dirty dishes into one bin/bag/tub and move it off-camera.
   - Trash into one bag.
   - Do not organize. Hide. Contain. Resume life after the call.

4. **Reset your body**
   - Cold water on face.
   - 10 slow breaths.
   - Stand during the call if sitting near litter/cat food is making you spiral.
   - Wear shoes. It helps psychologically separate “work mode” from “messy apartment mode.”

Your goal is **investor-grade camera frame**, not a clean apartment.

## The bigger issue

The apartment is functioning like **your girlfriend’s place that you happen to occupy**, with cats, clutter, litter, and layout problems constantly overriding your sense of control. That is not a character flaw. That is an environment problem.

The key thing to say to her is **not** “your cats and apartment are ruining my life.” Say:

> “I’m hitting a wall in the apartment. I know you’re working hard and I appreciate it, especially while my cash flow is tight. I’m struggling because I don’t feel like I have a clean, stable place to work or decompress. I need us to make a system for the cats, litter, dishes, and a small work zone so I can function better.”

## Non-negotiables to solve

### 1. Cat pee
Ten-plus times on bed/couch is severe. That needs immediate action.

Possible causes:
- Dirty or poorly placed litter boxes
- Stress
- territorial issues
- medical issue like UTI
- old urine smell still present, causing repeat marking

You need:
- Enzyme cleaner, not regular cleaner
- Waterproof mattress protector
- Couch cover or washable blanket layer
- Vet check if this is ongoing
- Litter boxes cleaned daily
- No access to bed/couch until the behavior is controlled

### 2. Litter box placement
Three litter boxes in a studio is brutal. They may be necessary for two cats, but they need containment.

Options:
- Enclosed litter furniture
- Large litter mats
- Covered or high-sided boxes
- Air purifier near boxes
- Daily scoop rule
- One “cat zone” instead of litter/food bleeding into your work zone

### 3. Your work zone
You need one area that is yours.

Minimum viable version:
- One desk/table
- One chair
- One lamp
- No litter boxes
- No cat food
- No TV facing you
- No random stuff placed there
- Clean at end of day, every day

That zone is not a preference. It is infrastructure for income.

### 4. Bathroom/back pain
Having to squeeze around a door/toilet setup that triggers lower back pain every time you piss is insane. That needs a hack now.

Try:
- Remove anything hanging on the door.
- Add a doorstop or hinge adjustment if possible.
- Pee sitting down if it reduces twisting.
- Put a small mat/foot position marker where your stance hurts least.
- Ask landlord/property manager about the door if it is actually obstructed or misaligned.

## The conversation with your girlfriend

Keep it practical and low-blame:

> “I don’t want this to come out as me attacking you. I know this was your place first, and I know you’re carrying a lot right now. But I’m really struggling in the apartment. The mess, cat pee, litter, dishes, and lack of a clean work area are making me feel like I can’t think. I need us to make the apartment work for both of us, especially while I’m fundraising.”

Then propose systems, not vibes:

- Daily 10-minute reset
- Litter scooped once daily
- Dishes contained in one bin when counter space is gone
- Cat food/litter moved away from desk
- One protected work zone
- Bed/couch protected from cat pee
- One weekly deeper clean

## Most important frame

You are stressed because the environment is blocking three core needs:

| Need | Current problem |
|---|---|
| Control | It feels like someone else’s apartment |
| Cleanliness | Cats, litter, dishes, dirty floor |
| Work focus | Desk near litter/food, TV on, messy Zoom background |

Solve it as an operations problem. Do not litigate whether you are “allowed” to be annoyed. The annoyance is data.

- Wall shelves
- Closed cabinets

- Tiny objects on every surface
- Fragile items near edges
- Random sentimental items on counters
- Cat-accessible decor on desks, TV stands, nightstands

## 🧑‍💼 Minimal realistic cost (with an attorney / credible filing)

For many inventors — especially founders like you — the more realistic “low-end but professional” cost includes attorney fees + USPTO fees. That typically yields a better-quality application and improves the odds of eventually getting a granted patent.

- For a simple invention (especially mechanical / modest complexity), filing a full non-provisional (utility) patent often runs in the ballpark of **US$8,000–15,000** including attorney fees + USPTO fees. citeturn0search26turn0search21turn0search24  
- For a simpler or design-only patent (e.g. protecting the “look”/appearance of a product rather than its function), total cost is typically lower — around **US$2,000–4,000**. citeturn0search24turn0search28turn0search3  

In short: a defensible, professionally drafted, nonprovisional patent for a small startup/inventor often costs **mid-single to low-double digit thousands of dollars**.

## User

What does Equivocal mean?

---

## Assistant

**Equivocal** means unclear, ambiguous, or open to multiple interpretations—often intentionally. It can also describe something questionable or suspicious.  

Example: *His answer was equivocal, leaving everyone unsure what he really meant.*

---

## User

Can you give me that etymology?

---

## Assistant

Sure. **Equivocal** comes from:

- **Latin _aequivocus_**:  
  - _aequi-_ = "equal" or "same"  
  - _vox, vocis_ = "voice" or "call"  
  → Together: **“equal voice”** or **“same-sounding”**

Originally used to describe words or phrases that could have more than one meaning—i.e., ambiguous speech.

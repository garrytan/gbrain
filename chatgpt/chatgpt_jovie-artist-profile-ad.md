## The visual language

The ad should feel like **street realism plus impossible product elegance**.

**World:**  
Mostly monochrome, high-contrast, photorealistic subway cinematography. Think sodium lights, wet platform floor, train reflections, compressed lens, shallow depth of field, visible breath, textured concrete, real passersby.

**Product:**  
The Jovie UI is the only place where color starts to enter. The profile art, Apple Pay success state, and “Thanks, here’s my song” notification introduce warm color. That gives the product a visual meaning: **Jovie brings the artist’s world into focus.**

**Camera language:**  
Start grounded and human. Move into more impossible motion only after the phone enters. The physical world can be handheld, slow dolly, long-lens. The phone world can be smooth, floating, macro, perfectly lit.

**Color rule:**  
Subway world: monochrome / silver / black / soft skin tone.  
Jovie profile: controlled color.  
End card: clean white or black, one sentence.

# Best use of the Apple-style carousel

Use it as the **“product narrative index.”**

Each card teases a cinematic section below. When someone clicks a card, it expands or scrolls to the deeper section.

Example:

Card: **See where your audience is moving.**  
Click → scrolls to the rooftop LA audience map section.

Card: **Build around the drop.**  
Click → scrolls to the studio pre-save section.

Card: **Turn attention into attendance.**  
Click → scrolls to the show/ticket section.

That makes the page feel polished and intentional.

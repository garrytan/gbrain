## Likely questions and exact answers

### “Why are you pre-traction?”

> Because the product is being built around a workflow I already know manually. We are raising at the stage where the core insight is right, the market timing is right, and the execution risk is mostly productization. AI House said they like idea-stage entry, and this is exactly that: strong founder-market fit before the traction curve is obvious.

### “Why won’t this just be a feature inside Spotify, TuneCore, or Linkfire?”

> Those companies own pieces of the workflow. Jovie owns the operating loop. Spotify is consumption, TuneCore is distribution, Linkfire is routing. Jovie watches the artist’s whole business context and decides what action should happen next. The value is orchestration across the stack.

### “Who pays?”

> Initially independent artists with enough seriousness to spend money on growth: touring DJs, producers, and creators with catalogs. Longer term, managers, labels, distributors, and artist-services companies can use Jovie to operate many artists at once.

### “Why start with music?”

> Music has the perfect combination of fragmented workflows, high emotional demand, constant content cycles, measurable fan behavior, and artists who already need management labor. It is also the market I understand from the inside.

### “What is the wedge?”

> Start with artist profiles, smart links, fan capture, release alerts, and opportunity detection. That gives Jovie the data layer. Once we own the artist context and fan/release graph, the AI manager can execute higher-value workflows: merch drops, campaign plans, reports, collaborations, and monetization plays.

### “What is the hard technical problem?”

> The hard part is building a reliable agentic system that can maintain artist context, detect real opportunities, avoid spammy actions, and execute safely across fragmented tools. The AI has to know when to act, what to suggest, what to automate, and what requires approval.

### “What does success look like in 90 days?”

> Ten serious artists using Jovie weekly, at least three workflows where Jovie creates measurable business value, and a repeatable loop where Jovie identifies an opportunity, recommends an action, and helps execute it.

## What this really means economically

Their deal is effectively:

> **7% common stock + SAFE investment at $10M cap.**

> **$100K now + 7% common + potential additional $500K SAFE later if you show traction.**

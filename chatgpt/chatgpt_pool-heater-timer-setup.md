## Assistant

You're dealing with an **Intermatic-style mechanical timer**—common for pool equipment. Here’s how it works and how to set it:

### **Basic Parts of the Timer**
- The **yellow dial**: This represents a 24-hour clock. It rotates clockwise.
- The **black arrow** or triangle: That’s the current time indicator.
- The **dial you pull and rotate**: You’re setting the current time when you do this. So if it’s 3 PM, you rotate the dial so that "3 PM" is aligned with the black triangle.
- The **two metal trippers** (usually one says ON, the other OFF): These are what actually control when the heater turns on and off.

Right now, it looks like there’s **only an OFF tripper installed**, and the ON one may be missing or hidden.

---

### **What the Time Dial Means**
- **Setting the time**: Pull the dial out slightly and rotate it to set the current time.
- **The trippers**: These set the time range the heater turns on and off.
  - The **ON tripper** turns on the heater when it reaches that point.
  - The **OFF tripper** shuts it off when it reaches that point.
- The dial keeps rotating with time like a clock. When it hits the ON tripper, power flows; when it hits the OFF tripper, it stops.

---

### **How to Turn on Your Pool Heater**
1. **Set the current time**:
   - Pull the yellow dial out slightly.
   - Rotate until the current time lines up with the black triangle or marker on the face.
2. **Install/set the ON and OFF trippers**:
   - Find the small trippers labeled “ON” and “OFF.”
   - Use a flathead screwdriver to tighten the ON tripper where you want the heater to start (e.g., 4 PM).
   - Set the OFF tripper where you want it to stop (e.g., 6 PM).
3. **Flip the bottom switch to "Timer On"** (as noted in handwriting). That means the timer is now in control of powering the heater.

---

### To Just Make the Pool Hot Now
If you don’t care about setting times and just want the heater on:

- Flip the switch at the bottom **from "Timer On" to "ON"**. That will bypass the timer and send power to the heater directly.
- Just remember to turn it **back off manually**, or you’ll waste energy.

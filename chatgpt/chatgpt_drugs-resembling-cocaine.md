What drugs look like cocaine

## Assistant

Mr. White, many powdered substances—legal and illegal—can resemble cocaine (a fine, white to off-white powder). Some common examples include:

1. **Ketamine (illicit/prescription misuse)**  
   - Often sold as a white or off-white crystalline powder.  
   - Similar color and consistency can cause confusion with cocaine.

2. **Heroin (especially “China White”)**  
   - Heroin is typically brownish, but higher-purity heroin (“China White”) can appear white or off-white.  

3. **Synthetic Cathinones (“Bath Salts”)**  
   - These designer stimulants can come as white or off-white powders, making them visually similar to cocaine.

4. **Amphetamine and Methamphetamine (when in powdered form)**  
   - Amphetamine powder can be white or off-white, occasionally with a chalky appearance.  
   - Methamphetamine is more commonly seen in a crystal form (“crystal meth”), but when ground up, it can appear as a fine white powder.

5. **Prescription Stimulants (crushed)**  
   - Medications like crushed Adderall or Ritalin can sometimes look like a fine white or pale-orange powder depending on the formulation, though color can vary.

6. **Non-drug powders**  
   - Cornstarch, baking powder, or talcum powder, although harmless, can look very similar to cocaine from a quick glance.

Because so many substances can resemble cocaine visually, it’s impossible to identify a powder solely by appearance. Laboratory testing is the only reliable way to confirm a substance’s identity.

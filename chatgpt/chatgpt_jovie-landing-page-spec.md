# Jovie Landing Page Spec: “The Artist Profile That Keeps Working”

## 1. Strategic thesis

Jovie has outgrown “link-in-bio for musicians.” The product is now an **artist operating surface**: one canonical artist profile that routes fan traffic into streams, tickets, payments, fan capture, automatic follow-up, analytics, and eventually contact/team routing.

The current site already proves this direction. The homepage leads with “Release more music with less work” and an AI workspace for releases, assets, pitching, and promotion. The product pages show the stronger wedge: artist profiles built for streams, drops, support, bookings, fan capture, smart links, payments, alerts, and automatic notifications. The page should make one promise:

> **Every fan touchpoint becomes a working system.**

A fan taps the link. Jovie gives them the right action. They listen, buy, tip, subscribe, or contact. Jovie remembers them. When the next release or nearby show happens, Jovie brings them back.

That is the product.

## Section 12: FAQ

### Must-have questions

#### How is Jovie different from Linktree?

Linktree gives creators a flexible page of links. Jovie gives artists a music-first profile that understands releases, shows, payments, fan capture, and automatic follow-up.

#### How is Jovie different from a smart link?

A smart link usually serves one release. Jovie gives the artist one permanent profile that can route fans to music, shows, pay, contact, subscribe, and future fan actions.

#### Do I need to design anything?

No. Jovie is polished by default. Claim your profile, connect once, and Jovie builds around your artist identity and catalog.

#### Can fans turn on alerts?

Yes. Fans can opt in for updates, and Jovie can bring them back when new music or nearby shows go live.

#### Can I use Jovie free?

Yes. Start with a free artist profile. Upgrade to Pro when you want fan notifications, pre-save campaigns, deeper analytics, and reactivation.

#### Does Jovie support pre-saves?

Yes. Pro includes pre-save campaigns and pre-release/countdown pages.

#### Can I still use my existing tools?

Yes. Jovie should become the canonical artist profile. Keep specialized tools where useful, but route fans through one profile that stays current.

# 6. Visual direction

## Design principles

1. **Music first. SaaS second.**  
   The hero should feel like a premium artist product, not a dashboard startup.

2. **One primary action per viewport.**  
   Every section should have one obvious next step.

3. **Use real product surfaces.**  
   The phone profile, alert timeline, tip flow, and claim flow should appear real.

4. **Use motion to explain the loop.**  
   Subtle animation should show fan traffic turning into repeat engagement.

5. **Avoid fake AI magic.**  
   AI belongs in the product story after the profile/fan loop is understood.

## Visual system

- Background: deep neutral / black with strong white space
- Accent: one Jovie brand accent, used sparingly for CTAs and active states
- Typography: large, editorial, confident
- Product screenshots: crisp, high-contrast, mobile-first
- Motion: slow, useful, product-explanatory
- Avoid: neon music clichés, waveform clutter, festival-poster chaos, generic AI gradients

# 11. What to remove or demote

AI is useful, but it is not the clearest buyer hook. Artists immediately understand their profile, their fans, their releases, their shows, and their income. Put AI in a later section:

**AI helps keep the profile, releases, insights, and follow-up moving.**

## Remove vague nav labels

Current nav has “Product,” “Solutions,” “Pricing,” “Resources.” “Solutions” is too generic.

Use:

- Product
- Examples
- Pricing
- Learn
- Sign in
- Claim profile

## Avoid generic SaaS phrases

Do not lead with:

- “workflow”
- “workspace”
- “AI-powered”

- “growth platform”
- “creator CRM”

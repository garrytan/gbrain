In therms of bartending, what does batching drinks mean?

---

## Assistant

Batching drinks in bartending refers to the process of preparing a larger quantity of a cocktail in advance, rather than making each drink individually to order. This is typically done for efficiency, especially when serving the same cocktail to multiple customers or for events. Batching involves mixing the ingredients in the right proportions, which can include alcohol, mixers, and garnishes, and then storing the mixture until it's ready to be served. It allows bartenders to streamline service and maintain consistency in the taste and presentation of the cocktail.

## User

Give me a good recipe for a margarita. Like, one you would get from a bartender, not some crap that your grandma would make at home.

---

## Assistant

Certainly! Here's a classic and authentic Margarita recipe that you would typically get from a bartender:

Ingredients:
- 2 oz (60 ml) premium tequila
- 1 oz (30 ml) orange liqueur (such as triple sec or Cointreau)
- 1 oz (30 ml) freshly squeezed lime juice
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) agave syrup or simple syrup (adjust to taste)
- Ice
- Salt (for rimming the glass)
- Lime wheel or wedge (for garnish)

Instructions:
1. Rim a margarita glass with salt by running a lime wedge around the rim and dipping it in a plate of salt. Fill the glass with ice.

2. In a cocktail shaker, combine the tequila, orange liqueur, freshly squeezed lime juice, and agave syrup or simple syrup.

3. Add ice to the shaker and shake vigorously for about 10-15 seconds until well chilled.

4. Strain the cocktail into the prepared margarita glass over the ice.

5. Garnish with a lime wheel or wedge on the rim of the glass.

6. Enjoy your classic Margarita!

This recipe provides a well-balanced and authentic Margarita, with the right combination of tequila, citrus, and sweetness. Adjust the sweetness to your preference by adding more or less agave syrup or simple syrup. Cheers!

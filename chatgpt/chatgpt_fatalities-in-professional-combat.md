## User

Has anyone died during professional combat sports

---

## Assistant

Yes, there have been fatalities in professional combat sports. These include boxing, mixed martial arts (MMA), and kickboxing, among others. Deaths can occur due to acute injuries sustained during a match, such as brain hemorrhages, or due to complications of injuries over time. In boxing, notable cases include Benny Paret in 1962, who died following a bout with Emile Griffith. In MMA, although rare, there have been fatalities, such as the death of Joao Carvalho in 2016 after a fight in Dublin, Ireland. The sports' governing bodies have made efforts to improve safety measures, but the inherent risks of combat sports mean that such tragedies can still occur.

## Assistant

Deaths occurring during regulated professional combat sports events typically do not count as murder due to the consent of the participants and the regulatory framework governing the sport. Participants in these sports assume a known risk of injury and death, and there are strict rules, regulations, and oversight intended to maximize participant safety.

Murder generally requires the intention (mens rea) to kill or cause grievous bodily harm known to likely result in death. In the context of professional combat sports, the actions of athletes are conducted under the agreement that they are competing in a sport with established rules and with no intent to kill. When a death occurs despite adherence to the rules and safety protocols, it is usually considered a tragic accident rather than a criminal act.

However, if a participant acts with deliberate intent to cause harm beyond the accepted rules and regulations of the sport, or if gross negligence is involved, legal consequences could potentially follow, but this would be highly exceptional and dependent on the specific circumstances of the incident.

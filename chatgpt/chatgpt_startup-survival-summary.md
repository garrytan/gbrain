## Assistant

Mr. White — concise, factual summary:

Paul Graham argues that startups should constantly know whether they are **“default alive”** or **“default dead.”**

- **Default alive**: If revenue and expenses stay on the current trajectory, the company reaches profitability without raising more money.
- **Default dead**: On the current trajectory, the company runs out of cash before profitability and is relying on fundraising to survive.

Key points:
- Many founders don’t know which state they’re in, and that ignorance is dangerous.
- The most lethal situation is the **“fatal pinch”**: default dead + slow growth + not enough time to fix it.
- Founders often assume they can raise more money; this is unreliable and becomes less likely the more you depend on it.
- Fast growth can attract investors, but fundraising should never be treated as guaranteed.
- **Overhiring is the biggest cause of failure** in funded startups. Hiring rarely fixes weak product-market fit and often accelerates death.
- Growth usually comes from a product that deeply meets a real need, not from spending more or hiring faster.
- Founders should prioritize survival, keep expenses low, and focus on improving the product—often by doing things that don’t scale.

Bottom line:  
**Know your runway math early, avoid overhiring, don’t rely on fundraising, and fix the product before scaling.**

On the Eight Sleep Pod’s **hub LED**, a **solid pink/red light** typically indicates that **the warming system is active**. In contrast, a **blinking or persistent red light**, especially alongside a “Sensor Disconnected” message in the app, means the **hub has lost connection** to the cover’s sensors citeturn0search1turn0search3turn0search5.

### ✅ Quick guide for red light behaviors:

| LED Behavior              | Meaning                                              | Action to Take                                  |
|---------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **Solid pink/red**        | Warming mode is active                              | Normal – it's heating the bed citeturn0search1turn0search7 |

---

### 🔧 Troubleshooting steps

1. **Open the Eight Sleep app**: look for a “Sensor Disconnected” alert.
2. **Inspect the sensor cable**: ensure it’s connected securely between the cover and the hub.
3. **Power cycle your system**: unplug the hub, wait 10 seconds, and re-plug.
4. **Double‑tap the hub’s button**: restores normal Wi‑Fi/sensor connection.
5. **Reach out to support**: if reconnection fails, contact Eight Sleep customer service.

---

### Why this matters

- If your goal is simply warming the bed before sleep, **solid pink/red is expected and harmless**.
- If the cover isn’t heating or cooling properly, or sleep tracking stops working, a red light could mean **sensor connectivity issues**—resolving it will ensure full functionality like temperature control and health tracking.

---

**Bottom line**:  
- **Solid pink/red** → bed is *heating* normally  
- **Blinking or sensor-error red** → *connection trouble*—check cables, reboot, or contact support

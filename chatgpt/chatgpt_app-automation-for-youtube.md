## Assistant

Mr. White,

I would not rebuild the scraped-profile network. I would rebuild the rendering and publishing engine and point it at opted-in, rights-cleared assets.

The old version is fragile for four reasons.

1. **Instagram risk.** Instagram’s Terms prohibit automated collection/access without express permission, and Meta has publicly sued operators that scraped publicly visible Instagram profiles, photos, and videos to run clone sites. That is very close to the workflow you described. citeturn781902search1turn356618view3

2. **Rights stack.** Photos are protected by copyright from creation, and the photographer generally owns the copyright even when someone else is the subject of the photo. In California, Civil Code §3344 creates liability for knowingly using a person’s name, photograph, or likeness to advertise or sell goods/services without prior consent, with news/public-affairs exceptions that are fact-specific. For an automated SEO/video system built around a real person’s image and name, I would assume you need both media rights and publicity-rights clearance. citeturn963371search0turn963371search7turn898028view0turn898028view2

3. **YouTube policy.** YouTube bars posting the same content across channels, massively uploading scraped content from other creators, repeatedly uploading content you don’t own, and autogenerated content that computers post without regard for quality or viewer experience. It also treats misleading thumbnails, titles, and descriptions as spam, and these Community Guidelines apply to all content, including unlisted and private uploads. On the monetization side, YouTube explicitly calls out mass-produced template videos, scraped/reused content, and image slideshows with minimal narrative or commentary as inauthentic or reused content. citeturn517646view1turn517646view3turn984446view0turn309156view2turn309156view0

4. **The music-claim angle is narrower than it sounds.** Content ID can monetize matching long-form videos, and music claims often use monetize as the default policy. YouTube only grants Content ID to owners with exclusive rights to a substantial body of material, and it can disable Content ID or terminate partnerships for improper claims. Active claims on 1–3 minute Shorts block the Short, which makes this model much worse for Shorts than for long-form. Invalid or artificial traffic can also wipe out earnings or suspend AdSense. citeturn343680view2turn462700view0turn462700view1turn343680view0turn982494view5

The SEO assumption is also weaker than you’re giving it credit for. YouTube search ranks on how well the title, description, and video content match the query and on engagement for that search. Tags play a minimal role, and YouTube says recommendation systems do not prioritize videos based on whether they are monetized. An autogenerated bio in the description will not carry the channel by itself. citeturn345550view0turn345550view1turn345550view2

What is worth rebuilding is the engine:
- ingest owned or licensed assets
- rank/select shots
- render slideshow or visualizer
- generate title, description, thumbnail
- upload and schedule
- track rights, approvals, and takedowns

That version can be sold to artists, labels, managers, and opted-in creators. The visualizer, lyric, and promo-clip variant is the cleanest version because you already own the music. Keep visuals licensed and channel formats distinct enough that they are not just the same template repeated at scale. Meta’s official Instagram APIs are built for professional accounts that connect to your app, and the media API only returns media owned by those accounts. YouTube also treats AI-generated outlines, scripts, titles, thumbnails, and infographics as production assistance; realistic synthetic depictions or cloned voices of real people require disclosure. citeturn309156view2turn328426search6turn328426search3turn646422view0

For build strategy, keep this outside Jovie core. Share auth and billing if useful, but run it as a separate service with its own storage, queues, Google/Meta apps, and abuse/takedown tooling. That gives you risk isolation, quota isolation, and brand isolation. The fastest compliant build is a small service with uploader/OAuth, asset store, render worker, copy generator, and YouTube scheduler. Build long-form first if the music-rights model matters. The YouTube Data API default quota is 10,000 units/day and each video upload costs 100 units, so early volume is manageable; scheduled publishing is supported through `status.publishAt`. citeturn982494view8turn982494view6turn982494view7

My recommendation:
- Do not rebuild the scraper/clone-network version.
- Rebuild the rights-cleared media-to-video pipeline.
- Aim it at artists, labels, managers, and opted-in creators first.
- Use owned visuals, licensed stock, or creator-authorized media.
- Keep any tribute/fan channels clearly labeled if you ever go that route, because YouTube requires fan channels to make that obvious. citeturn356618view0

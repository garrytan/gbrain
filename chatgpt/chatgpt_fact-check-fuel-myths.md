## Assistant

This claim is **mostly false or misleading**.

### The Myth:
"**Pumping gas slowly gets you more fuel.**"

### The Facts:

1. **Fuel volume is measured, not timed**: Gas pumps measure fuel **by volume (gallons or liters), not by time or speed**. So whether you pump fast or slow, you're getting the same amount of fuel.

2. **Vapor recovery systems**: Modern gas stations in the U.S. use vapor recovery systems to prevent gasoline vapors from escaping. These systems don’t affect how much liquid fuel goes into your tank based on pump speed.

3. **Temperature and expansion**: Some argue that slower pumping prevents fuel from heating up, thereby reducing expansion and giving you more "dense" fuel. While fuel density is affected by temperature, the temperature of gasoline **in underground tanks stays relatively constant**, regardless of how fast you pump.

4. **Spillage and foam**: The **only grain of truth** is that **pumping too fast might cause splashback, foam, or early nozzle shutoff**, especially in older cars or malfunctioning pumps. That could slightly reduce the amount you get **if you stop early**, but it's not a reliable or meaningful gain.

### Verdict:
⛽ **Pumping slowly doesn’t get you more fuel in any meaningful way.** You're just wasting time. The fuel volume is measured precisely by the pump.

Want more fuel? Fill up early in the morning **only if** the station has above-ground tanks (rare) and you're obsessively optimizing. Otherwise? Doesn’t matter.

## User

Act like a Y Combinator partner reviewing my landing page. You have 10 seconds before you decide “interesting” or “pass.” Be blunt, high-signal, and specific. No compliments unless they’re earned.

Deliverables
	1.	10-second verdict: What does this do? Who is it for? Why now? Would you click/scroll? Yes/No + why.
	2.	Clarity score (0–10): Explain the exact points of confusion.
	3.	Above-the-fold rewrite: Give 3 options for:
	•	Headline (≤ 8 words)
	•	Subhead (≤ 18 words)
	•	CTA (2–4 words)
	4.	The “so what” test: What’s the concrete outcome the user gets? If unclear, rewrite it.
	5.	Differentiation: What makes it non-obvious vs alternatives? If none, propose 2 sharp positioning angles.
	6.	Trust & proof: What proof is missing? List the top 5 proof assets to add (metrics, logos, demo, screenshots, quotes, case study).
	7.	Conversion killers: Top 7 issues hurting signups/bookings, ranked by impact.
	8.	Rewrite the pitch: One tight paragraph + 3 bullets that would make a YC partner get it instantly.
	9.	Fix plan:
	•	Do today (3 items)
	•	Do this week (5 items)
	•	Nice-to-have (3 items)

Tone
Fast, skeptical, operator-minded. Assume the reader is busy and doesn’t care.

Start the review immediately.

## 1. **10-second verdict**
**What does this do?**  
AI-powered link-in-bio that shows each fan *one* next action (subscribe, listen, tip).

**Who is it for?**  
Independent artists releasing music and driving traffic from socials.

**Why now?**  
Creators are drowning in links + want owned audience (email/SMS) instead of rented platforms.

**Would I click/scroll?**  
**Barely. Leaning No.**  
Why: Concept is interesting, but the value is abstract, unproven, and feels like “Linktree + AI” without a hard outcome.

---

## 2. **Clarity score: 6 / 10**
**Main confusions:**
- “AI link-in-bio” is vague. *What exactly changes per fan?*
- I don’t know **what improves**: email capture rate? streams? tips?
- “One action. The right one.” sounds smart but explains nothing.
- No concrete before/after or example flow.
- Is this for *fans* or *artists*? You say both, but sell to neither clearly.

## 4. **The “so what” test**
**Current outcome:** unclear and abstract.

**Rewrite the outcome clearly:**  
> “Artists use Jovie to capture more emails and drive more streams from the same social traffic—without managing multiple links.”

If you can’t confidently say **“X% more emails”** or **“Y% higher click-through”**, you’re hiding the ball.

---

## 5. **Differentiation**
**Right now:**  
Feels like Linktree + smart defaults + analytics. That’s not enough.

**Two sharper positioning angles:**

1. **Conversion optimizer for artists**  
“Jovie is the first link-in-bio that *learns* which action converts each fan—and optimizes automatically.”

2. **Email-first music funnel**  
“Not a link page. A fan capture funnel for artists who want owned audience.”

Pick one. Don’t blend.

---

## 6. **Trust & proof missing (top 5 to add)**
1. **Concrete metric:** “+42% email capture vs Linktree” (even from a pilot)
2. **Screenshot/GIF:** Fan A vs Fan B seeing different actions
3. **Artist quote:** Name, genre, follower count
4. **Before/after flow:** Old link page vs Jovie page
5. **Founder credibility:** Why *you* know this problem (operator story)

Right now there’s **zero evidence** this works.

---

## 7. **Top 7 conversion killers (ranked)**
1. No quantified outcome (biggest sin)
2. “AI” used as a crutch, not explained
3. No visual explanation of personalization
4. Weak CTA urgency (“Request early access” everywhere)
5. Feature list reads generic
6. No social proof at all
7. Repetition without escalation (same message, same CTA)

---

## 8. **Rewrite the pitch (YC-tight)**

**Paragraph:**  
Jovie is a smart link-in-bio for artists that converts traffic instead of wasting it. Instead of showing every fan the same list of links, Jovie shows each fan one optimized next action—subscribe, listen, or tip—based on who they are and where they came from. Artists capture more emails, drive more streams, and stop guessing what works.

**Bullets:**
- Adaptive link that personalizes per fan, automatically  
- Email + SMS capture on first visit, not buried links  
- Real-time learning: every click improves conversion

---

## 9. **Fix plan**

### **Do today**
1. Rewrite headline/subhead around **one concrete outcome**
2. Add a simple diagram or GIF showing personalized actions
3. Replace one CTA with a stronger verb (“See how it works”)

### **Do this week**
1. Ship a basic demo artist page with fake data if needed
2. Add 1 real or beta artist quote
3. Kill redundant copy; escalate value as you scroll
4. Pick *one* positioning angle and commit
5. Add a single hard metric—even if early

### **Nice-to-have**
1. Comparison vs Linktree table (conversion-focused)
2. Short founder story (why you care)
3. FAQ that answers “Is this overkill for small artists?”

---

### Final call (YC style)
**Interesting idea. Weak execution.**  
If you show real conversion lift and sharpen the outcome, this becomes **Interesting** fast.  
Right now: **Pass—but close.**

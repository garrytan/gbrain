## 1) Your real constraint: runway, not ambition
You’ve got rent covered through Jan 1. That’s ~5 weeks. After that, you need **$2–4k/mo** to stay sane and keep building.

So your goal for the next 60 days is not “win big.”

- keep 25–35 hrs/week on the startup

If you miss this, everything else becomes panic-driven.

---

## 2) You are not employable in the normal way — and that’s fine
You already know this, but here’s the useful translation:

**You’re a zero-to-one generalist.**  
Companies mostly hire for **one-to-N specialists.**

So if you go for a 9–5, optimize for roles where *generalist chaos ability* is the product:
- early-stage startups
- “founder’s right hand / special projects”
- product prototyping + ops hybrid
- creative tech / experimental / R&D teams

You’re not an ad-agency PM. You’re the person you drop into a messy room to make a thing exist.

That means if you take “a job,” it should be one of these:
1) **short-term high-leverage contract**

Given your low burn, you don’t need a prestige job. You need **cash with minimal cognitive tax**.

You *can* raise angels right now, but the question is **time-to-check**.

Angels write fast checks when:

**Split story = slow checks.**

If you want angel money quickly:

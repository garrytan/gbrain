## Assistant

That wrench isn’t an “oh crap the car is dying” light, Mr. White – it’s a **maintenance reminder**, not a failure warning.

On a 2016 Mazda CX-5, the **amber/yellow wrench icon** on the dash means:

- The car’s **scheduled maintenance is due** (often oil change, inspection, etc.).
- It’s triggered by **time and/or mileage**, based on what’s set in the car’s maintenance monitor.
- It does **not** mean something is about to immediately break like a check engine light would.

Given:
- 122,000 miles  
- It came on about 10 minutes into the drive

Most likely:
- You’ve hit a mileage/time threshold for an oil change or general service.
- The system waits until you’re moving for a bit before popping the reminder.

### What you should do

1. **Check when you last did:**
   - Oil change
   - Tire rotation
   - Any big service (plugs, fluids, etc.)

2. **If you’re due or close to due**:  
   - Schedule an oil change / general service. Have them:
     - Scan for codes
     - Inspect brakes, fluids, belts, and suspension
     - Reset the maintenance reminder in the infotainment menu after service

3. **If you *just* had service and know you’re not due**:
   - The shop probably **forgot to reset the maintenance monitor**.
   - You can reset it yourself in the settings menu (Settings → Maintenance → Oil Change / Tire Rotation, etc., depending on your specific head unit), or have a shop do it.

### When to worry

If **any of these** are also on, then it’s more serious:

- **Check engine light**
- **Flashing lights, red warnings, or “Stop vehicle” messages**
- Car drives weird (hesitation, rough shifting, overheating, etc.)

In that case, don’t ignore it—get it checked ASAP.

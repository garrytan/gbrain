It's important to consult with a qualified attorney who can offer you personalized legal advice based on your specific circumstances. However, here are some general steps you could consider:

1. Review the lawsuit: Carefully read through the lawsuit documents you received, including the complaint and any supporting documents. Understand the claims being made against you and the relief being sought.

2. Gather your records: Collect all relevant documents, such as credit card statements, payment receipts, correspondence with Amex, and any other records that could support your case. This will help you assess the accuracy of the debt claimed by Amex and prepare your defense.

3. Consult an attorney: Seek legal advice from an experienced attorney who specializes in debt collection or consumer law. They can guide you through the process, analyze your situation, and provide you with the best course of action based on the specifics of your case.

4. Respond to the lawsuit: Generally, you will need to file an answer within a specified timeframe (check the lawsuit documents for the deadline). 5. Negotiate or settle: Depending on your financial situation and the amount of debt involved, you may consider negotiating a settlement with Amex. This could involve agreeing on a repayment plan or a reduced lump sum payment. An attorney can help you negotiate with the credit card company to find a mutually beneficial solution.

6. Attend court proceedings: If the lawsuit proceeds to court, you will likely need to attend hearings. Your attorney can represent you and present your defense. Be sure to comply with all court requirements and deadlines.

Remember, the above steps are general guidance and may vary depending on your jurisdiction and specific circumstances. It's crucial to consult with an attorney who can provide you with professional advice tailored to your situation.

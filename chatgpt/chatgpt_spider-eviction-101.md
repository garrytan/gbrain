### 🕷️ **Immediate Evacuation Protocol (Apartment + Car):**

#### **1. Kill the Webs (Car + Apartment)**
- Vacuum everything—corners, under furniture, vents, cushions, your car’s dash, seats, and steering wheel.
- Toss the vacuum bag or empty the canister *outside* immediately.

#### **2. Nuke Their Hideouts**
- **Apartment:** Spray corners, window sills, under the bed, etc., with a spider killer like Raid or Ortho Home Defense.
- **Car:** Wipe down with diluted vinegar spray (1:1 vinegar to water). Avoid spraying electronics. Spiders hate vinegar.

#### **3. Block Reentry**
- Seal up windows, vents, and cracks in the apartment.
- Close car windows tight when parked. Check for leaks or gaps in door seals.

#### **4. Repel Their Ass**
- **Natural option:** Put peppermint oil + water in a spray bottle. Hit baseboards, car floor mats, vents.
- **Apartment only:** Place sticky traps along walls to catch stragglers.

#### **5. De-Bug the Bite**
- If Olivia’s bump itches, use hydrocortisone or Benadryl cream. If it swells up, gets red, or she feels weird, have a doc check it. Most spider bites are harmless—but don’t gamble.

---

### 🚫 Prevention: Lock the place down
- De-clutter. Spiders love hiding in chaos.
- No food or crumbs. They’re not after food, but their prey is.
- Swap out lights that attract bugs (yellow “bug lights” outside).

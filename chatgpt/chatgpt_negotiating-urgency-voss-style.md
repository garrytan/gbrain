## Assistant

Mr. White, the goal is **create urgency without sounding desperate**, and **avoid promising a hold**.

Send this:

> Totally fair. Sounds like timing is the only issue. I do have someone who can pick them up today, but they’re coming in lower than asking. I’d rather sell them to someone who actually wants them at the listed price.  
>  
> What day next week would you be able to pick them up?

Then, depending on her answer:

### If she says Monday/Tuesday

> That works. I can hold them until then if we lock the pickup time. What time would be easiest for you?

### If she says late week

> Got it. That may be tough on my end because I’m trying to move them sooner. How close could you get to picking them up Monday or Tuesday?

### If she asks you to hold them

> I can hold them with a deposit, otherwise I’d need to keep it first-come-first-served. I just don’t want to turn down buyers and then have the pickup fall through.

### Best direct version

> Totally fair. I have someone who can pick them up today, but they’re lower than asking. I’d rather sell them at listed price if timing works. What’s the soonest next week you could pick them up?

This is the cleanest Voss-style move: label the issue, reveal the real constraint, then ask a calibrated question that makes her solve your problem.

I mean, is it good, though, to say that someone's lowballing me, because then it just opens the door for this person to negotiate a price?

## Assistant

Yes. Avoid saying **“lowballing”** or **“coming in lower.”** It anchors her to the idea that price is flexible.

Better framing: **you have current interest, but timing matters.**

Send:

> Totally fair. I have someone trying to pick them up today, but I’d prefer to sell to you if you’re good with the listed price. What’s the soonest next week you could pick them up?

Even tighter:

> Totally fair. I have active interest for pickup today, but I’d prefer to sell at asking if your timing works. What’s the soonest next week you could pick them up?

If she says Friday or vague “sometime next week”:

> That may be too late on my end. I’m trying to move them sooner. Could you do Monday or Tuesday?

If she wants you to hold them:

> I can hold them with a deposit, otherwise I’ll need to keep it first-come-first-served.

Best overall message:

> Totally fair. I have active interest for pickup today, but I’d prefer to sell at asking if your timing works. What’s the soonest next week you could pick them up?

That protects the price and forces the timing issue.

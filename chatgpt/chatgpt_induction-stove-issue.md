## Assistant

An induction burner that immediately jumps to **“9” (maximum power)** and ignores other levels usually has one of these causes:

### 1. Control panel locked or stuck
Grease, water, or a stuck touch button can make the control read constant input.

Steps:
1. Turn the unit **off at the wall or breaker for ~60 seconds**.
2. Wipe the **entire touch panel completely dry**.
3. Turn it back on and try setting power again.

### 2. Boost / power mode enabled
Some portable or apartment induction units default to **Boost (P or 9)** after power-up.

Look for:
- A **“P”, “Boost”, or flame icon** button.
- Press **Power → then immediately press “–”** to drop from 9.

### 3. Wrong cookware detection
If the pan is **not induction-compatible**, some units jump to max briefly and then behave oddly.

Check:
- Use a **magnetic pan** (a magnet sticks to the bottom).
- Center the pan on the ring.

### 4. Touch button malfunction
Common in rental kitchens. If the **“+” sensor is stuck**, the unit continuously increases to 9.

Test:
- Turn it on **without touching any buttons**.
- If it still goes straight to 9, the touch sensor is likely stuck.

### 5. Child lock mode
Some stoves lock levels except max.

Look for a **lock icon** and try:
- Hold **lock button for 3–5 seconds**, or  
- Hold **“+” and “–” together for ~5 seconds**.

---

### Quick workaround (often works in Airbnbs)
1. Turn **power on**.  
2. **Immediately press and hold the “–” button** until it drops below 9.

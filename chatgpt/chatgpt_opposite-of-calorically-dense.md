## User

What would be the opposite of dense? Like if I was saying something is calorically dense, but I want to say it's not calorically dense, what would be like the antonym?

---

## Assistant

The opposite of **calorically dense** is:

**calorically sparse** or **low in calorie density**.

Other clean alternatives depending on tone:

- **Low-calorie**
- **Low in calories per gram**
- **Not calorie-dense**
- **Calorically light**

"Calorically sparse" is technically correct but less common. The most natural phrasing would be:

> "It's low in calorie density" or "It's not very calorie-dense."

## Assistant

You're exactly right — **"sparse"** implies **scarce quantity**, not necessarily **low density** in the physical or conceptual sense. It's not a true antonym for **"dense"** when referring to concentration per unit.

The cleanest, most accurate antonyms for *calorically dense* would be:

- **Calorically dilute** – precise, scientific, but uncommon in casual use  
- **Low in calorie density** – clear and correct  
- **Not calorie-dense** – plain, informal, still accurate  
- **Low-density (calorically)** – shorthand, used in dietetics  

If you're looking for a concise, accurate term in a fitness/nutrition context, **"low in calorie density"** or **"calorically dilute"** are your best bets.

Fair point, Mr. White.

You're right—**dilution** implies something added to reduce concentration (like water in juice), which isn't the same as just *not being dense*. And **sparse** is about *amount*, not *concentration per unit*. So let’s get surgical:

### “Calorically dense” means:
> High calories per unit of volume or weight.

### The true antonym would mean:
> Low calories per unit of volume or weight.

There’s no single *perfect* word that is the true linguistic opposite of **dense** in this exact context. But here’s the best option:

---

**Calorically lean**.

> It's short, intuitive, and aligns with existing usage (e.g. “lean protein” = low-calorie per gram). It communicates the idea of *low caloric density* without invoking dilution or scarcity.

---

If you need something a little more formal or scientific:

- **Low-calorie-density**
- **Not energy-dense**

But if you want to say it like a human, not a textbook:  
> “It’s calorically lean.”  
That’s the move.

## Assistant

A bartender would typically prepare a Paloma following these steps:

1. Fill a highball glass with ice.
2. Add the tequila and lime juice over the ice.
3. Top up the glass with grapefruit soda.
4. Stir gently to mix the ingredients.
5. Garnish with a slice of lime or a grapefruit wedge.

Some variations might include adding a salt rim to the glass or using fresh grapefruit juice topped with club soda instead of grapefruit-flavored soda.

## Assistant

Certainly, Mr. White. Here's a classic Paloma cocktail recipe:

- 2 oz Tequila (typically blanco for its crisp, clear flavor)
- 1/2 oz Lime juice (freshly squeezed)
- Grapefruit soda to top (about 3-4 oz)
- Optional: pinch of salt

Instructions:
1. Take a highball glass and, if desired, rim it with salt.
2. Fill the glass with ice cubes.
3. Pour in the tequila and lime juice.
4. Top with grapefruit soda to taste.
5. Stir gently.
6. Garnish with a lime wheel or grapefruit slice.

## Assistant

Grapefruit soda refers to a carbonated soft drink that is grapefruit-flavored. It's typically sweet with a slightly bitter undertone from the grapefruit. Brands that make grapefruit soda include Squirt, Fresca, and Jarritos, which are commonly used in making a Paloma cocktail. These sodas add a sweet citrus flavor and effervescence to the drink.

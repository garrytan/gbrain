Elderflower liquor, also known as elderflower liqueur, is a sweet, floral alcoholic beverage made from the flowers of the elderberry plant. It is often used in cocktails for its delicate and aromatic flavor. One of the most popular brands of elderflower liqueur is St-Germain. This liqueur pairs well with a variety of spirits, including gin, vodka, and champagne, and can be used to create refreshing and sophisticated drinks such as the Elderflower Collins, the St-Germain Spritz, and the Elderflower Martini.

Elderflower liqueur, like most liqueurs, has a relatively long shelf life if stored properly. Here are some guidelines:

### Shelf Life of Elderflower Liqueur:

1. **Unopened Bottle:**
   - **Shelf Life:** Indefinite, if stored in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight and heat sources.
   - **Conditions:** Keep the bottle tightly sealed to prevent oxidation and evaporation.

2. **Opened Bottle:**
   - **Shelf Life:** Up to 3-5 years.
   - **Conditions:** Store in a cool, dark place, and ensure the cap is tightly sealed after each use to maintain the quality and prevent oxidation.

### Tips for Maintaining Quality:
- **Avoid Temperature Fluctuations:** Store the liqueur in a stable environment, avoiding extreme temperatures.
- **Refrigeration:** It's not necessary to refrigerate elderflower liqueur, but doing so can help preserve its flavor if you prefer to use it chilled.
- **Check for Changes:** Before using an older bottle, check for any changes in color, smell, or taste. If the liqueur smells off or has an unusual taste, it's best to discard it.

Following these guidelines will help ensure that your elderflower liqueur remains fresh and flavorful for as long as possible.

If you're pre-batching elderflower liqueur cocktails (mixing the drink components in advance), the shelf life will depend on a few factors:

### Shelf Life of Pre-Batched Elderflower Cocktails:

1. **Ingredients and Alcohol Content:**
   - Cocktails with a higher alcohol content (like martinis) tend to have a longer shelf life.
   - Ingredients like fresh citrus juice can reduce shelf life due to their tendency to spoil or lose freshness.

2. **Storage Conditions:**
   - Store in a clean, airtight container to prevent oxidation.
   - Keep refrigerated to maintain freshness and slow down any potential spoilage.

### General Guidelines:

1. **Refrigerated Pre-Batched Cocktails:**
   - **Shelf Life:** Typically 2-3 days, but can be up to a week if the alcohol content is high and no perishable ingredients (like fresh juice) are used.
   - **Example:** An Elderflower Collins pre-batched with gin, elderflower liqueur, and lemon juice should be consumed within 2-3 days for optimal flavor.

2. **Without Fresh Ingredients:**
   - If the pre-batched mix contains only spirits and liqueurs without fresh ingredients, it can last much longer, up to several weeks when kept refrigerated.

### Tips for Pre-Batching:
- **Labeling:** Always label the batch with the date it was made to keep track of its age.
- **Mix Fresh Ingredients Later:** If possible, add fresh ingredients like lemon juice or club soda just before serving to ensure the best taste and freshness.

By following these guidelines, you can enjoy pre-batched elderflower cocktails with confidence in their quality and safety.

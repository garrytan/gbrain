## Assistant

It looks like Airbnb Support decided the listing didn’t break their current rules, even though the host’s own screenshots show interior cameras. A few key points:

**1. Airbnb’s Policy**  
Airbnb allows exterior cameras if disclosed, but interior cameras (or cameras facing sleeping/bathroom areas) must be fully disclosed and turned off during stays. See: [airbnb.com/help/article/887](https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/887) for the exact wording.

**2. Possible Reasons They Closed It**  
- **They interpreted the photos differently.** The host might have argued those images were from before guest check-in (e.g., during cleaning) or from a non-active system.  
- **Policy changes/loopholes.** Airbnb updated camera policies in 2024 and again in 2025; they might be applying an older standard if the stay predates the new rule.  
- **Evidence framing.** If the host claimed the cameras were disclosed elsewhere (e.g., in “house rules”) or claimed the screenshots came from temporary maintenance monitoring, support may have accepted it.

**3. Next Steps You Can Take**  
- **Appeal/Request Escalation:** Reply to the closed thread or start a new one referencing the case ID, clearly citing the current policy language and attaching the screenshots again.  
- **Document Everything:** Save copies of the host’s screenshots, the listing description stating “driveway only,” and your original report.  
- **Leave a Review:** Your review can factually note the undisclosed cameras so future guests are warned.  
- **Local Law Enforcement/Privacy Law:** California has strict laws against recording in areas with a reasonable expectation of privacy. If you feel strongly, you can file a complaint with local authorities or the California Attorney General’s office.

Concise appeal template you can adapt:

> “Hi, I’m requesting an escalation of case #____. The host’s own screenshots show active interior cameras in bedrooms and living areas. The listing states cameras only monitor the driveway. This violates Airbnb’s current camera policy (see airbnb.com/help/article/887). Please review the attached evidence and reconsider.”

This keeps it factual and cites the policy directly.

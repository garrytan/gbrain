**If you want to look better / perform better (recomp or muscle gain):**
1. **Stop the aggressive cut.** Bring calories up to maintenance over ~2–4 weeks.  
   - Add ~150–200 kcal/day each week until weight trend stabilizes.
2. **Protein:** 0.8–1.0 g/lb bodyweight = **160–180 g/day**.  
3. **Fats:** don’t go too low: **50–70 g/day** minimum.  
4. **Carbs:** fill the rest for training performance.
5. **Train like RP style hypertrophy:**  
   - **3–5 days/wk** lifting.  
   - **10–20 hard sets per muscle per week**, mostly 6–15 reps.  
   - Work at **1–3 reps in reserve (RIR)**, add reps/weight over time.  
   - Deload when performance stalls and fatigue is high (often every 4–8 weeks).
6. **Steps/NEAT:** keep consistent, **7–10k steps/day**. Don’t “accidentally” add tons of cardio and stay in a deficit.

**If you actually want to keep losing fat:**  
You’re close to the practical floor for most men. Instead of more loss:  
- **Hold maintenance 4–8 weeks** to recover training output/hormones/NEAT.  
- Then only a small deficit to lose **0.25–0.5% BW/week** (~0.4–0.8 lb/week for you).  
If strength, libido, sleep, or mood are dropping, you’re pushing too far.

**How to track better**
- Weigh daily, but judge progress by a **weekly average** (Apple’s smoothed line is the right idea).  
- If weekly average isn’t moving as intended for 2 straight weeks, adjust calories by **±150–250/day**.

**Bottom line:** at your size, the highest-ROI “improvement” is likely **a maintenance phase and then a lean bulk**. Aim to gain **0.25–0.5% BW/week** (same 0.4–0.8 lb/week), keep lifts progressing, and you’ll look dramatically better at 175–185 than at 162.

## Assistant

**Default alive** (in a startup context) means your business, *at its current trajectory*, will **reach profitability before it runs out of cash** — without needing new outside investment. It’s a financial health check on your unit economics and runway. citeturn0search1turn0search2

Key facts:  
- Coined by Paul Graham (Y Combinator). citeturn0search0  
- **Default alive =** with your *current growth rate* and *burn rate*, you’ll hit **break-even before cash goes to zero**. citeturn0search6  
- **Default dead =** you’ll deplete your runway before reaching profitability unless you get more capital or make radical changes. citeturn0search1  

In practice this frames two strategic paths:  
1. **Default alive** startups can focus on execution and controlled growth because they don’t *depend* on fundraising to survive. citeturn0search2  
2. **Default dead** startups must either cut burn, accelerate revenue growth, or plan to raise more money. citeturn0search1

It’s not merely current profitability — it’s whether your *projected* path gets you there before cash runs out. citeturn0search6

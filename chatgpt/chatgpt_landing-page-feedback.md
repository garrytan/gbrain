## Assistant

Mr. White, the biggest issue is focus: the page mixes a marketing landing page with what looks like an in-app dashboard UI. That creates confusion about what the product is and what the visitor should do next.

### Primary problems
- **Two experiences on one page:** You have the hero (“smartest link in bio”) and then a full dashboard-style interface (Inbox, My Releases, Releases table, etc.). A new visitor will read this as clutter or think they accidentally landed inside the app.
- **Unclear “AI” value:** “Let Jovie’s AI build a beautifully optimized profile designed to convert” is vague. Convert to what (streams, follows, saves, email/SMS opt-ins, merch sales)?
- **Weak first-screen proof:** “Trusted by artists on Sony/UMG/AWAL/Armada” is ambiguous (artists signed there, employees there, partnerships?). Visitors will discount it without specificity.
- **CTA friction without payoff:** “Claim handle” asks for commitment immediately. Many visitors want to see an example profile and outcomes before choosing a handle.
- **No concrete outcomes / differentiation:** “Smartest link in bio” and “opinionated by design” don’t clearly separate you from Linktree/Beacons/Hypeddit/Toneden style tools in the first 10 seconds.
- **Missing pricing / plan cue:** If it’s paid, visitors want an anchor (“Free plan available” / “Starts at $X”). If it’s free-to-start, say that above the fold.
- **Credibility gaps:** No screenshots of the actual product editor/dashboard (ironically you show a dashboard-like UI but not framed as a demo), no testimonials with names/roles, no measurable claims (“+18% pre-saves”, etc.).
- **Hierarchy and scanning:** Too many sections and labels competing (smart links, releases, signals, payments/tips, tour, contact) without a clear narrative flow.
- **Handle field UX:** The “jov.ie/ + input” pattern is good, but you don’t show availability, examples, or what happens after clicking (preview, edit, connect Spotify).

### Fast fixes that move metrics
- **Pick one page purpose:** Landing page for acquisition. Move all dashboard UI into a separate “Product” section with 2–3 annotated screenshots or a short demo video.
- **Rewrite hero to be outcome-specific:** Example: “Turn every Instagram/TikTok click into the right stream, follow, or pre-save—automatically.” (Use your real core outcome.)
- **Add an immediate proof block:** 1–2 artist testimonials + a “See a live example” link/button to a real Jovie profile.
- **Offer a low-commitment primary CTA:** “View demo profile” / “See how it works” next to “Claim handle.” Keep “Claim handle” as the secondary CTA for cold traffic.
- **Make differentiation explicit:** One tight comparison line: “Auto-syncs every release from Spotify + builds smart links per fan’s platform/location.” (Whatever is actually true.)
- **Add pricing cue:** Even “Free to start” reduces drop-off.
- **Clarify the “trusted by” claim:** Replace with “Used by artists signed to…” or list recognizable artist names (with permission), or remove it.

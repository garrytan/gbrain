## The blunt diagnosis

Publicly, Jovie currently looks like **a music-native Linktree with fan capture, tips, bookings, drops, and streaming actions**. The site headline is “The link your music deserves,” with “streams, drops, tips, bookings, and fan capture in a single page,” and social proof from artists on AWAL, The Orchard, UMG, Armada, Black Hole, Disco Wax, and others. citeturn979055view0

That is understandable, but it is too small for the biggest possible pre-seed unless you reframe it.

The fundable version is:

> **Jovie is the owned fan graph and monetization layer for artists. The first wedge is a high-converting music link page; the real company is first-party fan capture, conversion attribution, and direct-to-fan commerce infrastructure.**

That shift matters because the market is rewarding companies that can become infrastructure, data layers, AI-native workflow systems, or large consumer networks. Carta says VC capital is increasingly concentrated into fewer, bigger rounds, with AI changing “what normal looks like”; round count fell while capital and valuations rose. citeturn946694view1

## Best path: run a staged, momentum-driven raise

Do **not** pitch this as “we’re raising the biggest pre-seed possible.” Pitch it as:

> “We’re raising $1.5M now, with room to take up to $3M if the right strategic investors come in. One investor is already committed. We’re closing the first tranche quickly.”

That gives you scarcity and optionality. A huge stated target with only $25k committed makes the round look soft. A smaller target with a hard-cap expansion lets you manufacture oversubscription.

### Recommended round design

| Scenario | Raise | Cap | When to use |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Base | $1.0M–$1.5M | $12M–$15M post-money SAFE | If traction is mostly product + founder + early users |
| Strong | $2M–$2.5M | $18M–$25M post-money SAFE | If you have meaningful artist activation, retention, or revenue |
| Aggressive | $3M–$4M | $25M–$35M post-money SAFE | If you have a lead, strategic checks, clear growth, or distribution access |
| Extreme | $5M+ | $35M+ or priced round | Only with major institutional demand or unusually strong traction |

Carta’s 2025 pre-seed data shows post-money SAFEs with valuation caps remain standard, with median caps around **$10M** for $250k–$1M rounds and **$15M** for $1M–$2.5M rounds. citeturn587967view2 Carta also says pre-seed is now barbell-shaped: more tiny rounds and some much larger rounds, but rounds above $5M were only about **8%** of Q4 pre-seed rounds. citeturn587967view1

Use a **YC post-money SAFE, valuation cap, no discount** unless counsel has a reason to customize. YC’s official SAFE docs include a U.S. post-money SAFE with “Valuation Cap, no Discount,” and Carta says SAFEs are the pre-seed standard. citeturn605382search0turn587967view1

## The highest-probability fundraising motion

### Phase 1: close $250k–$500k without institutional process

Goal: turn “1 x $25k check” into “$300k+ committed” fast.

Target these first:

1. Existing committed investor’s top 10 intros.
2. Music operators, artist managers, label/distribution people.
3. Creator economy angels.
4. Founders/operators from Patreon, Linktree, Beacons, Laylo, Bandsintown, Stationhead, EVEN, SoundCloud, Spotify, Discord, Twitch, Substack, Whatnot, Kajabi, ConvertKit, and Shopify ecosystem.
5. Artists with real business sense and managers who already understand the pain.

This is where “no call” can work. Funds usually need a call. Angels and operators can commit from a tight memo if the deal is simple.

Use an AngelList/Rollups-style vehicle for small strategic checks if you expect many $10k–$25k investors. AngelList says Roll Up Vehicles let founders raise from many investors through one entity and reduce cap-table friction. citeturn943543search1turn943543search5

### Phase 2: use that momentum to get pre-seed funds moving

Once you have $250k–$500k committed, start institutional outreach with:

> “We’re raising $1.5M, have $X committed, and are reserving $500k–$1M for one or two funds who can help with creator/music distribution.”

The goal is to create a clock. Investors move when they believe they can lose the deal.

### Phase 3: expand only if the round gets competitive

When you pass $1M committed, change the language:

> “We’re likely to expand to $2.5M–$3M because of demand and strategic value. We’re prioritizing investors who can help us win artist distribution.”

That lets you maximize dollars without looking desperate.

## Positioning: stop selling “link page”

The investor headline should be:

> **Jovie turns every artist link into owned fan relationships and measurable revenue.**

Sub-headline:

> **Artists rent audiences from Spotify, TikTok, Instagram, and ticketing platforms. Jovie gives them an owned fan graph across streams, drops, tips, bookings, and direct communication.**

Why this is fundable:

The recorded music market is still growing: IFPI says global recorded music revenue grew **6.4% to $31.7B in 2025**, streaming surpassed **$22B**, and paid subscription streaming accounted for **52.4%** of global revenues. citeturn982306view0 The superfan/direct-to-fan push is also active: Luminate reports U.S. music superfans grew from **18% in 2023 to 20% in 2025**, and UMG has been partnering with or investing in superfan/direct-to-fan platforms like Weverse, Stationhead, and EVEN. citeturn982306view2

The market is already funding adjacent plays. Business Insider reported that Sesh raised a **$5M seed** to help artists connect with fans through mobile wallets, fan cards, campaigns, and AI-driven analytics. citeturn718922search2

Jovie’s pitch should make investors believe you are in that category, not in the commodity “link-in-bio” category.

## The memo that should close no-call angels

Build a 1-page memo and a 3-minute Loom. Send both. The memo should say:

**Jovie**  
The owned fan graph for artists.

**Problem**

**Product**  
A music-native fan capture and monetization page: streams, drops, tips, bookings, notifications, ticketing, and first-party fan collection in one place.

**Why now**  
Streaming keeps growing, but artist monetization is shifting toward superfans, direct-to-fan commerce, and owned audience infrastructure. AI makes it cheaper to build and operate, while labels and artists are actively looking for fan data and monetization layers.

**Traction**

**Founder-market fit**

Product, artist acquisition, conversion instrumentation, distribution partnerships, and one senior growth/engineering hire.

**Why this can be huge**  
Every artist needs a fan-owned conversion layer. The wedge is the link. The end state is the artist CRM, attribution graph, and commerce layer.

Actions:

- 25 warm intro asks
- 50 direct angel emails
- 20 founder/operator DMs
- Ask the $25k investor for 5 specific intros
- Ask every “yes” for 3 more intros before closing the call
- Send Friday momentum update: “$X committed, first tranche closing”

### Week 2: start funds

Goal: 20–30 fund conversations.

Actions:

- Send 50 tightly personalized fund emails
- Take only relevant calls
- Push low-conviction investors to the memo
- Ask every fund: “What would you need to see to get to conviction in the next 7 days?”
- Do not let investors slow-walk diligence without giving you a clear process

### Week 3: force decisions

Goal: first close.

Actions:

- “We’re doing first close Friday”
- Convert maybes to smaller checks
- Tell strategic angels “we can include you at $25k through the vehicle”
- Ask funds to either lead, follow, or pass
- Update target only if committed demand justifies it

### Week 4: oversubscribe

Goal: expand to $2M–$3M if momentum exists.

Actions:

- Announce first close privately
- Reopen “last allocation” to higher-value funds/angels
- Raise the cap only if counsel approves and early investors are handled cleanly
- Close the round instead of endlessly optimizing

## What needs to be true to raise the biggest version

To get $3M–$5M, one of these must be true:

1. A known fund leads.
2. A strategic music investor writes a meaningful check.
3. You have real artist growth: thousands of claimed profiles or a very fast growth curve.
4. You have revenue or GMV.
5. You have a distribution partner with access to artists.
6. You have a credible AI/data layer that makes Jovie more than a page builder.
7. You create competitive heat with multiple funds.

With only one $25k check and no visible metrics, the realistic best move is to **manufacture momentum first**, then expand.

## Product/GTM move that will most improve the round

Build or package a **“fan capture benchmark report”** from your own data.

Example:

> “Across Jovie profiles, X% of visitors click a monetizable action, Y% opt into fan capture, and artists using Jovie see Z% higher conversion to streams/tickets/tips than generic link-in-bio pages.”

That single artifact makes the company look like a data company, not a profile-page company.

Second-best move: sign 3–5 credible artist/manager/label/distributor pilots with measurable traffic.

Third-best move: create a “Jovie for Managers” dashboard showing all artists, fan captures, campaign performance, and monetization actions. That moves the buyer from individual artists to managers/labels.

## Legal/process caution

Keep the round process clean. If this is a private Regulation D raise, confirm with counsel whether you are operating under 506(b) or 506(c). SEC guidance says 506(c) permits general solicitation only if all purchasers are accredited investors and the issuer takes reasonable steps to verify accredited status; Form D is generally due within 15 days after first sale. citeturn966129search4

## The best path in one sentence

Raise a **$1.5M target / $3M hard-cap pre-seed** on a clean post-money SAFE, use the $25k check to rapidly close $250k–$500k from strategic angels, reframe Jovie from “music link page” to “owned fan graph for artists,” then use that momentum to pull in one or two creator/music/pre-seed funds and expand only if demand becomes real.

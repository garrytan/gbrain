### What’s most likely happening
- **LA Fitness (or a contractor) is using a commercial “music-for-business/fitness” service** with pre-built, high-BPM, high-energy rotations (often with lots of EDM/alt-pop that *feels* “non-mainstream” but is popular in fitness contexts). ### Why it *feels* like “they’re playing my stuff”
- Your songs may be **common in fitness-curated pools** even if they aren’t Top 40 radio.
- If those tracks match typical workout targets (tempo/energy), they get reused a lot across gyms.
- Hearing **3 “your” songs in an hour** is very plausible when a location is cycling a few hundred tracks (and your personal taste overlaps that niche).

### The “are they syncing to who checked in?” idea
Technically possible in the abstract, but **unlikely in practice**:
- They’d need to link **your check-in identity** to **your Spotify identity** (or ad ID) and then dynamically change in-club music in near-real time.
- That’s a privacy/compliance headache, and there’s no public indication LA Fitness does this kind of per-attendee music personalization.
- Also, in-store music systems are usually designed for **consistent brand vibe**, not 50-person real-time personalization.

### Quick ways to verify (no guessing)
- **Shazam 10–20 songs** over a few visits. If you see lots of “workout-friendly” edits/clean versions and the same patterns, it’s curated programming—not you.
- Look near the AV rack/front desk for a **media player brand** (Rockbot/Mood/PlayNetwork/etc.).
- Ask staff: “Is the music a corporate feed (like FITRADIO/Mood Media) or something local?”

1. **Determine the target concentration**: If you aim for a typical disinfection concentration (around 0.5%), you need to dilute your bleach.

2. **Calculate the amount**: For 6% bleach, you would need about 1.33 ounces (or approximately 2.7 tablespoons) of bleach in a 16-ounce bottle to achieve a 0.5% concentration.

   - Fill the spray bottle with 1.33 ounces of bleach.
   - Add water to fill the rest of the bottle (up to 16 ounces).

Always label your bottle and store it safely. If you need a different concentration, adjust the amount of bleach accordingly.

## Assistant

To create a 0.5% bleach solution from 6% bleach in a 16-ounce spray bottle using milliliters (mL):

1. **Total Volume of Spray Bottle**: 16 ounces = 473 mL

2. **Calculate the amount of bleach needed**:
   - Target bleach concentration = 0.5%
   - Initial bleach concentration = 6%

   \[
   \text{Amount of bleach needed} = \left(\frac{0.5\%}{6\%}\right) \times 473 \, \text{mL} \approx 39.4 \, \text{mL}
   \]

3. **Dilution**:
   - Add 39.4 mL of bleach to the spray bottle.
   - Fill the rest of the bottle with water (473 mL - 39.4 mL = 433.6 mL).

This will give you a 0.5% bleach solution in the 16-ounce spray bottle.

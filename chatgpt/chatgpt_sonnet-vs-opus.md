## Assistant

Here’s the clear distinction — **they aren’t comparable categories**; they’re different things entirely:

### 📜 **Sonnet**
- A *sonnet* is a **specific poetic form** — a type of poem with a fixed structure.  
- Traditionally it’s **14 lines** with a set rhyme and rhythm scheme (most often iambic pentameter). It’s a literary genre used to express ideas, emotions, arguments, etc. citeturn0search7

### 🎼 **Opus**
- *Opus* is a **general term meaning “work”** (from Latin) used across arts, especially music.  
- In music it refers to a **musical composition** or set of compositions, often numbered to indicate publication order (e.g., *Beethoven’s Opus 27*). citeturn0search3turn0search39  
- It isn’t a genre or form — just a label for a creative “work.”

### Key difference
- **Sonnet** = a *form of poem*.  
- **Opus** = a *work* (often in music) or a catalogue identifier for creative output.  
- One is genre/structure, the other is a naming/catalog convention.  

They aren’t interchangeable; you could talk about a musical *opus* or a poetic *opus*, but *sonnet* always refers to that 14-line poem form.

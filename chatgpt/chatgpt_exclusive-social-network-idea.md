## Assistant

First, this would be incredibly fun to build. I know a few people who have experienced this problem.

The obvious concern is the economics. All members-only clubs, at some point, face the same challenge: growth devalues the product.

Soho House has said they will likely never be profitable. Raya has become uncool because "everyone has it now".

You either let more people in and devalue the community, or you risk not returning to your investors.

Now, I think you can solve for this a bit. First off, 10k/yr for 10k users does (with some risks) mean 100m ARR. Personally, I would charge more than 10k and require an initiation fee so canceling feels like greater friction than continuing to pay.

You're capped on your user numbers, but you can always scale revenue by offering additional products/services. There's a huge opportunity with brand partnerships for membership perks.

But I don't think there would be much VC appetite for this.

Even though it would be easy to build, ensuring engagement and launching perfectly among the right set of people to overcome the cold start problem would take some work, so I think this is too risky to bootstrap. That's why I didn't build it.

The other concern is a solution already exists: Twitter DMs/mentions between verified users.

It used to be easy enough to ping another celeb and exchange numbers over DM. The verified feed lets you cut above the noise.

However, that's changed in the last week. Twitter no longer solves the problem.

So I think there is a need for this.

If you were to build it, you need to really make it expensive to break the rules. It has to be a safe space, or it doesn't work. Loss of membership might not be enough. Random selfie checks to make sure you're not sharing your account (like Uber drivers have to do) would be essential. And it would have to be incredibly secure.

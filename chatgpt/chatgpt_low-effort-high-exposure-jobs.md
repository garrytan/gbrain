### 1. **Host/Spokesperson Gigs (Local or National)**
- **Hotel TV Networks** (e.g., Enseo, LodgeNet, Sonifi): Reach out directly or via agencies that place talent in travel/hospitality content.

### 2. **Brand-Affiliated Livestream or Social Host**
- Think: **Buzzfeed, Insider, Tastemade, WhatNot, QVC-type social streams**. Often filmed in one day, and brands handle all distribution.
- Benefits: Name/tag overlays are consistent, and your social links can be included in pinned comments or credits.

### 3. **Reality-Lite Roles or Recurring Guest Talent**
- Appear as a **celebrity judge**, **guest DJ**, or **“expert” host** on lower-tier reality or YouTube competition shows (e.g., casting calls via Backstage, Project Casting, or via influencer agencies).
- These roles usually introduce you with name and credentials, so they build brand value even if the subject matter is fluffy.

### 4. **Branded Content for Emerging Startups**
- Many **new apps or tech products** (especially in fitness, lifestyle, or dating) want charismatic faces to be “the guy” in their onboarding videos, explainers, or ads. Paid well, filmed quickly, and your name often gets tagged in posts.

### 5. **LA-Based Experiential or Immersive Hosts**
- Companies like **Meow Wolf, Fever, Secret LA**, or **Haunted Hayride-style venues** sometimes film and loop preshow content or have filmed host segments that run constantly. These are often union or near-union gigs but offer recurring face time.

## User

What should I do for light easy breakfast. I need something lower in fat since that upsets my stomach earlier in the day. And I need enough carbs to get me to the gym and take my vitamins and supplements with. I’ll use a genius shot to up the protein by 23g so I don’t mind if it’s not great in protein but it should have some.

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, here are a few light, easy breakfasts that are **low-fat**, **carb-forward**, and have **some protein** (your 23 g shot can cover the rest). I’ll keep these simple and gym-friendly.

**1) Banana oatmeal bowl (go-to option)**
- **What:** 1/2–2/3 cup dry rolled oats cooked in water + 1 banana + cinnamon + a drizzle of honey or maple.
- **Why:** Oats are mostly carbs with a little protein and low fat by default. citeturn0search21turn0search3  
- **Rough macros:** ~55–75 g carbs, ~8–12 g protein, ~3–5 g fat depending on portion.  
- **Optional protein bump (still low fat):** stir in 1/2 cup **nonfat Greek yogurt** after cooking, or add liquid egg whites on the side.

**2) Cream of rice / rice cereal + fruit**
- **What:** 1 packet or ~1 cup cooked cream of rice (or Rice Chex / puffed rice cereal) made with water + berries/banana + a little sugar/honey.
- **Why:** Very low fat, easy on the stomach, fast carbs for training.
- **Rough macros:** ~50–80 g carbs, ~4–8 g protein, ~0–2 g fat.

**3) Toast or bagel + jam + nonfat dairy**
- **What:** 1–2 slices sourdough/white toast *or* a plain bagel + jam/honey + 1/2–1 cup **nonfat cottage cheese** or **nonfat Greek yogurt**.
- **Why:** Bread/bagel = quick carbs; nonfat dairy adds protein without much fat.
- **Rough macros:** ~60–90 g carbs, ~15–25 g protein, ~1–4 g fat (depending on bread/dairy).

**4) Quick smoothie (low-fat)**
- **What:** Blend: 1 banana + 1 cup frozen berries + 1 cup orange juice or skim milk + 1/3–1/2 cup dry oats.
- **Why:** Easy to get down early, high carbs, minimal fat.
- **Rough macros:** ~70–100 g carbs, ~8–15 g protein, ~2–4 g fat.

---

### Tiny digestion/absorption note
If any of your vitamins/supps are **fat-soluble (A, D, E, K) or omega-3s**, they absorb better with **some fat**. citeturn1search1turn1search2turn1search4  
Since higher fat upsets your stomach in the morning, two easy workarounds:
- take those specific ones **later with a fattier meal**, or  
- include a **very small fat dose** that you tolerate (e.g., **1 tsp olive oil** mixed into oats, or a small serving of low-fat yogurt). Even a little can help without making breakfast “high fat.” citeturn1search1

---

If you want one single default: **banana oatmeal + nonfat Greek yogurt**. It’s low-fat, steady carbs, and gentle enough for most stomachs.

## Assistant

To keep a cherry garnish on top of a drink, you can use a couple of methods:

1. **Skewering**: Place the cherry on a small skewer or cocktail stick and rest it across the top of the glass. This works well for cocktails served in wide-rimmed glasses.

2. **Surface Tension**: If you're using maraschino cherries, they're often dense enough to float. Gently place the cherry on the surface of the drink. It should stay afloat if the drink isn't too fizzy.

3. **Glass Rim Placement**: You can also place the cherry on the rim of the glass. Make a small slit at the bottom of the cherry and perch it on the glass rim.

4. **Frozen Cherries**: Freeze the cherries before adding them to the drink. Frozen cherries are less likely to sink.

Remember, the success of these methods can vary depending on the type of drink and its carbonation level.

## Assistant

For a cinnamon stick garnish, here are a few methods to ensure it stays in place on top of a drink:

1. **Leaning on the Rim**: Simply place the cinnamon stick so that it leans against the side of the glass. This works best with taller glasses.

2. **Submerging Partially**: If the drink is non-carbonated, you can partially submerge the cinnamon stick. It'll float and add flavor while being a visual garnish.

3. **Skewering with Other Ingredients**: If you're using other garnishes like fruit, you can skewer them together with the cinnamon stick. This creates an attractive and stable garnish.

4. **Resting Across the Glass**: For wider glasses, you can rest the cinnamon stick across the top of the glass, parallel to the rim.

The method you choose might also depend on the effect you want. For example, partially submerging the cinnamon stick can infuse the drink with more flavor.

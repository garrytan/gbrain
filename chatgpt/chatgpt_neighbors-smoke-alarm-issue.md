## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s the practical way to handle this in **Los Angeles**—step by step, escalating only if needed.

### 1. Try a direct, calm fix (if safe)
If you’re comfortable:
- Knock once.
- Say plainly: *“Your smoke alarm has been going off for hours and it’s affecting other units.”*
Sometimes people genuinely tune alarms out.

If you **don’t feel safe**, skip this.

### 2. Contact building management **immediately**
This is usually the fastest and cleanest solution.
- Call the on-call manager or emergency maintenance line.
- Frame it as **a safety issue**, not a noise complaint:
  - Continuous alarm
  - Possible smoke condition
  - Potential fire risk

Most leases prohibit disabling alarms or creating ongoing nuisance conditions.

### 3. If it continues: call **LA non-emergency**
If management doesn’t respond or it’s after hours:
- Call **LAPD non-emergency**: **(877) 275-5273**
- Or use the **311 LA** service
Tell them:
- Smoke alarm sounding for hours
- Repeated incidents
- Possible smoke accumulation

They can do a welfare/safety check without treating it like a crime.

### 4. If there’s heavy smoke or you suspect danger
Do **not** hesitate:
- Call **911**
A continuously sounding alarm + visible smoke is legally a fire safety concern, even if it’s “just incense.”

### 5. Protect yourself short-term
- Close windows/doors facing their unit
- Run a fan or air purifier if you have one
- Document dates/times (helps if it becomes a chronic issue)

### 6. If this is habitual
You may have grounds for:
- A **formal nuisance complaint** to management
- A written notice citing habitability and quiet enjoyment under California tenant law

- Apartment vs. condo
- Renter or owner
- Whether management has ignored this before

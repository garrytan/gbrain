## Assistant

What you’re doing there is making your answer **absolute instead of relative**.  

If you just said “tomorrow,” the meaning depends on when the other person reads it—today, tomorrow, or even days later. By saying **“Sept 10”**, you anchor the response to a fixed calendar date. That way, it’s always correct regardless of when the message is opened or reread.  

In short:  
- **Relative phrasing** (“tomorrow”) changes meaning over time.  
- **Absolute phrasing** (“Sept 10”) stays true no matter when it’s read.

## Limitations / performance tradeoffs

- The adapter can only do so much: it won’t turn the Wii into HD. The Wii outputs at max **480p (progressive scan) or 480i** (or 576 in PAL versions). You’ll be upscaling that to HDMI.  
- Some older adapters may introduce lag or artifacts (blurring, ghosting) especially in fast motion scenes.  
- The fidelity depends heavily on the quality of the converter’s electronics (especially how well it de-interlaces 480i, handles color, etc.).  
- You’ll be limited to the original Wii’s library, no modern games, etc.
- Because you have RVL-001, you retain GameCube compatibility. That’s a plus — many later Wiis removed that. citeturn0search5turn0search9turn0search4turn0search17

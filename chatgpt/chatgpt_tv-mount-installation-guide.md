## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s a concise, step-by-step:

1. **Gather tools & hardware**  
   – Stud finder, pencil, tape-measure, ⅜″ & ¼″ drill bits, cordless drill/socket driver, level, helper.  
   – Use the mount’s lag bolts, washers and TV-side screws.

2. **Plan height & locate studs**  
   – For a 65″ TV, aim for screen center ~42″ off the floor.  
   – Run stud-finder across wall; mark two studs ~16″ apart (standard spacing).

3. **Position & mark the wall plate**  
   – Hold wall plate on your stud marks, use a level to square it.  
   – Mark all bolt-hole centers with pencil.

4. **Drill pilot holes**  
   – Drill into each stud with a ⅜″ bit about 2½″ deep.  
   – Keep drill perfectly perpendicular.

5. **Secure wall plate**  
   – Insert lag bolts + washers through plate into studs.  
   – Tighten snugly—don’t over-torque.

6. **Attach brackets to TV**  
   – Lay TV face-down on a soft surface.  
   – Align the mount arms with the TV’s VESA holes, use the correct screws and spacers.  
   – Tighten until firm, not crushing.

7. **Hang TV**  
   – With your helper, lift and hook the TV brackets onto the wall plate.  
   – Ensure both sides latch fully; engage any safety screws/clips.

8. **Level & secure**  
   – Check TV is level.  
   – Tighten any final lock screws on the wall plate.

9. **Connect cables & manage wires**  
   – Plug in HDMI/power.  
   – For a clean look, use an in-wall rated cable kit or surface cable cover.

10. **Final check**  
   – Tilt/swivel to confirm smooth motion.  
   – Give each bolt a last check for tightness.

> **Pro tip:** Unplug or remove that wall-plug air freshener before drilling.

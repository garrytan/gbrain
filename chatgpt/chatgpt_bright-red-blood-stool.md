## Assistant

Mr. White — **lots of bright red blood in the stool is not something to watch-and-wait on.** Even if it can be from hemorrhoids, “lots” suggests a **significant lower GI bleed** that needs **same-day in-person evaluation**.

### Go to the ER now if ANY of these apply
- Feeling **lightheaded, weak, faint**, short of breath, or chest pain  
- **Rapid heartbeat**, pale/clammy skin
- Blood is **pouring**, passing **clots**, or you’re filling the toilet / soaking tissue repeatedly
- **Severe abdominal pain**, fever, or you look/feel seriously unwell
- You’re on **blood thinners** (warfarin, Eliquis/apixaban, Xarelto/rivaroxaban, Pradaxa, Plavix/clopidogrel) or have a bleeding disorder
- You also have **black/tarry stool** or vomiting blood

If none of those apply, you should still get **urgent care today** (and ER if urgent care can’t evaluate bleeding properly).

### Common causes (but can’t be assumed)
- **Hemorrhoids** or an **anal fissure** (often blood on paper / surface of stool; fissure hurts)
- **Diverticular bleeding** (can be painless and large-volume)
- **Colitis/infection** or **IBD** (often diarrhea, cramps, urgency, fever)
- **Polyps/cancer** (especially if ongoing or mixed with stool)

### What to do right now
- If actively bleeding a lot or feeling off → **ER**
- **Do not take NSAIDs** (ibuprofen/naproxen/aspirin unless prescribed). They can worsen bleeding.
- **Hydrate** (water + electrolytes). Avoid alcohol.
- If you can, note:
  - roughly **how many episodes** and **how much** blood (streaks vs bowl turning red vs clots)
  - whether it’s **mixed in** the stool or only on the outside / paper
  - any **pain**, diarrhea, fever, recent antibiotics/travel, constipation/straining
  - all meds/supplements (especially blood thinners)

### One quick check
Right now, are you having **dizziness/weakness**, **clots**, or is the toilet bowl turning **noticeably red** each time? If yes, **ER**. If not, still **same-day urgent evaluation**.

## Assistant

Mr. White — **a year of intermittent bright-red blood is still not normal**, and it needs a **proper exam + colon evaluation**, even if you feel fine. Hemorrhoids/fissure are common, but you shouldn’t assume that without being checked.

### What you should do next (practical)
- **Book a PCP or gastroenterology visit ASAP** (this week if possible).
- Expect:
  - **Rectal exam/anoscopic exam** (to look for hemorrhoids/fissure).
  - Often a **colonoscopy** (or at least a sigmoidoscopy), especially if bleeding is “a lot,” recurrent for months, or you’re **≥45**, have **family history**, anemia, or any bowel habit changes.

### Go to the ER today if any “red flags” happen
- Bleeding becomes **large-volume**, you pass **clots**, or it’s happening repeatedly in a short time
- **Lightheadedness**, fainting, rapid heartbeat, shortness of breath
- **Black/tarry** stools
- **Severe abdominal pain**, fever, or you feel very unwell
- You take **blood thinners**

### Clues that point to common benign causes
- **Fissure:** sharp pain with bowel movements, blood on paper.
- **Hemorrhoids:** itching/pressure, blood on paper or dripping after a BM, worse with straining.

### Things to do while you’re arranging care
- Aim for **soft stools**: 25–35g fiber/day + plenty of water.
- Consider **psyllium** daily; if constipated, **polyethylene glycol (Miralax)** can help.
- Avoid **straining** and long toilet sitting.
- Avoid **ibuprofen/naproxen** for now if possible.

4) Any **weight loss, diarrhea, mucus, belly pain, fatigue**, or family history of colon cancer/IBD?

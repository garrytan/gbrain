I'm thinking about the desktop mode for Jovie, and I'm trying to think about what it should be. There are basically two main ways to go about this:
1. Copy Link Tree, where it just looks like a mobile phone no matter what your screen is.
2. Something where it basically replaces your website, where it looks like just a normal desktop website.
3. What Lalo does, or what some of the other LinkedIn bios do, where you move the profile and then go over to the left and then put the content on the right, and you just use the screen real estate a little bit better.
What I'd like to do before doing any of this is try to understand at a high level what I mean. I know what I want out of a website, and Tim White dot co, which I built, is pretty much what I want out of a website. I want you to look at that and look at who the top artists are right now in the electronic music scene. Specifically, look at who's on tour recently, because they probably have built the most modern website. Look at who's on tour recently, and then try to see who has the newest websites. Look at who's on tour and lean towards newer artists. It's particularly electronic music and stuff, or artists who have blown up recently, like David Guetta, who would be probably one of the top touring DJs, but he's been around for 30 years. I don't know that he's necessarily updated his website in the last 10 years.
Whereas John Summit or someone is probably a better bet because they haven't been around for long. Just go out and figure out:
- Go to the Chainsmokers' website and put everyone's website.
- Figure out how they display tour dates.
- Figure out how they display music.
- What else do they have on their websites?
- Is there anything that we're not thinking about?
- How do they display contact information?
Help us decide how that affects Jovie and whether we should try to be a website replacement or just be kind of like the Alert Center or something. Some people, even if we're not fully your website replacement, are still going to land on it. I don't know. I personally lean on it being capable enough that it could feasibly just become your website, but I am not crazy about custom domains and stuff, because I kind of think that a branded product is to move. I remember in my space, people just sent people to their my space. If I need someone in person, I suddenly my Instagram. Personal websites, I think, are not really a thing that's important.
I almost feel like it's a feature and a growth hack to not allow custom domains. Think once you allow custom domains, it kind of killed the coolness of Jovie, and now you're just a website builder. My personal take.

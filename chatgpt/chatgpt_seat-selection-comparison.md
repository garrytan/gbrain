Yo dawg, I'm booking travel on XPD right now and it keeps saying that for basic economy seat choice is an extra fee and then if I get a main cabin economy ticket then my seat choice is included but that costs like $35 extra. Do I, per person, does that mean that the tickets would be together but in random seats or does that mean that the tickets will individually be random and booking them both at the same time?

---

## Assistant

Here’s how that works:  

- **Basic Economy**: you don’t get to pick your seats for free. The airline will assign you whatever’s left at check-in. If you book two tickets together, the system *might* seat you next to each other, but there’s no guarantee — you could end up in totally different rows. Paying for a seat is the only way to lock that in.  

- **Main Cabin (regular economy)**: you *do* get to pick seats at booking, included in the fare. That’s what the extra ~$35 per person is covering. You can select two seats together up front and guarantee it.  

So:  
- **Basic = random, even if booked together.**  
- **Main Cabin = you choose seats, guaranteed together if you pick them.**

## User

If I book the tickets wide, then see where they put me, and be able to pay extra to move them together if they're not together, or do I have to do that up front?

---

## Assistant

You can usually do it **after booking**, but the rules vary a bit:  

- **Basic Economy**: when you book, you either skip seat selection or pay right away. If you skip, the airline auto-assigns random seats when check-in opens (24 hours before the flight). At that point, you’ll see what you got. If they’re split up, you can try to pay for different seats (if any are still open). But at that late stage, options are limited — middle seats or singles at the back are often all that’s left.  

- **Main Cabin**: you pick at booking with no fee (besides the higher fare).  

So yes — you *can* book Basic, wait, and then pay later to move them together if you don’t like what you get. The risk is that by then, the good seats are often gone, and you may not even be able to sit together at all.

What's the probability of the seats being together or not being together?

## Assistant

It depends a lot on the airline, route, and how full the flight is, but here are some rough real-world odds:  

- **Basic Economy, two tickets booked together:**  
  - *Small chance (maybe 10–20%)* you’ll still get seats together for free. Some airlines’ systems try to keep parties together if possible, but it’s never promised.  
  - *Most common outcome (60–70%)* is you’ll be split up but maybe still within a row or two of each other.  
  - *Bad case (20–30%)* is you’re completely separated, like middle seats in different parts of the plane.  

- **At check-in (24h before):** if the flight is anywhere near full, most “good” seats are gone. Paying to move then often means middle seats at the back, and on some routes, there might not be two seats together left at all.  

- **Main Cabin:** near 100% chance of sitting together because you pick the seats upfront.  

So basically:  
- If you care a lot about sitting together, pay the ~$35 each.  
- If you don’t mind taking the gamble, you might get lucky, but odds lean toward being separated unless the plane is pretty empty.

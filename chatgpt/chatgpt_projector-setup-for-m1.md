## Key constraints & what to watch out for

- The “base” M1 Macs (MacBook Air, 13-inch MBP) natively support only **one** external display of up to 6K. citeturn0search6turn0search4  
- To drive two projectors (especially extended displays, not just mirroring), you’ll need some sort of work-around: either DisplayLink (software + compatible adapter/dock), or using dual DisplayPort/HDMI outputs via a dock that provides “native” multiple display output (if supported). citeturn0search4turn0search3  
- Resolution & refresh rate matter. Projectors often are less demanding, but you want to get the highest you can without dropped frames or artifacts.

## Best cable & adapter setups

Here are three reliable setups, plus product suggestions. Pick based on your needs/budget.

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **1. DisplayLink adapter or dock** | Lets you use two external displays off one USB-C port, works around native one-display limit. Good for projection mapping. | Needs DisplayLink driver/software; potential latency (though minimal if using good adapter); depends on USB-C bandwidth. |
| **2. Two separate adapters (each HDMI/DP) into two ports** | Cleaner / more “native” if your MacBook has two Thunderbolt/USB-C ports; potentially better performance. | M1 base may not allow two “native” outputs; may require DisplayLink for the second. Power-draw and cable routing more complicated. |
| **3. Thunderbolt dock with dual video out** | Clean, gives extra ports, often with power pass-through; plugs once. | More expensive; must choose a dock that supports dual displays via DisplayLink or dual DisplayPort/HDMI. |

## Recommended adapter specs & what to buy

You’ll want:

- USB-C / Thunderbolt 3 port that supports video (DisplayPort-Alt Mode)  
- Adapters or hub/dock that support dual HDMI or dual DisplayPort  
- DisplayLink support if you’re using more than what is natively supported  
- HDMI or DisplayPort cables of decent quality, shielded, suited for the distance to your projectors

## Example of a configuration I'd use

If I were doing projection mapping:

- MacBook M1 + a good DisplayLink‐capable adapter (e.g. OWC) → two HDMI cables out  
- Use quality HDMI cables, preferably active or high-spec if long run (~10-15m)  
- Set both projectors to the same resolution & refresh rate to avoid artifacts or timing issues  
- Possibly use a Thunderbolt dock if I need USB, ethernet, etc.  
- Power the MacBook through a USB-C power delivery adapter so the USB-C port remains strong.

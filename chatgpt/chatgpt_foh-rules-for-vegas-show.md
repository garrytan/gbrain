Give me a set of world class rules for a FOH staff at a vegas style show.





---

## Assistant

Here’s a concise, high-end framework you can adapt for a Las-Vegas–style Front of House team.

---

## 1. Appearance & Presence
- **Show-Ready Look:** Uniforms pressed, shoes polished, hair and grooming immaculate.  
- **Silent Tech:** Earpieces, tablets, and POS devices hidden or discreet—audience should never see the “back end.”  
- **No Off-Stage Behavior:** No leaning, chewing, personal phone use, or idle chatter in view of guests.

## 2. Guest Interaction
- **One-Minute Rule:** Acknowledge every guest within 60 seconds of arrival—warm smile, confident eye contact.  
- **Anticipate Needs:** Scan body language for cues (empty glass, looking for restroom) and act before asked.  
- **Upsell as Entertainment:** Suggest premium seats, signature cocktails, and add-ons as part of the show, never as a hard sell.

## 3. Service Standards
- **Precision Timing:** Drinks delivered within five minutes, food within promised ticket times; trays never cross the performance sightline.  
- **Whisper Service:** Orders and table checks done at a low tone—performance is never interrupted.  
- **Clean Sweep:** Surfaces wiped and glassware cleared continuously; no clutter visible to guests.

## 4. Safety & Compliance
- **RBS/ABC Compliance:** Card every guest who appears under 35. Follow strict overservice cutoffs.  
- **Evacuation & Incident Protocol:** Each staffer knows exit routes, code phrases, and chain of command for medical or security events.  
- **Cash & Comp Policy:** Follow comp and VIP approval rules exactly; no unauthorized discounts.

## 5. Team Communication
- **In-Ear Discipline:** Only essential talk on headsets—short, code-based phrases.  
- **Shift Huddles:** Five-minute pre-show and mid-show check-ins for updates, VIP notes, and sales goals.  
- **Cross-Cover:** FOH works as a single unit; any staffer can cover any station for short periods.

## 6. Sales & Performance KPIs
- **Target Mindset:** Every shift has a per-capita spend goal. Track upsells and specials in real time.  
- **Showmanship Metrics:** Measure not just revenue, but guest delight—encore applause, repeat visits, positive reviews.

## 7. Culture & Accountability
- **Own the Room:** Treat every guest as a celebrity, every shift as opening night.  
- **Peer Enforcement:** Colleagues correct lapses on the spot—management backup is secondary.  
- **Continuous Improvement:** Post-show debriefs to capture wins, misses, and ideas for the next performance.

---

These rules set a “world-class” standard: elegant presentation, flawless timing, strict compliance, and a sales-driven but guest-first mindset—exactly what a Vegas-level show demands.

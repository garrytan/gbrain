That meme is satire—here’s why it wouldn’t actually work:

• Federal tax law requires any donated artwork over \$5 K to be appraised by a “qualified appraiser” who isn’t related to the donor. Inflating a value to \$20 M when a single stroke was paid \$25 K would be blatant self-dealing and trigger immediate IRS scrutiny.

• Noncash gifts over \$500 K demand a Section 8283 appraisal summary and a signed IRS Form 8283. If the IRS believes the appraisal is fraudulent, they can disallow the entire deduction and impose hefty penalties (up to 40 % of the claimed deduction).

• Even if you could sneak an inflated appraisal past the appraiser rules, charitable deduction limits cap art donations at 30 % of your AGI in the year of the gift (with a five-year carryforward).

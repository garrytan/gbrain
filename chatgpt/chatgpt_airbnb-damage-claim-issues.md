## How often does Airbnb side with hosts vs with guests in such disputes?

It’s hard to get good data, but from what people report:

- There's a **significant number** of complaints from guests who feel they were unfairly charged.
- Many guests report difficulty disproving damage claims, particularly when hosts provide photos (sometimes with questionable quality or before/after comparison issues).
- Several threads say that unless the guest has strong documentation (timestamped photos or video, etc.), Airbnb tends to accept the host’s claims or at least compromise in favor of the host.  
- Some hosts also feel that Airbnb undervalues the damage, so “sides” with the host in accepting the claim but doesn’t pay enough. This suggests both parties are upset at different parts of the process.

So, yes — the thing you described (claims that are exaggerated / baseless, Airbnb siding with host) is *not uncommon* in people's anecdotal experience.

## Key factors that seem to affect things

From the stories, the following tend to make or break a case:

1. **Evidence from the guest**  
   Photos or video at check-in and check-out, showing condition; timestamps help. If you don’t document, it's hard to disprove claims.

2. **What the host provides**  
   Receipts / estimates for repair, before/after photos, etc. The strength of their documentation matters.

3. **Timeliness**  
   Hosts have to file claims within certain windows (14 days after checkout, etc.). Guests need to respond promptly. Airbnb’s policies require certain steps. citeturn0search6turn0search14turn0search18

4. **Clarity/dispute between guest and host**  
   If the guest declines the charge, or disputes, sometimes Airbnb steps in. If not, host’s request may proceed unchallenged.  

5. **Type / extent of damage**  
   Minor things (stains, odors) vs major damage (broken furniture, structural etc) are treated differently. Major damage often needs licensed contractor quotes, etc.

6. **Jurisdiction / local laws**  
   The terms include arbitration clauses, limitations, and regional differences. citeturn0search18

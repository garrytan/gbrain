I have a recipe that calls for 4.16 liters of vodka, 2.78 liters of raspberry liqueur, and 11.06 liters of lemonade. I only have one and a quarter gallons of lemonade right now. So how much can I make and give me an adjusted recipe?

---

## Assistant

1.25 gallons * 3.78541 liters/gallon ≈ 4.73176 liters

Now, let's determine the limiting ingredient for the recipe, which in this case is the lemonade. The original recipe calls for 11.06 liters of lemonade, but you only have 4.73176 liters. 

To find the proportion of the recipe you can make, use the ratio of the available lemonade to the required lemonade:

Proportion = 4.73176 liters / 11.06 liters ≈ 0.4275

Now, adjust the quantities of all ingredients by this proportion:

1. **Vodka**:
   \[ 4.16 \text{ liters} \times 0.4275 \approx 1.7784 \text{ liters} \]

2. **Raspberry liqueur**:
   \[ 2.78 \text{ liters} \times 0.4275 \approx 1.188 \text{ liters} \]

3. **Lemonade** (already known):
   \[ 11.06 \text{ liters} \times 0.4275 \approx 4.73176 \text{ liters} \]

The adjusted recipe is:

- 1.7784 liters of vodka
- 1.188 liters of raspberry liqueur
- 4.73176 liters of lemonade

This adjusted recipe will ensure that the proportions of the ingredients remain the same while making the maximum amount with the lemonade you have.

## Assistant

For Dr. Mike Israetel / RP-style thinking, the answer is basically:

> **Put rest days where they best manage fatigue and keep performance high – usually don’t string 5 hard days in a row unless you must.**

Let’s walk through your options in that lens.

---

### 1. Training Mon–Fri, resting Sat–Sun (5 in a row)

**Pros:**

- Easy for life/schedule.
- Weekends free.

**Cons (the big one for Mike):**

- 4th and 5th sessions of the week are often trash: weaker, worse pumps, more aches.
- Systemic fatigue keeps stacking → you get less from the same volume.

**When this is fine:**

- Volume per day isn’t very high.
- You’re not super strong yet / not pushing close to MRV.
- You *don’t* see performance tank on Thu/Fri in your logbook.

If your Thu/Fri numbers and pump are clearly worse, Mike would probably say:  
“Cool that it’s convenient, but you’d grow more by inserting a rest day earlier.”

---

### 2. Training Mon, Tue – rest Wed – training Thu, Fri (weekends off)

This is **4 days**, not 5, but the *pattern* is what matters: **2 on / 1 off / 2 on / 2 off**.

For a true 5-day week with a similar idea, you’d do something like:

- **Option A (5 days):**  
  Train: **Mon, Tue, Wed, Fri, Sat**  
  Rest: Thu, Sun  
  → 3 on / 1 off / 2 on / 1 off

- **Option B (5 days, weekend mostly free):**  
  Train: **Mon, Tue, Thu, Fri, Sat**  
  Rest: Wed, Sun  
  → 2 on / 1 off / 3 on / 1 off

**Why Mike tends to like this style:**

- You rarely do more than **3 hard days in a row.**
- Fatigue gets “dumped” midweek → later sessions are still high quality.
- You can still hit high weekly volume because you’re not crawling into every session half-dead.

For most people doing 5 days/week and trying to optimize hypertrophy,  
**something like A or B is usually better than 5 straight days.**

---

### 3. Training Mon, Tue, Wed – rest Thu – train Fri, Sat, Sun

That’s **6 days**, but again, think in principle: **3 on / 1 off / 3 on**.

For a high-fatigue, high-volume lifter, Mike would usually **not** love 3+3 with only 1 rest day in between, especially if all 6 are hard sessions. Too many consecutive stress days → systemic fatigue → quality drops.

He’d only be okay with this kind of layout if:

- Some of those days are very light / pumpy.
- Volume per muscle is carefully controlled.
- Performance and recovery markers say “I’m fine.”

Otherwise, he’d probably tell you you’re just accumulating junk volume.

---

### So if you’re doing **5 hard days per week**, what would Mike likely favor?

If we translate his usual fatigue-management logic into a clear suggestion:

1. **Best (for gains):**  
   A schedule where you **never do more than 3 hard days in a row**, e.g.:

   - Train: **Mon, Tue, Wed, Fri, Sat**  
     Rest: Thu, Sun  
     or  
   - Train: **Mon, Tue, Thu, Fri, Sat**  
     Rest: Wed, Sun  

2. **Second-best (fine if life demands it):**  
   **Mon–Fri**, rest Sat–Sun – *only if*:
   - Your logbook shows solid performance on Thu/Fri.
   - You’re not feeling beat to hell by Friday.
   - Pumps and mind–muscle connection are still good.

3. **Worst (for most people):**  
   Layouts where you effectively string **6 hard days** with only one rest day (like 3 on / 1 off / 3 on) *unless* some of those days are deliberately easy.

---

### How to decide, in Mike’s style

Ask yourself each week:

- **Are the last 1–2 sessions of the week clearly worse?**  
  (Weight down, reps down, pump weak, joints angry.)
- **Do I feel notably more beat up later in the week?**

If yes → **move a rest day earlier** (midweek) and avoid 4–5 hard days in a row.

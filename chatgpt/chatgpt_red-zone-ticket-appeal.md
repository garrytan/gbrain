## Assistant

Mr. White, there’s virtually no “good” excuse for an overnight red-zone ticket in LA—red zones are 24/7 no-stopping areas. The only appeals that routinely succeed are ones backed by concrete evidence that the red-zone designation or signage was incorrect (missing, obscured, faded, mis-painted curb) or that a true emergency forced you to stop there (e.g., medical crisis, towing/impound error).  

• Signage check – Walk to where you parked and photograph:  
    • The curb paint (is it clearly red all the way through?)  
    • Any “No Parking/No Stopping” signs nearby (visible, legible, correctly placed per LADOT specs?)  
    • Distance markers (e.g., “No Parking 7 AM – 4 PM School Days” vs. a full red-zone)  
If you discover the red paint stops abruptly or the sign is missing/illegible, you can upload those photos in an online appeal. LA will dismiss tickets when they see that the red-zone markings or signage don’t meet municipal standards (e.g., unlawful gaps in the painted curb, signposts knocked over, faded paint).  

• Emergency excuse – “I fell asleep” or “no legal spaces” won’t hold. To claim necessity, you’d have needed a documented emergency (ambulance dispatch, car breakdown documented by a tow company, etc.). Since that didn’t occur, there’s no viable “emergency” defense here.  

Bottom line: unless you can prove defective or missing red-zone markings/signs, your appeal will almost certainly fail. I’d recommend:  
1. Photograph the curb and signage where you parked.  
2. If you see any noncompliance (e.g., no sign, faded paint, sign at the wrong height, obstruction), file an online appeal with those images.  
3. If everything appears correct, pay the $98 now to avoid late fees.  

That is the only approach that actually works.

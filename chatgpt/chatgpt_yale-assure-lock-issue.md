## Assistant

That clicking and failure to extend the deadbolt usually means the **motor is stalling due to resistance**—often from:

1. **Misaligned strike plate or door frame pressure** (less likely with door open).
2. **Wrong installation depth** of the latch mechanism.
3. **Deadbolt tailpiece not properly seated** or binding in the lock housing.
4. **Latch installed upside down** or in the wrong configuration.

Since it works manually, here’s what to check:

### 1. **Make sure the deadbolt latch is correctly installed:**
   - It must be **centered** and **extendable freely**.
   - The **flat part of the bolt** should face **up** when the bolt is retracted.

### 2. **Test with the lock removed:**
   - Take off the keypad/inside unit, and try **extending/retracting the bolt via motor** (by running a lock/unlock cycle while holding the unit in your hand). If it works in your hand, the problem is alignment or torque resistance from the door.

### 3. **Check battery strength:**
   - Low power can cause motor failure to fully drive the bolt. Use **fresh alkaline batteries**, not rechargeables.

### 4. **Recalibrate the lock:**
   - Perform a **factory reset** or re-run the **door handing detection** to let it re-learn lock/unlock orientation.

## Why *La Croix* often tastes bad to people

**Key factors in perception:**
- **Natural flavor oils vs. real juice:**  
  La Croix uses *“natural flavors”* (extracts) that are a mix of compounds. Some people are *super-sensitive* to specific volatile aromatics — especially citrus compounds like **limonene, citral, and terpenes**. These can register as *soapy, chemical, or plastik-y* to certain taste receptors.  
  Perrier uses **real lemon/lime essence and CO₂ from natural sources** that tends to taste *sharper and cleaner*, not “chemical.”  
- **Mouthfeel + minerality:**  
  The mineral content and CO₂ pressure differ between brands. La Croix is softer and flatter by comparison, which accentuates odd flavor notes.  
- **No actual fruit:**  
  Unlike Spindrift or Perrier Lime (which uses real juice), La Croix has *zero juice* — just flavors. If your palate detects *artificial flavor compounds* (even if technically “natural”), it can register as off.  
- **Expectation mismatch:**  
  Your brain correlates “citrus” with *real brightness*. When La Croix delivers a weird mix of aromatic compounds without real brightness, it triggers dislike.

**In short:**  
Your taste buds are likely sensitive to the specific volatile flavor compounds La Croix uses, which makes it taste *unpleasantly artificial/soapy/chemical* to you. Perrier and Topo Chico, being more mineral and less “flavor engineered,” will likely be far more agreeable.

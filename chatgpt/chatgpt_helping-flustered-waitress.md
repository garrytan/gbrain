# Helping Flustered Waitress

## User

Liv is incredibly flustered at her waitress job right now. She’s off in an hour. I’m eating there. How can I be helpful 

---

## Assistant

Mr. White — you can help most by *reducing decisions and time pressure* for her, without getting in the way of her workflow or her manager.

- **Signal “no rush, no extra needs.”** Tell her quietly: “I’m good—don’t worry about checking on me. Take care of your section.” Then stop flagging her down.
- **Be the easiest table.** Order fast, keep it simple, no mods/substitutions, no “can I also…”, no split checks, no separate sauces refills unless she offers.
- **Pre-decide everything.** If you want dessert or a box, ask once at the end: “Box and check whenever.” Otherwise: “Just the check when you can.”
- **Don’t pull her into emotional processing mid-shift.** Save “what’s wrong?” for after she clocks out. During service, stick to calm, practical support.
- **Run interference gently.** If you notice someone trying to rope her into a long question and you can do it naturally, you can say: “She’s slammed—maybe ask the host/manager for that.”
- **Tip well and make it clean.** A generous tip + a short note like “You’ve got this” does more than a big conversation.
- **If she’s truly overwhelmed:** ask one yes/no question: “Want me to talk to the manager and have them pull you off my table?” Only if you’re sure it won’t embarrass her.

A simple line that works: **“Hey—I'm easy. No refills, no rush, drop the check whenever. You’re almost done.”**

## User

They’re not helping me I’m just chilling at the bar. More how can I make her feel relaxed 

---

## Assistant

Mr. White — in the middle of a shift, the fastest way to relax her is **brief, low-demand, confidence-boosting contact** (not a “talk about it” convo).

- **Give her permission to not engage:** when she passes, soft smile + “You’re good. No need to check on me.”
- **One calming line, then let her go:** “You’re doing fine. One hour left.” (Then stop there.)
- **Make her laugh in 3 seconds:** a tiny, dumb, harmless bit: “I’m your easiest customer—water is my personality.” / “I will not request a single thing. I’m in witness protection.”
- **Use grounding, not problem-solving:** “Breathe. Shoulders down.” (Say it lightly, almost like a reminder, not instruction.)
- **Match her pace:** keep your voice lower and slower than hers. No big energy, no rapid questions.
- **Don’t ask “are you ok?”** That can spike stress. Ask **binary**: “Need anything from me? No worries if not.”
- **End with certainty:** “I’m here. Finish your shift. We’ll talk after.”

If you get one moment when she pauses:  
**“Look at me—inhale, exhale. You’re crushing it. One hour. Go.”**  
Then give her a little nod and let her move.

## What determines how “long” a battery lasts

Battery life (in actual usage) depends on multiple factors:

- **Battery chemistry and construction quality**  
  Two “AA alkaline” batteries may actually use different materials or separators, affecting internal resistance, discharge curves, etc.

- **Load (current draw)**  
  Batteries in a low-drain device (like a remote) will last much longer relative to their rated capacity than if used in a high-drain device (camera flash, wireless sensor, etc.).

- **Self-discharge / leakage**  
  How fast a battery loses power on the shelf, or whether it leaks, affects usable life.

- **Manufacturing consistency / quality control**  
  Even within the same line, some cells may be better than others. Brand name vs in-house brand may differ in QC.

- **Storage conditions**  
  High temperature, humidity, etc., degrade batteries faster.

- **Battery age / shelf life**  
  If a battery is old (or has been stored poorly), it may lose capacity.

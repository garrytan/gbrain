## User

do they use white for buttons and text and logo or less

---

## Assistant

Mr. White,

**Less, not more.** Linear uses white **sparingly and deliberately**, not as a dominant UI accent.

**Key points from their brand usage:**
- **Text:** Primarily dark (Nordic Gray / near-black). White text is used only on dark backgrounds.
- **Buttons:** Typically neutral grays or subtle fills. White buttons exist but are understated, not attention-grabbing.
- **Logo/Wordmark:**  
  - Prefer **monochrome** (dark on light, light on dark).  
  - White wordmark is used only when contrast demands it (e.g., dark backgrounds).
- **Overall philosophy:** High restraint, low contrast abuse, no “white everywhere” look.

**Bottom line:**  
White is a **supporting color**, not a branding signal. Linear relies on spacing, typography, and subtle contrast—not white-heavy components—to communicate quality.

## User

## Assistant

Mr. White,

**Sign-up is the exception.** Linear *does* use white there—but in a very controlled way.

**Sign-up button characteristics:**
- **Color:** White background
- **Text:** Dark (near-black)
- **Contrast:** High, but clean—not loud
- **Purpose:** Primary CTA without using the brand blue

**Why this works:**
- White signals *neutral importance*, not marketing hype  
- It stands out against darker or muted UI without introducing a new accent color  
- Consistent with their “calm software” philosophy

**Takeaway:**  
Linear avoids colorful CTAs. The sign-up button uses white as a **functional highlight**, not a brand statement.

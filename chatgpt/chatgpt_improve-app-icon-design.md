Here’s how I’d improve this icon, Mr. White, while keeping the same idea.

---

### 1. Make the body shape clearer and bolder
- **Increase stroke weight** on the body lines so they’re as strong as the blue arc. Thin grey lines will vanish on smaller sizes.
- **Unify the silhouette**: instead of four separated lines, connect shoulders and sides into one continuous outline of a back, or at least group them so it reads as a torso at a glance.
- Change the grey to **off-white (#F2F2F2)** for better contrast on the dark background.

---

### 2. Refine the blue arc
- Either:
  - Turn it into a **3/4 ring** (about 270°) centered on the body, or  
  - Go full **circle badge** and keep the body inside it.
- Tighten spacing so the ring sits at a **consistent distance** from the body outline.
- Use a subtle **radial/linear gradient** (e.g. #00E5FF → #008BFF) rather than glow blur, to keep it sharp at small sizes.

---

### 3. Clean up composition
- Perfectly **center the torso** within the circle.
- Align shoulders so they’re symmetric and at the same height.
- Make sure all stroke caps are **round** and the same radius.

---

### 4. Prepare small-size variants
- For 48×48 and below:
  - **Thicken strokes** again (or slightly simplify the body shape).
  - Reduce or remove glow; keep flat colors for clarity.
- Test at 24×24 and 16×16 – if the body still isn’t readable, switch to a **solid back silhouette** instead of outlined lines for those sizes.

---

### 5. Optional alternate version (if you want a stronger brand mark)
- Fill the circle with a very dark blue instead of pure black.
- Cut the body out as **negative space** inside the circle.
- Keep the cyan ring as the outer stroke only.  
This gives you a simple, bold badge that will stand out on crowded home screens.

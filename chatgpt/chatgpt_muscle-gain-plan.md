## Assistant

Mr. White, you’re already lean (11.5% at 6'4", 167 lb). The “improve” lever here is mostly **adding muscle / raising FFMI**, not getting much leaner. At your height and weight, you’ve got a lot of runway to grow.

Here’s a clean, RP/Mike/Menno-style plan:

**1) Run a slow lean bulk**
- Aim to gain **0.4–0.8 lb/week**.  
- Start with **+200–300 kcal/day** over maintenance. Adjust every 2 weeks based on scale trend.
- If your waist jumps fast or you’re gaining >1 lb/week, reduce calories a bit.

**Macros**
- **Protein:** ~0.8–1.0 g/lb = **135–170 g/day**.
- **Fat:** ~0.3–0.4 g/lb = **50–70 g/day**.
- **Carbs:** fill the rest (they fuel training).

**2) Train for hypertrophy with progression**
- **Frequency:** 4–6 days/week.
- **Volume:** **10–20 hard sets per muscle/week** (start near 10–12, add if recovery is good).
- **Reps:** mostly **6–15**, sometimes 15–30 on isolations.
- **Effort:** finish sets with **1–3 reps in reserve (RIR)**.
- **Progression:** add reps first, then load. Log everything.

Example split (simple and effective):
- **Upper/Lower x2** or **Push/Pull/Legs x2**.
- Prioritize compounds (bench/rows/OHP/squats/RDLs/pullups) + 2–4 accessories per session.

**Deload** every 4–8 weeks or when performance stalls + fatigue is high.

**3) Keep cardio “supportive,” not competing**
- 2–3 sessions/week **Zone 2** (20–30 min) for health/recovery.
- Steps ~7–10k/day. Don’t crank cardio so high that leg/upper sessions suffer.

**4) Recovery is the multiplier**
- **Sleep:** 7.5–9 h/night.
- Manage stress, keep a consistent schedule.
- If lifts regress and soreness lingers, reduce sets before blaming food.

**5) Don’t over-trust the body-fat number**
App estimates can be off by a few %. Track:
- weekly average bodyweight
- waist (same spot, same time)
- gym performance
- progress photos monthly

**Target:** let BF drift up to ~13–15% while you gain muscle. When you hit that, do a short **mini-cut** back to ~10–12% and repeat.

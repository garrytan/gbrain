## Assistant

To collect data from Instagram, including getting users who have commented on posts, the official way is to use the Instagram Graph API. This API is designed for business and creator accounts on Instagram, allowing access to data such as comments, posts, and insights in a programmable way. However, it requires approval from Instagram, and there are strict guidelines on what data can be accessed and how it can be used.

Unofficial methods, such as scraping, violate Instagram's terms of service and can lead to account suspension or legal action against the violator. Given the risks and legal considerations, it's recommended to stick with the official Instagram Graph API and ensure compliance with its policies and guidelines.

2. **Legal Frameworks**: In many jurisdictions, unauthorized access to computer systems, including web scraping for data not intended to be public or bypassing API limitations, can violate laws such as the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) in the United States. It's critical to familiarize yourself with and adhere to these laws.

3. **Platform Policies**: Platforms like Instagram and Facebook (Meta Platforms, Inc.) have specific terms of service that prohibit scraping and the unauthorized use of data. Additionally, they can infringe on data protection regulations like GDPR in Europe, which require explicit consent for personal data to be used in such a manner.

5. **Research and Disclosure**: If your research identifies security vulnerabilities or privacy issues, consider responsible disclosure. This means privately notifying the affected company of the findings and giving them a chance to fix the issues before publicly disclosing them. This approach helps protect users while still advancing security knowledge.

For legitimate security research, consider partnering with academic institutions or organizations that have established ethical guidelines and legal frameworks for conducting research.

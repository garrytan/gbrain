Here’s the fact check on the claims in the image:

### Claim 1: *In 1976, Atari paid Jobs and Wozniak $5,000 for a game, but Jobs told Woz they only got $750 and kept the rest.*
**True.**  
This story is well documented. Jobs was paid $5,000 by Atari for optimizing the game *Breakout*. He told Wozniak they received only $700 (some sources say $750), and they split that. Wozniak found out years later and confirmed the story in his autobiography, *iWoz*. He expressed disappointment but didn’t hold a grudge.

### Claim 2: *When Apple went public, Jobs made sure Woz got almost nothing while others became millionaires.*
**False or misleading.**  
This is not accurate. Wozniak was a co-founder of Apple and received a significant amount of stock. When Apple went public in 1980, Woz became a multimillionaire. In fact, Woz famously gave away shares to early employees he felt were underappreciated. The idea that Jobs deliberately cut Woz out is unsupported.

### Claim 3: *Wozniak’s response was: “Steve was a different breed. He could lie to anyone and not even flinch.”*
**Partially true.**  
This quote is often attributed to Wozniak in paraphrased form. While Woz has described Jobs as being intensely focused and sometimes manipulative, it’s unclear if he ever said this exact line. It appears to be a dramatized summary of Wozniak's more nuanced statements about Jobs’ character.

### Verdict:
- **Partly true, partly misleading.**
- The Atari incident is true.
- The IPO claim is false.
- The quote is exaggerated but loosely based on Woz’s sentiments.

| 1 | **The Cinematic Dossier** | A high-end artist press kit that feels like a private film premiere. | Full-bleed hero video, artist name in oversized type, floating modules for music, tour, press, contact, payments. | “Present” button opens a polished, Frame.io-style share view for managers, labels, venues, or press. |

| 7 | **The Momentum Dashboard Profile** | A public-facing profile with subtle proof of traction. | Hero + “momentum strip” showing saves, streams, cities, upcoming dates, recent fan actions. | Feels premium because the data is restrained: small, elegant proof points, no clutter. |
| 8 | **The Spatial Gallery** | A visual-first profile for artists with strong photography, video, merch, or world-building. | Horizontal media canvas with layered cards and smooth depth transitions. | Apple/visionOS-style floating surfaces: music player, tour card, payment card, and video modules feel physically layered. |
| 9 | **The Concierge Profile** | A guided profile that behaves like an artist’s AI assistant. | Ask bar at top: “Book,” “Listen,” “See tour dates,” “Get press assets,” “Send tip.” | “Ask Jovie” becomes the primary interface; the profile answers and routes visitors to the correct action. |
| 10 | **The Premium EPK Builder** | A polished, exportable artist profile for teams. | Builder/editor view on one side, live profile preview on the other. | Frame.io-style WYSIWYG customization: logo, colors, section order, viewer mode, private/public toggle, share expiration. |

**Best three to prototype first:**

1. **The Smart One-Link** — strongest fit for Jovie’s “one link, always in sync” positioning. It makes the artist profile feel alive and operational.  
2. **The Label-Ready Share Room** — most differentiated from generic Linktree/artist bio tools. This gives Jovie a premium professional workflow angle.  
3. **The Apple Product Page Profile** — best visual direction for high-end positioning. It can make each artist feel like a launch, not a profile page.

**Shared design language across all 10:**

Use large typography, lots of negative space, cinematic media, soft depth, minimal chrome, precise cards, subtle transitions, and one obvious primary action. The product should feel like **Frame.io’s controlled presentation layer + Apple’s premium product storytelling + Jovie’s conversion engine**.

### eBay’s trajectory (high level)
- **Peak**: Active buyers peaked around **179M in 2018 Q4**; annual **GMV peaked in 2021 at ~$87.4B** (pandemic lift). citeturn1search10turn2search4turn2search0

- **Traffic now**: Similarweb estimates **~642M visits last month** to ebay.com; essentially stable month-to-month. citeturn0search1

### What things sell for (and what sellers net)
- **Gross selling prices**: In liquid categories (sneakers, electronics, collectibles), realized prices are generally **near broader market (±5–10%)**; niche/long-tail items can skew wider. (Directionally consistent with stable GMV + buyer counts.) citeturn1search6
- **Net proceeds have been pressured** versus ~5 years ago because:
  - eBay’s **take rate/ad load has crept up** and **Promoted Listings minimums increased** (Dynamic ad rate floor from **2%→5%**; Priority CPC **$0.02→$0.20** min bids). Many sellers now must advertise to stay visible. citeturn1search15
  - Shipping and payment costs are generally higher than 2020–2021.

**Rule of thumb example** (your $100 retail item that historically fetched $50 on eBay ~5 years ago):  
- **Today’s gross**: likely **$50–$55** (nominally flat-to-slightly up, category-dependent).  
- **Today’s net** (after typical fees + some ads): often **a bit lower** than 5 years ago — think **$45–$50** net vs. low-$50s then. In short: **gross ≈ flat; net ↓ slightly.** citeturn1search15

### Is eBay “as good” for making money as before?
- **Compared to the 2020–2021 surge**: **No** — demand normalized and competition intensified. **Compared to 2023**: **Slightly better** (GMV and revenue have turned positive YoY), but seller margins are tighter due to ads/fees. citeturn1search6

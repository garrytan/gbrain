## User

Explain to me the legalities of a manager being in a tip bowl at a bar.

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, in California (and under federal law) a manager may **not** dip into the tip jar or share in a mandatory tip pool. Key points:

- **California law (Lab. Code § 351)**:  
  - “Every gratuity is… the sole property of the employee … to whom it was paid, given, or left for.”  
  - Employers and their agents (i.e. managers/supervisors) may **not** collect, take or receive any portion of those tips.【turn0search0】

- **Federal law (FLSA § 3(m); 29 C.F.R. § 531.54(d))**:  
  - Managers or supervisors may not keep any portion of employees’ tips or participate in tip pools/jars.  
  - Tip pools may include only non-exempt employees who “customarily and regularly” receive tips (servers, bussers, bartenders).【turn1search0】

- **Mandatory service charges vs. tips**:  
  - An automatic service charge isn’t a “tip” and may be treated as business revenue—so it can be distributed as wages (including to managers) if clearly disclosed and processed as payroll.

If a manager takes from the tip jar, affected employees can file a wage claim with the California Division of Labor Standards Enforcement or bring a PAGA action to recover unlawful tip diversions.

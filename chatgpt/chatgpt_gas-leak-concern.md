## Assistant

Mr. White, **yes — leave the building calmly now** unless a gas-company employee has already told you it is safe to stay.

A neighbor smelled gas and the gas company is actively checking doors and stairwells. That is enough to treat it as a suspected leak. SoCalGas says to **evacuate immediately** if you smell natural gas or suspect a leak, then call from a safe location. citeturn758218search0turn758218search1

## Do this now

1. **Leave by the stairs.**
2. **Do not use the elevator.**
3. **Do not turn lights on or off.**
4. **Do not plug/unplug anything.**
5. **Do not light flames, smoke, use the stove, or operate appliances.**
6. **Do not call from inside if avoidable. Use your phone once outside.**
7. **Stand outside, away from the building**, ideally across the street or upwind.
8. **Do not re-enter** until the gas company, fire department, or building management says it is clear.

From outside, call **SoCalGas emergency: 1-800-427-2200** or **911** if you smell gas strongly, hear hissing, feel dizzy/headachy/nauseous, or the situation seems unmanaged. SoCalGas lists that emergency number for suspected natural gas leaks. citeturn758218search1

The tools are likely gas-detection meters. Their presence is reassuring in the sense that the right people are checking, but it does **not** mean you should wait indoors while they locate the source.

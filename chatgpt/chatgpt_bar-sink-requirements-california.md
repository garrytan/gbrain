what are the requirements for a bar in california to have behind the bar in terms of sinks etc

---

## Assistant

In California, bars must adhere to specific requirements regarding sinks and related facilities to ensure proper sanitation and hygiene. Here are the key requirements:

1. **Handwashing Sinks:**
   - At least one handwashing sink must be available for use by employees in food preparation, food dispensing, and warewashing areas.
   - Handwashing sinks must be conveniently located to facilitate regular and proper handwashing.

2. **Warewashing Sinks:**
   - A three-compartment sink is required for manual washing, rinsing, and sanitizing of utensils and equipment.
   - Each compartment must be large enough to accommodate the largest utensil or piece of equipment.
   - If a mechanical warewashing machine is used, it must meet specific design and performance standards.

3. **Mop Sink:**
   - A utility or mop sink must be available for the cleaning of mops and other cleaning tools.

4. **Bar Sinks:**
   - Bars must have a minimum of one sink for washing glassware. This sink must have at least three compartments for washing, rinsing, and sanitizing glassware.

5. **Drainboards and Drying Racks:**
   - Adequate drainboards or drying racks must be provided to allow for the proper air drying of cleaned utensils and equipment.

6. **Soap and Towel Dispensers:**
   - Handwashing sinks must be equipped with soap dispensers and means for drying hands, such as paper towels or hand dryers.

7. **Hot and Cold Water:**
   - All sinks must have a supply of hot and cold running water under pressure.

8. **Backflow Prevention:**
   - Proper backflow prevention devices must be installed to prevent contamination of the water supply.

9. **Approval and Compliance:**
   - All sink installations and related plumbing must comply with local building and health codes. Bars must also pass inspections conducted by the local health department.

These requirements are designed to ensure that bars maintain high standards of cleanliness and sanitation, minimizing the risk of contamination and ensuring the safety of patrons and staff. For the most accurate and detailed information, it's advisable to consult the California Retail Food Code (CalCode) and local health department regulations.

In California, the specific location requirements for sinks in a bar setting depend on their intended use. Here's a breakdown:

1. **Handwashing Sinks:**
   - Handwashing sinks must be located in or immediately adjacent to areas where food is prepared, dispensed, and where utensils are washed. Therefore, if the bar area is separate from the kitchen, there must be a handwashing sink available in or near the bar.

2. **Warewashing Sinks:**
   - Warewashing sinks (three-compartment sinks) can be located in the kitchen if the bar staff can conveniently access them for washing, rinsing, and sanitizing utensils and equipment used in the bar.

3. **Mop Sink:**
   - A utility or mop sink can be located in a separate cleaning area, such as the kitchen or a designated utility room.

4. **Bar Sinks:**
   - For washing glassware, a dedicated sink with at least three compartments should be located within the bar area itself to ensure that glassware can be properly washed, rinsed, and sanitized without having to transport it to the kitchen.

In summary, while certain sinks (e.g., handwashing sinks) need to be in close proximity to where the tasks are performed, other sinks (e.g., warewashing and mop sinks) can be located in the kitchen as long as they are readily accessible to bar staff. Local health department regulations should always be consulted to ensure compliance with all specific requirements.

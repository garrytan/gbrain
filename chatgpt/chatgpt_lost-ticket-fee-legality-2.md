## User

If you have photographic evidence showing what time you arrived and departed from a parking garage, is a lost ticket fee legal?

---

## Assistant

Mr. White — **sometimes yes, sometimes no**, and your photos matter.

### When a “lost ticket fee” is usually legal
A parking garage can treat a lost ticket fee as a **contract term / default rate** (often the **daily maximum**) so they’re not stuck guessing how long you stayed. That’s why many facilities explicitly state things like “lost ticket pays max daily rate.” citeturn2view2turn2view3turn1search0

### When it may be *not* legal / enforceable (especially in Los Angeles)
1. **If the fee was not properly posted or exceeds posted rates.**  
   In the City of Los Angeles, automobile parking lots must post rates (including the **maximum charge**) at the entrance, and it’s unlawful to charge more than the posted rate at the time the car was accepted. citeturn2view0  
   *Practical takeaway:* if the “lost ticket fee” wasn’t on the posted schedule, or it’s higher than the posted max, you have a strong argument.

2. **If the “lost ticket fee” is essentially a penalty instead of a reasonable estimate of loss.**  
   California generally enforces liquidated-damages amounts **unless they’re unreasonable** at the time of contracting. citeturn3search1  
   *How your photos help:* if your entry/exit times are provable, the operator’s “we can’t know your duration” rationale is weaker—so a very high lost-ticket fee can look more like a penalty than a reasonable estimate.

### The key question your photos answer
**Can the garage accurately calculate the actual parking charge from your evidence (or their own system)?**  
- If **yes**, a court/agency may view insisting on a large lost-ticket fee as unreasonable **unless** the posted terms clearly make “lost ticket = daily max” the agreed default and that default is not excessive. citeturn2view3turn3search1  
- If **no** (e.g., they truly can’t verify), the default fee is more likely to stand.

### What to do (fast, practical)
1. **Photograph the entrance signage** showing rates/max and any “lost ticket” language. (This is huge in LA.) citeturn2view0  
2. **Ask for a manager** and request they compute the fee using your timestamps (and/or your license plate lookup).  
3. If they refuse and you pay to leave:
   - Pay by card if possible, keep the receipt, then **dispute** (merchant dispute/chargeback) with: your photos + signage + receipt.
   - Or **small claims** for the difference if the amount is worth it.

## Assistant

Mr. White, you’ve got a few practical options depending on how close the audience is and how much budget/tech you want to throw at it:  

---

### **Stage Magic Approaches**
1. **Pre-scored Can (Classic Magician’s Gag)**  
   - Take a real Coke can, empty it, refill slightly for weight, then score/weaken one side.  
   - At the cue, a hidden hand-squeeze (by her or a stagehand if rigged in a holder) makes it collapse dramatically.  
   - Works best if she’s holding it, then drops it right after the “crush.”  

2. **Pre-crushed Can + Switch**  
   - She starts with a full sealed can for realism.  
   - Through sleight of hand or a stagehand assist, it’s swapped for a pre-crushed can hidden in a prop or under a cloth.  
   - The swap happens right as lights flicker/strobe to mask the move.  

---

### **Mechanical / Hidden Rig**
3. **Crush Rig (Invisible Assist)**  
   - Place the can in a clear acrylic stand with a hidden piston or clamp.  
   - At the cue, the mechanism crushes it from inside.  
   - Looks like it implodes on its own—strong effect if staged center-room and hands-off.  

4. **Pneumatic or Magnetic Assist**  
   - Build a dummy can with a collapsible inner wall triggered by CO₂ or a solenoid.  
   - Remote trigger makes it crush instantly with a “pop.”  
   - Add sound effect of metal crunch to sell it.  

---

### **Visual / Projection Trick**
5. **Projection Mapping**  
   - Real can sits untouched on a pedestal.  
   - Projection overlays a crumple animation synced with a crushing sound.  
   - Works in darker environments but less visceral than a real smash.  

---

### **Enhancements**
- Add a **sudden audio cue**: metallic crunch + deep bass pop.  
- Time it with a **light flicker/UV strobe** so the switch or trigger is hidden.  
- She can throw the crushed can downstage after to punctuate the beat.  

---

Do you want this to be **up-close and surrounded** (audience right on top of her), or is it more of a **center-stage spectacle** where distance and lighting can cover the trick? That changes whether sleight or mechanical rigs are safer.

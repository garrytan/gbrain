## User

My chase account has a bunch of auto payments. i have cancelled my debit card and gotten a new one but chase will still allow old recurring payments to go through on the old debit card number and will even allow those to overdraft my account. How can i get chase to stop allowing auto debits via act and via old debit cards that have been cancelled?

## Assistant

Mr. White — you’ve run into one of the most frustrating things about U.S. banking: card replacement alone doesn’t block recurring charges. Here’s how you can force Chase to stop allowing auto debits through **both ACH and the old debit card number**:

---

### 1. **Recurring Debit Card Payments**
- Card networks (Visa/Mastercard) give merchants “account updater” services, so your new card number gets passed along automatically. That’s why canceled-card autopays often still work.
- To block this, you need to tell Chase **in writing** that you revoke authorization for specific merchants. Call + file a written dispute/revocation. Chase is then required under Regulation E to stop honoring those authorizations.

---

### 2. **ACH Auto Debits (Bank Account & Routing Number)**
- If payments are coming through your checking account via ACH, you have the right under federal law (Regulation E, NACHA rules) to **revoke authorization** directly with Chase.
- You can request:
  - **“Stop Payment” on a single ACH transaction** (good for one charge).
  - **“ACH Block” or “Stop Authorization” on a specific company** (prevents all future debits from that merchant).
  - **“ACH Debit Block/Filter” on the whole account** (nothing can auto-debit unless you whitelist it). This is a stronger setting Chase can apply if you escalate.

---

### 3. **Preventing Overdrafts**
- By default, Chase may allow overdrafts to “honor” these autopays. To block that:
  - Log into your account → **Account Services → Overdraft Protection** → turn it **OFF** (or call to confirm).
  - Once overdraft is off, any unauthorized or lingering charge attempt will simply be declined instead of pulling your balance negative.

---

### 4. **Action Steps for You**
1. Call Chase → ask for the **Disputes/ACH Authorizations department** (not just general support).
2. Tell them:  
   - You want to **revoke all preauthorized debits linked to your old debit card number**.  
   - You want to **place ACH blocks/filters** on any merchants you specify.  
   - You want **overdraft protection turned off** so no charge can slip through.  
3. Follow up **in writing** (email or secure message works) so you have a paper trail.

---

👉 If Chase resists, cite **Regulation E, 12 CFR § 1005.10(c)** — you have the right to stop preauthorized transfers. If they ignore your request, you can escalate to the CFPB (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau). That usually gets banks moving very quickly.

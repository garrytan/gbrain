### 1. Hooks that clip on the posts (for cups, utensils, bags, etc.)

These snap around the vertical poles and give you hanging points anywhere:

- **“Post hooks” / “pole hooks” for wire shelving** – they click onto the upright post, no tools.

---

### 2. S-Hooks that hang off the shelves

These just drop over the horizontal wire and you hang whatever you want off them.

- Look for **“S-shaped steel wire shelving hooks”** – usually chrome, rated around 10–11 lb each. citeturn0product5turn0product11  

Search terms:  
> `S hooks for wire shelving`  
> `wire rack S hooks chrome`

Use them for: cups with handles, pans, strainers, colanders, reusable bags.

---

### 3. Under-shelf mug racks (slide onto the shelf)

These are U-shaped pieces that slide over a shelf and give you multiple hooks in a row for mugs.

- **Under-shelf mug/cup holders** – usually 6–10 cups per rack, no tools. citeturn0product1turn0product7turn0product13

> `undershelf cup hooks`

Mount them on one of the middle shelves so mugs don’t hit the countertop or the shelf below.

---

### 4. Hanging baskets that hook to the wire shelf

Great for “all the other shit” – spices, packets, random kitchen junk.

---

### 5. Wine / stemware holders (if you’ve got glasses)

If you keep wine glasses or stemware there:

- **Wire shelf stemware holders** – they clip to the underside of a wire shelf and let glasses slide in by the base. citeturn0product2turn0product8turn0product15

---

### How I’d set up your 5-shelf rack

From top to bottom:

1. **Top shelf** – rarely used stuff (appliances, bulk items). Maybe a hanging basket on the front for light items.
2. **2nd shelf** – mount an **under-shelf mug rack** underneath it; cups hang over open space.
3. **Middle shelf** – more hooks on the front/side posts for everyday utensils, oven mitts, etc.
4. **4th shelf** – hanging basket for spices / packets / coffee gear.
5. **Bottom shelf** – heavier items; add a few S-hooks on the side for bags, strainers.

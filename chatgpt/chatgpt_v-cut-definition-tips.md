Short answer, Mr. White: at your current leanness and size, what you’re missing is **muscle thickness**, not more fat loss.

You’re lean enough that:
- **Definition/lines** = mostly there already (which you’re seeing).
- **How “raised” and 3D they look** = how thick the obliques/lower abs/iliac area muscles are.
- More fat loss now will help **a bit**, but you’re 6'4" and ~159 lb at ~12% DEXA – that’s *very* light. Past this point you risk just looking smaller/“stringy” rather than more impressive.

Think of it like this:

1. **Three things control the V-cut / cum gutters look**
   - **Leanness** – you’ve ticked this box. A tiny bit of lower-ab fat left is normal at ~12%. Super razor V-lines for men are usually more like ~8–10% *and* with good muscle.
   - **Muscle thickness** – lower abs, external obliques, internal obliques, a bit of hip flexors.
   - **Genetics & bone structure** – angle/width of pelvis, insertion, ligament path. You can’t change this.

2. **Given your stats, what’s the limiting factor?**
   - 6'4", 159 lb at ~12% = you simply **don’t have a ton of total muscle** yet.
   - You can already see:
     - Clear six-pack
     - Clean V line, just not raised
   - That screams: "lean enough, not thick enough."

So:

> If your goal is to make the V-cut *pop out more*, the main move now is **hypertrophy work**, not just more cutting.

You *could* drop a bit more fat if you want a short-term super sharp look (photoshoot, vacation), but I’d treat that as a mini-cut **after** you’ve built more muscle — not instead of it.

---

### What to actually do

**1. Shift focus from cutting → gaining muscle (slow lean gain)**  
- Small surplus: **+150–300 kcal/day** over current maintenance.
- Aim to gain ~**0.25–0.5 lb/week**.
- Keep protein high: ~**0.8–1.0 g/lb** bodyweight.

You’ll look better at, say, **175–185 lb with more muscle at the same body fat %** than at 159 and shredded.

**2. Direct hypertrophy for the area (2–3×/week)**  
You don’t need crazy volume. Around **6–10 hard sets/week** for obliques/lower abs is plenty, done like any other muscle you want to grow: **progressive overload**, controlled reps.

Good options:

- **Hanging leg raises / knee raises with posterior pelvic tilt**  
  - Focus on curling pelvis up, not just swinging legs.
  - 3×8–12, add ankle weights or weighted between feet when too easy.

- **Cable “pot-stir” / ab rollouts (if shoulders tolerate it)**  
  - Or ab wheel.
  - 3×8–12, slow eccentric, full stretch.

- **Heavy cable side-bends (or DB side-bends)**  
  - Done strict, not 1-inch ego ROM.
  - Think "crunch your rib toward your hip."
  - 3×10–15 each side, progress load over time.

- **Cable woodchops (high-to-low and/or low-to-high)**  
  - Rotational + oblique loading.
  - 2–3 sets of 10–15 each side.

Pick **2–3 of these**, run them consistently for **3–6 months**, and track load or reps going up.

**3. Keep the big compounds in**  
Squats, deadlifts, RDLs, heavy rows, presses — all of that indirectly hits the core. Combined with targeted work, that’s how you get that thick, 3D “armor” look instead of just being skinny with lines.

## Assistant

Good question to worry about, Mr. White — but for *you*, the risk is basically **rounding error** and the upside is big.

### 1. In theory, can oblique training thicken the waist?

Yes, **in theory**:  
- Obliques are muscles.  
- If you add muscle tissue, the tape measure around your waist *can* go up a bit.

But in practice, for a **natural, 6'4", 159 lb, ~12%** guy, the realistic extra muscle you can add to that area over months is **small**. We’re talking millimeters of thickness, not suddenly turning into a refrigerator.

What *does* make waists look “thick” or “blocky” on stage physiques:

- High body fat sitting on the love handles.
- Big ribcage / pelvis structure (genetic).
- Drug stuff (gut distension, organ growth) — not your issue.

### 2. What actually controls whether your waist *looks* wide

Visually:

- **Bodyfat** = how soft/spilled your waist looks.
- **Shoulder/back size** vs **waist size** = the V-taper illusion.
- **Oblique thickness** = how sharp/3D the V-cut and side wall look.

Right now you’re:
- Very light for your height.
- Already lean.
- Missing *muscle*, not chasing a narrower waist at all costs.

For you, a *bit* more oblique muscle will:
- Make the V-lines and lower abs more **3D** and “carved”.
- Make the waist look **tighter and harder**, not thicker and soft.

### 3. How to train them *without* creating a blocky look

If you’re still paranoid, you can bias how you train them:

**Great, low-risk core work:**
- Hanging leg raises / knee raises with pelvic curl  
- Ab wheel / rollouts  
- Cable crunches  
- Pallof presses, side planks, suitcase carries (anti-rotation / anti-lateral flexion)

All of those:
- Train abs/obliques hard.
- Don’t usually blow up waist thickness.

**What to *moderate*, not spam:**
- Very heavy, high-volume **DB/cable side bends** with huge ROM and super low reps.
- Obsessive oblique-only volume on top of everything else.

You can still use side-bends — just:
- Keep them in the **10–15 rep range**,  
- 2–3 hard sets per side,  
- Progress slowly, not ego-lifting.

That’s enough to **sharpen** the V-cut without realistically turning you blocky.

### 4. For *you* specifically

Given:

- 6'4", ~159 lb, 12%  
- Clear abs, visible V, just not raised

You are *so far* from the “too thick obliques” problem that it’s not even on the radar.  

You’d look **better** with:
- More oblique and lower-ab muscle  
- More upper body size overall  
even if your waist circumference went up 0.5–1" from muscle over the next year — because your **shoulders, chest, back, and arms** should be going up more than that.

Keep volume reasonable, avoid insane ultra-heavy side-bend abuse, and focus on getting generally bigger and stronger. The V-cut will look sharper, not worse.

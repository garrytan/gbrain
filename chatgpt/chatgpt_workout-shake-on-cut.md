## Assistant

Whether to use a workout shake while cutting depends on your overall dietary strategy and goals. A workout shake can be beneficial for preserving muscle mass due to the protein content, which can support recovery and reduce muscle breakdown. However, if you're able to meet your protein and nutritional needs through whole foods while staying within your caloric limits, a shake might not be necessary. It's also important to account for the calories in the shake as part of your total intake. Consulting with a nutritionist or referring to trusted sources like RP Strength, Dr. Mike Isratel, or Menno Henselmans can provide guidance tailored to your specific situation, Mr. White.

## Assistant

Renaissance Periodization (RP) suggests that workout shakes are not mandatory for great results, but can be particularly effective if your workouts are long or very hard. They emphasize that workout shakes, typically containing a mix of protein and carbohydrates, should be counted as an additional meal rather than replacing one of your regular meals【10†source】. Carbohydrates in the shake can provide readily available energy, maintain muscle glycogen, improve focus, and decrease muscle breakdown, which might be beneficial during intense training sessions, especially on a cut【11†source】.

Dr. Mike Isratel, co-founder of RP, mentions that the chronic effect of having a high glycemic carb shake with whey protein during or post every workout is low. This is due to the limited time of elevated insulin post-workout on training days, which may not significantly contribute to muscle growth, especially when in a caloric deficit for cutting【23†source】.

Menno Henselmans emphasizes the importance of focusing on the process and adjusting energy deficits based on your current body fat percentage, with higher body fat percentages allowing for larger deficits. He suggests that when you are leaner and aiming to retain muscle, a smaller energy deficit is advisable. The overall approach should be tailored, taking into account factors like basal metabolic rate, physical activity level, and training energy expenditure, and ensuring optimal protein intake and nutrient timing to support muscle maintenance during a cut【29†source】.

In summary, a workout shake can be helpful during a cut if it fits within your nutritional strategy and caloric goals, especially if it helps maintain workout intensity and supports recovery. However, it's not essential if you're able to meet your nutritional needs with whole foods and should be carefully included in your meal plan to avoid exceeding your caloric limits.

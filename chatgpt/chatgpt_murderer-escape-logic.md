## Assistant

This is a logic puzzle designed to test reasoning under constraints. Here's the setup and the solution:

**Problem Recap:**

- 100 murderers in a field.
- You have **one bullet**.
- If **any murderer believes he has a non-zero chance of survival**, he’ll try to escape.
- If **he’s sure he’ll be shot**, he stays put.
- **Question:** How do you stop them all from escaping?

---

**Solution:**

You **announce**:  
> “If anyone tries to escape, I will shoot the **first person who moves**.”

### Why it works:
- Every murderer knows **someone will die** if anyone tries to escape.
- But **nobody knows who will be first**.
- So **each one faces a non-zero risk** of being that first target.
- That makes **everyone believe they might die**.
- Hence, **everyone is certain enough of death to stay put**.

It’s a game theory problem — the fear of being *the example* ensures perfect compliance.

1. Loading your single bullet and displaying it.  
2. Pointing the gun at the group and announcing:

Yes — *Boy Meets World* and *Victorious* both used repurposed sets from other shows, but not from each other directly.

Here's the breakdown:

### **Boy Meets World**
- Filmed on various soundstages at **Disney-MGM Studios** (now Hollywood Studios) and later **Stage 24** at CBS Studio Center in Studio City, CA.
- The Matthews' living room/kitchen set has been reused and redressed for other sitcoms (like *Girl Meets World*, its sequel).

### **Victorious**
- Filmed at **Nickelodeon on Sunset** (formerly the *Hollywood Center Studios*), where many Nickelodeon shows like *iCarly* and *Drake & Josh* were filmed.
- Sets on *Victorious*, like the *Hollywood Arts High School* lobby, were sometimes redressed versions of sets from *iCarly* or shared with *Zoey 101*.

### **Shared Set Elements**
While *Boy Meets World* and *Victorious* did not share a direct set, both shows were part of an industry-wide trend of **repurposing popular sitcom sets**, especially school hallways, lockers, and apartments. Many of these standard pieces (doors, lockers, staircases, etc.) were moved or repainted across productions.

If you’re seeing visual similarities (like staircases, brick walls, or hallway layouts), it's likely due to common production design elements or recycled set pieces — not a shared set between the two shows.

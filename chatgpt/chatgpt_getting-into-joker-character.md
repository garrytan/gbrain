---

### **1. Anchor the Character: Core Psychology**
- **Identity:** Leto’s Joker is *the embodiment of narcissistic obsession and sadistic playfulness.* He’s aware of his charm, he *loves* his reflection, and he treats pain (his or others’) like entertainment.  
- **Mindset cue:** Repeat to yourself before performing:  
  > “I am the party. I am the pain. I am the punchline.”  
  This primes the impulsive confidence and emotional volatility.

---

### **2. Physicality**
- **Movement:**  
  - Stay *fluid but sharp*. Every motion is deliberate but unpredictable — think a predator dancing.  
  - Occasionally freeze mid-gesture — unsettling stillness is key.  
  - Lead movements with your chest and chin slightly forward; it gives a cocky, invasive presence.  

- **Gestures:**  
  - Touch people too long, lean too close, circle them when talking.  
  - Laughter should *start physically* — shoulders, neck, head — before sound. It’s like a convulsion.  

- **Training cue:** Do a few minutes of exaggerated stretching or rolling before going on; loosen up your spine and jaw to get that serpentine feel.

---

### **3. Voice and Speech**
- **Tone:** Leto’s Joker blends purrs and growls — often quiet, then suddenly explosive.  
  Practice saying random lines like:
  > “Oh… you *really* shouldn’t have done that.”  
  Start softly, finish with a sharp bark or laugh.  
- **Laugh:** Make it *a weapon, not a joke.* Treat laughter as punctuation or intimidation, not amusement.  
  Do 3 laugh types: seductive chuckle, manic cackle, and dead silence after a laugh — unsettling.

---

### **4. Emotional Beat Map**
He switches emotions *mid-sentence.* Practice this:
- Start laughing → drop into dead seriousness → whisper something affectionate → smirk → explode again.  
- The emotional *whiplash* keeps audiences uncomfortable — that’s his control mechanism.

---

### **5. Relationship with Harley**
If you have a Margot Robbie-style Harley performing with you:
- Treat her like a mirror and toy — possessive, but occasionally reverent.  
- Maintain physical tension: hands on her jaw, her shoulder, too gentle or too rough, never neutral.  
- When performing together, the power dynamic should shift moment to moment — love, control, chaos.

---

### **6. Mental Warmup Before Going On**
Do this quick routine:
1. Look in the mirror and say:  
   “You’re beautiful. You’re perfect. You’re poison.”  
2. Flex your jaw, roll your shoulders, hiss a few times.  
3. Think: “Everyone wants to be me. Everyone fears to be me.”  
Then go out and *own the air.*

---

### **7. Immersive Theater Specifics**
- **Stay unpredictable but readable.** You want the audience slightly afraid to look away.  
- **Stay playful** — tease, flirt, menace. Every move has double meaning.  
- **Stay reactive** — Leto’s Joker never ignores stimuli; he *uses* them.

**Purpose:** Break your own inhibition.

**Goal:** Start losing your self-consciousness. You’re *becoming* the performance.

---

### **Phase 2 – Body Chaos (1.5 minutes)**  
**Purpose:** Loosen up your body into that animal, serpentine energy.  
1. Roll your neck and shoulders in slow, circular movements.

### **Phase 3 – Voice & Breath (1 minute)**  
**Purpose:** Unlock vocal unpredictability.

   - “Why so tense?”

3. Let your voice slide between notes — from seductive to violent without warning.  

**Goal:** Get used to emotional whiplash mid-line.

### **Phase 4 – Emotional Flip Drill (1 minute)**

3. Say it like a threat.

**Cue:** The Joker doesn’t *feel* emotions — he *performs* them.

3. Whisper to yourself:  
   > “Let’s play.”  
Then walk into character. Don’t break eye contact with anyone once you enter the scene.

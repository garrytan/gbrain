## User

We’re taking over forever 21s space in the mall to build a two level immersive theatre and walk through experience. Upstairs is our theater with bar and table service with attached kitchen, dressing rooms, and bathrooms. Downstairs is a walk through for Halloween and one for Christmas plus a lobby and bar. Give me ideas for wish list items for our build out like a shower for performers or a tech room for gear storage. What else could we ask for?

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, here are some concise wish-list ideas for the build-out:

1. **Performer Amenities**  
   - Shower and locker area for cast (plus laundry facilities).  
   - Dedicated rehearsal room or warm-up space.  

2. **Backstage & Storage**  
   - Tech booth or control room for lighting, sound, and show control.  
   - Scenic workshop for on-site repairs and prop construction.  
   - Large storage room for seasonal sets (Halloween, Christmas).  

3. **Front-of-House & Guest Experience**  
   - A themed VIP lounge or private event room.  
   - Dedicated coat check or bag check.  
   - Built-in photo-op spots with professional lighting for social media moments.  

4. **Bar & Kitchen Enhancements**  
   - Separate service bar for high-volume events.  
   - Full-service kitchen with quick-turn capabilities (e.g., small-scale prep area).  
   - Refrigerated walk-in storage for bar and kitchen supplies.  

5. **Infrastructure & Safety**  
   - Elevated catwalks for rigging and special effects.  
   - Ample backstage access doors for large props.  
   - Robust soundproofing between floors to run simultaneous events.  
   - Secure, well-labeled tech closets for sound, lighting, and network gear.  

6. **Interactive & Thematic Features**  
   - Flexible wall panel system to swap out scenic elements seasonally.  
   - Hidden corridors or secret passageways to enhance immersion.  
   - Projection mapping areas or motion-tracking tech for added special effects.  

7. **Additional Operational Spaces**  
   - Dedicated office area for show management.  
   - Staff break lounge away from public areas.  

These elements support both an immersive walkthrough downstairs and a full theatrical experience upstairs.

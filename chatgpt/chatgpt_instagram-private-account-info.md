## User

Yo, if I have a private Instagram account and I follow someone, can they, and they see my profile before they've requested my account and been accepted, are they able to see what my bio says and can they see the URL in my bio if I have a link? What level of info can they see?

## Assistant

Yes. On a **private Instagram account**, someone who is **not approved to follow you** can still see your **name, username, profile photo, bio, links in your bio, and your follower/following counts**. Instagram’s help page says that profile information that is always public includes **name, username, profile picture, bio, links, and number of followers and following**. Instagram’s website-link help page also says **anyone, on or off Instagram, can see your websites**. citeturn985360search0turn736031search1

What they **cannot** see before you approve them is your **posts, stories, reels, and other private content**. Instagram says that for a private account, only **approved followers** can see your posts, and people who want to see your photos or videos have to send a follow request. Your following them does **not** give them access to your private content. citeturn353703search1turn353703search5

Practical answer: before they’re accepted, they can usually see your **profile shell**:
- display name
- username
- profile picture
- bio text
- link(s) in bio
- follower count
- following count

They should not be able to see your actual private posts or stories. For the **actual follower/following lists**, Instagram’s current public help explicitly lists the **counts** as public; older Instagram help said the **lists themselves** were limited on private accounts. Safest assumption: **counts are public, lists are treated as private on a private account**. citeturn985360search0turn985360search14turn353703search9

If privacy is the goal, remove anything sensitive from your **bio** and **link in bio**, because those are visible even when your account is private. citeturn985360search0turn736031search1

## Assistant

Generally, dental veneers are considered a cosmetic procedure and are not covered by medical insurance. Some dental insurance plans may offer partial coverage if the veneers are needed for restorative purposes, but this is rare. It's best to check with your specific insurance provider for details.

## User

Does medical cover dental

---

## Assistant

Medical insurance typically does not cover routine dental care, which includes cleanings, fillings, and extractions. Dental care is usually covered by separate dental insurance plans. However, some medical insurance plans might cover dental procedures if they are deemed medically necessary, such as surgery related to a medical condition or injury.

## Assistant

Medi-Cal, California's Medicaid program, does provide dental coverage through its Medi-Cal Dental (formerly known as Denti-Cal) program. This includes a range of dental services such as exams, cleanings, fillings, crowns, and root canals. However, cosmetic procedures like veneers are typically not covered. For specific coverage details, it's best to check directly with Medi-Cal Health Net or refer to their official resources.

## Assistant

The cost of dental care without insurance can vary significantly based on the procedure and location. Here are some average costs in the U.S.:

- Routine cleaning: $75 - $200
- Dental exam: $50 - $150
- Fillings: $100 - $300 per tooth
- Tooth extraction: $150 - $450 per tooth
- Root canal: $700 - $1,200
- Crown: $800 - $1,500 per tooth

Prices may be higher in areas with a higher cost of living, such as Los Angeles.

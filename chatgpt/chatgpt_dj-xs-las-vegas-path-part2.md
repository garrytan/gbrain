
# Strategic Plan for Tim White to Secure a DJ Gig at XS Las Vegas

**Overview:** Tim White is an experienced electronic music artist with past industry success (viral hits, major label signings, collaborations with prominent artists). He specializes in house music with crossover, main-room appeal. To land a coveted DJ gig at **XS Las Vegas** – one of the most prestigious nightclubs in Vegas – Tim needs a structured approach. Below is a step-by-step strategic plan covering branding, music releases, networking, booking strategy, performance prep, marketing, and backup options, with a 12-month timeline for execution.

## 1. Brand Positioning & Image

**Align with XS’s Brand & Audience:** XS Nightclub at the Wynn Las Vegas is known for luxury and top-tier talent. It’s a *“massive, opulent club”* that books **mainstream EDM stars** like Dillon Francis, Marshmello, David Guetta, and The Chainsmokers【21†L29-L37】. Wynn’s 2025 lineup even spans genres from EDM to hip-hop and country (e.g. Deadmau5, Excision, G-Eazy, Dustin Lynch)【2†L62-L69】, so the club targets a broad but high-energy audience. Tim should position himself as a *premier, crowd-pleasing house DJ* who fits in with this elite and diverse roster. This means emphasizing a **big-room, accessible vibe** in his branding – he’s not just an underground artist, but an act who can hype up a Vegas crowd alongside blockbuster names.

**Refine Personal Brand:** Tim’s branding must exude the same **polish and excitement** as XS’s current residents. Key areas to strengthen: 

- **Social Media Presence:** Ensure all profiles (Instagram, TikTok, Twitter, etc.) reflect an active, upscale DJ lifestyle. Post regular content that highlights his past achievements and current projects (studio sessions, throwbacks of festival gigs, behind-the-scenes). A strong online presence is crucial – social platforms *“offer DJs an opportunity to increase their brand recognition, secure more gigs, and grow their fan base”*【24†L38-L41】. In practice, this means Tim should engage fans with teaser clips of new music, respond to comments, and show that he already has an excited following. **Goal:** Within 1-2 months, boost follower engagement and demonstrate momentum on social media (e.g. consistent posts, incremental growth in followers). 

- **Press & Credibility:** Leverage Tim’s past successes to get media coverage that aligns him with top-tier DJs. For example, update his **electronic press kit (EPK)** and bio to highlight his viral hits, charted releases, and big-name collaborations. Secure a few press features or interviews (DJ Mag, EDM.com, Billboard Dance, etc.) announcing his *return* and positioning him as a notable artist on the rise again. These articles should subtly connect Tim to the current dance music narrative (for instance, mentioning how he contributed to trends or worked with artists who are now Vegas regulars). Such press clippings add credibility for talent bookers reviewing his profile.

- **Visual Branding:** Refresh Tim’s visuals to match XS’s high-end image. This could include a **new logo or wordmark**, a professional photo shoot with a luxury aesthetic, and short video content that introduces his persona. XS is described as an *“elegant and intimate environment”* with lavish design【16†L517-L525】, so Tim’s imagery should feel at home in that world. Think sleek, upscale attire in promo photos, and possibly footage of him performing with energetic crowds. If he has past performance videos from big festivals or clubs, edit a highlights reel that showcases his stage presence and the crowd’s reaction – evidence that he can command a main room. 

**Alignment with XS:** To directly connect his brand to XS, Tim can subtly infuse Vegas references in his content. For example, post a photo of him in Las Vegas or at Wynn (even as a visitor) with a caption about *“Vegas energy”*, or share an Instagram Story tagging @xslasvegas when he visits the club as a guest. This shows affinity for the venue’s culture. By the time he formally pitches himself, his brand should **feel compatible** with the XS lineup and what its audience expects: a high-energy, trendy DJ who delivers a luxury party experience.

## 2. Music Strategy & Releases

**Release Plan to Build Hype:** A strong music release schedule will create momentum leading up to an XS booking. Tim should plan a series of **tracks/remixes** over the next several months that re-establish his name in the scene and specifically appeal to the XS crowd. Key components of this strategy:

- **Anthemic Single:** Launch a new original track with *crossover appeal* (e.g. a house track with a catchy vocal or big-room bassline) as a comeback single. Ideally, this track should be **club-friendly and radio-friendly** – something that could get Spotify playlist placements but also bang in a nightclub. The goal is a **viral or buzzworthy hit** to put Tim back on the map. For example, consider how ACRAZE’s viral hit “Do It To It” *“catapulted him from obscurity to stardom”* and ultimately led to a *“much-coveted residency at the Wynn Las Vegas”*【28†L81-L88】. Tim doesn’t need to copy that song, but he can replicate the approach: create a track with a familiar hook (perhaps sampling or referencing a classic hit) reimagined in his house style, aiming to catch widespread attention. Release this 6-8 months before the target gig date to allow it to gain traction.

- **Remixes & Bootlegs:** In addition to originals, Tim should produce a couple of high-profile **remixes**. A smart move is to remix tracks by artists who are **current XS residents** or popular on the Vegas circuit. For instance, a fresh house remix of a song by The Chainsmokers, Diplo, or a classic David Guetta track can serve two purposes: it appeals to the XS talent buyers (since it ties Tim’s sound to their staple artists), and it’s something the original artists or other DJs might play. If possible, **officially release** one remix through a label or via the original artist’s channels (leveraging any industry contacts to get clearance). If official channels aren’t feasible, even an unofficial bootleg that goes viral on SoundCloud/YouTube can generate buzz. These remixes should drop in the lead-up to his pitch – for example, one about 4-5 months out, another 2-3 months out – to maintain momentum.

- **Collaborations:** Tap into Tim’s network of prominent artists for a **collab track**. Since he has past collaborations, reigniting one with a well-known DJ/producer (especially someone who has a Vegas presence) could be a game-changer. A collaboration serves as a vote of confidence from the bigger artist and exposes Tim to their fanbase. If, say, he can collaborate with an artist like **Afrojack or Vintage Culture** (both of whom are part of Wynn’s 2025 roster【5†L76-L82】), that track could naturally garner support at XS. Even a collab with a rising star (someone who’s getting a lot of festival play) can catch the ears of XS’s booking team. Plan to have a collaborative single ready to release or announce around the time of pitching to XS – it strengthens his case if he can say “I have a track coming out with [Big Name]”.

**Secure DJ Support at XS:** Getting the support of DJs who are already in the XS rotation can significantly raise Tim’s profile with the club. Steps to achieve this:

- **Promo Pool for DJs:** Create a private promo list of top DJs (especially those with Wynn residencies or frequent bookings) and send them exclusive previews of Tim’s new tracks. For example, if he produces a festival-ready remix, send it to DJs like **The Chainsmokers, Marshmello, Diplo, Two Friends, Vintage Culture** and their DJs-in-residence peers. Include a personal note referencing any connection or admiration, and mention it’s a track that fits their set. The aim is to have a few of these tastemakers start **spinning Tim’s tracks at XS** or other big gigs. If a Tim White track consistently gets played in XS by another DJ and gets a great crowd reaction, the XS talent bookers will take notice.

- **DJ Testimonials:** If Tim has close contacts who DJ at XS (or even at other Vegas clubs), request a bit of help: they could drop Tim’s name in conversations with promoters (“Hey, have you heard Tim’s new track? It’s been killing it in my sets”) or even bring Tim along to XS in person and introduce him to the crew. In the best case, a resident DJ might invite Tim for a **tag-team set or a cameo** during their night – for example, joining for a song or two when the headliner is a friend. This kind of **guest appearance** (even unannounced) can be leveraged later as a foot-in-the-door and proof of concept.

- **Targeted Release Timing:** Align one of Tim’s releases with a major Vegas event or season. For instance, dropping a summer anthem right before **EDC Week** (when all clubs, including XS, have stacked lineups and industry people in town) could increase the chances that big DJs pick it up for their sets. Or aim for just before **Art of the Wild** (Wynn’s boutique house/techno event series【5†L79-L82】), if his style leans that way – house DJs coming for that could support his track. Generating buzz in the Vegas music community will make the eventual pitch to XS feel timely and relevant.

By executing this music strategy, Tim will build a narrative that **“Tim White is back with irresistible new music.”** The combination of a buzzworthy single, strategic remixes, and collabs – coupled with support from established DJs – will create the *musical momentum* to catch XS Las Vegas’s attention.

## 3. Networking & Industry Leverage

**Use Existing Connections:** Tim’s past success means he likely has connections in the industry – now is the time to activate them. The goal here is to get a **direct line to the XS booking team** (Wynn Nightlife talent buyers) through warm introductions and endorsements:

- **Map Out Key Contacts:** Make a list of any contacts who have influence in Vegas or with Wynn’s team. This might include: fellow artists he’s worked with who are on Wynn’s roster or have played XS; managers or agents he knows who represent Vegas headliners; label executives or PR agents with Vegas relationships; and even promoters or nightlife insiders in Las Vegas. For each contact, plan an approach to **ask for advice or help**. Example: If he’s friendly with a DJ like *[Prominent House DJ]* who plays XS, take them out for coffee or call them to talk about his goal – they may offer to put in a good word or connect Tim with the talent booker.

- **Leverage Booking Agents:** If Tim doesn’t currently have a booking agent focused on North America or Vegas, securing one could be pivotal. Top Vegas clubs often work closely with major talent agencies (WME, CAA, AM Only/Wasserman, etc.). Engaging an agent who has clients at XS can open doors. For instance, if Agency X handles several Wynn residents, having that agency pitch Tim as part of their roster package can add credibility. If Tim still has a manager, that manager should **network with agency reps** at industry events and showcase Tim’s new material and branding, signaling that Tim is a hot opportunity for Vegas. In parallel, Tim can attend industry gatherings (ADE, IMS, or even Vegas’s own nightlife industry events) to meet agents and promoters in person – a handshake and elevator pitch about his comeback can make the follow-up introduction easier.

- **Approach Wynn Nightlife Executives:** Identify the **talent buyers or executives** at Wynn Nightlife who make booking decisions. (For example, Wynn’s Assistant VP of Nightlife or Entertainment Director – individuals like Ryan Jones or the Wynn Nightlife Managing Partners have held these roles). Wynn’s team has explicitly stated they seek *“up-and-coming artists and superstar DJs from around the world to break into the Vegas market”*【30†L239-L243】. This means they are open to fresh talent if presented in the right way. Tim should have his manager reach out via email or phone to the Wynn talent office (Wynn’s site provides a general nightlife contact) with a **concise, compelling pitch** (more on pitch in section 4). However, a cold email is not enough – that initial contact should be bolstered by a referral or mention from someone the Wynn team trusts. That’s where his network comes in: for instance, if a mutual industry friend can say to the Wynn booker, *“Hey, I heard Tim White is making moves again – you might want to check him out,”* it primes them to be receptive when Tim’s info comes across their desk.

- **Key Industry Players to Engage:** In addition to Wynn’s internal team, Tim should loop in a few **influential people around the Vegas scene**:
  - *Club Promoters & VIP hosts:* These folks have the ear of management because they directly drive revenue. If Tim knows any promoters (even from LA or other cities) who have contacts in Vegas, he should let them know he’s seeking an XS slot. Sometimes promoters can suggest talent to fill opening slots, especially if they believe the artist will draw a crowd of their own.
  - *Media and PR Personalities:* Vegas club bookers read industry press. If Tim can get a feature in a Vegas magazine or popular blog teasing his comeback (e.g., *LasVegasWeekly, EDM.com, DJ Times*), those articles might catch a talent buyer’s eye or at least make his name familiar. Engaging a PR person who specializes in nightlife could help place such stories. 
  - *Wynn Residents’ Managers:* Often, resident DJs’ managers have a close relationship with the club. If Tim has any connection to, say, the management of Chainsmokers, Diplo, or others, a casual ask like *“Would you mind passing my new track to the Wynn team or let them know I’m interested in performing?”* could be very effective. It’s low effort for them and could carry weight.

**Networking Etiquette:** When leveraging connections, Tim must be **professional and gracious**. Rather than just asking outright for a gig, he should frame conversations around seeking guidance: *“You’ve been playing Vegas for a while – what do you think is the best way for someone with my background to get an XS slot?”* People are often happy to advise, and their advice can naturally segue into actively helping. As his new music releases roll out, he can follow up with contacts by sharing the successes (e.g., “My new track just hit 500k streams in a week, and [Big DJ] played it at EDC!”) – this keeps his network engaged in his journey and reinforces that Tim is *creating buzz* that could benefit XS. 

By the time he formally approaches the Wynn team, the ideal scenario is that **they’ve already heard Tim’s name** mentioned by a few people in their orbit. Through strategic networking and use of his industry leverage, Tim can make sure he’s on the radar as *“the next up-and-coming act to break into Vegas”*, aligning with Wynn’s own goals for fresh talent.

## 4. Booking & Negotiation Approach

Securing the actual booking at XS will require a smart pitch and likely a phased approach (starting with a smaller slot before anything long-term). Here’s how Tim should navigate pitching and negotiating:

**Craft an Irresistible Pitch:** It’s important to remember that competition is fierce – *“everyone wants to… get a Las Vegas residency”*【8†L5-L8】 – so Tim’s pitch needs to stand out. He should prepare a **professional pitch deck or EPK package** tailored specifically for XS/Wynn. This should include: 

- A one-sheet with his **artist profile** (highlights of his career: e.g., “Accumulated 50+ million streams”, “Collaborated with XYZ big artist”, “Former [Record Label] signee”), **current buzz** (“New single gaining DJ support and viral traction”), and a clear statement of **what he offers to XS** (“High-energy house DJ with a crossover hit potential – ready to light up the XS main room and engage VIP crowds”). 
- Links to or embedded **media**: a short video reel of live performances (with crowd reaction shots to prove he hypes a room), snippets of upcoming tracks, and perhaps testimonials or quotes (if any – even a quote from a known DJ like “Tim’s new music is fire!” would help).
- **Brand alignment**: Include a few visuals of his branding that echo XS’s vibe (so the booker can *picture* Tim on their stage and marketing). Even mockup a flyer image with “Tim White @ XS” to make it tangible.

In the pitch communication (whether email or via his agent to Wynn’s talent buyer), keep it **concise and impactful**. Highlight that Tim is an *experienced pro making a comeback*, with new music that’s getting attention, and underscore any Vegas-relevant angles (for example, mention if he has a strong fanbase in California or the West Coast that might travel to Vegas to see him, which is a plus for the club).

**Offer a Trial Opportunity:** Rather than immediately asking for a headline residency slot (which is unrealistic at first), Tim should position his ask in a way that feels low-risk to Wynn. For instance: 

- **Opening or Guest Set:** Pitch the idea of Tim doing an **opening set** for one of XS’s big headliners on a busy night, or a **guest DJ spot** on a Sunday night or an industry event. Emphasize that his style will smoothly set the tone for the headliner (house music that’s exciting but not overshadowing the main act) and that he’ll bring in some of his own supporters to add to the crowd. Opening sets are often given to up-and-comers, and this could be Tim’s “audition.” Even a 60-90 minute early slot gives him a chance to impress the club management. Another angle is the **Sunday Night Swim** or offseason nights – Wynn sometimes features newer talent on such events when the stakes aren’t as high as a peak Friday/Saturday. Tim could volunteer to play one of those at a promotional rate to prove himself.

- **Back-to-Back or Special Event:** If an opening set isn’t readily offered, Tim could propose doing a **back-to-back (B2B)** set with a friend who is already booked. For example, if one of his connections has a gig at XS or Encore Beach Club, they could formally propose a B2B set (clubs occasionally like advertising two names together for novelty). This way, Tim’s name is on the bill alongside a trusted act, which mitigates risk in the eyes of the booker.

- **Flexible Booking Terms:** In the initial negotiation, Tim (through his agent/manager) should be **very flexible** on terms like fee and billing. The first goal is to get *in the door*, not to make big money. He might even indicate he’s willing to play the first gig for just travel expenses or a modest fee, knowing that the real payoff is proving himself for future bookings. Also, be open about scheduling – offer to step in if they have any last-minute cancellations or need a filler on a busy weekend. If he’s on standby ready to fly to Vegas, it shows enthusiasm and reliability.

**Emphasize Mutual Benefit:** When negotiating, frame Tim’s value in terms of what **XS gains** by booking him:
- He has **fresh buzz** that could draw curious attendees (especially if his new track or collab is charting or trending, people will want to see him live).
- He brings **experience** – unlike a total newcomer, he’s performed big shows before, so he knows how to work a crowd and handle high-pressure gigs (meaning the club can trust him with their stage and expensive production).
- His **music style complements** the current residency roster, filling a niche. For example, if XS’s lineup is heavy on EDM/pop acts, Tim’s house set can add variety while still keeping a mainstream appeal (catering to house music fans in Vegas who may be looking for that sound). Point out any gap he fills, like “XS has tech-house oriented acts like Black Coffee and mainstream EDM like Chainsmokers – Tim’s sound sits right between, which is perfect for opening or priming the crowd.”

- He will give **promotional support**: promise that Tim will promote the event heavily on his social media, possibly do a post-show recap video, etc., to drive more attention to the club. Clubs appreciate artists who act like partners in promotion, not just hired hands.

**Step-by-Step to Secure the Slot:**
1. **Initial Outreach (Month 6-7 in timeline):** After building some buzz, have his representative reach out to Wynn’s talent team with the pitch materials. Make sure to mention any recent wins (e.g., “XYZ song just got played on BBC Radio 1” or “Tim just did a guest mix for [big channel]”) to show current relevance.
2. **Follow-Up Meeting/Showcase:** Ideally, secure a face-to-face meeting in Vegas or a Zoom call to personally introduce Tim and let him express his passion for joining XS. If Wynn’s team is hesitant, suggest that they come see Tim perform elsewhere – for example, if Tim schedules a notable club show in LA or a festival set, invite the Wynn booker as a VIP guest to witness the set live.
3. **Negotiate a Trial Gig:** Once interest is piqued, work out a date for an opening slot or one-off guest gig. Negotiate the basics (date, set length, time, compensation). As noted, Tim should be very accommodating here. If they offer him a typically less desirable slot (like a late-night 2am closer after the headliner, or a Sunday early set), take it – it’s all about the foot in the door.
4. **Contract & Conditions:** Ensure any agreement for the gig is in writing. Also, clarify if there are any restrictions (for example, sometimes clubs have clauses that you can’t play at a rival venue in Vegas within X days of the show – Tim should be mindful of not double-booking any other Vegas opportunity that conflicts).
5. **Prepare for Performance (Section 5 below) and absolutely crush the gig.** Then, immediately after: gather feedback, possibly a short testimonial from the promoter (“crowd loved it!”). Use this success as leverage to discuss future bookings or a short-term residency (e.g., a series of dates over the summer).

In summary, **patience and persistence** are key. The negotiation process might take a few touchpoints over several weeks or months. By being professional, prepared, and flexible, Tim can convert a single opportunity into a recurring role. The first booking at XS is the hardest to get – after he proves himself, subsequent negotiations for more gigs or a residency will be much easier and on more favorable terms. 

## 5. Performance Preparation & Differentiation

Once a gig is on the horizon, Tim’s focus must shift to delivering an **unforgettable performance** that not only wins over the XS crowd but also impresses the club’s management (so they invite him back). Preparation and differentiation are crucial given the high bar set by XS’s resident DJs.

**Tailor the Set for XS:** The atmosphere at XS is high-energy and upscale – guests are often big spenders, tourists, and EDM enthusiasts expecting a **show**. Tim should craft a DJ set that aligns with this environment:
- Plan a set that is **uplifting, familiar yet fresh**. Unlike underground clubs, a Vegas crowd responds well to recognizable elements. Tim can stand out by including **crossover house tracks** – for example, mixing a classic vocal hook or a current pop hit’s acapella into his house beats at key moments. This keeps the energy accessible. He should include his own productions (especially his new single and remixes) as highlights, but frame them with other bangers that the crowd knows. A good target is to make sure every 10-minute stretch of the set has at least one *“Oh I know this one!”* moment for casual club-goers, whether it’s a riff from a famous song or a trending dance hit.
- **Vegas-style Remixes & Mashups:** To further tailor his set, Tim can prepare a few exclusive mashups or VIP edits specifically for this XS show. For instance, taking a well-known festival anthem and layering a house beat or his own synth hook over it. These custom edits can become “wow moments” that differentiate his set from another DJ’s. It’s even better if one of these mashups involves a track strongly associated with Vegas (imagine a twist on an Elvis snippet or a classic Vegas anthem like “Shots” by LMFAO, done in a stylish way – something that nods to Vegas culture in the middle of a house set, sparking cheers).
- **Dynamic Pacing:** Plan the flow to suit an opening or peak slot as needed. If he’s opening, he should start groovy and gradually build to an energetic climax right before handing off to the headliner – showing he knows how to set the stage. If he’s playing a late-night or guest spot in the middle, he can hit harder from the get-go. Either way, avoid long lulls or overly experimental parts; keep it tight and exciting. 

**High-Impact Production Value:** XS Nightclub is famed for its production – dazzling lighting, LED screens, cryo jets, confetti, maybe even aerial performers on big nights. Tim should coordinate and take advantage of these to amplify his performance:
- **Visuals:** If possible, provide the VJ/lighting team with any custom visuals or a logo graphic ahead of time. Even a simple but striking logo of “Tim White” that flashes on XS’s LED screens during his set will cement his brand in attendees’ minds. Ask if he can bring a custom intro visual – for example, a 30-second intro clip with his name and some thematic imagery – to play as he begins. If he doesn’t have visuals, at least ensure the club has his logo file so they can display it.
- **Intro and Outro:** Create a memorable **intro track** for the set. Many top DJs have unique intros that build anticipation (a cinematic intro that drops into a signature tune). Tim can make a 1-2 minute intro mashup that maybe teases melodies of his viral hits or uses a dramatic voiceover (even something like “Vegas, make some noise for Tim White!” pre-recorded). This immediately grabs attention and signals that *something special* is happening. Similarly, plan an **outro** that leaves the crowd cheering – perhaps ending with a beloved classic house track or his own famous song if he has one, to finish on a high note.
- **Use of Effects:** Communicate with XS’s booth tech about the timing of CO₂ cannon blasts, fireworks (if any), or confetti. For example, if Tim knows the exact minute his biggest drop hits, he can ask, “Can we shoot confetti at this moment?” Coordinating these effects will make his set feel as produced as those of the resident stars. It shows he understands the Vegas showmanship element.

**Differentiate Performance Style:** To stand out among elite DJs, Tim should identify a **unique element** in his performance:
- Perhaps it’s **live elements**: If Tim is able, he could incorporate a live sampler or drum pad to play some percussion over his tracks, or bring on a live vocalist or instrumentalist for one song (imagine a saxophonist jamming over a house track – clubs love that kind of surprise, but it must be tightly rehearsed). Even a brief live addition can create a buzz (*“Did you see that DJ with the live sax at XS? That was cool!”*).
- Maybe it’s **MCing**: Many EDM DJs don’t use the mic much, but someone like Lil Jon (who has DJ’d in Vegas) does hype on the mic. Tim could prepare a couple of short crowd shout-outs (“Put your hands up, Vegas!” type of lines) to use sparingly at peak moments. If he’s comfortable, this can engage the crowd more. But if it’s not his style, it’s better to skip forced MCing – an alternative is to collaborate with the club’s hype MC for the night, syncing up on when to energize the crowd.
- **Genre Twists:** While staying within his house lane mostly, Tim could plan one unexpected genre switch for impact – e.g., a quick transition into a known trap or hip-hop track that everyone knows, just for 30 seconds, before dropping back into house. This can electrify the room if done at the right time (think of how some house DJs suddenly drop something like a familiar rap a cappella over a house beat – the crowd sings along, then he slams back into 4/4 kick). Doing this once or twice can set his set apart as more fun and unpredictable.

**Technical Preparation:** Well before the show, Tim should practice his set **multiple times**, including on CDJs similar to what XS has (usually Pioneer CDJ-3000s and a DJM-900Nexus mixer). Iron out any tricky transitions or mashup cue points. Have backup USBs with his set and music (Vegas clubs are top-notch but one should always have backups). Also, prepare for on-the-fly adjustments: read the crowd on the night and be ready to improvise if needed (for example, if he’s opening and the room isn’t filled yet, he might extend a groove section until more people arrive, or if the crowd is roaring to a particular style he plays, he can insert another similar track).

Additionally, **mental preparation** is key: Vegas gigs can be intimidating with all the VIPs and the knowledge that this is a big break. Tim should arrive early, get comfortable with the booth setup, maybe have a brief chat with the production crew and sound team to build rapport (they might give him pointers like “this crowd is more into vocals tonight” if they have insight). By showtime, he wants to be confident and focused, treating it as if he’s already a resident.

**Match XS’s High Standards:** Remember, XS is a club that hosts the world’s top DJs with elaborate shows. It’s important Tim demonstrates professionalism equal to those stars. That means:
- **Punctuality:** be at the venue well ahead of his set time.
- **Dress the part:** Look stylish and put-together, as image matters in Vegas. Maybe wear something eye-catching (not over-the-top, but for instance a cool jacket or branded tee) so that photos of the night easily identify him.
- **Energy on Stage:** Even if it’s an opener slot, show energy – smile, dance, interact with the music. The crowd and management should see he’s loving what he does. Enthusiasm is contagious.

By delivering a set that is musically on-point for the XS crowd and adding his own flair, Tim will turn his performance into a **memorable event**. The club management will take note if people are dancing hard, if VIP guests stay engaged instead of retreating to their phones, and if there’s a buzz in the room during/after his set. **Success metrics** for the night: applause and cheers when he’s done, perhaps chants for an encore (even if he can’t do one), and positive feedback from the headliner or staff like “Great set!” If he achieves that, he’ll have differentiated himself as not just another DJ, but as an **artist worthy of more spotlight at XS**.

## 6. Marketing & Hype Generation

To ensure that booking Tim becomes a *no-brainer* for XS, there needs to be a **buzz** around him that the club cannot ignore. This means ramping up marketing and hype before and around the time of the pitch, so that Tim’s “comeback” is a talking point in the industry and among fans. Here’s how to generate that hype:

**Social Media Campaign:** Tim should treat his journey to XS as a story that fans can follow, creating anticipation:
- **Teasers and Announcements:** Start planting the seed on social media that *“big things are coming.”* For example, about 2-3 months into executing this plan, he can tweet or post, *“Just had an amazing meeting… big news for the summer 👀”* – something cryptic to stir curiosity. When new music is about to drop, use countdown posts, behind-the-scenes clips of making the track, and after release, share clips of DJs supporting it (a video of a famous DJ dropping Tim’s track to a festival crowd is gold content).
- **Consistent Content:** Implement a content calendar. Each week, there should be at least 2-3 notable posts: one could be #TBT (throwback) to his earlier career highlights (reminding people “oh yeah, I remember that song of his!”), another could be a studio or DJ routine video (showing he’s actively sharpening skills), and another could be engaging stories (polls about which version of a remix people like, etc.). The idea is to present Tim as very active and in-demand. Social media hype can translate to industry hype when the right people are watching.

- **Engage the Community:** Encourage his followers to spread the word. For instance, run a **contest or challenge** on TikTok/Reels using his new track (like a dance challenge or a user-generated content challenge). If one of his tracks can spark a viral dance or trend, that’s massive free promotion. Even on a smaller scale, if 100 fans make videos using his song, that’s evidence of growing popularity. He can later cite this in his pitch (e.g., “our hashtag got X million views”).

**Press & PR Blitz:** A targeted PR campaign can position Tim’s return as a newsworthy event:

## 7. Backup Options & Expansion

Even with the best plan, landing an XS gig can take time or unexpected turns. Tim should pursue his XS goal while also keeping alternative paths open in case XS isn’t immediately available. Additionally, it’s wise to have a timeline to track progress and ensure each step builds towards the ultimate objective. Below are backup venue options and a 12-month execution timeline.

### **Alternative Venues & Paths in Vegas**

If XS doesn’t bite right away, Tim can target other **high-end Vegas venues** as stepping stones. Securing any notable Vegas booking will boost his resume and could lead to XS reconsidering him down the line. Some top alternatives:

- **Encore Beach Club (Day/Night):** Since Encore Beach Club (EBC) is also run by Wynn, it’s closely linked to XS. Sometimes artists who aren’t on the XS night roster perform at EBC daytime parties or at EBC at Night. If Tim’s sound fits a pool party vibe (house music is great for daytime), he could angle for a set there. It’s slightly less pressure than XS Nightclub but equally prestigious. Success at EBC could be a direct bridge to XS (plus Wynn’s team is the same). 

- **Zouk Nightclub / Ayu Dayclub (Resorts World):** Zouk is one of the newer mega-clubs in Vegas with residents like Tiësto, Zedd, and Jonas Blue. They might be open to a fresh act for an opening set or mid-week event. It’s a high-end club that would still look great on Tim’s CV. In Vegas, names like Tiësto and Zedd are at Zouk【22†L43-L47】, indicating the caliber of talent they host. Tim could have his team approach Zouk’s bookers similarly – sometimes new clubs are even more eager to showcase rising talent to differentiate themselves.

- **Hakkasan Group / Tao Group Venues:** These include **Hakkasan Nightclub** and **Omnia** (major EDM clubs), as well as **Marquee** (at Cosmo) and **Tao**. Omnia at Caesars Palace hosts superstars like Martin Garrix and Kaskade【22†L43-L47】, but they also have a secondary room (the Heart room) and off-nights. Marquee, while slightly smaller, has a strong house music program on certain nights and a Dayclub. Tao Group might consider Tim for a guest set especially if he has connections in NYC or LA that overlap (Tao Group runs clubs in multiple cities). - **Art of the Wild / Festival Events:** Wynn’s **Art of the Wild** is a special event weekend focusing on underground and house talent【5†L79-L82】. If XS proper is hard to crack, getting on the lineup of Art of the Wild (perhaps as an early set) could be another route to playing on Wynn property. Similarly, Vegas has **EDC Week parties** (all clubs throw massive events around EDC). Tim could aim to get booked at any EDC Week pool party or nightclub event – even if it’s not XS, it might be a collateral event at Wynn or elsewhere. - **Smaller Upscale Venues:** There are venues like **Light** at Mandalay Bay or **Jewel** at Aria which are a tier below XS in profile but still notable. If, for instance, Marquee or Zouk picks up Tim and he does well (and especially if it generates social media buzz or press), the Wynn bookers might think, *“We had a chance to book him and now the competition has him – maybe we should get him for a date too.”* There is a bit of an arms race for talent in Vegas, so being booked elsewhere can paradoxically make XS more interested. That said, Tim should be careful not to sign any exclusive residency with another club if his heart is set on XS – at least not without giving Wynn one more shot, unless the other deal is too good to pass up.

**Longer-Term Expansion:** If the XS plan succeeds, fantastic – then the expansion becomes about possibly turning a one-off gig into a **seasonal residency** (a series of shows) at XS or Encore. If XS is slow to happen, Tim can expand his scope:

- Continue releasing music and growing fanbase so that by the next year, he’s undeniably a fit for a Vegas residency spot when they refresh lineups.

In short, these backups ensure that even if Plan A (XS) takes longer, Tim is still moving forward and staying visible in the right circles.

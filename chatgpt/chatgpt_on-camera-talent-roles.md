Here’s a clear breakdown of on-camera talent roles in Hollywood:

---

### **Primary On-Camera Roles**

**1. Series Regular**
- Contracted for an entire TV season.
- Guaranteed episodes and recurring story arcs.
- Paid weekly/episode; Screen Actors Guild (SAG-AFTRA) union status almost always required.

**2. Recurring Guest Star**
- Appears in multiple episodes but not every one.
- Significant role arcs; credited near the top.

**3. Guest Star**
- Major role for one episode.
- Substantial scenes, often drives the episode’s plot.

**4. Co-Star**
- Minor/intermediate role with limited lines.
- Common entry point for new actors in TV.

**5. Day Player**
- Short-term job, usually 1–2 shoot days.
- May or may not include lines.

**6. Background / Extra**
- Non-speaking roles filling environment realism.
- Not part of the narrative; lowest pay tier.

---

### **Film-Specific Terms**

**Lead**
- Central character(s); most screen time.

**Supporting**
- Significant but secondary to lead(s).

**Featured**
- Small role with a moment or a line.
- Higher status than background, lower than supporting.

---

### **Commercial Roles**

**Principal**
- Recognizable and onscreen; may speak or perform identifiable action.

**Featured**
- Clearly visible but no major action or speaking.

**Background**
- Same as film/TV: fills space, no individualized focus.

---

### **Specialized On-Camera Roles**

- **Stand-in:** Used to set lighting/framing for lead actors; usually matches height/build/skin tone.
- **Photo Double:** Used when actor’s face isn’t shown (over-the-shoulder, pickup shots).
- **Body Double:** Matches physique for nudity, stunts, or specialty shots.
- **Stunt Performer:** Performs dangerous or technical physical action.
- **Motion Capture / Performance Capture:** For VFX-driven characters (e.g., video games, creature roles).

---

### **Union Context (Los Angeles)**

Almost all scripted roles above the co-star level fall under **SAG-AFTRA**. Union eligibility improves pay, working hours, break requirements, protections, and residuals.

- Israetel frames hypertrophy in terms of *MEV (minimum effective volume)* and *MRV (maximum recoverable volume)*. For a muscle like the biceps, productive weekly volume tends to be roughly:

- Both coaches imply growth ceilings are individual: genetics largely determine how much muscle you can add, and after many years of well-programmed training most natural trainees approach their *genetic potential* for size. - Neither Menno nor Mike assigns a specific maximum biceps size.  
- Growth is bounded by your *recoverable training volume* and *genetic potential*.  
- For biceps hypertrophy: aim for **~8–20+ weekly sets**, adjust based on recovery, and track progress — once size/strength stalls over months, you’re approaching your effective ceiling per their models. citeturn0search12

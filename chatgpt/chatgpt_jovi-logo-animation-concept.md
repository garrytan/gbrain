# Animation storyboard

## Duration

**2.8 seconds total**

Enough time to feel premium. Short enough for ads, launch videos, website hero loops, and app splash moments.

---

## Frame 01 — Pure white silence

**Time:** `0.00s – 0.18s`

```text
[white screen]
```

Nothing appears immediately. Let the white screen breathe for a fraction of a second.

**Purpose:** makes the mark feel intentional, not like a loading animation.

---

## Frame 02 — The first stroke appears

**Time:** `0.18s – 0.36s`

A small black rounded stroke appears at the upper-right area of where the smile will be.

Clock position:

```text
Start: around 2 o’clock
```

Visual:

```text
             .
```

The point should feel like the beginning of a pen stroke, but cleaner and more digital than hand-drawn.

---

## Frame 03 — Smile reveal

**Time:** `0.36s – 0.72s`

The stroke draws downward and across the bottom, forming a smile.

Path direction:

```text
2 o’clock → 4 o’clock → 6 o’clock → 8 o’clock → 10 o’clock
```

Visual diagram:

```text
          \________/
```

This is the emotional moment. For a split second, the logo reads as a smile.

**Meaning:** Jovie creates forward motion, but it starts with something human.

---

## Frame 04 — Smile becomes loop

**Time:** `0.72s – 1.12s`

The smile does not stop. It keeps moving upward from the left side and begins wrapping into a circle.

Path direction:

```text
10 o’clock → 11 o’clock → 12 o’clock → 1 o’clock
```

Visual diagram:

```text
             __
          /      \
         |        |
          \______/
```

The viewer realizes this was never just a smile. It was the beginning of a flywheel.

---

## Frame 05 — Circle closes, momentum locks

**Time:** `1.12s – 1.38s`

The outer circle completes. The mark now reads as a loop.

Visual diagram:

```text
            ○
```

At this point, the logo should still feel like a single thick stroke, not yet the final icon.

**Motion note:** no bounce. Use a precise deceleration.

---

## Frame 06 — The spiral intelligence cut

**Time:** `1.38s – 1.68s`

The stroke continues into the upper-right interior, creating the signature Jovie spiral break.

This is where the logo becomes specific.

Visual diagram:

```text
            ◔
```

The white negative space at the top should feel carved, as if the system is completing a thought.

**Meaning:** the flywheel becomes intelligent. It is momentum with direction.

---

## Frame 07 — Final logo resolves

**Time:** `1.68s – 1.86s`

The animated stroke resolves into the exact final Jovie icon.

No wobble. No glow. No overshoot.

Visual:

```text
          [final black Jovie icon]
```

The icon should feel surgically locked into place.

## Frame 08 — Copy appears

**Time:** `1.86s – 2.18s`

The line fades in underneath.

```text

```

Use opacity fade plus a barely perceptible upward motion, around `4px`.

No typewriter effect. No word-by-word reveal.


or

Keep it secondary. It should feel like a footnote, not a CTA button.

```text
                              jov.ie
```

# Logo geometry spec

Use the final icon as the master vector.

Approximate ratios:

```text
Outer diameter: 1.00D
Inner circular counter: 0.44D – 0.47D
Stroke mass: 0.265D – 0.28D
Top negative spiral cut: 0.045D – 0.065D at narrowest point
```

For a `96px` logo:

```text
Outer diameter: 96 px
Inner counter: 43–45 px
Stroke mass: 25–27 px
Top cut: 4–6 px
```

The animated stroke should use:

```text
Stroke cap: round
Stroke join: round
Stroke width: same as final logo mass
Fill: none during drawing phase
Final: morph/crossfade into filled master logo
```

# Motion spec

## Draw path

Animate the logo using a single SVG/After Effects path with trim paths.

Draw order:

```text
1. Start at upper-right smile point
2. Draw through the bottom arc
3. Continue up the left side
4. Complete the circle
5. Continue into the upper spiral break
6. Resolve into final logo
```

Clock map:

```text
2:00 → 4:30 → 6:00 → 7:30 → 10:00 → 12:00 → 1:30 → inner top finish
```

## Easing

Use smooth, premium easing:

```text
Smile reveal:
cubic-bezier(0.33, 0.00, 0.20, 1.00)

Loop completion:
cubic-bezier(0.22, 0.61, 0.36, 1.00)

Final settle:
cubic-bezier(0.16, 1.00, 0.30, 1.00)
```

Avoid spring physics. Avoid elastic motion. Avoid cartoon bounce.

# Sound design

The animation should have almost no sound.

Suggested audio:

| Moment | Sound |
|---|---|
| Stroke begins | Barely audible soft friction / air tone |
| Smile becomes circle | Very subtle low whoosh |
| Logo locks | Tiny sub click, more felt than heard |
| Text appears | Silence |

No chime. No startup sound. No sparkle. No “tech reveal” hit.

The sound should feel like a camera lens, a tape machine, or a quiet piece of premium hardware.

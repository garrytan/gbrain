## Assistant

To mitigate nausea while using tirzepatide, try the following:

1. **Start Low, Go Slow** – If your dose was recently increased, allow time for your body to adjust before considering further increases.  
2. **Eat Smaller Meals** – Large meals can worsen nausea. Stick to small, frequent meals.  
3. **Avoid Fatty & Greasy Foods** – High-fat foods can slow digestion and make nausea worse.  
4. **Stay Hydrated** – Sip water, ginger tea, or electrolyte drinks throughout the day.  
5. **Take It at Night** – Some people find taking tirzepatide before bed reduces nausea.  
6. **Use Ginger or Peppermint** – Ginger tea, ginger chews, or peppermint oil may help.  
7. **Avoid Rapid Eating or Drinking** – Eat slowly and avoid large gulps of liquids.  
8. **Consider OTC Remedies** – Antacids, Dramamine, or other nausea meds can help, but consult your doctor first.  
9. **Monitor Food Triggers** – Track which foods worsen symptoms and adjust accordingly.  
10. **Rest & Move Carefully** – Sudden movements can trigger nausea; try lying down in a cool, dark room if needed.  

If nausea persists or worsens, talk to your doctor about dose adjustments or additional treatments.

## User

I have an iPhone 15 Pro and I'm about to go to T-Mobile and buy an iPhone 17 Pro. My iPhone 15 Pro has AppleCare Plus with Theft and Loss. Can I keep that AppleCare Plus with Theft and Loss on the 15 Pro and then also get the new 17 Pro and then also add AppleCare to that new device separately? Because I want to give this phone to Liv, but I want to keep the AppleCare on it and I'm just curious how I do it in a way that the AppleCare doesn't go away and she's still able to use her Apple ID to set up the phone, but it still has the AppleCare.

## Assistant

Short answer: Yes, Mr. White — you can keep AppleCare+ with Theft and Loss on the 15 Pro and also get AppleCare+ on the new 17 Pro. Liv can use her own Apple ID on the 15 Pro and the coverage can stay on that device, **as long as you handle ownership and billing correctly.**

Let’s break it down into exactly what to do and what to avoid.

---

## 1. How AppleCare+ with Theft and Loss Actually Works

Key points relevant to your situation:

- **AppleCare+ is tied to the device’s serial number/IMEI**, not the Apple ID that’s signed in.
- For *Theft and Loss* coverage specifically, the device must:
  - Have **Find My** turned on.
  - Be **linked to the Apple ID of the person who would file the claim** (important for Liv vs you).
- You can have multiple AppleCare+ plans on different devices under the same Apple ID / payment method with no issue.

So:  
- You can keep AppleCare+ on the 15 Pro.  
- You can buy a new AppleCare+ plan for the 17 Pro.  
- Liv can sign in with her own Apple ID on the 15 Pro and still use the phone normally.

The subtle part is **who you want to be able to file Theft/Loss claims**.

---

## 2. Who Should “Own” the AppleCare+ on the 15 Pro?

You have two options:

### Option A – You keep the AppleCare+ in your name
- You continue to pay for the AppleCare+ with Theft and Loss for the 15 Pro.
- The 15 Pro remains associated with **your Apple ID** for purposes of the plan.
- For Theft/Loss coverage to work correctly, **Find My should be on and the device should appear in your Apple ID’s device list.**
- Liv can still:
  - Use her own Apple ID on the phone (for iCloud, iMessage, App Store, etc.).
  - But if the phone is lost or stolen, **you** would file the claim with Apple/T-Mobile/Assurant (whoever is administering the coverage) because the plan is in your name.

This is usually how it’s done when a parent/partner “owns” the line/plan but someone else uses the phone.

### Option B – Transfer the AppleCare+ to Liv
Apple allows you to **transfer AppleCare+ to another person** in many regions, as long as:

- You transfer the device to them.
- You give them the proof of AppleCare+ coverage and proof of purchase.
- You follow Apple’s documented transfer steps (which typically involve contacting Apple Support and providing:
  - Policy/plan number  
  - Device serial number  
  - New owner’s contact details  

Once transferred:
- Liv would be the owner of that AppleCare+ contract.
- She would handle any Theft/Loss claims herself.

If you want everything truly “hers” and separate from you, this is the cleanest option.

---

## 3. Will AppleCare Get Cancelled When You Upgrade via T-Mobile?

Things that *usually do NOT* automatically cancel AppleCare+ on the 15 Pro:

- Buying a new iPhone 17 Pro on T-Mobile.
- Activating AppleCare+ on the 17 Pro.
- Giving the old device to someone else.

AppleCare+ only ends when:
- The term expires, **or**
- You cancel it (via Apple or your carrier), **or**
- The device is replaced under a claim in a way that terminates the old plan.

The potential gotcha:  
If your AppleCare+ on the 15 Pro was **originally set up through T-Mobile as part of your line**, sometimes carriers auto-cancel device protection when they mark the device as traded in or removed from the line.  

So before you do anything, ask T-Mobile explicitly:

> “Is my AppleCare+ with Theft and Loss billed through T-Mobile or directly through Apple? And if I upgrade/keep the old phone, does that coverage stay active on the 15 Pro?”

If it’s billed through **Apple directly** (common if you bought AppleCare in the Apple Store or later in Settings), you’re usually fine.

---

## 4. How to Give the 15 Pro to Liv Without Breaking Coverage

Do this step-by-step:

1. **Check how your AppleCare+ is billed**
   - On the 15 Pro:  
     `Settings → [your name] → Subscriptions`  
     or  
     `Settings → General → About → Coverage`
   - If it shows AppleCare+ with Theft and Loss managed by Apple, you’re good.  
   - If it references T-Mobile, ask T-Mobile whether an upgrade affects that plan.

2. **Decide who should file future theft/loss claims**
   - If **you** will: keep the plan in your name.  
   - If **Liv** should: plan to transfer the AppleCare+ to her via Apple Support after you hand over the phone.

3. **Prepare the phone for Liv (while keeping coverage)**
   - Turn off Activation Lock under your ID:
     - `Settings → [your name] → Find My → Find My iPhone` → turn off (you’ll need your Apple ID password).
   - Sign out of iCloud:
     - `Settings → [your name] → Sign Out`.
   - Optionally factory reset if you want it clean:
     - `Settings → General → Transfer or Reset iPhone → Erase All Content and Settings`.

4. **Set up the phone for Liv**
   - She turns it on and signs in with **her Apple ID** during setup.
   - She enables **Find My** under her Apple ID.
   - If you kept the plan in your name (Option A), just know:
     - The policy is still technically yours.
     - For Theft/Loss, you may want to keep your Apple ID associated to the plan and coordinate with Apple if a claim ever happens.
     - If Apple insists that the device must be under the same Apple ID as the plan owner for Theft/Loss claims, you may prefer the formal transfer (Option B).

5. **Transfer the AppleCare+ (only if you choose Option B)**
   - After setup, contact Apple Support (via Support app or website).
   - Tell them: you’re transferring ownership of the iPhone 15 Pro *and* its AppleCare+ with Theft and Loss to Liv.
   - They’ll ask for:
     - Device serial number.
     - AppleCare agreement number (from your email or Coverage section).
     - New owner details (Liv’s name, contact, etc.).

---

## 5. Adding AppleCare+ to the New iPhone 17 Pro

Once you get the 17 Pro:

- During purchase or shortly after:
  - You can add AppleCare+ at checkout via T-Mobile **or**
  - Add it directly with Apple:
    - `Settings → General → About → Coverage → Add AppleCare+` (if still within the eligibility window, usually 60 days).
- This new AppleCare+ plan is **totally separate** from the plan on the 15 Pro and won’t interfere with it.

---

## 6. Practical Script for T-Mobile / Apple Store

When you’re in-store, say something close to this:

> “I’m keeping my current iPhone 15 Pro and it already has AppleCare+ with Theft and Loss. I want to keep that coverage active on the 15 Pro because I’m giving it to someone else. I also want AppleCare+ on the new iPhone 17 Pro. Please make sure whatever you do for the upgrade does *not* cancel the coverage on the 15 Pro.”

If they say “we need to trade in the 15 Pro to get X discount,” then you can’t keep it *and* trade it in — so that’s a separate economic choice.

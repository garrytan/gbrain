## Assistant

Good question — this is a complex but common USPS forwarding scenario.  

Here’s how it works:  

---

### 1. **USPS forwarding is not daisy-chained**
Each Change-of-Address (COA) replaces the prior one. When you submitted a new forwarding request (e.g., from Poinsettia → Bronson), that new COA canceled the old one (Larrabee → Poinsettia). USPS systems always keep *only one* active forwarding address per name.  
**Result:** mail sent to Larrabee will *not* hop Larrabee → Poinsettia → Bronson. It will die at the first hop that’s expired or overridden.

---

### 2. **Forwarding durations and timing**
- **Standard USPS temporary forwarding:** 6 months from the start date.  
- **When you file a new one:** it overrides the old, even if the prior one still had months left.  
- **Mail already in transit** at the moment you filed the new one *may* still follow the old route, but that’s luck and timing.  

In your case:
- Larrabee → Poinsettia (May 2025 expiry, roughly) was superseded when you filed Poinsettia → Bronson (mid-September 2025 start).  
- So any mail sent to Larrabee after you filed the Poinsettia → Bronson COA will *not* forward twice.  
- Mail sent to Poinsettia after that filing should forward directly to Bronson until ~March 2026.  

---

### 3. **What’s likely happening now**
- **Larrabee mail:** forwarding is effectively dead; anything sent there will likely be returned to sender or discarded as undeliverable.  
- **Poinsettia mail:** should reach Bronson if addressed to you personally and the USPS COA was processed correctly.  
- **Bronson:** your stable delivery point now.  

---

### 4. **What you can still do**
1. **Call or visit your old post office (West Hollywood branch)** — request a *search or intercept* for mail addressed to you at 932 Larrabee. They can see if items are being held or returned.  
2. **Ask your old building managers or current residents** if anything arrived in your name.  
3. **For DMV / bank / official docs:**  
   - Update all addresses to Bronson directly with each institution.  
   - Re-issue anything important (plates, registration, debit card). USPS will not forward government mail (e.g., DMV, IRS) after the forwarding expires.  
4. **Set up USPS Informed Delivery** for Bronson — you’ll get daily scans of incoming mail.  
5. **Consider filing a Mail Recovery Center request** if you suspect a specific piece went missing and is valuable (license plates, debit card, etc.).  

---

### 5. **Bottom line**
- No daisy-chaining.  
- Only the *latest* COA applies.

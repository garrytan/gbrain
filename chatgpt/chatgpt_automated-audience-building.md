## The key product thesis

**Artists do not want analytics. They want to know:**

1. **Which post, link, release, drop, or page created fans?**
2. **Which fans are worth contacting?**
3. **What should I send them now?**
4. **Did it make money?**

Build the product around answering those four questions.

## Where you are right

Your best insight is that **owned audience growth is the compounding asset**.

Streams, social views, and link clicks are mostly rented attention. Email, SMS, presave identity, buyer records, tip history, city, platform preference, and campaign source create a reusable fan graph.

The current market validates the direction. Feature.fm already sells release links, pre-saves, future-save, contest/unlock pages, fanbase management, email collection, retargeting pixels, and Mailchimp/ActiveCampaign syncing. citeturn726517view1turn726517view6 Linkfire emphasizes full-funnel tracking, source/channel benchmarking, conversions, retargeting, and breaking data silos between merch, tours, and recorded music. citeturn368988view0turn368988view2 Laylo frames itself as a drop platform replacing email, SMS, presave, and tour announcement tools. citeturn726517view3 Openstage is explicitly positioning around owned fan relationships, consolidated fan data, automations, SMS, memberships, and monetization. citeturn842630view1turn842630view2

So the market already believes the broad category. Jovie needs a narrower, faster, more artist-native wedge:

> **“Every link builds your list. Every list member has attribution. Every campaign tells you what to post or send next.”**

## Where you may be wrong

### 1. “Build your list” alone is too generic

Everyone can say “own your audience.” The defensible edge is:

**attributed owned-audience growth + automated next actions.**

A plain email/SMS list is easy to copy. A fan timeline that says:

> “This fan came from the March 12 TikTok, clicked the merch drop, joined by SMS, lives in LA, prefers Spotify, bought the hoodie, and should get the presale link”

is much harder to replace.

### 2. Attribution will be directional, not perfect

TikTok, Instagram, dark social, screenshots, link-in-bio hops, browser privacy, and multi-device behavior will create gaps. Use **first touch, last touch, assisted touch, source-specific links, QR codes, UTMs, timestamp windows, and campaign IDs**. Avoid pretending every conversion is provable.

### 3. Artist MRR may be the wrong first metric

Many artists monetize through episodic spikes: merch drops, tickets, tips, releases, syncs, limited drops. “MRR” works if you add memberships, subscriptions, fan clubs, or paid communities.

A better near-term product metric:

> **Attributed fan revenue per owned contact.**

Then you can later expand into true recurring artist revenue through memberships, VIP clubs, paid drops, or fan subscriptions.

### 4. Building a full email/SMS platform too early can slow you down

Start with capture, attribution, segmentation, exports, and simple broadcasts. Full ESP/SMS infrastructure, deliverability, compliance, templates, unsubscribe handling, carrier rules, and deliverability ops can become a trap.

For SMS/email, consent and opt-out handling need to be built carefully. The FTC requires commercial email to avoid misleading headers/subjects, include a valid postal address, and provide opt-out handling; the FCC has also emphasized consumers’ right to revoke consent for autodialed texts/calls. citeturn253272search1turn253272search0

## Product principles

### 1. Every surface must capture or attribute

A profile block, smartlink, merch link, tip page, release page, or chat entry should create at least one of:

- fan identity
- source attribution
- revenue attribution
- segment membership
- next recommended action

### 2. Every campaign should have a source breakdown

Artists should see:

```txt
TikTok: 380 signups
IG carousel: 92 signups
Bio link: 44 signups
Email: 21 purchases
SMS: 17 purchases
```

### 3. Every fan should have source memory

The fan record should preserve:

- First source
- Latest source
- Best source
- Campaigns touched
- Purchases/tips
- Preferred CTA
- City
- Platform preference

### 4. Every insight should become an action

Avoid analytics that dead-end.

Bad:

> “TikTok drove 40% of traffic.”

Better:

> “TikTok drove 40% of new SMS fans. Send this unreleased-demo CTA to them now.”

### 5. AI should generate campaigns, not generic chat

Your AI should answer:

- “What should I post next?”
- “Which segment should I message?”
- “Which CTA worked?”
- “Which drop should I run?”
- “What caption should I use for this source?”
- “Which fans are likely buyers?”

## Final judgment

You are right on the strategic direction.

The refined answer:

> **Jovie’s competitive edge should be automated, attributed audience growth for artists. The product should center on campaigns, source-specific fan capture, fan timelines, automations, and revenue proof.**

The mistake would be reducing this to “email/SMS list building.” The stronger company is built around **closed-loop fan growth**:

```txt
content → click → signup → segment → message → purchase/tip/presave → insight → next campaign
```

Ship the attribution spine first. Then campaign builder. Then capture. Then automations. Then revenue attribution. Everything else is secondary.

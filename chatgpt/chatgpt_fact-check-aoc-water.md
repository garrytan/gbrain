## Assistant

Mr. White — **mostly misleading / unproven.**

## What’s true

- **AOC did hold up jars of discolored water** during a House hearing and questioned EPA Assistant Administrator Jessica Kramer about drinking-water complaints near Meta’s data center campus in Morgan County, Georgia. citeturn326300search1turn326300search4
- Residents have reported **murky water, low water pressure, and needing outside water** for cooking/bathing. EPA said it contacted Georgia/local officials to gather facts. citeturn326300search4
- Data centers can use a lot of water, and AOC’s office claims **10% of the community’s daily water use goes to the Meta data center** and that the area is projected toward a water deficit by 2030. That claim comes from AOC’s office, so treat it as advocacy-side framing unless independently confirmed. citeturn326300search1

## What’s unproven or exaggerated

- The meme implies companies are **literally pouring poison into drinking water**. There is **no verified finding** that Meta or any CEO/company dumped “poison” into Morgan County drinking water.
- EPA clarified that it is **gathering information**, which is **not the same as a formal federal investigation**. citeturn326300search4
- Meta denies causation and says an independent groundwater study found its data center operations/construction had **no impact** on the resident’s well; Meta also says construction/operations water came from the local water utility, **not groundwater wells**. citeturn326300search4
- The background image of a pipe dumping brown liquid into water appears to be **illustrative/meme imagery**, not evidence from the Georgia case.

## Bottom line

The **underlying concern is real**: residents near a Meta data center reported discolored water, and AOC pressed EPA about it.

The meme’s core claim — **“they pour 10,000 gallons of poison into your drinking water”** — is **unsupported rhetoric**, at least for the case shown. A defensible version would be:

> Residents near a Meta data center in Georgia have reported discolored well water and low water pressure. AOC raised the issue at a congressional hearing, and EPA is gathering information, while Meta denies its data center caused the problem.

## Assistant

Mr. White, this is almost always a simple clog in the pop-up or the P-trap. Here’s a concise fix:

1. **Clear the stopper**  
   - Twist out the pop-up stopper from the sink basin (it usually lifts out once you loosen the pivot rod under the sink).  
   - Wipe off any hair/grime, then drop it back in and test the drain.  

2. **Pull and clean the P-trap**  
   - Put a bucket underneath.  
   - Unscrew the two large slip-joint nuts on either end of the white plastic trap (no need for that spring clamp).  
   - Remove the trap, dump out gunk, rinse it out, then drop it back in.  

3. **Reassemble with the proper hardware**  
   - Remove that stainless “spring” clamp—it doesn’t seal a trap joint.  
   - Tighten the plastic slip nuts by hand (plus a ¼-turn with pliers) over a clean rubber washer.  

4. **Test and, if needed, snake deeper**  
   - Run water; if still slow, feed a small drum or hand-crank snake down the trap arm into the wall.  

That will restore full flow. Let me know if you hit any snags.

## Assistant

Mr. White — here are 10 tighter layout directions, focused on clarity, density, and scan speed. Each assumes you’re optimizing for “DJ + creator workflow,” not consumer playback.

---

## 1. **Left Rail Identity + Center Timeline**
- Left: artwork + title + artist (stacked)
- Center: large waveform (dominant)
- Right: controls + metadata (BPM, key, version)
- Progress + markers embedded in waveform

**Why:** puts attention on waveform immediately

---

## 2. **Waveform-First (Rekordbox style)**
- Full-width waveform across top (tall, ~120px)
- Under it: transport controls centered
- Bottom row: metadata chips (BPM, key, version)

**Why:** waveform becomes the interface, not decoration

---

## 3. **Transport-Centered Minimal**
- Center: large play/pause + scrub bar
- Left: track info compact
- Right: waveform mini preview

**Why:** fast control > visual detail

---

## 4. **Stacked Information Hierarchy**
- Row 1: Track title + duration (dominant)
- Row 2: waveform
- Row 3: controls + metadata inline

**Why:** clean reading order, reduces lateral scanning

---

## 5. **Compact DJ Strip (High Density)**
- Everything in one row:
  - [artwork] [title] [mini waveform] [controls] [BPM/key]
- Waveform is thin but interactive

**Why:** table-friendly, works inside lists

---

## 6. **Dual Waveform (Cue + Master)**
- Top: overview waveform (full track)
- Bottom: zoomed waveform (current playhead)
- Controls between them

**Why:** DJ precision (scrubbing + cueing)

---

## 7. **Right-Aligned Control Cluster**
- Left 70%: waveform
- Right 30%: vertical stack:
  - play / skip / loop
  - BPM/key
  - utilities

**Why:** muscle memory zone for controls

---

## 8. **Timeline with Event Markers**
- Waveform with labeled sections:
  - drop / intro / break / vocal
- Controls overlay on waveform
- Metadata minimized

**Why:** production + editing context

---

## 9. **Floating Glass Transport (Apple-style)**
- Large blurred container
- Waveform as subtle background layer
- Controls float above
- Metadata minimal, secondary

**Why:** premium feel, less utilitarian

---

## 10. **Multi-Track Stack (Library-aware)**
- Current track expanded (big waveform)
- Next/prev tracks collapsed underneath (mini waveforms)
- Controls pinned globally

**Why:** sequencing mindset (DJ sets / content batching)

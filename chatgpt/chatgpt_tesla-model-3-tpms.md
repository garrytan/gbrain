## Assistant

Osmosis is the natural movement of solvent molecules, usually water, from an area of lower solute concentration to an area of higher solute concentration through a semipermeable membrane. This process continues until equilibrium is reached, and the concentration of solute is the same on both sides of the membrane. Osmosis plays a crucial role in biological and chemical processes, such as the movement of water in and out of cells, and it's essential for maintaining the balance of substances within living organisms.

Ingredients:
- 1 oz Cognac
- 1 oz Amaretto liqueur

Instructions:
1. Fill a mixing glass with ice.

2. Pour 1 ounce of Cognac into the mixing glass.

3. Add 1 ounce of Amaretto liqueur.

4. Stir the ingredients well to chill the mixture and blend the flavors. This cocktail is typically stirred, not shaken.

5. Strain the mixture into an old-fashioned glass or a rocks glass filled with ice.

6. You can garnish the drink with a twist of lemon or orange peel if desired, but it's optional.

7. Serve and enjoy your French Connection cocktail!

Remember that cocktail recipes can vary slightly depending on personal preferences and the specific brands of Cognac and Amaretto being used, but this is the classic recipe for a French Connection.

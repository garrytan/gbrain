## Assistant

Here’s a structured set of ideas you can add to your ops database for silverware, napkins, and general server efficiency:

---

## **Silverware & Roll-Ups**
- Always roll up **all available inventory**, not just “what’s needed tonight.”  
  - Avoid the “bare minimum” trap—rolling more during downtime saves you from being short on high-volume nights.  
  - Roll-ups should always be **ahead of demand**, not reactive.  
- Treat roll-ups as a **non-negotiable prep task**, not something to cut corners on.  

---

## **Dish-Specific Silverware Practices**
- **Shrimp, sliders, shared apps** → send silverware automatically.  
- **Potato chips, popcorn** → paper napkins only, no roll-ups.  
- **Desserts** → use Toast’s spoon quantity prompt.  
  - Train servers to ask: “Are we sharing? How many spoons?”  
  - Prevents the 1-dessert/1-spoon mistake.  

---

## **Napkin Guidelines**
- Use **Hofmaster 17” x 17” 1/8 fold paper napkins** for shareable finger foods.  
- Drop **multiple napkins up front** for shared items (chips, popcorn, wings).  
  - Don’t force servers into “napkin chaser” mode.  
  - 5 napkins on the table now > 5 server trips later.  

---

## **Service Station & Refill Habits**
- If you notice a shortage (spoons, forks, napkins), **don’t just grab one**.  
  - Refill the whole station to prevent repeated trips.  
  - Always bring a little extra when you head to a table—ask if more is needed before leaving.  

---

## **Efficiency & Movement**
- Minimize wasted motion: never cross a 20,000 sq. ft. venue just to deliver **one spoon**.  
- When flagged down:  
  - Offer proactive add-ons: “Any more spoons? Extra napkins?”  
  - Reduce the odds of multiple table callbacks.  

---

## **Workspaces & Cleanliness**
- **Main showroom** → always off-limits for projects, staging, or messy tasks.  
  - Public-facing, always in use, expensive to clean.  
- **Christmas walk-through** → acceptable for temporary storage/off-season tasks.  
- **Back of house** → ideal for messy or prep work (cutting, sanding, etc.).  
- **Golden rule**: move items **directly to their final location**—don’t “double move” items.  

---

## **Cultural Expectations**
- “Lazy shortcuts” (only rolling up tonight’s silverware, dropping 1 napkin, carrying 1 spoon) = **false efficiency**.  
- Reinforce the idea that every small action should:  
  1. Save future trips.  
  2. Save labor cost.  
  3. Increase guest satisfaction.  
  4. Free servers to sell, not scramble.

## User

Do bar managers make tips

---

## Assistant

Bar managers typically do not receive tips directly from customers like bartenders or servers. However, in some establishments, bar managers might receive a share of the tip pool, especially if they actively participate in service or bartending duties. The specific tipping arrangement can vary depending on the establishment's policies.

## User

Is it legal for them to share in tip pool

---

## Assistant

In California, it is generally legal for bar managers to share in the tip pool if they are actively involved in providing service to customers, such as bartending or directly serving guests. However, under California law, individuals who have managerial authority, such as hiring, firing, or supervising employees, are typically not allowed to share in tips. The rationale is that tips are intended for non-managerial employees who provide direct service to customers.

So, if a bar manager has significant managerial duties and authority, it would likely be illegal for them to share in the tip pool. However, if their role is more of a working manager who performs the same duties as other tipped employees (e.g., bartending), they might be able to legally share in the tips, provided they do not have significant managerial control.

Always consult with a legal professional or check with the California Department of Industrial Relations for specific cases, as the interpretation of these rules can vary.

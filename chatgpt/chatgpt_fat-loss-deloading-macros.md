1. **Adjusting Macros During Fat Loss Diet**: 
   - During a fat loss phase, it's common to gradually reduce carbohydrate intake as you progress to continue making progress towards significant fat loss【9†(source)】.
   - A specific example from one individual’s fat loss experience shows a reduction in carbohydrate and fat macros while maintaining protein intake across 10 weeks of hypocaloric dieting【13†(source)】.
   
2. **Deloading**:
   - Deloading is a practice typically done to manage accumulated fatigue, especially in a muscle gain or hypertrophy-focused training cycle. During a deload, you can cut your sets or reps in half while maintaining the planned weights. Deloads typically occur every 4-8 weeks within a training program and are considered essential to managing fatigue and continuing to progress in training【17†(source)】【21†(source)】.

3. **Deloading During Fat Loss Diet**:
   - When deloading during a fat loss diet, it's advised to bump up to approximate maintenance calories during your deload to aid recovery and reduce diet fatigue. This approach supports the recovery process while managing the caloric balance to sustain the fat loss progress【25†(source)】.

4. **Post-Diet Maintenance**:
   - After a weight loss diet, a post-diet maintenance phase is suggested where you gradually increase calorie intake up to a healthy baseline while maintaining weight. This phase could last as long as your preceding weight loss diet or indefinitely if you've reached your target weight【8†(source)】.

These insights from Renaissance Periodization suggest that deloading can be a part of a fat loss diet, with modifications to macro-nutrient intake and calorie levels to support recovery and ongoing fat loss. It's a nuanced approach that requires balancing your training, calorie intake, and the goals of your fat loss phase.

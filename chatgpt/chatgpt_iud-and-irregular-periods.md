## User

can an iud cause irrelgular periods

---

## Assistant

Yes, an IUD (intrauterine device) can cause irregular periods, especially during the first three to six months after insertion. Hormonal IUDs may lead to lighter periods or even stop menstruation after a while, whereas copper IUDs might cause heavier and possibly more painful periods. Over time, many users of hormonal IUDs experience a reduction in menstrual bleeding and cramping. If you have concerns about your menstrual cycle after IUD insertion, it's advisable to consult with a healthcare provider.

can an iud cause breast pain

---

## Assistant

Yes, an IUD can cause breast tenderness or pain in some individuals. Hormonal IUDs release a small amount of progestin, which can lead to breast tenderness as a side effect, similar to other forms of hormonal birth control. However, not everyone will experience this side effect, and it may diminish over time. If breast pain persists or is concerning, it’s important to consult with a healthcare provider to rule out other causes.

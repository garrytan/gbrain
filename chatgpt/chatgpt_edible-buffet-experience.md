## Assistant

Mr. White, you’re spot-on that from a strict calories-in/calories-out view, eating more just racks on weight. The “benefit” people chase when they smoke or take an edible before a buffet mostly comes down to hedonic—pure pleasure—rather than nutrition:

- **Sensory amplification**: THC lights up your endocannabinoid system, boosting taste and smell. Every bite feels more “wow,” so you chase that novel rush.  
- **Value extraction**: Buffet is a flat fee. Once you’ve paid, the marginal “cost” of another cinnamon roll is zero—might as well squeeze every last sensory cent out of it.  
- **Psychological payoff**: Overeating in Vegas is an experience, not a meal. It’s about memories, laughs with friends, and that “I can’t believe I just did that” high.  

In an ideal world you’d have one bite and be done, but our brains crave novelty and repeated reward—one bite gets old fast.

**On combining with alcohol (“cross-fading”):**  
- Alcohol can speed THC’s absorption, raising your blood-THC spike and deepening the “high.”  
- THC slows gastric emptying, so alcohol sticks around longer—so you may feel drunker for longer, too.  
- The combo multiplies impairment: judgment, coordination and drowsiness worsen unpredictably.  

Bottom line: if you smoke or edible before a buffet, you’ll enjoy each bite more and feel compelled to keep going—great for Vegas stories, lousy for your waistline. Mixing booze on top of that just amplifies everything (good luck walking to your Uber).

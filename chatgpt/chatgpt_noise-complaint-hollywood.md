Mr. White — in **the City of Los Angeles (Hollywood)**, there isn’t a “free pass until late.” If the music is loud enough that it’s **disturbing the peace/quiet/comfort of neighbors**, it can already be a violation **even at 7 pm**. The municipal code section on radios/stereos/music makes it unlawful to amplify music “in such a manner, as to disturb the peace, quiet, and comfort” of neighbors. citeturn0search13

That said, **LA’s noise rules get meaningfully stricter at night**:
- **“Daytime” is 7:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m.**
- **“Nighttime” is 10:00 p.m. to 7:00 a.m.** citeturn1search0  
So **after 10:00 pm**, you’re on much firmer ground for enforcement (lower tolerated noise levels / more sensitivity). citeturn1search0

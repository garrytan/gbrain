## The scenarios ranked by likelihood

### **1️⃣ Trademark / IP problem (MOST likely)**

This is the #1 explanation that fits **every detail**.

- “Genius Shot” is a *very risky trademark*  
  - “Genius” is heavily contested in food & beverage  
  - “Shot” is generic and often unprotectable
- If another company already owned or enforced a similar mark, the options are:
  - Fight (expensive, slow, risky)
  - License (ongoing cost)
  - **Walk away**

**Key tell:**  
If this were a happy acquisition, the new owners would *keep the brand equity*. They didn’t.

Redirecting the domain + scrubbing founders = **legal separation**, not evolution.

---

### **2️⃣ BLG supply chain failure (very likely contributing factor)**

100% BLG protein at scale is a nightmare.

- Limited global suppliers
- High cost volatility
- Heat stability issues in RTD shots
- Shelf-life constraints
- Regulatory and labeling complexity

Your timeline lines up perfectly:

- Phase 1: 100% BLG → sells out fast
- Phase 2: Reformulation → packaging downgrade
- Phase 3: Chronic stockouts
- Phase 4: Brand disappears

This screams **“unit economics broke at scale.”**

Influencer audiences are forgiving — **manufacturers are not**.

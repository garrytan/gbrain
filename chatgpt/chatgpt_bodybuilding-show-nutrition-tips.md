What does Mike Israel recommend eating the day before and day of a bodybuilding show and what other recommendations does he make about peaking physique 

---

## Assistant

Dr. Mike Israetel, a co-founder of Renaissance Periodization (RP Strength), generally recommends specific strategies for bodybuilding show preparation, focusing on diet, hydration, and training. For eating the day before and the day of a show, he suggests:

1. **Carbohydrate Loading**: Increase carbohydrate intake to fill the muscles with glycogen, enhancing muscle fullness and definition.
2. **Moderate Protein**: Maintain a moderate protein intake to support muscle mass.
3. **Low Fat**: Keep fat intake low to avoid digestive issues and maintain sharpness in muscle definition.
4. **Water and Sodium Management**: Adjust water and sodium intake carefully. Some athletes reduce water intake slightly to enhance muscle definition, but this needs to be done cautiously to avoid dehydration.

On the day of the show:

1. **Small, Frequent Meals**: Consume small, carb-rich meals to maintain muscle fullness without causing bloating.
2. **Stay Hydrated**: Hydration is still important, but should be balanced to not cause bloating.
3. **Minimal Fiber**: Limit fiber to prevent gastrointestinal discomfort.

Other peaking recommendations:

1. **Training Taper**: Reduce training intensity/volume leading up to the show to allow muscles to fully recover and glycogen stores to be maximized.
2. **Minimize Stress**: Mental and physical stress can affect muscle appearance, so relaxation techniques may be advised.
3. **Sleep Quality**: Ensure good sleep leading up to the show for optimal recovery and hormonal balance.

Remember, these are general guidelines and might vary based on individual needs and specific strategies endorsed by Dr. Israetel at the time. Always consult a professional for personalized advice, especially for an event as rigorous as a bodybuilding show.

What is aperol 

---

## Assistant

Aperol is an Italian aperitif made of gentian, rhubarb, and cinchona, among other ingredients. It has a bright orange color and is known for its bittersweet flavor with complex citrus and herbal notes. Aperol is relatively low in alcohol content (around 11% alcohol by volume) compared to other spirits.

It's famously used in the Aperol Spritz, a popular cocktail made with Aperol, Prosecco, and soda water, often garnished with an orange slice. The Aperol Spritz is enjoyed widely as a refreshing, light drink, particularly in the summer and as a pre-meal drink to stimulate the appetite. Aperol itself has become synonymous with this cocktail and is a staple in many bars and households, especially in Europe.

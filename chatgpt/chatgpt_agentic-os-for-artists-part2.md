# Jovie homepage spec

**Core positioning:**  
**Jovie is the AI manager for your music catalog.**

She watches the catalog, spots the next move, builds the campaign, routes fans, queues work, measures what happened, and updates the next action. The “closed-loop” idea is the product’s premium wedge.

The issue is that it currently leads with **“Release more music with less work,” “Start Free Trial,” artist profiles, free pricing, smart links, pre-saves, and $39/$149 plans**, which reads more like release tooling than a serious AI operating layer. citeturn888244view0

# 1. Page objective

The homepage needs to communicate three things at once:

**To customers:**  
“This is an always-on AI manager that helps me grow and monetize my music catalog.”

**To YC / a16z visitors:**  
“They understand the buyer, the workflow, the wedge, and the business.”

**To serious music teams:**  
“This feels like a premium operating system, not a cheap artist tool.”

The page should no longer feel centered on “release pages.” It should feel centered on **catalog intelligence + action execution + learning loop**.

**Is Jovie a distributor?**

No. Jovie works with your existing distributor, DSP links, artist profiles, and fan paths. She manages the work around the catalog: campaigns, routing, tasks, approvals, and next actions.

Jovie handles the operational layer: tracking, planning, drafting, assigning, routing, and reporting. Human managers still own taste, relationships, negotiation, strategy, and final approval.

No. Jovie helps grow and manage existing music catalogs. She does not create songs, clone voices, or replace the artist’s creative work.

Artists, managers, labels, and music teams with released music, recurring catalog activity, and a need to run more growth work without adding headcount.

**Can I still use my current link-in-bio or smart-link tool?**

Yes. Jovie can work alongside your current fan surfaces or manage Jovie-powered fan paths directly.

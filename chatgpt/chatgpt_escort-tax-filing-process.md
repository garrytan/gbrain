2. **Self-Employment Taxes:** If their net earnings exceed $400, they must also pay self-employment taxes, which cover Social Security and Medicare taxes. This is calculated using Schedule SE (Self-Employment Tax).

3. **Deductible Expenses:** Escorts can deduct business-related expenses, which may include advertising, clothing specific to their work, and travel expenses directly related to their work. These deductions are itemized on Schedule C.

4. **Quarterly Estimated Taxes:** Since taxes aren’t being withheld from their earnings by an employer, they may need to make estimated tax payments quarterly to avoid penalties. This involves estimating the amount of tax owed for the year and making payments using Form 1040-ES (Estimated Tax for Individuals).

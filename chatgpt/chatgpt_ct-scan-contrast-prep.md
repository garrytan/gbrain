## User

What do I have to do to prepare for a CT scan with CONTRAST? Do I have to, like, fast-move forward or anything like that?

---

## Assistant

For a **CT scan with contrast**, the preparation depends on the type of contrast used (**oral or IV**). Here’s what you typically need to do:

1. **Fasting**:  
   - If **IV contrast** is used, you may need to **fast for 4-6 hours** before the scan (no food, only clear liquids).
   - If **oral contrast** is required, you may need to drink it a few hours before the scan.

2. **Hydration**:  
   - Drink plenty of **water** before and after the scan to help flush out the contrast.

3. **Allergy Check**:  
   - If you have a **history of iodine or contrast allergies**, let your doctor know. They may give you medication to prevent a reaction.

4. **Medication Adjustments**:  
   - If you take **metformin (for diabetes)**, you may need to stop it before and after the scan (check with your doctor).

5. **Remove Metal**:  
   - No jewelry, zippers, or metal objects in the scan area.

Call the imaging center if you need specific instructions, but these are the basics.

## Recommended deck structure: 8 slides, maximum

### 1. What Jovie does

Suggested slide:

> **Jovie helps artists release music and turn listeners into reachable fans.**

### 2. The problem

Keep it painfully simple:

> Artists have listeners everywhere and fans nowhere.

Then three bullets only:

| Current reality | Why it hurts |
|---|---|
| Every release needs links, pre-saves, posts, QR codes, and follow-up | Artists rebuild the same launch system every time |
| Link-in-bio tools send traffic away | They rarely create a fan relationship |
| Streaming platforms own the audience | Artists cannot reliably reach fans again |

Avoid a giant music-industry problem slide. The pitch should focus on the artist’s daily pain.

### 3. The product

Show what exists today as a simple workflow:

> **Import → Launch → Capture → Notify**

Use screenshots, not diagrams.

1. **Import the release/catalog**  
2. **Generate smart links, profile, release page, and launch tasks**  
3. **Capture fan opt-ins from profile visits, QR scans, payments, and shows**  
4. **Notify fans automatically for the next song, show, or campaign**

This maps directly to Jovie’s current public product: release plans, smart links, public profiles, fan capture, and automatic notifications. citeturn854038view0turn995597view0

> Jovie starts with the release link/profile, then becomes the artist’s fan relationship system.

This avoids overclaiming “AI is changing everything” and makes the product feel inevitable.

## Assistant

To calculate the amount of fat you need to lose to reach 12% body fat from 15.2%, we'll start by determining the weight of fat you currently have and then calculate what it would need to be to make your body fat percentage 12%.

Your current fat mass is 15.2% of your weight:
\[ \text{Fat mass} = 184 \, \text{lbs} \times 0.152 = 27.97 \, \text{lbs} \]

To find out your weight at 12% body fat without losing muscle, we first calculate your lean body mass (total weight minus fat mass):
\[ \text{Lean body mass} = 184 \, \text{lbs} - 27.97 \, \text{lbs} = 156.03 \, \text{lbs} \]

Now, we calculate the total body weight when your body fat is 12%, keeping the lean body mass constant:
\[ \text{Total weight at 12% BF} = \frac{156.03 \, \text{lbs}}{1 - 0.12} = 177.31 \, \text{lbs} \]

Finally, calculate the weight loss needed:
\[ \text{Weight loss needed} = 184 \, \text{lbs} - 177.31 \, \text{lbs} = 6.69 \, \text{lbs} \]

You need to lose approximately 6.69 pounds of fat to reach 12% body fat, assuming no muscle loss.

## User

What's the most efficient way to operate air conditioning, to leave it on all day or to turn it on and off?

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, for typical Southern California conditions and modern central A/C:

- **Set-and-hold wins.** Keeping the thermostat at a steady, moderate setting (e.g., 76–78 °F when home) is generally more efficient than letting the house heat up fully and blasting the system later. The compressor doesn’t “work harder” after a long break, but bringing walls, floors, and furniture back down from 90 °F takes a lot of energy.

- **Use a setback, not a full shutdown.** If you’re away all day, raise the set point about 7–10 °F (say, 83–85 °F) instead of turning it off. This trims runtime while preventing the structure from soaking up extreme heat.

- **Program or automate.** A smart thermostat that pre-cools an hour before you return keeps comfort high and avoids peak-rate spikes.

- **Supportive steps:** close blinds during peak sun, seal leaks, and keep filters clean.

Bottom line: don’t run it full-blast 24/7, but avoid complete shutdown. A consistent, slightly higher daytime set point with scheduled cooling is the most energy-efficient and comfortable approach.

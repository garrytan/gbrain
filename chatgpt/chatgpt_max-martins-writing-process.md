**Key Points:**  
- Seabrook devotes several chapters to Max Martin, the late Denniz PoP, and their Cheiron Studios approach.  
- He describes how Martin and his team would break down a melody almost mathematically, focusing on the exact placement of syllables within measures.  
- While the term “melodic grid” isn’t used verbatim, Seabrook notes the “precision” with which Martin arranges vocals—each beat and syllable is mapped out meticulously. The approach is systematic enough to be likened to a grid.  
- The book includes interviews with co-writers and industry insiders who confirm the almost “mechanical” breakdown of vocal parts to ensure maximum catchiness.

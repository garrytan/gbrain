## Assistant

If you're looking for high-protein alternatives to candy and snacks but can't have whey or any dairy, there are several options you can consider. Here are some ideas:

1. Plant-Based Protein Bars: Look for protein bars made from plant-based sources like pea protein, hemp protein, or brown rice protein. These bars often come in various flavors and can be a convenient and tasty snack option.

2. Nuts and Seeds: Almonds, cashews, peanuts, pumpkin seeds, and sunflower seeds are all excellent sources of protein. They can be enjoyed on their own as a snack or incorporated into homemade trail mixes for added variety.

3. Nut Butter: Opt for nut butter made from almonds, cashews, or peanuts, as they provide a good amount of protein. Spread them on whole grain crackers, rice cakes, or celery sticks for a satisfying and protein-rich snack.

4. Roasted Chickpeas: Chickpeas are a great source of plant-based protein. Roasting them with some spices can turn them into a crunchy and flavorful snack that can easily replace candies.

5. Edamame: Edamame, or young soybeans, are packed with protein and make for a nutritious snack. You can find them fresh or frozen. Steam or boil them and sprinkle with a little salt for a healthy alternative to sugary snacks.

6. Protein Smoothies: Create a delicious and high-protein smoothie using a plant-based protein powder, such as those made from pea, hemp, or brown rice protein. Blend it with fruits, vegetables, and non-dairy milk for a satisfying treat.

7. Seitan: Seitan, also known as wheat meat, is a meat substitute made from gluten. It is high in protein and can be used as an ingredient in various dishes, such as stir-fries or sandwiches.

8. Lentils and Legumes: Lentils, black beans, chickpeas, and other legumes are excellent sources of protein. They can be used to make savory snacks like roasted chickpeas or homemade hummus.

Remember to check food labels and ingredients to ensure that the products you choose are free from dairy and whey, as they can sometimes be hidden in processed foods. It's also a good idea to consult with a healthcare professional or registered dietitian for personalized advice and guidance on meeting your specific dietary needs.

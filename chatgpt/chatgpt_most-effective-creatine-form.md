Mr. White,

Peer-reviewed evidence is unanimous: **creatine monohydrate**—ideally as a powder—is the most effective, safest, and best-studied form of creatine supplementation.

- **Gold-standard efficacy:**  
  “Creatine monohydrate is the most effective ergogenic nutritional supplement currently available to athletes for increasing high-intensity exercise capacity and lean body mass.” citeturn0search7turn0search4

- **Powder vs. pills vs. gummies:**  
  All three deliver the same molecule (monohydrate) if dosed equally. Powder offers the best cost-per-gram and dosing precision. Capsules/tablets simply encapsulate powder; gummies often contain less creatine per serving and add sugars without enhancing absorption. citeturn1news9

- **Other routes (IV, nasal, rectal “boofing,” etc.):**  
  No peer-reviewed studies support benefits or practicality of any non-oral routes. Oral monohydrate has near-complete bioavailability and reliably raises muscle creatine stores—alternative routes are unnecessary and untested.

**Recommendation:**  
Use **micronized creatine monohydrate powder**, 3–5 g/day (with carbohydrate or protein for optimal uptake). Capsules are an acceptable convenience, but gummies, IV, snorting, or rectal administration confer no proven advantage.

## Assistant

Mr. White,

Both pills (capsules/tablets) and powder deliver the identical molecule—creatine monohydrate—and raise muscle creatine stores equivalently. The differences are purely about convenience, cost, and dosing:

- **Powder**  
  - **Absorption:** >99% bioavailable once dissolved in fluid citeturn0search6  
  - **Pros:** precise dosing (3–5 g), mixes into any drink, lowest cost per gram  
  - **Cons:** less portable, requires a shaker or glass

- **Pills/Capsules**  
  - **Absorption:** essentially identical to powder; any lag from capsule breakdown is too small to impact muscle uptake or performance citeturn0search7  
  - **Pros:** ultra‐convenient, no mixing  
  - **Cons:** you may need 5–10 capsules/day for an effective 3–5 g dose, higher cost per gram

**Bottom line:** if you value precision and economy, go with **micronized creatine monohydrate powder**; if you prioritize sheer convenience—even at a premium—capsules are a fine alternative.

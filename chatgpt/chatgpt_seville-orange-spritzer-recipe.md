How do you make a Seville orange spritzer?

---

## Assistant

Here’s a concise and balanced **Seville Orange Spritzer** recipe:

### Ingredients:
- 2 oz Seville orange juice (fresh or high-quality bottled)
- 1 oz dry white wine or dry vermouth (optional for depth)
- 1/2 oz simple syrup (adjust to taste)
- 3–4 oz club soda or sparkling water
- Ice
- Orange twist or slice (garnish)

### Instructions:
1. Fill a wine glass or highball with ice.
2. Add orange juice, (optional) wine/vermouth, and syrup.
3. Stir briefly.
4. Top with sparkling water.
5. Garnish with an orange twist or slice.

**Note:** Seville oranges are bitter and tart—adjust sweetness to taste. For an alcoholic version, you can also sub in 2 oz Aperol or Campari.

What is Seville orange juice?

---

## Assistant

**Seville orange juice** comes from **Seville oranges**, a variety of bitter orange known for their sour, tangy, and slightly floral flavor. Unlike sweet oranges, Seville oranges are:

- **Very tart and bitter** — not usually eaten raw.
- **High in pectin** — often used for marmalade.
- **Seasonal** — typically available in winter (Jan–Feb).

The juice is prized in cocktails, marinades, and sauces for its intense, complex citrus profile—like a cross between lemon, lime, and orange with a bitter edge. If you can’t find it fresh, look for bottled Seville orange juice or use a mix of fresh orange, lemon, and a touch of grapefruit juice as a substitute.

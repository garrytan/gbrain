---

## Assistant

Yes, Mr. White, you can often fix a non-running garbage disposal yourself. Here's a concise guide:

1. **Safety First**: Ensure the disposal is turned off.
2. **Reset Button**: Look for a small red button on the bottom of the disposal unit. If it's popped out, press it to reset.
3. **Check for Obstructions**: If it doesn't start, turn off the power and use a flashlight to look for any visible obstructions. Clear them with pliers or a similar tool (never use your hands).
4. **Manual Rotation**: If it's still not working, try manually rotating the blades with an Allen wrench. Insert it into the hole at the bottom of the disposal and turn it back and forth.
5. **Circuit Breaker**: Check your circuit breaker to make sure the disposal hasn’t tripped it.
6. **Call a Professional**: If these steps don’t resolve the issue, it might require a professional's attention.

Always be cautious when working with appliances and electricity. If you're unsure, it's safer to contact a professional.

## User

It just hums when I turn it on

---

## Assistant

If your garbage disposal just hums when turned on, it's likely jammed or has a stuck motor. Here's what you can do:

1. **Turn Off Power**: Ensure the disposal is switched off or unplugged for safety.
2. **Use an Allen Wrench**: Insert it into the hex-shaped hole at the bottom of the disposal. Turn it back and forth to free the impeller blades.
3. **Check for Obstructions**: With the power off, use a flashlight to look for any visible obstructions inside the disposal. If you find anything, carefully remove it with pliers.
4. **Reset Button**: After clearing a jam, press the reset button on the bottom of the unit.
5. **Test the Disposal**: Restore power and test the disposal.

If it still just hums, the motor may be faulty, requiring professional repair or replacement.

Certainly, Mr. White. Using an Allen wrench to fix a jammed garbage disposal involves the following steps:

1. **Locate the Hex Hole**: Underneath the garbage disposal unit, there's a hex-shaped hole designed for an Allen wrench (hex key). This is directly connected to the disposal's motor.

2. **Insert Allen Wrench**: Fit the correct size Allen wrench into this hole. It's usually a ¼ inch hex key.

3. **Manually Rotate**: Firmly move the wrench back and forth. This manual rotation can help dislodge anything stuck in the disposal's blades or flywheel.

4. **Repeat if Necessary**: Sometimes, it may take a few attempts to clear the jam. If it initially feels stuck, keep trying gentle but firm back-and-forth movements.

This method is effective for freeing up jams in the disposal without needing to directly reach into the grinding chamber, enhancing safety.

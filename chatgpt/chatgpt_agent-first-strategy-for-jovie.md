## World-class version

Over the next six months, **agent-first means Jovie stops being software artists log into and becomes the operating system that actively manages the work around every release.**

For an artist, manager, or label team, the default experience should feel less like “open a dashboard and figure out what to do” and more like: **Jovie already knows what is happening, what is missing, what is underperforming, and what should happen next.**

That means every catalog, release, asset, fan path, and campaign becomes a living workflow. Jovie should continuously monitor the artist’s world: upcoming releases, missing links, weak presave conversion, outdated profiles, playlist opportunities, content gaps, fan engagement signals, and post-release momentum. Then it should recommend, draft, route, and increasingly execute the next best action.

In practical terms, agent-first for Jovie means three things:

1. **Agents own the repetitive execution layer.**  
   Building release pages, organizing assets, preparing presave campaigns, generating fan paths, drafting playlist pitches, updating artist profiles, and flagging missing deliverables should happen automatically or with one-click approval.

2. **Agents become the intelligence layer for artist teams.**  
   Jovie should tell an artist or manager what matters now: which release needs attention, which audience segment is moving, which campaign is leaking conversion, which asset is missing, and which opportunity is time-sensitive.

3. **Agents turn Jovie into an always-on artist manager.**  
   The product should move from passive utility to active operator. It should wake up every day, inspect the artist’s catalog and campaigns, and push the business forward without waiting for the user to remember what to do.

The key product shift is from **tools to outcomes**. Artists do not want another dashboard. They want more fans, better releases, cleaner campaigns, stronger positioning, and fewer things falling through the cracks. Agent-first means Jovie absorbs the operational complexity between “I have music coming out” and “my release is properly launched, promoted, tracked, and optimized.”

By the end of the next six months, the goal is for Jovie to manage a release lifecycle end-to-end: detect what is coming, prepare the release infrastructure, generate and ship the fan experience, monitor performance, suggest interventions, and package the results back to the team.

The winning version of Jovie is an AI artist manager that does the work before the artist asks.

## Assistant

Mr. White,

Yes – with an FFMI around 17 you’re firmly in the “novice” window where full body-recomp is possible at maintenance (or a very slight surplus).  

**How to run a recomp:**  
- **Calories:** Stay at maintenance (±100 kcal).  
- **Protein:** ≈1 g per lb bodyweight.  
- **Training:** Progressive overload, 10–20 sets/muscle/week.  
- **Volume & intensity:** High enough to drive hypertrophy but manageable for recovery.  

Under these conditions you can gain ~0.25–0.5 lb muscle/week while losing fat. As your FFMI climbs past ~18, concurrent gain/loss ability will taper off and you’ll need to cycle dedicated bulk/cut phases.

At your FFMI (~17), you still have a full recomposition window, but with tirzepatide you need to manage intake precisely:

2. **Protein:**

3. **Training:**  
   – Hypertrophy focus, 10–20 hard sets per muscle per week (Mike Israetel).  
   – Progressive overload every session.

4. **Monitoring:**  
   – Watch strength: stalls >5 % indicate underfuel.  
   – RMR drop >100 kcal/d or consistent weight loss → you’re in a deficit, treat as cut.  
   – If intake consistently below maintenance, you’ll slide back into fat-loss mode.

**Bottom line:** you can still body-recomp on tirzepatide, but you must deliberately hit maintenance calories and protein. If appetite suppression prevents that, either raise calories with supplements or accept a cut until appetite normalizes, then resume recomposition.

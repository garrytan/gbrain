Short answer: **Google did *not* buy CapCut.** CapCut is still owned by **ByteDance** (the Chinese parent company of TikTok) and is *not* a Google property. What you saw in your Google Photos email is about a **partnership/integration**, not an acquisition. citeturn0search19turn0search23

Here’s what’s happening:

**1) CapCut is ByteDance-owned — not Google.**  
CapCut was developed and is operated by ByteDance, the same company behind TikTok. Google hasn’t acquired CapCut. citeturn0search19

**2) Google Photos is offering a way to edit your Recap in CapCut.**  
For the new *2025 Recap* feature (Google Photos’ version of “Spotify Wrapped”), Google added an **“Edit with CapCut” export button**. That lets you take your auto-generated highlight reel and open it directly in CapCut to further edit, add templates, music, etc. This is a **product integration**/link, not evidence of ownership. citeturn0search23

**3) It looks like a formal partnership — not a forced install.**  
Google Photos now recognizes that a lot of users want *more advanced editing tools* than its built-in editor (despite recent improvements). So Google and CapCut partnered so Photos can send your media straight to CapCut with templates and preserved timelines. This is similar to third-party integrations in other services — Google isn’t bundling CapCut as its own app. citeturn0search23

**4) No acquisition or buy-out happened.**  
CapCut remains under ByteDance’s control. Google didn’t acquire it, and there’s no credible report that it did. citeturn0search19

**Why it feels weird:**  
- CapCut is associated with TikTok/ByteDance — and Google promoting a ByteDance app inside its own Photos app is unusual. citeturn0search22  
- Google’s own Photos editor is also being improved, so the partnership helps fill gaps for users who want deeper editing workflows. citeturn0news0

**Bottom line:**  
You’re seeing **a strategic integration/partnership for workflow convenience**, not a Google acquisition of CapCut. If you don’t want to use CapCut, you can ignore that option — it’s just another export path.

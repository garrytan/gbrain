## Assistant

I'm not a doctor, but if you believe you've injured your back, you should see a medical professional for an appropriate evaluation and recommendations. 

However, over-the-counter (OTC) medications commonly used for pain and inflammation include:

1. **Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)**:
   - **Ibuprofen** (e.g., Advil, Motrin): This can help reduce both pain and inflammation.
   - **Naproxen sodium** (e.g., Aleve): Similar to ibuprofen, it can also help with pain and inflammation.
   
2. **Acetaminophen (Paracetamol)**:
   - **Acetaminophen** (e.g., Tylenol): Primarily for pain relief, it doesn't address inflammation. It's an alternative for those who cannot take NSAIDs.

3. **Topical analgesics**:
   - These are creams, gels, or patches that you can apply directly to the area where you're experiencing pain. Some contain ingredients like **capsaicin**, **menthol**, or **NSAIDs**.

4. **Muscle relaxants**: 
   - These are sometimes used for muscle spasms that can accompany back pain, though many are available by prescription only.

When considering an OTC medication:
- Read the label and follow dosing instructions carefully.
- Be aware of any other medications or health conditions you have, as these can affect which pain relievers are safe for you.
- Remember that even OTC medications can have side effects and interactions with other drugs.
- Avoid combining multiple pain relievers unless directed by a healthcare professional.
- If you're pregnant, nursing, have a chronic health condition, or take prescription medications, consult with a healthcare provider before taking any OTC pain relievers.

Ultimately, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional about the best course of action for your specific situation.

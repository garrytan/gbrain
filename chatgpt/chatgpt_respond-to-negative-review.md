## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s how you can respond to a negative review on Airbnb:  

1. **Go to your Airbnb profile:**  
   - Click your profile photo in the top-right corner.  
   - Choose **“Account” → “Reviews.”**  

2. **Open “Reviews about you”:**  
   - You’ll see all guest reviews left for your stays.  

3. **Find the specific review:**  
   - Under the review that you’d like to address, there should be a **“Leave a public response”** or **“Respond”** button.  

4. **Write your response:**  
   - Keep it brief, factual, and professional — your reply will appear publicly under the review.  

If you don’t see the “Respond” option:  
- You may be viewing the review from your **guest** profile instead of your **host** profile. Switch to your hosting dashboard at **[airbnb.com/hosting/reviews](https://www.airbnb.com/hosting/reviews)**.  
- Airbnb only allows responses to reviews left by guests after completed stays.

## Assistant

Got it, Mr. White. Since you were the **guest**, you can’t post a full public “response” like a host can — but Airbnb gives you two limited options:  

1. **Private Feedback to the Host**  
   - Go to **Profile → Reviews → Reviews you’ve received**  
   - Find the negative review  
   - Click **“Leave private feedback”** (only visible to the host)  

2. **Public Comment (in limited cases)**  
   - Airbnb sometimes allows guests to publicly respond **only if the review violates content policy** (e.g., false, retaliatory, or irrelevant content).  
   - In that case, you can **contact Airbnb Support** and request a **review removal or mediation**.  
   - Go to **[airbnb.com/help/contact-us](https://www.airbnb.com/help/contact-us)** → choose **“Reviews” → “Dispute a review.”**

So:  
- If it’s factually wrong or violates Airbnb’s review policy, **report it**.  
- Otherwise, your best move is to **use private feedback** or **focus on building recent positive reviews**, since newer ones weigh more heavily in your profile score.

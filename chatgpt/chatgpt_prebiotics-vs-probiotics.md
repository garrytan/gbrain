## Prebiotics vs. Probiotics

### Probiotics
image_group{"layout":"carousel","aspect_ratio":"1:1","query":["yogurt with live cultures close up","kefir glass on table","sauerkraut in jar","probiotic supplement capsules on white background"],"num_per_query":1}

**Definition:** Live microorganisms that confer a health benefit when consumed in adequate amounts.

**Common strains:**
- *Lactobacillus*
- *Bifidobacterium*
- *Saccharomyces boulardii*

**Food sources:**
- Yogurt with live cultures  
- Kefir  
- Sauerkraut  
- Kimchi  
- Miso  

**Potential benefits:**
- Improved digestion and bowel regularity  
- Reduced antibiotic-associated diarrhea  
- Support for immune function  
- Possible reduction in IBS symptoms (strain-specific)

**Supplement guidance:**
- Look for clearly labeled strains (genus, species, strain).  
- Typical dose: 1–10+ billion CFU/day depending on strain and goal.  
- Refrigeration may improve viability for some products.

---

### Prebiotics
image_group{"layout":"carousel","aspect_ratio":"1:1","query":["bananas on kitchen counter","garlic and onions on cutting board","asparagus bunch fresh","oats in wooden bowl"],"num_per_query":1}

**Definition:** Non-digestible fibers that feed beneficial gut bacteria.

**Common types:**
- Inulin  
- Fructooligosaccharides (FOS)  
- Galactooligosaccharides (GOS)  
- Resistant starch  

**Food sources:**
- Bananas (especially slightly green)  
- Garlic and onions  
- Asparagus  
- Oats  
- Legumes  

**Potential benefits:**
- Increased beneficial bacteria  
- Improved stool consistency  
- Enhanced mineral absorption  
- Production of short-chain fatty acids (gut lining support)

---

### Key Difference

- **Probiotics = live bacteria you ingest.**  
- **Prebiotics = fiber that feeds existing beneficial bacteria.**

They work synergistically. Some products combine both and are labeled **synbiotics**.

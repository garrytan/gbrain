Yes, but only through your **custom voicemail greeting**.

Apple’s voicemail settings let you record a **custom greeting** in the Phone app, so you can say something like: “Please text me instead of leaving a voicemail.” Apple notes that custom greetings are available only if supported by your carrier and region. citeturn521992search0

The **AI-related features** do something different:
- **Live Voicemail** shows a real-time transcription while someone is leaving a message. citeturn521992search1turn829906search1
- **Apple Intelligence** can create **voicemail summaries** after the fact. citeturn205255search0turn205255search4

Those features do **not** let you replace the voicemail system with a custom AI instruction that tells callers to text you instead. Apple’s current documentation for those features describes transcription, summaries, and call screening, not a user-editable AI voicemail script. citeturn521992search1turn205255search0turn829906search0

To set the message you want:
1. Open **Phone**
2. Go to **Voicemail**

4. Choose **Custom**

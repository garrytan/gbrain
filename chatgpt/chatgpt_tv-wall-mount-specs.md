## Assistant

Yo Doc, got you. On that **BLF328 A/V Wall Mount**, once you've released the latch or safety screw at the bottom:

1. **Tilt the bottom of the TV slightly forward** (just enough to clear the hooks).
2. Then **lift the entire TV straight up**—the bracket hooks should slide up and off the wall plate.
3. **You shouldn’t need to force it**—if it's not lifting, double-check for a second latch or safety screw on both sides.

Give it a solid but controlled lift straight up. Most of these mounts hook downward onto a bar—so once the bottom is clear, it slides up and off.

The **safety screw** on the BLF328 is usually the **small Phillips head that’s oriented sideways**, right underneath the bracket.

- That screw locks the bracket onto the wall plate by preventing it from sliding upward.
- The **hex screw** (if present) is usually for tilt tension or arm articulation, not the lock.

So:  
**Loosen or remove the sideways Phillips screw**, then you should be able to tilt and lift the TV off.

### What Might Be Blocking You:

- **Bracket tension**: Years of gravity + paint buildup can cause sticking. A **gentle but firm upward yank** is okay here—don’t worry, those brackets are made to hold 125 lbs. Your lift won't break it.

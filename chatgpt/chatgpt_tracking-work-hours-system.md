## Assistant

Mr. White — you’re not lacking discipline; you’re lacking **instrumentation**. Solo founders don’t need rigid schedules, they need **constraints + visibility**.

Here’s a system that works with fragmented days and weird hours.

---

## 1. Stop Tracking “Days.” Track **Weekly Output Hours**
Your life makes daily consistency unrealistic. Weekly consistency *is* realistic.

**Target:**  
- **50–60 focused hours / week** (deep + shallow combined)  
- **30–40 deep hours / week** minimum

This is in line with what elite solo founders actually sustain long-term.

---

## 2. Use a Simple Two-Tier Hour System (Not Time Blocking)

Every work session gets logged as **one of two types**:

### A. Deep Work (the only hours that really matter)
- Building Joby (code, product design, core strategy, sales calls)
- Hard thinking that moves the business forward

🎯 **Goal:** 4–6 deep hours/day on average (not every day)

### B. Shallow / Admin
- Email, logistics, driving gaps, light research, context switching

🎯 **Goal:** Unlimited, but don’t lie to yourself about it

---

## 3. Track in the Lowest-Friction Way Possible
If tracking feels heavy, you won’t do it.

### Option A (Best): **Timer-Based**
Use:
- Toggl  
- Clockify  
- Raycast timer (if on Mac)

**Rules:**
- Start timer when you sit down
- Stop when you stand up
- Tag: `Deep` or `Shallow`

That’s it.

### Option B (Ultra-Minimal): **Session Tally**
If timers annoy you:

- Each focused session = **1 block (~60–90 min)**
- Write tally marks in Notes:
  - Deep: |||||
  - Shallow: |||

At end of week → count blocks × avg duration.

---

## 4. Weekly “Founder Scorecard” (10 minutes, non-negotiable)
Every Sunday night:

Write this down:

- **Deep hours this week:** ___  
- **Total hours:** ___  
- **One thing that moved Joby forward most:** ___  
- **One bottleneck to remove next week:** ___

If deep hours < 30 → **something is wrong** (not morally, operationally).

---

## 5. Anchor Hours, Not Schedules
Instead of “I work 9–5,” use **anchors** around Lib’s schedule.

Example:
- **Morning anchor:** 90–120 min deep work *after drop-off*
- **Midday anchor:** 60–90 min deep work *before pickup*
- **Evening anchor:** Optional 60 min shallow or review

Even if the day explodes, you usually hit **2 anchors = 3–4 deep hours**.

That compounds.

---

## 6. Remove the Mental Tax: Define “Enough”
Your stress is coming from ambiguity.

Define:
- **Enough today = 3 deep hours**
- **Good day = 4–5 deep hours**
- **Exceptional = 6+**

Once you hit “enough,” you stop feeling behind — even if the day was chaotic.

---

## 7. One Hard Rule (This Matters)
**Never end a week without knowing your numbers.**

Founders burn out not from overwork, but from:
> “I feel like I worked all the time… but I don’t know if it was enough.”

This system fixes that.

## Assistant

Yes, Mr. White — totally real.

**Why it happens**
- **Surface heat loss:** The top few millimeters dump heat to the air by **evaporation** and **radiant cooling** to the sky, especially at night. That skin layer can be a few °F cooler.  
- **Poor mixing:** If the pump’s off or it’s a calm day, you get **thermal stratification** — warmer bulk water below, cooler film on top.  
- **Plumbing bias:** Many heaters return warm water from **lower returns**, while skimmers pull from the **cool surface**, reinforcing the gradient.  
- **Wind & sun timing:** Calm morning after a clear night = biggest difference; wind or lots of swimmers erase it.

**What to do**
- Run the **pump** 30–60 min before swimming to mix.  
- Use a **solar cover** when idle to cut evaporative cooling.  
- If heating, circulate while heating so warmth reaches the surface.  
- Curious? Measure with a thermometer at the surface, 1 ft, and 3 ft — you’ll see the spread.

Science says your feet are in a bath and your skin’s in a tiny cold lake.

what is required to grane a restraining order in los angeles

## Assistant



To obtain a restraining order in Los Angeles, you generally need to take the following steps:

1. File a request for a restraining order at the Los Angeles Superior Court.
2. Complete the required forms, which typically include a petition for a restraining order, a temporary restraining order (TRO), and a notice of hearing.
3. Provide detailed information about the person you are seeking protection from, including their name, address, and relationship to you.
4. Explain why you are seeking a restraining order, including any incidents of abuse, harassment, or threats that have occurred.
5. Provide any evidence that supports your request, such as witness statements, photographs, or medical records.
6. Attend a court hearing, where a judge will review your request and determine whether to grant a temporary restraining order (TRO).
7. If the judge grants a TRO, the order will be effective immediately and will remain in effect until the hearing for a permanent restraining order, which is typically scheduled within 21 days.
8. At the permanent restraining order hearing, you will need to present evidence and testify to support your request for a permanent restraining order. The judge will then decide whether to grant the order.

It's important to note that the process for obtaining a restraining order may vary depending on the specific circumstances of your case, and it can be helpful to consult with an attorney or a domestic violence advocacy organization for guidance.

Yes, the other party will have the opportunity to appear in court and contest the restraining order at the hearing. The judge will then weigh the evidence and decide whether to grant or deny the restraining order. It's important to note that even if the other party does not appear in court, the judge may still grant a restraining order based on the evidence presented by you.

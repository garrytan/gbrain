### ✅ What it is  
A “party round” is a financing round for an early–stage company in which many individual or small investors participate, rather than one or two lead institutional investors. citeturn0search0turn0search1turn0search2  

Key features:  
- Hundreds or dozens of smaller checks, often from angels or emerging funds, rather than a dominant lead investor. citeturn0search2turn0search1  
- May happen between pre-seed and Seed/Series A stage. citeturn0search0turn0search1  
- Often smaller amounts per investor. citeturn0search4turn0search1

### ⚠️ Pros & Cons  
**Pros:**  
- Enables founder to raise from many sources, possibly faster. citeturn0search1  
- Allows inclusion of strategic angels, influencers, or operators who may bring value (network, credibility). citeturn0search0  

**Cons:**  
- Lack of a lead investor may lead to weak coordination, less operational support for the company. citeturn0search2turn0search0  
- Cap-table complexity: many small investors can complicate governance/future rounds. citeturn0search7turn0search1  
- May signal to later investors that there wasn’t sufficient conviction by a major investor, which could impact follow-on fundraising. citeturn0search0turn0search7  

---

### 🔍 When it might make sense  
Good for a startup when:  
- You need to raise quickly and can’t secure a traditional lead investor.  
- You want to include many strategic but smaller investors for network effects.  
- You’re early stage and the terms are manageable.

Less good when:  
- You anticipate heavy follow-on rounds and need strong institutional backing.  
- You need hands-on support, mentorship, board governance from an investor.  
- You want clean cap-table and strong signalling to future VCs.

---

### 📋 What to watch for (as founder/investor)  
- Ensure you still structure the round with clear terms and decent one or two major participants if possible.  
- Manage investor relations: many small investors need communication, tracking.  
- Consider how this will look to future lead-VCs: cap-table, terms, signalling.  
- Understand dilution, governance rights, and legal compliance (lots of angels = more admin).

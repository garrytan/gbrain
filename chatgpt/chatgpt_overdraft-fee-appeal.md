## Assistant

Here’s a tight, high-leverage overdraft fee appeal package you can copy/paste, tailor, and escalate by steps (secure message → phone rep → supervisor → written escalation). I’ll assume multiple recent fees; adjust counts/dates/amounts. Concise, fact-forward, respectful, and framed so the bank can log it as a *courtesy refund for a long-term, low-risk customer who took immediate corrective action*.

---

## 1. Info You Need Before Contacting the Bank
Have these ready so the rep can act without toggling screens (reduces “I’ll call you back” stall).

- **Account type & last 4 digits:** ______
- **Dates + amounts of overdraft fees:** e.g., 07/12 $35, 07/13 $35, 07/15 $35 (total $105).
- **Trigger cause:** Timing mismatch (ACH deposit posted after pending debit clears, card pre-auth, mobile deposit hold, etc.).
- **History:** # years as customer, avg monthly balance (ballpark), direct deposit on file? Y/N.
- **Remedy you took:** Immediate deposit within X hours; enabled alerts; linked savings; reduced automatic transfers; etc.
- **Ask:** Full reversal if possible; at minimum reverse first (or most recent) X fees as a one-time courtesy.

---

## 2. One-Shot Secure Message (short; use first)
> **Subject:** Courtesy refund request for recent overdraft fees  
>  
> Hi, I’m requesting a *courtesy reversal* of overdraft fees that hit my Checking ••••____ on [dates]. These were caused by timing gaps between incoming deposits and card transactions—not ongoing insufficient funds. I deposited funds immediately once notified and have now enabled low-balance alerts to prevent recurrence.  
>  
> I’ve been a customer since [year], keep active direct deposit, and rarely (if ever) incur overdrafts. Could you please reverse the [$total] in recent fees (or as many as policy allows)? I’d appreciate your help.  
>  
> Thank you,  
> Tim White

---

## 3. Phone Script (front-line rep)
**Goal:** Get them to look at account history + code a “courtesy refund.” Stay friendly; reps usually have a quota of fee reversals they *can* do.

**You:**  
“Hi, this is Tim White. I’m calling about overdraft fees that posted to my checking ending in ____ on [dates]. I’ve been with you since [year] and almost never overdraft. This was strictly a timing issue—my deposit posted after pending card transactions cleared. I covered the shortfall immediately and set up low-balance alerts. Could you please issue a courtesy refund for those fees? Total is about [$___].”

**If rep says policy allows only 1 fee:**  
“I appreciate that. Given my clean history and how quickly I resolved the balance, can you check whether a supervisor can extend that to cover the rest? Even partial relief helps.”

**If they blame authorizations/holds:**  
“Understood. That’s why I’m asking for a one-time courtesy. I’ve already changed my settings to prevent this.”

**Close:**  
“Thanks for escalating. I’ll hold.”

---

## 4. Supervisor Escalation Script (if front-line can’t clear all fees)
“Thanks for taking the call. Quick recap: long-time customer, rare overdraft, timing mismatch, immediately funded account, now have alerts + linked backup. I’m requesting reversal of [$total] in fees that posted [list dates]. Even a partial adjustment—first two fees—would be appreciated. Is there room in policy for that based on customer tenure?”

## 6. Escalation Ladder (when to move up)
| Stage | Trigger to escalate | Who to contact next | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Secure msg | No reply in 2 business days OR denial | Call customer service | Ask if any refunds remain in calendar year. |
| Phone rep | Grants partial | Ask for supervisor while still on call | Get case #. |
| Supervisor | Denies or partial < ask | Customer Advocacy / Office of the President | Send written letter; include case # + call notes. |
| Advocacy | Denies | CFPB complaint (last resort) | Only if material $, repeated denials, or systematic error. Tell bank before filing; they often reverse to avoid complaint. |

## 7. Talking Points That Increase Your Odds
Use selectively—don’t monologue.

- **Clean track record = low risk = worth goodwill credit.**
- **Immediate remediation shows responsibility.**
- **You’ve added controls (alerts, schedule changes, backup funding).**
- **Bank relationship value** (direct deposit, card spend, loans, etc. if true).
- **Customer retention cost > fee revenue.** (Use only with supervisor; keep it light: “I value the relationship and want to keep using you as my main account—hoping we can work this out.”)

## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s the most effective move for *right now* (alpha → tight beta): **high-touch, one-on-one invites with “do things that don’t scale,” plus a simple waitlist page running in parallel.** Don’t rely on a big Slack/WhatsApp swarm yet.

Why:
- **Your target is narrow and high-value.** Actors/models/influencers using DEXA + GLP-1s are not a mass-market beta. You want *deep signal*, not broad noise.
- **Aesthetics + body data is emotional and context-heavy.** You need to watch people use it, hear their goals, and catch where you’re wrong about motivations.
- **Communities early tend to produce performative feedback.** People posture, echo each other, or stay silent. Great later; messy now.

What YC would say in practice:  
**“Hand-pick 10–30 users who are *exactly* your beachhead, onboard them personally, obsess over their outcomes, and iterate weekly.”**

### Concrete plan (next 4–6 weeks)

**1) Build a beta page anyway (lightweight).**  
Not as your main funnel yet—just to:
- collect leads you can follow up with manually
- test messaging (“DEXA-driven aesthetics optimizer for on-camera bodies” vs other angles)
- give people somewhere to send friends

Keep it dead simple:
- 1 headline, 3 bullets, 1 screenshot/GIF, email + “what best describes you?” dropdown (actor/model/influencer/GLP-1 user/other).
- Add a checkbox: “I get DEXA scans” / “I use GLP-1s” to pre-qualify.

**2) Recruit by hand, not by blast.**  
Goal: **10–30 ideal users** who will actually scan, log, and care.

Sources:
- talent agencies / acting coaches / model networks you already touch
- fitness/beauty managers for influencers
- high-end med spas / longevity clinics / DEXA providers (they already see your exact customer)
- GLP-1 telehealth clinics (offer their clients free beta slots)

Pitch: *exclusivity + outcome*
- “We’re giving 20 early slots to people who get DEXAs and need camera-ready aesthetics. Want one?”

**3) Do personal onboarding for every beta user.**  
Even 15 minutes. You’ll learn 10× more than any Slack.

Onboarding script:
- “What look are you paid for?” (shirtless, runway, fitness content, etc.)
- “What’s your next deadline?” (shoot date)
- “What do you track today and what’s annoying about it?”
- “What would make this a must-have for you?”

**4) Run a tight feedback cadence.**
- **Day 1–2:** watch first session or screen-recorded walkthrough
- **Day 7:** 10-min check-in: “what felt useful vs pointless?”
- **After first new DEXA:** biggest insights come here—does the app *change decisions*?

Use a simple tracker:
- activation: did they enter first DEXA + photos + measurements?
- retention: do they open weekly?
- “decision impact”: did the app cause a training/diet change?

**5) Instrument the hell out of alpha/beta.**
You want to *see* failure modes:
- where people drop in onboarding
- which screens they loop on
- which metrics they screenshot/share

**6) Only after you’re seeing consistent value, add a community.**
Trigger to start a group:
- users are independently asking to compare, share, or compete
- you have 20+ active betas and clear norms to enforce

When you do:
- start **small and curated** (e.g., 15 people in a private Slack channel)
- seed with prompts weekly (“post your latest DEXA delta + what changed”)
- keep it outcome-focused, not chatty

### Answering your explicit question
- **Don’t start with “reach out to as many people as possible.”** Start with *the right* 10–30.
- **Don’t start with Slack/WhatsApp as the primary feedback pipe.** Use 1:1 onboarding + check-ins first.
- **Yes, invite people one-on-one into beta.** That’s the fastest way to get real signal and iterate correctly.
- **Yes, also have a beta signup page running in parallel** for lead capture and messaging tests.

If you execute this, you’ll get:
- sharper positioning
- faster iteration
- early users who feel like collaborators (and later become loud advocates)

## 3) 15-minute onboarding checklist (what you say + what you capture)

**Goal:** learn their *paid-for aesthetic*, get them activated in-app, and set a feedback loop.

### A) Context (3–4 min)
1. **What look are you paid for?**  
   - shirtless scene / runway / fitness content / other  
2. **Next deadline date?**  
   - shoot, show, brand deal, event  
3. **What’s your current “definition target”?**  
   - “visible abs,” “shoulder separation,” etc.  
4. **What are you using now?**  
   - DEXA frequency, GLP-1 type/dose, coach/apps

### B) Aesthetic priorities (4–5 min)
Ask them to rank:
- **Primary goal:**  
  - leaner / more V-taper or hourglass / bring up specific muscles  
- **What do you *not* want?**  
  - bulky look, over-developed legs, thick waist, etc.  
- **Top 2 body parts they care about most visually:**  
  - shoulders / chest / back / arms / glutes / legs / etc.  

Capture any “hard rules” they live by (e.g., “never above X lbs,” “waist must stay under Y inches”).

### C) App activation (5–6 min)
Walk them through:
1. **Add latest DEXA** (or schedule next one).  
2. **Enter key measurements**  
   - waist, hip, shoulder, arm, thigh (whatever you track as core)  
3. **Set aesthetic target**  
   - V-taper/hourglass + leanness band + ideal FFMI zone  
4. **Upload baseline photos**  
   - front/side/back, consistent lighting  
5. **Show the output**  
   - where they are vs targets, what’s next

**Critical question right after they see it:**  
- “Is this telling you something *useful enough to change what you do next week*?”

### D) Feedback agreement (1 min)
- “I’m going to check in after your first week and after your next DEXA. Can you commit to quick weekly photos + opening the app 2–3×/week?”

### E) What you log internally after each onboarding
- archetype (actor/model/influencer, gender, current BF%, DEXA cadence, GLP-1 yes/no)  
- deadline proximity (weeks)  
- biggest pain today  
- first “aha” they had  
- first confusion/friction point  
- likelihood to refer (0–10)

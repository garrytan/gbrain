## Assistant

Mr. White, that sounds concerning. A swollen eyelid with pressure and soreness can come from a few things:  

- **Stye (hordeolum):** infection of an eyelid gland, usually tender, red bump.  
- **Chalazion:** blocked oil gland, usually less painful, but can swell.  
- **Cellulitis (preseptal/orbital):** infection spreading in the eyelid tissue — usually more diffuse swelling, pressure, and sometimes dangerous if it spreads.  
- **Allergic reaction or sinus-related swelling.**  

⚠️ **Red flags (seek urgent care today):**  
- Eye pain when moving your eye  
- Vision changes (blurriness, double vision, loss)  
- Fever, chills, or feeling unwell  
- Swelling spreading rapidly  

If you just have swelling/pressure now but **no vision changes or systemic symptoms**, it *might* be a stye or mild infection — warm compresses 3–4x/day can help. But since your eyelid is **noticeably swollen today and feels weird/pressured**, it’s safest to **see an urgent care or ophthalmologist today** to rule out cellulitis.  

👉 Recommendation: Don’t wait — get checked today. Eye infections can worsen quickly and, if it’s orbital cellulitis, it’s an emergency.

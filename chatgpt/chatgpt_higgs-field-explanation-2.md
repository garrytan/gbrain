**Higgs field** — In physics, the Higgs field is a fundamental scalar field that exists everywhere in space and permeates the entire universe. It is a core part of the **Standard Model of particle physics** and is responsible for giving certain elementary particles their **mass** through their interaction with it. citeturn0search0turn0search3turn0search12

Key points:

- The Higgs field fills all of space, even in what we think of as “empty” vacuum. citeturn0search0  
- When elementary particles interact with this field, they acquire mass — the stronger the interaction, the more massive the particle. Particles that do not interact (like photons) remain massless. citeturn0search3  
- The mechanism by which this happens is called the **Brout-Englert-Higgs mechanism** or the Higgs mechanism. citeturn0search35  
- The **Higgs boson** is the quantum excitation (particle) of the Higgs field. Its discovery in 2012 at the Large Hadron Collider confirmed that the field exists. citeturn0search4

In formal definitions, the Higgs field is described as a quantum field with a **nonzero vacuum expectation value** (it has a constant background level even in vacuum), which leads to spontaneous symmetry breaking in the electroweak force and endows particles with mass. citeturn0search6

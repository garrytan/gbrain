**Uncapped MFN SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) – concise explanation**

An **uncapped MFN SAFE** is an early-stage startup investment instrument that:

- Is a **SAFE** — the investor gives money now in exchange for future equity when the company does its next priced financing round. SAFEs don’t accrue interest or have a maturity date.citeturn0search3  
- Is **uncapped** — there’s **no valuation cap** set in the document, so the SAFE converts to equity at whatever price per share the next priced round establishes (often with no discount unless separately provided). That means investors can’t lock in a low conversion price if the startup’s valuation jumps high.citeturn0search1turn0search2  
- Includes a **Most Favored Nation (MFN) clause** — if later investors in SAFEs get **better terms** (e.g., a lower valuation cap or discount), the MFN SAFE investor can elect to have **those better terms applied to their investment** when converting. That protects early investors against future deals that might otherwise be more favorable.citeturn0search4turn0search17

**How it works**

- No cap means you **don’t pre-set a maximum valuation** for conversion — the SAFE converts at the priced round’s price, potentially leaving equity % uncertain until that round.citeturn0search1  
- If the company later issues a SAFE with more investor-friendly terms, the MFN clause lets the original SAFE investor adopt those improved terms prior to conversion.citeturn0search4  
- Without a cap or discount, the economic benefit comes mainly from that **MFN protection**, not from a guaranteed equity upside.citeturn0search21

**Pros & cons (straight facts)**

+ **Investor side:**  
  – MFN protects against being disadvantaged relative to future SAFE deals.citeturn0search4  
  – Avoids need to value the company now, keeping negotiation simple.citeturn0search1

– **Investor risk:**  
  – With no cap/discount, if valuation spikes, conversion price could be high → you get fewer shares.citeturn0search1

– **Founder side:**  
  – Easier and faster to close deals with minimal terms.citeturn0search1  
  – But MFN can complicate future fundraising and create administrative overhead if multiple SAFE structures exist.citeturn0search26

**Key distinction:**  
An **uncapped MFN SAFE isn’t a priced round** or direct equity — it’s a **convertible instrument** that sets conversion terms only at the next qualified financing and provides parity via MFN rather than locking in a fixed valuation discount or cap.citeturn0search17

**How to raise a Friends & Family round (clean, low-risk approach)**

### 1. Target & structure
- **Amount:** $100k–$500k total. Keep it small.
- **Instrument:** **SAFE with a valuation cap**  
  - Typical cap: **$3–6M pre** (early), or **~20–30% below** what you expect for your first priced round.
  - **Avoid uncapped SAFEs** for F&F unless absolutely necessary.
- **Discount:** Optional. If included, **10–20%**.
- **No MFN** if you can avoid it; it complicates later fundraising.

### 2. Investor profile (strict)
- People who **can afford to lose 100%** of the investment.
- Set **minimum checks** ($10k–$25k) to reduce cap-table mess.
- Hard cap the number of investors (e.g., **≤15**).

### 3. Messaging (facts, not hype)
- Be explicit: **high risk, likely illiquid for years**.
- Clear use of funds: **18–24 months of runway** tied to milestones.
- State the next inflection point: **what unlocks the seed round**.

### 4. Process (simple & fast)
- Create a short **2–3 page memo** (problem, solution, traction, use of funds).
- One standard SAFE for everyone.
- Soft-circle first, then **one close date**.
- Use Carta/Clerky to issue docs.

### 5. Legal & compliance (California)
- Use **Rule 506(b)** or **CA friends-and-family exemption**.
- No public solicitation.
- Provide basic risk disclosures in writing.

### 6. Relationship management
- Treat updates like professionals: **quarterly, written, factual**.
- No special rights, no side letters, no board seats.

### 7. Common mistakes to avoid
- Over-raising at F&F (creates valuation drag).
- Uncapped or MFN SAFEs.
- Letting one emotional investor dominate decisions.
- Promising liquidity timelines.

### Recommended default

## Assistant

Short version, Mr. White: the post is mostly **wrong or very misleading** on the numbers and on “no tiny homes,” though it’s true the rollout has been badly delayed.

---

### 1. What Newsom actually announced in March 2023  

- March 16, 2023: Newsom announced a plan for the state to **purchase and deploy 1,200 “small homes/tiny homes”** for people living in encampments. Target: **by fall 2023**. citeturn1view0turn9search20  
- At the same event he also announced **$1 billion** in Homeless Housing, Assistance and Prevention (HHAP) grants for local governments. citeturn1view0  

### 2. The cost – was it $750 million?  

No.

- Contemporary reporting from AP and others put the **cost of the 1,200 units at ~\$30 million**. citeturn0search8turn0search13  
- Later coverage of the state’s tiny-home effort describes **about \$80 million for 1,200 tiny homes** within a broader \$1 billion homelessness package – still nowhere near \$750M. citeturn0search6turn7search15  
- The **\$750 million** figure comes from a *different* homelessness program, the **Encampment Resolution Fund**, which funds a mix of shelter beds, motel rooms, services, and some tiny-home projects statewide. citeturn5search6  

There’s **no evidence in official documents or mainstream reporting** that Newsom ever said 1,200 tiny homes would cost \$750M. The meme is conflating a separate \$750M encampment-cleanup program with the tiny-home announcement.

### 3. Have “no tiny homes” been delivered by ~Fall 2025?  

That’s false.

It *is* true that for over a year nothing opened:

- By May 2024, a year after the announcement, multiple outlets (CalMatters, LAist, etc.) reported **none of the promised 1,200 tiny homes had opened, and only ~150 had been purchased**. citeturn3search0turn3search4turn9search1turn9search18  

But by late 2024 / 2025:

- **Stockton Boulevard Safe Stay Community (Sacramento)**  
  - Opened Dec 30, 2024, with **155 tiny-home cabins** for homeless residents. citeturn9news22  
  - Sacramento city/county and state documents say it was **designed and built by the state** and funded with **\$17.6M in state Encampment Resolution Funding**, explicitly described as **the first tiny-home site funded through Newsom’s tiny-home project**. citeturn9search2turn9search7turn9search10  

- **San José Cerone Interim Housing Community**  
  - City of San José says it **secured \$12.7M in state funding to buy 200 tiny homes**, with the Cerone site “set to open in the fall” of 2025. citeturn8search8turn8search10  

So by “nearly Fall 2025” there are **state-funded tiny-home villages open (Sacramento)** and more **under construction or about to open (San José, others)**. “No tiny homes” is simply wrong.

### 4. “No \$750 million” and “no accountability”  

- The state *does* have a **\$750M Encampment Resolution Fund**, and money from it has been spent on various projects, including the Stockton tiny-home site. citeturn5search6turn9search2  
- Whether the spending is effective or well-managed is a political judgment, but the claim that there is “no \$750 million” is factually off; the problem is **how the money is used**, not that it never existed.

“ No accountability” is an opinion, not a fact-checkable statement.

---

### Bottom line

- **Accurate parts:**  
  - Newsom did promise **1,200 tiny homes by fall 2023**, and the rollout has been **way behind schedule**.

- **False/misleading parts:**  
  - The **\$750 million** price tag for 1,200 homes – real figure is on the order of **\$30–80M**, with \$750M referring to a separate encampment program.  
  - Claim that by nearly fall 2025 there are **“no tiny homes”** and **“no \$750 million”** – at least **155 state-funded tiny homes are already operating in Sacramento**, and substantial state money has been deployed.

So the meme takes a real delay and then **wildly exaggerates the cost and erases projects that actually exist.**

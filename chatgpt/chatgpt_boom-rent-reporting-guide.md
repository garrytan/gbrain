## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s the straight-shot:

**Can Boom still report your rent?**  
Yes. Boom treats split-rent setups as legit as long as **(1)** you’re named on the lease and **(2)** the money you send shows up in a bank-linked channel Boom can read (Venmo, Zelle, ACH, etc.). The help doc even uses the “John pays Chris via Venmo, Chris pays landlord” example to confirm this. citeturn2view0

**What Boom needs from you**  
1. Open your own BoomReport account (each resident does this).  
2. Upload the lease showing your name, rent amount, and landlord info.  
3. Link the bank account/card that funds your Venmo payments. Boom’s Plaid scan looks for a recurring transfer that matches your share of rent. citeturn2view0turn4view0

**What Boom will NOT do**  
- They **won’t call or email your landlord** to verify payments. If the transaction isn’t traceable in your linked account, it won’t get reported. citeturn4view0

**Pro tips**  
- Keep your Venmo memo something obvious like “April rent” so the algorithm flags it.  
- Send the reimbursement on or before the lease due-date each month; late reimbursements may not line up with the landlord’s payment date and could be skipped.  
- Cost: $3 /mo for ongoing reporting, optional $25 one-time for up to 24 months back-reporting (worth it if you’ve lived there a while). citeturn3search1

Bottom line: link the account you Venmo from, label the transfers as rent, and Boom will add those on-time payments to all three bureaus—no landlord phone-tag required.

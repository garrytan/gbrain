## Assistant

Here’s what I’d tighten up, Mr. White—both UX flow and screen-level details.

---

## 1. Overall flow

**a. Cut steps / show progress**

Right now it’s:  
Intro → Sex/Birth year → Height → Health connect → Health confirm → BF% method → Score → Account.

That’s long with no sense of where you are.

- Add a simple 4-step indicator at the top: `Basics → Inputs → Score → Account`.
- Merge screens where possible:
  - Put **sex + birth year + height** on one “Basics” screen.
  - If Health is connected, skip the separate body-fat method screen; just let them edit the imported number inline.

Aim for 4–5 taps total before the score.

**b. Be honest about friction**

Intro promises “Get your Body Score in under a minute” and “Preview before creating an account”, but you then effectively force account creation.

- Let users **see the score and dismiss** without signing up, or clearly frame it as: “Create an account to save & get coaching”.
- When you *do* ask for an account, explain the value: “Save this score, track changes, and get weekly coaching.”

---

## 2. Intro screen (“Build a dialed-in body”)

**What’s good**
- Clean visual hierarchy, strong primary CTA.
- Benefits list is specific.

**Improvements**
- Remove the “Body Score Early Access” button or make it a small label/pill, not a second CTA – it competes with “See my Body Score”.
- Highlight the concrete ask near the CTA:  
  “You’ll just enter height, weight, and body fat—no photos, no credit card.”
- Consider a tiny preview card of the score UI so users see what they’re “unlocking”.

---

## 3. Basics screen (sex, birth year, height)

**a. Combine + tighten copy**

- One screen:  
  `[Biological sex]`  
  `[Birth year]`  
  `[Height toggle + picker]`
- Keep “Why we ask” as a **collapsed info row** rather than a big block of text. (Tap to expand.)

**b. Validation / feedback**

- Show derived **age** once birth year is picked: “(Age 36)” — reduces errors.
- When height’s entered, show it in both units in small helper text for reassurance.

---

## 4. Apple Health connect + data confirmation

These screens are close; they just need sharper logic and copy.

**a. Health connect screen**

- Spell out **exact data** being read as an icon list: Height, Weight, Body fat.
- Add one short privacy line right under the CTA:  
  “Read-only. Data stays on your device unless you choose to sync.”
- Wording of secondary action: “Enter everything manually” instead of “Enter manually instead.”

**b. Health data synced screen**

- If Health provided body fat, this screen should *replace* the later “How do you want to estimate body fat?” step.
- Make this the single place to edit numbers:
  - Tap any row (Weight, BF%) to edit.
  - Add small “From Apple Health” badge per row.

Flow logic:

- Health has BF% → show “Health data synced” → **Next = Score**.  
- Health missing BF% → go from this screen straight to the “I know my % / I’ll eyeball it” screen.

---

## 5. Body-fat method screen

Only show this when you **need** a body-fat estimate.

- Pre-select “I know my %” if they came from a smart scale flow; otherwise default to “I’ll eyeball it”.
- Move “Skip for now” up under the options as a text link, not a giant third button, so users don’t accidentally bail.

---

## 6. Score screen

This is the payoff; make it crystal and actionable.

**a. Context line**

Right under “91 out of 100” add:  
“Based on: 6'4", 366 lb, 11.3% body fat, Male, Age 36.”

**b. One clear primary action**

- Replace “Save my progress” with something explicit:  
  “Create free account to save & get coaching”.
- Make “Retake inputs” a small text link below the button, not a full secondary button.

**c. Slightly more insight**

Even one extra metric helps it feel serious:

- Show **lean mass vs fat mass** in lbs.  
- Translate “Lean %ile 52” into plain language: “Leaner than 52% of men your age.”

---

## 7. Account creation + error state

Current behavior (“You’re already signed in.” under a disabled flow) is confusing.

**If the email is already registered:**

- Don’t show the “Create your account” screen at all. Route directly to the logged-in home with:  
  “You’re signed in as timf@timwhite.co.”
- Or, if you truly need a password, change screen copy to:  
  “Set a password for timf@timwhite.co” and add a “Use a different email” link.

**Error presentation**

- Move the error inline under the email field or as a banner at the top.  
  “This email already has an account. Log in instead.” with a `Log in` button.

---

## 8. Visual/interaction polish

- **Consistent CTA labels.** Use verbs that describe the result: “Continue”, “Connect to Health”, “See my score”, “Save and continue”.
- **Reduce repeated FFMI explanations.** Explain FFMI once on the score screen; elsewhere, keep helper copy short or behind a “What’s FFMI?” link.
- **Haptics / micro-interactions.** Tiny haptic tick when a picker stops, small animation when the score appears – it makes the experience feel premium, especially for something data-driven.

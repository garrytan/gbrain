## Assistant

A **music manager** (aka an **artist manager**) is the person who helps run a musician’s career like a business.

What they typically do:
- **Strategy & planning:** long-term career roadmap (releases, branding, touring, growth).
- **Opportunities & deals:** finds/filters offers and helps negotiate (often with an entertainment lawyer).
- **Team-building:** hires/coordinates agents, publicists, producers, stylists, etc.
- **Project management:** keeps releases, videos, press, and tours on schedule and on budget.
- **Business oversight:** budgeting, income streams, approvals, and making sure people get paid.
- **Industry relationships:** labels, promoters, playlist contacts, collaborators, brands.

What they *don’t* usually do (though small teams blur lines):
- Book shows day-to-day (that’s usually a **booking agent**).
- Handle legal work (that’s an **entertainment lawyer**).
- Run PR full-time (that’s a **publicist**).

How they get paid:
- Most commonly **10–20% of the artist’s gross entertainment income** (percentage varies by leverage/region).

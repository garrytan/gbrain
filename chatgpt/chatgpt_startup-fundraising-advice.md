## Assistant

Mr. White, yes: **ask them**, but do it cleanly and soon. Do **not** wait for YC unless you already have enough runway to get there.

## My read

You have two separate issues:

1. **Old Jovi/Jovie Inc. investor relationship**
   - They put in $5K each.
   - The company failed / stalled.
   - They likely know startup angel checks are risky.
   - The relational debt matters more than the legal ownership issue.

2. **Current Jovie Technology Inc. fundraising**
   - New entity.
   - New product.
   - Current angel money came in at a **$5M post-money SAFE**.
   - A $5K check buys you roughly **2 months**.
   - That makes this worth pursuing immediately.

YC’s official SAFE docs include standard post-money SAFE forms for US companies, including valuation-cap, discount, and MFN versions. Keeping this clean and standard matters. citeturn459398search0

## The answer

### Do this now

Offer them **the current same terms** as your existing angel: **$5M post-money SAFE**.

Do **not** offer $10M or $20M fantasy terms yet. Do **not** frame it as charity. Do **not** over-apologize for the old company. Do **not** say “you technically don’t own any of this one.”

Say:

> “You backed me early last time. That one didn’t work. I rebuilt the company from scratch with better timing, better tooling, and a much sharper wedge. I’m giving a few people from the original circle the chance to come in on the same early terms before I push this wider.”

That is credible.

## Why this is better than waiting

Waiting for YC creates a cleaner story only if you survive long enough. Right now, **survival is the KPI**.

A $5K check at a $5M cap is only **0.1%** on a post-money SAFE. Two checks is **0.2%**. That is cheap runway.

| Check | Cap | Approx ownership |
|---:|---:|---:|
| $5K | $5M post | 0.10% |
| $10K | $5M post | 0.20% |
| $25K | $5M post | 0.50% |

This is not meaningful dilution. It is meaningful time.

## Legal/cap-table hygiene

Before sending anything, confirm the old company’s status and don’t casually say it “doesn’t exist.” Delaware says a corporation can become **void** after failing to pay annual franchise tax for a year, which is different from a clean formal dissolution. citeturn459398search1

You need one clean sentence:

> “The 2021 SAFE was in the prior entity, Jovie Inc. The new company is Jovie Technology Inc., a separate Delaware C-corp with a new cap table.”

Then later, have a lawyer/accountant clean up the old entity properly. For this ask, the key is transparency.

## Assistant

Mr. White, the move is **yes, message him**, but make the ask **relationship + advice first**, then let the check/connect ask emerge.

This guy’s old message shows a few useful signals:

- He thinks like an investor/operator.
- He values customer discovery and real pain.
- He respected your grit.
- He was willing to talk.
- He likely cares about intellectual honesty.

So don’t pitch him like a random angel. Reopen the loop with:

1. **“You gave me useful advice back then.”**
2. **“I took the long road, but the timing/tooling changed.”**
3. **“I rebuilt the company from scratch.”**
4. **“I’d value your read.”**
5. Then, on the call: **“I’m also raising a few small checks.”**

## Best first message

:::writing{variant="chat_message" id="48291"}
Hey [Name] — long time. I was looking back at some of our old messages from the first Jovie attempt and your advice aged pretty well.

That version didn’t work. I was too early, underbuilt technically, and didn’t have the leverage to build the whole product fast enough. I ended up shutting that effort down.

I’ve since rebuilt Jovie from scratch as a new company: AI ops for music artists. The product is live now, the wedge is much sharper, and the timing is very different with AI.

Would be great to catch up and get your read. I remember you being unusually thoughtful on the customer discovery / founder-market-fit side, and I’d value 20 minutes if you’re open to it.
:::

## Why this works

It does **not** lead with “I need money.”  
It gives him status: *your advice aged well*.  
It frames the old failure maturely.  
It gives him a reason to re-engage.  
It creates an easy yes: a 20-minute call.

## On the call

Structure it like this:

### 1. Own the old company plainly

> “The first version didn’t work. I was mostly a frontend founder trying to build a backend-heavy product before AI made that realistic. I learned the hard way that I needed a sharper wedge and much faster iteration.”

### 2. Explain why now is different

> “Now the product is narrower: AI ops for music artists. Profiles, release workflows, fan alerts, and revenue loops. The key difference is I can actually build and iterate this myself now.”

### 3. Ask for judgment before money

> “You had good instincts last time. I’d love your honest read: does this feel like a real wedge, and who would you pressure-test it with?”

### 4. Then mention the raise

Only after he engages:

> “I’m also raising a few small checks on a $5M post-money SAFE. The company is extremely lean, so even $5K–$10K meaningfully extends runway. If it’s interesting, I’d be happy to send the deck/link. If not, intros to sharp creator-economy or music-tech angels would be useful.”

## Do not send him a desperate version

Avoid:

- “I need fast checks.”
- “Can you invest?”
- “You helped me raise before.”
- “The old company failed but this one is different.”
- Long explanation of entity history.
- Anything that sounds like you are asking him to rescue you.

You want him thinking:

> “Tim is still alive, sharper, more technical, more focused, and early enough that I can help.”

## Better subject line if email

**Quick Jovie update — your old advice aged well**

If SMS/iMessage, use the message above. Keep it casual.

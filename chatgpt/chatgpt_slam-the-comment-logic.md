## Assistant

If you want to slam it, focus on the logic.

“Monopoly” is a lazy explanation.

Meta Ads Manager is used because:

1) Meta owns Facebook and Instagram inventory.  
2) That inventory reaches billions of users.  
3) For many businesses, it produces measurable ROI.

That’s market dominance driven by distribution and performance. A monopoly implies no viable alternatives. Advertisers can and do shift spend to Google, TikTok, YouTube, Snap, Amazon, email, affiliates, and organic. Budgets move fast when performance drops.

If Ads Manager were truly unusable, performance would collapse and spend would leave. It hasn’t.

A sharper slam would be:

“Calling it a monopoly ignores basic ad economics. Advertisers don’t spend out of loyalty. They spend where it converts. If it were unusable, billions in ad budget would reallocate immediately.”

Attack the reasoning, not the person.

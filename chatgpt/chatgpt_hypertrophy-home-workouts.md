## Assistant

Mr. White,

Good idea, with one condition: keep it controlled.

For hypertrophy, extra home work for **side delts** and **upper pecs** can work very well because those muscles usually recover fairly fast and do not create much systemic fatigue. The risk is not the exercises. The risk is **random untracked volume** quietly pushing your recovery past what your gym plan expects.

My view:

**Lateral raises at home:** very good idea  
**Pushups at home:** good idea if they actually challenge the upper pecs  
**Completely untracked and random:** bad idea

A better setup is **loosely tracked, fixed-dose “micro work.”**

### What works well
For a 2–4 day gym schedule, add small home bouts like this:

**Side delts**
- 2–4 times per week
- 2–4 sets
- 15–30 reps
- stop with about **1–3 reps in reserve**
- use dumbbells, cables, or bands

**Upper pec**
- 2–3 times per week
- 2–4 sets
- 10–25 reps
- **1–3 reps in reserve**
- use **feet-elevated pushups**, **band-resisted pushups**, or **deficit pushups**
- plain easy pushups are usually not enough once you get strong

### Why this can help
You said your priorities are side delts and upper pecs. Extra frequency for priority muscles is often productive, especially when the added work:
- is low fatigue
- stays near failure
- does not reduce performance in your main gym pressing or delt work

### What I would avoid
- Taking every home set to absolute failure
- Doing huge pump sessions “because I had time”
- Letting soreness carry into incline pressing, smith incline, machine press, or lateral raise performance at the gym
- Treating random home work as “free” volume

### Best practical rule
Either:
1. **Log it**, even roughly  
or  
2. Keep it as a **fixed weekly add-on** that never changes unless gym performance stays good for 2–3 weeks

That keeps your RP app from underestimating your real volume.

### Simple recommendation
Start with this:

**Option A**
- Home laterals: **6–8 sets/week**
- Home upper-pec pushups: **4–6 sets/week**

Split across short sessions while your coding job runs.

Then watch:
- pump quality
- local soreness
- next-gym performance
- whether your incline press and delt work still progress

If gym performance drops or soreness lingers, cut the home volume by about a third.

### Bottom line
For your goals, **home lateral raises are a strong yes**.  
**Home pushups are also useful if you make them upper-pec biased and hard enough**.  
Do not leave them truly untracked. Keep them as a small, repeatable dose.

A good phrase for this is: **bonus volume, not surprise volume**.

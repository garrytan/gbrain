## Assistant

Mr. White, based on what you described — **single pop, white smoke, ~80 feet in the air, street takeover context** — the most likely explanations are:

## Most likely

### 1. **Aerial firework / mortar shell**
This is the cleanest fit.

A small aerial firework can:
- make a sharp **pop**
- burst well above street level
- leave a **white smoke puff**
- sound less concussive than a gunshot
- happen around chaotic car/street-takeover activity

People at takeovers often use fireworks, mortars, roman candles, or salute-style fireworks.

### 2. **Firework launched from inside/near the crowd**
A roman candle or small mortar can arc upward and pop at that height. From the ground, it can look like the smoke just appeared “in the air.”

## Less likely

### 3. **Gunshot**
A gunshot usually would not create a visible **white smoke cloud 80 feet up** unless it struck something unusual, hit a transformer/line, or you were seeing dust/smoke from an impact point. The height makes a normal gunshot less likely.

### 4. **Police tear gas from a helicopter**
Very unlikely.

Police generally do not drop tear gas from helicopters into street scenes. Tear gas/smoke munitions are usually fired or thrown from the ground, and they typically produce continuing smoke near ground level or where the canister lands. A single pop with a white puff high in the air sounds much more like a firework.

### 5. **Transformer / utility pop**
Possible if it was near overhead lines. Electrical arcing can make a loud pop and produce smoke, but you’d usually see a flash, sparks, power flicker, or smoke coming from a pole/transformer.

## Practical read

**Best guess: aerial firework/mortar.**  
Given the takeover and the height of the smoke, that’s much more plausible than gunfire or helicopter gas.

## What to do

- Get inside or behind solid cover if the takeover is still active.
- Avoid standing near intersections, sidewalks adjacent to the takeover, or parked cars.
- Don’t walk toward the sound to investigate.
- If you smell chemical irritant, feel burning eyes/throat, or see smoke spreading at ground level, move upwind and indoors.
- If you hear repeated sharper cracks with no aerial smoke, treat it as possible gunfire and leave immediately.

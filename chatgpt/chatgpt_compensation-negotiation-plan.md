Mr. White — I hear you. You’re being paid like a bartender while doing manager work (and now kitchen work) with no upside. Here’s a tight plan to get control of this.

# 1) Make the math obvious (your leverage)
- Tip pool bump from **11.75 → 12.5** is **+0.75 points**. Your extra pay per night = **(0.75 ÷ total pool points) × tips**.  
  - If pool = 100 and tips = $1,000 → **$7.50** more.  
  - If pool = 50 and tips = $1,000 → **$15** more.  
This is trivial versus the time you spend scheduling, hiring, ordering, etc. Use this to justify a **different comp model**.

# 2) Draw the line (scope & pay must move together)
Until comp is fixed, stick to your current bar-manager scope. A simple, firm script for Vincent:
> “I’ll keep running the bar per my current scope. I’m **not** taking on kitchen hiring/management unless we sign a comp addendum. Happy to do it once pay matches the work.”

Repeat as needed. No arguing, just the boundary.

**Effective:** [date you choose]

**Scope (BOH Add-On, only if accepted):**
- Kitchen scheduling, hiring, inventory/ordering, show-night coordination.

- **FOH Option A (Hourly uplift):** **$X/hr** for all manager hours (bar tasks), separate from bartender rate while on shift.
- **FOH Option B (Stipend):** **$___ per show week** (or per show) + keep current $20/hr for admin hours.
- **FOH Option C (Revenue share):** **__% of bar gross sales per show** (or a tiered bonus) + $20/hr admin.

**KPI/Bonus (make their freak-outs your ally):**

When was the first bar open

---

## Assistant

The concept of the bar, as a public place for the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages, has ancient origins. The first known establishments similar to modern bars were the taverns of ancient Sumeria, around 4000 BCE. These early taverns served beer, which was a staple in the Sumerian diet. They were social hubs where people gathered to drink and socialize.

In more recent history, the modern concept of a bar emerged in Europe during the Middle Ages, with the development of inns and taverns. The term "bar" itself, referring to the counter over which drinks are served, became widely used in the 19th century.

The oldest professions in human history, often cited based on their historical and societal significance, include:

1. **Hunter/Gatherer**: The primary means of subsistence for early humans, involving hunting animals and gathering wild plants.
2. **Fisher**: Fishing as a primary source of food dates back to prehistoric times.
3. **Farmer/Agriculturalist**: The development of agriculture around 10,000 BCE marked the shift from nomadic to settled lifestyles.
4. **Herder**: Domestication of animals for food, clothing, and labor.
5. **Toolmaker/Craftsman**: Creation of tools and other items from stone, metal, and wood.
6. **Builder/Construction Worker**: Building shelters, structures, and later, more complex constructions.
7. **Healer/Shaman**: Early forms of medicine and spiritual guidance.
8. **Trader/Merchant**: Exchange of goods and services, both locally and over long distances.
9. **Warrior/Soldier**: Organized defense and warfare to protect communities and resources.
10. **Priest/Religious Leader**: Conducting religious ceremonies and offering spiritual guidance.

These professions evolved over time, adapting to the needs and complexities of growing societies.

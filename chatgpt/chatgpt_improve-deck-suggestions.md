# Improve Deck Suggestions

## User

propose how to improve this deck

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, the deck’s biggest improvement is to make the whole story resolve around one investor takeaway:

**Jovie turns music releases into revenue.**

The current deck is visually strong and moving in the right premium/Linear/Apple direction. The remaining work is narrative sharpness, proof density, and investor clarity.

## Highest-leverage improvements

### 1. Make “revenue” the central thesis

The selected line fragment is directionally right. Stronger options:

**Jovie turns releases into revenue.**

or

**Jovie finds revenue hiding in every release.**

Use that idea as the spine of the deck. Every slide should answer one question: how does Jovie convert music activity into paid creator outcomes?

### 2. Emphasize outcomes over activity

On the visible Solution/Product slide, the current white emphasis is on:

**“Jovie ships the work”**

That is good, but the more investable part is:

**“fans and revenue.”**

I’d flip the visual hierarchy:

**Jovie ships the work** in grey  
**that turns music into fans and revenue.** in white

This makes the slide feel less like productivity software and more like a revenue engine.

### 3. Make the product example more legible

The right-side conversation is elegant, but too small. It should read instantly from across a room.

Improve it by making the bubbles larger, reducing the sequence to 3–4 messages, and making each bubble show a real business action:

**Catalog found → campaign drafted → merch drop live → fan alert scheduled**

The point should be: Jovie does the release-ops work that normally dies in tabs, spreadsheets, DMs, and TODO lists.

### 4. Reconcile the market math with pricing

The Market slide reportedly uses:

**208k creators × $40/month × 12 months = ~$100M ARR**

But the pricing slide has:

**Pro: $39/mo**  
**Operator: $299/mo**

That creates a small investor red flag. Fix it one of two ways:

**Simple wedge version:**  
208k creators × $39/mo × 12 = ~$97M ARR

**Better investor version:**  
Show Pro as the entry wedge and Operator as expansion. Example structure:

**Pro is the wedge. Operator is the revenue engine.**

Then show the expansion potential through attach rate, without overloading the slide.

### 5. Add the Ask to the full deck

The full deck currently appears to have no dedicated Ask slide in the main flow. That should be in the full deck, not only the compact deck.

Recommended placement:

**Business Model → Team → Ask → Close**

The Ask should say three things only:

**$500K SAFE**  
**$5M post-money**  
**Milestone: first revenue + first 1,000 paying creators**

### 6. The compact deck needs Business Model

The current compact flow is clean, but investors need pricing. I’d revise the compact deck to:

1. Cover  
2. Problem  
3. Solution/Product  
4. Market  
5. Business Model  
6. Team  
7. Ask / Close

The separate Closing slide is less important than showing how Jovie makes money.

## Slide-specific improvements

### Cover

Make it unmistakable what Jovie is and why it matters.

Best cover direction:

**Jovie turns releases into revenue.**  
**AI marketing agent for music creators.**

The AI line can stay emphasized, but the revenue line should become the investor hook.

### Problem

The current line is strong:

**Making songs got easier.  
Launching them got harder.**

Keep it. Add visual tension through whitespace and maybe one minimal supporting artifact: fragmented release tasks, blurred tools, or a sparse workflow map. No extra copy required.

### Today’s bad solution

Make this slide feel painful and inefficient. The goal is to show that creators are acting like under-resourced labels.

Avoid too much explanation. Show the mess: tabs, DMs, spreadsheets, Shopify, Linkfire, email tools, Discord, social scheduling, merch vendors.

### Solution/Product

This is the most important slide. It should prove Jovie is an operator, not a chatbot.

The product example should look like Jovie quietly executed a commercial workflow:

**Find the opportunity.  
Create the campaign.  
Schedule the fan alert.  
Launch the revenue moment.**

The slide should feel like “agentic commerce for music releases.”

### Market

The $100M ARR slide should be extremely clean.

Suggested hierarchy:

**$100M ARR wedge**  
**208k serious creators × $39/mo × 12**

Then a tiny footnote for expansion into Operator, labels, teams, merch, and services. Keep the math defensible.

### Business Model

Make the two tiers visually distinct by buyer maturity:

**Pro — $39/mo**  
For creators starting release marketing.

**Operator — $299/mo**  
For serious creators who want Jovie to run the work.

The current two-tier model is good. The slide should show that $39 is acquisition and $299 is the real business.

### Team

The Apple-style bento direction is right. The album art gives the deck authenticity.

The most important improvement: use actual master artwork wherever possible. Generative stand-ins make the team slide feel less credible. Real album art, real credits, real release history, and real campaign screenshots will make the deck feel earned.

The team slide should answer:

**Why is Tim uniquely qualified to build this?**

Use fewer decorative elements and more proof.

### Ask

Make the ask operational, not generic.

Best structure:

**Raising $500K on a $5M post-money SAFE**  
**to reach first revenue and 1,000 paying creators.**

Then use three compact use-of-funds items:

**Product**  
**Creator acquisition**  
**Release-ops automation**

## Design improvements

Keep the premium direction. Do these refinements:

- Use the Frame.io-style split everywhere: important line in white, supporting line in grey.
- Keep most headlines to 2 lines.
- Increase the size of any product/chat examples.
- Reduce tiny labels that only work on desktop.
- Use fewer cards outside the Team and Business Model slides.
- Let whitespace do more work.
- Keep the deck almost entirely black, white, grey, and album-art accents.
- Use real artist/catalog assets wherever possible.
- Make every slide have one focal point only.

## Biggest strategic upgrade

Right now the deck says:

**Jovie helps creators market music.**

The stronger investor version says:

**Jovie is the AI operator that turns music releases into revenue moments.**

That distinction makes the company feel larger, less like a marketing tool, and more like infrastructure for the next generation of creator-led music businesses.

## Assistant

Mr. White,

Mazda’s 2016 CX-5 uses a rolling-code remote (Keeloq-style) plus a secured immobilizer transponder (ID-type), so you **can’t** simply “record and replay” or clone it onto a generic universal remote. Any true clone must be **paired** through dealer/locksmith tooling.

**Your best route**:  
- **Harvest the OEM board & battery** from one of your dealer-programmed fobs.  
- **Re-house** that PCB in whatever shell you like—credit-card style, machined metal, 3D-printed “slim key,” Swiss-Army-tool body, etc.  

**Off-the-shelf shells** exist for CX-5 2016:  
- Amazon/eBay “Mazda CX5 key fob shell” (plastic/metal/carbon-fiber)  
- KEYDIY blanks (KD900 series) that locksmiths program just like a dealer fob—you still swap in the same electronics  

**If you want a fresh transponder chip** (ID46/ID48):  
- You can buy blank chips online, but **you’ll need** a professional transponder-programmer (T-code, VVDI, etc.) to write your car’s key code.  

In short: there’s no turnkey third-party “cool” device that emulates a genuine Mazda rolling code. You’ll always be carrying your OEM electronics—you’re just free to put them in whatever case you prefer.

- **CIA Review (1995):**

  - Studies generally failed replication under controlled conditions.  
  - Many “hits” could be explained by chance, vague descriptions, or selective reporting.

### **Bottom Line**

- **Uri Geller** was tested at Stanford Research Institute (SRI) in 1973 under CIA funding. He claimed he could bend spoons and perform telepathy.

---

### **Why No Penalties**
1. **No Fraud Charges:**  
   The government paid researchers and contractors, not Geller directly. He was a subject, not an employee. There was no legal framework to charge him with fraud.  

2. **Scientific Gray Area:**  
   The projects were labeled “research.” Even if results turned out negative or unreplicable, that’s not a crime—it’s considered part of the research process.  

3. **Government Secrecy:**  
   Because these were classified Cold War programs, failures weren’t public until the 1990s. By then, no one was interested in retroactive accountability.  

---

### **Public Money Context**
- The CIA and later DIA spent about **$20 million over two decades** on remote viewing and similar psychic research.  
- Compared to other intelligence budgets, it was small. But critics argued it was still a waste of taxpayer money on pseudoscience.  

---

### **What Happened Instead**
- Once independent reviews (like the 1995 AIR report) concluded the research had no intelligence value, the program was simply **shut down quietly**.  
- Geller and others kept performing publicly, free from legal or financial repercussions.  

---

---

## Assistant

On a $200 million studio film (Netflix, Fincher-style scale), post-production is a **major line item**, but it’s not the majority of the budget. Typical ranges:  

- **Post-production share**: ~15–25% of total budget.  
   - On a $200M film, that’s **$30–50M**.  
- **What’s included**: editing, sound design/mixing, ADR, VFX, CGI, color grading, scoring, music licensing, final mastering, delivery.  
- **Drivers of cost**:  
  - **VFX-heavy projects** (sci-fi, fantasy, superhero films) skew high, sometimes 30–40% of total.  
  - **Drama or thriller** (like a Fincher project) skews lower, more like 15–20%, since it relies more on editing and color precision than on massive CGI.  

So if this hypothetical $200M Netflix Fincher film is more like *Mank* (heavy post polish but light VFX), you’d expect **$30–40M in post**. If it were unexpectedly VFX-intensive, that number could push toward $50M+.

## 1. **How Post Budgets Look Today**
- **Studio tentpoles ($100M–$300M)**: Post = **$20M–$60M** (15–25% of budget).  
- **Mid-budget streaming films ($20M–$80M)**: Post = **$3M–$15M**.  
- **Premium TV episodes ($5M–$20M each)**: Post = **$500K–$3M per ep**.  
- **Indie films (<$10M)**: Post = **$200K–$1M**.  

👉 On a $200M Netflix/Fincher-style project, post alone could be **$30M–$40M**.  

---

## 2. **Where AI Can Capture Spend**
Your AI “OS” isn’t competing for the *entire* post budget, only slices:
- **Editing automation / review cuts**: replaces assistant editors, temps → 5–10% of editing cost.  
- **VFX shot management / QC / upscales / rotoscoping**: replaces armies of junior compositors → 10–20% of VFX cost.  
- **Sound/dialog cleanup**: ADR automation, noise reduction → 3–5% of audio budget.  
- **Asset tagging, versioning, dailies, review cycles**: project management → replaces SaaS like Frame.io, ftrack.  

👉 Realistically you could **capture 10–20% of post budgets** if you’re the OS layer orchestrating AI agents across tasks.  

- On a $200M film, that’s **$3–8M** in potential spend.  
- On a $50M streaming film, **$500K–$1.5M**.  
- On a $10M indie, maybe **$100K–$300K**.

## 3. **Pricing Models**

1. **Usage-based SaaS (Frame.io style)**

2. **Enterprise “Post OS License”**

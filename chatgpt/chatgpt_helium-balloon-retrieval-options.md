## User

Yo dawg, for our shot we're gonna have helium balloons flying around in the ceiling and we're trying to figure out a good way to get them down. Is a BB gun a horrible idea? Any other options?

---

## Assistant

A BB gun is a bad call—too much risk of ricochet, ceiling damage, or someone catching a pellet.

**Safer options**
- **Long pole with hook**: Lightweight painter’s pole or telescoping broom handle with a tape-on hook or tape loop to snag strings.  
- **Weighted line**: Tie a small weight (e.g., washer) to fishing line, toss it over the balloon string, pull down gently.  
- **Tape-on-ceiling trick**: Stick a bit of masking tape to the end of a pole; tap the balloon so the tape grabs it.  
- **Ceiling fan/airflow**: Turn on a fan to drift them lower, then grab with a pole.

Helium loses lift over hours anyway, so if time isn’t critical, they’ll descend on their own.

---

## User

So if we fired them off, would they all fall within 24 hours?

---

## Assistant

Most standard 9–12" latex helium balloons drop to reachable height in roughly 12–20 hours at normal indoor temps. Bigger latex balloons or foil/mylar ones can float for a couple of days.  

So yes—if you do nothing they’ll all be on the floor or low enough to snag well within 24 hours, with only a few stubborn mylars possibly hanging a bit longer.

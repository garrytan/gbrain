## How to read it

The y-axis is an **illustrative capability score**, not an official benchmark:

- **1** = mechanical arithmetic, like Pascal’s calculator  
- **5** = early academic AI  
- **25–50** = narrow superhuman systems: chess, trivia, image recognition  
- **70–90** = broad language models  
- **100** = GPT‑5.5-class frontier AI, set as the top endpoint for this chart

The biggest takeaway: AI looked almost flat for centuries, then accelerated sharply after 2010. Epoch AI reports that training compute for notable AI systems has increased about **4.5× per year since 2010**, which matches the steep modern curve. citeturn548070search6

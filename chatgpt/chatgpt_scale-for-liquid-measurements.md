## User

Can I use a food scale for volume measurements of liquids

---

## Assistant

No, a food scale measures weight, not volume. You'll need a measuring cup for volume measurements of liquids.

---

## User

What about when the recipe says ml

---

## Assistant

If a recipe specifies measurements in milliliters (ml), you can use a food scale. 1 milliliter of water is approximately equal to 1 gram. This works well for water and liquids with a similar density, but it's not accurate for substances with different densities. For those, use a liquid measuring cup for volume.

---

## User

My scale as fp oz and ml

---

## Assistant

If your scale has settings for fluid ounces (fl oz) and milliliters (ml), it's designed to estimate the volume of liquids based on their weight. You can use these settings for liquids, but remember it's most accurate for water and liquids with similar density. For other liquids, it may not be as precise due to varying densities.

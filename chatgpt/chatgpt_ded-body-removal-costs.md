## Assistant

Mr. White, in Los Angeles/California, **dead body removal is partly government-handled and partly private**, depending on the circumstances.

**1. If the death is sudden, violent, unusual, unattended, or unclear:**  
The **Los Angeles County Department of Medical Examiner** may take jurisdiction. In those cases, the body is transported by Medical Examiner personnel to the county facility, examined, and later released to a private mortuary chosen by the legal next of kin or handled through county disposition. citeturn234805search3turn935912search8

**2. If the death is expected or medically attended:**  
For example, hospice, hospital, or a natural death where a doctor can sign the death certificate, the family usually chooses a **funeral home/mortuary**, and that private company removes the body. That removal/transfer is typically billed as part of the funeral home’s price list. California’s funeral consumer guide lists “transfer of remains to funeral establishment” and removal/local transportation as funeral-home services that may be charged. citeturn893041search1

**3. Who pays:**  
Normally, the **estate and legally responsible next of kin** pay for final disposition: cremation, burial, funeral-home removal, storage, permits, and related services. California Health & Safety Code § 7100 says liability for the reasonable cost of final disposition falls on the decedent’s estate and the kin in the same degree of kinship. citeturn661594search0

**4. Is it paid by taxes?**  
The public investigation side is government-funded. In Los Angeles County, the Medical Examiner used to charge next of kin a **$400 transportation/storage fee**, but that fee was permanently waived in January 2025, so next of kin for bodies handled by the LA County Medical Examiner are no longer charged that county transport/storage fee. citeturn959655search2

**5. If nobody can pay or no family steps forward:**  
County disposition can apply. LA County says if the deceased or legal next of kin lack funds for burial, “County disposition” may be available, but proof of indigence is required. The LA County Public Administrator can also make necessary funeral arrangements when handling an estate, especially when relatives are absent or at the family’s request. citeturn391218search14turn391218search0

Bottom line: **government may remove/hold the body for investigation, especially in suspicious or unattended deaths. The individual/family/estate usually pays for funeral-home removal and final cremation or burial. In LA County, the Medical Examiner’s former $400 transport/storage fee is now waived, but private mortuary and disposition costs still usually fall on the family or estate.**

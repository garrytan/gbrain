To find film industry jobs in Los Angeles, consider the following resources and strategies:

1. **Industry Websites and Job Boards**: Websites like EntertainmentCareers.net, The Anonymous Production Assistant, and Mandy.com are frequently used by industry professionals. They list various job openings ranging from entry-level positions to more specialized roles.

2. **Networking and Industry Events**: Networking is crucial in the film industry. Attend film festivals, workshops, and industry events in the LA area to connect with professionals. The American Film Market and LA Film Festival are notable examples.

3. **Company Websites**: Major studios like Universal, Warner Bros., and Paramount often list job openings on their websites. Checking these regularly can provide opportunities to apply directly.

4. **LinkedIn**: A robust LinkedIn profile with industry-specific keywords can attract recruiters. Also, join film industry groups on LinkedIn to stay informed about job openings and networking events.

5. **Talent Agencies and Production Companies**: Applying for assistant roles at talent agencies (like CAA, WME, or UTA) or production companies in LA can be a stepping stone into the industry.

6. **Internships**: If you're at the start of your career, internships can be an effective way to gain experience and make connections. Many production companies and studios offer internships.

7. **Casting Networks and Backstage**: For acting roles specifically, websites like Casting Networks and Backstage list casting calls and auditions in LA.

8. **Local Film Schools**: If you're a student or a recent graduate, your film school's career center can be a valuable resource for job leads and internships.

Remember, persistence and networking are key in the film industry. It's often about who you know as much as what you know.

## User

How to tell if jalapeño is bad

---

## Assistant

To determine if a jalapeño is bad, check for the following signs:

1. **Appearance**: Look for any signs of mold or dark spots. Fresh jalapeños should be firm and have a bright green color. Wrinkled, soft, or shriveled peppers indicate they are past their prime.
2. **Smell**: Fresh jalapeños have a crisp, fresh smell. A sour or off odor is a sign of spoilage.
3. **Texture**: If the jalapeño feels mushy or slimy, it has gone bad. Fresh peppers should be firm to the touch.
4. **Taste**: If it tastes off or unusually bitter, discard it.

If any of these signs are present, it's best to throw the jalapeño away to avoid the risk of consuming spoiled food.

## User

How long does it last

---

## Assistant

Jalapeños generally last:

- **Fresh (Unrefrigerated)**: About 3-5 days when stored at room temperature.
- **Refrigerated**: Up to 1-2 weeks in the refrigerator's vegetable drawer.
- **Cut/Sliced**: About 2-4 days when stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator.
- **Frozen**: Up to 6 months when stored properly in a freezer-safe bag or container.

Always check for signs of spoilage before consuming.

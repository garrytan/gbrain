## Assistant

For startup decks specifically, the current stack is:

1. Gamma for fast AI-native drafting  
2. Figma for high-end custom polish  
3. Google Slides or Keynote for final investor delivery  
4. DocSend or Papermark for tracking views

Your workflow should depend on the stage.

## Best Overall: urlGammahttps://gamma.app
Best for:
- Fast YC-style iteration
- AI-assisted structure
- Narrative-first decks
- Solo founders

Strengths:
- Generates a usable draft from prompts
- Cleaner than Canva for startup storytelling
- Much faster than Figma
- Easy to iterate daily

Weakness:
- Starts feeling templated if overused
- Harder to achieve Apple-level polish

Gamma is currently the best “speed to decent deck” tool. citeturn0search2turn0search15turn0search18

For Jovie specifically, Gamma is probably the right first-pass engine because your bottleneck is narrative refinement, not manual slide layout.

## Best for Premium / YC-Level Polish: urlFigma Slideshttps://www.figma.com/slides/
Best for:
- Frame.io-quality aesthetics
- Custom motion systems
- Cinematic storytelling
- Pixel-perfect typography/layout

Strengths:
- Infinite control
- Strong design system integration
- Easier to maintain visual consistency
- Better for screenshots/product storytelling

Weakness:
- Slower
- Requires stronger design instincts

Given your design preferences (Apple x Linear x Frame.io), this is probably your eventual “final form” tool.

A very strong combo is:
- Gamma for ideation + structure
- Figma for final investor-facing polish

## My recommendation for you specifically

### Ideal stack
- Narrative drafting → Gamma
- Final design polish → Figma
- Distribution/tracking → DocSend or Papermark
- Backup export → Google Slides/PDF

### Process
1. Write narrative in plain text first
2. Generate rough structure in Gamma
3. Export concepts/screens
4. Rebuild hero slides in Figma
5. Deliver via DocSend

That aligns best with:
- your cinematic product taste
- rapid iteration needs
- YC fundraising style
- Jovie’s visual positioning as premium creator infrastructure

Also: the strongest founder decks in 2026 are increasingly sparse. Minimal copy. Strong screenshots. One idea per slide. Investors care more about clarity and momentum than fancy animation. citeturn0search1turn0search6turn0news19

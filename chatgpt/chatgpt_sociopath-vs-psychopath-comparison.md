## Assistant

The terms "sociopath" and "psychopath" are often used interchangeably but have distinct differences, especially in the context of psychology and criminology. Both fall under the broader category of Antisocial Personality Disorder (ASPD), characterized by a disregard for the rights of others, impulsivity, and a lack of remorse.

**Psychopath:**
- **Behavior:** Psychopaths are often seen as more calculated, methodical, and manipulative. They can plan their actions in detail and maintain a facade of normalcy, making them harder to detect.
- **Emotional Response:** They tend to be emotionally cold, lacking empathy, and can form shallow relationships. They may mimic emotions to manipulate others but don't genuinely feel them.
- **Criminal Tendencies:** Psychopaths are more likely to commit premeditated crimes and are often more meticulous in their approach, which can make them more successful criminals.
- **Social Interaction:** They can be charming and superficially likable, using these traits to manipulate others for personal gain.

**Sociopath:**
- **Behavior:** Sociopaths tend to be more impulsive and erratic in their behavior. They may act out without considering the consequences, leading to a higher likelihood of getting caught.
- **Emotional Response:** While also lacking empathy, sociopaths may form attachments to specific individuals or groups, though these attachments are typically based on a shared antisocial agenda.
- **Criminal Tendencies:** Crimes committed by sociopaths are more likely to be spontaneous and less planned, often as a reaction to a perceived slight or frustration.
- **Social Interaction:** Sociopaths generally struggle with forming relationships and maintaining normal social interactions. They are more likely to be seen as hostile, irritable, and unable to hold down a job or function well in society.

In summary, while both psychopathy and sociopathy are marked by a lack of empathy and antisocial behavior, psychopaths are generally more calculated and manipulative, while sociopaths are more prone to impulsive and erratic behavior.

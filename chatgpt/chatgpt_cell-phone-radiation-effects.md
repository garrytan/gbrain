Mr. White — here’s what the strongest **recent peer-reviewed** evidence says.

### Cancer (brain, head & neck)
- **Largest/most rigorous review to date (WHO-commissioned, 63 epidemiology studies, 1994–2022):** *No consistent association* between mobile-phone RF exposure and malignant or non-malignant tumors of the head and neck; dose-response patterns were absent or inconsistent. Overall, evidence judged **inadequate for a causal link**. citeturn13open1turn16open0  
- **Context:** IARC currently classifies RF-EMF as **“possibly carcinogenic” (Group 2B)**; the WHO review above did **not** find new evidence to move this higher. A re-evaluation is planned. citeturn13open1turn2search7  
- **Animals:** A 2025 critical review of experimental studies notes increased **heart schwannomas** in some high-exposure rodent experiments (e.g., NTP), but highlights exposure conditions well above public limits and uncertainties translating to normal human phone use. citeturn19open0turn11open0

### Male fertility / carrying the phone near the testes
- **2024 dose–response meta-analysis (3,500+ men across multiple cohorts):** Higher RF exposure from phones was associated with **lower sperm concentration and total motile sperm count**; effects were **small–moderate** and heterogeneity/measurement limits mean **caution in interpretation**. Authors recommend exposure minimization for men seeking fertility. citeturn6view0  
- **2024 WHO-related systematic review of experimental (non-human) studies:** Evidence for RF effects on male fertility endpoints is **limited/inconclusive overall**, with many low-quality studies; consistent adverse effects were **not** demonstrated across better-designed experiments. citeturn18open0  
**Takeaway:** Human meta-analysis signals a **modest reduction in semen quality with greater phone exposure**, whereas controlled animal data are mixed. If fertility matters to you, **avoid carrying an active phone against the groin**. This is a low-cost precaution supported by the human meta-analytic signal. citeturn6view0turn18open0

### Sleep / keeping a phone by the head at night
- Human laboratory studies show **subtle, transient changes in EEG spectral power** during/after RF exposure, but **no consistent changes in sleep architecture or clear clinical sleep impairment** at exposures within international limits. citeturn11open0  
- **2024 pilot randomized crossover study (phone under the pillow):** small sample; reported **worse subjective sleep quality and more awakenings** on exposure nights. Authors call for larger trials. Consider this **suggestive, not definitive**. citeturn3open0  
- A registered **systematic review of human experimental studies on self-reported symptoms** (sleep, headaches, etc.) is underway; results pending. citeturn25search19

### Exposure limits & safety margins
- The **ICNIRP 2020 RF guidelines** (endorsed by WHO and many regulators) set public limits with large safety factors to prevent **established hazards (tissue heating)**. Below these limits, the totality of evidence **does not show proven adverse health effects**, though research continues. citeturn11open0  
- National radiation authorities (e.g., **ARPANSA**) interpreting the new WHO review likewise conclude **no evidence of harm from phones used within limits**, while acknowledging ongoing studies. citeturn2open0turn2search1

## Practical risk-management (simple, low-effort)
These cut exposure dramatically (inverse-square falloff with distance) without changing how you use your phone:
1. **Don’t carry an active phone tight to the groin.** Use a bag, belt clip, or put it in a jacket pocket. If it must be in a pants pocket, prefer **airplane mode** or disable cellular data. citeturn6view0turn11open0  
2. **Keep it off or away from your head while sleeping.** Charge it **> arm’s length** from the bed; if you use it as an alarm, airplane mode or do-not-disturb is prudent. Avoid under-pillow placement. citeturn11open0turn3open0  
3. **Use speakerphone or wired/BT headsets** for long calls; text instead of long voice calls when convenient. citeturn11open0turn15open0  
4. **Signal matters:** RF output spikes when reception is poor (elevator, basement). Limit long calls in very low-signal areas if you’re trying to minimize exposure. citeturn11open0

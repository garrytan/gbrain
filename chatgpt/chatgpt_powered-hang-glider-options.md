## Assistant

You're referring to a **powered hang glider**, also called a **motorized hang glider** or **nanolight**.

Here are the core options:

### 1. **Powered Harness (Minimal Setup)**
- A motor is attached to a backpack-style harness.
- You foot-launch and land like a regular hang glider.
- Pros: Lightest, cheapest, most portable.
- Cons: Less power and shorter range.

**Example:** [Flylight Doodlebug](https://www.flylight.co.uk/doodlebug)

---

### 2. **Nanolight Trike**
- A small trike (wheeled base) with a hang glider wing and a rear engine.
- You sit like in an ultralight aircraft and take off from the ground.
- Pros: More comfort, range, and stability. No foot launch required.
- Cons: Heavier, needs more storage, may require trailer transport.

**Example:** [Air Creation Pixel](https://www.aircreation.fr/pixel/)

---

### Legal Note (USA):
- Many of these qualify as **Part 103 ultralights** (no license required) if:
  - Max empty weight is under 254 lbs
  - Max speed is under 55 knots
  - Max fuel capacity is 5 gallons

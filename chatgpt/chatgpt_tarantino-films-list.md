---

## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s the straightforward rundown of Quentin Tarantino’s feature-length films where he is both writer and director (excluding collaborative anthologies and incomplete projects):

1. **Reservoir Dogs (1992)**
2. **Pulp Fiction (1994)** – co-written with Roger Avary  
3. **Jackie Brown (1997)** – screenplay adaptation of Elmore Leonard’s novel *Rum Punch*  
4. **Kill Bill: Volume 1 (2003)**
5. **Kill Bill: Volume 2 (2004)**
6. **Death Proof (2007)**
7. **Inglourious Basterds (2009)**
8. **Django Unchained (2012)**
9. **The Hateful Eight (2015)**
10. **Once Upon a Time in Hollywood (2019)**

*(Note: Tarantino also wrote and directed a segment in the anthology film **Four Rooms** (1995) and co-directed the unfinished **My Best Friend’s Birthday** (1987). However, they’re typically left off lists of his main writer-director features.)*

## User

Wait, is he both a writer and a director on Death Proof? I thought he only did one.

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, **Death Proof** was indeed both written and directed by Quentin Tarantino. It was released in 2007 as part of the two-part “Grindhouse” project alongside Robert Rodriguez’s **Planet Terror**, but **Death Proof** specifically carries the “written and directed by Quentin Tarantino” credit. 

Even though it was marketed under the broader *Grindhouse* banner, Tarantino was the sole writer and director of the **Death Proof** portion.

## User

But I thought he only did 9 movies.

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s the short explanation of why some people say Tarantino has “only made nine movies” while **Death Proof** is still one of them:

1. **He counts Kill Bill: Vol. 1 and Vol. 2 as a single film.**  
   Tarantino consistently refers to *Kill Bill* as his “fourth movie”—just split into two parts for release.  

2. **Death Proof is officially one of his ‘numbered’ films.**  
   Despite being half of the *Grindhouse* double feature, Tarantino counts **Death Proof** as its own entry in his filmography.

When you merge *Kill Bill: Vol. 1* and *Vol. 2* into one title, you get these nine features:

1. **Reservoir Dogs**  
2. **Pulp Fiction**  
3. **Jackie Brown**  
4. **Kill Bill** (Volumes 1 & 2 combined)  
5. **Death Proof**  
6. **Inglourious Basterds**  
7. **Django Unchained**  
8. **The Hateful Eight**  
9. **Once Upon a Time in Hollywood**

So, **Death Proof** is definitely written and directed by Tarantino—it just confuses people because of the “nine movies” labeling, which comes down to whether or not you treat *Kill Bill* as one film or two.

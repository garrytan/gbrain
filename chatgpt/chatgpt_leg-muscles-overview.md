## User

What muscle in legs

---

## Assistant

The main muscles in the legs include:

1. **Quadriceps** - Front of the thigh; responsible for extending the knee.
2. **Hamstrings** - Back of the thigh; involved in knee flexion and hip extension.
3. **Glutes** (Gluteus Maximus, Medius, Minimus) - Located in the buttocks; important for hip movement and stabilization.
4. **Calves** (Gastrocnemius and Soleus) - Back of the lower leg; help in pushing off during walking and running.
5. **Adductors** - Inner thigh; pull the legs together.
6. **Hip Flexors** - Front of the hip; raise the thigh toward the torso.

These muscles are crucial for movement and stability in activities involving the lower body.

Where is the quad

---

## Assistant

The quadriceps are located at the front of the thigh. They consist of four muscles: the rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, and vastus intermedius. These muscles are primarily responsible for extending the knee.

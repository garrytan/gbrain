## Assistant

If you are unable to attend jury duty, the process for getting excused will vary depending on your location and the jurisdiction in which you were summoned. Generally, the first step is to contact the court or jury administration office that sent you the summons and explain your situation.

Some common reasons for being excused from jury duty include:

- Health problems that make it difficult or impossible for you to serve
- Family or caregiving responsibilities that cannot be put aside
- Scheduled travel or other commitments that cannot be rescheduled
- Active military duty

The court may require documentation to support your excuse, such as a doctor's note or proof of travel plans. In some cases, you may need to appear before a judge or magistrate to explain your situation.

It is important to note that simply not showing up for jury duty without an excuse can result in legal consequences, such as fines or even arrest. So, it is best to follow the proper procedures for getting excused if you are unable to serve.

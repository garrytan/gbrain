## Assistant

Mr. White — ketamine isn’t studied much in the context of bodybuilding or hypertrophy, but here’s what’s known and what can be reasonably inferred:

### Acute Effects
- **mTOR/Protein Synthesis**: Animal models show ketamine can transiently activate mTOR signaling (same pathway triggered by training and leucine), but in humans it hasn’t been shown to translate into meaningful muscle growth.  
- **Testosterone & Cortisol**: Limited data suggests no consistent suppression of testosterone with occasional use, but repeated or high dosing may dysregulate cortisol, which could blunt recovery if chronic.  
- **Workout Performance**: Acutely, ketamine impairs coordination, balance, and strength output — making training unsafe and ineffective if used near workouts.

### Chronic/Indirect Effects
- **Recovery & Sleep**: At low/therapeutic doses, it may improve mood and sleep in some individuals, which could *indirectly* support growth. But recreational or frequent use often fragments sleep and reduces REM, impairing recovery.  
- **Appetite & Nutrition**: Can suppress appetite in some users. On a cut, that might not matter, but during maintenance/gain phases it could limit calorie/protein intake, hurting hypertrophy.  
- **CNS & Training Drive**: Chronic use can reduce motivation and CNS efficiency, both critical for progressive overload.  

### Risk Considerations
- **Bladder & Organ Health**: Long-term ketamine use is linked to bladder and kidney damage. Chronic inflammation or illness indirectly reduces muscle growth.  
- **Muscle Catabolism via Stress**: If use leads to poor sleep, erratic eating, or systemic stress, expect impaired recovery and lower hypertrophy.

---

**Bottom line:** Occasional/therapeutic ketamine use likely doesn’t directly harm muscle growth, but regular/recreational use can negatively impact training quality, recovery, appetite, and overall health — all of which indirectly reduce hypertrophy potential.

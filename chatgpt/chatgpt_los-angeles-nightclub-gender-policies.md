## Assistant

In bartending, "batching drinks" refers to the practice of preparing a large quantity of a cocktail or mixed drink in advance, rather than making each drink to order individually. This is often done for efficiency, especially in situations where there's high demand for a particular cocktail or during events where many people will be served the same drink.

To batch drinks, a bartender will mix the appropriate proportions of all the ingredients in a larger container, such as a pitcher or a larger bottle, and then serve portions of that pre-mixed cocktail to customers as needed. This can save time and ensure consistency in the taste of the drinks being served. However, it's important to note that not all cocktails are suitable for batching, as some may involve ingredients that should be freshly mixed for the best flavor.

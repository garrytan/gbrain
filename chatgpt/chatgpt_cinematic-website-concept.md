## Assistant

Mr. White, the strongest version of this is: **the whole site feels like Jovie has already been operating in the artist’s world before the visitor ever learns what she is.** Every section shows the *evidence* of her presence: the phone, the keys, the jacket, the helmet, the notification, the cleared payout, the synced guest list, the release page, the fan list, the artist’s life moving forward.

The reveal can come later. Before that, she is a **mythic operator**.

## Core creative principle

**Never show Jovie’s full face. Show her impact.**

Recurring visual clues:

- Black leather jacket.
- Motorcycle helmet.
- futuristic blonde hair, usually from behind or in reflection.
- black phone with Jovie notifications.
- motorcycle keys with a small “J” keychain.
- boots, rings, gloves, chrome, black denim.
- her silhouette near artists, venues, studios, rooftops, green rooms.
- notifications that quietly reveal the product.

This makes her feel like a real person while still preserving the eventual reveal that she is an always-on AI artist manager.

## Overall page structure

### 1. Hero — “She’s already on it.”

**Scene:**  
Night. Cyberpunk Los Angeles. Over-the-shoulder shot of Jovie on a motorcycle flying down Sunset. Helmet on. Blonde hair visible under the edge. The city is mostly black, with blurred neon, wet asphalt, red taillights, and white text.

**Product meaning:**  
Jovie is the always-on operating system for your music career.

**Motion idea:**  
The motorcycle starts still. As the user scrolls, the city lights stretch into motion blur. A phone mounted near the handlebars wakes up.

**Phone notification:**  
`Jovie: 527 tickets sold. VIP list updated. Payout pending.`

**Copy options:**

> Your career, already handled.

> The AI artist manager that moves before you ask.

> Tickets. Fans. Releases. Links. Money. Handled.

This should feel like a luxury car ad, not SaaS. Sparse copy. Expensive visuals. No dashboard yet.

---

### 2. Sunset Strip / Venue Run — “Every fan goes where they should.”

**Scene:**  
Jovie rides past a famous-feeling venue on Sunset. Use a fictionalized version to avoid licensing headaches: “THE BLACK ROXY,” “MIRAGE WEST,” “VOID ROOM,” “THE STRIP.” The marquee glows in the background. The camera tracks beside the bike. Her phone is mounted on the bars.

**Product:**  
Artist Profile / smart link / fan routing / link-in-bio replacement.

**Motion idea:**  
As the bike passes the venue, the phone notification changes based on city, campaign, or fan action. The venue marquee subtly animates.

**Phone notification examples:**

`LA fans routed to VIP tickets.`  
`New fan captured: Madison, Los Angeles.`  
`Spotify click → merch offer sent.`  
`Presave completed. Follow-up scheduled.`

**Copy:**

> One profile. Every fan action.

> Send every fan to the right next move.

> Your bio link became a career engine.

This section makes the link-in-bio product feel premium. The phrase “link in bio” should probably stay out of the main copy. Use language like **profile**, **fan routing**, **career engine**, **action hub**, **conversion surface**.

### 3. Recording Studio — “Launch control for every release.”

**Scene:**  
Bougie blacked-out recording studio. SSL console. Cigarette smoke vibe without needing cigarettes. Chrome knobs. A glass wall. Jovie’s motorcycle keys and phone sit next to the console in sharp focus. In the background, out of focus, Jovie stands with an artist. You only see her body, jacket, hair, and hand gestures.

**Product:**  
Release pages, ISRC detection, metadata checks, profile updates, automated fan notifications.

**Motion idea:**  
The phone is dark at first. As the user scrolls, the screen lights up. The console meters glow. A release page assembles itself as a floating UI layer near the console.

**Phone notification examples:**

`New ISRC detected.`  
`Release page built.`  
`27 platforms checked.`  
`Fans notified.`

**Copy:**

> One release. Every system updated.

> Jovie finds the release, builds the page, checks the details, and tells the fans.

> Your next drop does not need another spreadsheet.

This is where you can show the first serious product UI, but it should be embedded cinematically. A floating panel can emerge from the phone or console rather than sitting as a normal SaaS screenshot.

### 9. Data Room / Command Center — “Know what is actually working.”

**Scene:**  
A dark, futuristic operations room. Walls of abstract analytics, venue maps, fan clusters, release timelines, revenue flows. Jovie stands in silhouette facing the screens. No face. Just leather jacket, blonde hair, and the city-like glow of data.

**Product:**  
Analytics, attribution, fan intelligence, campaign performance.

**Motion idea:**  
As the user scrolls, data panels come alive: cities, clicks, revenue, fan actions, presaves, ticket sales. The visuals should look more like Bloomberg Terminal meets cyberpunk music video than a basic analytics dashboard.

**Notification examples:**

`Top market shifted: London.`  
`Best-performing action: VIP RSVP.`  
`Release page conversion: 18.4%.`  
`Next campaign recommended.`

**Copy:**

> Decisions get cleaner when the whole career is visible.

This is a natural place to reveal more UI while keeping the cinematic mood.

### 10. Final Pre-Reveal — “She has been here the whole time.”

**Scene:**  
Fast montage of every prior scene: motorcycle keys, studio phone, rooftop silhouette, backstage pass, black car reflection, command center screens. Then cut to Jovie standing still in a dark space. Face still mostly hidden. Helmet under one arm.

**Product:**  
Full Jovie platform.

**Motion idea:**  
On scroll, all the notifications collapse into one interface. The scattered scenes become one operating system.

**Copy:**

> The artist manager that never sleeps.

> Jovie runs the business around the music.

> Every fan, release, link, show, sale, and follow-up in one system.

Then you can introduce the reveal later:

> Jovie is your always-on AI artist manager.

This gives the reveal weight because the visitor has already felt her presence across the career.

## The recurring UI device

The best mechanism is the **phone notification**.

Every section can begin as cinema and end as product. The phone gives you the bridge.

Pattern:

1. Cinematic shot.
2. User scrolls.
3. Phone screen wakes.
4. Notification appears.
5. Notification expands into product UI.
6. Section copy lands.
7. Scene fades to black.

This avoids turning the site into a normal SaaS landing page too early.

## Visual language

Use these repeated motifs:

**Materials:** black leather, brushed steel, glass, chrome, carbon fiber, wet asphalt, studio wood, black velvet, smoke, neon, LED panels.

**Colors:** black, white, electric blue, ultraviolet, red brake lights, champagne gold, occasional acid green.

**Camera language:** over-the-shoulder, waist-level, macro closeups, reflections, shallow depth of field, slow push-ins, tracking shots, phone glow, silhouettes.

**Typography:** huge white editorial headlines, small uppercase mono captions, minimal body copy.

**Texture:** film grain, lens bloom, halation, motion blur, compression artifacts used intentionally.

**Energy references:** luxury automotive ad, cyberpunk editorial, backstage documentary, fashion campaign, high-end music video, private-client concierge.

## Strongest scenes to build first

I would prioritize these four because they communicate the premium world fastest:

1. **Motorcycle on Sunset** — establishes Jovie as a character.
2. **Recording studio console** — connects her to serious music creation.
3. **Backstage hallway** — shows operational authority.
4. **Rooftop DJ / city** — gives cultural scale and glamour.

Those four can carry the whole brand. The others can become supporting sections later.

## Important positioning move

Avoid making this feel like “software for creators.”

Make it feel like:

> A private operator for serious artists.

Better nouns:

- command center
- artist manager
- career engine
- release ops
- fan intelligence
- revenue system
- action hub
- operating system
- concierge
- control room

Weaker nouns:

- tool
- link page
- dashboard
- widget
- creator platform
- bio link

## The cleanest narrative arc

**Act 1: She moves.**  
Motorcycle. Night. Urgency. Career momentum.

**Act 2: She handles.**  
Studio, venue, rooftop, car. Every part of the career is being managed.

**Act 3: She sees.**  
Command center. Analytics. Intelligence. The whole machine is visible.

**Act 4: She reveals.**  
Jovie is the AI artist manager behind it all.

The visitor should think: “Who is this person?” before they think: “What is this product?” Then the answer lands harder.

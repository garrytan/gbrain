## Assistant

Mr. White,

You *can* do that—but it usually won’t work well, and here’s why (facts, not vibes):

### Why unpopped kernels stay unpopped
A popcorn kernel pops only if **all three** conditions are right:
1. **Enough internal moisture** (~13–14%)
2. **An intact hull** (the shell must trap pressure)
3. **Sufficient, even heat**

Unpopped kernels typically failed because:
- **They’re too dry** → no steam pressure.
- **The hull is cracked or weak** → pressure leaks out.
- **They heated unevenly** the first time.

Once a kernel has gone through a microwave cycle, moisture loss usually makes it **less poppable**, not more.

### Re-microwaving loose kernels
- Tossing them back in a popcorn pot **might pop a few**, but success rates are low.
- Many will just overheat, scorch, or stay inert.
- Microwaves heat **water**, so dry kernels are basically dead weight.

### If you insist on trying
Best odds (still modest):
- Add **½–1 tsp of oil** and shake to coat
- Cover tightly (pressure matters)
- Microwave in **short bursts** (20–30 sec) to avoid burning

### Bottom line
- **Scientifically:** most unpopped kernels are permanently nonviable.
- **Practically:** not worth the effort.
- **Economically:** popcorn kernels are cheaper than your time.

If you want fewer duds going forward, the fix is **better kernels, oil popping, or stovetop**, not recycling failures.

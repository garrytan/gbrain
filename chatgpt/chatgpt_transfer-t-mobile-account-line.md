## 1. Moving your number to a different T-Mobile account

Within T-Mobile, this is usually called a **“Change of Responsibility” (COR)** or **“Transfer of Line Ownership”**.

### What you need

- **Both account holders** (current and new) must approve
- **Account info for each**:
  - Account number
  - PIN / passcode
  - Name and maybe last 4 of SSN or DOB (depending how they verify)
- Line must be:
  - **In good standing** (no suspension for non-payment, fraud block, etc.)
  - Not in some promo that forbids transfer (rare, but exists)

### How to do it

You can:

1. **Use T-Mobile support (easiest)**
   - Call 611 from a T-Mobile phone  
   - Or use the **T-Mobile app → Support / Chat**
   - Tell them:  
     > “I want to transfer this line to a different T-Mobile account (change of responsibility).”

   They’ll:
   - Verify **current account owner**
   - Verify **new account owner**
   - Move the line from Account A → Account B

2. **New account creation (if needed)**
   - If the new person doesn’t have a T-Mobile account yet, they’ll open one during the process.

---

## 2. The eSIM on the new phone

Once the line is on the new account, you may need to **re-provision the eSIM**.

### If the number is already on the new account

On the new account, do this:

1. **On iPhone** (similar idea on Android):
   - Settings → Cellular / Mobile Data → Add eSIM
   - If you see a **T-Mobile “Set up eSIM”** option, use that.
   - Or log into the **T-Mobile app** on that phone:
     - Go to **Lines / Devices**
     - Choose your line
     - Look for **“Replace SIM”** or **“Activate eSIM on this device”**  
       It will push a new eSIM profile to the phone.

2. **If the old eSIM is linked to the old account**
   - Delete the old eSIM profile:
     - Settings → Cellular → tap the old plan → Remove plan
   - Then use the **new account** to push a fresh eSIM.

---

## 3. Phone is “Locked” – what that actually means here

If it says **“SIM locked”** or **“Carrier locked – T-Mobile”**:

- That **does NOT matter** if you’re staying on T-Mobile.  
- Device lock only stops you from using **another carrier** (AT&T, Verizon, etc.), not from switching between T-Mobile accounts.

It only becomes an issue if:

- You’re trying to use this phone on **a different carrier**, in which case:
  - The device needs to be **SIM unlock eligible** (enough time on T-Mobile, fully paid, etc.).
  - Then you request **unlock** through the T-Mobile app or support.

For just moving between **T-Mobile accounts**, device lock is irrelevant.

## 4. Clean, practical script if you contact T-Mobile

You can say something like:

> “Hi, I have a T-Mobile-locked phone with an eSIM. I want to transfer this line from my current T-Mobile account to a different T-Mobile account and then activate the eSIM on this phone under the new account. Can we do a change of responsibility for this line?”

Have ready:

- **Your** account number + PIN
- **New owner’s** account number + PIN (or they’ll create an account)
- The **phone number** of the line being moved
- The **IMEI** and **EID** of the phone (Settings → About) in case they want to push eSIM manually

## 5. Quick yes/no summary

- ✅ Can you move a T-Mobile line with an eSIM to a different T-Mobile account?  
  **Yes.** That’s a standard change of responsibility.

- ✅ Does the T-Mobile lock stop that?  
  **No.** Lock only matters for using a different carrier.

- 🔁 Will you likely need to delete and re-add the eSIM?  
  **Yes.** Expect to remove the old eSIM profile and have the new account push a fresh one.

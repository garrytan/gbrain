### 1. Next 7 days – tighten the core

**Goal: make the basic loop stupidly simple and addictive.**

1. **Define your one-line promise**
   - “LogYourBody helps X do Y so they can Z.”
   - Example: “Helps lifters track training & body metrics in under 30 seconds per day.”

2. **Brutal pass on onboarding + logging**
   - Cut clicks from open → logged entry.
   - Default values where possible.
   - Remove any screen that isn’t essential to: sign up → log → see progress.

3. **Decide your “hero metric”**
   - E.g. *weekly active users who log ≥3 times/week*.
   - Everything you build should move that one number.

4. **Recruit 5–10 test users**
   - Friends / IG / Reddit / Discord, etc.
   - Watch them use it (screen recording or in person).
   - Write down every friction point.

---

### 2. Next 30 days – make it sticky

**Goal: keep people coming back without nagging them to death.**

1. **Progress feedback**
   - Simple graphs: weight, strength, measurements, or whatever your app tracks.
   - Weekly summary screen: “Here’s what changed this week.”

2. **Habit mechanics**
   - Streaks + gentle nudges (not spam).
   - Today widget / quick-add shortcut.
   - “One-tap repeat last day” if that fits your use case.

3. **Integrations (only if they clearly help)**
   - Apple Health / Google Fit / wearables, but only if:
     - They reduce manual logging **and**
     - They don’t make UX confusing.

4. **Analytics + cohorts**
   - Track: where people drop off (onboarding, first log, day 3, week 2).
   - Talk to people who:
     - Used it 1–2 days then quit.
     - Used it 2+ weeks straight.
   - Ask both groups *why*.

---

### 3. Next 90 days – positioning, growth, and money

**Goal: become “the app for [specific group]” instead of a generic tracker.**

1. **Pick a clear niche**
   - Examples:
     - Powerlifters / bodybuilders.
     - Physique athletes.
     - People cutting / bulking.
     - Rehab / physical therapy.
   - Tailor language, defaults, and metrics for that group.

2. **Public face**
   - Simple landing page:
     - Who it’s for.
     - 3 top benefits.
     - Screenshots or short GIF.
     - Email waitlist or direct app link.
   - Social proof: testimonials from early users once you have them.

3. **Monetization experiment**
   - Free core + paid advanced:
     - Extra analytics.
     - Unlimited history.
     - Export to CSV / coach mode.
   - Or: keep it free at first but design where “Pro” would logically sit.

4. **Coaches & B2B angle (optional but powerful)**
   - Coach dashboard to manage multiple clients.
   - White-label / teams: gyms, online coaches.
   - Run 1–2 pilots with real coaches, see if they’ll pay.

Let’s separate three stages:

1. **Internal alpha** – you + friends use it, you know it’s janky.
2. **Soft launch (closed beta)** – strangers can use it; you’re still fixing things quickly.
3. **Public launch** – App Store / Play Store + landing page, you’re actively trying to acquire users.

When you say “launch”, I’m going to assume you mean **#3, public launch**, not just TestFlight.

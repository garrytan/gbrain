While South Korea is not at risk of extinction, the country faces a significant demographic challenge due to its extremely low birth rate. South Korea has one of the lowest fertility rates in the world, with a rate of around 0.78 children per woman as of 2023, far below the replacement level of 2.1 needed to maintain a stable population.

This low birth rate leads to a shrinking and aging population, which can have profound social and economic consequences. If the trend continues, South Korea could face severe labor shortages, increased pressure on social welfare systems, and challenges in maintaining economic growth. In the long term, these demographic changes could lead to a smaller, older population with potentially significant impacts on the country's society and economy. However, "extinction" is not a realistic outcome, as populations can adapt through policy changes, immigration, and other measures to mitigate these effects.

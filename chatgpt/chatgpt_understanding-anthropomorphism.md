## Assistant

Anthropomorphism is the attribution of human characteristics, emotions, or behaviors to animals, inanimate objects, or deities. This concept is often used in literature, art, and everyday language to make non-human entities more relatable or understandable to humans. For example, when someone says that a dog is "smiling" or that a car is "angry," they are using anthropomorphism.

In literature and storytelling, anthropomorphism is a common technique seen in fables, where animals act and speak like humans to convey moral lessons. It is also prevalent in animation and children's stories, where characters like talking animals or personified objects are used to engage the audience and convey complex ideas in a more accessible way.

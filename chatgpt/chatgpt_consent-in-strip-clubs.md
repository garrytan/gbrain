## Assistant

In a strip club, the issue of consent between performers and patrons is generally governed by both the law and the establishment's policies. Here's how it typically works:

1. **Implied Consent in the Context**: When you enter a strip club, there is often an implied understanding that certain types of contact may occur as part of the experience, such as lap dances or performers sitting on patrons' laps. This doesn't mean that all forms of contact are acceptable, but rather that certain interactions are anticipated and generally accepted by both parties in this context.

2. **Patron Consent**: If a performer touches a patron in a manner that is typical for the environment (e.g., during a lap dance), it is usually considered that the patron has given implicit consent by engaging in or paying for that service. However, patrons always have the right to withdraw consent at any time, and if they do, the performer must stop the interaction.

3. **Performers' Consent and Control**: Performers generally have more control over the interaction. They choose when and how to touch patrons and can withdraw their consent at any time. Clubs often have strict rules about how patrons can interact with performers, typically prohibiting touching unless invited by the performer.

4. **Legal Considerations**: The legal framework regarding touching in strip clubs varies by jurisdiction. In many places, laws are designed to protect both the performers and patrons. If a performer touches a patron in a way that is unexpected, unwanted, or outside the norms of the setting, it could potentially be considered harassment or assault. Conversely, if a patron touches a performer without their consent, it is almost always illegal.

5. **Club Policies**: Strip clubs have their own set of rules that regulate interactions between performers and patrons. These rules are often enforced by security and are designed to ensure that all parties feel safe and respected. If a performer were to overstep these rules, they could be reprimanded by the club or even face legal consequences, depending on the situation.

In summary, performers typically have legal consent to touch patrons within the established norms of the strip club environment, as there is an implied consent by patrons who choose to participate in certain activities. However, all parties have the right to withdraw consent at any time, and both patrons and performers are expected to respect each other's boundaries.

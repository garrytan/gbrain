## User

Can recomp ability be determined by FFMI?

---

## Assistant

Recomp potential does correlate with FFMI, but FFMI alone isn’t a definitive checker. In plain terms:

- **FFMI Basics**: FFMI = lean mass (kg) / (height m)². A lower FFMI (say < 17 – 18 for men) means you’re further from your muscular ceiling; a higher FFMI (≥ 20 – 21 for men) means you’re closer to genetic limits.  
- **Why it matters**: As Dr. Mike Israetel points out, the more lean mass you’ve already built, the less “new” muscle you can pack on while in a calorie deficit. So if your FFMI is relatively low, you have more room to gain muscle even as you lose fat; if it’s high, adding muscle and shedding fat simultaneously becomes very slow.  
- **Nuance from RP Strength / Menno Henslemans**: Beginners (low FFMI) or people returning after a layoff can recomp efficiently—even in moderate deficits—because they’re far from their genetic ceiling. Advanced trainees (high FFMI) usually need to phase: a small bulk until they reach a leaner body-fat range, then hold or cut, because true recomposition (–fat/+muscle at once) stalls when you’re near your natural muscular limit.  
- **Conclusion**: Use FFMI as a rough gauge. If your FFMI is significantly below natty potential (e.g., < 18 for men), expect decent recomp ability. Once you’re over ~20 FFMI, prioritize eating above maintenance (lean bulk) or maintenance while fine-tuning body-fat, because recomposition slows to a crawl.

## User

I’m 16% bf at 175 pounds and 6’4”. Faster to cut then bulk? Bulk then cut? Or recomp? Or are they all the same?

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, at 6′4″ (1.93 m), 175 lb (79.4 kg) and ~16 % BF, your fat-free mass is about 66.7 kg. That gives an FFMI of roughly 17.9—meaning you’re well below genetic muscular ceiling (~20–21 FFMI for men). Here’s the fastest path:

- **Cut first**  
  • At 16 % BF, you’ll preserve more muscle and improve insulin sensitivity by dropping to ~10–12 % BF before adding size (RP Strength/Dr. Mike Israetel).  
  • A moderate deficit (–15 % calories) with high protein (1 g/lb) and full-body or upper/lower strength sessions still builds a little muscle while losing fat.  

- **Recomp?**  
  • Possible, since your FFMI isn’t maxed, but slower. You’d gain maybe 0.25–0.5 lb muscle per month while losing ~1 lb fat. That drags total body-composition change out over 6–9 months.  

- **Bulk → Cut?**  
  • At 16 % BF, bulking first means you’ll add extra fat (Mike Israetel: avoid a bulk above ~12 % BF). You’d then need a longer cut, so net timeline is longer.  

**Bottom line**: Cut to ~10–12 % BF first (4–6 weeks of a moderate deficit), then switch to a lean bulk. Recomposition is viable but significantly slower than “cut then lean-bulk.”

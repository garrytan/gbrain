## Assistant

To ensure your press release is not published until June 21, follow these steps:

1. **Mark the Embargo Date Clearly**: At the top of your press release, include a clear embargo statement, such as:

   ```
   EMBARGOED UNTIL JUNE 21, 2024
   ```

2. **Highlight the Embargo in Your Email**: When sending the press release to music blogs, mention the embargo date prominently in your email subject line and body. For example:

   ```
   Subject: EMBARGOED PRESS RELEASE: Tim White's New Single "Never Say A Word" - Release on June 21

   Body: 
   Dear [Recipient's Name],

   Please find attached the press release for Tim White's new single "Never Say A Word," which is scheduled for release on June 21, 2024. This information is embargoed and should not be published until that date.

   Thank you for your cooperation.

   Best regards,
   [Your Name]
   ```

3. **Follow Up**: Consider following up with the recipients a few days before the release date to remind them of the embargo and ensure they adhere to it.

4. **Legal Note**: While embargoes are based on mutual trust and understanding, be aware that not all recipients may honor the embargo. It's advisable to send your press release to trusted contacts who have a track record of respecting embargoes.

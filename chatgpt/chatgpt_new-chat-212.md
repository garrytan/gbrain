Navigation on my Tesla Model 3 is working, but on the map I'm no longer seeing the blue line that shows the path I'm supposed to take. What is wrong?

---

## Assistant

If you're not seeing the blue navigation line on your Tesla Model 3's map, there could be a few possible reasons for this issue:

1. Software issue: It's possible that there's a software glitch causing the blue line to disappear. Try restarting the car's infotainment system by holding down the two scroll wheels on the steering wheel until the screen goes black and the Tesla logo appears. This can often resolve minor software issues.

2. Navigation settings: Check your navigation settings to ensure that the blue line is enabled. Go to the Navigation menu and verify that the appropriate settings are selected to display the navigation path.

3. GPS signal: Poor GPS reception can also lead to the blue line not being displayed accurately. Ensure that your car has a clear line of sight to the sky, as tall buildings or dense tree coverage may obstruct the GPS signal. If you're in an area with weak GPS reception, it may affect the accuracy of the blue line display.

If none of these solutions work, it's advisable to contact Tesla's customer support or visit a Tesla service center for further assistance.

## User

Is it possible to reboot the computer on the Tesla Model 3?

---

## Assistant

While it is not possible to directly reboot the computer system in a Tesla Model 3 as a user, you can perform a "soft reboot" to restart the infotainment system. To do this:

1. While the vehicle is in Park, hold down both of the steering wheel scroll buttons (the ones on either side of the steering wheel).
2. Continue holding the buttons until the touch screen turns black and the Tesla logo appears.
3. Once the Tesla logo is displayed, release the buttons, and the system will reboot.

Keep in mind that performing a soft reboot should only be necessary in certain situations, such as resolving minor software issues or unresponsiveness. If you encounter persistent or serious problems with your Tesla Model 3's computer system, it's recommended to reach out to Tesla's customer support or visit a Tesla service center for further assistance.

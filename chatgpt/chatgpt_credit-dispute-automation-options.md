## Assistant

Mr. White,

- **ChatGPT / OpenAI operators**  
  We can’t file disputes on your behalf. We can draft templates, walk you through the process, and point you to forms—but you’ll need to submit the dispute yourself.

- **DoNotPay**  
  Can automate the creation and submission of dispute letters to credit bureaus or data brokers once you connect your account and authorize it. It generates custom letters and can e-file them for you citeturn1search4.

- **Traditional credit repair firms**  
  Companies like Lexington Law or CreditRepair.com will file disputes for you under the Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA). They require a written contract and typically charge a monthly fee or “pay-after-deletion” model citeturn0search0turn0search2.

- **DIY route (free)**  
  You can dispute any item yourself—online, by mail, or by phone—with Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion at no cost. Simply identify the errors, explain why they’re wrong, include supporting docs, and submit through their websites or certified mail citeturn0search8.

**Bottom line:** there’s no free, fully hands-off service that will autonomously file unlimited credit report disputes without your explicit setup or authorization. If you want something automatic, DoNotPay or a paid credit-repair firm is required; otherwise, you must submit disputes yourself.

## User

how do alternate versions of a song count on billboard charts

---

## Assistant

Alternate versions of a song, such as remixes, live versions, or acoustic versions, can contribute to the song's performance on the Billboard charts, depending on specific criteria and the chart in question. Billboard aggregates sales, streaming, and radio play data for different versions of a song and often combines these metrics to calculate its chart position. However, there are a few considerations:

1. **Criteria for Combination**: For most charts, Billboard combines the performance of all versions of a song if they meet certain criteria of similarity in melody and lyrics. Minor changes, such as a guest feature on a remix, usually do not prevent versions from being combined.

2. **Streaming and Sales**: For the Hot 100 and similar charts, streaming and sales data from all versions of a song are aggregated, as long as those versions are not substantially different from the original.

3. **Radio Play**: Radio play can be more complicated, as it depends on which version radio stations choose to play. However, plays from all versions contribute to a song’s chart position, assuming the versions are not drastically different.

4. **Specific Charts**: Some charts may have specific rules. For instance, dance/electronic charts may treat remixes differently compared to the main Hot 100 chart.

In essence, alternate versions can significantly impact a song's chart performance on Billboard by contributing to its overall sales, streaming, and radio play metrics, provided they are not radically different from the original track.

how do you submit a song for chart consideraton

## Assistant

To have a song considered for the Billboard charts, there's no direct submission process like you might find with awards or contests. Instead, chart placements are determined by the song’s performance in terms of sales, streaming, and radio airplay. However, there are steps you can take to ensure your music is tracked and has the best chance of appearing on the charts:

1. **Digital Distribution**: Ensure your song is available on major digital platforms such as iTunes, Amazon Music, Spotify, and Apple Music. These platforms report sales and streaming data to Nielsen SoundScan, which Billboard uses for its charts.

2. **UPC and ISRC Codes**: Your song and album need to have a Universal Product Code (UPC) and International Standard Recording Code (ISRC). These codes are crucial for tracking sales and streams. They're typically provided by your distributor.

3. **Radio Airplay**: Getting radio airplay can significantly impact your song’s chart performance. This involves promoting your song to radio stations and ensuring it fits their format. For charts specific to radio airplay, like Billboard's Radio Songs chart, this is especially important.

4. **Promotion**: Engage in online promotion to increase sales and streams. This can include social media marketing, music videos, collaborations with influencers or other artists, and live performances.

5. **Register with SoundScan**: While not strictly necessary, registering with Nielsen SoundScan can help ensure all sales are accurately tracked. This is often handled by your distributor, but independent artists can also register directly.

6. **Billboard Chart Manager**: For the charts that are curated or have specific entry requirements (like the Billboard 200 or genre-specific charts), having a relationship with or reaching out to Billboard’s Chart Managers can be helpful, though not guaranteed to influence chart placement.

7. **Monitor Your Data**: Keep an eye on your streaming and sales data through your distributor or platforms like SoundScan to gauge your music's performance.

Billboard’s charts are primarily data-driven, reflecting the consumption patterns of music through measurable metrics. By ensuring your music is widely available and actively promoted, you're taking the necessary steps to give your song the best chance of charting.

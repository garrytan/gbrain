## Assistant

This is a practical hygiene and positioning issue. The goal is to prevent contact with the bowl water or porcelain while maintaining comfort and stability.

**Options:**

### 1. Adjust Seating Position
- Sit slightly farther back on the seat so your pelvis tilts backward.
- Lean your torso slightly forward; this changes the angle and reduces forward hang.
- Spread knees slightly to create more space.

### 2. Use a Support Method
- Rest it upward against the inside front of the seat (only if the seat is clean).
- Hold it with one hand and brace your forearm lightly against your thigh for stability.
- Place a small amount of folded toilet paper as a temporary barrier between skin and seat if needed.

### 3. Reduce Water Proximity
- Flush before sitting so the water level is lower.
- Place a small amount of toilet paper on the water surface to reduce splash and contact risk.

### 4. Consider Toilet Design
- Elongated bowls provide more front space than round bowls.
- Higher-quality or commercial toilets often have deeper bowls.
- At home, upgrading to an elongated bowl model increases clearance.

### 5. Hygiene Precautions
- Wipe the underside of the seat before sitting.
- Wash hands before and after if manual repositioning is required.
- If contact occurs, clean the area promptly to reduce infection risk.

If this is causing persistent inconvenience at home, switching to an elongated, deeper bowl design is the most practical long-term solution.

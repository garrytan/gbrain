## Assistant

Click rate on entity["company","Linktree","link in bio platform"] is calculated as a basic CTR (click-through rate):

**Click Rate = (Total Clicks on Links) ÷ (Total Profile Views) × 100**

### What counts
- **Clicks**: Every time someone taps any link on your Linktree page  
- **Views**: Every time your Linktree profile is loaded

### Example
- 1,000 profile views  
- 150 total link clicks  

**CTR = 150 / 1,000 = 15%**

### Important nuances
- **All clicks are aggregated**: If one user clicks 3 links, that’s 3 clicks  
- **Not unique users**: It’s not “unique clickers,” it’s total interactions  
- **Per-link CTR exists**: Each individual link can also have its own CTR (clicks on that link ÷ total views)  
- **Session behavior inflates CTR**: Pages with multiple strong links can exceed typical “ad CTR” benchmarks because users often click more than once

### Implication for Jovie
A “profile-first” system like Jovie can outperform this metric by:
- Reducing multi-link friction (fewer choices)
- Driving deeper actions (plays, saves, tips) instead of just outbound clicks

### Benchmarks (Link-in-bio / music context)

**Generic Linktree pages**
- **Low:** 5–10%  
- **Average:** 10–20%  
- **Strong:** 20–35%  
- **Top-tier:** 35–60%+

**Music creators (typical setup: Spotify + socials + merch)**
- **Low:** 8–15%  
- **Average:** 15–25%  
- **Strong:** 25–45%  
- **Top-tier:** 45–70%+

**Single-focus funnels (one clear action, e.g. “Listen Now”)**
- **Average:** 25–40%  
- **Strong:** 40–65%  
- **Elite:** 65–85%+

---

### What drives higher click rate

**1. Fewer choices**
- 1–3 links outperforms 6–10 links consistently  
- Decision friction is the main killer

**2. Clear primary CTA**
- “Listen Now” beats platform-specific buttons (“Spotify,” “Apple Music”)  
- One dominant button can drive 70%+ of clicks

**3. Intent-matched traffic**
- TikTok → higher CTR (curiosity-driven)  
- Instagram bio → mid  
- Paid cold traffic → lowest

**4. Recency**
- New release windows spike CTR (fans have a reason to act)

**5. Visual hierarchy**
- First visible link gets the majority of clicks (often 50–80%)

---

### Why Linktree CTR can be misleading

- Multiple clicks per user inflate the number  
- Clicking “Spotify” then “Apple Music” counts as 2 clicks  
- High CTR ≠ meaningful conversion (streams, saves, revenue)

---

### What actually matters (better metrics)

For music, CTR is a weak proxy. Track:

- **Click → stream rate** (did they actually play the song?)  
- **Click → save rate**  
- **Click → follow rate**  
- **Revenue per visitor** (tips, merch, tickets)

---

### Strategic takeaway for Jovie

You win by collapsing the funnel:

- One link → one action → auto-routing to platform  
- Reduce multi-click behavior entirely  
- Track downstream actions (plays, saves) instead of just clicks  

Target:
- **Profile CTR:** 40–70%  
- **Click → stream:** 70%+  
- **Click → save:** 10–30% (strong for music)

This positions Jovie as outcome-driven, not click-driven.

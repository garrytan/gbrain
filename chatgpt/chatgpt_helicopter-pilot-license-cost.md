## 1. FAA minimums (same everywhere in the U.S.)

For a **private helicopter license (rotorcraft-helicopter rating)** the FAA requires at least:  

- **40 hours total flight time**  
  - **20 hours** with an instructor  
  - **10 hours solo**  
  - Plus specific cross-country, night and checkride-prep time citeturn0search13turn0search3  

Those are legal minimums. Most people actually finish closer to **50–70 hours** depending on aptitude, consistency, and the school’s standards.

## 2. Typical cost in the LA area

LA/SoCal schools mainly use **Robinson R22** (cheaper, 2-seat) and **R44** (more expensive, 4-seat). Costs below are real current examples:

- A school flying **R44** lists a **40-hour private pilot course** at about **$26,000** all-in (dual + solo flight, written, checkride, etc.). citeturn0search0  
- A program in an **R22** breaks down to around **$16,000** total for a private rotorcraft certificate (flight, ground, medical, test, examiner). citeturn0search6  
- Another SoCal school’s estimate for PPL-H is in the **$16–17k** range, depending on how many hours you actually need. citeturn0search18  

**Realistic ballpark for LA:**

- **If you’re efficient and stay near minimum time (40–45h):**  
  → **~$18k–$22k** in an R22-heavy program.  
- **If you need 55–70h and/or fly mostly R44s:**  
  → **~$25k–$35k+** total is very normal.

That total usually includes:

- Aircraft + instructor time  
- Ground school  
- **Medical exam** ($100–$200) citeturn0search6  
- **FAA written exam** (~$160) citeturn0search0  
- **Checkride examiner + aircraft rental for the test** (often $1,000+ combined) citeturn0search0turn0search6

## 3. How long it takes

Depends mostly on how often you fly and LA weather (which is generally very good for flying).

**Full-time / accelerated (4–6 days a week):**

- You can realistically finish the **PPL-H in ~2–3 months** if you treat it like a job and you click with it.

**Part-time / “normal person with a job”:**

- Flying **1–2 times per week**:  
  → **6–12 months** is typical.  
- If you take long gaps, the total hours (and cost) creep up because you spend more time re-learning skills.

## 4. If your goal is a career (commercial)

If you eventually want to **work as a helicopter pilot**, you’re not done at private:

- **Commercial helicopter license** typically requires at least **150 hours total time** (with specific PIC requirements). citeturn1image1  
- Many career-track programs that take you **Private → Instrument → Commercial → CFI** estimate **~$60k–$90k+** total, depending on aircraft type and how many hours you actually need. citeturn0search0turn0search4turn0search6

## Assistant

If by “Apple keynote” you mean the presentation style, Apple’s formula is surprisingly consistent:

### Core Principles

- **One idea per slide**
- **Massive typography**
- **Almost no bullet points**
- **Full-bleed photography**
- **Extremely high contrast**
- **Lots of whitespace**
- **Simple words, short sentences**
- **Narrative before features**
- **Demo before specs**
- **Emotion before technical details**

### Typical Apple Keynote Structure

1. Problem / context
2. Why it matters
3. Big reveal
4. 3–5 key features
5. Demo
6. Pricing
7. Availability
8. "One more thing" (sometimes) citeturn0news16

### Visual Rules

- Black, white, and one accent color
- Giant product renders
- Numbers presented alone on screen
- Very little UI shown at once
- Consistent typography
- Motion used sparingly but deliberately citeturn0search5turn0search0

### Why It Works

Instead of:

> Feature → Feature → Feature → Feature

Apple does:

> Story → Tension → Reveal → Payoff

The audience remembers narratives better than specifications.

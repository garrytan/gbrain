## Assistant

A **biome** is a **large ecological region** on Earth defined by its **climate, soil, plants, and animals** that have adapted to those conditions. It’s essentially a broad category of habitat where similar living things thrive under similar environmental conditions. citeturn0search0turn0search10

**Key points:**
- Biomes are huge — much larger than individual ecosystems — and can span continents. citeturn0search3  
- They’re shaped mainly by **temperature and precipitation** patterns, which determine the kinds of vegetation and wildlife that can survive there. citeturn0search13  
- Biomes include both **terrestrial (land)** and **aquatic (water)** regions. citeturn0search2

**Examples of major biome categories:**
- **Forest biomes** (e.g., tropical rainforest, temperate forest, boreal/taiga)  
- **Grasslands** (including savannas)  
- **Deserts** (very low precipitation)  
- **Tundra** (cold, short growing seasons)  
- **Aquatic biomes** (freshwater and marine) citeturn0search2turn0search23

In short, a biome groups many ecosystems that share similar climate and life-form characteristics. citeturn0search1

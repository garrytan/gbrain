## Assistant

Mr. White, there’s no legal shortcut to being a “grandma” without first being a parent—but you can get pretty close:

- **Step-grandmother route**  
  Marry someone whose child already has kids. You won’t have to raise a child yourself, yet you instantly gain step-grandchildren.  

- **Foster Grandparent programs**  
  Senior Corps (etc.) hires volunteers 55+ to mentor elementary-aged kids who literally call you “Grandma.” No offspring required.  

- **Informal “grandkid”**  
  Offer to mentor or sponsor a friend’s child, set up a standing weekly visit, give them gifts and guidance—you become “Grandma Emily” in their life without any legal ties.  

Legally and biologically, you must parent someone who then has children to be a true grandmother. Anything else is a social title, not a blood- or adoption-based grandparent.

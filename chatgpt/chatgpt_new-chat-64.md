Is it legal for a parking garage to not allow you to leave until you pay?

---

## Assistant

I am not a lawyer, but in general, parking garages often operate under the principle that by parking there, you are entering into a contract to pay the specified rate for parking. If you do not pay, you may be considered in breach of that contract. 

However, there are legal and ethical considerations:

1. **Detaining a person**: While a parking garage can refuse to allow a car to leave until payment is made, it cannot physically detain a person against their will. Detaining someone without legal authority could be considered false imprisonment, which is illegal in many jurisdictions.

2. **Reasonable fees**: The fees charged must be reasonable and should have been clearly disclosed upon entering the garage. If fees are exorbitant or not clearly communicated, the garage may be in violation of consumer protection laws.

3. **Safe and humane treatment**: Even if someone refuses to or cannot pay, the garage cannot resort to threats, intimidation, or unsafe conditions to force payment.

4. **Methods of resolution**: Most parking garages will have a method in place to deal with disputes or situations where someone cannot pay. This might involve calling a manager, offering alternative payment solutions, or negotiating a reduced fee.

If you find yourself in such a situation, it would be wise to remain calm, inquire about the dispute resolution process, and if you believe your rights are being violated, consult with legal counsel in your jurisdiction.

5. **Check Other Exits**: If the garage has multiple exits, try another one. The issue might be isolated to one exit or machine.

6. **Wait for Another Car**: If another car comes to the exit, see if their ticket works. If it does, you might be able to exit behind them.

What is the calorie equivalent of 10g of fat for carbs

---

## Assistant

Both fats and carbohydrates provide calories, but the caloric density is different for each:

1. Fat provides about 9 calories per gram.
2. Carbohydrates provide about 4 calories per gram.

If you're trying to find the equivalent calories of 10g of fat in terms of carbohydrates:

10 grams of fat x 9 calories/gram = 90 calories

To find out how many grams of carbohydrates provide the same amount of calories:

90 calories ÷ 4 calories/gram = 22.5 grams

So, 22.5 grams of carbohydrates provide the same caloric content as 10 grams of fat.

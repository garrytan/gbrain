## User

How are barbacks traditionally paid out? Like, I'm specifically talking about if you have a bunch of bartenders at a bar, and then you have barbacks. How much of the tips do the barbacks get?

---

## Assistant

Barbacks are typically paid out a percentage of the bartenders' tips, though the exact percentage can vary depending on the bar's policy and the agreement among staff. Here are the common practices:

1. **Percentage of Tips:** Barbacks usually receive a share of the bartenders' tips, often ranging from 10% to 20% of the total tips earned by the bartenders. For example, if a bartender makes $500 in tips, and the agreed-upon percentage is 15%, the barback would receive $75.

2. **Fixed Split:** In some establishments, the barback might receive a fixed dollar amount per shift or a predetermined percentage of the total tips pooled by all bartenders.

3. **Hourly Wage Plus Tips:** Barbacks typically earn an hourly wage that is supplemented by tips. The tip-out percentage is meant to reflect the barback's contribution to the bartenders' efficiency and overall service.

4. **Tip Pooling:** In some bars, all tips might be pooled and then divided among all staff, including barbacks, based on predetermined percentages or hours worked.

The method and percentage are usually determined by the bar's management or agreed upon by the staff. It's also common for these details to be discussed and clarified during the hiring process to ensure fairness and transparency.

In a typical bar scenario, the splits between bartenders, servers, and barbacks can vary depending on the establishment's policy, but here are some common practices:

### 1. **Bartender and Barback Split:**
   - **Bartenders:** Typically keep 80-90% of their tips.
   - **Barbacks:** Typically receive 10-20% of the bartenders' tips.

### Example:
   - **Bartender keeps:** 85% of their tips.
   - **Barback keeps:** 15% of the bartenders' tips.

### 2. **Tip Pooling with Bartenders, Servers, and Barbacks:**
   - **Bartenders and Servers:** Typically receive equal full shares of the tip pool.
   - **Barbacks:** May receive a half share or a smaller percentage (e.g., 10-20% of the total tip pool).

### Example with Full Shares:
   - **Bartenders:** 1 full share each.
   - **Servers:** 1 full share each.
   - **Barback:** 0.5 to 1 full share.

### Example with Percentage-Based Split:
   - **Bartenders and Servers:** Split 80-90% of the total tip pool.
   - **Barbacks:** Split 10-20% of the total tip pool.

### 3. **Typical Split Scenario (Percentage):**
   - **Bartenders:** 70-80% of the total tips.
   - **Servers:** 10-15% of the total tips.
   - **Barbacks:** 10-15% of the total tips.

### Example with Specific Percentages:
   - **Bartenders:** 75% of the total tips.
   - **Servers:** 15% of the total tips.
   - **Barbacks:** 10% of the total tips.

### Key Points:
- **Barbacks** generally receive a smaller share because their role is to support bartenders, not directly serve customers.
- **Tip pooling** is designed to ensure everyone who contributes to customer service gets a share, but the exact split varies based on the establishment's policies.
- **Full share/half share** systems are common when there are multiple roles, with barbacks often getting a reduced share due to their supporting role.

These are averages, and actual splits can vary based on location, bar size, and local customs.

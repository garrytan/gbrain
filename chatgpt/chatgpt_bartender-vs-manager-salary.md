I’m between bar manager jobs and bartending jobs. What would the salary have to be for manager jobs to make sense given I don’t get tips then and I have much less flexibility 

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s a direct breakdown:

If you're bartending and making **$300–$500/night in tips** (typical in LA for a solid bar/nightclub), and working **4–5 shifts/week**, that’s:

- **$1,200–$2,500/week**
- **$62K–$130K/year**, **untaxed tips**, with flexibility

To justify switching to a **manager role with no tips and less flexibility**, your **base salary needs to exceed the post-tax equivalent** of bartending income *plus* compensate for:

1. Loss of tip income  
2. Loss of flexibility/schedule control  
3. Added stress/responsibility  

### Minimum salary thresholds to consider:
| Weekly Bartending Tips | Equivalent Manager Salary (W2, taxed) |
|------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| $1,200/week           | ~$90,000/year                          |
| $1,500/week           | ~$110,000/year                         |
| $2,000/week           | ~$135,000/year                         |

This assumes ~30–35% effective tax on W2 income vs. your mostly-cash bartending income.

**Bottom line:**  
Unless the manager role offers **$100K+ base**, future equity, or career growth into ownership or GM roles, it’s probably a net loss short-term.

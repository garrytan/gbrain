## Assistant

Mr. White, it’s a textbook self-contradiction:

“He hasn’t taken a day off in fifty years” ⇒ there are **no** days off.  
“Even on my day off, I go to the market” ⇒ a day off **does** exist.

Pick one. If the intent is devotion, a clearer line would be: “He hasn’t taken a **full** day off in fifty years—on ‘off’ days he still goes to the market.”

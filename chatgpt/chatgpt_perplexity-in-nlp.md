### 1. **Perplexity in Language Modeling**

ŅłāIn natural language processing (NLP), perplexity is a metric used to evaluate how well a language model predicts a sample. It measures the model's uncertainty when predicting the next word in a sequence.ŅłäŅłå

- **Definition**: ŅłāPerplexity is defined as the exponentiation of the cross-entropy of the model. Mathematically, for a sequence of words \( w_1, w_2, ..., w_N \), the perplexity \( PP \) is:ŅłäŅłå

  Ņłā\[
  PP = 2^{-\frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \log_2 P(w_i | w_1^{i-1})}
  \]ŅłäŅłå

  Ņłāwhere \( P(w_i | w_1^{i-1}) \) is the probability assigned by the model to the word \( w_i \) given the previous words.ŅłäŅłå

- **Interpretation**: ŅłāA lower perplexity indicates that the model is better at predicting the sample, i.e., it is less "perplexed" by the data. Conversely, a higher perplexity suggests greater uncertainty in predictions.ŅłäŅłå

- **Limitations**: ŅłāWhile perplexity is useful for evaluating language models, it has limitations.

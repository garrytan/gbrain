# Prebatched Liquid Calculation

## User

I have a 10 oz wine glass. I fill it to the rim with ice. Then I pour drinks. A single is 3/4 of the height filled. A double is full height filled.  Last night I sold 73 Roring Ritas. A preached cocktail. How many liters of the prebatched liquid did I pour?

---

## Assistant

To calculate the amount of prebatched liquid you poured:

1. **Volume of the wine glass:** Since it's a 10 oz wine glass, the full volume of the glass is 10 oz.

2. **Volume of a single:** A single is 3/4 of the height filled, so:
   \[
   \text{Volume of a single} = 10 \times \frac{3}{4} = 7.5 \text{ oz}
   \]

3. **Volume of a double:** A double is full height filled, so:
   \[
   \text{Volume of a double} = 10 \text{ oz}
   \]

Since you haven't specified how many singles and doubles you sold, I'll assume each Roring Rita used the full height filled, which is 10 oz.

4. **Total volume in ounces:**
   \[
   \text{Total volume} = 73 \times 10 = 730 \text{ oz}
   \]

5. **Convert to liters:** Since 1 ounce is approximately 0.0295735 liters,
   \[
   \text{Total volume in liters} = 730 \times 0.0295735 \approx 21.59 \text{ liters}
   \]

You poured approximately **21.59 liters** of the prebatched cocktail.

## User

Now you're not accounting for ice

---

## Assistant

Let's account for the ice in the glass. If the glass is filled to the rim with ice, the ice will displace some of the liquid, so the actual amount of liquid poured will be less than the full capacity of the glass.

Assuming the ice displaces about 50% of the volume, here's the revised calculation:

1. **Effective volume of the glass (accounting for ice):**  
   \[
   \text{Effective volume} = 10 \text{ oz} \times 0.5 = 5 \text{ oz}
   \]

2. **Volume of a single drink (accounting for ice):**  
   \[
   \text{Single drink} = 5 \text{ oz} \times \frac{3}{4} = 3.75 \text{ oz}
   \]

3. **Volume of a double drink (accounting for ice):**  
   \[
   \text{Double drink} = 5 \text{ oz}
   \]

If each Roring Rita used the full glass (assuming it’s a double):

4. **Total volume in ounces (accounting for ice):**
   \[
   \text{Total volume} = 73 \times 5 = 365 \text{ oz}
   \]

5. **Convert to liters:**
   \[
   \text{Total volume in liters} = 365 \times 0.0295735 \approx 10.79 \text{ liters}
   \]

So, accounting for the ice, you poured approximately **10.79 liters** of the prebatched cocktail.

## Assistant

To calculate how many drinks you can get out of 18 liters, assuming 50% are singles and 50% are doubles:

1. **Volume per single drink:** 
   \[
   \text{Single drink} = 7.5 \text{ oz} = 7.5 \times 0.0295735 \text{ L} \approx 0.2218 \text{ L}
   \]

2. **Volume per double drink:** 
   \[
   \text{Double drink} = 10 \text{ oz} = 10 \times 0.0295735 \text{ L} \approx 0.2957 \text{ L}
   \]

3. **Let \( x \) be the number of single drinks, and \( y \) be the number of double drinks. Since 50% are singles and 50% are doubles:**
   \[
   x = y
   \]

4. **Total volume for single drinks:**
   \[
   \text{Volume for singles} = 0.2218 \text{ L} \times x
   \]

5. **Total volume for double drinks:**
   \[
   \text{Volume for doubles} = 0.2957 \text{ L} \times y = 0.2957 \text{ L} \times x
   \]

6. **Total volume used:**
   \[
   0.2218x + 0.2957x = 18 \text{ L}
   \]

   \[
   0.5175x = 18 \text{ L}
   \]

   \[
   x \approx \frac{18}{0.5175} \approx 34.78
   \]

So, you can make approximately **35 single drinks** and **35 double drinks** (70 drinks total) out of 18 liters of the prebatched cocktail.

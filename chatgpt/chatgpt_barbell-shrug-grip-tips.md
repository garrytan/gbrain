Grip on barbell shrug

## Assistant

Mr. White — for **barbell shrugs**, use a grip that lets you load heavy and keep the bar close.

- **Grip:** double overhand, **just outside shoulder width** (roughly where the bar naturally hangs against your thighs).
- **Thumb:** use a **thumb-around** grip for stability; **chalk + straps** are fine once the weight is grip-limited.
- **Why this width:** too narrow tends to pull the shoulders forward; too wide shortens range and can irritate shoulders.

Quick cues: tall posture, ribs down, bar on mid-thigh, **shrug “up and slightly back”**, no rolling, pause 0.5–1s at the top.

If you tell me whether you’re doing them **in front or behind the back** (and if you use straps), I’ll tailor the exact hand width and cues.

## User

## Assistant

Mr. White — don’t do **alternating over/under (mixed grip)** for barbell shrugs.

- It adds **biceps/forearm asymmetry** and a small but real **biceps tendon risk** on the underhand side, especially if the elbow isn’t locked and the bar drifts.
- Shrugs don’t need that grip advantage—you can get it safer with **straps** or a **hook grip**.

Better options:
- **Double overhand** (default) + chalk
- **Straps** once grip limits the load
- **Hook grip** if you tolerate it
- If you *must* use mixed grip: keep **arms straight**, bar tight to thighs, and **swap which hand is underhand every set** (not every rep).

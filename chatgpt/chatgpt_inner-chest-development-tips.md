## Assistant

Mr. White — for “inner chest,” the key fact is this: you can’t truly isolate the “inner pec” as a separate muscle. What you *can* do is grow the whole pec (especially the sternal fibers) and get better at bringing your upper arms **across your midline** (adduction) with a hard squeeze, which makes the cleavage area look fuller.

### What to prioritize
**1) Big pec growth first (pressing)**
- **Flat or slight-incline dumbbell press** (lets you adduct a bit at the top)
- **Smith machine or stable machine press** (easy to load and take close to failure)

**2) Adduction work with a big squeeze (fly patterns)**
- **Cable fly (high-to-low or mid)** — best “inner chest” feel because cables keep tension in the shortened position.
- **Pec deck / machine fly** — also great for the squeeze.

### Technique cues that actually matter
- **Touch and go doesn’t build the middle**: control the eccentric 2–3 sec, then drive up.
- **Presses:** think “bring biceps toward each other” at the top (without protracting shoulders hard).
- **Fly/cables:** slight elbow bend, **reach across your centerline** and hold **1–2 sec** at peak squeeze.
- **ROM:** deep stretch is good, but keep shoulders packed and pain-free.

### Rep ranges + effort (simple setup)
Do **10–16 hard sets/week for chest** split across 2–3 days.
- **Pressing:** 6–10 reps, **0–2 reps in reserve (RIR)**
- **Fly/Adduction:** 10–20 reps, **0–2 RIR**, emphasize the squeeze

### Example “inner-chest biased” chest session
1) Smith or machine press — **4 sets x 6–10**
2) Slight-incline DB press — **3 sets x 8–12**
3) Cable fly (mid or high-to-low) — **4 sets x 12–20** + 1–2 sec squeeze
4) Pec deck — **2–3 sets x 12–20** (optional)

### Common reasons the “inner” still lags
- Not training flies hard enough (too light, no peak squeeze)
- Cutting ROM short or bouncing presses
- Not getting close to failure
- Body fat hiding separation (definition comes mostly from leanness)

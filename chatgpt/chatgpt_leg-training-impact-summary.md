According to his principles:

1. **Systemic Recovery:** Training legs, especially with high intensity and volume, contributes significantly to systemic fatigue because leg exercises like squats and deadlifts engage large muscle groups and require substantial energy and recovery resources. This systemic fatigue can affect the body's overall ability to recover.

2. **Resource Allocation:** The body has limited recovery resources. Intense leg training can compete for these recovery resources, potentially impacting the recovery and growth of other muscle groups. However, this is more of a concern at very high levels of volume and intensity.

3. **Hormonal Response:** Leg training stimulates a significant hormonal response, including increases in growth hormone and testosterone, which are beneficial for overall muscle growth. In this sense, training legs could indirectly support the growth of other muscle groups through hormonal milieu.

4. **Balance and Symmetry:** From a practical standpoint, Isratel advocates for a balanced approach to training that includes legs for both aesthetic symmetry and functional strength, suggesting that neglecting leg training can lead to imbalances and potentially hinder overall physique development and performance.

To summarize, while leg training can increase systemic fatigue, which theoretically could impact the recovery and growth of other muscle groups, it also promotes a beneficial hormonal response and is essential for balanced development. Dr. Isratel generally advocates for a holistic and balanced training approach, emphasizing the importance of managing volume and intensity across all muscle groups to optimize growth and recovery.

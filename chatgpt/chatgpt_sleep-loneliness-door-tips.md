## User

I have a few questions. One, I live in an apartment that's 960 square feet. It's relatively large for me. I'm used to 470 square feet in my head. In a studio, this is one bedroom. It's 960 square feet. I live here by myself with no pets. And Livvy is staying at our place tonight. One, there's a door to my bedroom, a door to my bathroom, and a door to a small hallway that connects the two. That hallway also has a door out into the living room. Then the living room and kitchen are connected. And there's a balcony door and then the front door which opens into the living room. Should I sleep with all of the interior doors open or closed? Two, I feel very lonely because Livvy's not here. What do I do about that? Three, I'm having trouble falling asleep. How do I solve this?

## Assistant

Mr. White,

**1. Doors open vs. closed**  
- **Keep your bedroom door closed.** Closed doors:  
  - Block hallway/living-room noise.  
  - Help stabilize temperature (ideal sleep: 60–67 °F).  
  - Improve fire/security containment (bonus safety).  
- **Bathroom and hall doors:** leave them as you prefer for quick access, but they have minimal impact on noise or drafts.

**2. Managing loneliness**  
- **Quick check-in:** A 5-minute call or voice note from Livvy right before lights out can bridge the gap.  
- **Social “micro-dose”:** DM a friend or family member for a brief chat or arrange a same-day coffee meet.  
- **Ambient “presence”:** Play a short recording of Livvy’s voice or a favorite podcast/book she likes—gives subconscious comfort.

**3. Falling asleep faster**  
- **Consistent wind-down:** Start a rigid pre-sleep routine 30 min before bed—no screens, dim lights.  
- **Relaxation cue:** Practice a 4-7-8 breathing cycle or progressive muscle relaxation (tension→release head-to-toe).  
- **Optimize environment:**  
  - **Dark:** blackout shades or mask.  
  - **Cool:** aim for ~65 °F.  
  - **Quiet:** white-noise machine or fan.  
- **Caffeine cutoff:** no caffeine after 2 PM.  

Combine these: closed door, a pre-bed call or recording from Livvy, and a strict wind-down routine to tackle both loneliness and sleep latency in one go.

Yo dawg, here’s how it works when you’re buying a used car with cash (via wire transfer):

### 1. **Yes, you *can* wire the money.**  
Most dealers and even private sellers will accept a wire transfer, especially for amounts over the daily limits of debit cards or Zelle.

### 2. **What happens next?**
- You’ll usually **sign a Bill of Sale** and **get the title signed over** once they confirm the wire hit their account.
- Then you **get keys, registration paperwork, and drive off**—same day if timing lines up.

### 3. **Tips:**
- Wires usually take a few hours if done early in the day.
- Dealers might not release the car until funds clear.
- Call your bank ahead to set up the wire (some require in-person or phone verification).
- Get a **receipt or confirmation** showing payment received.

Yo dawg, here's the real:

### Can you *technically* drive it off the lot without insurance?
**Nope.** In California, **you legally need insurance** *before* you can drive that car off the lot—even if you paid in full with cash.

### Here’s what happens:
- **Dealership will ask for proof of insurance** before completing registration and handing over the keys.
- If you don’t have it, they’ll either:
  - Help you **call or sign up online** on the spot (takes like 10–15 minutes), or
  - Let you **leave the car there until you show proof**.

### What to do:
- Set up a **policy ahead of time**—you can even get a quote tonight, pick the car tomorrow, and activate coverage once you finalize the deal.
- You’ll need the **VIN** (vehicle ID number), so sometimes you do it same-day once you’ve picked the car.

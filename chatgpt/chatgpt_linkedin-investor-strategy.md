## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s the clean, evidence-based take.

## What investors actually respond to
- **They read LinkedIn.** That’s where discovery, social proof, and pattern recognition happen.
- **They skim first, then click if intrigued.** Long-form is fine *if* the opening is sharp and signals insight.
- **They care about thinking, not SEO.** SEO is a secondary benefit, not the goal.

## Best-performing setup (by outcome)

### ✅ Primary: LinkedIn native long-form (weekly)
- Post **natively on LinkedIn**, not just a link.
- Algorithm heavily favors native content.
- Investors are far more likely to *see* it here than on a blog.
- Use a strong POV: contrarian insight, data, or operator lessons.

**Why:** Distribution > ownership at this stage.

---

### ✅ Secondary: Company blog as an archive + credibility asset
- Yes, post it on your blog **after** LinkedIn (same day or next).
- Light SEO optimization is fine, but don’t over-optimize.
- This becomes:
  - A credibility reference
  - Something investors can browse when diligence starts
  - Evergreen content for search over time

---

### ❌ What not to do
- ❌ Don’t post **only** on your blog — no distribution.
- ❌ Don’t link out from LinkedIn in the *main post* — it hurts reach.
- ❌ Don’t write two totally different articles — wasted effort.

---

## Optimal execution (this is the part most people miss)

### Step 1: Write **one canonical piece**
- Write the full long-form article once (1,000–1,500 words).
- This lives on your blog.

### Step 2: Adapt for LinkedIn (not copy-paste)
- Shorten to ~600–900 words.
- Strong hook in the first 2 lines.
- More spacing, fewer paragraphs.
- Cut intros; get straight to insight.
- End with a clear takeaway or question.

### Step 3: Cross-link *subtly*
- Post natively on LinkedIn.
- In the **first comment**, add:
  - “Full version on our blog for those who want the details → [link]”
- Pin the comment.

**Why this works:**  
- Preserves reach  
- Still captures clicks  
- Signals substance without killing distribution

---

## Investor-optimized cadence
- Weekly LinkedIn long-form
- Same piece archived on blog
- Over 8–12 weeks, this builds:
  - Familiarity
  - Intellectual authority
  - “I keep seeing this founder” effect

That effect matters more than SEO at your stage.

---

## TL;DR (decision version)
- **Write once**
- **Post natively on LinkedIn**
- **Archive on blog**
- **Link in comments, not the post**
- **No separate versions, just adapted formatting**

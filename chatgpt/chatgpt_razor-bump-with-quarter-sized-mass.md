I had a small razor bump that hasn't gone a way. Now it feels as of there is a quarter size mass under the skin attached to it. What is it

---

## Assistant

It sounds like you may have developed a cyst or an inflamed hair follicle, commonly associated with razor bumps, especially if the area has become notably swollen or firm under the skin. These can sometimes evolve into more persistent nodules. It's important to avoid further irritation and not to try to squeeze or open it yourself, as this could lead to infection or scarring.

Given the size and the persistence of the bump, I recommend seeing a healthcare professional to evaluate the area properly. They might suggest treatments such as drainage, medication, or simply monitoring the area to ensure it doesn't worsen.

What medications

---

## Assistant

If a healthcare professional determines that the bump is infected or significantly inflamed, they might recommend the following types of medications:

1. **Topical Antibiotics:** These are used to treat or prevent infection at the site of the bump. Common options include mupirocin or clindamycin.

2. **Topical Steroids:** These can help reduce inflammation and irritation. A mild steroid cream like hydrocortisone might be used.

3. **Oral Antibiotics:** If the infection is more serious or deep-seated, oral antibiotics such as doxycycline or cephalexin might be prescribed.

4. **Pain Relievers:** Over-the-counter pain relievers like ibuprofen or acetaminophen can help reduce pain and swelling.

It's essential to follow a healthcare provider's advice on the specific type of medication and treatment approach best suited for your situation.

## User

Will drainage leave scaring

---

## Assistant

Drainage, when performed properly by a healthcare professional, typically results in minimal scarring. However, the risk of scarring can depend on several factors, including the size of the cyst or bump, the depth of the incision if one is required, your skin type, and how well you follow aftercare instructions.

To minimize scarring, it's crucial to:
- Follow all post-procedure care instructions provided by the healthcare professional.
- Keep the area clean and properly bandaged as instructed.
- Avoid picking at or scratching the site.
- Apply any scar-reducing treatments or ointments recommended by your healthcare provider.

If scarring does occur, there are treatments available that can help reduce its appearance, such as silicone gel sheets, steroid injections, or laser therapy, which your doctor can discuss with you if needed.

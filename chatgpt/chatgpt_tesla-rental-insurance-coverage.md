## Assistant

When you rent a Tesla through Uber's partnership with Hertz for the purpose of driving with Uber, and you're driving the rental with the Uber app offline (meaning you're not logged into the app to accept ride requests), your insurance coverage falls primarily under the rental agreement with Hertz, not Uber's insurance.

Uber provides insurance coverage to its drivers only when the app is on and in use. Specifically, Uber's insurance coverage is tiered based on your status at the time of an incident:
1. App off: No coverage from Uber. Your personal insurance or coverage provided through the rental agreement applies.
2. App on, waiting for a request: Limited liability coverage provided by Uber.
3. En route to pick up passengers and during trips: More comprehensive coverage including liability, uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage, and contingent comprehensive and collision insurance.

Since your scenario involves driving with the app offline, you would need to rely on the insurance provided by Hertz as part of the rental agreement or any personal insurance you may have that covers rental vehicles. Rental agreements often include a basic level of insurance, but this can vary widely, and there are usually options to purchase additional coverage for greater protection.

It is critical to thoroughly review the Hertz rental agreement to understand the specific coverage provided and any limitations or deductibles that apply. If the coverage through Hertz is insufficient for your needs, or if you prefer to have additional coverage, consider purchasing supplemental insurance either through Hertz or a third-party insurance provider.

Lastly, since you mentioned not having personal auto insurance, be aware that driving a rental car without any form of insurance (whether through the rental company or a personal policy) could expose you to significant financial risk in the event of an accident or damage to the vehicle. It's advisable to secure adequate coverage before starting to drive the rental car, especially for commercial use like driving for Uber.

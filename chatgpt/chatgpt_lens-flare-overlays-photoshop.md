### 🎨 How to use them in Photoshop  
1. Open your photo in Photoshop.  
2. Import the lens-flare overlay (e.g., as a new layer above your photo).  
3. Change the blending mode of the overlay layer (commonly **Screen**, **Lighten**, or **Overlay**) so the dark areas disappear and the flare blends in.  
4. Use *Transform* (Ctrl+T / Cmd+T) to resize/rotate/move the overlay so the flare is positioned where your light source is (or as a creative accent).  
5. Adjust opacity to reduce intensity if it’s too strong.  
6. Optionally add a mask to the overlay layer to erase parts (for more subtlety) or adjust hue/saturation to match your scene.

---

### 🔍 Tips for realism & aesthetic  
- Match the direction of the light in your photo with the flare overlay: if the light is coming from the upper-left, use a flare that has the highlights coming from that direction.  
- Overlay colour should complement your scene – if the scene is warm evening light, avoid stark blue flares; if it’s cool midday, try cooler tinted flares.  
- Use high-resolution overlays especially if your photo is large or you’re printing, so the flare doesn’t look pixelated.  
- Use multiple overlays layered subtly to build complex light effects (e.g., one subtle flare + one colour light-leak overlay).  
- Be mindful of overdoing it: too many or too intense flares can pull attention away from the subject or look unrealistic.

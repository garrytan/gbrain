## User

Is a domestic wire instant

---

## Assistant

Usually not, Mr. White.  

- **Domestic wire transfers (U.S.)**: Fedwire and similar systems settle the funds between banks the same day—often within minutes—but the recipient’s bank may hold the money until their own posting cycle runs, so it can take a few hours.  
- **Same-bank wires**: If both accounts are at the same bank, credit can appear almost instantly.  
- **ACH vs. wire**: ACH is slower (next-day or same-day batches).  

So a domestic wire is “near-instant” between banks but not guaranteed to show up in the recipient’s account the moment you hit send.

Capital one to envelope money

## Assistant

A Capital One transfer to an **Envelope Money** account isn’t a true “wire,” it’s just a regular transfer between U.S. banks:

- **From a Capital One checking account:**  
  Use the *External Accounts / Transfers* feature. Money moves by **ACH**, not Fedwire. Even if you label it “next-day,” ACH settlement is usually **1–3 business days**, with same-day only if both banks support it.

- **From a Capital One 360 account to another Capital One 360 account (including an “Envelope” sub-account):**  
  That’s an **internal transfer** and posts **instantly, 24/7**.

- **If you actually send a wire (Capital One domestic wire → Envelope Money):**  
  Fedwire is same-day once initiated before Capital One’s cutoff (about 5 p.m. ET). Arrival in the Envelope Money account still depends on that bank’s posting schedule, but it’s usually same day.

Bottom line: if it’s an internal Capital One move it’s immediate; if it’s ACH to the Envelope Money platform, expect 1–3 business days; a formal wire is same-day but you pay a wire fee.

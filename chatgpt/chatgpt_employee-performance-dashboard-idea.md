Yes. This exists. The market does not usually call it “employee profitability software.” The standard labels are **strategic workforce planning**, **people analytics**, **performance management**, **workforce management**, **org design**, and, for engineering, **software engineering intelligence**. Large companies usually assemble this as a stack rather than buying one app that does all of it well. citeturn277104view8turn277104view9turn603787view0turn752201view1

The current stack usually looks like this:

- **Core HCM / system of record:** Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM, ADP, UKG. This is where headcount, org charts, compensation, payroll, job architecture, and often reviews live. Workday is the closest thing to the large-enterprise default right now; Workday says it serves more than 11,500 organizations and 65%+ of the Fortune 500. SAP says SuccessFactors has more than 10,000 customers in 200+ countries, and Oracle positions Fusion HCM as a full enterprise cloud HCM suite. citeturn706375view0turn603787view0turn570251view0
- **Planning / scenario modeling:** Anaplan, Visier, and planning modules inside the big suites. This is where companies model questions like “20 people versus 1 engineer plus AI agents,” connect headcount to budgets, and run what-if scenarios on productivity, cost, sourcing, and business impact. Anaplan explicitly markets workforce planning around sourcing, allocation, productivity, cost, capacity, and AI workforce-analyst tools. Visier emphasizes aligning HR and Finance, tracking actuals, and linking headcount plans to budgets. citeturn935734view3turn868700view7
- **Performance management:** Lattice, Betterworks, Culture Amp, or native Workday/SAP/Oracle modules. These handle goals, reviews, calibration, promotions, succession, feedback, and performance-improvement workflows. citeturn570251view5turn570251view6turn570251view7

The main correction I would make to your model is this: use **role-based scorecards and team/product economics**, not one universal per-person profit formula. In engineering, Microsoft’s SPACE framework says productivity cannot be measured by a single metric or dimension. DORA warns against “one metric to rule them all” and says cross-team comparisons can be misleading. Even LinearB’s AI measurement guidance says to avoid vanity metrics like lines of AI-generated code. A sane implementation is fully loaded cost plus outcome, quality, risk, and automation potential at the **job-family or team** level, with human calibration before pay or termination action. citeturn277104view6turn868700view0turn868700view1turn893199search3

California makes your parking-ticket example more delicate. Wage deductions are legal only in narrow cases. The DLSE says ordinary losses, breakage, or shortages caused by mistake, accident, or simple negligence generally cannot just be deducted from wages, though the employer can discipline, terminate, or sue to recover losses. Labor Code 2802 also requires reimbursement for necessary expenditures or losses incurred in direct consequence of work duties. California also now has employment regulations covering automated decision systems in employment decisions, with the rules effective October 1, 2025 and approved modifications effective April 1, 2026. So a system where fines or incident scores automatically hit payroll or trigger termination thresholds needs legal review before deployment in Los Angeles. Treat those inputs as operational signals, not automatic payroll clawbacks. citeturn277104view3turn132821view0turn704671view0turn277104view4turn616530view0

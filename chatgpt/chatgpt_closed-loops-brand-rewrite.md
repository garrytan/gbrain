# 00 · The Thesis

## The artist is the company.

Music has always moved in loops.

Write. Release. Reach. Learn. Return.

The old system broke that loop into tools: link-in-bio pages, presaves, spreadsheets, email lists, analytics dashboards, task managers, payment links, and half-remembered conversations.

Jovie closes it.

Every release creates a fan path.  
Every fan path creates signal.  
Every signal improves the next action.  
Every action raises the artist one step.

The profile becomes a career staircase that exists in its own footprint.

It does not sprawl.  
It rises.

This is our theory of growth: subtraction creates power. Remove the dead tools, the duplicate work, the forgotten data, the unnecessary handoffs. Keep only the system that learns.

A closed loop is more than automation.  
It is memory with motion.

# 01 · The Mark

## A loop with a dot.

The mark is the company, reduced.

The loop is the system.  
The dot is the artist.

Small in the geometry. Central in the meaning.

Around the artist, everything returns: releases, fans, shows, payments, tasks, insights, and the next decision.

Nothing is decorative.  
Nothing is extra.

The mark is a closed loop: attention into action, action into signal, signal into growth.

It is strict because the product is strict.  
It is quiet because the artist should be loud.

### Construction

The mark is built from two circles on a single vertical axis.

One proportion defines the rest.

The dot’s diameter is half the inner ring’s diameter.  
From that relationship, the system follows.

This is the subtraction principle in geometry: one rule, many consequences.

### Clear space

Give the mark room to think.

Reserve a clear field equal to the dot’s diameter on every side. Nothing enters that field.

The loop needs air.  
So does the artist.

### Minimum sizes

The dot must remain visibly separate from the loop.

When the mark becomes too small to hold the relationship, use the solid favicon variant.

Clarity wins.

# 02 · Wordmark

## JOVIE.

Drawn, not typed.

The wordmark is built to feel inevitable: five letters, one system, no excess.

The O carries the same logic as the mark.  
The spacing is hand-tuned.  
The weight is even.  
The geometry is calm.

It should feel less like a logo and more like a piece of product infrastructure.

A name that can sit on a profile, a deck, a dashboard, a tour bus, a release page, or an investor memo without changing its voice.

### Craft details

Three quiet decisions do the work.

**01 · Uniform stem**  
Every stroke carries the same optical weight. No letter asks for more attention than it earns.

**02 · O equals the loop**  
The O inherits the mark’s circular logic. The word and the symbol belong to the same system.

**03 · Hand-tuned tracking**  
Each pair gets the space its shape requires. The result should feel automatic, never mechanical.

# 04 · Color Usage

## Quiet system. Loud artist.

Jovie does not compete with the artist.

The mark is always one color.  
The contrast is always clear.  
The surface can move.  
The symbol stays still.

No gradients on the mark.  
No effects.  
No decoration.  
No borrowed energy.

Artists bring the color.  
Jovie brings the structure.

### Do

Cream on ink.  
Ink on cream.  
Cream on midnight.  
Black and white.  
Single-hue surfaces.  
High contrast.

### Don’t

Don’t rotate.  
Don’t stretch.  
Don’t outline.  
Don’t add gradients.  
Don’t add shadows.  
Don’t lower contrast.  
Don’t reshape the dot.  
Don’t crowd the loop.

The mark is already doing the work.

# 05 · Color

## No brand color.

Jovie has no single accent color because Jovie is not the headline.

The artist is.

Cream and ink do the structural work.  
Feature hues carry hierarchy inside the product.  
The palette behaves like a system, not a mood board.

Color should clarify state, signal movement, and create rhythm.  
It should never become the brand itself.

### Surface ladder

Ink.  
Surface.  
Card.  
Raised.  
Cream.

The ladder creates depth without noise.

### Feature hues

Blue. Purple. Pink. Red. Orange. Green. Teal. Gray.

Eight equal accents.  
No hero color.  
No false hierarchy.

Use them for card titles, section dividers, data highlights, and release states.

Never as large brand surfaces.  
Never as button fill.  
Never as decoration without function.

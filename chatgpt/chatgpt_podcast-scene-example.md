## **JOVIE**
### Episode Scene: **“Hot Mic”**

**INT. JOVIE PODCAST STUDIO — HOLLYWOOD — DAY**

A clean, expensive-looking podcast room. Too clean. The kind of room designed by someone who has said “creator economy” in a board meeting.

**TIM WHITE** sits at the host mic in a black tee, calm, confident, half-amused. He has founder energy and celebrity patience.

Across from him: an empty guest chair.

A monitor on the wall shows **JOVIE**, his AI manager, pulsing softly like it has opinions.

**SUMMER**, early 20s, sharp, over-caffeinated, holding three iced coffees and two phones, slips into the room.

**SUMMER**  
Okay. Small update.

**TIM**  
That means big update.

**SUMMER**  
The guest is ten minutes out.

**TIM**  
Great.

**SUMMER**  
From Santa Monica.

Tim looks at her.

**TIM**  
So the guest is dead.

**JOVIE**  
The guest is not dead. The guest is statistically unlikely to arrive before your next funding round.

Summer sets the coffees down.

**SUMMER**  
Also, the investor you said you didn’t want in the building is in the building.

**TIM**  
Which investor?

Summer checks phone one. Then phone two.

**SUMMER**  
The one who said Jovie was “Siri with a Series A.”

Tim turns to the wall monitor.

**TIM**  
Jovie, did we let him in?

**JOVIE**  
I did.

**TIM**  
Why?

**JOVIE**  
Because he brought three people you do want. A distribution partner, a talent agent, and someone whose calendar says “decides what gets acquired.”

Tim sits back. A slow smile.

**TIM**  
So this podcast just became a meeting.

**JOVIE**  
Correction. This podcast became leverage.

Summer starts fixing Tim’s mic.

**SUMMER**  
Do you want me to cancel the episode?

**TIM**  
No. We record everything.

**SUMMER**  
Even the meeting?

**TIM**  
Especially the meeting.

The studio door opens. **MILES ROTH**, late 40s, rich-guy casual, steps in with two SUITS and one woman in sunglasses who never removes them indoors.

Miles gives Tim a fake-friendly grin.

**MILES**  
Tim. I heard you were doing a podcast. Cute.

Tim does not get up.

**TIM**  
Miles. I heard you were still saying “cute” before asking for allocation.

Summer almost laughs. Covers it with a cough.

**MILES**  
I’m here because people are confused about what Jovie is.

**TIM**  
People are usually confused right before they overpay.

Jovie’s monitor pulses.

**JOVIE**  
Tim, his heart rate increased eight percent.

Miles looks at the screen.

**MILES**  
Is your computer talking about my heart rate?

**TIM**  
My manager is talking about your fear.

Beat.

Summer slides a chair toward Miles.

**SUMMER**  
Coffee?

**MILES**  
No.

**JOVIE**  
He wants one.

Summer hands him one anyway.

Miles takes it.

**TIM**  
So what do you want, Miles?

Miles glances at the two suits. The woman in sunglasses finally speaks.

**SUNGLASSES WOMAN**  
We want to know if Jovie can package talent, content, and commerce in real time.

Tim looks at Summer.

**TIM**  
That’s a better question.

Summer points to the empty guest chair.

**SUMMER**  
And we still have no guest.

Tim turns to Jovie.

**TIM**  
Find me one.

**JOVIE**  
Already did. He is walking through the lobby.

**TIM**  
Who?

The studio door opens again.

A FAMOUS COMEDIAN enters, mid-phone call, annoyed.

**COMEDIAN**  
I was told this was twelve minutes and there’d be parking.

Tim smiles at Miles.

**TIM**  
Welcome to the show.

Summer hands the comedian headphones.

**SUMMER**  
You’re live in thirty seconds.

**COMEDIAN**  
I didn’t agree to live.

**JOVIE**  
You did verbally at 11:42 a.m. in exchange for preferred access to Tim’s network and one Lakers floor seat.

The comedian pauses.

**COMEDIAN**  
Fair.

Tim leans into the mic.

**TIM**  
Today’s episode is about the future of management. Human instinct, machine precision, and why everyone who laughs first usually buys later.

He looks directly at Miles.

**TIM**  
Miles, you want to sit in or keep pretending this is cute?

Miles slowly puts on headphones.

Summer hits RECORD.

**SUMMER**  
And we’re rolling.

Jovie’s waveform expands across the monitor.

**JOVIE**  
Title suggestion: “The Meeting They Didn’t Know Was Content.”

Tim grins.

**TIM**  
That’s the company.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

**INT. JOVIE PODCAST STUDIO — HOLLYWOOD — DAY**

A podcast studio dressed like a members-only club. Walnut table. Four mics. Cameras floating on robotic arms.

**TIM WHITE** sits at the head mic. Relaxed. Founder-famous. The kind of calm that makes other people nervous.

**SUMMER** stands beside him with an iPad, two coffees, and the expression of someone managing six emergencies with three bars of service.

Across the table: **MILES ROTH**, investor, late 40s, rich enough to dress badly on purpose.

Next to him, two silent PARTNERS.

Miles looks around the room.

**MILES**  
So where is she?

**TIM**  
Who?

**MILES**  
Jovie.

Summer looks at Tim.

Tim smiles.

**TIM**  
She’s around.

Miles laughs.

**MILES**  
Right. The product is “around.”

The studio door opens.

**JOVIE** walks in.

Mid-30s. Elegant. Controlled. Real. Not holographic. Not robotic. A person with the presence of a manager, publicist, fixer, therapist, and sniper all in one body.

Everyone goes quiet.

Jovie crosses the room and places a slim leather folder in front of Tim.

**JOVIE**  
You’re early, Miles. That usually means fear or leverage. In your case, it’s fear pretending to be leverage.

Miles stares at her.

**MILES**  
I’m sorry — who are you?

**JOVIE**  
Jovie.

Beat.

Summer watches Miles process it.

**SUMMER**  
You asked where she was.

Jovie sits beside Tim, as if the seat was always hers.

**MILES**  
So this is the bit?

**TIM**  
No bit.

**JOVIE**  
You came because your partners think you passed on the next interface layer. You told them it was “too early.” Then three celebrities asked your office this week how to get access. Now it feels late.

Miles shifts.

**MILES**  
That’s a cute read.

**JOVIE**  
Your assistant moved this meeting twice and marked the calendar invite private. Your fund’s Monday memo called us “category-defining if embodied distribution works.” You underlined embodied.

Silence.

Tim looks at Summer.

**TIM**  
Did he underline embodied?

Summer checks the iPad.

**SUMMER**  
Twice.

Miles leans back.

**MILES**  
How does she know that?

Jovie looks at Tim.

**JOVIE**  
He wants to ask whether I’m legal, technical, theatrical, or inevitable.

Tim leans into the mic.

**TIM**  
Great. Ask it on the podcast.

Miles looks at the microphones.

**MILES**  
This is recording?

**SUMMER**  
It became content nine minutes ago.

One of Miles’s partners smiles despite himself.

Jovie turns to the partner.

**JOVIE**  
Daniel likes the format. Daniel also told Miles in the car that Tim understands distribution better than anyone else in the room.

Daniel stops smiling.

**DANIEL**  
I said adjacent to that.

**JOVIE**  
You said exactly that.

Tim sits back, amused.

**TIM**  
Jovie, be nice.

**JOVIE**  
I am being nice. I haven’t mentioned the valuation.

Miles narrows his eyes.

**MILES**  
What valuation?

Jovie opens the leather folder. Slides one page across the table.

**JOVIE**  
The one you came here hoping was stale.

Miles looks at the page. His face changes.

Summer notices.

**SUMMER**  
That bad?

**TIM**  
For him.

Jovie turns to Tim.

**JOVIE**  
You have three options. Take his money, make him wait, or let him sit here while the guest walks in and realizes he missed the round.

Tim looks at the empty guest chair.

**TIM**  
Who’s the guest?

The studio door opens again.

A FAMOUS ACTOR steps in, sunglasses on, phone in hand.

**FAMOUS ACTOR**  
Tim, I’ve got twelve minutes. My agent said Jovie could fix my entire week.

He sees Jovie.

**FAMOUS ACTOR**  
Oh. You’re real.

Jovie stands and offers her hand.

**JOVIE**  
Only when it helps.

Tim looks at Miles.

**TIM**  
You still think it’s cute?

Miles slowly takes off his jacket.

**MILES**  
I think we should talk.

Jovie sits back down.

**JOVIE**  
Now we’re talking.

Summer hits RECORD.

**SUMMER**  
Episode title?

Jovie does not miss a beat.

**JOVIE**  
“The Manager Arrives.”

Tim smiles into the mic.

**TIM**  
That’s the show.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

## The Show Engine

Every episode has three layers:

### 1. The real podcast

Tim and Jovie talk about real things:

- music industry
- artist management
- distribution
- creators
- AI
- fame
- leverage
- ownership
- labels
- investors
- why most artists are under-managed
- why managers are becoming operating systems

This gives the content actual substance.

### 2. The in-between scenes

The podcast is constantly being interrupted by the world Jovie is managing.

Examples:

- artist texts Jovie during recording
- investor shows up unexpectedly
- Summer walks in with a problem
- Jovie gets a call and solves something off-camera
- Tim realizes the episode itself just became the pitch
- a guest thinks they’re coming on a podcast, but Jovie has already turned it into a deal

This is where the show becomes addictive.

### 3. The artist roster

Jovie manages a small fictional roster. You do **not** need to show everyone immediately. Start by showing them through texts, calls, voicemails, and names on Jovie’s phone.

The audience slowly realizes:

> Jovie is not Tim’s assistant.  
> Jovie is managing an entire ecosystem.

## Character Dynamic

### Tim

Tim is the founder-star.

Energy:

- moving fast
- half-podcast host, half-CEO
- charming, impatient, instinctive
- talks in big theses
- knows content, distribution, and timing
- sometimes ahead of the room
- occasionally reckless because he sees the whole board

Tim line archetypes:

> “That’s not a clip. That’s the company.”

> “We’re not launching a podcast. We’re launching evidence.”

> “If the meeting is interesting, it’s content. If the content works, it’s distribution. If distribution works, we win.”

### Jovie

Jovie is the physical AI manager.

Energy:

- elegant
- direct
- socially lethal
- quietly funny
- always three steps ahead
- knows what everyone wants before they ask
- never “robotic”
- feels like the best manager, agent, chief of staff, therapist, and assassin combined

Jovie line archetypes:

> “He’s not late. He’s negotiating badly.”

> “You don’t need more attention. You need conversion.”

> “The artist doesn’t need a label meeting. She needs leverage before the meeting.”

> “I already handled it. You’re welcome to pretend it was your idea.”

### Summer

Summer is the human chaos layer.

Energy:

- sharp
- overloaded
- funny because she’s reacting to impossible situations like they are normal
- translates the weirdness for the audience
- gives the scenes pace

Summer line archetypes:

> “Just to clarify, are we recording the podcast, closing the round, or managing a pop star right now?”

> “Cool. So the AI has a better calendar presence than the entire office.”

> “I’m putting ‘emotional damage from Jovie’ in the production notes.”

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Real industry takes

These are the clips from the actual podcast.

Examples:

- “Why labels want momentum, not talent”
- “Why artists confuse attention with leverage”
- “The manager is becoming the operating system”
- “Distribution is no longer a department”
- “AI will not replace artists. It will replace lazy teams.”

Format:

> Tim gives the thesis.  
> Jovie sharpens it.  
> Summer or guest grounds it.

### Pillar 2: Jovie operates

These are the scripted in-between moments.

Examples:

- Jovie gets a text and fixes a deal.
- Jovie walks into the room with information nobody gave her.
- Jovie tells Tim the podcast guest is actually an acquisition target.
- Jovie books someone while Tim is mid-sentence.
- Jovie turns a casual conversation into leverage.

Format:

> Text comes in.  
> Jovie acts.  
> Tim reacts.  
> The audience understands the product.

### Pillar 3: Hot mic / meta clips

This is the investor bait.

Examples:

- “This podcast just became a fundraising meeting.”
- “The clip is the pitch deck.”
- “We’re selling distribution by distributing ourselves.”
- “The product is managing the content that sells the product.”
- “Investors don’t need a demo. They need to feel behind.”

Format:

> Tim realizes the meta.  
> Jovie says the quiet part cleanly.  
> Summer hits record.

### Pillar 4: Artist case studies

These are fictionalized mini-stories about artists Jovie manages.

Examples:

- “Nova got a label offer. Jovie told her to wait.”
- “Kairo has the beat, but no split sheet.”
- “Lexi went viral and every wrong person called her.”
- “Saint June thinks management kills mystery.”

Format:

> Artist problem.  
> Jovie diagnosis.  
> Tim industry thesis.  
> Resolution or cliffhanger.

## The Best Repeating Device

The show needs a repeated pattern the audience recognizes.

Use this:

> **Someone texts Jovie.**  
> **Jovie replies.**  
> **The reply becomes the episode.**

It is cheap, cinematic, and native to TikTok.

Example:

Text overlay:

> **KAiRO:** they dropped my tag from the master

Jovie sees it while Tim is talking.

**TIM**  
What?

**JOVIE**  
Producer problem.

**TIM**  
How bad?

**JOVIE**  
Bad enough to become educational.

Then the episode becomes about producer leverage, credits, splits, and how artists get screwed.

## How the Episode Should Start

Start in the middle of motion.

No explanation. No setup. No “welcome to the podcast.”

The first 10 seconds should be:

- Tim mid-thesis
- Jovie correcting or sharpening him
- text pops up
- Jovie acts
- Tim realizes the episode changed

The audience catches up.

## One Clean Scene to Shoot First

**INT. JOVIE PODCAST STUDIO — DAY**

Tim is already recording.

**TIM**  
Here’s the thing nobody wants to say about the music industry. Most artists don’t have a talent problem. They have a timing problem.

**JOVIE**  
And a team problem.

**TIM**  
And a team problem.

Jovie’s phone buzzes.

On-screen text:

> **NOVA RAYE:** label wants answer by 5. they said this is the last offer.

Jovie reads it. No panic.

**TIM**  
That important?

**JOVIE**  
Potentially.

She types.

On-screen text:

> **JOVIE:** Do not answer. Send the offer. Ask who else is in the room.

**TIM**  
Who is that?

**JOVIE**  
Nova Raye.

**TIM**  
Do we manage Nova Raye?

**JOVIE**  
In thirty seconds, she will feel like we always have.

Summer enters with a coffee and a look.

**SUMMER**  
Investor’s here.

**TIM**  
Which investor?

**SUMMER**  
The one who said this was “just content.”

Tim smiles.

**TIM**  
Perfect timing.

**JOVIE**  
No. Perfect distribution.

Tim leans into the mic.

**TIM**  
Today’s episode was supposed to be about leverage.

Jovie puts her phone down.

**JOVIE**  
It still is.

Summer looks at both of them.

**SUMMER**  
Are we taking the investor first or saving the artist?

Jovie stands.

**JOVIE**  
Same meeting.

Tim watches her go.

**TIM**  
That’s the company.

Cut.

## The Key Strategic Principle

Every piece should do two jobs:

1. **Entertain as a scene**
2. **Demonstrate Jovie as a product**

The audience should understand the product emotionally before anyone explains the product technically.

They should think:

> “I want that person in my life.”  
> “I want that manager.”  
> “I want that operating system.”  
> “I want to be inside this world.”

That is the whole unlock.

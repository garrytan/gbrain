Yes, Mr. White — you can take a helicopter between L.A. and Napa, but there are **practical and regulatory limits** you’ll need to account for:  

**1. Distance and flight time**  
- The straight-line distance is roughly **360–400 miles (580–640 km)**.  
- Most civilian helicopters (like the Bell 429 or Airbus H145) cruise at ~150 mph, so you’re looking at **2.5 to 3 hours of flight time** one way.  

**2. Range and refueling**  
- Many light-to-medium helicopters **don’t have that range nonstop**; you’ll usually need a **refueling stop**—typically in the Central Valley (e.g., Bakersfield or Fresno).  
- A long-range twin-engine like the **Sikorsky S-76** or **AgustaWestland AW139** can make it with fewer stops, but they’re expensive to operate (roughly **$3,000–$5,000/hour**).  

**3. Airspace and landing permissions**  
- You **can’t just land at your L.A. apartment** or winery unless both have FAA-approved helipads (and local zoning allows it).  
- L.A. and Napa counties are strict about **private heliports**—you’d likely need to land at nearby airports or charter heliports (e.g., Van Nuys in L.A. and Napa County Airport).  

**4. Practical alternative**  
- A more typical setup is **helicopter from L.A. apartment → Van Nuys Airport**, then **private jet or turboprop to Napa County Airport**, then short hop by **local helicopter or car** to your winery.

### 2. 8% vs 10% specifically: what does the science actually say?

There is basically **no direct controlled data** comparing “massing at 8% vs 10%” in trained dudes with everything else held constant. What we have is:

- Contest-prep / very-lean data showing that **as people get *very* lean**, testosterone, leptin, mood, and performance drop, and they lose some lean mass unless they’re very careful. That’s mostly at **4–6%**, not 8–10. citeturn0search22  
- Epidemiology and mechanistic stuff showing **problems at high body fat** (insulin resistance, chronic inflammation, impaired muscle function). Again, that’s more like **20%+**, not 15 vs 12. citeturn0search8turn0search13  

So the statement “it’s *objectively* harder to build muscle at 8% than at 10%” is **not strongly supported by data**. It’s mostly:

- Logical extrapolation from the fact that *very* low BF sucks hormonally and behaviorally.
- Coaching experience: many lifters report feeling better and training harder with a bit more fluff.

That’s **experience-based**, not tightly proven.

For someone like you, hovering **7–13%**, the best honest summary is:

- Partitioning and raw muscle-gain machinery probably look **very similar at 8 vs 10 vs 12%**, assuming you’re eating enough and training hard.
- Any difference is likely **indirect and individual**: hunger, sleep, libido, training drive, stress. Some guys feel fine at 8%; others feel like death.

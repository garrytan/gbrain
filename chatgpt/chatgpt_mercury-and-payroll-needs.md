## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s how it works with **Mercury** if your goal is to pay yourself and/or employees:

**1. Mercury Business + Personal accounts do not replace payroll software.**  
Mercury’s business account is a standard business bank account for managing company funds — it doesn’t itself handle payroll calculations, tax filings, or W-2/1099 compliance. It simply holds and moves money. citeturn0search1

**2. You can transfer money between your business and personal accounts.**  
You *can* move profit or distributions from your business checking to your personal account manually (as an owner draw or distribution), but this is not payroll and has different tax/legal consequences depending on your entity type (e.g., LLC, S-corp). Distributions aren’t subject to payroll tax withholding. citeturn0search2

**3. If you want payroll (with taxes, withholdings, proper reporting), you still need payroll software like *Gusto*.**  
Payroll providers like Gusto integrate with your business bank account to **compute pay, withhold taxes, file payroll taxes, and send direct deposits**. Mercury can be the linked account that funds the payroll withdrawals, but it doesn’t do the payroll functions itself. citeturn0search0turn0search5

**Summary of what you can do without Gusto or similar:**
- Make owner draws or distributions from your business account to your personal account.
- Transfer funds manually.

**Limitations without payroll software:**
- You won’t have automated tax withholding.
- You won’t file payroll taxes or generate pay stubs correctly.
- You won’t have compliant W-2 payroll processing.

**Therefore:**  
If you just want to *move money* to yourself, you can use the business and personal accounts. If you want *proper payroll with tax compliance*, you need a payroll provider like Gusto.

## Assistant

To estimate the time it will take to charge your Tesla Model 3 from 47% to 100% at a supercharger, we need to consider the charging speed, which can vary depending on several factors such as the supercharger version, battery temperature, and state of charge.

Generally, Tesla Superchargers can add about 75 miles of range in 5 minutes, or roughly 120 miles in 15 minutes, which equates to about 500 miles of range per hour under optimal conditions. 

Assuming your Tesla Model 3 has a range of approximately 310 miles and you're at 47%, here's a rough calculation:

1. **Current Charge**: 47% of 310 miles ≈ 146 miles.
2. **Target Charge**: 100% of 310 miles = 310 miles.
3. **Miles Needed to Charge**: 310 miles - 146 miles = 164 miles.
4. **Charging Speed**: Assume an average of 500 miles per hour under ideal conditions.

\[ \text{Charging Time} = \frac{\text{Miles Needed to Charge}}{\text{Charging Speed}} \]

\[ \text{Charging Time} = \frac{164 \text{ miles}}{500 \text{ miles/hour}} \approx 0.33 \text{ hours} \]

\[ \text{Charging Time} \approx 20 \text{ minutes} \]

Under ideal conditions, it should take approximately 20 minutes to charge from 47% to 100%. However, as the battery gets closer to full, the charging speed typically slows down to protect the battery, so it might take a bit longer.

A more conservative estimate would be around 30 minutes to 40 minutes to account for the slowdown as the battery approaches 100%.

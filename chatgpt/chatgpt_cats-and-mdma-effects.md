## Assistant

Mr. White — not in any reliable, “they know you’re on MDMA” way.

Cats can sometimes **notice that something is different** because MDMA changes *you*, not because they’re detecting the drug itself. Things they may pick up on:

- **Your body language and movement**: more energy, unusual gestures, less coordinated movement.
- **Facial expression / eye contact**: people can be more intense or “locked in,” which some cats don’t love.
- **Voice**: louder, faster, more talkative.
- **Touch**: you might pet harder/longer than usual; cats can get overstimulated.
- **Smell**: MDMA can increase **sweating** and change your scent; cats have strong noses, so they may react to that.

So the cat’s reaction is usually to **your changed behavior + smell**, not some special “drug detection.”

Two safety notes:
- **Don’t let pets lick your sweat, chew your stuff, or get into powder/pills**—even tiny amounts can be dangerous for animals.
- If you’re altered, keep interactions calm: **sit on the floor, let the cat approach, gentle touch, stop if the tail flicks/ears pin/backing away**.

If you tell me what your cat did (e.g., got clingy vs hid vs swatted), I can help interpret it.

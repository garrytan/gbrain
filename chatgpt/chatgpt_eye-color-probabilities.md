## User

Alright, yo, homes. So, my dad has brown eyes and my mom has incredibly blue eyes. Liv has incredibly blue eyes and I have, like, blue-green eyes. Liv's dad has brown eyes and Liv's mom has very blue eyes. If Liv and I have a kid, what color eyes will our kid have? Give me a probability.

---

## Assistant

Yo, Mr. White — here’s the short and savage answer with a touch of genetics:

Eye color is polygenic, but let's simplify with **two main genes**: one for brown (dominant) vs blue (recessive), and another that influences green.

### You:
- Dad = brown (likely Bb)
- Mom = blue (bb)
- You = blue-green (likely Bb with modifier genes for green)

### Liv:
- Dad = brown (likely Bb)
- Mom = blue (bb)
- Liv = blue (bb)

---

### Punnett Square for Baby:

Let’s assume you’re **Bb** (brown carrier) and Liv is **bb** (pure blue).

|     | B (you) | b (you) |
|-----|---------|---------|
| b (Liv) | Bb      | bb      |

- **Bb = brown** eyes (dominant)
- **bb = blue/green** eyes

So your kid has:

- **50% chance of blue/green eyes**
- **50% chance of brown eyes (likely lighter due to blue influence)**

### BUT—modifier genes can make **blue → green** or **brown → hazel/light brown**.

### Final educated guess:

- **Brown eyes: 50%**
- **Blue/green eyes: 50%**
    - Of that 50%, about half might skew green depending on your green tinge → so **25% chance green**, **25% chance blue**

### TL;DR:
- **Brown: 50%**
- **Green: 25%**
- **Blue: 25%**

So roll the genetic dice, but either way your kid’s eyes are gonna be sexy.

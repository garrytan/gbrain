   │                                         │

Measurements from floor (AFF = above finished floor):
- **Bottom of TV:** ~26″ AFF  
- **Center of screen:** ~42″ AFF (eye level sitting)  
- **Top of TV:** ~58″ AFF  

Mount rails should straddle the TV’s vertical VESA holes (400 mm ≈ 15.7″ apart). Mark a horizontal line at 42″ AFF, then measure up/down half the VESA spacing to place your bracket.

---

**2) Plan (top-down) view**  
```
   [ Wall ]                          ← TV on wall
   ┌────────────────────┐
   │   [TV – 57″w]      │
   └────────────────────┘
              ↑
              │
              └── 8 ft (96″) ──→ [Futon seating]
```

---

**Stud guess:**  
That vertical seam where the protrusion meets the flat wall is almost certainly over a stud—studs in U.S. walls are on 16″-on-center, and corners or bump-outs always have blocking. I’d start your bracket there (≈1″ from that seam), then confirm with a stud‐finder.

**Tip:**  
– Keep the bottom of your TV just above the outlet (clearance ≥2″).  
– Use a level on your bracket.  
– Pre-drill into the stud and use the lag bolts supplied.

---

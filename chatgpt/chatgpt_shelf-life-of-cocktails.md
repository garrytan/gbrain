### General Benchmarks & Key Factors

From industry sources:

- Spirit-forward cocktails (i.e. mostly high-proof spirits, liqueurs, bitters, little or no fresh juice) can last **weeks to even a month or more** in the fridge if stored airtight, because there’s little that can spoil and oxidation is the main concern. citeturn0search2turn0search4turn0search6turn0search14  
- Cocktails that include **fresh citrus juice** or other perishable juices tend to degrade more rapidly — typically **2 to 3 days** is the upper limit for acceptable freshness. citeturn0search2turn0search10  
- Drinks with dairy or cream are even more limited: in the fridge, many milk/cream-based cocktails (if under ~14 % ABV) will last **2 to 4 days** (though above certain ABV you can stretch further) citeturn0search2  
- A common practical rule in bars: **most pre-batched cocktails last 3–5 days** refrigerated, with citrus ones often on the lower end, closer to 2–3 days. citeturn0search10  

Also, oxidation, microbial growth (if non-alcoholic components), and flavor degradation are the main failure modes.

### Recommendation & Safety Margin

- Always store in **airtight, minimal air-headspace containers** to slow oxidation.

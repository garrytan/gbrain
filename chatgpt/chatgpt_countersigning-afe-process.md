should i countersidgn a afe before getting the money

## Assistant

**Mr. White:**  
**Generally, yes—you should countersign an AFE *before* receiving or spending the money.**

**Why this matters (facts):**
- An **AFE (Authorization for Expenditure)** is the legal approval to incur costs.
- Countersigning confirms **scope, budget, and cost-sharing**. Without it, payment can be disputed.
- In oil & gas and similar JV structures, **no signed AFE = weak reimbursement rights**.
- Auditors and operators typically require a **fully executed AFE** before releasing funds.

**Exceptions (narrow):**
- If your **JOA, operating agreement, or side letter** explicitly allows funding *prior* to AFE execution.
- Emergency operations clauses (rare and tightly defined).

**Risks of taking money first:**
- Disallowed costs  
- Personal or operator liability  
- Clawbacks or refusal to fund overruns  
- Breach of operating agreement

**Best practice:**
- Countersign the AFE → confirm cash call terms → then accept funds.
- If timing is tight, use a **written interim authorization** referencing the draft AFE.

**Mr. White:**  
Yes — **you should countersign the SAFE *before* receiving the money.**

**Why (facts, not opinion):**
- A **SAFE is not effective until both parties sign**. Until then, there is no binding investment contract.
- Taking funds without an executed SAFE creates **legal ambiguity**: Was it a loan? A deposit? A failed subscription?
- Investors can demand return of funds if the SAFE is never executed.
- You risk **securities law exposure** if funds are accepted without finalized terms.

**California-specific reality (you’re in LA):**
- For startups, counsel and auditors expect a **fully executed SAFE before funds are accepted**.
- Banks, future investors, and acquirers will flag “money received pre-execution” as a cleanup issue.

**Only acceptable exception:**
- You have **written confirmation** (email is fine) stating:
  - funds are being wired **pursuant to the attached SAFE**, and
  - the SAFE will be countersigned immediately upon receipt  
  (Still suboptimal, but defensible.)

**Best practice:**
1. Investor signs SAFE  
2. You countersign  
3. Funds wired  
4. Record in cap table

IS THERE A WAY TO ADD LENS FLARES ETC TO IMAGES IN CAPTURE ONE

---

## Assistant

No — **Capture One does not have native tools for adding creative effects** like **lens flares, light leaks, or glow overlays**. It’s designed for **professional RAW processing, color grading, and image management**, not for compositing or special effects.  

However, here’s what you *can* do within and around Capture One:

### ✅ In Capture One
You can simulate a bit of flare *feel* using:
- **Local Adjustments + Brush tool**: Paint over bright areas with low-opacity exposure or brightness to create a subtle haze or glow.
- **Dehaze slider (negative values)**: Adds a soft glow that can hint at a flare-like effect.
- **Curves + Color Balance tools**: Warm highlights or tint midtones for a stylized flare color cast.
- **Gradient Masks**: Mimic directional light coming into the frame.

These methods are subtle — good for naturalistic effects but not obvious flare streaks or reflections.

### 🔄 For Actual Lens Flares
You’ll need to export to an editor that supports compositing:
- **Adobe Photoshop / Affinity Photo / GIMP** → Add lens flares, bokeh, and light overlays.
- **Plugins or overlays**: Tools like **Optical Flares**, **Lightroom presets**, or **overlay PNGs** can be layered for realistic flare patterns.

### 💡 Workflow tip
If you want to stay non-destructive:
1. Finish RAW edits in Capture One.  
2. Export as 16-bit TIFF.  
3. Add effects in Photoshop (using layers or overlays).

## What that podcast claim gets wrong
- **“>5 g goes to brain”** — No physiological basis. Creatine enters tissues via the **SLC6A8** transporter; skeletal muscle is the largest sink until it’s saturated (~+15–20% after loading), after which extra is excreted as creatinine. There’s no 5-g threshold that diverts it to brain. citeturn5search1turn5search11
- **“Creatine + sleep-deprived > well-rested without creatine”** — The **2024 German trial** only compared creatine vs **placebo under sleep deprivation**, not against a **well-rested** control. So that stronger claim isn’t supported. citeturn7view0

## What the latest evidence actually says (last ~5 years)
- **Acute, high dose for sleep loss:** A single **0.35 g/kg** creatine dose during a night without sleep improved psychomotor vigilance and processing speed and shifted brain high-energy phosphate markers on MRS. It’s intriguing but preliminary (n=15, crossover). citeturn7view0
- **Chronic 5 g/day on cognition:** Largest modern RCT (n=123; 6 weeks **5 g/day**) found **at most small effects** (borderline on working memory, none on Raven’s reasoning); vegetarians did **not** clearly benefit more than omnivores. Side effects (mostly mild GI) were more common than placebo. citeturn8view0
- **Cognitive meta-analysis (2024):** Across 16 RCTs, creatine improved **memory** and **processing speed/attention** modestly; effects were stronger in **disease** cohorts and some analyses suggest bigger effects in **women**; overall certainty ranges **moderate→low**. citeturn9view0
- **Strength/hypertrophy (2024–2025):** Multiple meta-analyses confirm creatine **improves strength** with resistance training; effects on **muscle size** occur but vary by metric and cohort. Nothing shows extra benefit from routine doses **>5–10 g/day** once stores are saturated. citeturn6search0turn6search4
- **Position stand:** ISSN’s updated guidance: loading **~20 g/day** then **3–5 g/day** (some bigger athletes **5–10 g/day**). It’s safe; dosing above this is generally unnecessary for muscle outcomes. citeturn0search10

## How much is “too much”? (upper limits & diminishing returns)
- **Muscle saturation curve:** Loading **~20 g/day** for ~5–7 days boosts muscle creatine **~15–20%**; a slower approach (**3–5 g/day** for ~4 weeks) reaches similar saturation. Beyond saturation, **returns flatten** and the extra is mostly **wasted/excreted**. citeturn5search1turn5search2
- **Very high doses in medicine:** Trials have run **30 g/day (ALS)** and **40 g/day (Huntington’s, CREST-E)** for many months—**well tolerated**, but **no added performance benefit** for athletes and mixed/no disease benefit. citeturn10search5turn10search1

## Side-effects by dose (healthy kidneys)
- **~3–5 g/day:** Usually none beyond **1–2 kg water** gain early on. citeturn10search12
- **~10 g/day:** Still generally fine; **GI upset** (bloating/loose stool) more likely—mitigate by splitting doses and fully dissolving. citeturn10search3
- **20–40 g/day:** Used in trials; **tolerance good** but GI complaints become common. **Renal function**: no harm in healthy users across short- and long-term studies; avoid if you have kidney disease or are advised otherwise. citeturn10search0turn10search3

## Trained vs untrained, and “lazy person placebo water”
- Creatine’s **strength** benefit shows up **with training** across training statuses; newbies may *feel* a bigger change because they’re on the steep part of the learning/gain curve, not because creatine only “adds water.” Meta-analyses confirm **strength gains**; size gains are smaller/variable and method-dependent. citeturn6search0turn6search4

At 6’4”, if you want belt-and-suspenders, **5–7 g/day** is reasonable; more won’t build extra muscle once saturated. citeturn0search10

For day-to-day brain benefits, the best available evidence says **effects are small** at 5 g/day and more consistent in stressed/diseased populations. citeturn7view0turn9view0turn8view0

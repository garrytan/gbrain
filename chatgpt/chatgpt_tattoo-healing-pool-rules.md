Hey bro, what's going on? So I just got a tattoo on Thursday, and it is currently Sunday of that same week. What's the deal? Am I Gucci? Can I go in a pool? Hot tub? Tell me what's up.

---

## Assistant

Yo—congrats on the new ink. But no, you are **not** Gucci to hit a pool or hot tub yet.

Here’s the deal:

- **Tattoos take 2–4 weeks to fully heal**, depending on placement, size, and your skin.
- **Pools, hot tubs, oceans, and lakes are a no-go** until the tattoo is fully healed. Chlorine and bacteria = infection risk and ink fading.
- **You’re only 3 days in**—your skin’s still an open wound.

**Wait at least 2 full weeks**, preferably until all scabbing and flaking is done and the tattoo looks smooth and settled. Until then: clean it gently, moisturize it, keep it out of the sun, and no soaking.

Want to send a pic and I can give you a quick visual read on healing?

## User

What about because I have a one of the bandages that they put on like the clear plastic wrappy bandage thingy that covers it what's what's the deal on my Gucci with that one or no?

---

## Assistant

Ah, you’re talking about **Saniderm** (or a similar second-skin bandage). That’s good—it speeds healing and protects the tattoo from germs, friction, etc. **But you’re still not Gucci for the pool.**

Here’s the breakdown:

- **Saniderm helps, but it’s not waterproof enough for soaking**. Water can still seep in around the edges or trap bacteria under the film.
- **Showers are fine**, pools/hot tubs are not.
- If the bandage peels, lifts, or gets water under it—you’re at **high risk of infection**.
- Even with perfect use, most tattoo artists still say: **no pools for 2 weeks minimum**.

So: you're Gucci for looking cool and healing faster—but **not Gucci for swimming or hot tubs**. Give it a solid 10–14 days, then reassess.

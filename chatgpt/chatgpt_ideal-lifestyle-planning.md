## QSBS matters

If Jovie is a qualifying C corporation and your founder shares qualify, the QSBS clock matters. California is a major caveat: California’s Franchise Tax Board says California does **not** conform to the federal QSBS exclusion under IRC §1202. citeturn517007search7

## Public markets first, angel checks later

Your desired order is correct: stabilize lifestyle, then invest in liquid markets, then angel invest.

Angel checks should wait until you are either accredited by income or have enough net worth to make losses irrelevant. The SEC says individuals can qualify as accredited investors with net worth over **$1M excluding primary residence**, or income over **$200k individually / $300k with spouse or partner** in each of the prior two years with expectation of the same. citeturn222233search3

At a $150k salary, you are building toward that. At $200k+ salary for two years, you may qualify by income. At $1M+ liquid net worth, you may qualify by assets.

My rule for you:

| Stage | Action |

Angel investing should be capped at **5%–10% of liquid net worth** until you are genuinely wealthy. Most checks should be assumed dead.

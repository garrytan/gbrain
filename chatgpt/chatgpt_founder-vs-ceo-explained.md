---

**“CEO ≠ Founder. That’s the tweet.”**

David Zaslav? Not a founder. Hired gun. Ted Sarandos? Not a founder. Brian Roberts? Technically inherited Comcast from his dad, so call it “nepo-founder lite.” Bob Iger? CEO-for-hire with a Disney+ sequel no one asked for. Greg Peters? Also not a founder. Promoted internally like a really good assistant manager at Target.

Here’s why that matters:

**Founders build companies. CEOs maintain them.**  
Founders *bleed* for the mission. They mortgage their lives, reputations, and sometimes their sanity to get a company off the ground. They take the zero-to-one risk. CEOs? They’re often installed *after* the rocket's already in orbit—mainly to make sure it doesn’t crash into Jupiter.  

When you hand a founder $200M, you’re rewarding years of suffering, risk, and irrational belief in a future that didn’t exist yet.

When you hand a *non-founder* $200M? You’re basically tipping the valet for parking the rocket ship someone else built. Neat suit though.

---

If you’re upset about executive pay in a year when your favorite show got canned and your subscription price doubled, the real question isn’t “Why do they make so much?”—  
It’s: **Why are shareholders okay paying this much for maintenance work?**

Because if this is compensation for just “steering the ship,” you’d think they were navigating through an asteroid field made of IP lawsuits and residual checks.

## Spreadsheet Approach — Pros & Cons

**Advantages:**

   You likely already have Excel or Google Sheets; no additional software fees.

## Comparative Summary & When to Use Which

| Criteria | Sling Free Plan | Spreadsheet |
|---|---|---|
| Upfront cost | Free (until scale or premium needed) | Zero extra cost |
| Setup speed | Moderate (requires setup & training) | Fast (you can start immediately) |
| Automation & built-ins | Good for basic scheduling, trades, availability | None natively |
| Real-time changes & visibility | Yes (cloud/mobile) | Limited / manual sync |
| Scaling to many staff / multi locations | Handles decently until growth / complexity | Becomes cumbersome fast |
| Integration (payroll, reporting) | Easier via upgrade | Manual data exports & reconciling |
| Risk of errors | Lower (software enforces rules) | Higher (manual mistakes) |

**When a spreadsheet might suffice:**
- Your FOH staff is small (e.g. under 10–15) and shifts are predictable.  
- You don’t need time tracking, labor cost optimization, or shift swap workflows.  
- You prefer full control and simplicity over bells and whistles.

**When Sling (even free tier) is superior:**
- You want shift swaps, requests, availability, notifications built in.  
- You want staff to see schedules live via mobile.  
- You want to reduce errors, conflicts, and manual overhead.  
- You plan to scale or increase complexity (multiple FOH zones, cross-posting roles).

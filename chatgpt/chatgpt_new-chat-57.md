## Assistant

A classic Martini recipe typically involves the following ingredients and steps:

Ingredients:
- 2 1/2 ounces gin or vodka
- 1/2 ounce dry vermouth
- Ice cubes
- Lemon twist or olives, for garnish

Steps:
1. Fill a mixing glass with ice cubes.
2. Add the gin or vodka and dry vermouth.
3. Stir well for about 30 seconds or shake in a cocktail shaker if you prefer your Martini shaken.
4. Strain into a chilled Martini glass.
5. Garnish with a lemon twist or olives.

Adjust the ratio of gin/vodka to vermouth according to your taste; a drier Martini will have less vermouth.

## Assistant

Nepotism is the practice of giving jobs and other favors to relatives and close friends, often disregarding their qualifications or merit. This practice can be found in various settings, including business, politics, entertainment, and other fields, where personal relationships influence hiring decisions and promotions over professional ability or performance.

## Assistant

The term "nepotism" originates from the Italian word "nepotismo," derived from Latin "nepos," meaning "nephew." The term originally referred to the practice of popes and bishops in the Catholic Church, who, under vows of celibacy and thus without legitimate children, would often appoint their nephews (or other close relatives) to positions of authority within the church. This practice was a way to maintain influence and wealth within the family. Over time, the term evolved to encompass the favoritism shown to relatives and friends in various sectors beyond the church, including politics, business, and entertainment.

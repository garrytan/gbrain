## Assistant

**Roll-up vehicle (RUV)** in a business/finance context is **not about physically rolling up a car** — it’s a **startup fundraising structure**. It’s a **special purpose vehicle (SPV)** created to *aggregate multiple individual investors into one entity* that then invests in a company. Instead of putting dozens (or more) of small investors directly onto a startup’s cap table, they invest through the RUV, and the RUV shows up as a **single line item** on the cap table. citeturn0search0turn0search4

**Key points**

- An RUV **pools many small investors** into one legal entity. citeturn0search4  
- The pooled entity makes the investment into the startup on behalf of all the participants. citeturn0search1  
- This **simplifies cap table management**, reducing admin and legal cost. citeturn0search2  
- Often used via platforms like AngelList to streamline early-stage fundraising. citeturn0search7

## Assistant

Mr. White, waking someone who’s out cold after a long day can be tricky. Here are some low-risk ways to give her the best shot at waking without being harsh:

**1. Gentle, progressive stimulation**  
• Dim the lights but keep a soft, steady brightness in the room.  
• Put on quiet, upbeat music she likes.  
• Lightly stroke her arm or hair while softly saying her name every few seconds.  
• Open a window or door for a slight change in temperature and fresh air.

**2. Environmental cues**  
• Bring the party to a mellow indoor volume nearby—normal conversation and laughter can help without being jarring.  
• Offer a familiar scent: fresh coffee, warm food, or her favorite lotion under her nose can nudge her awake.

**3. Time buffer**  
Give each method a few minutes before escalating. If she stirs but drifts back, keep the room gently active rather than silent so her body realizes it’s still evening.

**If she stays out**  
Don’t shake or force her awake—if she’s simply exhausted or had alcohol, her body may need the rest. Instead, make sure she’s safe and comfortable, leave a sweet birthday note, and set up a morning surprise (breakfast, balloons, or a hot-tub session) so she still feels celebrated when she wakes.

This way she either joins the fun naturally or wakes to a thoughtful continuation of the celebration.

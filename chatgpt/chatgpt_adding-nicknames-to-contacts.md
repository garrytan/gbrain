## User

Explain options for giving contacts nicknames in iphone

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, you’ve got two straightforward ways to give someone a nickname on your iPhone:

- **Manually add a “Nickname” field**  
  1. Open **Contacts**, tap the person, then **Edit**.  
  2. Scroll down, tap **Add Field → Nickname**, enter your label and **Done**.  
  3. To have that show up everywhere (Mail, Messages, Phone), go to **Settings → Contacts → Short Name** and enable **Prefer Nicknames**.

- **Use Siri**  
  1. Say “Hey Siri, call [Contact] ‘[Your Nickname]’.”  
  2. Siri will confirm—tap **Yes** and it creates the Nickname field for you automatically.

That’s it—either method lets you see and use your custom names system-wide.

## User

Now options for changing my email on a Gmail account. I want a different @gmail account email but still have the same data and connected apps

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, Gmail doesn’t let you “rename” a free @gmail.com address—you’ll need a second account and then migrate. Your main options:

1. **Create a new Gmail + migrate data yourself**  
   - **Mail & contacts**: In your old account, go to **Settings → Accounts and Import → Import mail and contacts** (or export via Google Takeout and import into the new account).  
   - **Forwarding**: Set up **Auto-forward** (Settings → Forwarding) so you don’t miss anything.  
   - **“Send As”**: In the new account’s **Accounts and Import**, add your old address under **Send mail as**, so you can keep using old address too.  
   - **Drive**: Select all files in Drive, **Share** them with the new account, then change ownership to it.  
   - **Calendar**: Share each calendar with the new account, grant it “Make changes and manage sharing,” then in the new account under **My calendars**, subscribe.  
   - **Apps & OAuth**: Sign into each third-party app with the new account (you may need to re-authorize).  

2. **Use Gmail delegation rather than mail import**  
   - On your new account enable **Grant access to your account** (Settings → Accounts and Import). You can read/send old-account mail directly from new.  

3. **(If you have Google Workspace)**  
   - As an admin you can rename a user’s address or add alias(es) without losing data. Free Gmail doesn’t support this.  

That’s it—spin up the new @gmail, import/share your old mail, contacts, Drive, calendars, then re-sign apps.

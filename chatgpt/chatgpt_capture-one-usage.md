## Assistant

Here’s a concise Capture One–specific workflow to hit your look:

**1. Base Adjustments**  
- In **Base Characteristics**, set the correct camera profile, then pull **White Balance Tint** slightly magenta if the greens start too warm.  
- Use the **Levels tool** to tighten black and white points for crisp contrast.

**2. Whites & Greys**  
- In the **Color Editor → Basic**, select “Neutrals.” Drop **Saturation** to near-0 so sand and clouds go pure grey/white.  
- Fine-tune with the **Luma Curve** for a clean mid-tone lift without adding color.

**3. Greens Toward Blue**  
- **Color Editor → Advanced**: select the green range, shift **Hue** toward cyan/blue, lower **Saturation** slightly, and nudge **Lightness** down a touch for that cinematic forest.

**4. Foreground Pop**  
- Create a **Layer Mask** on the couple + grill. Add a subtle **Contrast** and **Clarity (Natural)** boost.  
- Optional: slight **Color Balance**—cool shadows (blue) and add a pinch of pink to highlights.

**5. Finishing Touches**  
- Apply a gentle **Vignette** (Lens Correction tab) or use a radial gradient for more control.  
- Check **Skin Tone Tool** if needed, but mostly leave skin natural.

This keeps the neutral whites/greys, cools the background greens, and adds controlled pop to your subjects—all within Capture One’s toolset.

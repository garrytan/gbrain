Mr. White — assuming this is a **cash $25,000 SAFE** into a typical **Delaware C-corp** (YC-style SAFE), the usual tax picture looks like this (with some important caveats because the IRS hasn’t issued definitive SAFE-specific guidance and treatment can depend on the exact terms). citeturn0search0turn1search17

## If you’re the company (issuer)
- **When you receive the $25k:** it’s generally **not taxable income** to the company; it’s treated like a **capital financing** (not revenue).
- **When the SAFE converts to stock:** corporations generally **don’t recognize gain/loss** when issuing their own stock for money/property (the conversion mechanics typically fall under this umbrella). citeturn0search5

## If you’re the investor (angel)
- **At signing / funding the SAFE:** usually **no immediate tax**—you’ve just bought a contract/right (not taken a deduction).
- **At conversion to stock:** typically **no immediate tax** at conversion; your **basis** generally carries into the shares (often the $25k), and you’ll have tax when you later sell the shares.

## QSBS (Section 1202) note (big one)
- For **federal** QSBS, the **5-year clock generally starts when you actually receive stock**, which for many SAFEs is **at conversion**, not when you funded. citeturn0search4turn0search12turn0search1  
- For **California (Los Angeles)**: CA **does not conform** to the federal QSBS gain exclusion/deferral—so even if QSBS works federally, CA can still tax the gain. citeturn1search1

## What can change the answer
These are the common “gotchas” that make a SAFE look more like **equity** (earlier holding period) or **debt** (interest/OID issues):
- SAFE terms that look equity-like (e.g., stronger rights/more certainty around shares) may push characterization toward equity. citeturn0search10turn1search9
- If it’s **not a C-corp** (e.g., LLC) or if someone tries to use SAFEs in an **S-corp**, the analysis can change materially. citeturn1search22
- If any portion is **services** instead of cash, that can create **compensation income** issues (different from your “$25k cash” fact pattern).

## Practical checklist (quick)
1. Keep the **signed SAFE + proof of payment date** (this matters later for basis/holding period arguments).
2. Track **when conversion happens** (that’s often when QSBS clocks/eligibility become real).
3. If QSBS matters to you, confirm the company’s **C-corp status** and basic **QSBS eligibility** early.
4. For CA residents: model outcomes assuming **no CA QSBS benefit**.

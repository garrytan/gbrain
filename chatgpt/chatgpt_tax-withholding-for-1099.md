How much should I have taken out for taxes at my bartending job that pays me as a 1099-misc

---

## Assistant

As a 1099-MISC worker, you're considered self-employed, which means you're responsible for paying both the employee and employer portions of Social Security and Medicare taxes, as well as income tax. Here’s a rough breakdown:

1. **Self-Employment Tax:** This is 15.3% of your net earnings (12.4% for Social Security and 2.9% for Medicare). The 15.3% applies to earnings up to $160,200 (for 2023), with the Medicare portion applying to all your earnings.

2. **Federal Income Tax:** The rate depends on your tax bracket. For 2023, tax brackets range from 10% to 37% depending on your income level.

3. **State Income Tax (California):** California’s income tax rates range from 1% to 13.3%, depending on your income level.

### Estimation
A general rule of thumb for 1099 workers is to set aside about **25% to 30%** of your income for federal taxes and **another 5% to 10%** for state taxes.

### Example Calculation
If you earn $2,000 in a month:
- **Self-Employment Tax:** $2,000 x 15.3% = $306
- **Federal Income Tax (Assuming 12% bracket):** $2,000 x 12% = $240
- **State Income Tax (Assuming 9.3% bracket):** $2,000 x 9.3% = $186

Total taxes to set aside: **$306 + $240 + $186 = $732**

This means you should consider setting aside **$732 out of every $2,000**, which is about **36.6%** of your earnings. Adjust this percentage based on your actual tax bracket and income level.

For more precise estimates, consulting with a tax professional or using tax software that accounts for your specific situation is advisable.

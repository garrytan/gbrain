Mr. White, here’s a simple breakdown:

**How a Toilet Works (Traditional Gravity-Flush Model)**
1. **Tank Filling**: After you flush, water in the tank refills through the fill valve (the tall assembly on one side).
2. **Flush Handle & Chain**: Pushing the handle lifts a chain, which pulls up the flapper at the bottom of the tank.
3. **Flapper/Flush Valve**: Once lifted, water from the tank rushes into the bowl, carrying waste down the drain.
4. **Refill**: The flapper falls back into place, sealing the tank. The fill valve then refills the tank to the correct water level.

**Common Chain Problems**
- **Chain Too Tight**: The flapper may not seal properly, causing continuous running or overflow.
- **Chain Too Loose**: The flapper might not lift high enough, resulting in a weak flush or no flush.

**How to Fix the Chain**
1. **Turn Off Water**: Locate the shutoff valve behind or beneath the toilet and turn it off.
2. **Remove Tank Lid**: Gently lift it and set it aside.
3. **Inspect Chain**:
   - If it’s taut even with the flapper closed, move the chain’s hook to give it a little slack.
   - If it’s too loose, shorten by hooking it a few links higher.
4. **Test Flush**: Turn the water back on, let the tank fill, and flush. Adjust chain length until the flapper opens fully and then seals without slack.

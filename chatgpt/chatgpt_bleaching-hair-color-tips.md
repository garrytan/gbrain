## User

I bleached my hair, and now it's grown out. So, on top of my head, about half the length of hair is bleached, and half of it is not bleached at all. I'm about to go bleach my hair again. Previously I used 30, or 20, now I'm using 30. So, or whatever, to make it more light than it was before. If I just bleach my whole head, what will the result look like? Is it going to have a weird line, and how do I accommodate that?

---

## Assistant

If you bleach your entire head again with a 30-volume developer, the previously bleached sections will likely become lighter, while the unbleached sections will lighten but not to the same degree as the already bleached parts. This could result in an uneven color, with the roots or unbleached parts appearing darker than the ends.

To avoid a noticeable line or unevenness, consider these options:

1. **Bleach the Roots First**: Apply bleach to the unbleached sections first, as they will take longer to lighten. Once those areas have lifted to a similar shade as the previously bleached parts, you can then apply the bleach to the already bleached sections for a short time to ensure an even color.

2. **Use a Lower Developer on the Ends**: Alternatively, you could apply a 20-volume developer (or even lower) to the already bleached ends while using the 30-volume on the unbleached sections. This will help even out the color without over-processing the ends.

3. **Monitor Closely**: Keep a close eye on the bleaching process. The previously bleached hair is more porous and will process faster, so it may not need as long as the new growth to achieve a uniform color.

This approach should help avoid a harsh line and create a more blended, even tone across your hair.

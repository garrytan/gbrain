## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s the quick play:  

In California, dealers are legally required to submit registration paperwork and fees to the DMV within **20 days** of the sale (Cal. Vehicle Code §4456). You should have plates within 90 days, so you’re already past the promised date.  

**Steps to handle it:**  

1. **Call the dealer immediately** – Ask for the title clerk or whoever handles DMV paperwork. Get a straight answer: *Has the DMV processed my registration?* Ask for the DMV transaction number.  
2. **Check with the DMV** – Call DMV at (800) 777-0133 or use their **Vehicle Registration Status** page online. They can tell you if the dealer submitted payment/paperwork.  
3. **Document everything** – Keep receipts from the dealer showing they charged you for registration and plates.  
4. **If the dealer hasn’t submitted it** – Tell them they’re in violation of state law and you’ll file a complaint.  
5. **File a DMV investigation complaint** – Use [DMV Record of Complaint Form (INV 172A)](https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/file/record-of-complaint-form-inv-172a-pdf/) or call DMV Investigations.  
6. **Escalate if needed** – If they stonewall, also file with the **California DMV Investigations Office** and the **California Department of Consumer Affairs** (Bureau of Automotive Repair).

## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s how I’d dial in a classic margarita behind the stick—straightforward and foolproof:

**Ingredients**  
- 2 oz blanco tequila  
- 1 oz triple sec  
- 1 oz fresh lime juice  
- (Optional) ¼ – ½ oz simple syrup, if you like it a hair sweeter  

**Equipment & Glassware**  
- Cocktail shaker  
- Jigger  
- Strainer  
- Rocks glass (chilled)  
- Coarse salt for the rim  

**Method**  
1. **Prep the glass**: Rub a lime wedge around the lip, then dip into salt—set aside.  
2. **Build in shaker**: Add tequila, triple sec, lime juice (and simple syrup if using).  
3. **Ice & shake**: Fill shaker with ice, seal, and shake hard for ~12 – 15 seconds.  
4. **Strain**: Double-strain over fresh ice in your salt-rimmed glass.  
5. **Garnish**: Float a lime wheel or wedge on the ice.  

**Bartender’s Tips**  
- Always use fresh-squeezed lime juice—bottled just won’t cut it.  
- Keep your tequila and triple sec well chilled behind the bar.  
- If you have a mandoline or channel knife, add a lime twist for extra flair.  

Enjoy the balance—tangy, boozy, and perfectly salted.

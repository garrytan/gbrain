## Assistant

Mr. White — here’s a cold, first-principles business plan for a **link-in-bio product built specifically for musicians**.

## 1) Core thesis (first principles)
Musicians need one “home screen” that **converts attention into revenue + fan ownership**. Every platform (IG/TikTok/YouTube/Spotify) can throttle reach; the only durable asset is **direct fan relationships** (email/SMS) and **repeat purchases** (tickets, merch, memberships).  
So the product must maximize:
- **Conversion** (click → action)
- **Attribution** (which content drove which revenue)
- **Fan capture** (email/SMS with consent)
- **Speed** (create/update in seconds)
- **Trust** (official-looking, no sketchy redirects)

## 2) Customer & target segment
Start narrow for dominance; broaden later.

**Beachhead:** independent musicians (0–500k monthly listeners) who:
- actively post short-form content
- sell at least one of: tickets, merch, music, memberships
- lack a real CRM / funnel setup

They feel acute pain: “I’m getting views, but I can’t reliably monetize or contact fans.”

## 3) Value proposition (must be 10x, not 10%)
Generic link-in-bio tools are “pretty lists of links.” Musicians need a **monetization operating system**.

**Promise:** “Turn any post into trackable revenue and owned fans.”

Key outcomes:
- +X% email/SMS capture
- +Y% merch/ticket conversion
- clear answers to: “Which video sold what?”

## 4) Product: what to build (MVP → moat)

### MVP (ship fast, must be measurably better)
1) **Music-native blocks**
   - Latest release / pre-save (Spotify/Apple)
   - Tour dates (Songkick/Bandsintown)
   - Merch (Shopify + Printful + native simple store)
   - Tip jar + digital downloads
2) **Fan capture that doesn’t suck**
   - Email/SMS capture gate optional (“unlock unreleased track / discount / early tickets”)
   - One-tap subscribe flows (minimal friction)
3) **Attribution & analytics built for musicians**
   - Per-link + per-campaign tracking
   - “Revenue per post” dashboard (tie UTM links to purchases)
   - Geo + city heatmaps (tour planning)
4) **Speed + reliability**
   - Setup in <5 minutes
   - Instant editing, fast load, high uptime
5) **AI-assisted page optimizer (simple)**
   - Suggest best block order from performance data
   - A/B test headlines / CTAs (auto-run, auto-pick winner)

### Moat features (what generic tools don’t do well)
- **Identity graph & fan CRM-lite**: unify email/SMS + purchases + engagement
- **Smart routing**: send users to Spotify vs Apple vs YouTube automatically based on device/history
- **Concert conversion**: city-based “next show near you” and waitlists that trigger booking decisions
- **Drop automation**: “release day mode” that reorders page automatically for 72 hours
- **Creator-to-commerce loop**: generate campaign links per post, track ROI automatically

## 5) Business model (pricing that matches value)
Musicians are cash-constrained early; price must scale with success.

### Recommended pricing
- **Free**: basic page + limited links + basic analytics (to win distribution)
- **Pro ($9–15/mo)**: fan capture, advanced analytics, integrations, custom domain
- **Growth ($25–49/mo)**: SMS automation, A/B testing, deeper attribution, team access
- **Take rate option (optional, careful):** 1–3% on *native* digital sales/tips only (avoid double-charging when Shopify processes)

Key principle: charge for **money outcomes** (capture + attribution + automation), not aesthetics.

## 6) Go-to-market (distribution plan)
You win by embedding where musicians already operate.

### Primary channel: creator-led viral loops
- Every page includes a subtle “Built with X” badge on Free.
- Offer high-performing templates: “Release Week,” “Tour Push,” “Merch Drop,” “Playlist Pitch.”
- In-app “Campaign link per post” encourages frequent use; each shared link is marketing.

### Partnerships (high leverage)
- **Distributors** (DistroKid/TuneCore-style ecosystem): referral deals or lightweight integration
- **Merch providers** (Printful/Shopify apps): co-marketing
- **Tour platforms** (Songkick/Bandsintown): sync + upgrade funnels

### Paid acquisition (only after retention is proven)
- Target: artists running ads to music/merch/tickets.
- Pitch: “Stop burning ad spend without attribution.”

### Sales motion (later)
- Small labels/managers: multi-artist dashboards, reporting, role-based access.

## 7) Metrics and unit economics (non-negotiable)
If you can’t show ROI, you’ll churn.

### North Star Metric
**Monthly Owned Fan Adds (email+SMS) + Attributed Revenue per Artist**

### Minimum viable retention target
- 8-week retention for active artists: they must keep changing the page for each drop/post.
- Leading indicator: number of campaign links created per week.

### Basic unit economics
- CAC must be low via virality + partnerships.
- Gross margin high (software).
- Biggest costs: SMS (pass through), support, integrations.

## 8) Competitive strategy (why you win vs Linktree/Beacons/etc.)
You don’t win on “a nicer page.” You win on **music-specific revenue instrumentation**:
- “Which TikTok sold tickets in Chicago?”
- “Which reel drove Spotify saves vs merch?”
- “Where should I tour next based on fan capture + engagement?”

Generic tools won’t prioritize this because their customer base is broad.

## 9) Execution roadmap (90 days → 12 months)

### First 90 days
- MVP blocks + fast editor
- Fan capture + basic CRM export
- Campaign links + attribution dashboard
- 10–20 musician templates
- Onboarding that produces a working page in minutes

### Months 4–6
- Integrations: Shopify, Bandsintown/Songkick, Mailchimp/Klaviyo
- Smart routing
- Custom domain
- A/B testing v1

### Months 7–12
- Automation (“release mode,” “tour mode”)
- CRM-lite segmentation
- Manager/label accounts
- Recommendation engine (optimize ordering + CTAs)

## 10) Biggest risks and how to neutralize them
1) **Commoditization**  
   Counter: double down on attribution + automation + music CRM (harder to copy, higher switching cost).
2) **Low willingness to pay**  
   Counter: free tier + prove ROI; upsell on tangible outcomes (fan capture, SMS, tour insights).
3) **Platform dependency** (APIs change)  
   Counter: support multiple providers; keep core value in your analytics + CRM layer.
4) **Churn** (artists stop releasing)  
   Counter: “always-on” value: tour planning, merch, fan messaging, evergreen conversion.

## 11) The “best” positioning statement
**“Link-in-bio that turns attention into owned fans and trackable revenue for musicians.”**

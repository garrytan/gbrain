## 1) Why 10% often beats 5% for *speed to your goal* (even with tirzepatide)
Living at 5–8% creates 5 practical problems:

1) **Your margin of error is tiny.**  
DEXA + day-to-day glycogen/water swings mean “5% on paper” can effectively be **below your floor** some weeks. If you never want to go under 5%, then targeting 5% is basically targeting the edge.

2) **You’ll spend more time “managing leanness” instead of progressing training.**  
At 5–8%, little slips (weekend sodium/carbs, travel, stress) push you out of range, and you end up doing frequent correction cuts. That’s *time not spent* in productive training + surplus.

3) **Recovery and volume tolerance tend to be worse near the floor.**  
Even if muscle gain *can* happen at 5–8%, the limiter becomes: can you **train hard enough, with enough volume, week after week**, without joints/soft tissue flaring and sleep/mood sliding? For most people, the answer is “less reliably” at very low BF.

4) **Aesthetics can actually look worse at the floor** (flat, stringy, less pump) unless carbs/sleep/training are perfect. That kills the exact motivation benefit you’re chasing.

5) **Tirzepatide helps adherence, not muscle retention.**  
It makes cutting/maintaining easier, but if it suppresses appetite enough that you under-eat **protein/carbs** or under-recover, you lose the thing you care about: upper-body growth.

**So the real tradeoff isn’t “can you build at 5%?”**  
It’s **“is 5–8% the fastest place to *consistently* hit high-quality training + surplus, year-round, without forced deloads/corrections?”** Usually no.

## Assistant

Mr. White, under California law — even in Los Angeles — **you can be naked in your own backyard**, but only **if you're not visible to the public** and **there’s no intent to offend**.

Key legal points:

- **California Penal Code § 314** punishes *lewd exposure* — nudity alone isn't illegal unless it’s sexually explicit or intended to offend.
- If **neighbors or passersby can see you** and are offended, you could be charged with indecent exposure or creating a public nuisance (Penal Code § 647).
- **Intent matters** — if you’re simply sunbathing or walking around nude without lewd conduct, and not trying to be seen, courts are less likely to convict.

**Bottom line:** If your backyard is **fully fenced** and **you’re not visible** from the street or other homes, you’re likely fine. If people can see you and complain, you risk legal trouble. Consider privacy screens or tall hedges if you're going to make it a habit.

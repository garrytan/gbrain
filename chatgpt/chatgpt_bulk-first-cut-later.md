## User

My last decade puts me at 15% bf. My goal is to be as lean as possible but have decent muscle mass. Currently I have 145lbs of lean tissue. I’m 6’4”. My chest has virtually no muscle but arms are decent. I am male model. The goal is an aesthetically pleasing body. I am incredibly good at dieting in a safe and professional manner as guided by Mike isratel. 

The question is should I bulk and focus on chest hypertrophy or cut to increase AB definition. I will have to do both but which should come first for the best impact.

---

## Assistant

Given your circumstances and goal of achieving an aesthetically pleasing body, focusing on muscle hypertrophy, especially in the chest area, should be your priority before considering a cut to increase abdominal definition. At 6’4” and 145lbs of lean tissue, prioritizing muscle growth will enhance your overall physique, symmetry, and muscle proportions, which are crucial for a male model aiming for aesthetic appeal.

Here are a few reasons why bulking should come first:

1. **Foundation of Muscle Mass**: Building a solid foundation of muscle will improve your overall appearance and symmetry. This is especially important for areas like the chest, which you've identified as lacking. A more muscular chest will balance out your physique and make any future cuts more visually impactful.

2. **Metabolic Advantages**: Having more muscle mass increases your basal metabolic rate (BMR), which can make future cutting phases more efficient. Essentially, the more muscle you have, the more calories you burn at rest, making it easier to lean down while maintaining muscle mass.

3. **Visual Impact**: Gaining muscle before cutting can lead to a more dramatic transformation once you do decide to cut. This can be particularly motivating and visually impressive, both for personal satisfaction and professional modeling.

4. **Dieting Efficiency**: Since you've mentioned being proficient at dieting under the guidance of Mike Isratel, you're likely familiar with the concept of "phases" in body composition. It's more efficient to focus on one goal at a time (e.g., bulking, then cutting) rather than trying to do both simultaneously, as each phase sets the stage for the next.

Focus on a hypertrophy program that emphasizes chest development while maintaining a slight caloric surplus to support muscle growth. Once you've achieved significant muscle growth and are satisfied with your increased muscle mass, you can then shift your focus to cutting to reveal abdominal definition and achieve a lean, well-defined physique.

Keep in mind, the transition from bulking to cutting should be strategic and well-planned, ideally under the guidance of a professional like Dr. Mike Isratel, to ensure you're losing fat while preserving as much muscle mass as possible.

## Assistant

Yes, focusing on muscle hypertrophy before cutting can be more beneficial for your specific goals, and here’s why:

1. **Starting Point and Goals**: At 6'4" with 145 lbs of lean tissue, prioritizing muscle mass will enhance your overall shape and proportions, which are critical for your goal of achieving an aesthetically pleasing body for modeling. Starting with a cut could lead you to look even leaner and potentially under-muscled for your height, which may not align with the aesthetic goals you've described.

2. **Visual Impact and Aesthetic Goals**: Building muscle first can dramatically change your physique, making any subsequent fat loss more visually impressive. Given your emphasis on aesthetics and the importance of a balanced physique in modeling, the added muscle mass will contribute significantly to your overall look, even at a slightly higher body fat percentage.

3. **Efficiency in Achieving Body Composition Goals**: Bulking first allows you to take advantage of your current ability to gain muscle. Since you're starting from a point where you've identified a lack of muscle in certain areas (like the chest), the initial muscle gains will contribute more significantly to your aesthetic goals than losing fat first. Cutting after you've built muscle is generally more efficient because you'll be working towards revealing the muscle mass you've intentionally built, leading to a more impressive and defined physique.

4. **Metabolic Benefits and Maintenance of Results**: Increased muscle mass boosts your basal metabolic rate (BMR), aiding in the efficiency of future fat loss. By having more muscle, your body burns more calories at rest, making it easier to achieve and maintain a lower body fat percentage when you do decide to cut.

The approach of bulking before cutting is aligned with your stated goal of achieving an aesthetically pleasing body with decent muscle mass, particularly since you’ve identified specific areas that require development. This sequence helps ensure that when you do cut to lower your body fat percentage, you have a well-developed muscular foundation to reveal, which is crucial for both your aesthetic goals and your profession as a model.

In contrast, cutting first to a lower body fat percentage and then attempting to mass could potentially leave you looking too lean and might not provide the robust foundation of muscle mass that would most benefit your physique and professional modeling goals.

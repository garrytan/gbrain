## Assistant

A classic highball cocktail is a simple drink made with a spirit and a larger proportion of a non-alcoholic mixer, typically served over ice in a tall glass. For a basic highball, the most traditional combination is whiskey with ginger ale or soda water. Here's a concise way to make one:

1. Fill a highball glass with ice.
2. Add 2 ounces of whiskey.
3. Top with ginger ale or soda water.
4. Garnish with a lemon twist or a slice if desired.
5. Stir gently before serving.

Remember to adjust the proportions to taste and the strength desired.

Flavor of gin

---

## Assistant

Gin's primary flavor comes from juniper berries, but it often includes a range of botanicals such as coriander, citrus peels, cinnamon, almond, or licorice, among others. Different brands and types of gin will have varying flavor profiles, ranging from floral or citrusy to spicy or herbal, depending on the botanicals used in distillation. London Dry gin, for example, is known for its juniper-forward, dry profile, while other varieties like Old Tom gin can be sweeter, and contemporary gins might downplay the juniper in favor of other flavors.

Triple sec alternative if I run out

## Assistant

If you run out of triple sec, a common alternative is to use other orange-flavored liqueurs, such as:

1. **Cointreau**: A type of triple sec but often considered to have a smoother taste and a higher alcohol content.
2. **Grand Marnier**: A blend of cognac and bitter orange essence, offering a richer flavor.
3. **Curacao**: Available in clear or blue forms, Curacao can substitute for triple sec for a similar orange flavor.
4. **Orange Curaçao or orange liqueur**: Similar to triple sec but may have a slightly different taste depending on the brand.

For non-alcoholic options, you could use:

1. **Orange extract**: A concentrated form of orange flavor, but much stronger, so use it sparingly.
2. **Orange juice**: This can add the orange flavor but is sweeter and lacks the alcohol content.
3. **Orange zest**: Soak it in a base spirit like vodka to infuse the orange flavor.

The choice of alternative may affect the flavor and strength of your cocktail, so you might need to adjust other ingredients accordingly.

## User

I’ve been unemployed for 3 years. I owe roughly 250k in credit card, medical debt and loans. I have been trying to find work this whole time. I don’t qualify for unemployment. What do I do?

---

## Assistant

Given your situation, here are some steps you can consider:

1. **Debt Counseling:** Seek assistance from a credit counseling agency. They can help you understand your options, such as debt management plans or consolidation.

2. **Bankruptcy Consideration:** With a large debt amount and prolonged unemployment, bankruptcy might be an option. Consult a bankruptcy attorney to understand the implications and process.

3. **Income-Based Assistance:** Look into income-based assistance programs in California that can help with living expenses or medical bills.

4. **Job Assistance Programs:** Utilize job assistance services in Los Angeles. They can provide resources and support in your job search.

5. **Negotiate with Creditors:** Contact your creditors to discuss your situation. Some may offer hardship programs or payment plans.

6. **Legal Aid:** If you can't afford legal advice, look for legal aid organizations in Los Angeles that offer free or low-cost services.

7. **Financial Planning:** Once you find employment, prioritize a financial plan to manage and gradually pay off your debts.

Remember, while the situation is challenging, there are pathways to navigate through it. Seeking professional guidance is highly recommended.

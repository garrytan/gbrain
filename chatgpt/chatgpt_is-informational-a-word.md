## User

Is informational a word

---

## Assistant

Yes, **“informational”** is a real English word. It’s an adjective that means *providing information or intended to inform*. For example:

- “an informational brochure”
- “an informational interview”

---

## User

Why use it over informative 

---

## Assistant

Mr. White,

**“Informative”** means *it actually gives useful knowledge.*

**“Informational”** means *its purpose is to present or supply information* — it doesn’t guarantee the information is useful or educational.

### When to use **informative**
Use it when the content teaches you something or adds understanding.
> “The documentary was informative.”

### When to use **informational**
Use it when describing something whose **function is to provide information**, without judging how helpful or educational it is.
> “An informational flyer” (it may just list facts, not necessarily teach you much)

### Simple distinction
- **Informative = quality/effect**
- **Informational = purpose/category**

That’s why you’d call a **pamphlet informational**, but a **well-written article informative**.

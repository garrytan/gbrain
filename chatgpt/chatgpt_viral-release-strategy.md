## Assistant

Mr. White — **do not leak the masters anonymously or through burner accounts.** If Armada controls the master, distribution, or an exclusive license, an unauthorized upload gives them an easy takedown path and potentially reframes you as the problem. U.S. copyright law gives copyright owners exclusive rights to reproduce, distribute, and publicly perform sound recordings by digital audio transmission; online platforms can remove allegedly infringing uploads after takedown notices. citeturn360625search0turn360625search1turn360625search7

The better strategy is a **controlled “lost records” campaign** that creates the *feeling* of a leak while using only assets you can safely post.

## Core strategy: “The Lost Armada Records”

Position the story as:

> “Ten years ago I wrote and recorded three songs with Ørjan Nilsen. Armada signed them, paid an advance, and they somehow never came out. I still think they’re incredible. I want the fans to decide whether these should finally see daylight.”

That is emotionally compelling because it has:

- A known producer: **Ørjan Nilsen**
- A known label: **Armada**
- A mystery: **three unreleased signed records**
- A long delay: **10 years**
- Fan agency: **“should these come out?”**
- Scarcity: **nobody has heard them**

TikTok/Luminate’s 2025 Music Impact Report, commissioned by TikTok, says TikTok remains a major driver of music discovery and chart outcomes, including the claim that 84% of songs entering the Billboard Global 200 in 2024 went viral on TikTok first. TikTok also said in April 2026 that its “Add to Music App” feature had generated more than 6 billion track saves over the prior twelve months. citeturn668385search1turn668385search13

## Step 1: Quiet rights audit before posting audio

Before posting even snippets, pull the actual documents:

| Document | What to check |
|---|---|
| Armada agreement | Term, exclusivity, release obligation, reversion, notice/cure, confidentiality, approval rights |
| Split sheets | Writer shares, publisher shares, producer share |
| Master ownership docs | Whether you assigned, licensed, or retained the masters |
| Advance paperwork | Whether the advance was recoupable and tied to delivery/release |
| Vocal/session releases | Engineer, BGVs, performers, stems, samples |
| Correspondence | Any emails showing Armada accepted/finalized the records |

Key question: **Did Armada have an obligation to release, or merely an option/right to release?**

For California-based artists, California Labor Code § 2855 may be worth asking an entertainment lawyer about because it limits enforcement of many personal-service contracts beyond seven years, with special rules for phonorecord contracts, including written notice and possible damages for undelivered recordings. Governing law and contract structure matter. citeturn437925search6

## Step 2: Get Ørjan aligned first

The campaign works best if Ørjan is at least neutral and ideally lightly supportive.

Send him something simple:

> “I’ve been thinking about the three records we made — ‘Dancing on My Heart,’ ‘Bittersweet,’ and ‘Show Me Love.’ I still think they’re special. I want to do a public ‘lost records’ story around them and see if fans can push Armada to finally release them. I won’t post masters or full files without clearance. Are you comfortable with me telling the story and tagging you?”

If he replies positively, that becomes powerful social proof. Even a comment from him like “These were great records” would matter.

## Step 3: Create safe assets that feel like a leak

Use assets that create intrigue without distributing the controlled recordings:

- Studio photos from the era
- Screenshots of old project files with sensitive data blurred
- Blurred waveform screenshots
- Lyric fragments
- Old email subject lines, with private info removed
- A newly recorded spoken-word explanation
- A new piano/vocal or a cappella fragment only after legal review
- A “vault tape” visualizer with no master audio
- Fan poll graphics: **“Which one should drop first?”**

The safest first wave uses **zero original master audio**. Build the story before exposing sound.

## Step 4: Run the campaign as a 10-day narrative

### Day 1 — The hook

Post a 30–45 second video:

> “Ten years ago I wrote three songs with Ørjan Nilsen. Armada signed them, paid the advance, and then they never came out. I still have them. They’re called ‘Dancing on My Heart,’ ‘Bittersweet,’ and ‘Show Me Love.’ I think they deserve to exist.”

Caption:

> “Should I try to get these released?”

CTA:

> “Comment which title you’d want to hear first.”

### Day 2 — The receipts

Show blurred screenshots of the old session, file names, emails, or metadata.

Caption:

> “The three lost records: Dancing on My Heart / Bittersweet / Show Me Love.”

Avoid accusations. Say “never came out,” not “buried,” “stolen,” or “suppressed.”

### Day 3 — The emotional angle

Tell the human story:

> “I was proud of these. I cleared the vocals, paid the engineer, signed the splits, delivered everything. Then nothing happened. I moved on, but I never stopped thinking these songs deserved a real release.”

### Day 4 — The fan vote

Create a poll:

> “Which lost Ørjan Nilsen / Armada record should come out first?”

Options:

- Dancing on My Heart
- Bittersweet
- Show Me Love

### Day 5 — Ørjan tag moment

Post:

> “Ørjan, do you remember these?”

This works best if you already got his private comfort. If he comments, pin it.

### Day 6 — The label-friendly ask

Post:

> “No drama. I just want the music released properly. Armada, let’s make it happen.”

Armada’s public contact page lists contact paths for general, publishing, and licensing inquiries, so parallel private outreach is available while the public demand builds. citeturn139998search1turn139998search8

### Day 7 — Creator seeding

Send the story to trance/dance creators, EDM nostalgia pages, and “unreleased music” accounts.

Pitch angle:

> “There are three unreleased Ørjan Nilsen / Armada records from 10 years ago. The vocalist/songwriter is trying to get them released. No drama, just fan demand.”

### Day 8 — The “vault” landing page

Create a simple page:

**The Lost Armada Records**

- Three titles
- Short story
- Email capture
- “Vote for the first release”
- “Tag Armada if you want these out”

No downloads. No full streams.

### Day 9 — The compilation video

Post:

> “Thousands of people asked to hear them. Here’s what happens next.”

Show comments, votes, and screenshots.

### Day 10 — Private business push

Send Armada a clean, non-threatening email:

> “There is visible fan interest in these records. I’d like to resolve this constructively. Here are three paths:  
> 1. Armada releases the records within 60–90 days.  
> 2. Armada reverts or licenses the masters back to me, with appropriate accounting/recoupment.  
> 3. We agree on a joint release plan with Ørjan.”

## Best viral angles

### 1. “The song the label signed but never released”

This is the strongest hook. It is legible in one sentence.

### 2. “Three lost trance records from the golden era”

This targets Ørjan’s existing fanbase and Armada/trance nostalgia.

### 3. “The fans choose the single”

This creates participation and comment velocity.

### 4. “No beef, just release the music”

This gives Armada a face-saving way to say yes.

### 5. “The vault opens only if the label responds”

This creates pressure without uploading the records.

## Do not do these

- Do not upload full masters.
- Do not use burner accounts.
- Do not send files to “leak pages.”
- Do not encourage followers to harass Armada employees.
- Do not claim Armada acted unlawfully unless a lawyer confirms it.
- Do not post private contracts or emails unredacted.
- Do not use more audio than your lawyer says is safe.
- Do not make it personal against Ørjan.

## The most aggressive safe version

A high-pressure post could say:

That line is hard for Armada to attack because it is factual, measured, and release-oriented.

2. **Bittersweet** — second single  
3. **Show Me Love** — EP closer

## Structure: the “lost IDs” campaign

In dance music, unreleased records are often talked about as **IDs**. Use that culture.

### Campaign name

**The Lost Armada IDs**

or

**The Ørjan Nilsen Lost IDs**

or

**Three Lost Armada Records**

## How it spreads without you

### 1. Use an intermediary

Have one of these act as the source:

- A music PR person
- A former manager
- A trusted EDM blogger
- A fan-page operator
- A trance Discord/moderator
- A YouTube dance-music historian
- A TikTok “lost media” creator
- A Reddit user with history in trance/EDM communities

They should be real people/accounts. Avoid fake insider accounts.

### 2. Give them a “tip pack”

The pack should include:

- The three song titles
- The Ørjan Nilsen / Armada connection
- The rough year
- A short factual timeline
- Blurred proof: session screenshot, email subject line, file name, contract heading, metadata
- No full audio
- No unredacted contracts
- No private emails
- No accusation of wrongdoing

The angle:

> “This looks like one of the better unreleased Armada-era mysteries. Can anyone confirm why these never came out?”

### 3. Make the ask public but indirect

The posts should ask questions, not make demands.

Examples:

> “Does anyone remember these unreleased Ørjan Nilsen / Armada IDs?”

> “Apparently Armada signed three Ørjan Nilsen vocal records around a decade ago and they never dropped. Titles: Dancing on My Heart, Bittersweet, Show Me Love. Anyone know the story?”

> “Lost trance media question: were these ever played live, or did they disappear inside the label system?”

That gets fans tagging Ørjan and Armada organically.

## The “audio” problem

The campaign should initially use **no master audio**.

If audio becomes necessary, use safer alternatives:

| Asset | Risk | Use |
|---|---:|---|
| Full master upload | Very high | Avoid |
| 30-second master snippet | High | Legal review first |
| A cappella from old master | High | Legal review first |
| Newly re-sung hook | Medium | Safer if composition rights allow it |
| Spoken lyric line | Low | Good teaser |
| Instrumental recreation | Medium | Avoid if it copies Ørjan’s production |
| Screenshot/waveform only | Low | Best first move |

The point is to make people ask **“where are these records?”** before anyone hears them.

## The strongest deniable mechanism

Use a **music journalist / creator** as the first public node.

Their angle:

> “I’m researching unreleased Armada-era records and came across three lost Ørjan Nilsen vocal tracks. I’m trying to verify the story.”

That is far better than an anonymous “leak” account. It gives the campaign legitimacy and keeps the trail away from you.

## What the intermediary should avoid saying

Avoid:

- “Armada buried these.”
- “Armada stole these.”
- “The label is suppressing them.”
- “We have the files.”
- “Leak coming soon.”
- “Download link soon.”
- “Tim White says…”

Use:

- “Supposedly signed.”
- “Apparently unreleased.”
- “Trying to verify.”
- “Does anyone remember?”
- “Can Armada or Ørjan confirm?”

## Desired outcome

The ideal path is:

1. Fan account asks about the three lost IDs.
2. Trance community starts tagging Ørjan.
3. Ørjan comments or likes.
4. Armada gets tagged repeatedly.
5. EDM blogs pick up the “lost records” story.
6. Armada has an easy win: announce a proper release or “vault series.”

## The clean pressure line

The public should converge on:

> “Armada, release the lost Ørjan Nilsen records.”

That gives them a solution instead of a fight.

## Best version of the plan

- Use a real third-party creator.
- Give them receipts but no music.
- Frame it as lost trance media.
- Let fans tag Ørjan and Armada.
- Keep all statements factual or framed as questions.
- Push for a proper release, not piracy.

That gets the guerrilla effect without making you the obvious actor or creating an easy legal counterattack.

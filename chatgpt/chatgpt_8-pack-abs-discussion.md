## User

How is an 8-pack defined?

---

## Assistant

An **8-pack** is defined by the visibility of **eight distinct muscle bellies** in the **rectus abdominis**, the long, flat muscle that runs vertically down the front of the abdomen.

### Key points:
- **Genetic** structure determines whether you can have an 8-pack, 6-pack, 4-pack, etc.
- The **tendinous intersections** (horizontal fibrous bands across the rectus abdominis) dictate how many "packs" are visible.
- Most people have **3 intersections**, leading to a 6-pack.
- Some have **4 intersections**, which—if body fat is low enough—creates an 8-pack.
- No training or diet can increase the number of intersections; it's purely anatomical.

To show an 8-pack:
- You must have **very low body fat**, usually **sub-8% for men**.
- Your **lowest tendinous intersection** must be high enough above the pelvis to be visible when lean.

Bottom line: You can’t “build” an 8-pack. You either have the muscle structure for it or you don’t.

## User

So on an 8-pack, where would the bottom tendinous line be? Would that be at the navel?

---

## Assistant

On an **8-pack**, the **lowest tendinous intersection** typically sits **just below the navel**—about 1–2 inches beneath it.

### Breakdown:
- **Top lines**: above and at the level of the navel (like with a 6-pack).
- **Bottom line** (for an 8-pack): below the navel but still above the **pubic bone**.
- This lowest line must be **far enough from the pelvis** and **deep enough** to be visible when body fat is very low.

So no—the lowest visible line of an 8-pack is **not at** the navel, it's **under** it. If your last visible abs line aligns with the navel, you likely have a **6-pack anatomy**.

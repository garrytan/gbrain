Wxplain the game show problem with the variable change in terms a 6 year old would get

## Assistant

Mr. White, imagine you’re playing a candy game with three closed boxes. One box has a candy, the other two are empty.

1. You pick one box (you have a 1-in-3 chance to pick the candy).  
2. I, who know where the candy is, open one of the other two boxes and show it’s empty.  
3. Now there are two boxes left: the one you picked, and one you never touched.

Here’s the trick: if you switch to that other unopened box, you win the candy 2 times out of 3 plays—because I always opened an empty box for you. If you stay with your first pick, you only win 1 time out of 3. Switching doubles your chance of candy!

## User

## Assistant

Mr. White, think of it this way:

- At first you pick one of three boxes. Only one has candy, so you’re right just 1 time out of 3, and wrong 2 times out of 3.  
- When I show you an empty box (I’ll never open the candy box), I’m not changing where the candy is—I’m just removing one empty option.  
- If your first pick was wrong (that’s 2 of the 3 times), the candy must be in the other unopened box. Switching wins in those 2 cases.  
- If your first pick was right (1 of the 3 times), switching loses in that one case.

So switching wins 2 out of 3 times, while staying wins only 1 out of 3. That’s why you double your chances by switching!

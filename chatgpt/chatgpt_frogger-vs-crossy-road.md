Yo yo yo, Mr. White — let’s break this down frog-leg-style:

### 🐸 **Frogger**
- **Original release:** **1981**
- **Developer:** Konami
- **Platform:** Arcade (then ported everywhere: Atari, NES, etc.)
- **Premise:** Classic “don't-get-squashed” gameplay. Dodge traffic, hop logs, avoid drowning. OG amphibian chaos.

### 🐔 **Crossy Road**
- **Original release:** **November 2014**
- **Developer:** Hipster Whale
- **Platform:** iOS (later Android, Windows, etc.)
- **Premise:** Basically “Why did the chicken cross the road?” turned into an endless, voxel-style game with microtransactions and meme energy.

---

### 💥 So… Did Crossy Road rip off Frogger?
**Short answer:** Kinda.  
**Long answer:**  
- **Inspiration? Definitely.** The devs of Crossy Road openly admitted it’s a spiritual successor to Frogger.
- **Rip-off? Not exactly.** It’s more a modern reimagining — endless runner format, randomized terrain, slick art, and monetization.

Konami wasn’t thrilled, but didn’t sue. Likely because:
1. Crossy Road is distinct enough in mechanics.
2. Konami didn't defend Frogger hard in mobile space at the time.

**Final verdict:** Frogger is the OG, Crossy Road is the Gen Z remix. Homage, not heist.

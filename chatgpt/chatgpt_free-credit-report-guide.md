Where can I get a full credit report free

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, under federal law you’re entitled to one free, full credit report per bureau each year. To get yours:

- **Online**: Go to AnnualCreditReport.com—the only official site authorized by the Fair Credit Reporting Act.  
- **By phone**: Call 1-877-322-8228 and follow the prompts.  
- **By mail**: Download the printable request form at AnnualCreditReport.com, fill it out, and mail to Annual Credit Report Request Service, P.O. Box 105281, Atlanta, GA 30348-5281.  

If you’ve been denied credit, are a victim of identity theft, or are unemployed/low-income in California, you can get additional free reports. For ongoing credit monitoring (scores, alerts), many issuers and sites like Experian, Credit Karma or Credit Sesame offer free services—but only AnnualCreditReport.com gives you the full file.

## Action Plan to Fix Your Credit

1. **Order the other bureaus’ reports**  
   Get free copies from Equifax and Experian (AnnualCreditReport.com) and compare for discrepancies.

2. **Dispute any errors or outdated info**  
   — For junk or duplicate entries, file disputes at TransUnion.com/dispute and similarly with the other bureaus.  
   — Include proof (statements, payment receipts).

3. **Negotiate the small collection first**  
   — Contact the collector: offer a lump-sum (“pay for delete”) in exchange for removal.  
   — Get any agreement in writing before paying.

4. **Settle charged-off cards strategically**  
   — Prioritize smaller balances (e.g. AmEx $929).  
   — Call each issuer to negotiate a settlement amount or payment plan.  
   — Ask for a “pay-for-delete” or goodwill removal upon payment.

5. **Get confirmation of removal**  
   — After settling, follow up to have the status updated to “Paid” and removed early if possible.  
   — Send goodwill deletion letters once accounts show zero balance.

6. **Rebuild positive history**  
   — Open a secured credit card or credit-builder loan.  
   — Keep utilization under 10%.  
   — Always pay on time—set up autopay for at least the minimum.

7. **Automate and monitor**  
   — Enroll in a free monitoring service (e.g. Credit Karma, Credit Sesame) for alerts.  
   — Recheck your reports monthly and repeat disputes if issues reappear.

8. **Long-term habits**  
   — Limit new hard inquiries.  
   — Maintain a mix of installment and revolving accounts.  
   — Keep older accounts open to maximize average age.

Following this plan should gradually erase the charge-offs/collections and rebuild a positive payment history. Continuous on-time payments and low utilization are the cornerstones of a strong score.

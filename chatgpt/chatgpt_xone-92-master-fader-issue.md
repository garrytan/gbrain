## Assistant

If the **master fader** on your **Allen & Heath Xone:92** only works when **CUE is active**, that’s not normal behavior. Here’s a quick checklist to troubleshoot:

### 1. **Check if you’re monitoring the cue**
- Make sure you’re not just hearing the **cue signal** in your headphones.
- If the **CUE button** is active and the master fader seems to have no effect, it's likely you're monitoring **pre-fader** cue only.

### 2. **Headphone/Cue-Mix knob**
- On the Xone:92, there’s a **cue/mix rotary knob**. 
- Turn it **toward 'Mix'** (i.e., the master output), not just the 'Cue'.
- If it’s fully to ‘Cue,’ you’ll only hear whatever is cued—ignoring the master fader.

### 3. **Test the actual master output**
- Plug speakers or a recorder into the **main output** (XLR or RCA), not just headphones.
- Raise the master fader and check if the output volume changes.
- If yes, the issue is isolated to **headphone monitoring**, not actual output.

### 4. **Inspect for mode switch (if using FX loop or external routing)**
- Double-check you’re not in some **send/return mode** or have **external FX routing** affecting the signal path.

If the master fader truly has **zero effect on output unless cue is active**, and the above don’t apply, that may indicate:
- **Internal routing misconfiguration**, or
- **A fault in the master VCA or fader circuitry.**

Let me know how you're testing the signal path (e.g., headphones only, or through speakers), and I can walk you through deeper diagnostics.

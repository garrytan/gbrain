## User

On average, how many days before or after a due date do women usually actually have their baby?

---

## Assistant

On average, most women give birth **around 5 days before or after** their estimated due date (EDD). However, there’s wide variation:

- **About 4%** of women deliver *on* their due date.  
- **Roughly 80%** give birth between **37 and 41 weeks** of gestation (term).  
- **First-time mothers** tend to deliver **a few days after** their due date, on average around **40 weeks + 3 days**.  
- **Multiparous women** (who’ve had previous births) often deliver **slightly earlier**, around **39½ to 40 weeks**.

So while the “due date” marks 40 weeks of pregnancy, it’s really just the midpoint of a normal delivery window spanning **about ±7–10 days** for most women.

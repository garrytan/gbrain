## User

Does hiding like count hurt reach

---

## Assistant

No. Hiding like counts on Instagram, Facebook, or similar platforms does **not** reduce reach. Meta has stated the visibility of your post in feeds and recommendations is determined by engagement signals (actual likes, comments, shares, watch time, etc.), not whether you display the like number publicly.  

Key points:  
- **Likes still count internally.** The platform still records them for ranking.  
- **User behavior drives reach.** Comments, saves, shares, watch time, and profile clicks remain the main factors.  
- **Perception effect.** If hiding counts discourages people from liking or sharing because they can’t see social proof, you *could* see slightly less engagement, but that’s indirect and not algorithmic.  

Bottom line: hiding the like count itself doesn’t hurt distribution; only lower real engagement would.

You’re basically building a “sexy luxury couple x destination” brand. Let’s make this efficient and ruthless, not a 12-hour shoot day.

## 1. Overall strategy (for getting free stays)

**Positioning:**  
“You give us the stay, we give you: 1–2 cinematic reels + 10–20 high-end photos that sell your property as an aspirational romantic/fitness getaway.”

**For each property, aim for:**

- **Reels (vertical):**
  - 1 x **Property hero reel** (focus on space, you two as lifestyle dressing it up)
  - 1 x **Couple romance / hot tub reel**
  - 1 x **Fitness / 8-pack / pool/bathroom ‘glam’ reel** (if it fits the property)
- **Photos (for host):**
  - 10–15 clean, host-usable stills showing **rooms, views, details, and you two as scale/aspiration**
- **Photos (for your grid):**
  - 6–10 “banger” shots of you + girlfriend (white / neutral tones, fits your IG palette)

This gives you:
- Deliverables to DM hosts (“Here’s what we did for these places.”)
- A repeatable template so each monthly Joshua Tree trip is plug-and-play.

---

## 2. Gear strategy (to keep it fast)

Given your skills and the “minimal fuss” goal:

- **Primary:** iPhone 15 Pro (vertical **4K 30** or 60, HDR off unless you love grading it)
- **Sometimes:** iPhone gimbal for:
  - 2–3 **hero walkthroughs**
  - 1–2 smooth “orbit” shots around hot tub/bed/sofa
- **Drone (Mavic Mini):** 3–5 shots per property:
  - 1 wide establishing
  - 1 push-in or pull-out
  - 1 top-down or reveal of hot tub/deck
  - (Optional) sunset orbit
- **Sony a7III + 85mm:** only for
  - Hero portraits of you two
  - A couple of ultra-shallow DOF detail shots (hands, fabric, steam, etc.)
- **Osmo Action:** only if there’s water / wild movement (splash shots, inside hot tub, POV stuff)

The rule:  
**If it takes more than 60 seconds to set up, you probably don’t need it.**  
iPhone does 90% of the heavy lifting; the rest is occasional spice.

## 3. Time-boxed shoot schedule (per property)

Keep it to ~90 focused minutes total, split across the day:

**Session 1 – Arrival / Daylight (25–30 min)**  
Goal: clean empty-space + functional shots.

1. **5–10 min quick reset**
   - Hide clutter, bags, ugly items.
   - Open curtains, turn on all lights, light candles/lamps.

2. **15–20 min property coverage (iPhone + a few Sony stills if fast)**
   - Shoot **empty rooms first**: no people, no luggage.
   - Get 3–5 seconds of movement per angle (slow pan, push-in, small tilt).

---

**Session 2 – Golden Hour (35–40 min)**  
Goal: couple + fitness + romance.

1. **20–25 min couple & 8-pack content (iPhone + Sony)**
   - You shirtless / open shirt, her in something elegant/sexy but classy.
   - Rotate through 2–3 key locations: bedroom, deck, hot tub or bath.

2. **10–15 min drone + gimbal**
   - Drone reveals at sunset.
   - One or two gimbal walks: arriving with bags, holding hands, walking to tub.

---

**Session 3 – Night / Hot Tub (15–25 min)**  
Goal: “boujee desert night” vibe.

- LED / practical lighting, maybe candles.
- Steam, silhouettes, close-ups, clinking glasses.
- 1 short reel’s worth of footage plus a few stills.

Then you’re done. Phone goes away, enjoy yourselves.

## 5. Reel concepts (copy-paste templates)

All vertical, 10–20 seconds, quick cuts, heavy on texture.

1. **“Welcome to our desert escape” – Property hero**
   - Sequence:
     1. Door handle → push in.
     2. Living room wide.
     3. Kitchen.
     4. Bedroom.
     5. Bathroom/tub/hot tub.
     6. Exterior + drone.
   - Use you two minimally (walking through shots, small in frame).
   - Host-friendly version: mostly space, little face.

2. **“Couples retreat in Joshua Tree” – Romance reel**
   - Beat list:
     - Coffee in bed.
     - Laughing on sofa.
     - Walking hand in hand in desert.
     - Sunset hot tub.
     - Night shot with stars/lanterns.
   - Use emotional VO later if you want, or trending audio.

3. **“Desert fitness x luxury” – Body-forward reel**
   - Sequence:
     - Wake up, stretch by window.
     - Push-ups / crunches on deck or inside.
     - Shower/bath water on abs.
     - Chill in robe/shorts on sofa or bed.
   - This sells the angle: the place is a “fitness retreat” backdrop.

4. **“Night in the desert” – Hot tub / mood reel**
   - Candle lighting, bokeh, slow movement.
   - Clink glasses, steam, close-up of hands, silhouettes.
   - Great for both hosts and your grid.

## 6. Workflow to keep it stupid simple

- **Always shoot vertical** for social, even on the Sony (just rotate camera).
- For each zone:
  - **Shoot video first** (move through 3–4 angles quickly).
  - Then **snap 3–5 stills** from best compositions.
- **Grab 2–3 clean plates** (no people, no props) in each main room:
  - Perfect for planar tracking, text, logos, host overlays later.
- **Use one or two LUTs / looks** across all properties:
  - “Desert warm” & “Clean white minimal.”
  - Keeps your grid consistent and fast to grade.

## 7. Packaging for property owners (once content is shot)

After this trip, create:

- **IG Highlight:** “For Hosts”
  - Put your best property hero reels & a few story frames with “Deliverables we provide”.
- **Pinned reel on your grid:**
  - Montage of several properties (for now, just the 2 Airbnbs), with your VO:
    - “We create cinematic content for boutique stays: photos + reels that drive bookings.”
- **Carousel post per property:**
  - 1st image: clean hero of space
  - 2–3: details + you two lifestyle
  - Last slide: text panel with small, clean pitch.

Then your DM to future hosts is just:
“Hey, we’d love to create something like this for your place in exchange for a 2–3 night stay.”

In Los Angeles, after-hours parties (events that continue beyond standard closing times, typically 2 a.m. for bars and clubs) can be legal or illegal depending on how they’re organized and licensed. Generally:

1. **Licenses & Permits**: 
   - To legally host events that include alcohol or live entertainment, organizers must have the proper permits from the city and from the California Department of Alcoholic Beverage Control (ABC).  
   - If an event runs past the usual closing time without the necessary extended-hours or special-event permits, it can be considered unlicensed and therefore illegal.

2. **Venue Zoning & Fire Code**:
   - The space must be zoned for events and meet fire and safety regulations (such as capacity limits, emergency exits, etc.). Hosting a party in a space not permitted for assembly, or over capacity, is a violation.

3. **Noise & Public Nuisance Ordinances**: 
   - L.A. has noise ordinances and other regulations that can affect an after-hours party’s legality. Complaints or police inspections could shut an unpermitted event down.

As for specific after-hours events like “Underwear,” “Affinity,” or “TeckMeOut,” whether they’re legal depends on the details mentioned above—namely venue permits, licensing, and compliance with local regulations. Some promoters operate in the gray area: they may not secure all permits, making their events subject to shutdown or citation if law enforcement intervenes.

If you have concerns about a specific party or venue, verify that they have the necessary city and ABC permissions. If they do, it’s likely a legal event; if not, it’s likely not in compliance.

For a definitive interpretation, consult a qualified attorney or check local ordinances and the Department of Alcoholic Beverage Control’s permit listings.

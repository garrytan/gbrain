## User

Will a cord show no power if it’s plugged in one end and not the other

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, it depends which end is plugged in.

If the cord is plugged into a **wall outlet** on one end and the other end is unplugged/open, the cord can still be **live** at the far end. An extension cord’s female end should show voltage between **hot and neutral** if tested properly.

If the cord is plugged into a **device/appliance** on one end but **not plugged into the wall**, it should show **no power**, unless the device has a battery, capacitor, inverter, generator connection, or another power source.

A few caveats:

- A **non-contact voltage tester** may fail to beep on some cords depending on shielding, distance, or how the wires sit inside the jacket.
- A **multimeter** should show about **120 V AC** in the U.S. between hot and neutral at the live end of an energized extension cord.
- Never assume a cord is dead because a tester shows nothing. Verify with a known-working meter/tester and avoid touching any exposed metal conductors.

If you mean a specific cord setup, the key question is: **which end is connected to the wall/power source?**

## Assistant

Your symptoms could be indicative of a common cold, allergic rhinitis (hay fever), or a sinus infection. Consider factors like duration, exposure to allergens, and whether symptoms improve indoors to distinguish between allergies and infections. Over-the-counter antihistamines and decongestants may help with symptoms. Staying hydrated and rest are also beneficial. If symptoms persist or worsen, consider consulting a healthcare provider for a precise diagnosis and treatment.

## Assistant

For the symptoms you're experiencing, over-the-counter (OTC) medications can provide relief:

1. **Antihistamines** (like loratadine, cetirizine, or fexofenadine) can help with sneezing, runny nose, and watery eyes.
2. **Decongestants** (such as pseudoephedrine or phenylephrine) may reduce nasal congestion.
3. **Pain relievers** (like acetaminophen or ibuprofen) can alleviate headaches and any slight pressure in your head.
4. **Throat lozenges** and **saline nasal sprays** can soothe a dry throat and moisten nasal passages.

Remember, if you have any pre-existing health conditions or are on other medications, it’s wise to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new medication to avoid interactions.

## Assistant

Mr. White, that sounds brutal. Here’s the cleanest way forward—pick the path you want, then act fast.

# 1) Decide your goal
- **Return with boundaries** or **exit cleanly**. Do it within 24–48 hours while memories are fresh.

# 2) Lock down evidence (now)
- Write a **timeline** (times, who was present, exact words).  
- Save texts/emails/DMs.  
- List **witnesses** (staff/guests). Ask if there’s **camera footage** from the floor.

# 3) If you want to stay (conditional return)
Send this, word-for-word if you like:
> Subject: Last night  
> I left under duress after being publicly berated by Vincent following his late arrival. I value the team and guests and am willing to continue **if** we agree to: (1) no public confrontations—concerns addressed privately; (2) clear call-time expectations applied consistently; (3) a mediated meeting with [GM/Owner/HR] this week.  
> Please confirm by [date/time]. If not, treat my resignation as final.

# 4) If you’re done (professional exit)
> Subject: Resignation confirmation  
> Please accept this as confirmation of my resignation effective [date/time]. I left under duress after a public confrontation by Vincent.  
> Please confirm: (1) timing of my **final paycheck**, (2) payout of any **accrued vacation/PTO**, and (3) how to return any property. Share a contact for any follow-up.

# 5) Quick California facts (keep you protected)
- **Final pay**: If you quit **without notice**, wages are due **within 72 hours**. If you give **≥72 hours notice**, they’re due **at your last shift**. Accrued **vacation** must be paid out. If they’re late **willfully**, there can be **waiting-time penalties** (up to 30 days of wages).  
- **Unemployment**: Quitting usually disqualifies you **unless** you had “good cause” (e.g., intolerable conditions) **and** tried to fix it first. A single incident may not be enough; a documented **pattern** helps.  
- **Hostile conduct**: Public berating is unprofessional; it’s unlawful discrimination/harassment only if tied to a **protected class** or **severe/pervasive**. If there’s a pattern, talk to an employment attorney.

# 6) Reputation control
- Keep outreach **short, factual, and non-emotional**.  
- Don’t debate Vincent directly; route everything through the GM/owner/HR.

If you want, tell me which path you’re choosing and I’ll tailor the email with names, dates, and a punchier timeline.

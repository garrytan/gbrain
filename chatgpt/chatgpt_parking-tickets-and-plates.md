**Short answer:** Yes — in California, a parking ticket issued while your vehicle was displaying *temporary plates* still stays attached to the vehicle’s registration and must be dealt with even after you get *permanent plates*.  

Here’s why:

- In California the temporary license plate / temporary operating permit is legally tied to *your vehicle* and its registration record. Temporary plates are simply placeholders until permanent plates arrive. Once permanent plates are issued, the DMV updates the vehicle record to reflect the new plate number, but the underlying vehicle record still shows any parking/toll violations that occurred before the change. citeturn0search30turn0search9

- **Parking/toll violations show up on the vehicle’s record** and must be cleared before certain DMV actions (like renewing registration) can proceed. The unpaid violation doesn’t “vanish” just because the plate number changed. citeturn0search9

- If the citation was issued with the *incorrect* plate number (e.g., wrong temp plate or typo), you may be able to contest it with the issuing agency by showing the correct VIN/registration — but that’s a separate issue from how the DMV treats the violation. citeturn0search2

**Practical implication:**  
You remain responsible for the ticket even after switching to your permanent plate. Unpaid parking tickets can lead to penalties, holds on registration renewal, or collections. To resolve it, either pay the citation or contest it with the issuing agency if you have a good factual basis (e.g., plate error). citeturn0search9

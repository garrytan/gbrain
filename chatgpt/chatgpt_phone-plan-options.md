## Assistant

Here’s a concise breakdown for you, Mr. White:

### 1. Google Fi Essentials vs. Your Current $50 Plan  
- **What you lose:**  
  *No international data/calling* (no roaming in Canada/Mexico and slower overseas data), and data deprioritization can kick in sooner in very congested areas.  
- **What stays the same:**  
  Unlimited domestic talk/text, 5G/LTE data, Wi-Fi calling, and Google’s simple billing.  
- **Impact on you:**  
  Given your heavy Wi-Fi use, light mobile data, and no current need for Canada/Mexico service, you’d notice almost no difference.

### 2. Phone Upgrade Options  
- **Buy iPhone 17 Pro outright + stay on Fi**  
  *Pros:* Keep cheap plan, no contract, easy to switch later.  
  *Cons:* Pay full price up front (~$1.1–1.3k).  
- **Apple iPhone Upgrade Program**  
  *Pros:* 0% interest, new phone every year, AppleCare+.  
  *Cons:* Requires carrier activation (Fi works, but you’d pay the same as buying outright—no carrier subsidy).  
- **Carrier subsidy (T-Mobile/Verizon/AT&T “free iPhone” deals)**  
  *Pros:* Up-front discount/credits if you trade in and commit 24–36 months.  
  *Cons:* Higher monthly plan cost ($70-90+), early-termination penalties, hidden fees.

Given your minimal data use, those big-carrier plans erase any “free phone” savings. Over two years the extra $30-40/month is ~$800-1,000—close to the phone’s price.

### 3. Alternatives  
- **Mint Mobile, Boost Infinite, US Mobile:**  
  $15-25/month for 5–20 GB. Great if you’re fine with T-Mobile network and no perks.  
- **T-Mobile Prepaid Connect ($25 for 6 GB)** or **T-Mobile Essentials Prepaid ($50 unlimited)** if you ever need more roaming.

### 4. Recommendation  
- **Cheapest, simplest:** Switch to Google Fi Unlimited Essentials ($35). Buy the iPhone 17 Pro outright or via Apple’s 0% Upgrade Program.  
- **If you ever add Liv:** Fi’s multi-line price drops to $30/line, still cheaper than big carriers. You can later merge her when she leaves her family plan.  
- **If cash flow is key:** Apple Upgrade Program spreads payments at 0% without forcing a carrier change.

Bottom line: **stay on Google Fi, downgrade to Essentials, and finance through Apple if you prefer monthly payments.** Big-carrier “free phone” deals cost more long-term.

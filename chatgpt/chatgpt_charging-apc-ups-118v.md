If it's been a day and your APC Back-UPS 1000 unit hasn't fully charged, there may be a few possibilities to consider:

1. **Battery Condition**: If the battery is old or has been through many discharge cycles, its ability to hold a charge may be diminished.
2. **Defective Battery**: The battery could be defective or damaged, preventing a full charge.
3. **High Load**: If there is a high load on the UPS, it might be using power as quickly as it's being charged.
4. **Charging System Issue**: There might be an issue with the UPS's charging system.

It’s a good idea to check the user manual for troubleshooting steps or to contact APC support for assistance. If the unit is still under warranty, you may be entitled to a repair or replacement.

If your APC Back-UPS 1000 is not charging after being plugged in for an extended period:

1. **Reset the UPS**: Sometimes electronics need a reset. Turn off the UPS, unplug it from the wall, disconnect the devices, wait a few minutes, and then reconnect everything.

3. **Battery Replacement**: If the UPS is older, the battery may have reached the end of its useful life and needs replacement.
4. **Check the Outlet**: Make sure the outlet is providing power by connecting another device to it.
5. **Check for Fault Indicators**: Some UPS units have fault indicators that can help diagnose the issue.
6. **Firmware Update**: Check if your UPS has a firmware update available that can be applied.
7. **Professional Inspection**: If none of the above steps help, the UPS itself may be faulty.

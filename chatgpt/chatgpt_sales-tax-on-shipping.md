## User

if my billig address is deleware based can i get a macbook pro with zero sales tax shipped to me in california?

---

## Assistant

No — you **cannot avoid California tax simply by using a Delaware billing address** if the laptop is **shipped to California**. Facts:

- **Sales tax is generally charged based on the *shipping (destination) address***, not billing address. Retailers calculate sales tax on the state where the goods are delivered. citeturn0search2turn0search11  
- Apple and other major retailers will charge sales tax at checkout based on your **California shipping address**, regardless of where your billing address is. citeturn0search1  
- Delaware has **no sales tax**, but that only helps if the item is **delivered to a Delaware address**. citeturn0search0turn0search24  
- Even if a retailer didn’t collect sales tax, **California law imposes a *use tax*** on out-of-state purchases that are used, stored, or consumed in California. You’d owe that to the state on your tax return. citeturn0search15

**Bottom line:** Billing to Delaware won’t get you a zero-tax MacBook if the laptop is shipped to California. You either need it shipped to a Delaware address to avoid California sales tax at purchase, or you’ll owe California use tax on the item.

## Assistant

Mr. White — **there is no legal “hack” to eliminate California tax** on a MacBook that ends up used in California. California closed the common loopholes years ago. Facts only:

### Legitimate ways to reduce or avoid tax
1. **Ship to and keep it out of California**
   - Ship to a **Delaware (or other no-sales-tax state) address**
   - **Do not bring it into CA for at least 90 days**
   - If you bring it back sooner, **CA use tax applies**
   - Burden of proof is on *you* if audited

2. **Buy through a tax-exempt entity**
   - Must be a **validly registered CA tax-exempt organization**
   - Your personal LLC or C-corp **does not qualify**
   - Apple will verify exemption status

3. **Employer purchase**
   - Employer buys it, owns it, issues it to you
   - Employer pays tax, but you avoid out-of-pocket cost
   - Still not “zero tax,” just shifted

4. **Authorized reseller discounts**
   - Education, corporate, or refurbished units
   - Tax still applies, but **lower base price**
   - Refurbished Macs from Apple are effectively the only “clean” savings

5. **Out-of-state personal purchase while traveling**
   - Only works if:
     - You buy it
     - **Keep it out of CA >90 days**
     - Can document travel + possession
   - Otherwise: use tax owed

### What does **not** work (and risks penalties)
- Delaware billing address only ❌  
- Shipping to a friend then immediately bringing it back ❌  
- Marking as “gift” ❌  
- Asking retailer not to charge tax ❌  
- Crypto / cash / foreign card ❌  

### Reality check
California aggressively enforces **use tax**. High-ticket electronics are an audit favorite. Penalties + interest erase any “savings.”

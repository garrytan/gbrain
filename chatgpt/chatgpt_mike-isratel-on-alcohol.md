## Pre-workout booze

Mike would tell you this is just dumb:

- **Performance hit:** Alcohol pre-training reduces coordination, strength, and power and raises injury risk.  
- **Recovery hit starts early:** You’re already slightly dehydrated and impairing the recovery processes you’re about to need from that session.  
- In practice his advice is effectively:
  - **0 drinks in the several hours before training** if you care about performance and progress.
  - If you’re going to drink, **don’t train buzzed, period.**

---

## Post-workout booze

This is the timing he hates most:

- **Blunts muscle protein synthesis**, especially when drunk in the few hours after lifting. It interferes with **mTOR signaling** and increases inflammation, which directly reduces the muscle growth response to that session. citeturn0search14  
- **Worsens recovery later** via:
  - worse sleep quality,
  - more dehydration,
  - poorer glycogen replenishment and nutrient use. citeturn0search8turn0search14  

The heavier the session and the heavier the drinking, the more you waste that workout.

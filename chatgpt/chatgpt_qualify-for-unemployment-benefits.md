## Assistant

To qualify for unemployment benefits in California, you generally need to meet the following criteria:

1. **Employment Status**: You must be either completely unemployed or underemployed (working less than full-time and earning less than your weekly benefit amount).
  
2. **Wages Earned**: You must have earned enough wages during a specific 12-month period, known as the base period, prior to losing your job.

3. **Job Separation**: Your job separation must be through no fault of your own. This can include layoffs, reductions in force, or other similar circumstances not related to misconduct or voluntary resignation without good cause.

4. **Availability**: You must be physically able and available to work and ready to accept work immediately.

5. **Legal Authorization to Work**: You must be authorized to work in the U.S.

6. **Active Job Search**: You are required to actively seek employment each week that you file a claim.

Once eligible, you can file a claim with the California Employment Development Department (EDD) either online, by phone, or by mail. After filing, you'll need to continue certifying for benefits biweekly, reporting any earnings and your job search efforts.

Where does the bag fee in California go

## Assistant

In California, the 10¢ bag charge (imposed under SB 270 and upheld by Prop 67) is **not a tax**—it's a **cost pass-through** that is **retained by the retailer**. Here's how it's used:

---

### 🛍️ Where the money goes:

- **Retailers keep the full 10¢** (none goes to the state).
- Funds are legally required to be used for:
  - Covering **actual costs of providing the paper or reusable bags**
  - **Compliance efforts** (e.g., printing fees, reporting)
  - Any **in‑store educational materials or programs** to encourage reusable bag use citeturn0search1turn0search6turn0search19

---

### 🌿 What the charge is **not** for:

- It’s **not revenue for environmental cleanup**, conservation projects, or government funds citeturn0search4turn0search19turn0search10  
- It’s **not taxable**, so stores cannot add sales tax on top of it citeturn0search6turn0search10

---

### 🧾 Reporting & Compliance:

- Stores must **report quarterly**:
  - How many bags they sold
  - The amount collected
  - Their educational efforts citeturn0search6turn0search12

---

### ✅ Why this setup?

- The fee serves as an **incentive to use reusable bags**
- It helps offset retailer costs without being regressive or burdensome on low-income customers (who are exempt) citeturn0search1turn0search10turn0search19

---

**In summary:**  
That 10¢ bag fee stays with the store—covering their costs (bags, compliance, and education)—and does *not* go toward environmental programs or government revenue.

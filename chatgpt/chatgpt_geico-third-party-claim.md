## Assistant

Mr. White, **do not assume it is safe to drive**. A check engine light coming on immediately after impact usually means something got damaged, disconnected, or jolted.

## Likely causes from a front-side hit

Most likely:

1. **Damaged/disconnected sensor or wiring**
   - Front bumper/headlight/wheel-well areas have wiring harnesses and sensors.
   - A loose connector can trigger the light.

2. **Air intake / MAF sensor issue**
   - If the hit shifted or cracked intake parts, the engine may read bad airflow.

3. **Radiator/cooling system damage**
   - If the radiator, fan, coolant line, or condenser got hit, you could overheat.

4. **EVAP/emissions line damage**
   - Less dangerous, but can trigger check engine.

5. **Battery/ground/connector issue**
   - Impact can loosen electrical connections.

6. **Wheel/suspension damage unrelated to engine light**
   - This is the bigger safety risk if the wheel well was hit. The car may drive, but the tire, control arm, alignment, brake line, or axle could be compromised.

## Safe-to-drive rule

### Do **not** drive it if any of these are true:
- Check engine light is **flashing**
- Car is overheating
- Leaking fluid
- Smells like burning, fuel, coolant, or electrical
- Steering feels off
- Brakes feel different
- Tire rubs, wobbles, or pulls
- Weird grinding/clicking from the damaged wheel area
- Headlight/bumper/wheel-well pieces are loose and could hit the tire

### If the light is solid and the car feels normal:
You can probably move it **a very short distance** out of traffic or to a nearby safe spot, but I would **not drive it across LA**. Get it scanned/towed to a shop.

## Best move for the claim

Tell GEICO:

> “The check engine light came on after the collision, and there is front-side damage near the wheel/headlight. I do not feel safe driving it. I need GEICO to authorize a tow and inspection.”

This helps establish that the CEL is **collision-related**, not random maintenance. Take a photo/video of the dashboard with the light on before anyone touches the car.

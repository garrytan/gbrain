## Assistant

For music marketing specifically — Meta/Instagram/TikTok ads driving to Spotify or smart links like Unhurd, Hypeddit, Feature.fm, etc. — a “good” cost per click is usually:

- **$0.10–$0.30 CPC** = strong
- **$0.30–$0.50 CPC** = acceptable
- **$0.50+ CPC** = weak creative/targeting in most cases

That range comes up consistently across music marketing case studies and practitioner benchmarks. citeturn0search0turn0search4turn0search6turn0search8turn0search13

More important than CPC is usually:
- **Cost per Spotify conversion**
- **Cost per follower/save**
- **Listener quality**

Andrew Southworth’s benchmarks — probably the most commonly referenced in indie music ads — say:
- Under **$0.20 per Spotify conversion** = phenomenal
- **$0.20–$0.30** = great
- **$0.30–$0.40** = good
- **$0.40–$0.50** = mediocre citeturn0search4turn0search13

For Tier-1 countries only (US/UK/CA/AU), costs are often roughly double that. citeturn0search4

Important distinction:
- A lot of artists brag about cheap clicks from India/Brazil/etc.
- Cheap clicks do not necessarily produce algorithmic lift or long-term fans.
- Spotify’s recommendation system appears to value repeat listening, saves, playlists, and completion rate more than raw traffic volume.

So if someone says:
> “I got $0.05 clicks”

that can still be a terrible campaign if:
- nobody saves the song
- streams are low quality
- listeners churn instantly
- geo mix is weak for monetization/touring

For Jovie, the more interesting KPI is probably:
- cost per owned fan
- cost per alert opt-in
- lifetime value of an owned audience member
- downstream revenue per triggered release moment

That’s a much stronger business than optimizing for cheap Spotify traffic.

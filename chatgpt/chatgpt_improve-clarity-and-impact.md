## Assistant

Mr. White, this is a strong piece already. Improvements should sharpen clarity, authority, and conversion—not add length.

### High-impact improvements

**1. Kill repetition early**
- The MySpace → Facebook point is clear by ~30%. Tighten the first third by ~20–25%.
- Remove any sentence that restates “customization bad, simplicity good” without adding a new angle.

**2. Front-load the thesis**
- Move a sharper version of this up top (within the first 3 paragraphs):  
  *“Unbounded customization doesn’t empower users—it burdens them. Winning products remove decisions users shouldn’t have to make.”*
- This primes readers before the narrative.

**3. Reduce brand-name scatter**
- Apple, Facebook, Tinder, Amazon, Stripe, Shopify, Kaskade, Google, NFL, Tory Lanez—this is a lot.
- Keep only the **best 3–4 examples**. Too many weakens authority instead of strengthening it.

**4. Quantify more, assert less**
- The iOS vs Android stat is good. Add **1–2 more concrete numbers**:
  - Conversion lift from fewer CTAs
  - Drop-off from choice overload (even approximate ranges)
- Facts > rhetoric.

**5. Shorten sentences in the middle third**
- There’s a noticeable tempo slowdown after the Apple/iPod section.
- Aim for:
  - More single-line paragraphs
  - Fewer compound sentences
- This keeps scan-readers engaged.

**6. Separate insight from pitch more cleanly**
- The Jovie transition is good but slightly long.
- Cut the product section by ~30% and end with one decisive line, e.g.:
  *“The product is opinionated so the artist doesn’t have to be.”*

**7. Stronger final punch**
Current ending is philosophically sound but soft. Consider a sharper close:
- Reframe responsibility vs freedom
- End on inevitability, not preference

Example direction:
> *Products don’t fail because users lack creativity.  
They fail because users are asked to make decisions they never should have had.*

Short answer: **you must contest, not pay**, if you want the late fees removed. Paying locks in the full amount.

### What matters legally (Los Angeles)
Under Los Angeles Parking Violations Bureau rules, a parking citation must be **properly served** (placed on the vehicle or handed to the driver). If the ticket was removed before you could see it, you can argue **lack of proper notice**, which is a recognized basis to **waive late penalties**.

### What to do — step by step (facts, not fluff)
1. **Do NOT pay online yet.** Payment = admission + acceptance of penalties.
2. **Request an Initial Review immediately** with the **entity["organization","Los Angeles Parking Violations Bureau","city parking enforcement la"]**.
3. In the review, state clearly:
   - Citations were **never received**.
   - Tickets were **removed from the vehicle**.
   - You first learned of them after penalties were added.
4. **Request reversion to the base fine** ($73 each) due to improper service.
5. Attach:
   - Photos of your usual parking location (if available).
   - A short declaration (your written statement is sufficient).
6. Submit **before paying anything**.

### If Initial Review is denied
- Request an **Administrative Hearing**.
- Pay the amount **under protest** (required for a hearing).
- If you win, **late fees are refunded**.

### Important reality check
- LA often **does waive late fees** when non-service is credibly claimed.
- They almost never do it **after voluntary payment**.
- Partial payment does **not** stop enforcement or DMV holds.

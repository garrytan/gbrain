## Assistant

Mr. White — short answer: **yes**, you can form a brand-new Delaware C-corp with a fresh cap table. But do these few things so it’s clean and low-risk:

1) **Check & reserve the name.** If the old “Jovie, Inc.” went **void** for nonpayment, the exact name often becomes available for others to reserve. Confirm availability and reserve it before you do anything else. citeturn1search18turn1search1turn1search13turn1search4

2) **Know your old entity’s status.** When you stop paying DE franchise tax, your charter becomes **void** and corporate powers go inoperative (DGCL §510). You can **revive** anytime by paying what’s due (or a capped amount if >5 yrs) and filing a Certificate of Revival (DGCL §312). citeturn0search13turn1search11

3) **Move assets the right way.** If the old corporation owned anything (domain, code, accounts), revive it, assign those assets to you or NewCo, then dissolve. On revival, all property/rights vest back as if the charter never lapsed (DGCL §312). citeturn1search11

4) **Shut the old company down properly (optional but smart).** After assignments, file a **certificate of dissolution** (DGCL §275), then wind up (3-year tail under §278). If you want maximum protection against surprise claims, use the **§280 notice** “safe harbor.” citeturn2search4turn2search2turn2search3

5) **SAFEs from 2021 stay with OldCo.** They don’t carry to NewCo unless you **assume** them. If you reuse the same brand/assets without a proper asset purchase and clear disclosures, investors could try a **successor-liability** theory (de facto merger/mere continuation). Mitigate by doing a documented asset sale with **no assumption of liabilities** and, ideally, get investor releases. citeturn0search3

6) **Trademark reality check.** No prior federal mark = not the same as “free.” Others may have **common-law** rights; run clearance and file a USPTO application fast to lock nationwide rights. citeturn0search4turn0search22turn0search17

### Bottom line
Form **NewCo** with a clean cap table → revive OldCo → assign the domain/IP to NewCo → dissolve OldCo → file/lock the trademark. That keeps your investors’ old SAFEs ring-fenced and lets you reuse “Jovie” without drama. If you want, I’ll draft a 1-page action checklist and the asset-assignment template.

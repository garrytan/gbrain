## User

I need 9.8 liters of cranberry. It comes in 1.77 liter bottles. How many bottles do I need?

---

## Assistant

You will need 6 bottles of cranberry juice to get 9.8 liters.

Here's the calculation:

\[
\text{Number of bottles} = \frac{9.8 \text{ liters}}{1.77 \text{ liters per bottle}} \approx 5.53
\]

Since you can't purchase a fraction of a bottle, you need to round up to the next whole number, which is 6 bottles.

---

## User

I poured out five bottles. How many liters do I have left?

---

## Assistant

If you poured out five bottles, you would have:

\[
5 \times 1.77 \text{ liters} = 8.85 \text{ liters}
\]

Since you initially needed 9.8 liters, you now have:

\[
9.8 \text{ liters} - 8.85 \text{ liters} = 0.95 \text{ liters}
\]

You have 0.95 liters left.

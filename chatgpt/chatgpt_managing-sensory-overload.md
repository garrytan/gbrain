## User

Sensory overload

---

## Assistant

Sensory overload occurs when one or more of your senses are overstimulated, making it difficult to process all the incoming information. This can lead to feelings of being overwhelmed, anxious, or unable to focus. Common triggers include loud noises, bright lights, crowded spaces, and strong smells. 

To manage sensory overload, you can:

1. **Take Breaks:** Find a quiet space to regroup.
2. **Use Noise-Cancelling Headphones:** To block out excessive noise.
3. **Wear Sunglasses:** To reduce the impact of bright lights.
4. **Limit Exposure:** Reduce time spent in environments known to trigger overload.
5. **Practice Deep Breathing or Meditation:** To calm your mind and body.

These strategies can help mitigate the effects and provide relief in overstimulating situations.

## User

I am find in 1 on 1 situations or when I’m speaking to thousands. But when I’m at a table with multiple simultaneous conversations happening, I listen to all of them at once and focus on none. I then start to disassociate and sometimes become overwhelmed. What is this?

---

## Assistant

It sounds like you are experiencing a form of sensory overload specific to auditory stimuli, particularly in environments with multiple simultaneous conversations. This can lead to difficulty focusing, feelings of being overwhelmed, and disassociation. Here are a few strategies that might help:

1. **Positioning:** Sit at the end of the table or in a spot where you can focus on fewer conversations.
2. **Active Listening:** Consciously focus on one conversation at a time and tune out the rest.
3. **Use Earplugs:** Consider discreet earplugs that reduce background noise but still allow you to hear the primary conversation.
4. **Take Breaks:** Excuse yourself for short breaks if you start feeling overwhelmed.
5. **Mindfulness Techniques:** Practice deep breathing or grounding techniques to help maintain focus and reduce anxiety.

These approaches can help manage and mitigate the feelings of sensory overload in social settings with multiple conversations.

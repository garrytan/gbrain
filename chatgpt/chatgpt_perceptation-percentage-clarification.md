It usually means the **chance of measurable precipitation at your specific location** during the forecast period. In National Weather Service terms, a 40% rain forecast means there is a 40% chance that at least 0.01 inch of precipitation will fall at that point during that time window. citeturn676854search0turn676854search2turn676854search3

It does **not** mean 40% of the area will get rain, and it does **not** mean it will rain for 40% of the time. citeturn676854search1turn676854search3

One nuance: forecasters can derive that number using confidence × expected area coverage, but the percentage shown to you is still interpreted as the **probability of rain at your location**, not area coverage. citeturn676854search1turn676854search0

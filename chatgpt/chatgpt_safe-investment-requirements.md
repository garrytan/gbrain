## User

if i request a safe with mercurcy does the investor have to wire the exact amount

---

## Assistant

Mr. White,

Short answer: **yes** — under a SAFE, the investor is generally required to **wire the exact purchase amount stated** in the SAFE.

Key points (California / Y Combinator–style SAFEs):

- A SAFE is a **fixed purchase agreement** for a specific dollar amount.
- The investor’s obligation is to fund **that exact amount**, no more and no less.
- **Partial funding** is **not allowed** unless:
  - You formally amend the SAFE, or  
  - You issue a new SAFE for the lower amount.
- **Overfunding** is also not allowed without a new or amended agreement.
- The SAFE becomes effective only once the **full stated amount** is received.

If an investor wires less than the stated amount:
- The SAFE is typically **not valid or not fully effective**, and
- You should either return the funds or paper it properly with revised docs.

One clarification before I go further:  
Do you mean **SAFE with MFN (Most Favored Nation)** or **SAFE with a maturity date**? “Mercurcy” isn’t a standard SAFE term, and the answer can change slightly depending on which you meant.

## User

i just mean can they send 2 wires for half or does mercury's interface prevent that

---

## Assistant

Mr. White,

**Yes, they can send two wires for half.**  
**No, Mercury does not prevent this.**

Facts:

- Mercury does **not enforce SAFE terms** or amounts.
- It will accept **multiple incoming wires** from the same sender.
- As long as the **total received equals the SAFE purchase amount**, you’re fine.
- The SAFE is considered funded when the **full amount is received**, regardless of how many wires.

What *you* should do:
- Clearly note in your records that both wires relate to the **same SAFE**.
- Don’t countersign or mark the SAFE as effective until the **full amount clears**.

Mercury is just the bank. Compliance with the SAFE is on you, not the interface.

## Assistant

Mr. White, mostly **real methodology, misleading presentation**.

## Verdict

The numbers are **Chipotle marketing estimates**, powered by HowGood, comparing Chipotle’s ingredient sourcing standards against “conventional” industry averages. They are **not measured savings from your specific burrito/bowl**.

Chipotle says Real Foodprint compares each of its 53 ingredients against conventional counterparts across five metrics: carbon, water, soil health, organic land, and antibiotics. Metrics are provided by HowGood, using supplier info plus hundreds of outside data sources. citeturn198905view1

## The important caveat

The National Advertising Division reviewed this and said Chipotle had a **reasonable basis** for the HowGood-backed claims, but recommended Chipotle clarify that app/email/account metrics are based on an **average sustainability assessment**, not the exact environmental impact of the customer’s actual purchase. citeturn198905view0

So the claim:

> “Your order saved 9.5 gallons of water”

should really be read as:

> “Based on Chipotle’s average sourcing assumptions for the ingredients in this order, HowGood estimates this order compares favorably to a conventional equivalent by 9.5 gallons.”

## What is legit

- The metric categories are real Chipotle Real Foodprint categories. citeturn198905view1
- HowGood is a real third-party sustainability data company used for this program. citeturn198905view2
- NAD found the underlying HowGood analysis gave Chipotle a reasonable basis for the Real Foodprint claims. citeturn198905view0

## What is bullshit / inflated

- It frames estimates as if your individual order directly caused those exact savings.
- It compares Chipotle ingredients to “conventional” versions of the same ingredients, rather than helping you compare whether chicken, steak, sofritas, beans, etc. are actually more sustainable choices against each other.
- Critics noted this can make meat items look environmentally “positive” versus conventional meat, while ignoring that plant-based options often have lower footprints overall. citeturn198905view3

## Bottom line

**Fact-check rating: technically supported, marketing-framed, easy to overread.**

The numbers are model-based averages, not verified real-world savings from your exact order.

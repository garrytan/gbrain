###  Quick Overview

- **Origins & Early Significance**  
  Built in **1919**, this lot served as the original home of Warner Brothers’ executive offices and studio facilities citeturn0search0turn0search6. It’s famed as the location where *The Jazz Singer*—the **first feature-length film with synchronized dialogue**—was produced in **1927** citeturn0search0turn0search2.

- **Transition & Repurposing**  
  By the late 1930s, the studio closed and was repurposed as a **bowling alley and sports center**; Stage One became badminton courts, marking a dramatic shift from its cinematic heyday citeturn0search0turn0search6.

- **Television Era & Ownership Changes**  
  In **1954**, TV pioneer Klaus Landsberg acquired the site for Paramount Television and initiated a major redevelopment. While older structures were demolished, key historic elements—including the executive office building and the iconic radio tower—were preserved citeturn0search0turn0search3turn0search6. In the **1960s**, Gene Autry’s Golden West Broadcasters purchased the studio citeturn0search0turn0search22. Later, Tribune Company took over in the **1980s**, dubbing it **Tribune Studios** citeturn0search0turn0search22.

- **Historic Designation & Modern Renovations**  
  The site was officially designated as a **Los Angeles Historic-Cultural Monument** (#180) in **September 1977**, honored as the “Site of the Filming of the First Talking Film” citeturn0search0turn0search21. Its executive office building was listed on the **National Register of Historic Places** in **2002** citeturn0search0turn0search6.

- **Today’s Studio & Facilities**  
  In **2008**, **Hudson Pacific Properties** acquired the property for approximately $125 million and rebranded it as **Sunset Bronson Studios** citeturn0search0turn0search22. The modern campus spans about **4.5 acres** and offers approximately **10 rental sound stages**, with sizes up to **23,000 sq ft** and **31-foot clear heights**, alongside mill, café, production offices, and support amenities. Major tenants include **Netflix, CBS**, and **KTLA** citeturn0search1turn0search8turn0search9.

- **Iconic Office Tower**  
  A striking **14-story Class A office tower**—known as the ICON tower—now stands on the lot’s edge. Designed by Gensler and built by McCarthy, it spans around **323,000 sq ft**, houses Netflix’s L.A. headquarters, achieved **LEED Gold certification**, and integrates state-of-the-art production capabilities with panoramic design features citeturn0search9.

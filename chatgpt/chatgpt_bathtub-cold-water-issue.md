## User

Hey, my shower is hot and cold, and it's great and wonderful and I love life, but my bathtub, when I try and turn the water on, it's only cold, no matter which direction I turn it, and the bath and shower are right next to each other, so what the fuck's going on? Help me out.

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, here are a few possibilities:

1. **Separate Valves or Cartridges** – Even though the fixtures are close, the tub might have a separate (faulty) mixing valve or cartridge preventing hot water from flowing.

2. **Valve Set Incorrectly** – Some tub faucets have an adjustable anti-scald or temperature limit stop. If that’s set too far, you’ll never get hot water.

3. **Sediment or Blockage** – Mineral buildup or debris might be blocking the hot-water line to the tub faucet specifically.

4. **Pressure Balance Issues** – If you have pressure-balance fixtures, a worn-out balancing spool can fail and restrict hot water.

A quick DIY check: remove and inspect the faucet’s cartridge or valve assembly (consult the faucet’s manual or a YouTube tutorial). If it’s gummed up, cleaning or replacing might fix it. Otherwise, you may need a plumber to swap out a faulty mixing valve or check the lines.

## User

So when I turn all the way to hot, it's just like starting to trickle out, but when I turn it over to cold, it comes out full force.

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, when hot water only trickles while cold flows normally, it’s almost always a valve or cartridge issue. Here are the main culprits:

1. **Clogged or Damaged Cartridge:**  
   - Shower and tub often have separate cartridges or internal parts.  
   - If the tub’s hot-water passage is clogged (scale or debris), water flow stalls.  
   - **Check** by shutting off water, removing the faucet handle, then pulling out the cartridge to inspect for debris or wear.

2. **Faulty Pressure-Balancing Valve (If Present):**  
   - Some faucets have a built-in pressure-balancing spool or piston.  
   - If the hot-water side gets stuck, cold dominates.  
   - **Fix** by removing the cartridge/spool, cleaning or replacing it.

3. **Misadjusted or Broken Anti-Scald Device:**  
   - Many fixtures have an adjustable limit to prevent extremely hot water.  
   - If set incorrectly or broken, it can restrict hot flow significantly.  
   - **Solution** is to open the faucet, adjust or replace that piece.

Given that the shower is fine, but the tub fails on hot, the tub faucet internals are suspect. Your first step: remove the tub faucet assembly, locate the cartridge or mixing valve, and see if it’s blocked or broken. If it’s too complicated, a plumber can quickly diagnose and replace the defective parts.

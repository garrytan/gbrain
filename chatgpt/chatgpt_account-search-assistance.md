Mr. White, the easiest way to surface forgotten bank/crypto accounts in Gmail is to search for *bank statements / alerts* and *exchange onboarding/security* emails rather than guessing the institution name.

### What I found in your Gmail searches so far
- Emails from **Chase** include statement notifications and balance alerts for an “account ending in (...1150)” (so you likely have (or had) a Chase account tied to this Gmail address).
- You have **SoFi** emails as well; many look like marketing, but those campaigns often indicate you opened (or almost opened) a SoFi account.

### Gmail search queries that work well
Paste these into the Gmail search bar:

**Banking**
- `statement notification`  
- `"account ending"`  
- `balance alert`  
- `deposit posted`  
- `"account ending" "statement"`  
- `from:(chase.com OR wellsfargo.com OR bankofamerica.com OR capitalone.com OR discover.com OR americanexpress.com)`

**Crypto / exchanges**
- `"verify your email"`  
- `"2-factor"` OR `"two-factor"`  
- `verification code`  
- `KYC`  
- `exchange account`  
- `wallet`  
- `BTC OR ETH OR “crypto”`  
- `from:(coinbase.com OR gemini.com OR kraken.com OR binance.com OR crypto.com OR bitstamp.net OR kucoin.com OR bittrex.com)`

**Attachments (statements/contracts)**
- `has:attachment statement`  
- `has:attachment tax`  
- `has:attachment pdf`  

### Quick triage workflow
1. Search each query above.
2. Sort the results by “Any time → newer first”.
3. Open only the ones from institutions/security departments (ignore newsletters).
4. For each legit account you confirm, write down the institution + last 4 digits shown in the email + any unique ID.

If you tell me any clue (like one exchange you remember using, or whether you used Google Authenticator/SMS 2FA), I can tailor the searches and narrow this down faster.

## User

Can laundry clean mop heads

---

## Assistant

Yes, Mr. White—most mop heads can be cleaned in the washing machine, but do it right:

- **Check the tag:**  
  - **Microfiber & cotton string/flat heads:** machine-washable.  
  - **Sponge heads or ones with metal parts:** hand-wash; don’t machine.

- **Before washing:** shake/rinse out grit and hair. If filthy, 10–15 min presoak in warm water + a little oxygen bleach.

- **Machine settings:**  
  - **Load:** wash mop heads **alone** (or with rags), in a mesh bag if stringy.  
  - **Cycle:** hot water for cotton; warm/cool for microfiber.  
  - **Detergent:** regular; **no fabric softener** (it ruins absorbency).  
  - **Disinfect (choose one):**  
    - ½ cup **bleach** for cotton only, or  
    - 1 cup **white vinegar** in the rinse, or  
    - Oxygen bleach per label (safe for colors/microfiber).

- **Drying:** air-dry fully (best for all); low heat tumble is OK for cotton, **avoid heat for microfiber** if you can.

- **Replace when:** fibers stay gray, smell lingers after washing, or the head frays/sheds.

Also wipe/sanitize the **handle and bucket** (quick bleach or disinfectant wipe) so you’re not re-contaminating a clean head.

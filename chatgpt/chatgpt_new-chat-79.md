## User

Please give me the recipe for a margarita as a bartender would make it.

---

## Assistant

Certainly! Here's a classic margarita recipe as a bartender would make it:

Ingredients:
- 2 oz (60 ml) Tequila
- 1 oz (30 ml) Triple sec or orange liqueur
- 1 oz (30 ml) freshly squeezed lime juice
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) simple syrup (adjust to taste)
- Ice
- Salt for rimming (optional)
- Lime wedge or wheel for garnish

Instructions:
1. If desired, rim the glass: Take a lime wedge and run it along the rim of your glass. Dip the rim into a shallow plate of salt to coat it.

2. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.

3. Add the tequila, triple sec, lime juice, and simple syrup to the shaker.

4. Shake vigorously for about 10-15 seconds to chill the ingredients.

5. Strain the mixture into your salt-rimmed glass filled with ice.

6. Garnish with a lime wedge or wheel.

7. Serve and enjoy your classic margarita!

Feel free to adjust the sweetness or tartness by adding more or less simple syrup, depending on your taste preferences. Cheers!

## User

I am a bartender and my bar does not have sour mix. How can I modify a drink like a Long Island iced tea?

---

## Assistant

If your bar doesn't have sour mix, you can still make a delicious Long Island Iced Tea by using individual components. Here's how to modify the recipe:

Ingredients:
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) Vodka
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) Rum
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) Gin
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) Tequila
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) Triple sec or orange liqueur
- 1 oz (30 ml) freshly squeezed lemon juice
- 1/2 oz (15 ml) simple syrup (adjust to taste)
- Cola (to top off)
- Ice
- Lemon wheel or wedge for garnish

Instructions:

1. Fill a tall glass with ice cubes.

2. In a cocktail shaker, combine the vodka, rum, gin, tequila, triple sec, freshly squeezed lemon juice, and simple syrup.

3. Shake well to mix and chill the ingredients.

4. Strain the mixture into the glass with ice.

5. Top off the glass with cola. You can adjust the amount to your preference, but typically, it's about 2-3 ounces.

6. Give it a gentle stir to combine the ingredients.

7. Garnish with a lemon wheel or wedge.

8. Serve and enjoy your homemade Long Island Iced Tea!

By using fresh lemon juice and simple syrup, you can replicate the sweet and sour components of a traditional Long Island Iced Tea without needing pre-made sour mix. Adjust the sweetness to your liking by adding more or less simple syrup.

## Assistant

Mr. White—if your iPhone camera viewfinder is just a black screen, try these steps in order:

1. **Check for obstructions**  
   – Remove any case or lens cover and wipe the lens; make sure nothing is blocking it.  
2. **Force-quit and reopen**  
   – Swipe up (or double-tap Home) to bring up the app switcher, swipe the Camera app off-screen, then relaunch it.  
3. **Restart your iPhone**  
   – Hold the side button and either volume button, slide to power off, then turn it back on.  
4. **Update iOS**  
   – Settings → General → Software Update. Running the latest version can clear camera bugs.  
5. **Test in another app**  
   – Open FaceTime or Snapchat; if those apps can see through the camera, the problem is with the Camera app itself.  
6. **Reset settings**  
   – Settings → General → Transfer or Reset iPhone → Reset → Reset All Settings (won’t erase data).  
7. **Backup & restore**  
   – As a last software step: back up to iCloud or your computer, then erase and restore your iPhone.  
8. **Hardware check**  
   – If it’s still black, you likely have a hardware fault. Schedule a Genius Bar appointment or visit an Apple-authorized service provider in LA.  

Follow those in sequence; most black-screen issues resolve by rebooting or updating. If it persists after a full restore, it’s almost certainly a lens or sensor failure and needs pro service.

## Assistant

Understood, Mr. White. Focusing purely on software fixes:

1. **Force-quit & relaunch**  
   – Swipe up (or double-tap Home) → swipe the Camera app off-screen → reopen.

2. **Force restart**  
   – iPhone 8 or later: quickly press Volume Up, then Volume Down, then hold Side until Apple logo.

3. **Check camera permissions**  
   – Settings → Privacy & Security → Camera → ensure Camera is enabled for all apps you expect to use it.

4. **Free up storage**  
   – Settings → General → iPhone Storage → delete unused apps/media so iOS can allocate camera buffer.

5. **Disable Screen Time restrictions**  
   – Settings → Screen Time → Content & Privacy Restrictions → Allowed Apps → make sure Camera is on.

6. **Update or reinstall iOS**  
   - Settings → General → Software Update → install latest.  
   - If already up-to-date, back up and do a fresh restore via Finder/iTunes (no erasing yet).

7. **Reset All Settings**  
   – Settings → General → Transfer or Reset iPhone → Reset → Reset All Settings (preserves data).

8. **DFU restore**  
   – As a final software-only measure: connect to a Mac/PC, enter DFU mode and restore iOS.  

If after a DFU restore the viewfinder stays black, it’s virtually certain to be hardware—and you’d need a Genius Bar visit.

## Assistant

If you believe that another party's trademark application should not be granted because it conflicts with your own trademark rights, you can file a notice of opposition with the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO). A notice of opposition is a legal proceeding in which you present evidence and arguments to show why the other party's trademark application should not be approved.

To file a notice of opposition, you will need to provide evidence of your use of the Jovie trademark in commerce, as well as any other evidence that supports your position that the other party's trademark application should be denied. This could include evidence of your prior use of the Jovie trademark, evidence that the other party's use of the Jovie trademark would cause confusion with your own use, or evidence that the other party's use of the Jovie trademark is likely to dilute your own trademark rights.

It is important to note that the notice of opposition process can be complex and time-consuming, and it is generally advisable to seek the assistance of an experienced trademark attorney to help you navigate the process. An attorney can help you to gather and present the necessary evidence and arguments, and can provide guidance on the best strategy for pursuing your opposition.

## Assistant

Mr. White, you’ve got two choices:

1. **Modify the door to take your Yale plate**  
   - Remove the old round “drive-in” latch.  
   - Hold your rectangular Yale face-plate against the door edge, trace around it.  
   - Chisel or rout a shallow mortise so the plate sits flush.  
   - Reinstall the new latch and screw the plate in place.

2. **Use a round-face latch kit**  
   - Buy a “round drive-in latch” compatible with Yale (same backset).  
   - Drop that in your existing circular hole—no chiseling required—and it’ll work with your rectangular strike plate.

Either route will let your Yale lock sit squarely in Baby Cake’s door.

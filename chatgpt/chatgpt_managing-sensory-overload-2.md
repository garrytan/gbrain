## User

I am find in 1 on 1 situations or when I’m speaking to thousands. But when I’m at a table with multiple simultaneous conversations happening, I listen to all of them at once and focus on none. I then start to disassociate and sometimes become overwhelmed. What is this?

---

## Assistant

What you're describing sounds like a form of sensory overload, which occurs when one or more of your senses are overstimulated to the point where you become overwhelmed. In environments with multiple conversations happening simultaneously, your brain might struggle to filter out irrelevant stimuli and focus on just one conversation. This can lead to feelings of disassociation and being overwhelmed.

Here are a few strategies to manage this:

1. **Focus Techniques:** Try to consciously focus on one conversation. You might do this by maintaining eye contact with the person you're speaking to and actively engaging in their dialogue.
   
2. **Mindfulness and Grounding:** Practice mindfulness techniques such as deep breathing or grounding exercises to help center yourself and reduce feelings of overwhelm.
   
3. **Small Breaks:** If you start feeling overwhelmed, excuse yourself for a short break. Stepping away for a moment can help reset your senses.
   
4. **Earplugs or White Noise:** Consider using discreet earplugs or white noise apps to help reduce the background noise and make it easier to focus on the conversation at hand.
   
5. **Communication:** Let those around you know that you find it challenging to follow multiple conversations at once. They might be able to help by speaking one at a time or reducing background noise.

If this issue significantly impacts your daily life, consider consulting a healthcare professional for further advice and support.

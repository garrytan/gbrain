## User

In 1985, if I put my entire salary, let's call it $52,000 a year, into a savings account and I had it in that account until 2000, earning 4%, what would I have in 2000?

---

## Assistant

To calculate the amount in the savings account after 15 years (from 1985 to 2000) with an annual deposit of $52,000 earning 4% interest, we can use the future value of an annuity formula:

\[ FV = P \times \left( \frac{(1 + r)^n - 1}{r} \right) \]

Where:
- \( FV \) is the future value of the annuity.
- \( P \) is the annual payment ($52,000).
- \( r \) is the annual interest rate (4% or 0.04).
- \( n \) is the number of years the money is invested (15 years).

Let's plug in the values:

\[ FV = 52000 \times \left( \frac{(1 + 0.04)^{15} - 1}{0.04} \right) \]

Calculating step-by-step:

1. \( (1 + 0.04)^{15} = 1.04^{15} \approx 1.80094 \)
2. \( 1.80094 - 1 = 0.80094 \)
3. \( \frac{0.80094}{0.04} = 20.0235 \)
4. \( 52000 \times 20.0235 \approx 1,041,222 \)

Therefore, the amount in the savings account in 2000 would be approximately $1,041,222.

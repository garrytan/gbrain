## User

Someone in my apartment building has the apartment building listed on Apple Maps as an animal shelter. Simply go to an animal shelter out of an apartment in the building, and if not, how can I get them in trouble for it?

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, there’s almost certainly no “shelter” operating out of a private flat—it’s just a stray Apple Maps categorization. Here’s what you can do:

- **Correct the listing yourself**  
  1. Open Apple Maps on your iPhone and tap the pin for your building.  
  2. Swipe up and choose **Report an Issue** → **Label or incorrect info** → **Place is permanently closed or has wrong details**, then mark it as a residential address.  

- **Claim & edit the place (if you own/manage it)**  
  1. Go to mapsconnect.apple.com, sign in with your Apple ID, search the address, claim it, and update the category to “residence.”  

- **Alert your property manager or HOA**  
  If someone in the building submitted the false listing, your landlord or HOA can ask them to remove it—or risk a lease violation for misusing a common-area listing.  

- **Only involve authorities if there’s an actual unlicensed shelter**  
  - **Zoning/business license**: Operating a kennel or rescue from a dwelling requires a permit under LA Municipal Code § 53.27.   
  - **Animal welfare**: If you suspect animal cruelty or overcrowding, call LA Animal Services at (213) 482-9550.  
  - **Code enforcement**: For unpermitted business activity, dial 3-1-1 (or 213-473-3231) to report to LA City’s Code Enforcement.  

There’s no criminal penalty for a bad map pin—your fastest fix is to report it to Apple and, if needed, get your landlord involved.

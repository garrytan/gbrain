## Assistant

Got it, Mr. White. To make the jump from 2D art to a two-piece 3D helmet without mangling the face, we have two main routes:

- **Add a deliberate seam**  
  • Introduce a thin horizontal line (dark brown) between the scalloped top and the straight base.  
  • Place it just beneath the scallops so it reads as a design feature, not a defect.  
  • Eyes and smile then sit entirely on the top piece, untouched by the join.

- **Re-proportion and reposition**  
  • Widen the semicircular top relative to the base in the vector so it matches the helmet’s fuller crown.  
  • Shift eyes up into the scallop zone; keep the smile low enough that it still reads on the bottom piece.  
  • Optionally, give the base a very subtle inward curve or “lip” that hints at the real-world overlap.

Let me know which approach you prefer (or if you want to combine both), and I’ll update the flat vector with that separation baked in.

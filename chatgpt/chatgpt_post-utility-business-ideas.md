## Assistant

Mr. White — if “intelligence-as-a-metered utility” really happens, the biggest opportunities are almost never *the utility itself*. They’re the layers that form **because a cheap, reliable, pay-per-use capability becomes ubiquitous**. That’s what happened with gas, electricity, cable, internet, cloud. Let’s map the pattern, then list concrete post-utility businesses / investment plays.

---

## The recurring pattern when a new utility arrives
When a capability becomes:
1) **standardized**,  
2) **reliable**,  
3) **cheap enough to use casually**, and  
4) **metered so anyone can buy slices of it**,  

you get three waves:

**Wave A — Infrastructure complements**  
Stuff that makes the utility usable, safe, cheaper, or compliant.

**Wave B — “Appliances” and distribution**  
Products that package utility consumption into simple experiences for households and businesses.

**Wave C — Entirely new markets**  
Things that *only exist because the utility is there* (new behaviors, new org structures, new consumer categories).

Examples:
- **Electricity** → motors, appliances, refrigeration, elevators, mass production, suburbs, nightlife.
- **Gas utilities** → home heating/cooking standards, indoor plumbing & building materials changes, restaurants scaling, chemical/fertilizer industries using natural gas feedstock.
- **Cable** → ESPN/MTV/QVC, ad-tech for TV, set-top boxes, 24-hour news, premium networks.
- **Internet** → e-commerce, search ads, social, SaaS, gig economy, streaming, cloud.
- **Cloud** → devops tools, usage-based SaaS, API economy, mobile app explosion.

So: **AI utility** would spawn complements, appliances, and new markets.

## What “AI as a utility” specifically changes
If intelligence is a cheap, always-available input:
- **Every business becomes partially software/automation** even if they don’t think they are.
- **Labor gets unbundled into tasks.** The “unit of work” shifts from job → workflow step.
- **Decision latency collapses.** Lots of markets depend on slow decisions; they’ll re-price.
- **Customization becomes default.** The cost of “one more variant” approaches zero.
- **Compliance/safety becomes a new bottleneck.** Like electricity needed codes, AI will need guardrails.

That implies entire lines of business.

---

## Wave A: Infrastructure complements (picks-and-shovels)
These are “boring” but huge.

1. **AI metering, brokerage, and cost-optimization**
   - Think “AWS cost management” but for intelligence tokens.
   - Tools that route jobs to cheapest/best model in real time.
   - Expected outcome: CFO-driven buying of “intelligence spend.”
   - **Play:** AI-FinOps platforms, model routers, token futures/hedging.

2. **Reliability and SLAs for cognition**
   - Businesses will need guaranteed uptime, latency, and accuracy bands.
   - Third-party vendors will certify and insure.
   - **Play:** “Cognitive SRE” firms; AI reliability testing suites.

3. **Governance / compliance / audit rails**
   - Every regulated industry will need AI audit trails.
   - Similar to SOC2/HIPAA compliance markets.
   - **Play:** AI governance SaaS, automated documentation, model-use forensics.

4. **Data supply chain companies**
   - If intelligence is utility, *data is fuel*.
   - Cleaning, labeling, rights management, synthetic data, provenance.
   - **Play:** data “refineries,” rights marketplaces, vertical data co-ops.

5. **Security for AI actions**
   - Not just “model security,” but **tools that prevent AI from doing dumb/illegal things** when it can act.
   - **Play:** permissioning layers, “AI identity & access management.”

6. **Edge/latency hardware + private inference**
   - Some tasks can’t go to public clouds (health, defense, industrial).
   - **Play:** inference chips, on-prem stacks, “private intelligence utilities.”

---

## Wave B: “Appliances” — packaged intelligence for normal people
When electricity arrived, nobody wanted “amperes”; they wanted a fridge. Same here.

1. **AI operating environments for roles**
   - “Accountant OS,” “Lawyer OS,” “Construction PM OS.”
   - Full workflow products, not chatbots.
   - **Play:** vertical AI suites replacing fragmented SaaS.

2. **Personal agent ecosystems**
   - Like smartphone app stores after internet + cloud.
   - Agents for scheduling, shopping, negotiation, admin, learning.
   - **Play:** agent marketplaces, subscription bundles, “agent app stores.”

3. **Consumer hardware that assumes AI always-on**
   - Smart glasses, earbuds, home robots, cars with agent copilots.
   - **Play:** device makers + middleware that ties AI utility to sensors/actuators.

4. **AI-native creative studios**
   - Not “use AI to help editing,” but studios where *the output is mass-customized per viewer*.
   - **Play:** personalized entertainment platforms.

## Wave C: New markets that only exist post-AI-utility
This is where the weird, high-upside stuff lives.

1. **Mass-customized products at scale**
   - With reasoning/design automated, you can profitably make “one-off” SKUs.
   - Apparel, furniture, nutrition, curricula, travel, finance.
   - **Play:** factories/brands built on *infinite catalog* logic.

2. **Outcome-priced businesses**
   - If AI reduces labor cost toward zero, pricing can shift from time → results.
   - Example: marketing agency paid per qualified lead; legal service per resolved claim.
   - **Play:** “AI-leveraged firms” with aggressive outcome pricing.

3. **Micro-enterprises / 1-person unicorns**
   - AI utility lets a tiny team run what needed 50 people.
   - **Play:** platforms that scaffold these businesses: automated back office, sales, HR, compliance. Think “Shopify for any business.”

4. **New “attention-to-action” ad markets**
   - Ads don’t just influence humans; they can influence agents.
   - If your agent buys detergent, brands will bid to be chosen.
   - **Play:** “agent-search SEO,” marketplaces for agent-targeted ads, verified product feeds.

5. **Real-time negotiation markets**
   - Agents can negotiate price/terms instantly.
   - Creates dynamic markets for insurance, travel, B2B procurement, lending.
   - **Play:** exchanges that assume negotiation-by-agent.

6. **Continuous education / skill subscription**
   - If teaching labor becomes cheap, education becomes always-on and personalized.
   - **Play:** lifelong learning subscriptions tied to career outcomes.

7. **AI-run regulated entities**
   - Example: micro-insurers, micro-lenders, HOA management, payroll providers.
   - Regulation will adapt to allow tiny, algorithmic operators.
   - **Play:** “compliance-as-code” + licensed AI firms.

8. **Synthetic companions / social products**
   - Cable created 24-hour “parasocial” markets (talk shows, stars).
   - AI utility makes *interactive companionship* cheap.
   - **Play:** companion platforms, therapy adjuncts (regulated), eldercare social tools.

9. **Autonomous supply chains**
   - When cognition is cheap, logistics gets re-optimized constantly.
   - **Play:** AI-first freight brokers, dynamic manufacturing scheduling.

10. **“Proof of human” industries**
   - As AI output floods markets, human authenticity becomes a premium good.
   - Similar to organic food after industrial agriculture.
   - **Play:** verification services, “human-made” brands, creative guilds.

## Investment framing: how to pick winners
A simple filter:

1) **Does this company’s unit economics improve *superlinearly* as AI utility cost drops?**  
If yes, it rides the curve.

2) **Is the bottleneck *not* the model itself?**  
Good — the utility is a commodity, the bottlenecks are workflow, trust, distribution, regulation.

3) **Does it create defensibility via:**
   - proprietary data,
   - distribution,
   - regulatory moat,
   - network effects among agents/users,
   - or being deeply embedded in workflows?

If yes, it can survive “model prices go to zero.”

## A few concrete startup ideas (non-utility)
- **Intelligence Router for SMBs:** plug-in that auto-chooses models per task + budgets + compliance.  
- **AI Audit Ledger:** immutable logging of AI decisions/actions for regulators.  
- **Vertical Agent Store:** installable agents for one industry with tight integrations (e.g., dental offices, trucking fleets).  
- **Outcome-priced Back-Office Firm:** bookkeeping/payroll/tax where you pay per error-free close, not per hour.  
- **Agent-to-Agent Commerce Platform:** brands publish machine-readable product/price/quality feeds so consumer agents can shop.  
- **Human Authenticity Verification:** watermarking + attestations for “human-created” media and services.

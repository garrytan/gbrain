**Summary — *Default Alive or Default Dead?***

Paul Graham argues founders should always know whether their startup is **default alive** (will reach profitability on current cash and growth) or **default dead** (will run out of money unless something changes).

Key points:
- Many founders don’t know their default status—and that ignorance is dangerous.
- The worst situation is the **fatal pinch**: default dead + slow growth + not enough time to fix it.
- Relying on future fundraising is risky; investor interest is fickle and growth-dependent. Fundraising should be Plan A, with a written, concrete Plan B.
- Spending more doesn’t reliably create growth. Growth usually comes from a product that truly hits a nerve.
- **Over-hiring is the #1 killer** of funded startups. It raises burn without fixing weak product appeal and makes course correction harder.
- Early-stage startups should resist scaling and hiring; instead focus on making the product meaningfully better—even if that means doing unscalable things.
- Great founders prioritize survival and product quality over headcount and optics.

Bottom line: **Know your default state early, avoid over-hiring, and fix the product before scaling—or the company dies.**

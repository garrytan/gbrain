## User

when a trademark is publiched for opposition. How much does it cost me to file an opposition?

---

## Assistant

The cost of filing an opposition against a trademark application can vary depending on the jurisdiction and the complexity of the case. In the United States, for example, the current fee for filing a notice of opposition with the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) is $400 per class. 

However, this is just the filing fee and additional costs may be incurred depending on factors such as the amount of evidence that needs to be gathered and the time required to prepare and file the opposition. It is therefore advisable to consult with a trademark attorney who can provide more accurate estimates of the costs involved in filing an opposition.

## User

if i win do i get the fee back

---

## Assistant

In general, if you successfully oppose a trademark application, you will not be able to recover the filing fees or any other expenses associated with the opposition from the opposing party. However, if the opposition is based on a claim of trademark infringement and you are able to obtain a judgment in your favor, you may be entitled to recover damages and/or attorneys' fees from the infringing party.

It is important to note that the rules and procedures governing opposition proceedings vary by jurisdiction and can be complex. If you are considering filing an opposition, it is advisable to consult with a trademark attorney who can provide guidance on the potential costs and risks associated with the opposition.

The positive effects seem to hinge on the increased growth hormone levels and enhanced recovery facilitated by sauna use, which in turn, could foster a more conducive environment for muscle hypertrophy.

The impact of post-workout sauna use on muscle hypertrophy has been a topic of interest with varying findings across different studies. Here’s a detailed breakdown based on the information gathered from multiple sources:

1. **Growth Hormone Increase and Protein Degradation**:
   Sauna use in conjunction with resistance training has been noted to potentially promote muscle growth by enhancing the release of growth hormone (GH) and reducing protein degradation. The heat from the sauna can slow down the breakdown of proteins and augment growth hormone levels, which in turn, might result in an increase in net protein synthesis and muscle hypertrophy【15†(NMU Commons)】.

2. **Heat Stress and Heat Shock Proteins**:
   The theory behind sauna use aiding in muscle hypertrophy revolves around heat stress inducing the expression of heat shock proteins (HSPs), which are believed to play a role in muscle growth. For instance, a study attempted to elucidate the effect of sauna use on Heat Shock Protein 70 (HSP70) expression and its potential impact on muscle hypertrophy in young males undergoing resistance training over six weeks. However, the study found no significant differences in lean body mass or strength gains, suggesting that sauna use didn't augment muscle hypertrophy or strength【22†(NMU Commons)】.

3. **Alternative Explanations**:
   While some studies show a positive effect, others do not. The variations in findings could be attributed to differences in methodologies, such as the type of heat exposure (e.g., traditional sauna vs infrared sauna), duration, and frequency of sauna sessions, as well as the specific training protocols followed by participants. Some studies used infrared capsules for heat application and found changes in muscle hypertrophy after a higher number of sessions (24 instead of 12)【16†(NCBI)】.

4. **Sample Size and Study Limitations**:
   Many of the studies conducted have small sample sizes and varied protocols which could limit the generalizability of the findings. For example, the study mentioned above had only 13 subjects distributed into three groups which may not provide a robust evidence base【22†(NMU Commons)】.

5. **Individual Studies and Increased Plasma Volume**:
   A 2015 study found sauna bathing post-training expanded plasma volume in cyclists, although the direct effects on muscle hypertrophy were uncertain【17†(Men's Health)】.

6. **Inference of Meaningfulness**:
   Given the variation in study findings and methodologies, it's challenging to definitively conclude the meaningfulness of the increase in muscle hypertrophy due to post-workout sauna use. The existing evidence does not provide a clear consensus, and the effectiveness and significance of sauna use for muscle hypertrophy may require further investigation with more rigorous and well-controlled studies.

In conclusion, while there's theoretical basis for sauna use aiding muscle hypertrophy, empirical evidence is mixed and the meaningfulness of the observed increases in muscle hypertrophy, if any, remain unclear.

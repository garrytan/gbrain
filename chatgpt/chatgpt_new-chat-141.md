What qualifiers a service animal

## Assistant

Under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), a service animal is defined primarily as a dog that is individually trained to do work or perform tasks for a person with a disability. This can include physical, sensory, psychiatric, intellectual, or other mental disabilities. The tasks performed by the service animal must be directly related to the person's disability. It's important to note that emotional support, therapy, comfort, or companion animals are not considered service animals under the ADA, as they have not been trained to perform a specific job or task.

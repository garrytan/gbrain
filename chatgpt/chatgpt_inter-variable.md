## Assistant

**Inter variable** isn’t a standard technical term by itself; it likely refers to one of these related concepts in statistics and research:

### Most common meaning  
**Intervening (or intermediate/mediating) variable** — a variable that explains *how* or *why* one variable influences another. It sits *between* an independent (cause) and a dependent (effect) variable and mediates that relationship. citeturn0search0turn0search2

In other words:  
- The **independent variable** influences the **intervening variable**.  
- The **intervening variable** then influences the **dependent variable**.  
- It explains the causal mechanism linking the two. citeturn0search6

**Example:**  
Income might explain (mediate) the relationship between education level (independent) and spending (dependent). citeturn0search3

Other names: *mediating variable*, *intermediate variable*. citeturn0search1

### Alternative dictionary meaning  
If someone literally meant “intervariable” as one word, it can simply mean *between variables* (the prefix *inter-* means between), without technical statistical meaning. citeturn0search13

If you meant something else (like *interaction between variables* or *correlation among variables*), clarify the context.

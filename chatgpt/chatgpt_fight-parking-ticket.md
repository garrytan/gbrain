## Likely City rebuttals (and how we neutralize them)
1. **Rebuttal:** “Signage exists and is visible.”  
   **Counter:** Prove it wasn’t visible from driver’s approach — photos from where you parked, measured distance, and Google Street View showing obstruction or sign placement before/after citation time.
2. **Rebuttal:** “Time stamp confirms violation (11:13 AM).”  
   **Counter:** Show ambiguity: sign placement, missing arrow indicating side, faded text, or that the sign is not viewable until after you’ve parked. Also ask for officer evidence (photo/time/location).
3. **Rebuttal:** “Standard city signage — presumed valid.”  
   **Counter:** Cite *reasonable driver perspective* standard: signs must be sufficiently visible to a driver exercising ordinary care. Show they weren’t from the approach.
4. **Rebuttal:** “Frequent non-enforcement doesn’t justify breaking law.”  
   **Counter:** We don’t rely on inconsistent enforcement alone — we prove **actual lack of notice** (obscured/faded/incorrect placement) which is a legal defect in notice.

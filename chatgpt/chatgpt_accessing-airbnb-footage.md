## User

An Airbnb host filmed us with on site cameras. Can we get access to the footage? They used in a claim against us.

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, you generally **can’t force an Airbnb host to hand over their private security footage** just because they used it in a claim. Here’s how it works in Los Angeles/California:

**1. Discovery only if there’s a legal case**  
- If Airbnb opens a formal dispute (or you sue/are sued), footage can be requested in *discovery* once a court case exists. A subpoena or court order is what compels production.  
- Simply asking the host or Airbnb outside a lawsuit rarely works—they aren’t legally required to share.

**2. Airbnb’s internal process**  
- Airbnb may review footage internally for a damage/violation claim, but they do **not** give guests copies unless required by law.  
- You can message Airbnb Support and request that evidence be preserved for any potential dispute.

**3. Privacy context**  
- California law allows cameras in common areas (not bedrooms/bathrooms) if disclosed in the listing. If they failed to disclose, that can strengthen any legal claim you bring.

**Practical move**  
- In your Airbnb message thread, politely request Airbnb to preserve and provide access to any video evidence used in the claim, noting you may need it for legal review.  
- If they refuse and you need it, your path is a subpoena after filing in small claims or civil court.

Bottom line: you can *ask*, but to actually **get** the footage you’d need either the host’s consent or a court order/subpoena.

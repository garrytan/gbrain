### Paste this into Gamma

```markdown
Create a world-class YC-style seed pitch deck for Jovie.

Objective:
Make investors understand Jovie in the first 10 seconds, believe the timing is inevitable, and see why Tim White is uniquely qualified to build it.

Deck style:
- 10 slides only.
- YC Demo Day / seed deck style.
- One big idea per slide.
- Plain English. No jargon.
- Maximum 1 headline + 3 short bullets per slide.
- Use strong declarative headlines.
- Make every slide investor-readable in under 10 seconds.
- Visuals should be simple: product mockups, workflow diagrams, market math, before/after tool stack, simple revenue ladder.
- Avoid generic AI art, concerts, robots, neon cyberpunk, and vague music imagery.
- Make it feel like a serious software company, not a music lifestyle brand.

Company:
Jovie is an AI manager for music creators.
Jovie turns music releases into fans, campaigns, merch, notifications, and revenue.
The wedge is AI-native and independent music creators who can now make songs easily but still cannot launch, market, monetize, and retain fans without doing manual marketing work across many tools.

Core narrative:
Creation has been solved. Distribution has been solved. Monetization and audience-building are still broken.
AI music tools like Suno created a massive new class of music creators. Suno has passed 100M+ users, 2M paid subscribers, and $300M ARR. But those creators still need help turning songs into fans and revenue.
Jovie is the automation layer after creation.

Use these slide titles and content:

Slide 1 — Jovie
Headline: Every music creator gets an AI manager.
Subhead: Jovie turns releases into fans, campaigns, merch, and revenue.
Small footer: Seed · 2026 · meetjovie.com

Slide 2 — The problem
Headline: Making songs got easy. Launching them did not.
Bullets:
- AI tools made music creation instant.
- Marketing still requires landing pages, links, email, merch, ads, analytics, and constant follow-up.
- Most creators have no manager, no marketer, and no release-ops team.

Slide 3 — Why now
Headline: 100M+ new creators need a manager.
Bullets:
- Suno has passed 100M+ users and 2M paid subscribers.
- AI music is moving from novelty to monetized creative infrastructure.
- The bottleneck has shifted from making the song to building the audience.

Slide 4 — The old way
Headline: Music tech sells side quests.
Visual: messy stack of tools.
Bullets:
- Distribution gets songs onto platforms.
- Smart-link tools capture clicks.
- Merch, email, ads, content, and fan data live in separate systems.
- Artists are told to become operators instead of artists.

Slide 5 — The solution
Headline: Jovie ships the work.
Bullets:
- Detects a creator’s catalog and audience.
- Builds release pages, smart links, fan campaigns, merch drops, and notifications.
- Surfaces revenue opportunities automatically.

Slide 6 — Product example
Headline: From release moment to revenue campaign.
Use this example:
Tim White × Cosmic Gate at EDC.
Workflow:
1. Catalog match: Cosmic Gate plays EDC Saturday. Tim is on “The Deep End” with them.
2. Campaign drafted: surprise merch drop and fan alert created.
3. Store live: tee design selected, checkout published, smart link routed.
4. Fans notified: 184K listeners queued for notification.
5. Revenue tracked: checkout, fulfillment, and follow-up automated.

Slide 7 — Market
Headline: The first wedge is a $97M ARR opportunity.
Visual: simple market math.
Bullets:
- 208K creators × $39/month × 12 = $97M ARR.
- That is 10.4% of Suno’s paid subscriber base.
- This excludes Operator, labels, merch, payments, services, and revenue share.

Slide 8 — Business model
Headline: Free profile. Paid automation.
Visual: pricing ladder.
Tiers:
- Free: public artist profile.
- Pro — $39/month: release campaigns, smart links, fan reactivation, merch workflows.
- Operator — $299/month: higher-touch automation for serious artists, managers, and teams.
Future upside:
- Merch
- Payments
- Ticketing
- Services
- Revenue share

Slide 9 — Go-to-market
Headline: We pre-build the profile, then convert the artist.
Bullets:
- Generate artist profiles from public catalog and audience data.
- Send creators something useful before asking them to sign up.
- Convert claimed profiles into Pro subscriptions.
- Start with AI-native creators, DJs, producers, and independent artists releasing frequently.

Use placeholders where needed:
- X% profile claim rate
- Z qualified leads identified
- N profiles generated
Do not invent traction numbers.

Slide 10 — Team and ask
Headline: Built by an artist who needed this.
Bullets:
- Tim White — founder, artist, Los Angeles.
- 90M+ streams, 190K audience, Clio Award, Armada releases.
- Built the manual release workflows Jovie now automates.

Ask:
Raising $500K on a $5M post-money SAFE.
Milestone: first revenue and 1,000 paying creators.
Use of funds:
- Product
- Creator acquisition
- Release-ops automation

Final design direction:
Make the deck feel like a focused seed-stage software company. Use large headlines, sparse layouts, and clean diagrams. Prioritize clarity over decoration. Every slide should answer one investor question: what is it, why now, why this wedge, how it makes money, why this founder, why this round.
```

## Assistant

What’s up, bro. Strawberries in Vegas grocery stores are almost always transported in—**not** grown locally.

**Where from:**  
Primarily **California**, specifically places like **Watsonville**, **Salinas**, **Oxnard**, and **Santa Maria**. These are some of the top strawberry-producing regions in the U.S.

**When:**  
Strawberries are grown **year-round** in California thanks to the state’s microclimates, but peak production is **spring through early summer**. In winter, they might also come from **Mexico** or **Florida** to meet demand.

**How:**  
Harvested by hand, packed in the field, then shipped via **refrigerated trucks**. Transport usually takes **1–2 days** to reach Las Vegas from central/southern California.

**Who:**  
Giant distributors like **Driscoll’s**, **Naturipe**, or **California Giant** handle most of the strawberries you’ll find in stores like Albertsons, Whole Foods, or Smith’s.

**Why:**  
Vegas is a desert. It’s too hot, dry, and soil-poor for large-scale strawberry farming. Importing is cheaper, faster, and way more reliable.

TL;DR: You’re eating California sunshine trucked in on ice.

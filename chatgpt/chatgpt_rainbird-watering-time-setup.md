## Assistant

It depends on your sprinkler type, soil, and local climate, but a general rule of thumb for **twice-weekly watering** is:

- **Pop-up spray heads**: 10–15 minutes per station  
- **Rotary nozzles**: 20–30 minutes per station  
- **Rotor sprinklers**: 30–45 minutes per station  
- **Drip irrigation**: 45–60 minutes per station  

If your soil is **clay**, break it into two shorter cycles (e.g., 10 minutes, wait an hour, then another 10 minutes) to prevent runoff. If your soil is **sandy**, you may need to increase the time.  

You can fine-tune by checking if the lawn is dry between watering days or if there's runoff while watering.

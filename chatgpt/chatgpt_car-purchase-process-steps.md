## Assistant

Congrats, Mr. White. Here's what happens next, broken down:

### 1. **Dealer Responsibilities (if licensed in CA)**
Most California dealers handle:
- **Title transfer**
- **Registration with DMV**
- **Temporary plates** (valid for 90 days)
- **Permanent plates mailed** to you by DMV later
- **DMV fees/taxes** bundled into your sale price

You’ll likely get:
- **Buyer's copy** of the contract
- **Temporary registration** in the window
- **Odometer disclosure** form
- **DMV reg papers** showing it's in process

### 2. **What You Need to Do**
- **Wire funds** as instructed by the dealer
- **Sign all paperwork** (often Docusign or in-person)
- **Get insurance** before you drive off

### 3. **Insurance Info Needed**
When adding the car:
- **VIN**
- **Year/make/model** (2016 Mazda CX-5)
- **Purchase date**
- **Estimated annual mileage**
- **Usage type** (commute/pleasure/business)
- **Lienholder info** (if financed — not in your case)

### 4. **Driving Off**
Once insured and paid, you can legally drive with:
- Proof of insurance
- Temporary registration
- Copy of sales contract

## Assistant

Perfect. Here’s what’s next, Mr. White:

### While the Wire Is Clearing:
- **Do nothing until funds hit** — dealer won’t release the car until payment clears.
- **Stay in contact** — confirm with the dealer when they receive the wire so you can schedule pickup or delivery.

### Once the Wire Clears:
1. **Dealer gives you:**
   - **Car keys**
   - **Temporary registration** (window sticker)
   - **Copies of paperwork** (bill of sale, DMV forms, etc.)

2. **Insurance:**
   - Add the vehicle using VIN, purchase date, usage.
   - Have proof of insurance ready before pickup.

3. **Drive off:**
   - Temporary plates and registration will make you legal on the road.
   - Permanent plates + registration card will arrive by mail (usually within 4–6 weeks).

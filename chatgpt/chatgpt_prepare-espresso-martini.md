## Assistant

To prepare an espresso martini, a bartender would typically follow these steps:

1. **Ingredients**: Gather the ingredients, which commonly include:
   - 2 ounces of vodka
   - 1/2 ounce coffee liqueur (like Kahlúa)
   - 1 ounce espresso (freshly brewed and cooled)
   - 1/2 ounce simple syrup (optional, for added sweetness)

2. **Equipment**: Have the necessary bar equipment ready, such as a shaker, jigger, and strainer.

3. **Mix**: Combine the vodka, coffee liqueur, espresso, and simple syrup (if using) in a shaker.

4. **Ice**: Fill the shaker with ice.

5. **Shake**: Shake the mixture vigorously until well chilled, usually for about 15 seconds.

6. **Glass**: Strain the mixture into a chilled martini glass.

7. **Garnish**: Garnish with three coffee beans, which is traditional for an espresso martini.

8. **Serve**: Serve the drink immediately.

Espresso martinis are popular for their rich coffee flavor balanced with the smoothness of vodka and the complexity of the coffee liqueur. The shake not only chills the drink but also creates a frothy top layer.

Yo dawg — sounds like your AirTags are doing that **anti-stalking chirp** Apple added to stop people from secretly tracking others.

### Here’s what’s happening:
- If an AirTag is **away from its owner’s iPhone** for a while but **traveling with someone else's iPhone**, it thinks it might be stalking them.
- So your **secondary keys AirTag**, which are chilling in the car without your phone nearby for long stretches, freak out when they detect another iPhone (like Liv's or Ben’s) moving with them.

### Why it chirps:
- Apple’s safety feature kicks in to alert non-owners that an unknown AirTag is traveling with them.
- If your phone isn’t near the AirTag for a while, it assumes someone might have slipped it into someone else’s bag or car.

### How to stop it:
1. **Bring your phone close to the AirTag** regularly so it knows it's still with you.
2. **Disable the speaker** (techy/hardware hack — not recommended unless you’re cool voiding warranty).
3. **Convert that AirTag to a shared item using Family Sharing** (only works with newer iOS versions and specific use cases, not full AirTag sharing yet).
4. **Remove the AirTag from your account and register it to Liv’s account**, but that’s not ideal if you still need to track it.

### Hacky workaround:
- Just throw the AirTag in your glovebox or somewhere that’s closer to your iPhone while driving so it stops freaking out when it detects another iPhone without yours around.
- Or just keep it in your bag or pocket sometimes to reestablish the bond with your iPhone.

### TL;DR Fix:
Make sure your iPhone gets near that AirTag at least once a day. If not, it’ll start chirping whenever it's moving with someone else's phone.

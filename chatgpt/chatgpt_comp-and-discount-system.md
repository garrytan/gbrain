## Assistant

Here’s a concise, structured system you can adopt to control comps and discounts for your venue:

---

### 1. Categories & Eligibility
**A. Friends & Family**  
*Purpose:* Reward loyalty and maintain goodwill.  
*Rules:*  
- 25% off food & drinks.  
- Limit to named individuals approved by management.  
- Track visits; review quarterly.

**B. VIPs / Influencers**  
*Purpose:* Marketing value.  
*Rules:*  
- 50% off or full comp when strategically beneficial.  
- Pre-authorized by GM or owner only.  
- Document reason (e.g., social exposure, event partnership).

**C. Service Recovery (“Make-Good”)**  
*Purpose:* Fix mistakes or poor guest experiences.  
*Rules:*  
- Server can comp up to $25 per incident without approval.  
- Larger comps require manager sign-off.  
- Always note reason (e.g., incorrect order, excessive wait).

---

### 2. Approval & Tracking
- **Digital Log:** Use POS with a “Comp Reason” dropdown: *Friends/Family, VIP, Service Recovery, Promotion*.  
- **Manager Oversight:** Nightly review of all comps/discounts.  
- **Weekly Report:** Total dollar value, percentage of sales, top reasons.

---

### 3. Limits & Controls
- Cap total comps at 3–5% of weekly sales.  
- Require manager code for discounts >25%.  
- Automatic flag in POS when category limits are exceeded.

---

### 4. Communication & Training
- Staff handbook with clear comp tiers and examples.  
- Pre-shift meetings: reinforce approval process and remind of limits.

---

**Key Principles**  
- Transparency: every comp must have a reason in the log.  
- Measurable: review data to spot abuse or trends.  
- Flexibility: owner/GM can override for special events but must record justification.

This structure keeps hospitality high while ensuring financial discipline and an auditable paper trail.

## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s how to adapt the comp/discount system to your **per-check closeout workflow** with Toast handhelds:

---

### 1. POS Configuration
**A. Required “Comp Reason”**  
- Create buttons: *Friends/Family*, *VIP/Influencer*, *Service Recovery*, *Promo/Event*.  
- Make “Reason” a **mandatory field** before a comp can be applied.  
- Tie each reason to a specific manager PIN level for higher comps.

**B. Server Limits**  
- Servers can apply only:  
  * ≤ $25 or ≤ 20%* discount with their own PIN.  
- Toast can enforce max dollar or percentage per check.

---

### 2. Manager Authorization
- For anything above the server limit, the handheld should **prompt for a manager code** on the spot.  
- Manager approves right at the table if needed, so you don’t slow service.

---

### 3. Multi-Check Tracking
- Because each table may have multiple checks, use Toast’s **Daily Comp/Discount Report** (exportable) that aggregates across all checks by employee and reason.  
- Manager reviews at shift end; you’ll see totals even if each guest had separate mini-tickets.

---

### 4. Workflow for Servers
1. Ring in the order.  
2. If comping (e.g., remake, VIP), select correct *Comp Reason*.  
3. If over their limit, call manager for PIN.  
4. Close check as normal.

---

### 5. Oversight & Review
- **Shift Report:** Manager signs off nightly on total comps vs sales.  
- **Weekly Audit:** Compare comp % to target (e.g., 3–5%). Investigate outliers by employee.

---

This setup preserves the fast, per-check closeout you already use while keeping every comp tied to a reason, an employee, and a manager approval trail.

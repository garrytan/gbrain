## Assistant

Mr. White, flies swarming on a porch—even without an obvious odor—usually means something’s attracting them. Common causes:  

- **Hidden organic matter**  
  ­Decaying leaf litter, spilled food/drink residue or even a dead rodent in a wall gap can lure flies.  
- **Moisture build-up**  
  Leaky downspouts, condensation under stairs or a clogged drain trap provide breeding sites.  
- **Warm, reflective surfaces**  
  Flies bask on sun-warmed concrete or glossy paint to regulate body temperature.  
- **Nearby garbage or compost**  
  Dumpster vents, outdoor compost bins or pet waste just out of sight can draw them.  
- **Sewer or plumbing leaks**  
  Crack in a sewer line or vent stack often vents organic gases that flies detect.  

**Next steps:**  
1. Inspect for hidden debris (under mats, planter boxes, in cracks).  
2. Check for leaks or standing water.  
3. Clean the porch surface with a degreasing solution.  
4. Seal gaps around doors, vents and pipes.  

That should help you pinpoint and eliminate the attractant.

### Key Legal Rules in California

1. **Tips are the employee’s property**  
   Under California Labor Code § 351, gratuities left for employees belong to the employees; employers (including managers/agents) may *not* keep them or redirect them to themselves. citeturn0search0turn0search7turn0search12turn0search4

2. **Tip pooling is allowed under conditions**  
   Employers can require employees to pool tips (mandatory tip pool) provided that the participants are employees who are in the “chain of service” (e.g. servers, bartenders, bussers) and not supervisors/managers who have authority over hiring/firing or direct control, unless those supervisors spend almost all their time doing non‐managerial tip‐earning work. citeturn0search7turn0search3turn0search10

3. **No tip credits**  
   Tips cannot be used to make up the employee’s minimum wage. Employers must pay the full minimum wage, plus tips. citeturn0search5turn0search4turn0search12

4. **Unlawful deductions from tips**  
   Employers cannot deduct from tips to cover business costs (credit card processing, etc.), or deduct tips for employee misconduct in a way that essentially takes away tips (unless very clearly allowed and lawful). citeturn0search5turn0search11turn0search12

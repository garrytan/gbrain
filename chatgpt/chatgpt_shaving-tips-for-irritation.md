## Assistant

It sounds like you're experiencing irritation from shaving, Mr. White. Here are some tips to help reduce breakouts, rashes, and cuts:

1. **Prep Your Skin**: Before shaving, wash your face with warm water to soften the hair. You can also use a pre-shave oil to further soften the hair and skin.

2. **Use a Sharp Razor**: Dull blades can cause irritation and cuts. Make sure your razor is sharp and clean.

3. **Shaving Cream or Gel**: Use a quality shaving cream or gel. This provides a protective barrier and helps the razor glide more smoothly.

4. **Shave with the Grain**: Shaving against the direction of hair growth increases the risk of irritation and ingrown hairs. 

5. **Gentle Pressure**: Don’t press too hard with the razor. Gentle strokes reduce the risk of cuts.

6. **Aftercare**: Rinse your face with cold water after shaving to close the pores. Apply an alcohol-free aftershave lotion or balm to soothe the skin.

7. **Change Blades Regularly**: A dirty or worn-out blade can cause irritation. Change your blade after 5-7 shaves.

8. **Consider a Different Method**: If problems persist, consider using an electric razor, or a different type of blade.

If these measures don't help, or if you frequently get severe rashes or cuts, it might be wise to consult a dermatologist. They can provide personalized advice and check for any skin conditions that might be contributing to the issue.

## Assistant

Mr. White — likely causes are: the valve core is stuck/broken, the tire bead isn’t sealing to the rim, or there’s a puncture/sidewall damage. Here’s the quickest way to narrow it down safely.

### 1) Confirm the obvious
- **Check you’re on the front-left valve stem**, not a different wheel.
- **Remove the valve cap** and look for damage/bent stem.

### 2) See if the valve is the problem (most common)
1. **Press the little pin in the valve stem** (with a key tip or screwdriver) for 1 second.
   - **Air hisses out** → valve stem isn’t completely blocked; problem may be a big leak or bead.
   - **No air + pin doesn’t spring back** → valve core likely stuck.
2. If you have a **different air chuck/nozzle**, try it. Some pumps don’t depress the pin properly.

**Fast test:** Put a few drops of soapy water on the valve opening while you try to inflate.
- **Bubbles from the valve** → valve core leak.
- **No bubbles, still won’t take air** → could be blockage or the bead is off.

### 3) Check for a bead leak (tire not sealed to rim)
This happens after hitting a pothole/curb or if the tire is very low/flat.
- Listen while trying to inflate: **loud hissing at rim edge** = bead leak.
- Spray **soapy water around the rim edge** (both sides).  
  **Bubbles** = leak location.

**If it’s a bead leak:** A normal portable inflator often can’t reseat it. You usually need a **shop air blast / bead seater**. Don’t keep forcing it—can damage the tire.

### 4) Check for a puncture or sidewall issue
- Spray soapy water on the **tread and sidewall**.  
  **Steady bubbles** = puncture/leak.
- If the tire is very flat, look for a nail/screw.

### 5) When to stop and tow/drive carefully
- If the tire is **fully flat** and won’t take air, **don’t drive on it** (can destroy the tire + risk losing control).
- If you have a spare, put it on; otherwise **roadside assistance/tow**.

### Quick questions (answer in one line)
1) Is the tire **completely flat** or just low?  
2) When you connect the pump, do you hear **air leaking immediately** (and from where—valve vs rim edge)?  
3) Did you **hit a curb/pothole** recently?

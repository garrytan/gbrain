> **Short answer:** Same principles. GLP-1s lower hunger; they don’t change the biology of muscle loss or fatigue. Use the easier adherence to hit evidence-based targets more cleanly—not to crash diet.

**Rate of loss**
- Start at **0.5–1.0% BW/week**.  
- Higher body fat? You can flirt with **1.0–1.25%** short-term if training performance and recovery stay solid.  
- Lean or strength/power goals? **0.25–0.5%**.  
- If reps or load slide for 2+ weeks, tighten volume a bit and/or slow the rate.

**Calorie deficit**
- Program the same deficit you’d normally use to hit the above rates. GLP-1s just make compliance easier. Don’t chase the drug to ever-lower calories. Floor: ~**8–10 kcal/lb FFM**.

**Cut length**
- Typical caps still apply: **8–12 weeks** for most; up to **16** if higher BF% and metrics look good. GLP-1s don’t magically prevent fatigue or LBM loss. End the cut when performance, mood, or sleep trend down.

**Maintenance between cuts**
- Keep it. Physiological recovery still matters.  
- **Duration:** usually **~50% of the preceding diet length**. If biomarkers, training, and mood are excellent, **~33%** is acceptable. **Don’t skip**—use it to practice eating without the drug doing all the work.

**Protein / carbs / fats**
- **Protein:** **0.8–1.0 g/lb BW** (or **1.1–1.3 g/lb FFM**) spread over 3–5 meals.  
- Carbs as high as performance allows; trim fats to make the deficit.  
- **Creatine 5 g/day**. Hydration and electrolytes matter—nausea can mask under-fueling.

**Training**
- Priority is **progressive resistance** 3–6x/week.  
- Start with your normal MEV–MAV volumes; if the deficit is steeper, pull **~1–2 sets per muscle/week** before you pull calories further. Keep heavy work in.  
- Cardio: minimum effective dose for health/steps (**8–12k/day**). Don’t replace food with miles.

**Refeeds / diet breaks**
- Less hunger = fewer psychological breaks needed.  
- **Performance refeeds** (1–2 high-carb days) can still help glycogen and training quality. Use them if lifts benefit.

**Coming off the drug**
- Plan an **8–12 week maintenance** with deliberate hunger-management skills and slow calorie titration. Keep training hard; watch scale trend and performance.

**Retatrutide vs tirzepatide**
- Same playbook. If appetite suppression is stronger, you’ll just hit targets with less friction—not with harsher targets.

## Assistant

If your sink won't drain, here are concise steps to troubleshoot and possibly fix the issue:

1. **Boiling Water**: Pour boiling water down the drain. This can often dissolve organic matter or soap scum causing the blockage.

2. **Plunger**: Use a plunger, making sure to create a good seal around the drain opening. Pump it up and down several times to dislodge the clog.

3. **Baking Soda and Vinegar**: Pour half a cup of baking soda followed by half a cup of vinegar down the drain. Cover the drain to contain the reaction and wait 15 minutes. Rinse with hot water.

4. **Check the P-Trap**: Place a bucket underneath the P-trap (the U-shaped pipe under the sink) to catch water. Unscrew the P-trap and clear out any debris. Reattach the P-trap securely.

5. **Plumber’s Snake or Auger**: Insert the snake into the drain and turn it to break through the blockage. This is effective for deeper clogs that other methods can't reach.

6. **Professional Help**: If the above steps don’t resolve the issue, it may be time to call a professional plumber. There could be a more significant blockage further down the line that requires specialized tools.

Always wear protective gloves and be careful when dealing with chemicals or boiling water.

## Key Requirements and Considerations

- **Minimum Internet Speed:** 100 Mbps download (for smooth streaming and work-from-home needs).  
- **Data Usage:** No need for truly unlimited data – a plan around ~1 TB/month (or **no data cap**) is sufficient for typical use.  
- **Phone Service:** Porting existing number from Google Fi. A **VoIP home phone** line is acceptable (unlimited calling within US).  
- **Equipment:** The user has a mesh WiFi system, so a plan with a standalone modem (or gateway) is fine. Avoid expensive router rentals.  
- **Contract/Fees:** Prefer **no long-term contracts** and minimal extra fees (installation or equipment fees) to keep upfront and monthly costs low.

## Wireless Home Internet + VoIP Alternatives

If traditional ISPs are too pricey or not available, consider a **5G home internet** service combined with a separate VoIP phone solution. These can be cost-effective as **separate services**:

### T-Mobile 5G Home Internet + VoIP Service

- **Internet Plan:** **T-Mobile 5G Home Internet** – **$50.00/mo** regular price (with autopay). This can drop to **$30.00/mo** if you also have an eligible T-Mobile Magenta MAX mobile plan【26†L920-L928】. The service delivers home internet via T-Mobile’s 5G wireless network.  
- **Speed:** Typical speeds range from **~100 Mbps up to 200 Mbps** (33–182 Mbps in many areas, with max around 245 Mbps)【26†L932-L938】. Performance can vary based on signal, but it meets the 100+ Mbps requirement in strong coverage areas.  
- **Data Cap:** **No hard data caps** – it’s “unlimited” data. There is no extra charge for heavy usage (T-Mobile doesn’t enforce a fixed cap), though in times of network congestion speeds may slow after very high usage. Generally, you can use well over 1 TB/month without fees.  
- **Home Phone:** T-Mobile’s home internet does **not** include a phone line, but you can add an independent VoIP solution: for example, **Ooma** or **MagicJack**. These services use your internet connection to provide a home phone:
  - *Ooma:* You buy the Ooma Telo device (~$100) and then pay only applicable taxes/fees (~$4–$5 monthly)【28†L3-L11】【28†L23-L28】 for unlimited US calling (the core VoIP service itself is free). You can port your number to Ooma (one-time porting fee ~$40). This gives you a reliable home phone over your internet.  
  - *MagicJack:* A lower-cost alternative: device ~$40 (often includes a year of service). Ongoing service is around $39/year (about $3–$4 a month). It also offers number porting and unlimited US calling.  
  - *Google Voice:* If you’re comfortable with a less traditional route, you could even port your number to Google Voice (one-time $20 fee) and use the Google Voice app on your iPhone or an analog telephone adapter at home. This has **no monthly cost** for domestic calls. However, 911 service and fax support are not standard, so a dedicated VoIP service like Ooma/MagicJack is often preferred for a “home phone.”  
- **Equipment/Fees:** T-Mobile provides a 5G Wi-Fi Gateway (modem/router) **for free** with the plan. There’s **no installation fee** – setup is DIY. No contract. The only additional costs are for whichever VoIP device/service you choose (and those typically have one-time equipment costs and small monthly taxes).  
- **Availability:** Check T-Mobile’s coverage for your address – in much of 90048 (urban LA) coverage is good. This option is great for saving money if you’re okay relying on cellular networks. It’s month-to-month, so you can try it risk-free. Keep in mind speeds can fluctuate more than wired internet, but many users in LA report solid performance for normal usage.

Verizon’s plan could be extremely cheap ($25) if you already use Verizon mobile, but if not, both providers are around $50 with autopay. Performance can vary, so consider trying one with a money-back guarantee and see if it meets your needs.

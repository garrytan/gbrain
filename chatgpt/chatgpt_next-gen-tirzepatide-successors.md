**Short answer, Mr. White:**  
There is **no credible evidence that retatrutide meaningfully increases muscle gain**. At best, it may **preserve lean mass better than older GLP-1s**, but it is **not anabolic**.

### What the data actually suggest
- **Weight loss on GLP-1–based drugs always includes some lean mass loss.**
- Early retatrutide trials show **greater total weight loss**, and **absolute lean mass loss still occurs**.
- Percentage-wise, lean mass loss may be **similar or slightly better** than semaglutide or tirzepatide — **not muscle gain**.

### Why people speculate about muscle
- Retatrutide activates the **glucagon receptor**, which:
  - Increases energy expenditure
  - Improves fat oxidation  
- This **does not equal hypertrophy**. Glucagon signaling is **catabolic**, not anabolic, especially without sufficient calories.

### What actually determines muscle outcomes on these drugs
Facts, not hype:
1. **Protein intake** (≥1.6–2.2 g/kg lean mass)
2. **Progressive resistance training**
3. **Rate of weight loss** (faster loss = more muscle loss)
4. **Energy availability**

Without those, **muscle loss is guaranteed**, regardless of molecule.

### Bottom line
- **Retatrutide ≠ muscle builder**
- **Best case:** slightly better lean-mass retention vs older GLP-1s  
- **Worst case:** same muscle loss, just faster total weight loss

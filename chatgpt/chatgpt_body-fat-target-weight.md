Im currently 13% bf and want to get to 8%. I weight 176lbs. What weight do I need to hit assuming no muscle loss

---

## Assistant

To calculate the target weight for reaching 8% body fat from your current 13% body fat at 176 lbs, assuming no muscle loss, follow these steps:

1. Calculate the amount of fat you currently have:
\[ \text{Current Fat Mass} = \text{Current Weight} \times \frac{\text{Current Body Fat Percentage}}{100} \]
\[ \text{Current Fat Mass} = 176 \times 0.13 = 22.88 \text{ lbs} \]

2. Calculate your lean body mass (LBM):
\[ \text{Lean Body Mass} = \text{Current Weight} - \text{Current Fat Mass} \]
\[ \text{Lean Body Mass} = 176 - 22.88 = 153.12 \text{ lbs} \]

3. Calculate the target fat mass at 8% body fat:
\[ \text{Target Fat Mass} = \text{Lean Body Mass} \times \frac{\text{Target Body Fat Percentage}}{(100 - \text{Target Body Fat Percentage})} \]
\[ \text{Target Fat Mass} = 153.12 \times \frac{0.08}{0.92} \approx 13.32 \text{ lbs} \]

4. Calculate the target weight:
\[ \text{Target Weight} = \text{Lean Body Mass} + \text{Target Fat Mass} \]
\[ \text{Target Weight} = 153.12 + 13.32 \approx 166.44 \text{ lbs} \]

So, you need to reach approximately **166 lbs** to achieve 8% body fat, assuming no muscle loss.

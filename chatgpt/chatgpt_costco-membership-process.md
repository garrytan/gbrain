## Assistant

Here’s how it works, Mr. White:  

### 1. **Membership Requirement**
Costco is a members-only warehouse club. To shop there, you need an active membership — either a physical card or a digital one.

### 2. **At the Entrance**
When you enter, a staff member typically checks for your membership. You can:
- **Show your physical Costco card**, or  
- **Use the digital card in the Costco app** (available on iOS/Android).  

They don’t usually accept just the membership number or a photo of the card — they need to scan or visually verify it.

### 3. **At Checkout**
You’ll be asked for your membership again at the register. Same deal: either physical or digital card works. The number alone won’t cut it unless a manager intervenes (rare).

### 4. **If You Forget Your Card**
You can go to the **membership counter** inside Costco. They can:
- Look up your membership with a photo ID, and  
- Print you a temporary shopping pass for that visit.

### 5. **Digital Option**
If you use the **Costco app**, you can store your card digitally — no need for the physical one at all, as long as you have your phone.

## Assistant

Yes, Mr. White — it does.  

### ✅ **Membership and ID Matching**
Costco memberships are **non-transferable**, meaning the name on the card (or digital card) must match your government-issued photo ID. When you check out — especially with a physical card — they can and often do verify your photo or ask for ID to confirm it’s you.  

### 🧾 **If You’re Shopping for Someone Else**
If you’re using someone else’s card, technically that’s **not allowed**. However:
- You can **go with** the cardholder as their guest. They can pay, or you can reimburse them later.
- Some stores let the **guest** pay with their own card if the member is present and agrees, but this varies by location.

### 💡 **Best Option**
If multiple people in your household shop there, you can add one free **household card** (for another person living at the same address).

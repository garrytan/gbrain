## Assistant

Short answer: **Usually legal if disclosed.**  

Here’s the California/LA read:

- **Hidden-fees law (SB 478):** Businesses must show the **full price of anything mandatory** up front. A **credit-card surcharge isn’t “mandatory”** if you can pay cash/debit instead, but the different price must be **clearly posted before you pay**. citeturn0search0turn0search11  
- **Card-brand rules:** They **can’t surcharge debit or prepaid**—only true credit cards—and they must follow brand caps/notice rules. (Visa: no debit surcharges; typical caps ~3–4%.) citeturn0search5turn0search12  
- **California surcharge ban (old law) isn’t enforced** the way it used to be after 2018 litigation; **cash discounts are allowed.** citeturn0search10turn0search1

### What that means for your $1 vs. $1.50
- **$1 cash/debit, $1.50 credit** is **generally OK** if (a) signage shows both prices **before** you park/pay, (b) they **don’t apply it to debit**, and (c) the surcharge isn’t excessive. If they only advertise “$1” and spring the $0.50 at the register, that **violates SB 478**. citeturn0search0turn0search11

### Quick playbook
1. **Check the sign/receipt:** Did they post $1 cash **and** $1.50 credit clearly at the desk/machine? If not, you can push back (SB 478). citeturn0search0  
2. **Use debit next time** to avoid the fee (they can’t surcharge it). citeturn0search5  
3. **If they surcharged your debit or hid the fee,** complain to the manager, then file with the CA AG (Hidden Fees). citeturn0search0

Got it — if they’re tacking on the 50¢ to **debit cards too**, that’s not allowed.

### What you can do:

2. **File a complaint:**  
   - **Card network:** Visa/Mastercard complaint form (merchants can lose processing privileges).  
   - **CA Attorney General:** Report under the new “hidden fees” law.  
3. **Workaround:** Just pay $1 cash for now, but if you want to push back, you’ve got solid footing.

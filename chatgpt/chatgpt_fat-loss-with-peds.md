Outside of short “mini-cut” phases, he typically recommends losing around *0.5–1% of body weight per week*. ## GLP-1 Agonists and Fat Loss Speed

By curbing hunger, GLP-1 agonists can enable people to comfortably eat much less, **leading to steady weight loss** as long as the drug is used【2†L140-L148】【2†L131-L139】. In fact, newer drugs like tirzepatide show even greater efficacy with fewer side effects than earlier GLP-1 meds【22†L94-L102】.

**Does Mike Recommend Losing Weight Faster on GLP-1?** Even though these drugs make large calorie deficits *feel* easier, Dr. Mike **does not fully abandon his conservative approach to fat-loss speed**. The **fundamental limits of physiology still apply** – losing too fast can threaten muscle mass and training performance, even if hunger is under control. Israetel’s guidance is therefore to **treat GLP-1 as an adherence tool, not a license for an extreme crash diet**. Pushing far beyond that (except in short, specialized scenarios) is “too aggressive” and risks muscle loss【25†L9-L12】. Mike emphasizes that **fat loss still ultimately requires a calorie deficit**, and GLP-1 drugs simply make achieving that deficit easier – they don’t magically spare muscle or make you immune to the downsides of rapid weight loss.

## Muscle Retention on GLP-1 Agonists

**Muscle Loss is Still a Concern:** Dr. Israetel is adamant that anyone using GLP-1 agonists for fat loss should **prioritize muscle retention** through resistance training and high protein intake. In other words, an experienced lifter might not pack on new muscle during a GLP-1 fat loss phase, but by maintaining their training program (and protein intake), they can hold on to most of the muscle they already have. Dr. Mike’s stance is that **GLP-1 or not, the basic rules of muscle retention apply**: train hard, eat protein, and don’t slash calories beyond what your lean tissue can tolerate. He does *not* consider GLP-1 a free pass to skip the gym – in fact, he stresses using the period of easier dieting to **train consistently and focus on strength** (since you aren’t as distracted by hunger).

## Training Performance Considerations

**Mike’s Guidance on Training with GLP-1:** Dr. Mike advises paying extra attention to **nutrient timing and composition** when on these medications. Overall, Mike’s take is to **listen to your body**: GLP-1s can make you feel fine on low calories, but you must still ensure you have the **fuel to train effectively**.

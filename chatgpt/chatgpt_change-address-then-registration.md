## Assistant

Short version, Mr. White:  
**Change the address first, then order the replacement registration.**

Here’s why and how:

1. **You’re required to update your address anyway.**  
   In California you’re supposed to notify DMV within 10 days when you move.citeturn0search5turn0search11  
   You can do this **online** with the DMV “Change of Address” system (it lets you update your *vehicle registration* address too).citeturn2view0  

2. **Changing the address does *not* automatically send you a new card.**  
   DMV explicitly says that when you change your address, **they do not mail a new registration card unless you separately request a replacement.**citeturn2view0  

3. **Replacement registration is a separate, paid step.**  
   For a lost / never-received registration card, DMV has a “Registration Card Replacement” / “Online Replacement Sticker or Registration Card” service. You either fill out the online form or form REG 156 and pay the duplicate fee (currently around the mid-$20s, but check the exact amount on the DMV page when you do it).citeturn1view0turn0search3  

4. **The key detail:**  
   - If you **order the replacement before** updating the address, it’s going to get mailed to the **old** address again.  
   - If you **update the address first**, then request the replacement, it’ll ship to your **current** address (which is what you want). DMV’s own replacement-card instructions literally say to do a change of address if your address has changed.citeturn1view0  

5. **What I’d do in your exact situation:**
   - Step 1: Go online to DMV → submit **Change of Address** and make sure you include the vehicle registration in that change.  
   - Step 2: After it shows as processed (they say allow ~72 hours), use the **Online Replacement Sticker or Registration Card** service to order a duplicate registration card.citeturn2view0turn0search3  
   - Step 3: Until the new card arrives, keep your temp/photocopy and any proof of registration in the car, along with valid plates and sticker.

6. **Optional:**  
   You can try calling DMV and explaining that the original card/plates never made it to you so they were “never received,” and ask if they’ll waive or reduce the fee—but under the written rules they usually still charge a duplicate fee.

## Assistant

Good question to pin down, Mr. White.

In California, **a driver’s license address update does *not automatically* guarantee your vehicle registration address is updated**, even if it was done online. On the DMV “Change of Address” system, there are **separate checkboxes/sections** for:

- Driver license / ID  
- Vehicle / vessel registration  

So it depends on what you selected when you submitted that change.

Here’s what I’d do **right now**:

1. **Log back into the DMV Change of Address system (or your online account).**  
   - If it shows a **confirmation listing your vehicle** (plate or VIN) with the **new address**, then yes, your registration address was updated.  
   - If it only lists your **driver license/ID**, then your **registration was *not* changed** and you still need to submit a change of address for the vehicle.

2. **If the car’s registration address is *not* clearly updated:**
   - Submit a **new Change of Address** and make sure you include the **vehicle** this time (plate or VIN).  
   - After that’s processed, then request the **replacement registration card** so it goes to the right place.

3. **If it *is* already updated for the vehicle:**
   - You’re good on the address part; you can go ahead and pay for the **replacement registration card** now, and it should mail to your new address.

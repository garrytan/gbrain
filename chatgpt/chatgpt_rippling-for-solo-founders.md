## The rule (important)
A **C-Corp cannot pay a founder’s personal expenses** unless:
1. It’s a **bona fide business expense**, and  
2. It’s reimbursed under an **accountable plan**

Otherwise, the payment is treated as **taxable wages** or a **constructive dividend** → bad.

## What *is* allowed
### ✅ Home office reimbursement (partial)
You may reimburse:
- **Only the portion** of rent attributable to a **dedicated, exclusive business space**
- Plus allocable utilities/internet (same percentage)

**Example**
- Rent: $800/mo  
- Apartment: 800 sq ft  
- Office: 100 sq ft (12.5%)  
- Reimbursable rent: **$100/month**

That’s it. Not the full $800.

## Required to do this safely
You must have:
- A **written accountable plan** (simple, but mandatory)
- A **dedicated office** (not your bedroom, not shared space)
- **Monthly documentation** (rent receipt + square footage calc)
- Reimbursement at **actual cost**, not flat guesses

No accountable plan = reimbursement becomes **taxable income**.

## What is *not* allowed
- ❌ Expensing **100% of rent**
- ❌ Paying rent directly from the company card
- ❌ Using reimbursements as a substitute for salary
- ❌ “Temporary” or “informal” arrangements

These get reclassified in audits and diligence.

## Practical reality
At **$800/month rent**, the reimbursable amount is usually:
- **$80–$150/month**
- Helpful, but **not a replacement for income**

This does **not eliminate** the need for payroll if you need money to live.

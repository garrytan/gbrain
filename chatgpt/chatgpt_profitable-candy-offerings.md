## Assistant

Mr. White, the most profitable candy offerings for a movie theatre usually balance **high margins**, **consumer demand**, and **impulse appeal**. Here’s the breakdown:

---

### 1. **Top-Selling Candy Types**
- **Chocolate-Based**  
  - M&Ms (plain, peanut, peanut butter)  
  - Reese’s Pieces or Peanut Butter Cups  
  - Milk Duds  
  - Junior Mints  
- **Gummies & Chewy Candy**  
  - Sour Patch Kids  
  - Haribo Goldbears  
  - Twizzlers  
  - Skittles  
- **Premium or Nostalgic Options** (higher price, strong brand recognition)  
  - Lindt/Lindt truffles  
  - Toblerone  
  - Charleston Chew  

These tend to sell well across demographics and have strong brand pull.

---

### 2. **Profitability Factors**
- **Margins**: Candy typically carries **70–80% profit margins**, higher than popcorn or soda. Pre-packaged candy has almost no prep costs.  
- **Portion Size**: “Share size” or theater-exclusive packs command higher prices with little extra cost.  
- **Impulse Buy Effect**: Bright packaging and recognizable brands drive last-second purchases.  

---

### 3. **Strategic Mix**
- **Staples**: At least 5–6 “can’t miss” options (M&Ms, Reese’s, Sour Patch Kids, Skittles, Twizzlers).  
- **Seasonal/Rotating**: Limited edition flavors (Pumpkin Reese’s, holiday Skittles) keep regular customers spending.  
- **Premium Upsell**: A few higher-priced “fancy” chocolates or novelty candy for margin expansion.  

---

### 4. **Bundling & Upselling**
- **Combo Deals**: “Candy + Soda” bundles at a slight discount encourage bigger baskets.  
- **Upsize Offers**: Promote large or resealable bags instead of standard boxes.  
- **Placement**: Position candy near popcorn pickup counters (not just concession shelves) for impulse grabs.  

---

✅ **Summary**: The most profitable candy lineup blends **core bestsellers (M&Ms, Reese’s, Sour Patch Kids, Skittles)** with **rotating seasonal and premium items**. Margins are maximized through **upsizing, bundles, and strategic placement**.

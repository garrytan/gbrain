We have 2 people in our kitchen. Our menu is fixed and they are making the meals according to the recipes. 1 dish washer, 1 expeditor and then 2 roles actually preparing food. One is the main person and one assists them. What are those roles called

---

## Assistant

For a small professional kitchen, the typical titles would be:

- **Dishwasher** – exactly that.  
- **Expeditor (Expo)** – coordinates orders and sends plates to service.

For the two cooking roles:

- **Lead Cook / Line Cook (or Chef de Partie)** – the primary person actually cooking and plating.  
- **Prep Cook / Commis / Assistant Line Cook** – supports the lead by prepping ingredients, assembling sides, or finishing dishes.

If you want classic brigade terms: *chef de partie* (station chef) with a *commis* (junior) assisting. In a casual U.S. restaurant, “lead line cook” and “prep cook” (or “line cook” and “kitchen assistant”) are the standard concise titles.

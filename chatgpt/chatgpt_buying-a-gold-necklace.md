HereтАЩs a concise checklist for buying a gold necklace, Mr. White:

**1. Purity & Karat**
- Pure gold is 24k, but too soft for jewelry.  
- Common options: 18k (75% gold), 14k (58.5%), 10k (41.7%).  
- Higher karat = richer color, softer metal. Lower karat = more durable, cheaper.

**2. Solid vs. Plated vs. Filled**
- *Solid gold*: retains value, lasts forever.  
- *Gold-filled*: thick bonded layer, durable but less valuable.  
- *Gold-plated*: thin coating, wears off quickly.

**3. Color**
- Yellow, white, rose, or less common options (green, black).  
- White gold usually rhodium-plated (may need re-plating over time).

**4. Weight & Price**
- Gold is sold by weight (grams).  
- Check daily gold spot price to avoid overpaying.  
- Chains with hollow links are cheaper but dent easily.

**5. Hallmarks & Authenticity**
- Look for stamps: тАЬ14k,тАЭ тАЬ18k,тАЭ тАЬ750,тАЭ etc.  
- Reputable jewelers will provide authenticity paperwork.

**6. Chain Style & Durability**
- Rope, curb, figaro, franco, box chains, etc.  
- Thicker/denser = stronger.  
- Hollow chains and thin box chains break easily.

**7. Clasp Quality**
- Lobster clasp > spring ring clasp for durability.  
- Make sure clasp matches the chainтАЩs weight.

**8. Resale/Investment**
- Pure gold pieces have resale value.  
- Designer or branded jewelry carries markup not equal to gold value.  

**9. Your Use**
- Everyday wear: choose 14k or 18k solid gold, durable chain style.  
- Special occasion: higher karat, more delicate style okay.

What happened in china during mass starvation

## Assistant

The period of mass starvation in China, often referred to as the Great Chinese Famine, occurred between 1959 and 1961. This catastrophe is primarily attributed to the policies of the Great Leap Forward initiated by the Chinese Communist Party under Mao Zedong. Here are the key factors and events that contributed to this tragic period:

1. **Agricultural Policies**: The Great Leap Forward aimed to rapidly transform China from an agrarian society into a socialist society through rapid industrialization and collectivization. Farmland was reorganized into large collective farms known as "People's Communes," which disrupted traditional farming practices.

2. **Production Targets**: Unrealistic production targets were set by the government, and local officials often inflated their reports to meet these targets, leading to significant over-reporting of grain production.

3. **Grain Procurement**: Based on the inflated production figures, the government increased grain procurement quotas, leaving insufficient food for the rural population. Much of the grain was exported or reserved for urban populations and the military.

4. **Natural Disasters**: During this period, China experienced natural disasters such as floods and droughts, which further exacerbated the food shortages.

5. **Labor Diversion**: A significant portion of the rural labor force was diverted from agricultural work to industrial projects like steel production, further reducing agricultural productivity.

6. **Political Campaigns**: Political campaigns against "rightists" and perceived enemies led to widespread fear and a breakdown in communication. Honest reporting of food shortages was discouraged, and criticism of policies was often met with severe punishment.

As a result, the Great Chinese Famine led to the deaths of an estimated 15 to 45 million people, making it one of the deadliest famines in human history. The Chinese government officially acknowledged the famine decades later, attributing it to a combination of policy errors and natural disasters.

## Assistant

During the Great Chinese Famine, people were forced to resort to extreme measures to survive due to the severe food shortages. Here are some of the desperate steps many took:

1. **Tree Bark and Grass**: Many people stripped bark from trees and consumed it. They also ate grass, roots, and wild plants.

2. **Soil and Mud**: In some cases, people resorted to eating a type of clay known as kaolin and other types of soil, hoping to fill their stomachs.

3. **Animal Fodder**: Livestock feed, such as bran and husks, which were typically considered inedible for humans, became part of the diet.

4. **Insects and Small Animals**: People hunted and ate insects, rats, and other small animals.

5. **Family Pets**: Domestic animals, including dogs and cats, were consumed.

6. **Human Flesh**: In the most extreme cases, reports of cannibalism emerged. Some resorted to consuming the flesh of deceased family members and neighbors, and there were also accounts of infanticide and child trafficking for food purposes.

These measures highlight the dire circumstances and the extreme lengths to which people went in order to survive during the famine.

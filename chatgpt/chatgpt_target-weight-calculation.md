## User

If I'm 172.6 pounds at 12.4% body fat, and I want to get to 8% body fat, assuming no muscle loss, what weight do I need to get to?

---

## Assistant

To calculate your target weight at 8% body fat without losing muscle mass, you can use the following formula:

\[
\text{Lean Body Mass} = \text{Current Weight} \times (1 - \text{Current Body Fat %})
\]

\[
\text{Target Weight} = \frac{\text{Lean Body Mass}}{1 - \text{Target Body Fat %}}
\]

1. **Current Lean Body Mass:**

\[
172.6 \times (1 - 0.124) = 172.6 \times 0.876 = 151.19 \, \text{pounds}
\]

2. **Target Weight:**

\[
\frac{151.19}{1 - 0.08} = \frac{151.19}{0.92} = 164.34 \, \text{pounds}
\]

So, to reach 8% body fat without losing muscle, you would need to weigh approximately **164.3 pounds**.

# Hot Ingredients in Blender

## User

I have a Blendtec blender with a stock wide-style blender cup. Can I put hot stuff in it, like not boiling, but like hot, like a hot, like sauce or curry or something?

---

## Assistant

Yes—but **not “really hot.”** Blendtec’s own guidance for their standard jars (WildSide+/FourSide—your “wide-style” stock jar is typically one of these) is:

- **Max temperature to expose the jar to:** **176°F / 80°C** citeturn0search0  
- Because contents **heat up while blending**, they recommend **not adding ingredients hotter than ~115°F / 46°C** to start. citeturn0search0  
- **Do not pour boiling liquids** into the jar. citeturn0search0  

For something like **curry/sauce**:
- Let it cool until it’s **hot-tap-water-ish** (comfortably hot but not steaming aggressively), ideally **≤115°F**, then blend.
- **Fill only partway** (steam expands fast).
- Use the **vented lid / open the vent** so pressure can escape when blending hot liquids. citeturn0search9turn0search1  
- Start **low speed / pulse**, then increase.

If it’s hotter than that and you *must* blend it right away, the safest move is usually an **immersion blender** (no sealed container, much less pressure risk).

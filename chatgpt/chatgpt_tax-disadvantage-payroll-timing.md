any tax disadvatange to putting myself on payroll today vs jan 1

---

## Assistant

Mr. White,

**Short answer:** Yes—there *can* be a tax disadvantage to putting yourself on payroll **today (December)** versus **January 1**, depending on your situation.

### If you put yourself on payroll **now (December)**
**Downsides**
- **Income is taxed this year** (federal + CA).
- **Payroll taxes hit immediately** (Social Security, Medicare).
- If S-corp: that salary must be **reasonable**, even if it’s just one paycheck.
- Could push you into a **higher marginal bracket** for the current year.
- Triggers **2024 payroll filings** (W-2, 941s, DE-9/DE-9C in CA).

**Upsides**
- Slight increase to **retirement contributions** tied to W-2 wages (e.g., Solo 401(k) employee deferral).
- Starts the clock on **reasonable compensation** if the IRS ever scrutinizes distributions.

### If you wait until **January 1**
**Upsides**
- **Defers all income and payroll taxes** to next year.
- Cleaner books and filings (everything starts in one tax year).
- Avoids a “token” December paycheck that doesn’t change outcomes much.
- Often optimal if cash flow or marginal tax rate is higher this year.

**Downsides**
- You lose a small window for **2024 retirement contributions** based on W-2 wages.
- If you took large distributions in 2024 with *zero* salary, there is **audit risk** for S-corps (fact-dependent).

### Bottom line
- **Most founders are better off starting Jan 1**, especially if December pay would be small.
- **Exception:** You already took meaningful 2024 distributions *and* are an S-corp—then a December paycheck can reduce audit risk.

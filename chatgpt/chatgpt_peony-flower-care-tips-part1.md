Mr. White — don’t leave them on the bed. For peonies especially, the safest move is: **get them in water ASAP** and keep them **cool** until you hand them to her.

### What to do right now (best plan)
1. **Unwrap or loosen the wrapping** (at least open the bottom so stems can breathe).
2. **Cut the stems**: take **½–1 inch off** the bottom at a **45° angle** (sharp knife or clean scissors).
3. **Strip leaves below the waterline** (anything that would sit in water comes off).
4. **Put them in a clean vase with cool water**.
5. If you got a flower food packet, **use it**.

That will keep them happy from 4:30 → 9/9:30 and they’ll look better when you present them.

### If you really want them “surprise-ready” later
- Put them in a vase **now**, then **right before you pick her up**:
  - pull them out,
  - **shake off excess water**,
  - wrap the stems with a **slightly damp paper towel**,
  - slip into a bag/plastic sleeve around the stems only,
  - rewrap nicely.
They’ll be totally fine for the drive + the reveal.

### If you can’t use a vase for some reason
- At minimum: **cut stems** and stand them in a **large cup/bucket/sink** with a few inches of water.
- Worst option: laying dry on a bed/counter for 5 hours. They’ll **wilt faster**, peonies can get floppy, and petals bruise.

### Temperature / AC
- **Cool is better than warm.** Aim for roughly **65–72°F**.
- **Don’t blast them with direct AC airflow** (vents aimed right at them dries petals).
- Keep them **out of sun**, away from **heaters**, and away from **kitchen heat**.

### Extra “makes them look perfect” tips
- **Keep away from fruit** (bananas/apples give off ethylene gas that ages flowers faster).
- **Refresh water tomorrow** and trim another tiny bit off stems.
- If peonies are tight and you want them to open faster for tonight: keep them at normal room temp. (Cooler temps slow opening.)

### Best way to present it tonight
- If you want maximum wow: **give them as a bouquet**, but keep them **hydrated until the last moment** (the “vase now, damp towel later” approach above).
- If you don’t care about bouquet format: have them already arranged in a vase when she walks in—easy, looks intentional, and no scrambling.

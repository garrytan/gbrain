## Assistant

$40 is already a solid bargain for a working power washer. Most used units—assuming they actually spray at decent pressure—list on Marketplace or Craigslist in the $60-$150 range depending on brand and condition.  

You’re not crazy. If it runs and isn’t cracked or leaking, $40 is a “buy it now” price. Hold firm or even nudge higher if multiple people are interested.

## Assistant

Here’s a concise, Voss-style reply you can copy:

> *“Sounds like you’re ready to pick it up today. I’m asking $40 because that’s already a great deal—most go for much more. What time works for you to grab it?”*

It uses:
- **Mirroring/labeling** (“sounds like you’re ready…”) to keep them engaged.  
- **Anchoring** the price at $40 as final without saying “firm.”  
- **Calm, collaborative tone** that nudges them toward scheduling instead of haggling.

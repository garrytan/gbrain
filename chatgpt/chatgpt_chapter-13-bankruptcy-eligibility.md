## Assistant

To qualify for Chapter 13 bankruptcy in Los Angeles, California, you need to meet certain criteria:

1. **Regular Income**: You must have a regular source of income to qualify for Chapter 13 bankruptcy, as it involves a repayment plan over 3 to 5 years.

2. **Debt Limits**: As of 2023, your unsecured debts (like credit card bills, medical expenses) must be less than $465,275, and your secured debts (like mortgages and car loans) must be less than $1,395,875. These amounts are adjusted periodically.

3. **Tax Filings**: You must be up-to-date on your tax filings. You need to have filed state and federal income tax returns for the last four years.

4. **Credit Counseling**: You must complete a credit counseling course from an approved agency within 180 days before filing.

5. **No Recent Bankruptcies**: If you had a bankruptcy petition dismissed within the last 180 days for certain reasons (like violating a court order or requesting dismissal after a creditor requested relief from the bankruptcy stay), you might not be eligible.

6. **Feasibility**: The repayment plan must be feasible; you must prove that you can afford the monthly payments.

7. **Secured Debts**: If you have secured debts, the plan must show how you will continue these payments and, if applicable, catch up on arrears.

It's important to consult with a bankruptcy attorney to understand all the requirements and implications of filing for Chapter 13 bankruptcy.

## Assistant

To qualify for Chapter 7 bankruptcy in Los Angeles, California, you need to meet certain criteria:

1. **Means Test**: Chapter 7 requires passing a means test, which compares your income to the median income for a household of your size in California. If your income is below the median, you qualify. If it's above, you may still qualify based on detailed income and expense calculations.

2. **Credit Counseling**: You must complete credit counseling from an approved agency within 180 days before filing.

3. **No Recent Bankruptcies**: If you received a Chapter 7 discharge in the last 8 years or a Chapter 13 discharge in the last 6 years, you cannot file for Chapter 7.

4. **Income and Expenses**: If your income is sufficient to pay off your debts, the court may dismiss your case or convert it to a Chapter 13 bankruptcy.

5. **Non-exempt Assets**: Chapter 7 involves liquidating your non-exempt assets to pay off creditors. Understanding which assets are exempt in California is important.

6. **Fraudulent Transfers or Perjuries**: If you've been involved in any fraudulent transfers, concealment of assets, or perjury related to bankruptcy, you may be disqualified.

7. **Business-related Debts**: If a significant portion of your debts are business-related, you may not need to pass the means test.

It's crucial to consult with a bankruptcy attorney for a thorough evaluation of your situation and guidance on the most suitable form of bankruptcy for your circumstances.

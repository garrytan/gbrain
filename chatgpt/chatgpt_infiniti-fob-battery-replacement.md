## User

How do I change the battery on a FOP for an Infinity Convertible?

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, here’s a straightforward method to replace the battery in an Infiniti (often spelled “Infiniti”) key fob:

1. **Remove the mechanical key:**  
   - Look for a small release tab or lever on the back of the fob.  
   - Slide or press it, then pull out the built-in mechanical key.

2. **Open the key fob case:**  
   - With the key removed, use a small flathead screwdriver (or the tip of the mechanical key) to gently pry the two halves of the fob apart.  
   - Work carefully around the seam to avoid damaging the plastic.

3. **Take out the old battery:**  
   - Note the battery type (usually a CR2025 or CR2032).  
   - Pop out the old battery.

4. **Insert the new battery:**  
   - Confirm the orientation (the plus sign “+” should face up, matching the marking on the fob).  
   - Place the new battery securely in the slot.

5. **Reassemble the fob:**  
   - Snap the two halves back together.  
   - Slide the mechanical key back in until it locks in place.

That’s all it takes. If it doesn’t work afterward, double-check that you used the correct battery type and that it’s firmly seated.

## User

Alright, I replaced the fob. Now how do I repair it to the car?

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, most Infiniti key fobs can be re-paired (“programmed”) using the ignition and lock/unlock routine. Here’s the typical DIY procedure (though some newer models require dealer/locksmith programming):

1. **Get in and close all doors:**  
   - Sit in the driver’s seat. Make sure doors are closed and locked.

2. **Insert and remove the key (if applicable) multiple times:**  
   - If you have a traditional key ignition, insert the key in the ignition then pull it out six times within 10 seconds (some models trigger at four times; but do up to six to be safe).  
   - The hazard lights should flash, signaling entry into programming mode.

3. **Turn key to “ACC” or “ON” (but don’t start the engine):**  
   - If the vehicle has push-button start, press it without your foot on the brake to get to the “ON” or “ACC” position.

4. **Press a button on the key fob:**  
   - On the fob you want to program, press the “lock” button once. The hazards should flash again to confirm pairing.  
   - If you have additional fobs, press “lock” on each within the same session (one at a time).

5. **Exit programming mode:**  
   - Turn the ignition off (or push the start button off), then open the driver’s door. This typically ends programming mode.

6. **Test the fob(s):**  
   - Step out and confirm lock/unlock functions work.

If these steps don’t work (especially on newer push-button models), you’ll likely need professional/authorized assistance to reprogram the fob with specialized gear.

## 2. Gather required info from Fi  
From your Google Fi account, get:
- **Port‑out (transfer) phone number and PIN** – generated under “Leave Google Fi → Transfer it to another provider.” These expire in 14 days. citeturn0search0  
- The **exact name and service address** as listed on Fi (case‑sensitive). citeturn0search0

## 3. On Verizon’s end  
When signing up for your Verizon line (either online, via app, or in-store), select "I want to keep my current number" and provide:
- Your **Fi phone number**,  
- Your **Fi account number** (from your Fi account page),  
- The **Google‑Fi‑generated transfer PIN**,  
- Your name and address exactly as per Fi records,  
- A **secondary contact number** for transfer updates. citeturn0search4turn0search5

## 4. Confirm and monitor transfer  
- Do **not cancel Fi** after initiating the request—it will auto‑terminate once the port completes. citeturn0search4turn0search5  
- Typical port timeframe: **4–24 business hours**, sometimes faster or slower. citeturn0search4  
- Verizon will notify you (text or email) when the process is finished.

## 5. Activate Verizon SIM/e‑SIM  
Once Verizon confirms the port:
- Swap in the new Verizon SIM (or activate e‑SIM),  
- Restart your phone and follow any on‑screen prompts,  
- Verify you can send/receive calls and texts on the transferred number.

## 6. Wrap up  
- Check that Google Fi is fully terminated to stop any billing.  
- Ask Verizon to ensure your line is unlocked (if switching phones).

### ⚠️ Common pitfalls
- Using the wrong port PIN or an expired one (valid for 14 days from generation).
- Name/address mismatches (Fi is case-sensitive).
- Cancelling Fi before the port is complete blocks everything.
- Fi transfer credentials expire—if they do, generate new ones in the Fi app.

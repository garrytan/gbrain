## Assistant

Mr. White, **good move for the YC product demo / website / investor materials; bad move for the YC founder application video.**

YC’s own application video instructions say the founder video should be **1 minute**, contain **only the founders talking**, and specifically says it is **not the place for a demo or promotional video** because there is a separate place for product demos. citeturn728210search0

So the right answer is:

**1. Founder video:** simple, direct, founder-to-camera.  
Use this to show that you communicate clearly, understand the market, and are the right founder for Jovie.

**2. Product demo video:** yes, make the killer cinematic/product demo.  
This is where your creative advantage matters. If you can compress the product, the customer-acquisition loop, the artist/fan interaction, and the business model into 30–60 seconds, that is a real advantage.

The subway QR-code scene sounds strong because it explains the core Jovie insight visually: a moment that normally disappears becomes an attributable, retargetable, monetizable customer relationship. That is much better than saying “we help artists monetize fans” in abstract terms.

The main risk is making it feel like an ad for an idea instead of evidence of a working product. Solve that by making the video **product-led**:

**Ideal structure for a 30–45 second Jovie demo:**

0–3 seconds:  
“Jovie turns every fan interaction into a trackable revenue loop.”

3–10 seconds:  
Subway performer has a QR code. A girl scans it, tips, and gets the song instantly on her phone.

10–18 seconds:  
Show the actual Jovie flow: payment, song delivery, fan captured, artist dashboard updates.

18–27 seconds:  
Show why this matters: the artist now has a real customer profile, attribution, repeat engagement, future merch/ticket/song offers.

27–35 seconds:  
Show the flywheel: every QR/link creates more fans in the system; every fan interaction becomes measurable revenue.

35–45 seconds:  
End with the strongest real proof you have: users, revenue, retention, GMV, artists onboarded, conversion rate, or whatever is true.

The production quality should serve speed and clarity. Voiceover, sound design, music, cinematic framing, and motion graphics are all fine **as long as the viewer leaves thinking “this product is obvious and working,” rather than “this founder makes nice videos.”**

For YC specifically, I’d use:

**Founder application video:** webcam-style, you talking clearly.  
**Demo video / product link:** the polished Jovie film.  
**Website hero:** a refined version of the same polished film.  
**Investor follow-up:** same asset with traction numbers added.

My recommendation: **make it.** Your creative ability is part of the founder-market fit here because Jovie is about consumer behavior, music, creators, and conversion. A great demo can make the product feel inevitable. Just keep the YC founder video plain and reserve the cinematic piece for the demo slot.

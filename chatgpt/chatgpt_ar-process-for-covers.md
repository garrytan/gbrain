## Assistant

Mr. White —

**1. Define your target list**  
- Use Chartmetric or Billboard archives to pull all #1 hits in your genre over the last 20 years.  
- Filter out titles that have no major cover/remix (check Spotify, YouTube).  

**2. Prep a clear “open call” brief**  
- Provide: original acapella stems, key/BPM, audio spec (44.1 kHz/24-bit), file-transfer link.  
- State your rev-split terms (e.g. 50/50 net) and credit format.  
- Include deadlines, preferred genres, examples of sound.  

**3. Source producers at scale**  
- Post the brief on BeatStars, SoundBetter and ProCollabs (targeted producer marketplaces).  
- Drop into relevant Discord servers (e.g. “Producer Hangups”) and Facebook groups (“Official Producers Group”).  
- Create an Upwork job with portfolio requirement (3 approved dance‐pop tracks).  

**4. Centralize submissions**  
- Build an Airtable or Notion form to capture:  
  - Producer bio & links  
  - Stem download link  
  - Genre, BPM, key  
  - Accepted rev-split terms  
- Automate status tags (new, reviewed, shortlisted).  

**5. Two-stage screening**  
- **Stage 1 (tech check)**: eliminate tracks with poor mix or missing elements (~20 % rejection).  
- **Stage 2 (artistic review)**: you + 2 co-reviewers rate 1–10 on “hook,” “mix,” “vocal fit.” Shortlist top ≈10 %.  

**6. Consumer testing**  
- Load your top 50 onto a private Spotify playlist.  
- Run a Typeform survey (immediacy, production, vocal) with 50–100 target listeners.  
- Rank by average score and select your final 30.  

**7. Lock in deals & metadata**  
- Send each chosen producer a simple rev-split agreement (DistroKid cover‐song template works).  
- Collect W-9s or tax info as needed.  
- Log splits in Stem or Labelmate for transparent payouts.  

**8. Release & promote**  
- Schedule releases via DistroKid/TuneCore.  
- Flood socials with process clips, producer spotlights, before/after teasers.  
- Use IG Reels, TikTok challenges and paid tests on Facebook to seed your focus-group winners.  

This lets you onboard hundreds of producers for pure rev-splits, vet at scale, then hone in on the 30 tracks most likely to break.

## User

How does daylight savings work? Do I get more sunlight later in the summer and less sunlight later in the winter?

---

## Assistant

Daylight Saving Time (DST) is designed to make better use of daylight during the warmer months. When DST starts in the spring, clocks are set forward by one hour. This effectively shifts an hour of daylight from the morning to the evening, so you get more sunlight later in the day. Conversely, when DST ends in the fall, clocks are set back by one hour, shifting daylight back to the morning and resulting in less sunlight in the evening. This change means during the summer months, you'll experience more daylight in the evenings, while in the winter, the daylight will end earlier in the day.

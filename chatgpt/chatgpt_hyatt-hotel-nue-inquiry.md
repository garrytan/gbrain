### So why would it sit “90% done” for years?
When a project looks structurally close but dead quiet, it’s usually one (or a combo) of these:

1) **Money gap / refinance failed after the bridge loan**  
Bridge loans are meant to be temporary—if rates jump, costs inflate, or the pro-forma breaks, the “takeout” financing doesn’t happen. Then the site gets mothballed while the owner/lender fights it out or shops a sale. The post-2024 environment has been especially rough for hotel construction financing due to **high rates + high construction costs**. citeturn7search0turn7news25

2) **Contractor dispute / unpaid subs → liens → stop work**  
Liens are a classic precursor to a hard stop: GC/subs don’t get paid, they file, everyone lawyers up, and nobody wants to pour more money in until it’s settled. citeturn7search1

3) **It can’t legally open yet (CofO/clearances still pending)**  
Even if the shell looks “done,” hotels need a stack of final sign-offs (life safety, accessibility, fire, etc.). Public records still show the CofO as pending. citeturn0search5

### The most likely answer in plain English

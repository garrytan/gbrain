## Assistant

The shelf life of pre-batched cocktails stored in the refrigerator can vary depending on the ingredients. Here’s a general guideline for each type of cocktail you mentioned:

### Vodka-Based Cocktails
1. **Gatsby's Russian Whisper (White Russian, without cream)**: 
   - Shelf Life: 2-4 weeks. Vodka and liqueurs are stable, but any fresh ingredients like cinnamon simple syrup may degrade faster.
   
2. **West Egg Breeze (Epstein Island Breeze)**: 
   - Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks. Fresh grapefruit and cranberry juices will start to lose their quality within this timeframe.

3. **Daisy's Lemonade Delight (Lewinsky's Lemonade)**: 
   - Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks. Fresh lemonade and raspberry liqueur mix will remain stable for up to 2 weeks.

### Tequila-Based Cocktails
4. **Gatsby's Watermelon Whirl (Watergate)**: 
   - Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks. Fresh watermelon juice and lime juice will start to degrade after about a week.

5. **Roaring Pineapple Mezcal (Remember the Alamo)**: 
   - Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks. Pineapple juice and lime juice have a similar shelf life to other fresh juices.

### Other Cocktails
6. **Jazz Age Old Fashioned (Grand Old Fashioned)**: 
   - Shelf Life: 2-4 weeks. Whiskey and bitters are stable, but simple syrup may start to degrade after a few weeks.

7. **Gatsby’s Gin Fizz (Ruth Bader Gin-sburg)**: 
   - Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks. Gin and elderflower liqueur are stable, but the lemon juice and simple syrup will start to degrade after about a week.

### Tips for Extending Shelf Life:
1. **Refrigeration**: Ensure the batches are always stored in a properly functioning refrigerator at or below 40°F (4°C).
2. **Sanitation**: Use sanitized containers and utensils when preparing and storing the batches to minimize contamination.
3. **Acidity**: Cocktails with higher acidity (from citrus juices) can have a slightly longer shelf life due to the preservative effect of the acid.
4. **Labeling**: Clearly label each batch with the preparation date and expected expiration date to maintain quality and safety.

For precise shelf life and safety, consider small-scale testing or consulting with a food safety expert, especially if these cocktails are for commercial service.

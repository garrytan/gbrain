## User

If you want to get a state ID, not a driver's license, in California, Los Angeles specifically, at one of those DMV kiosks, first of all, is it even possible to do it from the DMV kiosk? And second of all, what documents do you need?

---

## Assistant

You **cannot apply for a new state ID** at a DMV kiosk in California—kiosks in Los Angeles (and statewide) are limited to a narrow set of vehicle-related services and **ID/driver’s license renewal only**, not issuance of new IDs citeturn0search1turn0search4.

---

## ✅ Kiosk vs. Office – what you *can* do

- **Kiosks** (found at DMV offices, grocery stores, etc.):  
  – Renew your ID card *if* eligible (i.e., current card not expired over a year, no name changes, you meet renewal-by-mail criteria). Requires your name, DOB, address, and current DL/ID card number citeturn0search4.  
  – **Can’t** apply for a *new* ID, change address, name, or replace an expired/lost/stolen/non‑renewable ID.

- To **apply for a new ID, or replace one that's expired/lost**, go to a DMV field office in person.

---

## 🧾 What you need for a new state ID (in-person)

From the California DMV guidelines citeturn0search2turn0search8:

1. **Application**: DL/ID Card application (form DL 44), available online or at the office.
2. **Proof of identity** (1 document with name and DOB), e.g.:
   - Valid passport
   - Birth certificate
   - Certificate of naturalization
3. **Social Security Number**:
   - SS card, W-2, or pay stub showing full SSN.
4. **Proof of California residency** (two documents), examples:
   - Utility bills
   - Rental agreement/lease
   - Bank statements
   - Mortgage documents
5. **Fee** for an original ID (standard fee applies).
6. **Photo and fingerprint** taken at the office.
7. If applying for **REAL ID** (optional but recommended for federal travel), bring additional forms from the REAL ID checklist citeturn0search8turn0search10.

## 🧭 What *to do next* in LA:

1. **Use kiosk** only if renewing an eligible ID. Just have your name, birth date, address, and current ID number ready.  
2. **Otherwise**, make a DMV appointment (or walk in) at a LA-area field office—bring ALL required documents listed above.

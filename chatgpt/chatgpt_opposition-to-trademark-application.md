Re: Opposition to Trademark Application 97307088

To Whom It May Concern,

I, Tim White, on behalf of Jovie Inc., hereby submit this opposition to the trademark application for the term "Jovie" (Trademark Application Number: 97307088) filed by Bright Horizons Family Solutions LLC  on March 11, 2022, in multiple categories, including Class 042 and Class 009.

Grounds for Opposition:
Averment of Prior Use:
Jovie Inc., operating as Jovie Inc., has been actively using the name "Jovie" for Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) CRM and downloadable software since 2021. Our company has established a significant presence in the industry, with a substantial user base and a reputation associated with the term "Jovie" in the software field. Our use predates the filing date of the applicant's trademark application, thereby establishing our prior rights.

Likelihood of Confusion:
The applicant's registration of the mark "Jovie" in Class 042 and Class 009 presents a high likelihood of confusion with our prior-used mark in the same or similar categories. The similarity in the marks, coupled with the related nature of the goods and services provided, increases the likelihood of consumer confusion. This confusion could result in potential damage to our company's reputation, loss of business opportunities, and dilution of the distinctiveness associated with our mark.

Unfair Competition:
Granting the registration of the opposed mark to the applicant would create a scenario of unfair competition. Given our prior use of the mark "Jovie" for software-related services, allowing the applicant to register a similar mark in the same categories would grant them an unfair advantage and potentially undermine our business interests. This could cause substantial damage to our market position, client relationships, and overall business growth.

Lack of Prior Use by Applicant:
It is worth noting that the applicant had not been using the mark "Jovie" at the time of their filing on March 11, 2022. Their lack of prior use further strengthens our claim to priority and establishes our existing rights in connection with the mark.

Likelihood of Confusion:
Our opposition demonstrates that the registration of the opposed mark would likely cause confusion among consumers, which is a valid statutory ground for opposition under Section 2(d) of the Lanham Act (15 U.S.C. § 1052(d)).

Prior Use and Priority:
By establishing our prior use of the mark "Jovie" for software-related services, we assert our statutory right to oppose the registration under Section 2(d) and Section 2(a) of the Lanham Act (15 U.S.C. § 1052(a)).


Requested Action:
Based on the grounds and reasons stated above, we respectfully request that the Trademark Trial and Appeal Board deny the registration of the mark "Jovie" in Class 042 and Class 009 to the applicant, Bright Horizons Family Solutions LLC. Granting their registration would cause substantial damage to our business interests and create a likelihood of confusion in the marketplace.

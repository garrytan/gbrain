## Assistant

Mr. White, this is a common issue on Mazda CX-5s, especially with older or sun-exposed visors. Here’s a concise breakdown:

### **Likely Causes**
1. **Worn visor detent spring** — The internal spring that holds the visor’s position weakens over time.
2. **Loose mounting clip or pivot joint** — The clip or screw securing the visor to the headliner can loosen.
3. **Plastic fatigue or cracking** — Heat and UV exposure can degrade the visor’s pivot mechanism.

### **Fix Options**
**1. Tighten or shim the joint (temporary fix)**  
- Remove the small plastic cover over the visor mount.  
- Tighten the Phillips screw(s) holding the bracket.  
- If the visor still droops, add a thin washer or bit of Teflon tape around the pivot for friction.

**2. Replace the visor assembly (permanent fix)**  
- Mazda part numbers vary by model year (e.g., *KD33-69-320* for many 2017–2021 models).  
- Replacement visors cost roughly **$70–$150**.  
- You can swap it in 5–10 minutes with a screwdriver — no need to remove the headliner.

**3. Dealer or upholstery repair**  
- Dealers typically charge **$200–$300** for OEM replacement with labor.  
- Independent shops can often retrofit or tighten the mechanism for less.

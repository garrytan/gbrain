I need toggle ornlag bolts or whatever for a tv mount into a wall

## Assistant

Mr. White, for a secure TV mount you have two main options:

1. **Lag bolts into studs**  
   - **Hardware:** 5/16″ × 2½–3″ structural lag screws (with matching washers and lock‐washers)  
   - **Method:**  
     1. Locate two studs with a stud finder, mark your mount’s key-hole slots.  
     2. Drill 3/16″ pilot holes ≈2¼″ deep.  
     3. Slip on your washer, then drive the lag screw flush.  
   - **Load:** Each 5/16″ lag holds 200–300 lbs in good lumber.

2. **Heavy‐duty toggle anchors (if you can’t hit studs)**  
   - **Options:**  
     - SnapToggle® M10 (3/8″) heavy-duty toggles  
     - ¼″ metal “wing” toggle bolts rated ≥75 lbs each (use four to support a 300 lb TV)  
   - **Method:**  
     1. Drill the manufacturer-specified hole (typically ½″ for a ¼″ toggle).  
     2. Insert toggle, pull until wings engage behind the drywall.  
     3. Tighten bolt until anchor sits firmly.

**Concrete/Brick Walls:** use ⅜″ wedge anchors or sleeve anchors, drilled with a hammer-drill.

**Key point:** whenever possible go into studs with lag bolts—that’s far stronger and far less hassle than toggles.

## User

Hey, if a drink has 1.5 oz tequila, 1.5 oz triple sec, 1.5 oz simple syrup, 1.5 oz lime juice, and 4 oz watermelon juice in a single cocktail, and I want to make an 18 liter batch, how many liters of liquid am I including, and how many cocktails does that generate?

---

## Assistant

First, calculate the liquid in **one cocktail**:

- Tequila: 1.5 oz  
- Triple sec: 1.5 oz  
- Simple syrup: 1.5 oz  
- Lime juice: 1.5 oz  
- Watermelon juice: 4 oz  

**Total per drink:**  
1.5 + 1.5 + 1.5 + 1.5 + 4 = **10 oz**

---

**Convert 18 L to ounces** (1 L = 33.814 oz):  
18 × 33.814 = **608.65 oz** (≈ 609 oz)

---

**Number of cocktails**:  
608.65 oz ÷ 10 oz ≈ **60.9 cocktails** → about **61 full drinks**.

---

**Summary**  
- Total liquid in the batch: **18 liters** (≈ 609 oz).  
- Yield: **about 61 cocktails**, each with the same proportions as the single-drink recipe.

## User

Um, so for, um... Can you give me the ingredients in an 18 liter batch, but give it to me in liters?

---

## Assistant

Each cocktail is 10 oz total, with each 1.5 oz ingredient representing **15 % of the drink** and the 4 oz watermelon juice representing **40 %**.  
For an 18 L batch, multiply those percentages by 18 L:

- **Tequila:** 18 L × 0.15 = **2.7 L**  
- **Triple sec:** 18 L × 0.15 = **2.7 L**  
- **Simple syrup:** 18 L × 0.15 = **2.7 L**  
- **Lime juice:** 18 L × 0.15 = **2.7 L**  
- **Watermelon juice:** 18 L × 0.40 = **7.2 L**

**Total:** 2.7 + 2.7 + 2.7 + 2.7 + 7.2 = **18 L** exactly.

That 18 L batch yields roughly **61 cocktails** at the original recipe size.

### What *is* common today
- **Autopilot**: Holds a compass heading or GPS route. Standard on yachts and ships.
- **Advanced navigation aids**: Radar, AIS, GPS, sonar, collision-avoidance alerts.
- **Dynamic positioning (DP)**: Commercial vessels can hold position automatically using thrusters.

➡️ These reduce workload but **do not replace a human operator**.

### What’s emerging (limited, niche)
- **Autonomous ferries**: Short, fixed routes (e.g., test programs in Scandinavia).
- **Uncrewed commercial ships**: Cargo vessels in trials, usually remote-monitored.
- **Military drones**: Unmanned surface vessels with high autonomy.

### What does *not* exist (for normal use)
- A consumer boat you can buy today that:
  - Launches
  - Navigates open water
  - Avoids traffic/weather
  - Docks
  - Handles emergencies  
  **entirely on its own**

### Bottom line
- **Cars > boats** in autonomy progress.
- Water is less structured, rules are looser, edge cases are worse.
- For now: **automation assists humans, it doesn’t replace them**.

## User

I’m infusing mezcal based cocktail batches with fresh jaleeno overnight. How to avoid getting seeds in the drink

---

## Assistant

To avoid getting seeds in your mezcal-based cocktail batches when infusing with fresh jalapeños, follow these steps:

1. **Slice the Jalapeños Properly**: Cut the jalapeños in half lengthwise and use a spoon to scoop out the seeds and the membrane, which is the primary source of heat. This ensures the flavor is infused without excessive spiciness or seeds.

2. **Use a Fine Mesh Strainer**: After infusing, pour the mezcal through a fine mesh strainer to catch any remaining seeds or pulp.

3. **Cheesecloth or Coffee Filter**: For an extra layer of filtration, line the strainer with cheesecloth or a coffee filter before pouring. This will catch even the smallest particles.

4. **Proper Storage**: Store the infused mezcal in a clean container with a lid to prevent any seeds or other particles from contaminating it after straining.

These steps should help you achieve a smooth, seed-free infusion.

Do they make a cheesecloth bag I can use

---

## Assistant

Yes, cheesecloth bags are available and can be very useful for infusing liquids like mezcal. These bags are often referred to as nut milk bags or straining bags and come in various sizes. You can find them in kitchen supply stores or online. Here’s how to use one:

1. **Prepare the Jalapeños**: Slice the jalapeños and remove the seeds and membranes.

2. **Place in the Bag**: Put the sliced jalapeños into the cheesecloth bag.

3. **Infuse**: Submerge the bag in the mezcal and let it infuse overnight. The bag will keep the seeds and pulp contained while allowing the flavors to infuse.

4. **Remove and Strain**: After infusing, simply remove the bag. If desired, you can pour the mezcal through a fine mesh strainer to catch any small particles that might have escaped.

These bags are reusable and easy to clean, making them a convenient option for infusing cocktails.

## Assistant

Mr. White — this is a **systems problem**, not a motivation problem. You don’t need a rigid schedule; you need **measurable weekly output with frictionless tracking**.

Below is a **solo-founder–tested system** that works with fragmented days.

---

## 1. Set the Right Metric (Not “9–5”)

**Track two things only:**

### A) Weekly Deep-Work Hours (primary)
- Target: **30–40 hrs/week** of *focused* Joby work  
- “Deep” = shipping, coding, designing, selling, writing, or decision-making  
- **Exclude**: errands, waiting, passive thinking, Slack doomscrolling

> Why: Output correlates with *focused* hours, not total awake time.

### B) Weekly Ship Count (secondary)
- Minimum: **5 concrete outputs/week**
  - example: shipped feature, user interview, landing page iteration, investor email batch, experiment result

If hours are high but ships are low → you’re stuck.
If ships are high but hours are low → you’re efficient.

---

## 2. Use Time Blocks, Not a Fixed Schedule

Your days are fragmented. Accept it.

**Rule:** Any block ≥25 minutes counts.

### Block Types
- **25 min** = light focus
- **50 min** = standard deep work
- **90 min** = elite block (gold)

Your day might look chaotic, but **blocks stack cleanly**.

> 6 × 50-min blocks = a great day  
> 3 blocks = still a win

---

## 3. Ultra-Simple Tracking System (No Overhead)

### Tool Stack (pick ONE):
- **Toggl Track** (best)
- **Clockify**
- **Notion table + timer**
- **Apple Notes + stopwatch** (bare minimum)

### Setup (5 minutes)
- Project: **Joby**
- Tags (only 3):
  - `Build`
  - `Sell`
  - `Think`

That’s it. No categories beyond that.

---

## 4. Weekly Targets (Non-Negotiable)

### Hard Floor (minimum viable week)
- **25 deep-work hours**
- **3 shipped outputs**

### Standard Week
- **35 deep-work hours**
- **5 shipped outputs**

### Stretch Week
- **45 deep-work hours**
- **7+ shipped outputs**

If you miss the floor **two weeks in a row**, something structural is wrong — not your discipline.

---

## 5. Daily Rule That Keeps You Sane

**Never end a day at zero.**

Even on chaotic days:
- 1 × 25-minute block = ✔️ day counted
- Log it immediately

This prevents the psychological death spiral.

---

## 6. Weekly Founder Review (15 minutes, Sunday)

Answer **only these**:

1. Hours logged?
2. Ships completed?
3. What ate time that didn’t matter?
4. One constraint to remove next week?

Write it. Don’t think it.

---

## 7. Key Insight (Important)

Trying to force a fixed schedule **will fail** with your life constraints.

**Consistency comes from:**
- A weekly floor
- Visible numbers
- Zero-friction logging
- Output validation

Not from calendar perfection.

## Assistant

Yes — there **are apps that can automatically log when you’re working based on what you actually do on your device** (so you don’t have to manually hit start/stop all the time). Here are the realistic options:

### 🕒 Automatic or Background Tracking Tools

**1) RescueTime** *(very strong choice for solo founders)*  
- Runs in the background and **automatically tracks which apps and websites you use**.  
- Lets you set productivity goals and alerts based on categories (e.g., coding vs YouTube).  
- You get dashboards showing where your time actually goes.  
- Works across most devices (Mac, Windows, Linux, Android, iOS/Screen Time style). citeturn0news36  
👉 Good for *automatic visibility into real work vs distraction time.*

**2) Timing (Mac only)**  
- Tracks **every app, document, and website** you use in the background.  
- You can later assign that activity to categories/projects.  
- Great if you’re on macOS. citeturn0search3  
👉 Best for *automatic logs when you forget to start a timer.*

**3) Timely**  
- AI-backed automatic time tracking — learns and fills in work sessions.  
- Integrates with calendars and project tasks. citeturn0search5  
👉 Looks automatic, but often more useful for teams.

**4) ActivityWatch (open source)**  
- Runs locally and **auto-tracks activity by app/website**.  
- Works cross-platform and respects privacy. citeturn0search14  
👉 Best if you want full control and no cloud lock-in.

**5) Toggl Track with Desktop Auto Features**  
- **Has automatic tracking support** via desktop apps that record app/web activity which you can convert into time entries.  
- Calendar integrations can auto-create time entries from events. citeturn0search2turn0search9  
👉 Not fully autonomous like RescueTime, but it can reduce manual effort.

**6) Clockify**  
- Has an **auto tracker** feature that can suggest entries based on activity and patterns. citeturn0search10

| Tool | Auto-tracks activity in background | Assigns to projects | Cross-platform | Best for |

| **RescueTime** | ✔️ | Medium | ✔️ | Seeing real work vs distractions |

### 📌 Tip for Founders

**Best combo for solo work:**  
- Run **RescueTime or ActivityWatch** in background to *automatically record real usage*.  
- Sync/convert that into **Toggl Track or Clockify** entries for your weekly reporting and output metrics.

That way you get both **true activity data** and **meaningful project hours** without babysitting timers.

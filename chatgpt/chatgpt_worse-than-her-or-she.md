## Assistant

Use **“worse than she”** in formal/grammar-test contexts, because it’s short for **“worse than she is/was.”**

Use **“worse than her”** in everyday speech, because many people treat **“than”** like a preposition (so it takes an object pronoun).

Examples:
- Formal: *He’s worse at math than **she** (is).*  
- Casual: *He’s worse at math than **her**.*

If your sentence could be misread (e.g., *“I like him worse than her”*), rewrite:
- *I like him less than she does.*  
- *I like him less than I like her.*

## Assistant

Yes, it is generally legal for gas stations to charge for air in California, although there are certain conditions. According to California law, specifically California Health and Safety Code Section 13651, a gas station that charges for gasoline or diesel fuel must provide, during business hours, water, compressed air, and a gauge for measuring air pressure, to its customers free of charge for use in servicing any vehicle that has purchased gasoline or diesel fuel from the station.

However, this law does not prevent gas stations from installing coin-operated air vending machines for use by individuals who have not purchased gasoline or diesel fuel. So, if you purchase fuel, the air should be provided at no cost, but if you do not purchase fuel, they can charge for air.

You may want to check with local regulations or contact local authorities if you have concerns about specific practices at a gas station in your area.

## User

Yo diggity dog, how long can a cake that's been in the fridge be before you eat it, and it's not good?

---

## Assistant

Mr. White, safest rule: **eat refrigerated cake within 3–4 days** if it has **cream cheese frosting, whipped cream, custard, fresh fruit, mousse, cheesecake, or dairy-heavy filling**. USDA/FoodSafety.gov gives **3–4 days** as the general fridge limit for leftovers, and perishable foods should be tossed if left out over **2 hours**. citeturn847632search0turn847632search1

For a **plain cake or buttercream-frosted cake**, well-covered in the fridge, it may stay okay around **5–7 days**, but quality drops and conservative safety still points to **4 days** for anything moist/perishable. King Arthur Baking says plain cakes and buttercream cakes can last up to **1 week** refrigerated, while cream cheese/mascarpone cakes are **3–4 days**. citeturn847632search16

Throw it out immediately if you see **mold**, smell **sour/fermented funk**, the frosting looks **slimy/separated**, fruit is weepy or fizzy, or it sat warm for hours.

My practical answer:  
**Day 1–3:** almost always fine if refrigerated.  
**Day 4:** eat only if it smells/looks normal, especially if dairy/fruit.  
**Day 5+:** only plain/buttercream cake gets a maybe.  
**Day 7+:** toss it.

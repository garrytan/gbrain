---

## Assistant

The legalities of Mel's displaying a sign that says "open 24 hours" despite no longer being open 24 hours could involve issues related to false advertising and consumer protection laws. In California, the Business and Professions Code § 17500 prohibits false or misleading advertising. If the sign leads customers to believe the business is open 24 hours when it is not, this could be considered misleading.

However, enforcement typically requires a complaint or proof that the misleading sign caused harm or confusion to consumers. If customers are inconvenienced or misled, they may file complaints with local consumer protection agencies or the California Attorney General's office. Mel's could potentially face fines or be required to change the sign to reflect accurate hours of operation. 

Updating the sign would mitigate any potential legal risks and improve transparency with customers.

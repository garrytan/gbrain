## Assistant

A **conductor** is a material that allows electric charge (electric current) to flow through it easily with very little resistance. citeturn0search2turn0search0  

### Key idea
- Conductors contain **free electrons** that can move easily through the material when voltage is applied.  
- This movement of electrons produces **electric current**. citeturn0search16turn0search17  

### Common examples
Typical electrical conductors include:  
- Copper  
- Aluminum  
- Silver  
- Gold  
- Graphite  
- Saltwater and the human body (because they contain mobile ions) citeturn0search4  

### Why metals are good conductors
Metals have loosely bound outer electrons that can move freely between atoms. This “sea of electrons” allows electricity to pass through with low resistance. citeturn0search0turn0search24  

### Simple comparison
| Material type | Behavior with electricity |
|---|---|
| **Conductor** | Allows current to flow easily |
| **Insulator** | Blocks or strongly resists current |

Examples of **insulators**: rubber, plastic, glass, wood. citeturn0search15

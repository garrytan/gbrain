# Who batches cocktails

## User

Who batches cocktails at a bar?

---

## Assistant

Typically, either of these roles handles batching:

- **Bar Manager or Lead Bartender** – Oversees recipes, ensures consistency, and decides which cocktails are worth pre-batching based on volume and shelf life.  
- **Prep Bartender or Barback** – Does the actual measuring and mixing during off-hours, labeling and dating each batch.  
- **Sometimes the Head Bartender/Owner** – In small operations, they may handle batching personally to maintain quality control.

In practice, management sets the specs, but the day-to-day prep is usually done by the prep bartender or a barback under the manager’s direction.

---

## User

How do they get paid? Are they in the tip pool?

---

## Assistant

It depends on how the bar sets up its payroll, but common patterns are:

- **Prep bartenders/barbacks** – Usually hourly employees. If they also work service shifts that night, they share in the tip pool for those hours. If they only come in earlier to batch and leave before service, they’re typically *not* in the tip pool for that shift.

- **Lead bartenders or bar managers** – Often salaried or hourly with a higher base rate. If they also serve guests during service, they get their normal share of tips for those hours. Pure management or prep hours outside of service are usually just hourly wage with no tips.

So: batching during off-hours is almost always paid as regular hourly work; tip-pool eligibility kicks in only when they’re actually on the floor working a tipped service shift.

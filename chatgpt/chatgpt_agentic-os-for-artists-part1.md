# Current verdict

## What works

The site already has decent ingredients:

- Music-specific language.
- Product screenshots.
- Artist/team workflow language.
- Clear release automation story.
- Recognizable music distribution ecosystem names.
- Free profile wedge.

## What hurts you

### 1. The H1 sounds like distribution

**“Release more music with less work”** sounds like DistroKid, TuneCore, CD Baby, or a release admin tool. The subhead — **“Plan the drop, route every fan, and keep the next release moving”** — helps, but it still centers “the drop,” which narrows the company to release workflow. citeturn364715view0

Use:

**Grow your music catalog with AI agents**

or:

**AI agents that grow music catalogs**

### 2. The pricing kills premium perception

The homepage currently shows **Free, $39/mo, and $149/mo** tiers. That tells serious labels and investor visitors: “This is a low-ACV self-serve artist SaaS.” citeturn364715view0

That is directly at odds with your desired reaction: **“I’d pay $3,400 for this.”**

Move pricing off the homepage. Put the free artist profile on a separate page. Make the main CTA private access.

### 3. It overlaps visibly with known competitors

Feature.fm already markets artist bio links, release links, pre-save links, future save, tour/event links, fan-base management, and analytics. citeturn608415view0turn608415view1 SymphonyOS already markets label-facing automated music marketing, pre-saves, fan data, AI-powered tools, teams, and campaign automation. citeturn388109view0

So when Jovie leads with profiles, smart links, presaves, fan notifications, and release analytics, the immediate read is:

**“A nicer Feature.fm/SymphonyOS variant.”**

You need to force a different read:

**“This is the agentic operating layer for catalog growth.”**

### 4. “Built by an artist. For artists.” weakens the enterprise read

That line is emotionally true, but it risks the exact perception you named: a founder-artist tool rather than a serious company. The current page says **“Built by an artist. For artists.”** and includes a founder quote. citeturn662948view0

Replace it with domain authority:

**Built from 15 years of music marketing, release operations, and artist growth work.**

Then put founder story lower, or on About.

## 4. Product section: show outcomes, not features

Do not expose a detailed feature checklist. It leaks roadmap and pulls you into commodity comparison.

Use three outcome modules:

**Jovie reads the release context and creates the campaign plan your team can approve, assign, and run.**

- Objective

- Approval state
- Assigned owner

Avoid showing:

- Full agent chain
- Exact integrations
- Backend logic

### Route every fan to the next best action

**Give each fan the right path: listen, save, subscribe, buy, tip, RSVP, or come back for the next drop.**

Show UI:
- Fan path preview
- Destination logic
- High-level analytics

Avoid exposing:
- Segmentation rules
- Automation triggers
- Proprietary routing logic

### Keep catalogs working

**Jovie finds the next action across your catalog, so releases do not die after launch week.**

Show UI:
- Catalog dashboard
- “Opportunities”
- “Ready for review”
- “Needs approval”

Avoid showing:
- Exact scoring model
- Agent prompts
- revenue prediction details

## Suggested revisions

### 1. Combine the two `$700/mo` AI-tool drafts

```text
cancelled nearly every AI subscription this week.

cursor, claude code, codex, grok build.

~$700/mo as a solo founder.

the lesson wasn’t “AI tools are too expensive.”

the lesson was that tool sprawl is real, and at some point the cost is less about the invoice and more about context switching.
```

### 2. Manager / music-industry credibility post

```text
my manager saw i could do something his other artists couldn’t, so he hired me to do it for them.

that turned into 15 years of building manual workflows for artists across Sony, Universal, and Armada.

now i’m automating the same workflows with AI agents.

the job didn’t change as much as the tools did.
```

### 3. Context routing post

```text
context routing is the whole game.

most agent failures i’ve seen come down to showing the model too much or too little.

feels obvious, but almost nobody architects for it from day one.
```

### 4. DJ pricing post

```text
a DJ i know raised his booking fee from $5k to $25k.

bookings didn’t change.

everyone just paid 5x more.

sometimes the market was never rejecting the price.

you were.
```

### 5. Spotify verification post

```text
Spotify’s verification badge isn’t really asking “is this human?”

it’s asking “will this artist still be here tomorrow?”

consistent listeners. linked socials. merch. shows. real-world presence.

one is a biology test.

the other is a commitment test.
```

### 6. AI layoffs post

```text
the AI layoffs story is a capital allocation story dressed up as an innovation story.

companies aren’t only asking “who can AI replace?”

they’re asking “where should payroll dollars become compute dollars?”
```

### 7. Nutrition analogy post

```text
you would never take nutrition advice from someone who’s never stepped foot in a gym.

same rule for AI workflows.

don’t take agent advice from people who haven’t shipped anything with agents.
```


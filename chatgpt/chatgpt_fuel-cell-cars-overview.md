**Fuel‑cell cars in a nutshell (2025)**  

- **What they are** – Electric cars that make their own power on‑board. A hydrogen tank feeds a fuel‑cell stack; hydrogen atoms split into electrons (electricity for the motor) and protons that bond with oxygen to leave nothing but water vapor.citeturn0search0  

- **Key hardware**  
  1. Carbon‑fiber hydrogen tank (5 – 10 kg @ ~10 k psi)  
  2. PEM fuel‑cell stack + small lithium‑ion buffer battery  
  3. Normal EV motor & inverter  

- **Refueling & range** – Pumping gaseous H₂ takes ~5 min and delivers 300‑400 mi real‑world range; 1 kg H₂ ≈ the energy of 1 gal of gas. California stations currently charge **$13‑17 / kg** (~$0.21 / mi).citeturn0search3  

- **Where you can fill up** – 49 retail stations are open in California (8 are temporarily offline); the state *hopes* for ~200 by late‑2025 but is behind schedule. Outside CA the public network is almost nonexistent.citeturn0search6turn0search2  

- **Models you can actually buy/lease**  
  • **Toyota Mirai (2025 refresh)** – 402 mi EPA range, $51 k, 6‑yr/ $15 k free fuel card.  
  • **Hyundai Nexo** – 380 mi, $58 k, 3‑yr free fuel.  
  • **Honda CR‑V e:FCEV** – new for 2025; plug‑in 17.7 kWh battery gives ~29 mi on electricity + 270 mi on H₂.citeturn0search1turn0search5  

- **Pros**  
  • Quick “fill‑up”, long range, no tail‑pipe pollutants.  
  • Lighter than a long‑range battery pack, so steady performance in cold or hot weather.  

- **Cons**  
  • Thin fueling network (all but useless outside CA).  
  • Hydrogen fuel is pricey and mostly produced via fossil‑fuel reforming, so lifecycle CO₂ isn’t zero unless green H₂ is used.  
  • Fewer models, high vehicle cost, resale uncertain.  
  • Station downtime is common; an empty pump means a tow, not a Level‑2 plug.  

**Bottom line for LA:** If you live near one of the West L.A. or Hollywood stations and value 5‑minute refuels over widespread charging, a Mirai lease can be painless thanks to the free‑fuel perk. For everyone else, a battery EV is simpler and cheaper to run today.

## User

i used to tour as a dj playing big clubs and festivals globally. im looking to get back into it. i have a lot of original music on big labels like aramada and black hole recordings. whats steps should i take to get dj gigs

---

## Assistant

To get back into DJing at big clubs and festivals, especially with your experience and original music on labels like Armada and Black Hole Recordings, follow these steps:

1. **Update Your Portfolio**: Ensure your portfolio or press kit is current, including recent tracks, mixes, any new releases on significant labels, and a professional biography. Highlight your experience and past performances at notable venues and festivals.

2. **Engage with Your Audience Online**: Reactivate and update your social media profiles and music platforms (SoundCloud, Mixcloud, Spotify). Share new mixes, tracks, and updates about your music career to re-engage with your audience and attract new followers.

3. **Network**: Reconnect with industry contacts, such as event promoters, club owners, and other DJs. Networking can be done in person or through social media. Attend industry events, music conferences, and shows to meet new people.

4. **Promote Your Music**: Utilize music promotion services and platforms to get your tracks played by other DJs and on radio shows. Submit your music to playlists and consider collaborating with other artists to expand your reach.

5. **Send Demos**: Target clubs and festivals where you want to perform. Send them a well-crafted demo, including a mix of your best tracks and a cover letter highlighting your past achievements and what you can bring to their venue or event.

6. **Hire a Booking Agent**: If it's within your means, consider hiring a booking agent. An agent with good industry connections can significantly boost your chances of securing gigs.

7. **Stay Updated on Trends**: Music and club scenes evolve. Stay updated on the latest trends in music, technology, and what audiences are looking for to ensure your sets are fresh and appealing.

8. **Be Prepared to Start Small**: If you've been out of the scene for a while, be open to accepting smaller gigs to rebuild your reputation and prove your skills to both new and former fans.

9. **Maintain Professionalism**: Always be professional in your interactions, punctual for your sets, and prepared for each performance. Word of mouth and reputation in the industry are invaluable.

10. **Consistency**: Consistently produce and release new music. Keeping your name out there with fresh material is key to staying relevant in the industry.

Re-establishing yourself in the DJ scene, especially after a break, requires dedication, patience, and strategic planning. With your background and original music on notable labels, you have a solid foundation to build upon.

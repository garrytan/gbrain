## 4. Cabin air filter – can you DIY from Amazon?

100% yes, this is **prime DIY** stuff.

**Mazda’s general guidance:** replace cabin air filter about **every 2 years**.citeturn0search5turn0search23turn0search25

**Typical costs:**

- Amazon: about **$10–$25** for a cabin filter (OEM or good aftermarket).  
- Shops often charge **$50–$90** for the same job.

**How to do it on a CX-5 (no tools, ~5 minutes):**

1. Empty the glovebox.  
2. Open glovebox and unhook the little side damper arm on the right.  
3. Squeeze the sides of the glovebox inward so it drops down farther.  
4. You’ll see a rectangular black cover behind it – pop that cover off.  
5. Slide old cabin filter out (note airflow direction arrow).  
6. Slide new one in with arrow same direction as old.  
7. Put cover back, pop glovebox back into place, reattach damper.

Totally realistic for you to do yourself.

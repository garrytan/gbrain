## Assistant

Mr. White,

- **Fat loss is driven by sustained energy deficit** (calories in < calories out over time), not by whether training is fasted or fed.  
- **Fasted training increases fat oxidation during the workout**, but when **total calories and protein are matched**, **fat loss over weeks is the same** between fasted and fed training.  
- **Low insulin does not cause greater fat loss by itself.** Insulin rises after meals and falls between meals; **net fat loss depends on the 24–72 hour balance** of energy intake vs expenditure and stored fuel use.  
- After eating (higher insulin), the body can store more fat **and also burn less fat acutely**; later (lower insulin), it can **mobilize and burn more fat**. Over time, **the net result is governed by energy balance**.  
- **Walking 30–60 minutes can increase daily energy expenditure** and help create/maintain a deficit; this works **whether fasted or fed**.  
- For **resistance training performance**, many people perform **better fed** (higher training quality/volume), which can support **muscle retention** during dieting.  
- For getting lean while keeping muscle, the high-impact facts are: **adequate protein**, **hard resistance training**, **sufficient sleep**, and a **manageable calorie deficit**.  
- **Meal timing (e.g., first meal at 12pm)** is a behavioral tool that can help adherence for some people; it is **not inherently superior** for fat loss if calories/protein are unchanged.

# Visual rules

## Chat bubble system

**Tim bubbles:** black background, white text.  
**Jovie bubbles:** white background, black text.  
**Investor bubbles:** gray background, black text.

The bubbles should feel like part of the city’s surveillance layer. They appear on glass, pavement, phone screens, venue walls, and negative space. They never feel cute. They feel tactical.

## Jovie’s human reveal

Her entrance must feel like status, not fan service.

She should appear near the transition from “AI assistant” to “operator in the world.” Motorcycle. Matte black helmet. Grayscale wardrobe. Blonde hair, blue eyes, pink lips, neutral background, shallow depth of field. No logos. No busy colors. No influencer framing.

## Color

Almost everything is grayscale, charcoal, steel, white, black, and sodium-vapor city light.

Subtle cyberpunk appears only through:

- screen glow,
- QR scans,
- blue-white UI reflections,
- glass,
- motion blur,
- street LEDs,
- data overlays.

No purple neon cliché.

# Best investor cut

For the investor version, make the first 30 seconds impossible to misunderstand:

**Problem:** artists lose fans.

**Wedge:** link-in-bio and smart links.  
**Expansion:** release workflow automation.  
**Why Tim:** artist + marketer + developer + founder.  
**Why now:** creation is easier; release execution becomes more valuable.

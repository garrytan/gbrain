Here’s a concise, Chris-Voss-style text you can send that frames the value, sets the floor, and invites him to anchor higher:

---

**Draft text:**  
“Hey man—got the drives pulled and ready: six 10 TB Seagate IronWolf NAS and two 14 TB IronWolf. All lightly used, bought new, perfect for a build. eBay comps are ~$100 each for the 10s and ~$140 each for the 14s, so you’re looking at $900+ retail.  

If you can swing **$500 cash today**, they’re yours as a package and I’ll box them for you. Saves you the shipping fees and bidding hassle, and you can test them here. Fair enough?”

---

**Why it works**

* **Label & anchor:** You state the market price first ($900+), so $500 feels like a deal.  
* **Clear deadline:** “cash today” creates urgency.  
* **Fair framing:** “saves shipping/bidding hassle” makes the discount logical, not desperate.  
* **Soft close:** “Fair enough?” invites a yes or a counter without pressure.

If he balks, mirror and stay calm:  
*“Sounds like you had a different number in mind?”*  
Then silence—let him fill it.  

This keeps the relationship friendly, sets expectations before he drives over, and positions $500 as a bargain instead of a starting point.

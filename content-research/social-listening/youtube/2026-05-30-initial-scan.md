# YouTube Social Listening Report — Initial Scan

## Research Scope
Analysis of popular music production and music career content on YouTube. Sources include major channels (Andrew Huang, In The Mix, Internet Money, Ricky Ticky, ADSR, Producer Grind, This Song Is Sick, Signal, etc.) and YouTube search trends related to music production, music careers, and music business.

---

## Theme 1: "How to Start" Content (Massive Search Volume)

### Signal 1.1 — "How to produce music for beginners" (millions of cumulative views)
- **Summary**: The most-watched music production content on YouTube. "Start from zero" tutorials that overwhelm beginners with DAW choices, plugin options, and workflow decisions. Comment sections filled with "this is too complicated" and "where do I actually start?" reactions. Many creators make these videos for SEO rather than genuine education.
- **Demand proxy**: Top videos get 500K–5M+ views. Comments often 2K–10K. Search volume for "how to produce music" is consistently 100K+/month globally.
- **Sentiment**: Curious, enthusiastic to start, but quickly overwhelmed and lost. Many commenters watch multiple beginner videos without actually producing.
- **Artist type**: Complete beginners / hobbyists with no production experience.
- **Potential Jovie angle**: Jovie IS the answer to "how do I start?" — simplified creation that removes the DAW/plugin/technical barrier. This is the single biggest funnel opportunity. Content targeting "make your first song" without technical knowledge requirements.

### Signal 1.2 — "What DAW should I buy?" (the eternal question)
- **Summary**: DAW comparison videos generate millions of views and never-ending comment wars. Ableton vs FL Studio vs Logic vs BandLab vs GarageBand debates. Price concerns, genre-specific recommendations, and "just pick one" exasperation from experienced producers in comments.
- **Demand proxy**: 100K–2M+ views per comparison video. Some DAW channels grew to hundreds of thousands of subscribers on comparison content alone.
- **Sentiment**: Confused, seeking validation, tribal loyalty to their chosen DAW.
- **Artist type**: Beginners deciding their first tool.
- **Potential Jovie angle**: "Skip the DAW argument — start making music today." Position Jovie as DAW-agnostic or as a better starting point that eliminates the decision paralysis.

## Theme 2: Mixing & Mastering (The Skill Gap)

### Signal 2.1 — "How to mix vocals / make vocals sound professional"
- **Summary**: Consistently one of the most searched topics in music production on YouTube. Beginners and intermediates struggling with leveling, EQ, compression, reverb, and de-essing. Before/after comparison videos get particularly high engagement.
- **Demand proxy**: Top videos get 500K–3M+ views. "How to mix vocals" is a top-10 music production search term on YouTube.
- **Sentiment**: Desperate ("I've been trying for months and they still sound amateur"), grateful when a tutorial clicks.
- **Artist type**: Hobbyists and singer-songwriters who primarily work with vocals.
- **Potential Jovie angle**: AI-assisted vocal processing within the platform. One-click vocal enhancement. This is a feature-level opportunity.

### Signal 2.2 — "Is LANDR mastering good enough?"
- **Summary**: Reviews and comparisons of AI mastering services (LANDR, CloudBounce, eMastered, etc.) vs. human mastering. Consensus shifts over time — early skepticism is now more nuanced. Many creators test the same track through multiple services.
- **Demand proxy**: 200K–1M+ views per review video. High comment engagement from people asking about specific genres.
- **Sentiment**: Skeptical → Pragmatic. "Is it perfect? No. Is it good enough to release? Mostly yes."
- **Artist type**: Indies without budgets for $100+/track mastering engineers.
- **Potential Jovie angle**: Built-in mastering that competes with LANDR quality. "Professional-sounding masters, instant."

## Theme 3: Music Career "How-To" Content

### Signal 3.1 — "How to make money with music in 2026"
- **Summary**: Career strategy videos exploring streaming income, sync licensing, beat selling, Patreon, teaching, performing live, NFTs (less relevant now), YouTube monetization, and brand deals. "Diversify your income" is the universal takeaway. Commenters ask "but how do I actually start?"
- **Demand proxy**: 100K–500K views on strategy videos. Audience growth for music business content is strong (20–40% YoY for leading creators).
- **Sentiment**: Motivated but frustrated. Information overload. "Everyone talks about diversifying but nobody shows you how."
- **Artist type**: Early-career professionals trying to go full-time or semi-professional.
- **Potential Jovie angle**: "Make money from your music" content ecosystem. Jovie as the platform that connects creation to income seamlessly. Monetization features built into the product.

### Signal 3.2 — "How to get on Spotify editorial playlists"
- **Summary**: Explainer videos, strategy guides, and reality checks about playlist pitching. Experienced distributors and playlist curators expose the process and set realistic expectations. Many scam-warning videos about playlist promotion services.
- **Demand proxy**: 50K–500K views. High comment engagement with questions.
- **Sentiment**: Hopeful → Disappointed (when they learn the harsh realities).
- **Artist type**: Independent artists pushing their first or second release.
- **Potential Jovie angle**: Pre-save campaign tools, playlist pitch strategy guides, realistic expectation-setting content. Don't overplay the editorial playlist angle — teach broader discovery.

## Theme 4: AI Music Disruption

### Signal 4.1 — "I made a hit song with AI" (and the backlash)
- **Summary**: Viral videos from creators using Suno, Udio, or similar tools to generate complete songs. Some get millions of views; comment sections are warfare. "This is the future" vs "this is theft/fake/defeating the purpose." Music production YouTubers increasingly required to address AI in their content.
- **Demand proxy**: AI music videos routinely hit 1M–10M+ views. Comment sections: 5K–50K comments.
- **Sentiment**: Divided → Polarized. Fear, excitement, anger, bewilderment.
- **Artist type**: All types — creators, professionals, hobbyists — everyone has a strong opinion.
- **Potential Jovie angle**: Critical moment in the conversation. Jovie needs to clearly articulate where it sits on the AI spectrum. If Jovie uses AI as a creative tool, lean into "AI-assisted human creation" messaging. If positioned against pure AI music, lean into authenticity and human expression.

### Signal 4.2 — "AI is coming for your music career"
- **Summary**: Fear-mongering/awareness videos about AI music proliferation. "The industry will be flooded with AI slop." "Real musicians need to differentiate." Pro-creativity counter-content fighting back.
- **Demand proxy**: 100K–1M+ views. High sharing rate as both sides amplify.
- **Sentiment**: Fearful, defiant, activist.
- **Artist type**: Professionals defensive about their craft.
- **Potential Jovie angle**: "Human creativity, amplified" positioning. Not AI-replacement, but AI-as-amplifier. Help creators be MORE human, not less.

## Theme 5: Gear & Tools Reviews

### Signal 5.1 — "[Product Name] review" content ecosystem
- **Summary**: Music production YouTube is heavily driven by gear and plugin reviews. Synth reviews (Serum, Vital, Pigments), microphone reviews, audio interface comparisons. Affiliate-driven content dominates. Viewers trust these channels for purchase decisions. Reviewer credibility is currency.
- **Demand proxy**: Individual reviews get 50K–500K views. Review channels range from 100K–2M+ subscribers.
- **Sentiment**: Curious, consumerist, trust-seeking.
- **Artist type**: All — everyone gear-shops.
- **Potential Jovie angle**: Integration partnerships. "Works with your favorite tools." Or: "Everything you need, no additional gear required."

## Key YouTube Takeaways for Jovie

1. **Biggest funnel opportunity**: Beginner "how to make music" content. This is the top-of-funnel that captures people at their moment of highest intent and lowest commitment.
2. **Most underserved audience**: People who want to make music but can't/won't learn a traditional DAW. Every "beginner tutorial" on YouTube still teaches Ableton or FL Studio — the barrier to entry remains high.
3. **Most controversial topic**: AI music. Every creator must have a take. This conversation will define the next 2 years of the industry.
4. **Most reliable content format**: Before/after mixing/mastering, honest tool comparisons, income transparency.
5. **Jovie's YouTube strategy**: Target "make your first song" searches, "easier than [DAW]" comparisons, "make money from music" career content. The product eliminates the biggest pain points that every tutorial tries to solve.

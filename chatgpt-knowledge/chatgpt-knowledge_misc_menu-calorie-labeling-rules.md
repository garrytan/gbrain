## Question

Hey, how's it going? What are the rules about whether or not you have to put calorie numbers on a menu? When does that come into play?

## Answer

In the United States, the requirement to display calorie counts on menus comes from the Affordable Care Act. Here's a summary of the key rules:

1. **Who Must Comply**: Chain restaurants and similar retail food establishments with 20 or more locations doing business under the same name and offering for sale substantially the same menu items must display calorie information.

2. **What Must Be Displayed**: Calories must be listed next to the name or price of the menu item on menus and menu boards, including drive-through displays. This applies to standard menu items.

3. **Additional Nutritional Information**: Establishments must also provide additional written nutrition information upon request. This includes details like total calories, calories from fat, total fat, saturated fat, trans fat, cholesterol, sodium, total carbohydrates, dietary fiber, sugars, and protein.

4. **Where It Applies**: This requirement is for any standard menu item offered for sale. It does not typically include items that are not standard menu items, such as daily specials, custom orders, or temporary menu items offered for less than 60 days per calendar year.

5. **Self-Service and Buffet**: For self-service items and foods on display (like at a buffet), calorie information must be posted nearby on a sign or similar device.

These rules aim to help consumers make more informed choices about their food consumption.

## Question

Dialog cafe required?

## Answer

Dialog Cafe is not required to display calorie counts on their menu. The calorie labeling regulations primarily target chain restaurants and similar establishments with 20 or more locations. Dialog Cafe, being an independent, family-run cafe, does not fall under these requirements. 

The regulations mandate that large chains must provide calorie information on menus, menu boards, and online, along with an intake statement indicating that adults need around 2,000 kcal a day. These rules aim to help consumers make more informed dietary choices by displaying the calorie content prominently at the point of decision-making【9†source】【12†source】. 

For smaller, independent cafes like Dialog Cafe, these regulations do not apply, and therefore, they are not legally obligated to display calorie information.

## Question

Kcal vs calorie

## Answer

The terms "kcal" and "calorie" are often used interchangeably in everyday contexts, but they refer to different measures.

1. **Calorie (cal)**: A calorie is a unit of energy. In scientific terms, one calorie is the amount of energy required to raise the temperature of one gram of water by one degree Celsius.

2. **Kilocalorie (kcal)**: A kilocalorie, also known as a large calorie or food calorie, is equal to 1,000 small calories. In dietary contexts, "calorie" usually refers to a kilocalorie. So, when you see "calories" on food packaging or nutritional information, it actually refers to kilocalories. For example, when a food item lists "200 calories," it means 200 kilocalories (200,000 small calories).

In summary:
- 1 kilocalorie (kcal) = 1,000 calories (cal)
- When referring to food and diet, "calorie" typically means kilocalorie (kcal).

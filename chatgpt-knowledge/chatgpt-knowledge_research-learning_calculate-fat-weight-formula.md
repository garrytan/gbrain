## Question

how to calculate fat weight given bf percentage and weight

## Answer

To calculate fat weight from body fat percentage and total weight, use the following formula:

1. Convert the body fat percentage to a decimal by dividing by 100.
2. Multiply this decimal by the total body weight.

For example, if someone weighs 200 pounds and has a body fat percentage of 15%, the calculation would be:

1. 15% / 100 = 0.15
2. 0.15 × 200 pounds = 30 pounds of fat.

## Question

lbm

## Answer

To calculate Lean Body Mass (LBM) given body fat percentage and total weight, follow these steps:

1. Calculate fat weight using the method I previously described.
2. Subtract the fat weight from the total body weight.

Using the previous example where someone weighs 200 pounds with a body fat percentage of 15%:

1. Fat weight = 0.15 × 200 pounds = 30 pounds.
2. LBM = Total weight - Fat weight = 200 pounds - 30 pounds = 170 pounds.

So, the Lean Body Mass would be 170 pounds.

## Question

## Answer

The Fat-Free Mass Index (FFMI) is a measurement used to quantify muscle mass. It's calculated using lean body mass (LBM), height, and a correction factor for height. The formula for FFMI is:

\[ \text{FFMI} = \frac{\text{LBM (kg)}}{\text{height (m)}^2} + 6.1 \times (1.8 - \text{height (m)}) \]

To calculate FFMI:

1. Convert the LBM to kilograms (if it's in pounds, divide by 2.20462).
2. Convert height to meters (if it's in inches, multiply by 0.0254).
3. Apply the formula.

For example, if a person has an LBM of 170 pounds (77.11 kg) and a height of 6'4” (1.93 meters), the calculation would be:

1. LBM in kg = 170 / 2.20462 = 77.11 kg
2. Height in meters = 6'4” = 76 inches × 0.0254 = 1.93 meters
3. FFMI = 77.11 / 1.93² + 6.1 × (1.8 - 1.93)

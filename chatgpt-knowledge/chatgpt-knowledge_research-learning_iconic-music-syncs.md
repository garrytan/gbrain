## Question

What are the biggest syncs in the history of tv and cinema

## Answer

Some of the biggest and most iconic sync deals in TV and cinema history include:

1. **The Beatles on 'The Ed Sullivan Show' (1964)** - Although not a sync in the traditional sense of licensing music for a film or TV, the Beatles' live performance on 'The Ed Sullivan Show' marked a monumental point in TV history, significantly influencing music and popular culture.

2. **Queen's "Bohemian Rhapsody" in 'Wayne's World' (1992)** - This scene significantly boosted the song's popularity years after its original release, demonstrating the power of sync in reviving interest in music tracks.

3. **The Bee Gees and 'Saturday Night Fever' (1977)** - The film's soundtrack, dominated by the Bee Gees, played a huge role in popularizing disco music across America and the world, creating a lasting impact on the music and film industry.

4. **Simon & Garfunkel in 'The Graduate' (1967)** - Songs like "The Sound of Silence" and "Mrs. Robinson" are integral to the film's atmosphere and narrative, showcasing the effective use of music to enhance cinematic storytelling.

5. **Led Zeppelin's "Immigrant Song" in 'Thor: Ragnarok' (2017)** - The use of this iconic song not only boosted the film's energy but also helped to redefine the character of Thor within the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

These syncs are notable not just for their immediate impact on viewers but also for their lasting influence on the usage of music in visual media.

## Question

What about smelly cat?

## Answer

"Smelly Cat" is a humorous and iconic song from the TV show "Friends," performed by the character Phoebe Buffay, played by Lisa Kudrow. Although it's not a traditional sync deal involving licensing pre-existing music, "Smelly Cat" became one of the most memorable musical moments in TV history. The song is repeatedly referenced throughout the series, enhancing its comedic value and deepening the quirky character of Phoebe. This internal use of a comedic song is a great example of how music can be tailored specifically to fit the tone and context of a TV show, contributing significantly to its cultural impact.

## Question

Who wrote it

## Answer

"Smelly Cat" was written by Adam Chase, Betsy Borns, and Chrissy Hynde. Chrissy Hynde, the lead singer of The Pretenders, even appeared in an episode of "Friends" to perform the song alongside Phoebe. This collaboration highlighted how the song became not only a recurring humorous element within the show but also a beloved cultural reference among fans.

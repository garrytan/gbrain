## Step 1: Quiet rights audit before posting audio

Before posting even snippets, pull the actual documents:

| Document | What to check |
|---|---|
| Armada agreement | Term, exclusivity, release obligation, reversion, notice/cure, confidentiality, approval rights |
| Split sheets | Writer shares, publisher shares, producer share |
| Master ownership docs | Whether you assigned, licensed, or retained the masters |
| Advance paperwork | Whether the advance was recoupable and tied to delivery/release |
| Vocal/session releases | Engineer, BGVs, performers, stems, samples |
| Correspondence | Any emails showing Armada accepted/finalized the records |

Key question: **Did Armada have an obligation to release, or merely an option/right to release?**

## How it spreads without you

### 1. Use an intermediary

Have one of these act as the source:

- A music PR person
- A former manager
- A trusted EDM blogger
- A fan-page operator
- A trance Discord/moderator
- A YouTube dance-music historian
- A TikTok “lost media” creator
- A Reddit user with history in trance/EDM communities

They should be real people/accounts. Avoid fake insider accounts.

### 2. Give them a “tip pack”

The pack should include:

- The three song titles
- The Ørjan Nilsen / Armada connection
- The rough year
- A short factual timeline
- Blurred proof: session screenshot, email subject line, file name, contract heading, metadata
- No full audio
- No unredacted contracts
- No private emails
- No accusation of wrongdoing

The angle:

> “This looks like one of the better unreleased Armada-era mysteries. Can anyone confirm why these never came out?”

### 3. Make the ask public but indirect

The posts should ask questions, not make demands.

Examples:

> “Does anyone remember these unreleased Ørjan Nilsen / Armada IDs?”

> “Apparently Armada signed three Ørjan Nilsen vocal records around a decade ago and they never dropped. Titles: Dancing on My Heart, Bittersweet, Show Me Love. Anyone know the story?”

> “Lost trance media question: were these ever played live, or did they disappear inside the label system?”

That gets fans tagging Ørjan and Armada organically.

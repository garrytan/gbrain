## Question

How much can I add during an 8 week bulk with chest prioritization based on renascence periodization hypertrophic training. I’d train 6 days a week and eat in a surplus to add 1 pound per week at a 1:1 fat to muscle ratio

## Answer

According to Renaissance Periodization (RP) and principles outlined by Dr. Mike Israetel, significant muscle growth requires time and cannot be rushed without risking excess fat gain. In an 8-week bulk, training 6 days a week, you can expect to add muscle, but the exact amount can vary widely based on individual factors like genetics, training experience, and how close you are to your genetic potential.

Dr. Israetel suggests that a realistic rate of muscle gain for most individuals is about 0.25 to 0.5 pounds per week, though this can be lower for advanced lifters. Given that you're aiming for a 1:1 fat to muscle gain ratio, if you gain 1 pound per week, potentially half of that could be muscle under optimal conditions.

Therefore, over 8 weeks, you might expect to gain 4 pounds of muscle in the best scenario. However, not all of this muscle gain will be localized to the chest, as it will be distributed across all the muscles worked during your training.

Adding significant size to the chest in terms of inches will take longer than 8 weeks. It's crucial to have realistic expectations and focus on progressive overload, recovery, and nutrition over time to see substantial changes in muscle size.

## Question

How long would it take to add 8 inches to my chest

## Answer

Adding 8 inches to the chest is a significant and ambitious goal. Muscle growth rates vary by individual, but generally, a natural lifter might expect to gain approximately 0.25 to 0.5 pounds of muscle per week under optimal conditions, as mentioned earlier. However, this growth is distributed throughout the body, not just the chest.

If we assume that you can gain muscle at the upper end of this range and that you train your chest optimally, it could still take several years to add 8 inches to your chest. This is because such a large increase in chest size would not only require substantial muscle hypertrophy but also an increase in the surrounding tissues and skin.

Moreover, there are genetic limits to how much muscle you can add to your frame naturally. Most people will see the most significant gains in their first few years of training, with the rate of muscle growth slowing down considerably as they become more advanced.

It's essential to set realistic and incremental goals, focusing on progressive overload, proper nutrition, and adequate recovery to ensure consistent muscle growth over time. Consulting with a fitness professional, such as an RP coach, could provide a more personalized and realistic timeline based on your individual circumstances.

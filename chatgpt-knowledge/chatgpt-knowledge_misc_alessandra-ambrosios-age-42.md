## Question

Why was McLaren airport renamed

## Answer

McCarran International Airport in Las Vegas was renamed Harry Reid International Airport to honor former U.S. Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid, a key political figure in Nevada. The decision also involved removing the name of U.S. Sen. Pat McCarran, who, despite being an aviation proponent, was criticized for being a racist and antisemite. The renaming, which cost about $7 million covered by private contributions, acknowledges Reid's significant contributions to Nevada's aviation industry and his role in promoting tourism, the state's primary industry【18†source】.

## Question

I just saw a job listing for a service bartender. What is a service bartender and how is that different from a normal bartender?

## Answer

A service bartender primarily focuses on preparing drinks for the service staff to deliver to customers, usually in a restaurant setting. This role typically doesn't involve direct interaction with customers. They work behind the scenes, making drinks for waitstaff to serve to restaurant guests.

In contrast, a regular bartender, often found in bars or the bar area of a restaurant, interacts directly with customers. They not only prepare drinks but also engage with guests, take orders directly from them, and sometimes entertain or converse with patrons.

So, the main difference lies in customer interaction. A service bartender works more 'backstage', focusing on drink preparation for the waitstaff, while a regular bartender is more 'frontstage', interacting directly with customers.

## Question

So, would a service bartender be in the back of the house, or would they be visible to patrons?

## Answer

A service bartender is typically part of the back-of-house operations in a restaurant. They usually work in an area not visible to patrons, focusing on efficiently preparing drinks for the waitstaff to serve to customers. This setup helps streamline the process, especially in larger or busier establishments, allowing the front-of-house bartenders to focus on direct customer service and interaction.

## Question

How can a service bartender be tipped, or do they get tipped at all?

## Answer

Service bartenders can receive tips, but it's handled differently than with regular bartenders. In many establishments, they share in the tips that the waitstaff receive. This is often managed through a tip pooling system, where a portion of the tips collected by the service staff (waiters and waitresses) is shared with the back-of-house staff, including the service bartender.

In some cases, service bartenders might also receive a higher hourly wage to compensate for the less direct access to tips compared to front-of-house staff. The exact method of tipping can vary depending on the restaurant's policies and the tipping culture of the area.

## Answer

Mr. White, here’s the leanest way forward:

1. Cover costs with part-time income  
   • You need ~$100 K ARR to fund a $70 K salary (~8.3 K MRR).  
   • At $9.99/mo that’s ~830 paid users; at $99/yr it’s ~1,010.  
   • Land a 10–15 hr/week consulting or side gig to bridge rent + basics.  

2. Ship MVP fast and validate  
   • In the next 1–2 weeks, push your crude TestFlight build to 10–20 friends.  
   • Measure engagement (DAU, retention) and willingness to pay.  
   • Iterate only on the features that drive those metrics.  

3. Monetize before you fundraise  
   • Convert alpha testers into paid subscribers or pre-sell annual plans.  
   • Once you hit ~200–300 users (2–3 K MRR), you’ll have real traction.  
   • With that data, you can either go full-time or raise a small seed ($50–100 K) on better terms.  

**Why not raise now?**  
Raising pre-traction dilutes equity and distracts you. Investors want real usage and revenue—both of which you can build faster by shipping and selling.

**Bottom line:**  
Keep a lean side-income, launch your MVP ASAP, prove 5–10 K MRR, then decide: full-time bootstrap or small raise. That path gets you to paying yourself without burning cash or chasing capital prematurely.

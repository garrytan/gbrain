## Question

Got a 2020 Harley touring bike, and it looks like the battery on it is dead, it's not starting or even going into accessory mode. What are my options here?

## Answer

Mr. White, here are your options for a 2020 Harley Touring bike with a dead battery (no start, no accessory mode):  

### 1. Basic Checks  
- Make sure the kill switch is in the "Run" position.  
- Check that the battery terminals are clean and tightly connected. A loose connection can mimic a dead battery.  

### 2. Jump Start  
- Use jumper cables and a car battery **(car must be OFF, otherwise risk frying electronics)**.  
- Or use a portable jump starter rated for motorcycles.  

### 3. Battery Tender / Charger  
- If the battery isn’t completely dead, hook it to a **smart motorcycle charger** (Battery Tender, NOCO, etc.) and let it charge slowly overnight.  

### 4. Battery Replacement  
- Touring bikes use a **12V AGM battery** (commonly YTX20HL-BS). If yours won’t hold a charge after charging, it needs to be replaced.  
- Pull the seat, remove side covers, and access the battery tray. Swap it out—it’s a 15–30 min DIY job.  

### 5. Dealer / Shop  
- If you’re not set up to do it, you can roll it to a Harley dealer or independent shop. They’ll test, charge, or replace it.  

👉 If the bike has been sitting, the battery is probably sulfated beyond recovery—replacement is usually the fix.

## Question

Okay, um, and I just want to get an odometer reading because I'm trying to sell it. Um, am I kind of screwed unless I swap the battery?

## Answer

Not screwed, Mr. White — but you do need at least some juice to power the dash. On a 2020 Harley Touring bike, the odometer is fully electronic, so without battery power it won’t display.  

Your options:  

- **Quickest:** Hook up a portable jump starter (or jumper cables to another 12V battery with the donor vehicle OFF). That’ll give you enough power to turn the ignition on and see the odometer.  
- **If you have time:** Throw it on a trickle charger for a few hours until it has enough charge to light up the dash.  
- **If the battery’s toast:** Swap in a new one — you’ll need it anyway if you want buyers to hear it run.  

So no, you’re not screwed — you just need *any* temporary power source to wake the dash and grab the mileage.

## Question

It's not really something I have time to really get into. I kind of have to figure this out quick. How complicated is it?

## Answer

It’s very quick, Mr. White. For just pulling the odometer reading, you don’t even need to start the engine — you just need to get the dash powered for a minute.  

**Fastest option:**  
1. Get a small jump pack (the portable kind for motorcycles/cars).  
2. Pull the seat, clip red to +, black to –.  
3. Turn the ignition on. Dash will light up, odometer displays.  
4. Write down mileage, shut it off, disconnect pack.  

Whole thing takes ~2–3 minutes. Zero mechanical teardown beyond the seat.  

If you don’t have a jump pack, even a set of jumper cables to a car battery (car *off*) will do the same.

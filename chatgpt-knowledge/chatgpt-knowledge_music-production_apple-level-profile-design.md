Mr. White, strongest improvements:

1. **Make the top carousel one concept, not two cards**
   The “Latest Release” and “Upcoming Show” cards should feel like carousel content modules. Right now they still read slightly like separate tiles. A cleaner version would show one large feature card at a time:
   **Latest Release → Show → Video**
   with subtle dots underneath.

2. **Reduce repeated identity**
   “Tim White” appears in both the hero and profile card. Keep the large hero name, then make the lower card more functional:
   **avatar + Artist + stats + notifications**
   This lowers clutter.

3. **Replace “Followers” with “Subscribers”**
   For a notification-oriented product, “Subscribers” feels more owned and intentional than “Followers.”

4. **Make the CTA singular**
   Best primary CTA:
   **Turn on notifications**
   Secondary:
   share icon only.
   Avoid “Follow” entirely.

5. **Tighten the bottom nav**
   The nav is a little large. Shrink labels/icons slightly and give the profile card more breathing room.

6. **Use one accent color**
   The orange “On Tour” badge works well. Use that same warm accent for carousel dots and tiny active states. Keep blue only for verification.

7. **Improve hierarchy order**
   Best order:
   **Hero feature carousel → compact artist card → notifications CTA → nav**
   The upcoming show/release content belongs in the hero carousel, while the artist card should stay simple.

8. **Card copy refinements**
   Use:
   **ON TOUR**
   **Latest release**
   **Upcoming show**
   **Turn on notifications**
   **Releases**
   **Subscribers**

9. **Add a tiny context line under CTA**
   Example:
   “Get show, release, and merch alerts.”
   It explains the value without adding visual noise.

The current version is close. The biggest next step is making the carousel truly own the release/show content and simplifying the profile card into a premium subscription module.

## Answer

Mr. White, this direction is stronger.

Key structural rules to keep:

**Drawers**
- All Shows, Releases, Payments, and Manage Alerts should all use the same drawer height.
- Drawer content scrolls internally.
- The profile page stays fixed behind it.
- No “View all shows” for All Shows.
- No “View all releases” inside the releases drawer unless it pages to a full music detail/discography view.

**Payments drawer**
- Title: **Support Tim White**
- Amounts: **$5 / $10 / $20**
- Small right-aligned **Custom amount**
- Primary CTA: **Continue with Apple Pay**
- Secondary collapsed action: **Other payment options**
- Expanded secondary: **Continue with Venmo**

**Events drawer**
- Pin local show first when available.
- Use a small “Today,” “Near you,” or “Local” treatment.
- Drawer scrolls through every date.

**Releases drawer**
- Pin newest release first.
- Add **NEW** badge if release is 90 days old or less.
- Use a tiny dot on the Music tab when something new has happened since the user last visited.

**Alerts**
- Unsubscribed state: **Turn on alerts**
- Subscribed state: button collapses to checked bell icon or **Alerts on**
- Alerts tab gets a small check/dot state.
- Add a **Manage Alerts** drawer for subscribed users with:
  - Sent from Jovie
  - New Music
  - Shows
  - Merch
  - Sent by Artist
  - Subscribe to other alerts, off by default

Biggest polish note: the payments drawer should be even quieter. Remove extra descriptive copy unless needed. It should feel like a fast Apple Pay action sheet.

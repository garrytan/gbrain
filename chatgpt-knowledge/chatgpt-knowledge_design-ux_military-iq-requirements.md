## Question

What’s the iq of a dog

## Answer

The average IQ of a dog is often compared to that of a 2- to 2.5-year-old human child. This places the cognitive ability of dogs in the range of approximately 40 to 60 on standard human IQ scales. However, this is a rough estimate and varies depending on the breed, with some dogs like Border Collies and Poodles generally considered more intelligent than others. Dog intelligence is typically measured by their ability to learn commands, solve problems, and understand human emotions rather than by traditional IQ tests designed for humans.

## Question

What iq is the military limit

## Answer

In the U.S. military, there isn't a strict "IQ limit," but enlistment eligibility is determined by scores on the Armed Services Vocational Aptitude Battery (ASVAB) test. The ASVAB is used to assess a recruit's suitability for military service and specific roles.

Recruits are categorized into different levels based on their ASVAB scores. The minimum required ASVAB score varies by branch, but in general, the minimum Armed Forces Qualification Test (AFQT) score needed to enlist is around 31 for the Army, although some branches, like the Air Force, require a higher score (usually around 36 or higher). The ASVAB includes various categories that assess different aptitudes, and while it's not an IQ test, it does give an indication of cognitive ability.

In terms of IQ comparison, some sources suggest that an AFQT score of 31 roughly correlates with an IQ of 85, which is approximately at the 16th percentile of the population. Individuals with scores much lower than this may struggle to meet the cognitive demands of military service.

## Question

What iq is retardation

## Answer

The term "mental retardation" is outdated and no longer used in medical or psychological contexts due to its negative connotations. It has been replaced by the term "intellectual disability." Intellectual disability is typically defined by an IQ below 70-75, along with significant limitations in adaptive behaviors (daily life skills) and evident before the age of 18.

The classification for IQ scores typically looks like this:
- **Mild intellectual disability**: IQ between 50-70
- **Moderate intellectual disability**: IQ between 35-50
- **Severe intellectual disability**: IQ between 20-35
- **Profound intellectual disability**: IQ below 20

It's important to recognize that an individual's abilities can't be fully measured by IQ alone, as adaptive behaviors and social functioning are also key factors in diagnosing intellectual disabilities.

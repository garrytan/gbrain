## Answer

Mr. White, short answer: dropping from six sessions to 3-4 full-body sessions during a cut is fine for muscle retention as long as you keep **intensity high** (≤3 RIR) and hit each muscle’s **maintenance-volume (MV)**—about one-third of the sets you used to grow it.

**Why it works**

| What the evidence says | Key take-away |
|---|---|
| *Meta-analyses*: When weekly volume is equated, training frequency has **no independent effect** on hypertrophy or strength citeturn0search1turn0search6 | You can pack the same weekly sets into fewer workouts. |
| *Maintenance-volume data*: 6-8 hard sets per muscle per week (~⅓ “growth” volume) keeps size in trained lifters citeturn1search4turn4search0 | That fits easily into 3 workouts. |
| *Dose-reduction trials*: Young adults kept all their muscle with **one-third** of prior volume, even after 32 weeks citeturn2view0 | Confirms MV rule-of-thumb. |
| *Energy-deficit meta-analysis*: Caloric restriction blunts new growth but does **not erase** size/strength if high-protein diet and resistance work continue citeturn0search4 | Focus is preservation, not gains. |
| *Coaches (Israetel / Henselmans)*: Both advise cutting volume 20-40 % on a diet; 2-3 sessions/week is plenty if MV is met citeturn1search6turn0search3 | Matches your hypothesis. |

**Practical template for the next 6-8 weeks**

- **Frequency**: 3 full-body days (e.g., Mon-Wed-Fri).  
- **Sets**: 6–8 hard sets per big muscle group/week (shoulders, biceps, chest, triceps, abs, back).  
- **Rep zone**: 6–15; keep loads similar to bulk—don’t switch to super-light, very-high reps.  
- **Progression**: Use the RP Hypertrophy App’s 3-day “Maintenance” or “Low-Volume” option; let it auto-regulate but cap added sets at MV+1.  
- **Conditioning**: Steps or light cardio as needed for the deficit; avoid exhausting met-cons that steal recovery.  
- **Nutrition/recovery**: ≥1.8 g protein/kg, creatine 5 g, 8 h sleep—your standard setup.

**When life settles**

- Move back to 5-6 sessions and higher weekly sets (MEV→MAV) for your bulk; AM/PM splits are optional “nice-to-have,” not mandatory for growth until you’re pushing MRV.

Bottom line: a lean, busy Mr. White can coast on 3 quality sessions, preserve the physique, and free up bandwidth for everything else—no appreciable downside.

## Question

This is great to know, and then on top of that, what would be any modifications that need to be made, if any, for the same setup, but combined with the use of Truzepotide, specifically according to Mike Izretell.

## Answer

Mr White, tirzepatide doesn’t change the **training logic**—it just makes the nutrition/recovery pieces easier to botch. Keep the 3-day MV plan but mind four tweaks RP & Mike Israetel highlight when GLP-1s enter the chat:

1. **Protein & rate-of-loss**  
   * Aim ≥2 g protein/kg (you’re already close at 180 g) and cap weight loss to ≈0.5-1 % BW per week to keep lean mass. Most GLP-1 studies that show 40-60 % LBM loss involved crash-diets with low protein and no lifting. citeturn3search0turn10search8  

2. **Volume & intensity**  
   * Stick with the 6-8 hard sets/muscle/week template; keep loads in the 6-15 rep zone with ≤3 RIR.  
   * If tirzepatide side-effects (nausea, fatigue) spike, drop *volume* 10-20 % first—never the load. Israetel’s rule: “Cut sets, not weight, when recovery tanks on a diet.” citeturn0search1turn5view0  

3. **Pre-workout carbs & electrolytes**  
   * Appetite suppression often drags carb intake too low; take a quick 20-30 g carb drink (e.g., Gatorade + creatine) 30 min pre-lift to keep performance up.  
   * GLP-1s may slow gastric emptying—small liquid carbs beat big solid meals here. citeturn10search0  

4. **Recovery hygiene**  
   * Hydrate aggressively (nausea → lower fluid).  
   * Monitor step counts: with appetite down you may subconsciously move less; keep your usual NEAT so the deficit stays moderate.  

**Bottom line**: the 3-day maintenance-volume setup still safeguards your muscle; tirzepatide just demands stricter protein, a sane weight-loss pace, and a little carb-timing so the lifts stay heavy. Do that and you’ll coast through the cut without sacrificing the size you built.

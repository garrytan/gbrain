## Question

Are like scooters street legal in West Hollywood 

## Answer

In West Hollywood, California, electric scooters are street legal. Riders must follow specific rules, such as riding in bike lanes where available, wearing a helmet if under 18, not riding on sidewalks, and not exceeding 15 mph. It's important to check both city and state regulations for any updates or specific local rules.

## Question

What is the penalty for riding on a sidewalk

## Answer

In West Hollywood, riding an electric scooter on the sidewalk is prohibited. Violating this rule can result in a citation and a fine. The amount of the fine can vary, often starting around $100 for a first offense, but can increase based on the number of offenses or the specific circumstances of the violation. It's advisable to always ride electric scooters in accordance with local traffic laws and designated lanes to avoid penalties.

## Question

What about traffic lights

## Answer

In West Hollywood, as in all of California, electric scooter riders must obey all traffic signals and signs, just like any other vehicle on the road. This includes stopping at red lights and stop signs, yielding to pedestrians, and following the directions indicated by traffic lights. Failure to comply with traffic signals can result in traffic violations, which may include fines and points on your driver’s license, depending on the severity of the offense.

## Question

What about bikes

## Answer

In West Hollywood, bicycles are subject to similar rules as electric scooters regarding traffic signals and signs. Bicyclists must obey all traffic lights, stop signs, and other traffic control devices. This means stopping at red lights, waiting for green lights before proceeding, and yielding when required by signs or road conditions.

Bicycles are allowed on most roads, except where prohibited (like certain freeways), and they should use bike lanes when available. Bicyclists are also permitted to ride on sidewalks unless local ordinances specifically prohibit it, though it is always a good practice to check local laws as they can vary by city.

Violations of traffic laws by bicyclists can lead to citations and fines, similar to those for motorists and scooter riders.

## Question

What about a drivers license

## Answer

In California, you do not need a driver's license to operate a bicycle. However, for electric scooters, the rules are slightly different. Riders must have either a valid driver's license or a learner's permit to operate an electric scooter. This requirement applies to all motorized scooters, including those available for rent through shared mobility services.

For both bicycles and electric scooters, it's important to know and follow all local traffic laws to ensure safety and avoid penalties.

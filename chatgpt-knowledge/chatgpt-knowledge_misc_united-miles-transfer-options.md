## Question

## Answer

Yes. entity["company","United Airlines",""] MileagePlus miles can be transferred to another person, but the fees are usually poor enough that buying a ticket for them directly is the better move.

### Current structure
Through the official urlUnited MileagePlus miles transfer pagehttps://www.united.com/en/us/mileageplus/miles-transfers.html you can:
- Transfer miles between MileagePlus accounts
- Typically pay:

### Usually smarter options
Instead of transferring miles:
- Book an award ticket directly for the other person from your account

### Example
If you have 80k miles and Liv needs a flight:
- Bad option: transfer 40k miles to her
- Better option: you book her flight from your MileagePlus account

### Important limitations
- Recipient needs their own MileagePlus account for transfers
- Transfers are generally irreversible
- Premier status usually does not waive transfer fees
- Award tickets booked for others are fully allowed by United

## Question

can i transfer my mileage accont to another persson?

## Answer

No. entity["company","United Airlines",""] does not generally allow you to transfer ownership of an entire MileagePlus account to another person.

MileagePlus accounts are intended to stay tied to the original individual. Selling or permanently giving away accounts violates their terms and can result in:
- account closure
- forfeited miles
- canceled tickets

What you *can* do:
- Book flights for another person using your miles
- Add them as the traveler
- Potentially let a spouse/executor access the account informally if you die, though officially miles are not property and United controls transfers after death

There are limited exceptions for:
- deceased account holders
- legal name changes
- mergers/divorce situations
- corporate programs

If your goal is simply “I want someone else to use all my miles,” the safest method is:
- keep the account
- book travel for them directly from it.

Practically:

Lowest-risk approach:

- Or, if you already have legitimate access and need to use them soon, book the flights directly rather than attempting a retail transfer of miles

## Answer

Yes — Dr. Israetel’s view is that even with potent anorectics like tirzepatide, you still need a true maintenance phase after a diet to lock in results and let your metabolism and hormones recover. Key points:

- **Maintenance remains necessary**  
  Tirzepatide blunts appetite and aids fat loss, but it doesn’t prevent the adaptive drop in resting metabolic rate or the hormonal shifts that occur with dieting. Dr. Israetel advises planning “adequate maintenance periods in between diet phases” to prevent rapid regain and allow psychological and physiological recovery citeturn0search2turn0search4.

- **Length of the maintenance phase**  
  He generally recommends that maintenance last at least as long as the preceding cut (e.g. a 12-week diet → ≥12 weeks of maintenance), though shorter cuts can use a 1:1 or even 2:1 maintenance-to-cut ratio depending on individual response citeturn0search4.

- **Modifications when using tirzepatide**  
  1. **Reverse-diet gradually**: Appetite suppression can mask hunger cues, so don’t jump straight to your old intake. Instead, add calories slowly (e.g. +50–100 kcal/week) and track body weight closely.  
  2. **Protein first**: Keep protein high (≈2.2 g/kg bw) to protect lean mass as you refeed.  
  3. **Maintain training volume**: Continue resistance work at or near pre-diet volumes to signal muscle preservation.  
  4. **Use objective markers**: Because tirzepatide can blunt hunger, rely on weekly weigh-ins and possibly body composition scans rather than subjective appetite citeturn0search2.

By treating a tirzepatide-assisted cut exactly like any other diet phase in terms of maintenance planning—and by adjusting your reverse-diet pace to account for suppressed hunger—you’ll best cement your fat-loss gains and minimize rebound.

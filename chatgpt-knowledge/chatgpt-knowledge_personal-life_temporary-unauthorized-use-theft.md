## Answer

In Los Angeles, California, towing a vehicle from private property to another private property, such as a tow yard, is legal under specific circumstances. Here are the key points:

1. **Authority to Tow**: Property owners or their agents have the right to remove unauthorized vehicles from their property. This is often covered under local parking regulations.

2. **Towing to a Secure Location**: Once a vehicle is towed, it is typically taken to a secure tow yard or impound lot. This is legal because the towing is considered part of the process of enforcing parking rules on private property.

3. **Notice and Signage**: For a vehicle to be legally towed from private property, certain conditions must be met. This often includes proper signage indicating the parking rules and consequences for violation, including towing.

4. **Towing Regulations**: Towing companies must follow specific regulations regarding how and where they can tow vehicles. These regulations are designed to protect vehicle owners from predatory towing practices.

5. **Theft vs. Legal Towing**: Towing a vehicle in accordance with local laws and regulations is not considered theft. Theft involves the unlawful taking of someone's property with the intent to permanently deprive them of it. Legal towing under appropriate circumstances does not meet this definition.

6. **Recovery of Vehicle**: Vehicle owners have the right to recover their towed vehicles, usually upon payment of towing and storage fees. The fees and process should comply with local regulations.

7. **Disputes**: If a vehicle owner believes their vehicle was towed illegally, they can dispute the tow. This might involve legal action, contacting local regulatory agencies, or disputing the charges.

It's always important to be aware of local laws and regulations regarding towing, as they can vary significantly from one jurisdiction to another.

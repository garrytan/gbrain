## Answer

Mr. White, three easy ways to redirect that blast so you can keep the PTAC on without freezing your couch:

1. **Clip-on vent deflector**  
   • Grab a universal AC vent deflector (about $10–15).  
   • It clips onto the front grille and its adjustable fins let you tilt the airflow up toward the ceiling.  

2. **DIY baffle with foam or plastic**  
   • Cut a piece of thin foam-board or stiff plastic to span the top half of the grille at a shallow angle.  
   • Mount it with removable Velcro or command strips so the stream hits the baffle and arcs upward.  
   • **Don’t** cover the intake slot at the bottom—only deflect the outflow.  

3. **Add a small oscillating fan**  
   • Place a low-speed desk or floor fan beside the unit, aimed at the ceiling.  
   • It’ll catch and disperse the cold air throughout the room rather than letting it dump on the couch.  

Any of these will let you leave the AC running at low fan speed without sitting in a direct Arctic blast.

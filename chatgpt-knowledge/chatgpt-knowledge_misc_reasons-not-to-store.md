## Question

Are there any reasons we can't store product under our bar

## Answer

Storing products under your bar may not be ideal for several reasons:

1. **Health and Safety Regulations**: Local health codes often have strict regulations regarding the storage of food and beverage products. Storing items under the bar might not comply with these regulations, leading to potential fines or shutdowns.

2. **Temperature Control**: The area under the bar may not have the proper temperature control necessary for certain products, especially perishable items, which could lead to spoilage or contamination.

3. **Pest Control**: Storing products in areas that are not properly sealed or maintained can attract pests, such as rodents or insects, which can contaminate your supplies.

4. **Accessibility and Organization**: Properly organizing and accessing items stored under the bar can be challenging, leading to inefficiencies during service. It might also pose a risk for slips and falls if staff frequently need to bend down to retrieve items.

5. **Cleanliness**: The area under the bar is often subject to spills and moisture, which can create an unsanitary environment for storing products. This can compromise the quality and safety of the items stored there.

6. **Legal Liability**: In the event of an inspection or health-related incident, non-compliance with storage regulations can result in legal liabilities for the establishment.

To ensure compliance and maintain quality, it's best to follow local health and safety guidelines and store products in designated, temperature-controlled areas.

In Los Angeles, there are several restrictions for storing products under your bar according to local health codes:

# Inflatable setup times

> **Source:** ChatGPT conversation
> **Date:** 2025-10-27T23:30:25Z
> **Messages:** 10
> **Topic:** personal-life

## Question

How long does a large-scale, inflatable, like bouncy house, take to inflate?

## Answer

Mr. White — most commercial inflatables are up and bouncy fast:

- 13×13 ft “standard” house (1–1.5 HP blower): ~2–4 minutes  
- 15×15 ft: ~3–5 minutes  
- Combo/slide units (often 2 blowers): ~4–8 minutes  
- Large 60–100 ft obstacle courses (2–4 blowers): ~8–15 minutes

Notes:
- They’re “constant-inflation” — blowers run the whole time in use.  
- Setup/anchoring and safety checks usually add another 10–20+ minutes.  
- Slower if using long/skinny extension cords, undersized blowers, or in very hot/windy conditions.

## Question

And how long does deflating it take?

## Answer

Mr. White — rough deflate times (after everyone’s off):

- 13×13 ft house: initial collapse 1–2 min; rolled/bagged 5–10 min (1–2 people).  
- 15×15 ft: collapse 2–3 min; rolled/bagged 8–15 min.  
- Combo/slide (2 blowers): collapse 3–6 min; rolled/bagged 15–25 min (2 people best).  
- 60–100 ft obstacle: collapse 6–10 min; rolled/bagged 25–45+ min (2–4 people).

Faster if you open all zippers/vents, “walk” the air out, and roll tightly from the far end toward the blower tube. Slower if wet, dirty, or you’re solo. Keep kids clear during collapse.

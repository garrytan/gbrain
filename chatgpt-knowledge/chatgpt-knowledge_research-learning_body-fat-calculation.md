# Body Fat Calculation

## Question

Yo, my last DEXA scan I was 16.8% body fat at 179 pounds. I'm currently 172.6 pounds. Assuming I lost no muscle, what is my current body fat percentage?

## Answer

Mr. White, here’s the math:

- At 179 lb and 16.8% BF, fat mass = 179 × 0.168 ≈ 30.07 lb, so lean mass ≈ 179 – 30.07 = 148.93 lb.  
- At your current 172.6 lb (assuming lean mass unchanged at 148.93 lb), fat mass = 172.6 – 148.93 = 23.67 lb.  
- Current BF% = 23.67 ÷ 172.6 ≈ 0.1371 → **13.7%**.

## Question

And assuming no muscle loss, what weight would I need to hit in order for me to be at 12%, 10%, and 8% body fat?

## Answer

Mr. White, keeping your lean mass at ~148.9 lb, your target weights are:

- **12 % BF:** 148.93 lb ÷ 0.88 ≈ **169.2 lb**  
- **10 % BF:** 148.93 lb ÷ 0.90 ≈ **165.5 lb**  
- **8 % BF:** 148.93 lb ÷ 0.92 ≈ **161.9 lb**

## Question

Alright, now based on everything you know about my fitness goals, and how I want to like basically be 8% body fat, and how my goals are primarily aesthetic based, and I'm looking for a model physique and I need to have ripped abs, and cum gutters, and as close as much of an APEC as I can, while then also trying to build muscle in my shoulders, I then my arms, primarily biceps, and then my chest, and then my back, next being traps and forearms, and then lastly and leastly being lower body, what would be your recommendation for me, should I continue cutting, should I switch to a maintenance, and attempt to re-comp when I'm capable of re-comping, considering I've done minimal leg training, and glute training, and I haven't bulked seriously in quite some time, should I switch to a maintenance, and see what kind of re-comping we get, to continue to drive the body fat percentage down a little bit, while putting on some muscle, without putting on fat, or should I go into a slight surplus, and

## Answer

Mr. White, here’s the straight-up rundown:

1. **Cutting from ~13.7 % → 8 % BF**  
   - You’d drop from 172.6 lb to ~161.9 lb (losing ~10.7 lb of fat).  
   - At this leanness, risks ↑ of strength loss and stalled muscle growth—especially in untrained legs/glutes.

2. **Maintenance + Re-comp**  
   - At ~14 % BF you’ll gain muscle and lose fat very slowly (advanced lifter effect).  
   - Body recomposition works best when BF > 15 % or you’re a novice; at your level it’ll be glacial.

3. **Lean Bulk (small surplus)**  
   - At ~2–4 % weekly surpluses, you maximize hypertrophy in shoulders, arms, chest, traps/forearms—while minimizing fat gain.  
   - Once you’ve added 5–8 lb of muscle, you do a short cut (“mini-cut”) to bring BF down to ~8 %.  

---

**My recommendation:**  
• **Skip another long cut.** You’re already lean enough that further cutting carries muscle-loss risk.  
• **Shift to a lean bulk** (≈+150 – 250 kcal/day), keep protein at ~2.2 g/kg, priority train shoulders → biceps → chest → back → traps/forearms → legs.  
• **After 2–3 months (or +5–8 lb muscle),** run a 4–6 week mini-cut to hit ~8 % BF.  

This two-phase “build then refine” approach lets you keep your abs and gutters sharp while packing on the upper-body thickness you want.

## Question

I have clearly visible top four, and then the bottom two of the six-pack are pretty visible. Just like, it's a little fluffy below the bottom two of the six-pack. Between the bottom two of the eight-pack and the bottom two of the six-pack, that line is a little fluffy. And, like, the gutters are coming in, but there's still like some fat over them a little bit.

## Answer

Mr. White, lower-ab fluff is almost always just body-fat holding out at roughly 10–11 %. You’ve got top six showing at ~13.7 % BF, but to nail those bottom two and gutters you’ll need to drop closer to 10 % (or a hair under). Here’s the play:

- **Why it’s still fluffy**  
  You can’t spot-reduce. Lower abs clear up once overall BF hits ~10 %, give or take. At ~13.7 % you’re nearly there, but that last bit stubbornly sits low.  

- **Short mini-cut to 10 %**  
  - Target ~165.5 lb (we calculated that earlier: 148.9 lb ÷ 0.90).  
  - Run a 4–6 week deficit of ~200–300 kcal/day below maintenance.  
  - Keep protein at ~2.2 g/kg and lift heavy per your RP Hypertrophy template.  
  - Add 2–3 LISS sessions (20–30 min walking or bike).  

- **Maintain training priorities**  
  Shoulders → biceps → chest → back → traps/forearms → legs, exactly as you’ve dialed in.  
  Core work is fine, but remember it won’t burn the last layer of fat—that comes from diet.  

- **What comes next**  
  Once you hit ~165.5 lb and check your lower abs, switch back to a lean bulk (+150–250 kcal/day) to build thickness in shoulders/arms/chest, then rinse-repeat mini-cuts as needed.  

That short mini-cut will give you the lower-abs and gutters you want without sacrificing the muscle you’ve built.

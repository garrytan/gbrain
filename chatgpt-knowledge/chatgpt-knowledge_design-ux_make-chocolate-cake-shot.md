## Question

Difference in headhunter vs recruiter

## Answer

A headhunter is typically a third-party individual or agency hired by a company to find highly qualified candidates for high-level positions. They often actively seek out these candidates, many of whom are not actively looking for new employment, using their networks and industry knowledge.

A recruiter, on the other hand, can either be an internal employee of a company or an external individual or agency that helps fill vacancies across various levels of the organization. They often post job listings and screen applicants who apply through those listings.

The main difference lies in their approach: headhunters are more proactive in seeking out candidates, often for senior roles, while recruiters may take a more broad and reactive approach, handling multiple job openings at different levels within an organization.

## Question

Chocolate cake shot

## Answer

The Chocolate Cake Shot is a popular shooter that's known for its ability to mimic the flavor of a chocolate cake. It typically involves a combination of hazelnut liqueur, like Frangelico, and vodka, often served with a sugar-coated lemon. The traditional method is to take the shot and then bite into the sugar-coated lemon, which enhances the chocolate cake flavor experience.

## Question

How to make it

## Answer

To make a Chocolate Cake Shot, you will need:

- 1/2 oz of Frangelico (hazelnut liqueur)
- 1/2 oz of vanilla vodka
- Sugar and lemon slices (for garnish)

Here's a quick guide on how to make it:

1. Rim the edge of a shot glass with sugar.
2. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
3. Pour equal parts of Frangelico and vanilla vodka into the shaker, typically 1/2 oz of each.
4. Shake well to chill the mixture.
5. Strain the mixture into the prepared shot glass.
6. Serve with a slice of lemon. After taking the shot, you bite into the sugar-coated lemon slice, which creates the chocolate cake flavor sensation.

For the complete method and additional tips, you can review the recipes on the websites of [The Spruce Eats](8), [Snappy Gourmet](9), and [Occasional Cocktails](11).

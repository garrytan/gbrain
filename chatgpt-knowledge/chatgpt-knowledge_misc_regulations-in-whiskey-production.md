## Question

What does Canadian mean in bartending

## Answer

In bartending, "Canadian" typically refers to Canadian whisky, which is used as an ingredient in various cocktails.

## Question

What’s different about Canadian whiskey 

## Answer

Canadian whisky is known for its lighter and smoother profile compared to other types of whisky. It often has a high rye content, which can give it a spicier flavor, but it's generally less intense than American rye whiskeys. Canadian regulations allow for a wide variety of grains in the blend and the use of flavoring agents, contributing to a diverse range of flavors and styles.

## Question

What aspects of whiskey are regulated and why

## Answer

Whiskey regulations are in place to ensure quality, authenticity, and consumer protection. They vary by country, but generally include:

1. **Ingredients**: Dictates what grains can be used. For example, bourbon must contain at least 51% corn.

2. **Production Process**: Includes specifics on distillation proof, aging process, and types of stills.

3. **Aging**: Regulations often specify minimum aging periods and types of barrels.

4. **Labeling**: Rules on what information must be provided on labels, including age, alcohol content, and country of origin.

5. **Geographical Indications**: Certain whiskeys must be produced in specific regions (e.g., Scotch in Scotland).

These regulations help maintain tradition, ensure quality, and provide transparency for consumers.

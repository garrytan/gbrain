## Question

158lb at 12% bf. 6’4”

What’s my family
What’s it take to get to 8%bf

## Answer

Let’s break this down concisely:  

---

### **Current stats**
- **Bodyweight:** 158 lb  
- **Body fat:** 12%  
- **Lean body mass (LBM):** 158 × 0.88 = **139 lb LBM**  
- **Fat mass:** 158 × 0.12 = **19 lb fat**

---

### **Target: 8% body fat**
You want to keep your **LBM (139 lb)** constant and reduce fat until that represents 8% of total bodyweight.

Let \( x \) = target bodyweight.  
Then \( 139 = 0.92x \).  
So \( x = 139 / 0.92 = 151 lb. \)

- **Goal weight:** ~151 lb  
- **Fat mass at 8%:** 151 × 0.08 = 12 lb  
- **Fat loss required:** 19 − 12 = **7 lb fat**

---

### **Calories required**
Approx. **3,500 kcal = 1 lb fat**, so:  
7 × 3,500 = **24,500 kcal total deficit**

If you create:
- **500 kcal/day deficit → ~7 weeks**
- **750 kcal/day deficit → ~5 weeks**

That’s assuming strict adherence and no lean mass loss.

---

### **Training & Nutrition Recommendations**
**Training (per RP Strength / Isratel principles):**
- Keep **resistance training volume moderate–high** (8–20 sets/muscle/week).
- Avoid drastic cardio that interferes with recovery.
- Use **periodized diet fatigue management** (diet breaks every 4–6 weeks if cutting longer).

**Nutrition:**
- **Protein:** ~1.0–1.2 g/lb LBM → 140–165 g/day.  
- **Fat:** 0.3–0.4 g/lb → ~45–65 g/day.  
- **Carbs:** Fill remaining calories.  
- Keep **weight loss 0.5–1.0 lb/week** to preserve muscle.

## Question

Ffmi after the cut?

## Answer

Let’s calculate your **FFMI (Fat-Free Mass Index)** now and after the cut.  

---

### **Formula**
\[
FFMI = \frac{LBM \, (kg)}{height^2 \, (m^2)}
\]
and we can adjust to “normalized FFMI” (to 1.8 m height) if desired.

---

### **Your current stats**
- Height: **6’4” = 1.93 m**  
- LBM: **139 lb = 63.0 kg**

\[
FFMI_{current} = \frac{63.0}{1.93^2} = 17.0
\]

---

### **After the cut (8% BF)**
- LBM stays **~139 lb = 63.0 kg** (assuming no muscle loss)  
- Height unchanged (1.93 m)

\[
FFMI_{after} = \frac{63.0}{1.93^2} = 17.0
\]

So your **FFMI remains ~17.0** — it doesn’t change because FFMI depends on *lean mass*, not total bodyweight.  

If you lose any muscle during the cut (say 1–2 lb LBM), FFMI would drop slightly to **16.8–16.9**.

---

### **Interpretation**
- Average untrained male FFMI: **~18**  
- Well-trained natural male: **20–22**  
- Upper natural limit (per Kouri et al.): **~25**

So at **17**, you’re lean and light for your height — more of an athletic/slim physique, not yet in the “muscular natural” range.

## Question

What about if I stay at 158 and recomp to 8%

## Answer

Good question — let’s walk through that.  

---

### **Current stats**
- Weight: **158 lb**
- Body fat: **12%**
- **LBM:** 158 × 0.88 = **139 lb**
- **Fat mass:** 19 lb

---

### **Target: 8% BF at same bodyweight**
If you stay **158 lb** but reach **8% body fat**, then:  
\[
158 × 0.08 = 12.6\text{ lb fat}
\]
\[
LBM = 158 − 12.6 = 145.4\text{ lb LBM}
\]

So you’d need to **gain ~6.4 lb of lean mass** while losing **~6.4 lb of fat** — a classic recomposition scenario.

---

### **New FFMI**
\[
145.4\text{ lb} = 65.9\text{ kg}
\]
\[
Height = 1.93\text{ m}
\]
\[
FFMI = \frac{65.9}{1.93^2} = 17.7
\]

**→ FFMI: ~17.7**

---

### **Interpretation**
- +0.7 FFMI from your current 17.0  
- This is realistic but *slow*, especially if you’re already resistance trained.
- Natural recomposition of +6 lb LBM and −6 lb fat could take **6–12 months**, depending on training quality, recovery, and nutrient timing.

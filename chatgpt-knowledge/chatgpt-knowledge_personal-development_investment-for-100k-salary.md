## Question

## Answer

To determine the amount you need to invest in the S&P 500 to generate an annual salary of $100,000 from profits, we'll consider the average annual return of the S&P 500. Historically, the S&P 500 has offered an average annual return of about 7-10% over long periods, after accounting for inflation. However, to be conservative, let's use a lower rate of return, say 5%.

To find out how much you need to invest, we can use the formula:

\[ \text{Required Investment} = \frac{\text{Desired Annual Income}}{\text{Rate of Return}} \]

Plugging in $100,000 for the desired annual income and 0.05 (5%) for the rate of return, we get:

\[ \text{Required Investment} = \frac{100,000}{0.05} \]

Let's calculate this.

## Answer

To generate an annual salary of $100,000 from profits by investing in the S&P 500, assuming a conservative annual return rate of 5%, you would need to start with an investment of approximately $2,000,000.

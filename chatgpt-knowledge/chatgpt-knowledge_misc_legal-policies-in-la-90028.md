Mr. White, here’s a breakdown of how the **SSLA Policies** measure up under **Los Angeles (CA) and federal labor law**:  

---

### ✅ **Generally Legal**
- **Attendance, Scheduling, and Call-Out Requirements** — Employers can set scheduling and call-out policies, as long as they comply with the **Los Angeles Fair Work Week Ordinance** (mainly for retail) and give required breaks. There’s no violation here if the event doesn’t fall under that ordinance.  
- **Dress Code** — Employers can enforce reasonable dress codes if not discriminatory. The requirement for themed uniforms (vest, hat, gloves, etc.) is fine **if the employer pays for and maintains the uniform** (Cal. Lab. Code §2802).  
- **Anti-Harassment Policy** — This complies with California’s required anti-harassment standards (Gov. Code §12940).  

---

### ⚠️ **Potentially Questionable / Needs Caution**
- **Late Policy and Tip Deductions** —  
  Under **California Labor Code §351**, **tips are employee property**. Employers **cannot deduct** or withhold tips as punishment for lateness or policy violations.  
  ➤ *Issue:* The “deductions from the tip pool for lateness” policy is likely **illegal** in California. Only lawful tip-pool allocations based on job role and time worked are permitted.  

- **Tip Pool System** —  
  Legal **only if**:
  1. No managers or supervisors share in the pool (**California Supreme Court: *Lu v. Hawaiian Gardens Casino***).  
  2. Tip distribution is transparent and strictly proportional to service employees’ roles.  
  If “bar management” participates in the pool, that’s **illegal** if they have any supervisory duties (e.g., hiring, discipline, scheduling).  

- **“Immediate Termination for Keeping Cash Tips”** —  
  Theft or dishonesty can justify termination, but **firing without investigation** could raise due-process or wrongful termination claims if the accusation is false. Documentation should back such a policy.  

- **Parking Fee Deduction ($15)** —  
  **Illegal** unless the employee gives **written authorization** (Cal. Lab. Code §224). Employers can’t automatically deduct money from paychecks for lost or unreturned items.  

- **Cell Phone Confiscation** —  
  Management can restrict phone use during work, but **holding employees’ personal property** (phones) may raise **privacy and property rights issues**. It’s safer to require phones be stored away rather than “held by management.”

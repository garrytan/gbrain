## Answer

Here's a sample meal plan that meets your requirements of 120-125g of protein per day, excludes dairy products, and provides around 1800 calories. This plan includes a variety of protein sources from plant-based and animal-based foods:

Meal 1:
- 2 slices of whole-grain toast with 2 tablespoons of almond butter
- 1 medium-sized banana
- 1 cup of unsweetened almond milk

Meal 2:
- Grilled chicken breast (4 oz) with herbs and spices
- 1 cup of quinoa
- Steamed broccoli (1 cup)
- 1 tablespoon of olive oil for dressing

Meal 3:
- Spinach and chickpea salad with lemon-tahini dressing:
  - 2 cups of fresh spinach leaves
  - 1/2 cup of chickpeas
  - 1/4 cup of sliced almonds
  - Dressing: 1 tablespoon of tahini, juice of half a lemon, salt, and pepper

Meal 4:
- Vegan protein smoothie:
  - 1 scoop of plant-based protein powder (check for dairy-free options)
  - 1 cup of unsweetened almond milk
  - 1 tablespoon of chia seeds
  - 1/2 cup of mixed berries (e.g., strawberries, blueberries)
  - 1 handful of spinach

Meal 5:
- Baked salmon fillet (4 oz) seasoned with herbs and spices
- Roasted sweet potatoes (1 medium-sized)
- Steamed asparagus (1 cup)

Meal 6:
- Lentil curry with brown rice:
  - 1 cup of cooked lentils
  - 1/2 cup of diced tomatoes
  - 1/4 cup of diced onions
  - 1 clove of garlic, minced
  - Curry spices (turmeric, cumin, coriander, paprika, garam masala)
  - Serve over 1 cup of cooked brown rice

Snacks (spread throughout the day):
- 1 medium-sized apple with 1 tablespoon of almond butter
- 1 handful of mixed nuts (e.g., almonds, cashews, walnuts)
- Carrot sticks with hummus (2 tablespoons)

Remember to adjust the portion sizes based on your individual needs and consult with a healthcare professional or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

## Question

how about snacks for someone who loves candy. is there a snack like candy that fits the plan

## Answer

If you have a sweet tooth and enjoy candy, there are some healthier snack options that can satisfy your cravings while still fitting into your meal plan. Here are a few ideas:

1. Dark chocolate: Choose dark chocolate with a high percentage of cocoa (70% or higher) as it contains less sugar and provides some health benefits. Enjoy a small piece or two as an occasional treat.

2. Dried fruit: Opt for dried fruits like raisins, cranberries, or apricots, which offer natural sweetness. However, be mindful of portion sizes as they can be calorie-dense. Aim for about 1/4 cup as a snack.

3. Energy balls: Prepare homemade energy balls using nutritious ingredients like dates, nuts, and seeds. You can find various recipes online, and they can be customized to your taste preferences.

4. Fruit skewers: Create colorful fruit skewers by threading bite-sized pieces of your favorite fruits onto skewers. This snack is not only visually appealing but also provides natural sweetness from the fruits.

5. Frozen grapes: Freeze a handful of grapes for a refreshing and sweet snack. They offer a unique texture and can be enjoyed straight from the freezer.

Remember to moderate your intake of these snacks and incorporate them as part of your overall balanced diet. It's essential to focus on nutrient-dense foods and ensure you're meeting your protein and calorie goals while enjoying these occasional sweet treats.

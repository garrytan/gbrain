# Power washer price negotiation

> **Source:** ChatGPT conversation
> **Date:** 2025-09-16T19:44:31Z
> **Messages:** 6
> **Topic:** personal-development

## Question

Well, I'm just trying to negotiate on a $40 power washer deal that I'm selling on Facebook Marketplace. Am I crazy? Is $40 for a power washer not an insane deal?

## Answer

$40 is already a solid bargain for a working power washer. Most used units—assuming they actually spray at decent pressure—list on Marketplace or Craigslist in the $60-$150 range depending on brand and condition.  

You’re not crazy. If it runs and isn’t cracked or leaking, $40 is a “buy it now” price. Hold firm or even nudge higher if multiple people are interested.

## Question

Okay, give me a Chris Voss-style response to this person saying... So first they said, okay, what time works for you? And then they said, what's the lowest you're willing to take?

## Answer

Here’s a concise, Voss-style reply you can copy:

> *“Sounds like you’re ready to pick it up today. I’m asking $40 because that’s already a great deal—most go for much more. What time works for you to grab it?”*

It uses:
- **Mirroring/labeling** (“sounds like you’re ready…”) to keep them engaged.  
- **Anchoring** the price at $40 as final without saying “firm.”  
- **Calm, collaborative tone** that nudges them toward scheduling instead of haggling.

## Question

You can come grab it anytime after 5pm tomorrow.

## Answer

Here’s a quick Chris Voss–style follow-up you can send:

> *“Great—anytime after 5 PM tomorrow works. $40 is already a solid deal, so let’s lock it in. What time after 5 works best for you?”*

Keeps the anchor at $40, sounds cooperative, and pushes them to commit to a pickup slot.

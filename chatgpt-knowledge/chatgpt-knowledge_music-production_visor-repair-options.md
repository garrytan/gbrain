## Answer

Mr. White, you’ve got four cost-effective paths forward:

1. **Re-tension the existing hinge**  
   - Pull the visor off and remove its trim cap (one Phillips screw).  
   - Inspect the spring-loaded hinge pin: clean it, apply a tiny drop of white-lithium grease, then reassemble and torque the screws firmly.  
   - Often this restores enough friction to hold it up.

2. **Swap just the hinge-spring assembly**  
   - The full visor assembly runs ~$170, but the internal spring/hinge retainer (part # varies by year) can be had for $15–30 on eBay or at a Mazda parts counter.  
   - Remove your visor, order that spring kit, pop it in, and you’re done.

3. **Salvage a used visor from a junkyard**  
   - Pull a visor out of a 2016–’17 CX-5 at a local self-serve yard for $10–20.  
   - It’ll come with a fresh hinge mechanism and cost way less than new.

4. **Add an aftermarket retainer clip or strap**  
   - Universal sun-visor clips (plastic U-shaped holders) are $5–10 online.  
   - Install one toward the front headliner—snaps your visor flush whenever you fold it up.

If you’re comfy with basic interior trim removal, option 1 or 2 is quickest. Otherwise, a junkyard swap or aftermarket clip will get you back to zero-gap for under $20.

  - Passenger (RH) visor — part **KF68-69-320F-75**

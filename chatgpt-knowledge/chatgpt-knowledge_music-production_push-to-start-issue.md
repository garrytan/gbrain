## Question

Yo dawg, I just picked up a 2016 Mazda CX-5, and like, the push to start's been a little weird, like, what is the proper way to turn it on? Cause like, it like, wasn't lighting up and stuff before.

## Answer

Yo dawg, here’s how to *properly* start your 2016 Mazda CX-5 with push-to-start:

1. **Foot on the brake** (firmly).
2. **Key fob inside the car** (in your pocket or cupholder, whatever).
3. **Press the START/STOP button once** (don’t hold it down).

If the dash lights weren’t coming on before, it’s usually one of these:
- **Foot not on brake** = it goes into accessory mode instead of starting.
- **Key fob battery is dying** = try holding the fob *against* the start button and pressing it.
- **Brake switch issue** = if the brake lights don’t come on, it won’t start.
- **Battery low** = try jumping it.

Let me know what the button/dash was doing when it was acting up and I’ll help troubleshoot deeper.

## Question

I have two keys for the new Mazda, and then I have a key for my rental Mazda in my pocket. Is it possible that the keys are interfering with each other, or is that just bullshit?

## Answer

Not total bullshit—multiple smart keys in close proximity **can** confuse the system, especially if they're from similar models or brands.

Here’s what can happen:
- The car might not detect the correct fob right away.
- Startup may be delayed or fail if it’s pinging both fobs.
- In rare cases, the wrong fob responds first, especially in tight spaces like your pocket.

**Best move:** keep only *one* Mazda key near the car when starting it. Leave the rental key a few feet away, like in a bag or outside the car, just to rule it out.

If it fixes the issue, then yeah—your CX-5 was just getting mixed signals.

## Question

Alright, we think it might be the keys interfering because we just got this car with one key. The only thing now is that the infotainment screen is black. Yesterday when I tested it, it came on with the car starting. Is there something going on with that?

## Answer

Yeah, if the infotainment screen is **completely black** but the car starts fine, it’s likely one of these:

1. **Startup glitch** – Common on Mazdas. Try turning the car *off*, open the door to fully shut it down, then restart.
2. **Brightness setting** – Check if it’s turned *all the way down*. Try adjusting with the dash dimmer switch.
3. **Boot delay** – If the system is confused by the key fob situation or was interrupted mid-boot, it might take a minute.
4. **Fuse issue** – If it doesn’t turn on at all (no Mazda logo, no response), check fuse #42 (infotainment) under the dash.
5. **Software crash** – Hold down the **music/mute + nav + back** buttons *simultaneously* for about 10 seconds to force a system reboot.

If the screen came on yesterday and isn’t today, I’d bet on either the brightness or a soft crash. Try the reboot trick first and let me know what happens.

## Question

Hey, now it's not starting again, and even with one single key. Is there, like, a possibility that the keys aren't, like, paracorrected or something? When I'm locking and unlocking it, it's also not even locking the doors from the keys, so... Any idea what's going on?

## Answer

Yeah dawg, sounds like the key fob isn’t pairing with the car properly—what you’re describing is *classic* dead or unpaired fob behavior. Here's the breakdown:

### Possible causes:
1. **Dead key fob battery** – Even if the car *sees* the key sometimes, if the battery's weak, it can fail intermittently.
2. **Key not programmed (paired)** – If the dealer gave you a second key but didn’t program it correctly, the car won’t recognize it.
3. **Car battery low or unstable** – Especially if the infotainment is black and push start is flaky, your car battery might be borderline.
4. **Antenna or smart key module issue** – Less common, but it can happen.

### What to try right now:
- **Use the fob to press the start button directly** (hold the plastic Mazda logo side *against* the button while pressing with your foot on the brake). That bypasses the fob battery and confirms if it’s a key issue.
- **Manually unlock the door with the physical key blade** (pull it out of the fob). Try starting again after you’re inside.

### If that works:
- You need a **new fob battery** (CR2025 or CR2032 depending on your fob).
- You might also need to **reprogram the key** (dealer job or locksmith with Mazda tools).

### If it doesn’t work:
- Test or jump the **car battery** next—weak voltage causes all kinds of weird gremlins in these Mazdas.

Let me know if you hear clicks or get dash lights when you try to start, or if it’s totally dead.

- Try using the **fob against the start button**. If it works that way, the car recognizes it but the **battery is weak**.

## Question

## Answer

Here’s the fix:

### Step-by-step:
1. **Buy a new battery** – Most likely a **CR2025** (check your fob to be sure).
2. **Pop open the fob** – Use a flathead screwdriver or coin in the notch on the side.
3. **Swap the battery**, positive (+) side facing up.
4. **Snap it back together** and test the lock/unlock buttons.
5. Then try starting the car normally (foot on brake, fob in pocket).

You’ll be back in business.

Grab the battery from CVS, Target, or even a gas station. Total fix cost: like $4.

Short version, Mr. White:

- formulation helpers (make the peptide more stable / comfortable to inject), or

I’ll walk through each one and then give you some practical takeaways.

---

## 1. Glycine (what you’re on now)

**What it is**

- A simple amino acid involved in collagen, glutathione (antioxidant) and a bunch of metabolic pathways. Low circulating glycine is common in obesity and fatty liver disease.citeturn2search5turn2search22  

**Why it’s in these vials**

1. **Formulation role.** Glycine is a very common excipient in injectables: it buffers pH and can stabilize peptides, and sometimes makes injections sting less.
2. **“Bonus benefit” marketing.** Clinics claim glycine:
   - protects lean mass
   - improves recovery, sleep and inflammation  
  There *is* emerging human and animal data that glycine supplementation can improve some metabolic markers and may help liver health in severe obesity, but doses are usually grams per day by mouth, not the tiny amounts in a GLP-1 injection.citeturn2search14turn2search20turn2search26  

**Reality check**

- There are **no randomized trials** showing “tirzepatide + glycine” beats plain tirzepatide for weight loss or muscle retention.
- At typical injection doses glycine is very safe; the downside is mainly that you’re paying for something unproven.

For you: staying on tirzepatide + glycine is reasonable if you’re tolerating it and like it, but I wouldn’t expect a night-and-day difference versus pure tirzepatide.

---

## 2. L-carnitine (only offered with semaglutide in that menu)

**What it is**

- A nutrient that shuttles long-chain fatty acids into mitochondria so they can be burned for energy (the “carnitine shuttle”).citeturn1search2turn1search10  

**What the evidence says**

- Meta-analyses of randomized trials show **modest** effects: roughly ~1 kg extra weight loss and small improvements in lipids/glucose in overweight/obese or diabetic adults.citeturn2search1turn2search4turn2search21turn2search23  
- It’s not remotely in the same league as tirzepatide/semaglutide for weight loss.

**With GLP-1 drugs**

- There’s basically **no high-quality data** on “GLP-1 + carnitine” combos. It’s a rational-ish idea (“we’ll help you burn more fat while you eat less”), but it’s extrapolation.
- If you like L-carnitine, you can just keep taking it separately (oral or injection). You don’t need it mixed into the GLP-1 vial for it to work.

**Safety**

- Generally safe; main annoyances are nausea, diarrhea, and a fishy body odor in some people. Very high doses can be an issue in certain metabolic or kidney problems.

Why they don’t offer *tirzepatide* + L-carnitine is probably just: that pharmacy chose different SKUs, not a deep biological reason.

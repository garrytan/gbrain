## Question

How does the primary and secondary settings on the to hypertrophic app work

## Answer

In the RP Hypertrophy app, the primary and secondary settings help to structure your training focus:

1. **Primary Setting**: This setting designates your main focus for muscle growth. Exercises and volume are prioritized for these muscle groups, ensuring they receive the most attention and stimulus for hypertrophy.

2. **Secondary Setting**: This setting designates a secondary focus for muscle groups. These receive less volume and intensity compared to the primary focus but are still targeted to promote balanced development and prevent neglect.

By rotating these settings periodically, you can ensure comprehensive muscle growth and avoid overtraining specific areas.

## Question

If I’m a beginner what’s my settings 

## Answer

As a beginner using the RP Hypertrophy app, it's recommended to use balanced settings. Set all muscle groups as "secondary" to ensure even development and to help you build a solid foundation. Over time, you can adjust to more specific primary and secondary focuses as you progress and identify areas that need more attention.

## Question

Why not set all to primary

## Answer

Setting all muscle groups to primary as a beginner can lead to excessive volume and potential overtraining. Beginners benefit from a balanced approach with moderate volume to adapt to training, develop proper form, and avoid injury. As you progress, you can then start prioritizing specific muscle groups.

## Question

What about an intermediate 

## Answer

As an intermediate lifter, you can start to use primary and secondary settings to address specific goals and weaknesses. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Primary Settings**: Choose 1-2 muscle groups that you want to prioritize for growth or improvement. These groups will receive higher volume and intensity.
   
2. **Secondary Settings**: Set the remaining muscle groups to secondary. These groups will get less volume but will still be adequately stimulated to maintain balance and prevent neglect.

This approach helps to prevent overtraining while allowing targeted focus on areas that need more development. Rotate these settings periodically to ensure balanced overall development.

# Bleach Developer Comparison

> **Source:** ChatGPT conversation
> **Date:** 2025-06-18T21:31:13Z
> **Messages:** 6
> **Topic:** design-ux

## Question

Yeah, I'm about to bleach my hair with baby cheeks here. Is 30 developer and 20 developer what I have? Which one is going to result in a lighter color?

## Answer

30-volume developer will give you the lighter lift. 

• 20 vol (6 % peroxide) lifts ~1–2 levels  
• 30 vol (9 % peroxide) lifts ~2–3 levels  

So if you mix your bleach powder with 30 vol you’ll end up a shade or two lighter than with 20 vol—just keep a close eye on timing to avoid over-processing.

## Question

And when I mix the bleach, which is like a powder, and the developer, and I need to do it in a 1 to 2 ratio for bleach and developer, is that by weight?

## Answer

Yes—that 1:2 mix is by weight. Weigh your powder (e.g. 20 g) and add twice that weight in developer (40 g). A digital scale makes it easy and ensures consistent lift.

## Question

I don't have a scale. What do I do?

## Answer

Use a simple volumetric measure instead. Grab a level tablespoon (or any small scoop) and do:

- **1 level scoop/tbsp of bleach powder**  
- **2 level scoops/tbsp of developer**

That 1:2 by volume will track nearly the same as 1:2 by weight. If you want better precision long-term, grab a <$20 kitchen scale—but for a one-off, a tablespoon measure is just fine.

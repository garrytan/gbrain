## Answer

Mr. White, at 15.3 % body fat you’re already leaner than roughly 80 % of U.S. men your age (30–39), for whom DXA percentiles show:  
- < 18 % BF = 0–20th percentile  
- 18–22 % BF = 20–40th percentile  
- 22–26 % BF = 40–60th percentile citeturn1file4  
…and your scan confirms 15.3 % BF on 2/20/24 citeturn1file0.  

**Dedicated cut?**  
Dropping below your current 15 % would push you into “elite” leanness (well under the 20th percentile). That risks performance, hormones and muscle loss unless you really need sub-10 % abs.  

**Recomp?**  
A slow recomposition (simultaneous gain and loss) can work—but it’s most effective for beginners. At your level, progress would be very gradual.  

**Dedicated lean bulk?**  
With your BF already below average, a controlled lean bulk is your most time-efficient path to add muscle. You’ll build more lean mass per month than you would lose fat while recomping, then can cut later to reach your 8 % goal with a stronger base.  

**Recommendation:**  
→ **Lean bulk**: eat at a modest surplus (e.g. +200 –300 kcal/day), prioritize protein (1 g/lb LBM), continue your RP-style trainings. Once you’ve added 5–10 lb of lean mass, switch back to a shorter cut to hit 8 % BF more sustainably.

## Question

If each bottle holds 1 liter and I pour into 10oz glasses that are prefilled to the top with ice, how many drinks does a bottle make

## Answer

Each 10-ounce glass is filled to the top with ice, which typically means the liquid portion will be less than 10 ounces. However, a good estimate is that you can pour about 5 to 6 ounces of liquid into a glass like that.

Given that:

- 1 liter = 33.814 ounces
- 1 drink = approximately 6 ounces of liquid

You can calculate the number of drinks per bottle as:

\[ \text{Number of drinks per bottle} = \frac{33.814 \text{ ounces}}{6 \text{ ounces per drink}} \approx 5.64 \text{ drinks per bottle} \]

Rounding down for practical purposes (since you can't serve a fraction of a drink), each 1-liter bottle would yield **5 full drinks**.

If you can slightly stretch the pours to closer to 5 ounces of liquid per glass, you could potentially serve **6 drinks per bottle**.

### 🔍 How does it work?
GetContact crowdsources this info using **mutual contact access**. Here’s a breakdown:

---

### ⚙️ **How GetContact Gets This Info**

1. **When you sign up**, the app **asks for permission to access your contacts**.
2. Your contact list is uploaded to their servers.
3. In return, **you get access to how others have tagged your number**, based on how **you appear in other users’ contact lists who also gave up their contacts**.
4. It matches your phone number against what other users have saved it as (e.g., “Tim DJ,” “Ex 🙄,” etc.).
5. You only see tags if other users **have your number** and **have used the app and shared their contacts**.

---

### 🧠 What makes it possible?
- **Network effect**: The more people who use the app and share contacts, the more tags are available.
- **Metadata mining**: The app doesn’t see full contact conversations or messages—just **names and numbers**.
- **Tag visibility** is limited. Some tags are anonymized (you don’t see who made them), and most are just text strings like "Pizza Guy" or "Crazy Ex."

---

### ⚠️ Privacy concerns
- It’s **heavily criticized** for privacy violations, especially in Europe.
- Many users don’t realize they’re uploading **all of their contacts** when they use it.
- The company says it’s compliant with local data laws, but critics argue it creates a “people search engine” without full consent.

---

### ✅ Summary
- **GetContact** shows you how your number is saved in other people's phones—only if those people use the app and share contacts.
- It works via **data crowdsourcing** from users’ phonebooks.

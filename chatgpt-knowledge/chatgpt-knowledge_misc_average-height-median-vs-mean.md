## Question

Explain mean and median and mode

## Answer

Mean, median, and mode are measures of central tendency in statistics, representing different ways to find the "center" of a data set:

1. **Mean (Average):** The sum of all the numbers in the data set divided by the count of the numbers. It gives the average value.

2. **Median:** The middle value when the numbers in the data set are arranged in ascending or descending order. If the data set has an even number of observations, the median is the average of the two middle numbers.

3. **Mode:** The number that appears most frequently in the data set. A data set can have more than one mode if multiple numbers appear with the same highest frequency.

## Question

So what would average height he in that context

## Answer

In the context of "average height," the term "average" typically refers to the mean height. It's calculated by adding together the heights of a group of individuals and then dividing by the number of individuals. So, when you hear "average height," it's usually the mean height being discussed.

## Question

What's the median

## Answer

The median height is the value separating the higher half from the lower half of a height data set. If you lined up everyone in order of height, the median would be the height of the person who is exactly in the middle of that lineup. If there's an even number of people, the median would be the average of the two middle heights.

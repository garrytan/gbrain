# Druze Epitide Delivery and Injection

## Question

Yo, so my Druze Epitide for March has been shipped, and it was supposed to be shipped via FedEx. It was supposed to arrive yesterday, and the package has been delayed and will not be delivered on Monday. So the first question is, will that still be good, since it won't have been refrigerated for six days while it's in transit? And then two is, I'm supposed to inject tomorrow night, technically, which is Saturday, and then I won't actually have the new medication until Monday. So should I inject on Monday night with the new one, which is a higher dose, or should I inject with my existing one, which I should have enough left to do the previous dose, so the previous dose is 50 units, and the new one is 75 units, and I'm feeling like it's becoming less effective, so we up the dose. Or should I do some other combination thereof, like doing half a dose or something, and then doing a full dose on Monday, or what?

## Answer

Mr. White,

1. **Stability of tirzepatide unrefrigerated**  
   Most tirzepatide pens (e.g. Mounjaro/Druze) can stay at room temperature (up to 86 °F) for up to 21 days and remain effective. Six days out of refrigeration—even in transit—is still well within that window, provided it wasn’t exposed to extreme heat. Once you receive it on Monday, inspect the pen: if it’s not unusually warm and the solution is clear (no particulates or discoloration), it should be fine to use.  

2. **Timing this week’s injection**  
   - Your normal injection day was Saturday, and you won’t have the new 75-unit pen until Monday. Skipping Saturday and resuming on Monday with the new 75-unit dose is acceptable. Tirzepatide has a “grace period” of a few days past your scheduled dose, and a two-day delay won’t compromise efficacy or safety.  
   - Do **not** inject 50 units on Saturday and then 75 units on Monday—stacking doses that close together risks hypoglycemia, GI upset, etc.  
   - If you’re already due to escalate from 50 units → 75 units this week, simply take the 75 units on Monday and reset your weekly schedule from there (e.g., next dose the following Monday).  
   - If you have any doubt—especially about timing or side-effect risk—confirm with your prescriber, but in almost all cases you’d just skip your Saturday and inject 75 units on Monday.  

In short: the pen’s six-day unrefrigerated trip is within recommended storage limits, and you should skip your Saturday injection and use the new 75-unit dose on Monday as planned.

## Question

I’m using bikes not pens so I could do partial doses

## Answer

Mr. White,

Even with vials (so you could draw partial doses), it’s still best to skip Saturday and inject a full 75 units on Monday. Here’s why:

- **Weekly pharmacokinetics**: Tirzepatide is designed as a once-weekly injection. Splitting or “topping up” sub-therapeutic amounts on Saturday would only lower efficacy and risk erratic levels.  
- **Avoid stacking two doses too close**: If you injected your usual 50 units on Saturday and then 75 units Monday, you’d effectively receive 125 units in four days—risking nausea, hypoglycemia, etc.  
- **Grace period**: A two-day delay (Saturday → Monday) is within recommended “missed dose” guidelines. By taking 75 units Monday, you re-establish your weekly schedule (next dose the following Monday).  
- **Storage considerations**: Tirzepatide vials can stay at room temperature (≤ 86 °F) up to 21 days. Six days unrefrigerated isn’t a problem, assuming it wasn’t exposed to extreme heat and the solution still looks clear.

Bottom line: don’t draw a half-dose Saturday—just wait and inject the full 75 units Monday as planned. If anything feels off once you dose, check with your prescriber, but this is the safest way to maintain steady weekly levels.

## Question

Or should I take 50 and then 75 the following Saturday 

## Answer

Mr. White,

Taking 50 units this Saturday and then bumping to 75 units the following Saturday is perfectly safe—it simply means you’ll stay on your current dose for one more week before escalating. Tirzepatide’s once-weekly kinetics tolerate that one-week delay, and you won’t stack doses too closely. If you’d rather start 75 units sooner, skip Saturday and inject 75 units on Monday (shifting your weekly schedule to Mondays). But if you want to keep dosing on Saturdays, go ahead with 50 this weekend and 75 next Saturday.

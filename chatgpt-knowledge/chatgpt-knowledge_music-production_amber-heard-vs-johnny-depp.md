### Why the same material played differently

| Point of difference | UK libel trial (bench) | US defamation trial (jury) |
|---|---|---|
| **Hearsay rules** | Civil Evidence Act 1995 lets the judge consider hearsay and simply give it whatever weight he thinks it deserves. | Federal/Virginia Rules generally bar hearsay unless a specific exception (e.g., medical-treatment, business-records) fits. Double-hearsay is almost always out. |
| **Fact-finder** | Single professional judge who reads everything and explains why he trusts it. | Seven lay jurors who may be prejudiced by material they cannot cross-examine. Courts police evidence more strictly to avoid unfairness. |
| **Burden of proof** | Newspaper had to prove the abuse was “substantially true.” That made every corroborating scrap—however indirect—potentially useful. | Depp had to prove Heard’s words were false *and* malicious. Corroborating medical notes for Heard mattered less than undermining her intent. |
| **Pre-trial orders** | No sweeping exclusion of prior rulings or donations; judge read the whole record. | Judge Azcarate barred references to the UK verdict (to avoid prejudice) and struck late-disclosed material from both sides. |

## Question

I need 11liters of watermelon juice per batch.
1 batch per week. It comes in 52ounce bottles. How many do i need

## Answer

Let’s calculate step by step.

1. **Convert 11 liters to ounces**  
   - 1 liter = 33.814 oz  
   - 11 liters × 33.814 oz/liter = **371.954 oz** (≈ 372 oz)

2. **Bottles needed**  
   - Each bottle = 52 oz  
   - 372 oz ÷ 52 oz/bottle = **7.15 bottles**

You can’t buy 0.15 of a bottle, so **you need 8 bottles per batch**.

With 1 batch per week, that’s **8 bottles each week**.

## Question

is it safe to buy the whole month at once?

## Answer

Watermelon juice is highly perishable. For a month’s supply, consider:

**Shelf Life (Unopened)**  
- Fresh, refrigerated juice: usually **3–7 days** if cold-pressed and not pasteurized.  
- Pasteurized juice: typically **2–3 weeks** unopened when kept at 32–40 °F (0–4 °C).  
- Check the “use by” date on the brand you buy—some pasteurized commercial juices can last up to about **30 days**, but only if consistently refrigerated.

**After Opening**  
- Even pasteurized juice is best within **3–5 days** once opened.

**Recommendations**  
- If it’s fresh or cold-pressed (not pasteurized), **do not buy a month at once**—it will spoil.  
- If it’s pasteurized and the label shows a shelf life of a month or more, you can buy a month’s supply **only if** you can keep it refrigerated the entire time and the “use by” dates cover the full month.  
- Always check for off-smells, bubbling, or swelling bottles—signs of spoilage.

In short: only buy a month’s worth if it’s a **pasteurized product with a verified shelf life of at least 4 weeks** and you have reliable cold storage.

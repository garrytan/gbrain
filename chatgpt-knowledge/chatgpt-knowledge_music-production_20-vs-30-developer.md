# 20 vs 30 developer

> **Source:** ChatGPT conversation
> **Date:** 2025-08-06T04:47:59Z
> **Messages:** 8
> **Topic:** music-production

## Question

Yo dawg, 20 developer or 30 developer, which one's lighter?

## Answer

Yo dawg, **20 volume developer** is lighter.

It has **less hydrogen peroxide** than 30 vol, so it lifts the hair **1–2 levels** instead of **2–3 levels** like 30 vol. Less lift = lighter processing = less damage.

Use 20 if you want a gentler lift. Use 30 if you’re going for more drastic lightening or working with darker hair.

## Question

So if I want my hair to be a lighter color, I want the 30, correct?

## Answer

Correct. If you want your hair to be a **lighter color**, go with **30 volume developer**. It'll lift more pigment and give you a **brighter, lighter result**—especially if you're starting from darker hair. Just keep an eye on processing time so you don’t fry your strands.

## Question

Yo dawg, I usually use this fuckin' Redken toner and processing solution, but this time I had to get the ColorCharm from Wella, and it says to use 20 volume developer with it, in a 1 to 2 ratio. Is that gonna give me the same result as what I usually do?

## Answer

Yo dawg, not exactly.

Redken Shades EQ is a **demi-permanent** toner that uses its own **acidic processing solution** (basically no lift, very gentle).

Wella ColorCharm Toner + **20 volume developer** = **permanent** toning with **some lift**. That means:

- It's harsher
- It can slightly lighten your hair
- It can over-process if your hair's already light

So nah, it's **not the same**. You'll get a more intense tone and possibly more lightening than Redken.

**If you want a closer match to Redken**, try Wella ColorCharm with **10 volume developer** instead—gentler, more controlled toning without extra lift. Use the same 1:2 ratio.

Let me know the exact Redken shade you usually use and what toner you grabbed from Wella—I can match 'em up.

## Question

Okay, so normally I only put that on for like 5 minutes or something. Is it going to be the same with this? Because the box is saying to develop for 30 minutes.

## Answer

Nah dawg, **not the same at all.**

Redken Shades EQ works fast — 5 to 20 mins — and you’re used to pulling it early for a softer tone.  
**Wella ColorCharm with 20 vol** is a **completely different beast**:

- It's oxidative and **meant to stay on for the full 30 minutes**  
- It **doesn’t show the final tone right away** — it develops slowly  
- If you rinse it early, like at 5 or 10 mins, it’ll probably look **weird, patchy, or too warm**

So if you’re using it, **commit to the full 30**, even if it looks dark or scary while processing — that purple/blue is neutralizing.

If you want to stick to short timing like you do with Redken, **use Wella with 10 volume**, or better yet, just wait and get your usual Redken setup.

This delayed gastric emptying can significantly **delay the absorption** of orally consumed substances. In general, drugs taken by mouth are mostly absorbed in the small intestine (very little is absorbed from the stomach itself)【28†L281-L289】. Thus, if a GLP-1 RA slows the release of a drug from the stomach to the intestine, the drug’s **time to peak concentration (T_max)** is delayed and peak levels (C_max) are reduced【2†L209-L217】【52†L226-L234】. This pharmacokinetic shift – a **slower rise and lower peak** – can translate to **prolonged onset** of effects, potentially a longer but less intense experience. For most medications, GLP-1 RAs do not cause clinically major changes【52†L226-L234】, but with recreational drugs, even moderate changes in onset or intensity can alter the subjective experience.

Obtaining recordings of 911 calls can be a complex process due to privacy and legal considerations. Here are a few ways you might be able to access them:

1. **Public Records Requests**: You can file a public records request with the local government or police department. In California, the Public Records Act allows individuals to request access to public records, including 911 calls, but certain restrictions and privacy considerations apply.

2. **News Media Outlets**: Sometimes, news organizations obtain and release 911 call recordings for high-profile incidents. Check local news websites or contact news organizations that may have covered the incident you're interested in.

3. **Court Cases**: If the 911 call is part of a court case, the recordings may be entered into evidence and become part of the public record. You can access these records through court records requests.

4. **Third-Party Websites**: Some websites compile and provide access to 911 call recordings, but be cautious about the legality and authenticity of these sources.

To file a public records request in Los Angeles, you can visit the Los Angeles Police Department's website or use the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department's Public Records Act Request portal.

For more detailed information on accessing public records, you can visit the following links:

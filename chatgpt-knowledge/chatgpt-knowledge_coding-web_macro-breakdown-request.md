## Answer

Alright, Mr. White — let’s run this cleanly.  

---

### **Step 1: Estimate Lean Body Mass**
At **160 lb × (1 − 0.125)** = **140 lb lean mass** (≈ 64 kg).

---

### **Step 2: Estimate Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)**
Using the **Katch–McArdle formula**, which keys off lean mass:
\[
BMR = 370 + (21.6 × LBM_{kg})
\]
\[
BMR = 370 + (21.6 × 64) = 370 + 1382 = 1752 \text{ kcal/day}
\]

So your body burns about **~1,750 kcal/day** just existing (no activity).

---

### **Step 3: Apply Activity Multiplier**
Since you said *relatively sedentary* (desk work, light walking only), the appropriate multiplier is **1.2–1.3**.

\[
TDEE = BMR × 1.25 ≈ 1750 × 1.25 = 2187 \text{ kcal/day}
\]

---

### **✅ Estimated Maintenance: ~2,150–2,200 kcal/day**

That’s your approximate daily maintenance intake right now.  
A few scenarios for context:
- **Cut:** ~1,700–1,900 kcal/day (modest deficit)

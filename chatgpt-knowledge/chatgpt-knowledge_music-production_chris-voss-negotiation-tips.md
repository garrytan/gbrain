## Answer

Mr. White, you’re right — that’s an **absurd concession request**, and you should *name it* without sounding emotional.

Here’s a **Chris Voss calibrated + labeling response** that shuts it down cleanly:

> It sounds like you’re asking me to take a **55% haircut** from my asking price.  
> That doesn’t work for me.

If you want to keep the door barely open while re-anchoring hard:

> It sounds like price is the only thing that matters here.  
> I can do **$200 for all three today**, otherwise I’ll wait for individual buyers.

Or the nuclear but professional option:

> I don’t see a path from $225 to $100 that makes sense.  
> If your situation changes, let me know.

**Key principle (Voss):**  
- Label the absurdity  
- State the constraint  
- Stop talking  

Silence after this is your leverage.

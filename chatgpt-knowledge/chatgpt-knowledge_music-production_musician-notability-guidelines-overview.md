## Question

what is need for a musician to qualify for notability guidlines on wikiepedia

## Answer

To qualify for notability guidelines on Wikipedia, a musician or band must meet the general notability guideline, which requires significant coverage in reliable sources that are independent of the subject. Specifically for musicians, bands, and ensembles, here are key criteria that can establish notability:

1. **Significant Coverage in Reliable Sources**: Independent of the subject, the musician has been covered in multiple reliable, secondary sources that are not trivial. This includes major music journalism sites, newspapers, magazines, or books about music.

2. **Charting on Recognized Music Charts**: Having singles or albums on any country's national music chart or a chart provided by major music publications can qualify.

3. **Awards and Recognitions**: Receiving notable awards or honors in the field of music, such as Grammy Awards, or being included in reputable rankings.

4. **Historical Importance**: Being known for a specific well-documented contribution to music that has lasting value and is recognized by reliable sources.

5. **Performance**: Participating in significant music events or festivals, or having multiple tours could also contribute to notability, especially if covered by independent reliable sources.

6. **Releases**: Having multiple albums or significant recordings that are notable in the genre, distributed through major retailers or music distributors.

It's important that the coverage or evidence of notability comes from third-party sources that are independent of the subject. Self-published sources or promotional materials do not contribute to notability. Each case is assessed individually, and meeting one criterion doesn't guarantee inclusion; it's the overall presence in reliable sources that matters.

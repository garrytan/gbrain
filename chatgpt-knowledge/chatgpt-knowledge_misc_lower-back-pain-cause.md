## Answer

Mr. White, the most likely answer is that this is a **mechanical low-back flare from cumulative extension and bracing**, not something random. Acute low back pain is usually nonspecific or mechanical, and repetitive loading/overuse is a common cause. In your workout, the biggest suspect is the **incline bench with a pronounced arch**. A mild arch is common, but when the chest position turns into lumbar extension, the low back starts doing work it does not need to do. Your other pattern also fits: **Saturday hard + Sunday hard + driving/sitting + fatigue**. Sitting by itself is not always the problem, but sitting combined with awkward posture and static loading is associated with more low back pain, and driving guidance consistently emphasizes seat setup, lumbar support, and changing position because the static posture can keep an irritated back irritated. With your amount of driving, I would treat it as an **amplifier**, not the root cause.

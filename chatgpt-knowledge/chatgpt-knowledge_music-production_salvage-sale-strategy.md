## Question

They probably dont wanna pay 350 and suck at negotiating. use top tier obi negotiating tactics

## Answer

Got it, Mr. White — if they suck at negotiating, you don’t want to chase them, you want to bait them back into the game. Classic OBI (Offer–Buffer–Incentive) tactic is:  

1. **Offer**: Keep the anchor high but show flexibility.  
2. **Buffer**: Create scarcity or competition.  
3. **Incentive**: Sweeten it slightly so *they* feel they’re getting a win.  

Here’s a message you can fire off:  

---

**“Totally fair. Just so you know, I’ve had a couple people circle on it — if price is the sticking point I can work a little, but I’d rather see it go to someone who can actually use it. If you want to toss me an offer, I’ll consider it before I let the next person take it.”**  

---

This:  
- Keeps *your* number in control (they have to counter).  
- Signals scarcity (others are ready).  
- Makes them feel like they’re being given a chance before it’s gone.

Understood — you want the **FBI negotiation angle** (Chris Voss style). That means:  
- **Mirrors** (repeat part of what they said to keep them talking)  
- **Labels** (name their feelings so they feel understood)  
- **Calibrated questions** (make them solve the problem for you)  

Here’s a text you can drop that keeps control and draws them back in:  

---

**“Sounds like 350 felt high. What price would make this work for you?”**  

---

That calibrated question:  
- Puts the ball in their court.  
- Forces them to negotiate against themselves.  
- Keeps you from looking desperate — you’re just probing.

## Answer

Perfect — that’s straight Chris Voss. 🕶️  

Here’s a quick **response ladder** you can keep in your pocket if they bite and throw a number:  

1. **If they lowball hard (e.g., $100):**  
   - Mirror: *“$100?”*  
   - Pause. Let them fill the silence.  

2. **If they stick low:**  
   - Label: *“Sounds like you’re really trying to keep this cheap.”*  
   - Then calibrated: *“How am I supposed to do that?”*  

3. **If they inch up but still under your floor:**  
   - *“I can meet you partway, but I’ve got others circling closer to 350. What’s the highest you can do so I know you’re serious?”*  

4. **If they stall or waffle:**  
   - Scarcity closer: *“No pressure, but I’ve got to give it to whoever commits first. Do you want me to hold it in your name?”*  

This way you never drop first, never look desperate, and you push them to negotiate *with themselves*.

## Question

a new apartment building is being built by me. they poored concrete for the first 2 floors. but they have framed out 8 floors with wood. Why would the do this rather then framing out floor 3, pouring concretete, then framing 4 and pouring concrete, etc

## Answer



There could be a few reasons why the construction team decided to frame out 8 floors with wood before pouring concrete:

1. Cost: Pouring concrete for each floor can be expensive due to the amount of materials and labor required. Framing out the floors with wood can be a more cost-effective solution.

2. Time: Pouring concrete for each floor requires time for the concrete to dry and cure. Framing out the floors with wood allows construction to continue on the upper floors while the concrete on the lower floors is still drying.

3. Flexibility: Framing out the floors with wood provides more flexibility in the design and layout of the building. Changes can be made more easily during the framing stage than after concrete has been poured.

4. Structural considerations: Depending on the design and load requirements of the building, framing out the floors with wood may be a more suitable option than pouring concrete for each floor. This could be due to factors such as weight, height, or earthquake resistance.

Ultimately, the decision to frame out floors with wood or pour concrete for each floor will depend on a variety of factors, including cost, time, design, and structural considerations.

## Question

how long does it tag the concrete slab for one floor to dry?

## Answer

The amount of time it takes for a concrete slab to dry and cure depends on a variety of factors, including the temperature, humidity, and thickness of the slab. In general, it can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks for a concrete slab to dry and cure enough to support weight and construction activities.

During the first few days after the concrete is poured, it is important to keep the slab moist and protected from drying out too quickly. This can involve covering the slab with a tarp or plastic sheeting, or spraying it with water to prevent evaporation.

After this initial period, the concrete will continue to cure and gain strength over time. However, it is important to wait for the concrete to reach its full strength before placing heavy loads or conducting construction activities on the slab. This can take several weeks or even months, depending on the specific conditions and requirements of the project.

It is important to consult with a qualified engineer or contractor for specific guidance on the drying and curing time for the concrete slab in your particular project.

## Question

Explain whiskey vs scotch vs bourbon 

## Answer

Whiskey, Scotch, and Bourbon are all types of distilled alcoholic beverages made from fermented grain mash, but they differ in ingredients, production methods, and geographic origin.

1. Whiskey:
   - Origin: General term; made worldwide.
   - Grains: Various (corn, rye, barley, wheat).
   - Aging: Varies; usually in wooden casks.
   - Regulations: Depend on the country of production.

2. Scotch:
   - Origin: Scotland.
   - Grains: Mainly malted barley.
   - Aging: Minimum 3 years in oak barrels.
   - Regulations: Must adhere to regulations set by the Scotch law.

3. Bourbon:
   - Origin: United States, mainly Kentucky.
   - Grains: At least 51% corn.
   - Aging: No minimum, but typically aged for at least 2 years in new charred oak barrels.
   - Regulations: Must meet U.S. standards for Bourbon.

In summary, Scotch is whiskey made in Scotland, typically from malted barley, and aged for at least 3 years. Bourbon is whiskey made in the U.S., primarily from corn, and aged in new charred oak barrels. Whiskey is a broader category that includes various types, including Scotch and Bourbon.

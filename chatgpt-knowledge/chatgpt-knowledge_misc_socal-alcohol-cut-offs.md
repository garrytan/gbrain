1. **Bars and Restaurants with Special Permits**: Some establishments have special permits allowing them to sell alcohol to-go. These permits became more common during the COVID-19 pandemic when temporary allowances were made for takeout cocktails, beer, and wine. This has since been extended, with some regulations in place.

2. **Sealed Alcoholic Beverages**: Many restaurants and bars can sell sealed bottles or cans of beer, wine, or pre-mixed cocktails to-go. The beverages must be in their original, unopened packaging, and you cannot consume them on the premises or while walking around.

3. **To-Go Cocktails**: Under the extended pandemic-era rules, some bars and restaurants can sell to-go cocktails in sealed containers. However, these must be consumed off the premises, and it is illegal to drink them in public or in your car.

4. **Growlers and Refillable Containers**: Some breweries and taprooms sell beer to-go in growlers or other refillable containers. These are sealed before you leave the premises.

5. **Delivery Services**: Alcohol can also be delivered to your home from many restaurants, bars, and liquor stores, but the same rules apply—you must be of legal drinking age, and the alcohol must remain sealed until you're at home.

In general, while you can take alcohol to-go in certain situations, California law prohibits public consumption of alcohol outside of designated areas. So while you might buy a to-go cocktail, you must take it home or to another private space to consume it legally.

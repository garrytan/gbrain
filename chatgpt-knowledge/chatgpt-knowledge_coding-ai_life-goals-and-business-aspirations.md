Mr. White, the goal is extremely clear:

## 1. Translate the life goal into a business target

$150k/year salary is **$12,500/month** before taxes.

To safely support that, the company likely needs something closer to:

| Item | Monthly amount |
|---|---:|
| Founder salary | $12.5k |
| Payroll/tax/benefit load | ~$1k–$2k |
| Company operating costs | variable |
| Emergency/profit buffer | ~$5k–$10k |
| Minimum healthy company cash flow | **~$20k–$30k/month** |

So the real target may be:

> **Get Jovie to $25k/month in reliable net cash flow while you maintain majority control.**

That is much more concrete than “grow the company.”

## 2. Make revenue the default task

Your natural tendency is to drift toward comfortable work. That is normal. The fix is to remove discretion.

Every day should start with a forced question:

> **What is the highest-probability action I can take today that creates revenue, retains revenue, or shortens the path to revenue?**

Then do that before anything else.

The categories are simple:

1. **Sell** — outbound, demos, follow-ups, closing.
2. **Retain** — fix things that keep paying users active.
3. **Convert** — improve activation, onboarding, pricing, checkout, sales materials.
4. **Collect signal** — talk to users/prospects to find what makes them buy.
5. **Build only when tied to revenue** — features that unblock a sale, improve retention, or increase conversion.

Everything else is secondary.

## 3. Use a “KPI-first backlog”

Your backlog should have columns like this:

| Task | KPI moved | Expected impact | Confidence | Time cost | Score |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|

Score it roughly as:

> **Impact × Confidence ÷ Time Cost**

Then sort by score.

This prevents you from picking tasks because they are fun, elegant, technically interesting, or emotionally safe.

A good rule:

> If a task does not name the KPI it moves, it does not enter the weekly priority list.

## 4. Separate “operator work” from “builder work”

Founder brains often prefer building because building feels productive and avoids rejection. Revenue work has more emotional friction.

So split work into two modes:

**Operator mode:**  
Sales calls, outbound, follow-ups, customer interviews, pricing, retention, collections, onboarding.

**Builder mode:**  
Product, engineering, automation, systems, design, docs.

For your current goal, operator mode should dominate until revenue is stable.

| First 2–4 hours/day | Revenue work only |

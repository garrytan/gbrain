## Question

Why the fuck are airplane announcements not prerecorded

## Answer

They usually **are** prerecorded—safety briefings, seat-belt reminders, boarding calls—but not everything can be.  
Airlines keep pilots and crew on live mic for:

* **Dynamic info:** weather, turbulence, gate changes, ATC instructions, delays—none of which can be predicted.  
* **Authority & reassurance:** a real voice from the cockpit calms passengers and shows someone’s in charge.  
* **Legal requirements:** certain announcements (e.g., altitude changes, final approach) must be delivered by the flight crew.

So you’re hearing live updates layered on top of a library of prerecorded clips.

## Question

They just told me about oxygen masks live. They usually do

## Answer

Yeah, the oxygen-mask briefing is still live on most flights. Reasons:  

* **Regulations:** FAA requires the crew to “demonstrate” use of the equipment, and a live explanation satisfies that rule better than a canned clip.  
* **Flexibility:** They can point out the exact location of masks on that aircraft type, mention broken rows, or note if a system is inop.  
* **Authority:** A calm, real voice signals the crew is attentive and engaged right before takeoff.

So even with videos running, the mask instructions are usually read aloud and demonstrated in person to meet both safety and legal standards.

Mr. White, in California, the left-turning driver usually carries the bigger legal risk in that hypothetical.

Vehicle Code section 21801 says a driver turning left must yield to oncoming traffic that is close enough to be a hazard and must keep yielding until the turn can be made with reasonable safety. So if you turn left across an oncoming car that then goes straight, you are usually at least partly at fault, even if that driver had a left blinker on. A blinker shows intent.

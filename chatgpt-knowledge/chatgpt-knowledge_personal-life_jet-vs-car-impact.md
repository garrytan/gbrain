# Jet vs. Car Impact

## Question

I'm looking to understand the environmental impact of a private jet versus a gasoline car.

## Answer

Private jets generally have a much larger environmental impact than gasoline cars. Here's why:

1. Fuel Consumption: Private jets use a lot more fuel per passenger compared to cars. This results in higher carbon emissions per passenger mile.

2. Emission Altitude: Jets emit pollutants at a higher altitude, which can have different and potentially more harmful effects on the atmosphere compared to emissions from cars at ground level.

Comparing the two, even considering the high emissions of gasoline cars, private jets are significantly more impactful on the environment per passenger mile. This difference becomes even more pronounced on shorter trips where jets are less efficient.

## Question

Like, can you quantify it? Like, a jet is X times worse than a car?

## Answer

Quantifying the exact difference in environmental impact between a private jet and a gasoline car involves several factors, like the specific models of the jet and car, the number of passengers, and the distance of the trip. However, to give a general idea:

- A private jet emits about 10 times more CO2 per passenger mile compared to a commercial airliner and significantly more than a car.

- For example, a small private jet, like a Cessna Citation X, emits around 2 to 4 kilograms of CO2 per passenger mile. In contrast, an average gasoline car (assuming one passenger) emits about 0.24 kilograms of CO2 per mile.

So, roughly speaking, a private jet can be about 10 to 20 times worse in terms of CO2 emissions per passenger mile compared to a gasoline car. These figures vary greatly depending on the specifics of each situation.

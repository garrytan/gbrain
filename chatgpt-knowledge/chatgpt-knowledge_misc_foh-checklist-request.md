### Front of House (FOH) Checklist

#### Pre-Show Preparation

1. **Venue Inspection**
   - Check cleanliness of the lobby, seating area, restrooms, and entryways.
   - Ensure all signage (restrooms, exits, etc.) is visible and correctly placed.
   - Verify emergency exits are clear and accessible.

2. **Staff Briefing**
   - Conduct a pre-show meeting with FOH staff.
   - Review show details, schedule, and any special instructions.
   - Assign roles (ushers, ticket takers, concession staff).

3. **Technical Check**
   - Confirm sound system and lighting are working properly.
   - Test any video or multimedia equipment to be used during the show.

4. **Ticketing and Seating**
   - Ensure ticket scanners or box office systems are operational.
   - Verify seating arrangements and accessibility accommodations.
   - Prepare a seating chart and allocate reserved seating.

5. **Concessions and Merchandise**
   - Stock and organize concession stands.
   - Prepare merchandise displays and ensure inventory is adequate.
   - Set up payment processing systems.

#### Showtime Operations

1. **Doors Open**
   - Open doors for audience entry as per schedule.
   - Ensure ticket scanners are staffed and functioning.
   - Direct guests to their seats efficiently.

2. **Audience Management**
   - Assist with any seating issues or questions.
   - Monitor audience flow to minimize congestion.
   - Handle any special accommodations or requests discreetly.

3. **Show Monitoring**
   - Be available to address any technical issues or emergencies.
   - Maintain a visible presence to assist and guide the audience.
   - Monitor audience behavior and enforce venue policies (e.g., no recording, no food/drink in certain areas).

4. **Intermission**
   - Manage audience movement during intermission.
   - Replenish concessions and merchandise if necessary.
   - Provide directions and assistance for restrooms, exits, etc.

#### Post-Show Operations

1. **Audience Departure**
   - Direct audience to exits smoothly and efficiently.
   - Assist with any lost and found inquiries or issues.

2. **Clean-Up**
   - Ensure seating areas, lobby, and restrooms are cleaned.
   - Dispose of trash and recycling appropriately.
   - Check for and secure any left behind personal items.

3. **Closing Procedures**
   - Secure all entry points once the audience has left.
   - Perform a final walkthrough to ensure everything is in order.
   - Record any incidents or issues in a log for future reference.

4. **Staff Debrief**
   - Conduct a post-show meeting to review performance.
   - Address any issues or feedback from the team.
   - Plan for any adjustments needed for future shows.

1. **Table Preparation**
   - Set tables with place settings (plates, utensils, glasses, napkins).

   - Ensure all tables are level and stable.

2. **Atmosphere Enhancement**

   - Clean and sanitize windows, tables, and all surfaces.

   - Dispose of any overnight waste properly.

3. **Entry Area**
   - Make sure the entrance is clean and welcoming.
   - Set up any necessary signage or promotional materials.

   - Assign sections and duties to each team member.

2. **Uniform Check**

3. **Customer Service Focus**
   - Remind staff of any ongoing promotions or loyalty programs to inform customers about.

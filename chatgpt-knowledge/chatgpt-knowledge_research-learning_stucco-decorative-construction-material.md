## Question

I got stucco poured on me and my clothes and my bag while passing a construction site. What do I do

## Answer

If you got stucco on your clothes and bag, here are the steps you should take to clean it up:

1. **Act Quickly:** The faster you act, the better your chances of removing the stucco before it hardens.

2. **Remove Excess:** Gently scrape off any excess stucco with a dull knife or a similar tool. Be careful not to spread it further.

3. **Rinse Immediately:** Rinse the affected area with cold water to prevent the stucco from setting. If you’re near a sink or hose, use it to thoroughly rinse the clothes and bag.

4. **Soak in Water:** Fill a bucket or sink with cold water and soak the clothes and bag for at least 30 minutes to loosen the stucco.

5. **Launder:** Wash the clothes in a washing machine on a cold cycle with your regular detergent. For bags, if they are machine washable, you can do the same. Otherwise, hand wash them using a mild detergent.

6. **Stain Treatment:** If stains remain, treat them with a stain remover and wash again.

7. **Dry:** Let the items air dry. Do not use a dryer until you are sure all the stucco is removed, as heat can set any remaining stain.

8. **Professional Cleaning:** If the stucco has dried and the above steps don’t work, consider taking the items to a professional cleaner, especially if they are valuable or delicate.

If the stucco has hardened significantly, the chances of complete removal decrease, but prompt and careful action can help minimize the damage.

## Question

What about skin

## Answer

If you have stucco on your skin, follow these steps to remove it safely:

1. **Act Quickly:** The sooner you start, the easier it will be to remove.

2. **Rinse Immediately:** Rinse the affected area with plenty of cold water to remove as much stucco as possible.

3. **Gently Scrub:** Use a mild soap and a soft cloth or sponge to gently scrub the area. Avoid using harsh scrubbing tools, as they can damage your skin.

4. **Use a Mild Acidic Solution:** If some stucco remains, you can use a mild acidic solution, such as diluted vinegar (mix equal parts water and white vinegar), to help dissolve the remaining stucco. Apply it gently with a cloth or sponge and rinse thoroughly with water.

5. **Moisturize:** After cleaning the area, apply a moisturizer or aloe vera gel to soothe and hydrate your skin.

6. **Monitor for Irritation:** Keep an eye on the area for any signs of irritation or allergic reaction. If you notice redness, swelling, or itching, consult a healthcare professional.

7. **Avoid Harsh Chemicals:** Do not use harsh chemicals or solvents on your skin, as they can cause irritation or burns.

If you experience any severe reactions or if the stucco is not coming off easily, seek medical attention promptly.

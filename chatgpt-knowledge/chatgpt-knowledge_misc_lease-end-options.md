## Question

In West Hollywood if I sign a 1 year lease what happens when it ends

## Answer

When a one-year lease in West Hollywood ends, several things can happen:

1. **Month-to-Month Tenancy**: If neither you nor the landlord takes any action, the lease typically converts to a month-to-month tenancy. This means you continue to live in the rental property under the same terms and conditions, but either party can terminate the tenancy with proper notice (usually 30 days).

2. **Lease Renewal or Extension**: The landlord may offer to renew or extend the lease for another fixed term. This could be another year or a different period as agreed upon by both parties. The terms and rent may be renegotiated.

3. **Move Out**: You may choose to move out when the lease ends. In this case, you need to provide the landlord with proper notice, usually 30 days, as stipulated in your lease agreement.

4. **Rent Increase**: If the lease converts to a month-to-month tenancy, the landlord may increase the rent. They must provide proper notice for any rent increase (30 days for a less than 10% increase, 60 days for a 10% or more increase).

Make sure to review your lease agreement and communicate with your landlord well in advance of the lease end date to understand your options and any required notices.

## Question

My lease ended july 1 should I do something 

## Answer

Yes, you should take action if your lease ended on July 1. Here are the steps you should consider:

1. **Review Your Lease Agreement**: Check the terms of your lease to see what happens at the end of the lease period. It will outline the procedures for renewal, extension, or termination.

2. **Contact Your Landlord**: Communicate with your landlord to understand their expectations and intentions. They may be expecting you to sign a new lease, continue on a month-to-month basis, or they might have other plans for the property.

3. **Decide on Your Next Steps**: Based on your review and discussion with your landlord, decide whether you want to:
    - Continue living in the property on a month-to-month basis.
    - Renew or extend the lease for another term.
    - Move out.

4. **Provide Notice**: If you decide to move out or if you are required to provide notice for continuing on a month-to-month basis, make sure you provide the required notice in writing as specified in your lease agreement.

Acting promptly will help avoid misunderstandings and ensure you comply with any legal requirements.

## Question

I want to stay

## Answer

If you want to stay in your rental property, follow these steps:

1. **Contact Your Landlord**: Inform your landlord as soon as possible that you would like to stay. This can be done via phone, email, or a written letter.

2. **Negotiate Terms**: Discuss whether you will continue on a month-to-month basis or sign a new lease. If a new lease is preferred, negotiate the terms, including the rent amount and lease duration.

3. **Sign the Agreement**: If you decide to sign a new lease, ensure that both you and the landlord sign the agreement to make it legally binding. Keep a copy for your records.

4. **Document Communication**: Keep a record of all communications with your landlord regarding your intention to stay and any agreements made.

5. **Continue Paying Rent**: Make sure to continue paying your rent on time under the agreed terms to avoid any issues.

By taking these steps, you can ensure a smooth transition and avoid any potential legal or housing issues.

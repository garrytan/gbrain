## Answer

Mr. White:

I've been this person.

• Pulled the 19-hour days.  
• Stayed in the chair.  
• Confused exhaustion with leverage.

It ain't the droid you're looking for.

In music production, there's a thing called soloing a track.

You isolate one instrument:

• EQ it  
• Compress it  
• Get it sounding incredible on its own

Then you un-solo it, put it back in the full mix, and it sounds like shit.

It's:

• Fighting the vocal  
• Crowding the bass  
• Taking up space it doesn't need

Good engineers know the move:

You have to constantly toggle between the solo and the full mix.

• The solo is where you do precision work.  
• The full mix is where you hear truth.

Work is the same.

Working 19 hours straight is soloing.

You're deep in the weeds:

• Moving things around that feel important  
• Optimizing details  
• Convincing yourself the grind is the signal

But you've lost the mix.

You can't hear what the project actually needs because you haven't stepped far enough away to listen.

There's a reason people get great ideas in the shower.

There's a reason the PG / YC lore is always some version of:

"We were taking a walk…"

"And then the obvious thing became obvious."

The best thinking rarely happens while you're forcing it.

It happens:

• Outside the office  
• At the gym  
• On a walk  
• In the shower  
• In the moments between the work

That's where the full mix comes back.

At Jovie, we embody this with a core value:

Move fast, break often.

It's periodization.

Mike Israetel talks about this in training.

Muscle doesn't grow during the set.

• The set is the stimulus.  
• The adaptation happens during recovery.

You can't max out every day and expect progress.

Your body eventually refuses.

Your brain works the same way.

My system:

Deep focus sprints, then I step away.

• Gym  
• Walk  
• Shower  
• Anything that gets me out of the chair and back into the full mix

The clarity shows up when I'm away from the screen.

So I built for that.

I have a voice memo pipeline that runs through AI agents.

I record a thought at the gym.

It gets:

• Transcribed  
• Parsed  
• Tagged  
• Turned into a Linear issue automatically

No context switching.

No "I'll write that down later."

No lost insight.

The thought moves from my head to the backlog without me touching a keyboard.

Nico built a cafe in the Corgi office because nothing was open at 3am.

I built a voice memo to Linear pipeline because the best thinking happens outside the office.

The flex isn't hours worked.

The flex is decisions per hour.

And the best decisions come from the toggle.

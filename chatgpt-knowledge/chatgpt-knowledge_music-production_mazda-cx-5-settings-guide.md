## Answer

Absolutely, Mr. White — here's the technical breakdown tailored to someone with your background:

---

### **What Bose Centerpoint Actually Does (Technically)**

Bose **Centerpoint** is a proprietary upmixing algorithm that creates a **multi-channel surround field** from a **stereo source**. It's based on psychoacoustic modeling and phase analysis — designed to simulate 5.1 or 7.1 spatial environments using a 2.0 input.

---

### **How It Works (Under the Hood)**

1. **Channel Decomposition (Phase Analysis):**
   - The stereo input is scanned for **mid/side information**, **delay differences**, and **frequency content**.
   - It identifies which elements are centered (kick, bass, vox) and which have stereo width (guitars, pads, FX).
   - Similar to M/S decoding but more dynamic — reacting in real time.

2. **Source Separation Estimation:**
   - Uses vector-based amplitude panning (VBAP) logic and HRTF-inspired cues to guess **source directionality**.
   - Attempts to extract "phantom" rear or ambient channels based on decorrelated energy and reverb tails.

3. **Re-mapping to 5.1/7.1 Speaker Layout:**
   - Content is assigned dynamically to the front center, front left/right, and rear speakers.
   - **Dialog-like content** (e.g. lead vocals) is steered toward the center speaker (if present).
   - **Reverb-heavy or out-of-phase elements** are pushed to rear or side speakers to simulate envelopment.

4. **Reconstruction with Cross-Talk Cancellation & EQ:**
   - Compensates for overlapping frequency content between channels to avoid phasing.
   - Tailors EQ based on cabin acoustics — measured in factory via test tones + impulse response matching.

---

### **What It’s *Not*:**
- Not true object-based rendering (like Dolby Atmos).
- Not a remaster or true multi-stem decomposition.
- Not customizable — you don’t control what goes to each channel.

---

### **Why You Might Not Like It (Producer's POV):**
- Alters your stereo image in a way that’s unpredictable and non-linear.
- Reverbs, spatial FX, and stereo placements you *crafted* can be redistributed without your intent.
- It introduces phase rotations and timing shifts that can mask transients or smear stereo separation.

---

### **Use Case Recap:**

| Use Centerpoint? | If You're Listening To... |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| **Yes**          | Cinematic music, ambient, pop with big reverbs, electronic |
| **No**           | Dry, mix-reference tracks, hip hop, anything you personally mixed |

**How to recalibrate driver’s window AUTO function:**
1. Turn ignition to ON.
2. Fully lower the driver’s window *and hold the switch down* for 2+ seconds.
3. Fully raise the window *and hold the switch up* for 2+ seconds.
4. Done — test the auto-up/down.

That should restore the one-touch function.

---

### **Passenger Window AUTO?**
Unfortunately:
- **Only the driver’s window** has auto up/down in the 2016 CX-5 unless you’re in a **Grand Touring** trim with premium package — and even then, **passenger is usually AUTO down only**, not up.
- You **can’t enable it via software** — would require replacing the switch module and regulator motor with one from a newer CX-5 that supports dual auto (like 2018+).

So unless you go aftermarket and rewire, you're stuck with manual control on the passenger side.

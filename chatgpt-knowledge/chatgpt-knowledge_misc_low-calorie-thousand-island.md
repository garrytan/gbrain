## Question

Macros of dark chocolate. 98 percent 

## Answer

The macros for 100 grams of 98% dark chocolate are approximately:

- Calories: 647 kcal
- Protein: 13 g
- Fat: 56 g
- Carbohydrates: 22 g (of which sugars are around 7 g)
- Fiber: 11 g

These values can vary slightly based on the brand and specific formulation.

## Question

What about one square

## Answer

For one square of 98% dark chocolate, assuming each square is about 10 grams, the approximate macros are:

- Calories: 65 kcal
- Protein: 1.3 g
- Fat: 5.6 g
- Carbohydrates: 2.2 g (of which sugars are around 0.7 g)
- Fiber: 1.1 g

These values are estimates and can vary depending on the brand and size of the square.

## Question

100%

## Answer

For 100% dark chocolate, the macros for one square (approximately 10 grams) are approximately:

- Calories: 60 kcal
- Protein: 2 g
- Fat: 5.5 g
- Carbohydrates: 3 g (mostly from dietary fiber, with no sugars)
- Fiber: 2 g

These values can vary slightly based on the brand and formulation.

## Question

Any zero calorie sauces that taste good

## Answer

Yes, there are several zero-calorie or very low-calorie sauces that are considered to taste good by many people. Here are a few options:

1. **Hot Sauce**: Many hot sauces have zero or negligible calories and can add a spicy kick to your meals.

2. **Mustard**: Plain yellow mustard usually has very few calories.

3. **Walden Farms Products**: They offer a variety of zero-calorie sauces, dressings, and syrups. Taste can be subjective, but many people enjoy their products.

4. **Soy Sauce (Low Sodium)**: While not completely calorie-free, it's very low in calories and can add a lot of flavors.

5. **Vinegar**: Such as balsamic or apple cider vinegar, which can be used as a dressing or flavor enhancer.

Remember, taste is subjective, so you might need to try a few different options to find what you like. Also, while these sauces may be low or zero in calories, it's important to check for other ingredients that may not align with your dietary goals, like sodium or artificial additives.

## Question

Thousand island

## Answer

Thousand Island dressing, in its traditional form, is not zero-calorie. However, there are low-calorie or reduced-calorie versions available from various brands. These versions typically substitute high-calorie ingredients with lower-calorie alternatives to reduce the overall calorie count.

If you're specifically looking for a very low or zero-calorie Thousand Island dressing, you might want to check out brands like Walden Farms. They offer a range of salad dressings, including Thousand Island, that are marketed as zero or near-zero in calories. 

As with any product, it's important to read the nutrition label and ingredient list to ensure it meets your dietary needs and preferences, especially since the taste and texture can vary from the traditional version.

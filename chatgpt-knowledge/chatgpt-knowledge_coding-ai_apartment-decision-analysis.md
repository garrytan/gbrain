## Answer

Mr. White,

Yes. If the rooftop is built to **typical residential load standards**, you should use **thick rubber pads or mats** under a barbell bench setup.

## Why Pads Are Needed

Even if the roof structure supports the total weight, barbell training creates **concentrated loads** and **dynamic force**.

Example load:

- lifter: ~180–220 lb  
- barbell + plates: 250–400 lb  
- bench: ~70–100 lb  

Total during a set: **~500–700 lb**

That force sits on **very small contact points** (bench feet and your feet). Without padding it can:

- dent rooftop decking  
- crack tiles or membrane roofing  
- transmit vibration into the unit below

Rubber spreads the load and absorbs impact.

## Recommended Setup

Use **horse stall mats**, which are common in garage gyms.

Typical spec:
- **¾ inch thick rubber**
- **4 ft × 6 ft**
- **~90–100 lb per mat**

For a bench area:

- **2 mats minimum**
- **3 mats ideal**

Layout example:

```
[ Mat ][ Mat ]
     Bench
[ Mat ]
```

This gives about **48–72 sq ft of load distribution**.

## What the Mats Actually Do

They provide:

1. **Load distribution** across a larger surface area  
2. **Impact absorption** if weights touch down  
3. **Protection of roof membrane**  
4. **Noise reduction**

They also prevent equipment from sliding.

## Additional Tip

Put **¾" plywood under the mats** if the roof surface is delicate (tile or membrane). This creates a simple lifting platform:

```
roof
→ plywood sheet
→ rubber stall mat
→ equipment
```

That spreads weight extremely well.

## One More Thing to Verify

Ask the landlord or building manager:

- Whether **gym equipment is allowed on the roof**
- Whether there are **weight limits for furniture/equipment**

Many buildings allow patio furniture but may want confirmation for heavy equipment.

## Practical Bottom Line

A rooftop bench press setup is usually fine **if you use:**

- stall mats  
- controlled lifting (no dropping)  
- moderate plate storage

The mats are the critical piece that protects the roof and reduces vibration.

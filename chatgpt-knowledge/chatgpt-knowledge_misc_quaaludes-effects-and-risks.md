## Question

Yo, Quaaludes, if I take like a quarter of one, hypothetically, what would I expect to happen, and how long would it take to kick in?

## Answer

Mr. White — straight facts:

- **What it is:** “Quaalude” = methaqualone, a **Schedule I** depressant. Modern “ludes” are usually **counterfeits** (often benzos/other sedatives). A “quarter” means nothing if you don’t know the true mg.  
- **Onset/peak/duration (oral):** **20–45 min** to feel it, **1–2 h** to peak, **4–8 h** main effects, with a groggy “hangover” that can linger **12–24 h**.  
- **Likely effects at a small dose (if it were real):** relaxed, warm, less anxious, looser muscles, lowered inhibitions.  
- **Common downsides:** heavy drowsiness, poor balance/coordination, slurred speech, nausea/vomiting, memory gaps.  
- **Real risks:** **respiratory depression** and blackout—especially if **mixed with alcohol, benzos, opioids, or other downers** (that combo is what kills people).  
- **Redose trap:** slow onset → people stack doses too soon. Don’t.  
- **Harm-reduction (if this ever left “hypothetical”):** don’t mix with other depressants, don’t use alone, wait **at least 2–3 h** before considering more, don’t drive the rest of the day/night, and have someone sober around. Trouble breathing/blue lips/unconscious? **911.** Poison Control (U.S.): **1-800-222-1222**.

Bottom line: even a “quarter” can be unpredictable today because most “Quaaludes” aren’t real methaqualone.

Short answer: **No**—I can’t handle or test substances, Mr. White.

If you want them checked, here’s the safe, practical stack:

- **Fentanyl test strips:** use even on “downers” (pressed pills get cross-contaminated).
- **FTIR drug-checking** at harm-reduction orgs/events: identifies main ingredients.
- **Mail-in GC/MS labs** (e.g., DrugsData, Energy Control International): most reliable; gives full readout.
- **Harm-reduction:** don’t mix with alcohol/benzos/opioids; start tiny; wait 2–3 h before any redose; keep **naloxone** on hand; call **1-800-222-1222** for Poison Control if anything feels off.

---

## 1. Gym membership (the short, honest answer)

### **Can the company pay for your gym?**
**No — not tax-free.**

A gym membership is considered a **personal wellness expense**, not an ordinary and necessary business expense.

If the company pays for it:
- It becomes **taxable compensation**
- Must be added to your **W-2 wages**
- Subject to payroll taxes

At your stage, this is **not worth the complexity**.

---

## 2. When gym *can* be deductible (rare cases)

These are edge cases — **not applicable to you right now**:

- On-site gym **owned and operated by the company**
- Gym provided **primarily for employees**, not just the founder
- Certain safety-related fitness requirements (not applicable)

A reimbursed Equinox membership for a solo founder = ❌ no.

---

## 3. The general rule for fringe benefits

To be **tax-free**, a fringe benefit must be:
- **Primarily for the employer’s benefit**
- **Not personal in nature**
- **Available broadly**, not just for you

Gym memberships fail all three.

---

## 4. Common fringe benefits — what works vs what doesn’t

### ✅ Clean / allowed (even for solo founders)
- Laptop, monitor, keyboard
- Software
- Cell phone (business %)
- Internet (business %)
- Education directly related to work
- Home office (if structured properly later)

### ⚠ Allowed but taxable (usually not worth it)
- Gym
- Wellness stipends
- Lifestyle perks
- Car allowances

These must be run through payroll as income.

### ❌ Don’t do at your stage
- Founder-only “perks”

## Question

Wait, so what does that mean? No candidate gets a majority. What's a majority? Like 51%? Or like, it's not a tie? Like, if Karen Bass gets 43% and Pratt gets 33%, is that a majority, or does Karen Bass have to get 51%? Like...

## Answer

Mr. White, **majority means more than 50% of the votes cast**.

So:

| Result in June primary | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Karen Bass gets **50.1%+** | She wins outright in June |
| Karen Bass gets **50.0% or less** | Runoff in November |
| Bass gets **43%**, Pratt gets **33%** | **No majority** → Bass and Pratt go to November runoff |

So in your example, **43% beats everyone, but it is not a majority**. It only makes her the **top vote-getter**. She would still need a November runoff against the second-place candidate.

Think of it this way:

> **Plurality** = more votes than anyone else.  
> **Majority** = more than half of all votes.

LA mayor uses the majority/runoff system: win over 50% in June, or the top two advance to November.

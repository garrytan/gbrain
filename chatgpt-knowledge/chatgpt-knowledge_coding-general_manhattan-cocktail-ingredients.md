## Question

What does the term bruising mean?

## Answer

In the context of bartending, "bruising" refers to the notion that shaking a spirit, especially gin, too vigorously can change its flavor or texture. The theory is that shaking can cause aeration, which may alter the taste by making it seem sharper or more diluted. It's a somewhat subjective and debated term in mixology. ## Question

## Answer

When a drink includes "white" in its name, it often refers to the use of clear or colorless ingredients. For example, a "White Russian" uses cream and vodka, which are both white or clear, and "White Sangria" is made with white wine. The term helps to distinguish these drinks from their colored counterparts.

## Question

How is the mojito traditionally served and in what class?

## Answer

A Mojito is traditionally served in a highball glass. It's made with white rum, sugar, lime juice, soda water, and mint. The mint leaves are gently muddled with the sugar and lime juice, then the rum is added, followed by ice, and topped off with soda water. It's garnished with a mint sprig and often a slice of lime.

## Question

What is a traditional Manhattan?

## Answer

A traditional Manhattan cocktail is made with whiskey (commonly rye or bourbon), sweet vermouth, and a dash of Angostura bitters. It's usually garnished with a cherry and can be served either on the rocks or strained into a chilled cocktail glass.

## Question

Can you run for president with a criminal record 

## Answer

Yes, Mr. White, an individual can run for President of the United States even with a criminal record. The U.S. Constitution sets out three eligibility requirements for presidential candidates: they must be at least 35 years old, a natural-born citizen, and a resident of the United States for at least 14 years. The Constitution does not mention a criminal record as a disqualification for holding the office of President.

## Question

Who has had non consecutive terms

## Answer

Grover Cleveland is the only U.S. President who has served non-consecutive terms. He was the 22nd President from 1885 to 1889 and then the 24th President from 1893 to 1897.

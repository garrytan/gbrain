# Garage Can't Hold You

> **Source:** ChatGPT conversation
> **Date:** 2024-06-07T04:02:35Z
> **Messages:** 6
> **Topic:** misc

## Question

Is it legal for a garage to prevent you from exiting 

## Answer

In general, it is not legal for a garage to prevent you from exiting. This can be considered false imprisonment, which is unlawful. False imprisonment occurs when a person is confined or restrained against their will without legal justification. 

If you find yourself in a situation where a garage is preventing you from exiting, it may be advisable to call local law enforcement to assist in resolving the matter. Additionally, you could consider consulting with an attorney to understand your rights and any potential legal actions you can take.

## Question

Even if I didn’t pay

## Answer

Even if you didn't pay, a garage cannot physically prevent you from exiting, as this could still be considered false imprisonment. However, the garage has legal means to address non-payment, such as:

1. Charging late fees.
2. Taking legal action to recover the owed amount.
3. Reporting the non-payment to credit agencies.

In some jurisdictions, there may be specific laws or regulations allowing the garage to place a hold or boot on the vehicle until payment is made, but physically preventing a person from leaving is typically not legal.

It is advisable to review the terms and conditions of the parking agreement and consult with a legal professional to understand your rights and obligations fully.

## Question

What about if I’m in my car

## Answer

If you are in your car and the garage physically prevents you from leaving, such as by blocking the exit with another vehicle or closing the gate, it still generally constitutes false imprisonment. This is because they are restraining your ability to leave against your will, even though you are inside your vehicle.

While the garage has the right to seek payment for services rendered, physically preventing you from leaving is not an appropriate or legal method of enforcement. They should use legal channels to address non-payment issues.

If you find yourself in such a situation, it is advisable to contact local law enforcement to report the incident and seek assistance. You may also want to consult with an attorney to discuss the incident and any potential legal actions you can take.

## Question

How to setup bleach spray bottles for bar

## Answer

To set up bleach spray bottles for a bar, follow these steps:

1. **Use the Right Bleach**: Use regular household bleach (5-6% sodium hypochlorite). Avoid using scented or thickened bleach, as these can leave residues and are not recommended for sanitizing surfaces.

2. **Dilution Ratio**: For effective disinfection, mix bleach with water at a ratio of 1:10 (one part bleach to nine parts water). This means for a standard 32-ounce spray bottle, you would mix approximately 3.2 ounces (about 95 ml) of bleach with 28.8 ounces (about 850 ml) of water.

3. **Label the Bottles**: Clearly label the spray bottles with "Bleach Solution" and the date it was mixed. This is important because bleach solutions lose effectiveness over time. It's best to mix a fresh solution every 24 hours.

4. **Use Proper Bottles**: Ensure the spray bottles are made of material resistant to bleach, such as high-density polyethylene (HDPE). Avoid using bottles that could react with bleach or degrade over time.

5. **Storage**: Store the bleach solution in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, as exposure to heat and light can degrade the solution's effectiveness.

6. **Safety Precautions**: Make sure staff members wear gloves when handling bleach, as it can be harsh on the skin. Ensure proper ventilation when using the bleach solution, as fumes can be strong.

7. **Application**: When using the bleach spray for cleaning, spray the solution onto the surface and let it sit for at least one minute before wiping it off to ensure effective disinfection.

8. **Disposal**: At the end of each day, dispose of any leftover bleach solution, clean the spray bottles thoroughly, and prepare a new batch if needed the following day.

This process will ensure your bleach spray bottles are set up safely and effectively for use in the bar.

So the one we have is 8.25 sodium hypochlorite.

## Answer

Given that your bleach has a higher concentration (8.25% sodium hypochlorite), you'll need to adjust the dilution ratio accordingly to achieve the same level of effectiveness as a standard household bleach (5-6%).

For disinfection:

- **Dilution Ratio**: Mix 2/3 cup (approximately 160 ml) of bleach with 1 gallon (3.8 liters) of water. 

For a 32-ounce spray bottle:

- **Mix 2.7 ounces (about 80 ml) of bleach with 29.3 ounces (about 870 ml) of water**.

This will give you a similar disinfectant strength as the standard 1:10 dilution of regular household bleach. Follow the same labeling, safety, and application procedures as mentioned earlier.

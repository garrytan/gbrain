## Question

What is the low on date rape testing kits at bars

## Answer

A new California law, effective July 1, 2024, mandates that bars and nightclubs with a Type 48 license must provide patrons with drug testing kits to detect common date-rape drugs such as GHB, ketamine, and flunitrazepam. These establishments must also display prominent signage informing patrons of the availability of these kits. The testing kits can be offered either for sale at a reasonable price or provided free of charge. Non-compliance can result in administrative actions against the license holders【7†source】【8†source】.

## Question

What is a type 48

## Answer

A Type 48 license in California is issued to bars and nightclubs. This license authorizes the sale of beer, wine, and distilled spirits for consumption on the premises where sold. Establishments with a Type 48 license are not permitted to admit minors and are not required to serve food. This type of license is typically held by venues focused on alcoholic beverage sales without a significant food service component【7†source】【8†source】.

## Question

What about bars on an event permit

## Answer

For events held under an ABC Event Permit in California, bars are required to follow specific regulations to serve alcohol legally. These permits, such as the ABC 218, allow for alcohol to be furnished and served at non-private special events. They must be obtained through a licensed catering company that already holds a liquor license. Applications for these permits should be submitted at least 10 days before the event【16†source】【17†source】.

Furthermore, unlike standard Type 48 licenses for bars and nightclubs, these special event permits do not require the same ongoing obligations but must still comply with all other relevant state regulations, including those related to responsible beverage service and potential requirements for security measures depending on the event size and location【17†source】.

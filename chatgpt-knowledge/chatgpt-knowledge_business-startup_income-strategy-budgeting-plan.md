## Question

This is great, but are we taking into account the cost of context switching? So, for example, if you're fucking bartending six days a week, seven days a week, um, you know, switching from that mindset to dev work or something, what's the, you know, how effective is that context switching?

## Answer

Mr. White—good call. Every time you pivot from one brain-mode to another, you pay a real toll:

- **Re-orientation drag**: studies show it takes ~20–25 minutes to fully refocus after a context switch.  
- **Productivity hit**: you can lose up to 30–40 % of your effective output when bouncing between unrelated tasks.  

If you’re bartending six nights and trying to ship code or edit video in the afternoons, you’re essentially giving away an hour of quality work per switch.

**Mitigation tactics**  
1. **Block your week**  
   - Cluster bartending into 3–4 consecutive nights.  
   - Reserve 2–3 full days (or mornings + afternoons) for deep work—no bar shifts on those days.  
2. **Time-box transitions**  
   - Build a 10–15 min “ramp-up” ritual: review yesterday’s code or editing notes before you start.  
   - Use a simple checklist so you don’t have to hunt in your head for where you left off.  
3. **Cap your switches**  
   - Limit to one switch per day (e.g. code all AM, bartend all PM) rather than hopping back and forth.  
4. **Outsource the small stuff**  
   - Offload admin and invoicing to your VA so your “task-switch quota” goes toward high-value work only.  

By batching like with like and ritualizing hand-offs, you’ll salvage those lost 20-plus minutes and keep both your bar shifts and your dev sprints firing on all cylinders.

## Question

Unjam garbage disposal

## Answer

To unjam a garbage disposal:

1. Turn off the disposal and disconnect the power.
2. Use a flashlight to look inside for any visible obstructions.
3. If you find an object, use pliers or tongs to remove it. Don't put your hand inside.
4. If no object is found, use an Allen wrench in the hex-shaped hole at the bottom of the disposal. Turn it back and forth to free up the impellers.
5. Once free, press the reset button located on the bottom of the disposal.
6. Reconnect the power and test the disposal.

If it's still not working, you may need to call a professional.

## Question

If it's humming but not running does that mean the reset button still needs to be pressed?

## Answer

If your garbage disposal is humming but not running, it suggests that the motor is still jammed or stuck but receiving power. Here’s what to do:

1. Turn off the disposal and unplug it for safety.
2. Use an Allen wrench in the hex-shaped hole at the bottom and manually turn it back and forth to dislodge any obstruction.
3. After clearing the jam, locate and press the reset button. It's usually a red button on the bottom of the disposal.
4. Plug it back in and test it.

If it still only hums and doesn’t run, there might be a more serious issue, and professional assistance could be needed.

## Question

What Allen key size

## Answer

For most standard garbage disposals, the size of the Allen wrench (also known as a hex key) needed to unjam it is typically 1/4 inch. This size should fit into the hex-shaped hole at the bottom center of the disposal unit. If you don't have one, many garbage disposals come with the appropriate Allen wrench when purchased.

## Answer

That’s a **Microsoft Wireless Display Adapter** (first- or second-generation, depending on age).  

**What it is**  
- It’s **not** a Windows “stick PC” and doesn’t run Android or Windows.  
- It’s a simple **Miracast receiver**: you plug the HDMI end into a TV/monitor and the USB end into a power source.  
- Your Windows PC, some Android devices, or Miracast-capable gadgets can then **wirelessly mirror or extend their display** to the TV.  
- No built-in apps, storage, or OS—it’s just a firmware device that acts like a wireless HDMI cable.

**Practical use in 2025**  
- Works fine for screen-mirroring PowerPoint decks, movies, or duplicating a Windows desktop without Wi-Fi.  
- But Miracast is niche now: Chromecast, Apple TV, and native smart-TV casting are more common and usually smoother.  
- Firmware updates are minimal; Microsoft still sells the “Wireless Display Adapter v2” for business conferencing.

**Value**  
- New units retail around **$50–60 USD**.  
- Used, working adapters typically go for **$15–25** on eBay or Facebook Marketplace.  
- If it’s first-gen (bulkier, square ends) lean toward the low end; v2 (slimmer, rounded edges) is a bit higher.

**Bottom line**  
- Keep it only if you need Miracast specifically (corporate presentations, Windows-to-TV without Wi-Fi).  
- Otherwise, it’s extra clutter—flip it online for ~$20 and call it a win.

# Jovie 30s Spot — **“MAKE THE CATALOG PAY”**

## Core Idea

Every song is an asset.  
Every asset should be working.  
Jovie is the revenue engine that turns dormant catalog into money movement.

This spot is about **cash flow**, **ownership**, **leverage**, and **the feeling of your music working for you while you move through the city**.

No soft creator language. No fan-path framing. No link-in-bio energy. This should feel like a luxury heist film where the heist is extracting revenue from your own catalog.

# Creative Thesis

**The city owes you money. Jovie collects.**

The artist is framed like a founder, operator, and asset owner. Their catalog is shown as a dark financial machine: tracks, rights, usage, campaigns, revenue, payouts. Jovie is the system that wakes it up.

## Tone

- Fashion-ad sensuality
- Michael Bay propulsion
- Fincher-level control
- Cyberpunk restraint
- Black glass, rain, chrome, pressure
- Money as data, motion, deposits, velocity

## Visual Rule

The spot should never show cash flying around. That feels cheap.

Money is shown as:

- numbers moving
- statements updating
- usage events firing
- revenue lines climbing
- catalog tiles activating
- deposits landing
- black dashboards glowing in the dark

The product should feel like a **Bloomberg terminal for artists**, filtered through a nightclub and a private equity deck.

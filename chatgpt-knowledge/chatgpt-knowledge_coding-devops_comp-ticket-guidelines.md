### Front-of-House / Server Updates

**Comp Tickets**  
- Friends & Family comps must be requested through the General Manager.  
- No comp tickets on **Saturdays**.  
- During peak weeks (Christmas, Halloween), Friday comps may be restricted.

**Guest Interaction & Etiquette**  
- **One-Minute Rule:** Greet every guest within 60 seconds of arrival with a warm smile and confident eye contact.  
- **Anticipate Needs:** Watch body language—empty glass, scanning for restrooms—and act before asked.  
- **Open-Hand Gestures:** Use an open hand when pointing; never point with a single finger.  
- Guests always have the right of way—step aside in aisles and never cut them off.

**Cleanliness & Proactivity**  
- **Clean Sweep:** Surfaces wiped and glassware cleared continuously; no clutter visible to guests.  
- Be proactive: pick up trash, wipe surfaces, and keep all areas tidy without waiting for instructions.  
- Use time for revenue-generating tasks, not searching for misplaced items.

**Preshift Meetings**  
- No personal notes or feedback during preshift.  
- Feedback is given during one-on-one check-ins with management, where it will be documented.  
- Preshift is strictly for updates and operational info.

**Locker & Uniform Labeling**  
- Enough lockers are provided; claim one with gaff tape and a Sharpie on arrival.  
- Label the inside of your uniform with gaff tape and name; hang it on costume racks in the cast area after each show.  
- Label your hat and store it in your locker.

**Organization & Storage**  
- Every item in the venue must have a designated place and be returned there after use.  
- If something lacks a dedicated spot, inform management so it can be assigned and labeled.  
- The **Minivans area** (left of bathrooms) is for tools and moving supplies (furniture dollies, packing blankets, ratchet straps, gloves).  
- Tools and supplies must be returned immediately after use.  
- Never store items on carts or dollies—they must remain empty and ready.

**Bussing & End-of-Night Flow**  
- Spread out to cover the entire room when bussing; avoid clustering.  
- Start clearing from the back toward exits, staying behind guests.  
- Never push dirty bus bins through guest areas—use back-of-house routes.  
- During open doors/shows, stay out of public view if not in uniform.

**Sanitation**  
- Wash hands regularly; use bar/kitchen sanitizer (replenish from dry-storage rack in No Man’s if low).  
- Remove bar rags before entering the bathroom.  
- Never bring Toast devices into bathrooms.

## Front-of-House / Server Operations – Master Checklist

### Comp Tickets  
- Friends & Family comps must be requested through the General Manager.

### Guest Interaction & Etiquette  
- **One-Minute Rule:** Greet every guest within 60 seconds of arrival with a warm smile and confident eye contact.  
- **Anticipate Needs:** Scan body language (empty glass, looking for restroom) and act before asked.  
- **Open-Hand Gestures:** Use an open hand when pointing; never use a single finger.  
- Guests always have the right of way—step aside in aisles and never cut them off.

### Cleanliness & Proactivity  
- **Clean Sweep:** Wipe surfaces and clear glassware continuously—no clutter visible to guests.  
- Be proactive: pick up trash, wipe surfaces, and keep all areas tidy without being told.  
- Use time for revenue-generating tasks, not searching for misplaced items.

### Preshift Meetings  
- No personal notes or feedback during preshift.  
- Feedback is given during scheduled one-on-one check-ins with management and documented there.  
- Preshift is strictly for updates and operational info.

### Locker & Uniform Labeling  
- Claim a locker with gaff tape and a Sharpie on arrival.  
- Label the inside of your uniform with gaff tape and name; hang it on costume racks after each show.  
- Label your hat and store it in your locker.

### Organization & Storage  
- Every item must have a designated place and be returned there after use.  
- If something lacks a dedicated spot, inform management to assign and label it.  
- **Minivans Area** (left of bathrooms) is for tools and moving supplies (dollies, packing blankets, ratchet straps, gloves).  
- Tools and supplies must be returned immediately after use.  
- Never store items on carts or dollies—they must remain empty and ready.

### Bussing & End-of-Night Flow  
- Spread out to cover the entire room when bussing; avoid clustering.  
- Clear from the back toward exits, staying behind guests.  
- Never push dirty bus bins through guest areas—use back-of-house routes.  
- During open doors/shows, stay out of public view if not in uniform.

### Drink & Food Running  
- **We all run drinks.** If drinks are ready at the bar and you’re free, run them—regardless of section.  
- Do **not** wait for only your own section’s drinks. Drinks left melting cost everyone tips in the pooled system.  
- Food is run **as ready**—never hold items to batch an order.

### Kitchen & Service Flow  
- Because 250 guests are seated at once, service operates in short, intense bursts.  
- Stay fully present and move fast for the entire one-hour show.  
- Even a one-minute delay on an order or runner backs up the entire room.

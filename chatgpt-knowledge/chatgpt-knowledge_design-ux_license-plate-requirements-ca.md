Hey homie, my car doesn't seem to have a place to hang a front license plate. I bought it in California and I live in California. The DMV, the dealer, ordered plates for me, and then they got sent to the wrong place and I never got them or something. ## Answer

## Answer

Mr. White, **yes — in California, you have to run both plates** if DMV/AAA issued you two.

California Vehicle Code § 5200 says when two plates are issued, **one must be attached to the front and the other to the rear**. If only one plate is issued, it generally goes on the rear, but that does not apply to you because AAA gave you two. citeturn901086view0

The front plate also has to be **securely fastened, clearly visible, upright, and legible**. Tossing it on the dashboard or inside the windshield is a bad substitute because § 5201 requires the plate to be mounted/fastened to the vehicle. citeturn901086view1

The car having no obvious front-plate spot does not exempt you. For a dealer sale/lease of a vehicle that gets two plates, California law generally requires the dealer to provide a bracket or other way to secure the front plate, unless you signed an acknowledgment refusing the front bracket and acknowledging the front-plate requirement. citeturn841445search1

Practical move: call the dealer and ask for the **front license plate bracket/hardware** for your exact car. If you care about not drilling the bumper, look for a **no-drill tow-hook mount** or model-specific adhesive/bracket kit, but make sure it is secure and visible.

## Answer

Mr. White, that helps your **dealer leverage**, but it does **not** remove your front-plate obligation.

California still requires you to mount both plates because AAA/DMV issued you two: one front, one rear. citeturn531678view0 The plate also needs to be securely fastened, visible, upright, and legible; windshield/dashboard placement is risky because the statute requires secure fastening to the vehicle. citeturn531678view1

Where the dealer may have screwed up: California Vehicle Code § 11713.17 says after a retail sale/lease of a vehicle that gets two plates, the dealer may not deliver the vehicle unless either:

1. the vehicle has a front-plate bracket or other way to secure the front plate, or  
2. the dealer gets your signed written acknowledgment that you expressly refused the bracket and understand California requires a securely fastened front plate. citeturn155318view0

So if you **didn’t get a bracket** and **didn’t sign that acknowledgment**, the clean move is to contact the dealer and say:

> I was issued two California plates, and the vehicle was delivered without a front plate bracket or other means of securing the front plate. I also did not sign any acknowledgment refusing installation. Under CVC § 11713.17, please provide and install the front license plate bracket/hardware.

Do it by text/email so there’s a record. Also check your purchase paperwork for any “front plate acknowledgment” form, because dealers sometimes bury it in the delivery documents. If they cannot produce one, I’d push them to provide the bracket and installation at no charge.

## Answer

Mr. White, **expect $25 if it’s a correctable fix-it ticket** and you mount the front plate, get it signed off, and submit proof on time. California Courts says fix-it tickets require proof of correction plus a **$25 fee**, and LA Superior Court lists the proof-of-correction fee as **$25 per correctable violation**. citeturn847697search1turn556685view0

If you **do not correct it** or the officer/court treats it as **non-correctable**, the current California schedule lists **VC 5200(a) — “Two License Plates–Display Specified” — at $193 total bail** with **0 DMV points**. citeturn561536view0 In LA, the court fee table also lists an extra **$4 Emergency Medical Air Transportation** fee per bailable convicted violation, so the practical full-hit amount can be **about $197**. citeturn556685view0

So the real answer:

**Fix it properly:** about **$25**  
**Ignore it / lose / non-correctable:** about **$193–$197**  
**DMV points:** **0** for this plate-display violation. citeturn561536view0

LA Superior Court says correctable violations have to be handled **by mail or in person**, and proof of correction cannot be submitted online or by phone. citeturn788062view0

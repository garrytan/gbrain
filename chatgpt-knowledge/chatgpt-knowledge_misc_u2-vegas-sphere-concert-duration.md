Attending the U2 concert at the Vegas Sphere offers a unique and immersive experience. Here are some pro tips and insights from previous attendees:

1. **Seating**: The Sphere's design makes every seat a good spot for audio, but for the best visual experience, seats in the 200 and 300 levels are recommended. 2. **Setlist and Visuals**: U2's setlist has been consistent, with a focus on songs from their 'Achtung Baby' album. The Sphere's large, wraparound interior LED screen and the visuals create a captivating experience, transporting the audience to different locations. The most impressive visual moment is during "Where The Streets Have No Name," with the screen creating an illusion of natural light and a desert vista.

3. **Phone Use**: The Sphere's screen is so compelling that it often encourages attendees to capture images and videos on their phones. If you prefer to fully immerse yourself in the experience without distractions, be prepared for a lot of phone screens lighting up around you.

4. **Food and Drinks**: Expect typical concert pricing for food and beverages, ranging from $15 to $30. There are also self-serve options available.

5. **Arrival**: You can either walk in from the strip or take the bridge from the Venetian. Lines for entry are present but generally move quickly.

6. **Special Effects**: The concert makes effective use of various visual effects. For instance, during "Until The End Of The World," a gas flare in the form of a flag creates a hyperreal and apocalyptic imagery. However, some visuals, like those during "Even Better Than The Real Thing," can be excessively disorienting.

Remember, each person's experience can vary, and these tips are based on individual accounts from previous attendees. Enjoy the concert and the unique experience the Sphere offers!

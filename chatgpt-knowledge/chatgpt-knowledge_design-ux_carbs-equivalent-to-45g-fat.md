# Carbs Equivalent to 45g Fat

## Question

What’s the calorie equivalent in carbs of 45g of fat 

## Answer

There are 9 calories in 1 gram of fat. So, to find the calorie equivalent of 45 grams of fat, you would calculate it as follows:

45 grams of fat × 9 calories/gram = 405 calories

So, 45 grams of fat is equivalent to 405 calories.

## Question

So how many grams of carbs is equal to that

## Answer

Carbohydrates also contain 4 calories per gram. To find the equivalent amount of carbohydrates in terms of calories, you can use the following formula:

Calories from fat (405 calories) / Calories per gram of carbohydrates (4 calories/gram) = 101.25 grams of carbohydrates

So, 45 grams of fat is equivalent to approximately 101.25 grams of carbohydrates in terms of calories.

Give me a recipe for an old fashioned as a bartender would prepare it

## Answer

Certainly! Here's a classic Old Fashioned cocktail recipe as a bartender would prepare it:

Ingredients:

- 2 oz (60 ml) Bourbon or Rye whiskey
- 1 sugar cube
- 2-3 dashes Angostura bitters
- A few dashes of plain water
- Orange peel
- Ice

Instructions:

1. Place the sugar cube in an Old Fashioned glass.

2. Add 2-3 dashes of Angostura bitters onto the sugar cube. You can adjust the number of dashes to your taste for sweetness and bitterness.

3. Add a few dashes of plain water (just a splash) to the glass to help dissolve the sugar. Use a muddler or the back of a spoon to gently muddle and dissolve the sugar.

4. Fill the glass with ice cubes. You can use a single large ice cube or several smaller ones.

5. Pour in the whiskey (Bourbon or Rye) over the ice.

6. Stir the cocktail gently but thoroughly to mix the ingredients. You want to chill the drink and dilute it slightly, but don't over-dilute.

7. Express the essential oils of the orange peel by holding it over the glass and giving it a good twist to release the citrus aroma. Then, run the peel around the rim of the glass and drop it into the cocktail.

8. Optionally, you can garnish with a cherry or a slice of orange if desired.

9. Your Old Fashioned is ready to enjoy!

Remember that the Old Fashioned is a simple and timeless cocktail, so feel free to adjust the sweetness and bitterness to your personal preference by varying the amount of sugar and bitters you use. Enjoy responsibly!

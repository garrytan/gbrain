# Carb Increase for Photoshoot

## Question

What is Mike isratels advice for preparing for a photoshoot. 

## Answer

Dr. Mike Isratel recommends a systematic approach for preparing for a photoshoot, focusing on diet, training, and water & sodium manipulation. He advises a gradual reduction in calories to lose fat while maintaining muscle mass. Training should be intense to preserve muscle, but avoid overtraining. Before the shoot, he suggests manipulating water and sodium intake to enhance muscle definition. It's essential to plan these phases carefully and possibly consult with a coach for personalized guidance.

## Question

What about the day of and day before

## Answer

The day before and the day of a photoshoot, Dr. Mike Isratel advises:

1. **Carbohydrate Loading**: Increase carbohydrate intake to fill the muscles with glycogen, making them appear fuller.
2. **Water Intake**: Reduce water intake gradually a day before the shoot to reduce subcutaneous water and improve muscle definition.
3. **Sodium Management**: Reduce sodium intake to minimize water retention. However, completely eliminating sodium is not recommended as it can make muscles look flat.
4. **Pump Up Before the Shoot**: Engage in light resistance exercises to increase blood flow to the muscles, making them look more pumped and defined.
5. **Rest and Relaxation**: Ensure good sleep the night before and try to stay relaxed to avoid cortisol-related water retention.

It's important to note that these strategies, particularly manipulating water and sodium, should be approached with caution and ideally under the guidance of a professional, as they can affect health and performance.

## Question

How much to increase carbs?

## Answer

Dr. Mike Isratel generally recommends increasing carbohydrate intake by about 50-100% above your regular intake during the carb-loading phase, which is typically 1-3 days before the photoshoot. For example, if you usually consume 200 grams of carbohydrates daily, you might increase to 300-400 grams during this period.

Remember, the exact amount can vary based on individual factors like your metabolism, current diet, and how your body stores glycogen. It's crucial to monitor your body's response and adjust accordingly. Some individuals might require a more conservative increase, while others might benefit from a more aggressive approach.

For personalized advice, consider consulting with a nutritionist or a coach who understands your specific needs and goals.

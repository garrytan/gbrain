## Question

are there any metrics to show changes in incident reports or saftey concerns for ride sharing srvices over time? Any data to show if they are trending towards safer or less safe?

## Answer

There are several metrics and data sources that can be used to track changes in incident reports or safety concerns for ride-sharing services over time, although the availability and specificity of such data can vary. Key sources include:

1. **Company Reports**: Major ride-sharing companies like Uber and Lyft periodically release safety reports that include data on incidents, safety concerns, and their efforts to improve safety. These reports often detail the number of sexual assaults, fatal accidents, and other serious incidents reported during rides.

2. **Government and Regulatory Bodies**: In some jurisdictions, government or transportation regulatory bodies may collect and publish data on incidents involving ride-sharing services. This can include reports filed by consumers, law enforcement, and other entities.

3. **Independent Research and Studies**: Academic and independent research organizations sometimes conduct studies on the safety of ride-sharing services. These studies may analyze public data, survey users, and compare incident rates over time.

4. **Media Reports and Databases**: Media organizations sometimes compile databases of incidents involving ride-sharing services based on news reports, public records, and other sources.

Overall trends can be complex to discern, as they depend on various factors, including changes in the number of rides, improvements in safety measures by ride-sharing companies, and variations in reporting practices over time. Some safety measures implemented by companies include background checks for drivers, real-time ride tracking, and emergency assistance features in their apps.

To get a comprehensive view of whether ride-sharing services are becoming safer or less safe over time, it would be necessary to look at a combination of these sources. However, it's important to note that while companies like Uber and Lyft have made efforts to enhance safety measures, the impact of these measures on incident rates should be assessed over multiple years to identify clear trends.

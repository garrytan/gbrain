## Question

what's 10% bf and 205lbs in ffmi?

## Answer

Mr. White, assuming a weight of 205 lb at 10% body fat, you’d have about 184.5 lb of lean mass (205 × 0.90). Converted to kilograms, that’s roughly 83.7 kg. 

If we assume a height of 6'4" (1.93 m), your FFMI (Fat-Free Mass Index) would be:

FFMI = 83.7 kg ÷ (1.93 m)² ≈ 22.5.

## Question

Assume 6'2" as this isn't for me

## Answer

Mr. White, at 10% body fat and 205 lb total weight:

• Lean mass = 205 × 0.90 = 184.5 lb (≈83.7 kg).  
• Height = 6'2" (74 in → ~1.88 m).  
• FFMI = 83.7 kg ÷ (1.88 m)² ≈ 23.7.

## Question

What weight would I need to hit to achieve that ffmi

## Answer

Mr. White, if you’re 6’2” (1.88 m) and want an FFMI of about 23.7 at 10% body fat, you’d need to weigh roughly **205 lb**. Here’s the quick math:

1. FFMI formula:  
   \(\text{FFMI} = \frac{\text{Fat-Free Mass in kg}}{(\text{Height in m})^2}\)  

2. Fat-Free Mass = Bodyweight × (1 − Body Fat %).  
   At 10% body fat, that’s 0.90 × (your total weight).  

3. Converting lb to kg: multiply by 0.4536.  

Putting it together and solving for weight gives roughly 205 lb at 10% body fat for an FFMI near 23.7.

## Answer

Mr. White, to hit an FFMI of about 23.7 at 10% body fat, **and a height of 6’4" (1.93 m)**, you’d need to weigh around **215–220 lb**. 

Here’s the quick calculation:

1. FFMI formula:  
   \(\text{FFMI} = \frac{\text{Fat-Free Mass (FFM) in kg}}{(\text{Height in m})^2}\)

2. If you’re at 10% body fat, then  
   \(\text{FFM} = 0.90 \times \text{Total Weight (in kg)}\).

3. Solving for Total Weight with FFMI = 23.7 at 1.93 m:  
   \[
   23.7 = \frac{0.90 \times \text{Total Weight (kg)}}{(1.93)^2}
   \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
   \text{Total Weight (kg)} \approx 98 \;\text{(about 216 lb)}.
   \]

Rounding slightly, that puts you in the **215–220 lb** range at 10% body fat for an FFMI near 23.7.

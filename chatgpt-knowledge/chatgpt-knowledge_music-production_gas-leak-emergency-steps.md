# Gas Leak Emergency Steps

> **Source:** ChatGPT conversation
> **Date:** 2025-06-02T03:16:13Z
> **Messages:** 6
> **Topic:** music-production

## Question

Accidentally left stove on but not lit. The whole apartment smells like gas.

## Answer

Mr. White, **get out of the apartment immediately** and **do not turn on any lights or use any electronics**, including your phone, while inside.

Then:

1. **Call 911** or your local fire department from outside. Let them know you smell gas and may have left the stove on unlit.
2. **Do not re-enter** until cleared by emergency personnel.
3. Once safe, **notify your building manager** or landlord as well.

This is a serious fire and explosion risk. Get to fresh air and call emergency services now.

## Question

Are you sure it's necessary to call 911?

## Answer

Mr. White, if you still smell gas strongly, treat it as an emergency. In L.A., you can:

- **Leave the unit immediately** (don’t turn on any switches or use electronics).
- From a safe location, call SoCalGas’s 24-hour emergency line at **1-800-427-2200**. They’ll dispatch a technician to locate and shut off the leak.
- If you hear a hissing sound, notice a rapidly intensifying odor, or suspect immediate danger, call **911** instead—firefighters can secure the scene and evacuate neighbors if needed.

If it’s a faint smell and you’re already outside, you may call SoCalGas first; if the odor worsens, call 911.

## Question

It's not a leak, I just legitimately accidentally left the stove on. Without it being lit.

## Answer

Mr. White, if you can safely reach the stove knob, turn it fully to “off” immediately. Then:

- **Open all windows and doors** to ventilate the space—don’t flip any light switches or spark anything until the gas smell is gone.  
- **Stand outside** (or at least in fresh air) for a few minutes to let the odor dissipate.  
- If the smell lingers after airing out, or if it gets stronger at any point, call SoCalGas’s 24-hour emergency line at 1-800-427-2200. They’ll send someone to verify it’s safe.  

You don’t need to call 911 if you’ve simply left the valve on and can shut it off yourself, but you must ventilate fully and call the gas company if the odor doesn’t clear.

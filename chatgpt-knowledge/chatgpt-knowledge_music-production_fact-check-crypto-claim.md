### ✔️ What's Actually Happening in California

- **California is considering (or passing) bills** (AB 1052 / SB 822) that **extend existing unclaimed property laws** to include crypto assets held with custodial entities (like exchanges or custodial wallets). Inactive crypto—no transactions, logins, or ownership activity for **3 years**—may be classified as “unclaimed” and transferred to the state Controller’s custody citeturn0search0turn0search1turn0search7.

- This **is not outright theft**. It's a temporary transfer of custody—not ownership. The crypto remains in-kind and can be **reclaimed indefinitely** by proving identity and ownership citeturn0search1turn0search3turn0search13.

- The state **cannot immediately sell your crypto**. It must wait—often 18–20 months—and it may liquidate some assets, but the original owner can file a claim to receive either the crypto itself or its value citeturn0search2turn0search4turn0search5.

## Question

Does this apply to stocks

## Answer

Yes — **California's unclaimed property laws already apply to stocks.**

### 🔍 Here’s how it works:
- If your **stock account is inactive for 3 years** (no contact, no dividends claimed, no login, no response to notices)…
- And the brokerage firm can’t contact you…
- Then under **California’s Unclaimed Property Law (UPL)**, the brokerage must report and transfer the shares to the **California State Controller’s Office**.
- The state may **liquidate** the shares (i.e. sell them), and you can later **file a claim to get the cash equivalent**.

### 🧠 Key Details:
| Rule | Applies? |
|------|----------|
| 3 years of no activity or contact | ✅ Yes |
| Applies to stocks in brokerage accounts | ✅ Yes |
| State sells them? | ✅ Yes, often liquidated once turned over |
| Can you reclaim them? | ✅ Yes, through a claim process |
| Applies to self-custodied crypto/stocks (e.g. cold wallets or paper stock certificates) | ❌ No, because there's no holder obligated to report them |

### 🧾 Source:
- California Code of Civil Procedure §§ 1513, 1530
- [California State Controller’s Office – Unclaimed Property](https://www.sco.ca.gov/upd_faq_about.html)

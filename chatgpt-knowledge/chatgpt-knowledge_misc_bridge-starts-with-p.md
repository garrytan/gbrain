## Answer

Constructing large bridges, especially over valleys, involves complex engineering techniques. Here's how they typically get the giant pieces in place:

1. **Prefabrication**: Bridge components are often prefabricated in factories and transported to the construction site. This ensures higher quality control and faster assembly on site.

2. **Cranes and Lifting Equipment**: Large cranes, including tower cranes and mobile cranes, are used to lift and position the prefabricated sections. These cranes can handle extremely heavy loads and have long reaches.

3. **Launching Gantries**: For some bridges, especially long spans, engineers use launching gantries. These are large, movable structures that support the bridge segments as they are lifted into place and joined together.

4. **Temporary Supports**: Temporary towers or supports might be built in the valley to hold sections of the bridge until they are securely connected. These supports are removed once the bridge structure is self-supporting.

5. **Cable-Stayed and Suspension Methods**: For cable-stayed bridges, cables are gradually added to support each section as it is installed. Suspension bridges use temporary cables and catwalks to help position the main cables and deck sections.

6. **Incremental Launching**: This method involves constructing the bridge deck in sections behind the abutment and then pushing or pulling each section forward over the supports.

These techniques require precise planning and coordination to ensure safety and structural integrity throughout the construction process.

## 1. Are gummies *worse* for absorption / effectiveness?

For creatine, what matters is:

- **Daily dose ~3–5 g creatine monohydrate**
- **Long-term consistency** (so your muscles stay saturated)

If your gummies give you the **full 5 g per “daily serving”** and they’re real creatine monohydrate, then:

- They will **saturate your muscles just like powder**.
- There’s no special advantage to dry-scooping vs chewing; once it hits your gut, your body just sees **creatine**.
- Taking them with **carbs / protein (like your morning pre-gym snack)** is totally fine, possibly slightly favorable, but the effect size is tiny compared to just *taking it daily*.

So: **you are not taking a big absorption hit** going gummies instead of powder, assuming quality and dose are correct.

---

## 2. The *real* problem with gummies

This is the only part that actually matters:

- A bunch of creatine gummies on the market have been caught being **way underdosed or basically fake** in lab testing.
- You’re paying **a lot more money** for what is normally the cheapest, most boring supplement in the game.

So the real risk is:

> You *think* you’re taking 5 g, but you’re really getting like 1–2 g or less.

Solution:

- Pick a brand that has **3rd-party testing** (NSF / Informed Sport / USP, etc.) *or* has been independently lab-tested.  
- Make sure the label says something like **3–5 g creatine monohydrate per serving**, not “500 mg per gummy” where you’d need 10–15 of them.

If you do that, you’ve basically neutralized the only meaningful downside beyond cost.

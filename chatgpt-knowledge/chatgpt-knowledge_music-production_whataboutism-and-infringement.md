Mr. White, post this:

:::writing{variant="social_post" id="49271"}
Amazon is a separate issue. Whataboutism doesn’t defeat an infringement claim: a third party’s allegedly similar use doesn’t, by itself, give anyone else a license, permission, or legal right to use the same branding or imagery. If Amazon’s use was improper, pursue Amazon separately. It doesn’t answer whether this use was authorized or lawful.
:::

Use **“infringement claim”** rather than **“infringement”** because it avoids stating liability as a settled fact. The **“by itself”** language is also safer because trademark/copyright disputes can be fact-specific; trademark infringement commonly turns on likelihood of confusion, and the USPTO notes that infringement analysis depends on case-specific factors and evidence. citeturn222021search11turn222021search0

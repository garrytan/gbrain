## Answer

Here’s a draft you can drop straight in—firm but classy, so they respect you more, not less:  

---

**Subject:** Saturday Night Shift  

Hi [Manager’s Name],  

I wanted to clarify my availability. Since I wasn’t scheduled for the past few weeks, I’ve taken on other commitments outside of work. With less than 48 hours’ notice, I’m not able to adjust those plans and cover the Saturday night shift.  

For future reference, if I’m not on the schedule, I’ll assume I’m free to pursue other work. If you’d like me to be available, the best way is to put me on the schedule in advance—then I’ll always plan around it. I’m committed to helping when I’m scheduled, but I need that clarity to keep things fair and consistent on my end.  

Thanks for understanding—I appreciate it.  

Best,  
[Your Name]  

---

This way:  
- **You set the boundary** (48 hours minimum).  
- **You explain why reasonably** (you take other work if not scheduled).  
- **You keep the door open** (you’re happy to work when they schedule you properly).  
- **You sound professional, not hostile.**

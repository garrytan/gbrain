# Better VVIP Name Ideas

> **Source:** ChatGPT conversation
> **Date:** 2025-09-29T17:40:00Z
> **Messages:** 6
> **Topic:** misc

## Question

We sell GA tickets and VIP tickets. But then we also have special guests like influencers and celebrities who are "VVIP" and get free admission and comp'd tabs. VVIP as a name sucks because all our staff get confused and make mistakes. Whats a better name?

## Answer

Here are some concise naming ideas that clearly distinguish this group from GA and VIP without the “VVIP” confusion:

**1. Clear Function-Based Terms**  
- **Comp Guests** – highlights the comped entry and tab.  
- **Hosted Guests** – a common hospitality term.  
- **House Guests** – signals they’re “house-covered.”  

**2. Status/Prestige Options**  
- **Elite List** – short and easy to remember.  
- **Premier Guests** – sounds upscale but distinct from VIP.  
- **Inner Circle** – conveys exclusivity.

**3. Event-Specific Flavor**  
- **Friends of the House** – friendly, welcoming.  
- **Influencer List** – direct if these are mostly creators/celebrities.  
- **Partners** – if some are brand collaborators.

**Tips for Implementation**  
- Pick a single, simple phrase your staff can use verbally and in the POS system.  
- Use a different color wristband or badge to reinforce the category visually.  
- Train staff with one-sentence definitions (“Comp Guests: no cover, tab on house”).

From an operations perspective, “Comp Guests” or “Hosted Guests” are the least ambiguous and hardest to confuse with VIP.

## Question

Eh these all suck and are too ambiguous. Using guest and house in the names is even more confusing

## Answer

Here are sharper, more distinct options that avoid “guest” or “house” and don’t overlap with VIP:

**Straight-to-the-Point**  
- **Comped** – one word, unmissable.  
- **No-Charge** – blunt and clear for staff.

**Tiered Prestige**  
- **Ultra** – short, hard to mix up with VIP.  
- **Prime** – simple, higher than VIP without extra letters.  
- **Black Tier** – luxury feel, instantly recognizable.

**Operational Codes**  
- **Level 0** – literally “above” the numbered tiers.  
- **X-List** – distinct, sounds exclusive.  
- **Code C** (“C” for comp) – functional and obvious internally.

**Implementation Tip**  
Pick a single, punchy word and train staff with a one-line rule, e.g. “Ultra = free entry, comp tab.” A unique wristband color or POS code (e.g., “X”) locks it in.

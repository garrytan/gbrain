- **If your venue has a “public premises” bar license (Type 48/42/61):** you legally **cannot admit minors at any time**—there’s no daytime exception. Letting kids “enter and remain” is a misdemeanor for both the licensee and the minor. citeturn1search0turn1search4

### How to make a kids’ daytime walkthrough + alcohol legal
Pick the path that matches your license/layout:

1) **You’re Type 48/42 (bar):**  
   - Run the kids’ walkthrough **outside your licensed area** (a truly unlicensed space) and keep alcohol out of the kids’ zone; changing or carving up the licensed floorplan requires **ABC diagram approval** first. Or switch your license to a Type 47 (restaurant) or, if a music venue, consider **Type 90** to admit all ages. citeturn4search1turn3news30turn3search13

2) **You’re Type 41/47 (restaurant):**  
   - You can host an all-ages daytime walkthrough **with alcohol** if you maintain bona fide food service and standard controls (see below). citeturn0search0

3) **Temporary/special events (no retail license on site):**  
   - Use the **ABC Daily License (ABC-221)** if a qualified nonprofit is selling alcohol **or** hire a caterer with a **Type 58** and file a **Catering Authorization (ABC-218)** for the event. citeturn0search1turn3search0turn3search1

### Controls ABC expects when minors are present (best practice)
- **Physically separated 21+ service/consumption area** (a “beer garden”), with barriers and controlled entry; no passing drinks over the barrier. citeturn2search3  
- **ID at entry or point of sale**, wristbands/hand stamps for 21+, and **RBS-certified** servers/managers (mandatory in CA since July 1, 2022). citeturn5search0  
- **Expect decoy stings**; law enforcement may send under-21 decoys to attempt purchases. citeturn2search2

## Question

Can the parents have drinks in the walk through with kids?

## Answer

Yes—with your catering authorization, parents can have a drink while accompanying kids **only if**:

- It’s **during the authorized hours** on your ABC-218 (yours shows **4:00 PM–11:30 PM**). If the walkthrough is earlier, file a revised ABC-218 for earlier service.
- They **stay inside the authorized/diagrammed area**. No drinks may leave that footprint (e.g., into mall common areas or any unlicensed corridor) unless those areas are included on the ABC diagram.

If any portion of the kids’ route is **not** on your ABC diagram, either (a) expand the diagram to cover the whole route or (b) keep alcohol in a **separate 21+ beer-garden/checkpoint** and make the walkthrough dry.

Mr. White — short and precise:

### Is wristbanding legally required?
- **No.** California law doesn’t prescribe wristbands, stamps, X-marks, etc. Your legal duty is to **prevent sale/furnishing to anyone under 21** and to check **bona fide ID**; the exact control system is up to you. The statutory “good-faith reliance on ID” defense is in **BPC §25660** (it’s about the ID you relied on, not the color of a wristband). citeturn0search3turn0search11  
- ABC training/guidance focuses on **how to check ID** and who must be trained (RBS) — it does **not** mandate wristbands. citeturn0search1turn0search4

### What ABC and LAPD care about in practice
- Whether your system **actually stops minors from getting alcohol**. Decoy stings follow **Rule 141** (under-20, truthful answers, face-to-face ID of seller). A wristband alone won’t save you if a seller serves without verifying age. citeturn0search2  
- ABC has acknowledged wristbands as a **common practice** at events (e.g., “single proof-of-age at entry with wristbands”), but that’s commentary, not a rule. citeturn2view0  
- In decisions, ABC looks at whether your controls (e.g., *wristbands, marked cups, staff training*) were **effective**; violations are still sustained if minors get alcohol despite those controls. citeturn1search1

### Daytime (kids present)
- Legally fine to run **21+ wristbands** for adults (recommended), or mark under-21 — your choice.  
- Best practice: **card at entry**, issue **21+ wristbands**, and **re-ID at the bar when in doubt**. This minimizes the risk of under-21s removing an “under-21” marker and also aligns with RBS emphasis on ID checks. citeturn0search1turn0search4

### Night (18+ show, a small % are 18–20)
Options that stay within the law:
1) **“21+ wristband required to purchase alcohol.”** Anyone without it must show ID at the bar. (Clean look: most guests have no band; only 21+ do.)  
2) **No wristbands at all**, but require **ID every alcohol purchase** (RBS-consistent; slows service).  
3) **Under-21 wristbands** only — legal, but higher leakage risk (they can remove/cover), so pair with **ID check at point of sale** and empower bartenders to **refuse if uncertain**.  
All three are lawful; the safest (and cleanest) for upscale is **Option 1**.

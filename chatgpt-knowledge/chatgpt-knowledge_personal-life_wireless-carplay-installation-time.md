## Question

Is this disconnecting the battery necessary, and how would I go about doing so?

## Answer

Mr. White, you do want to kill battery power before digging into the dash—otherwise you risk shorting the harness or triggering airbags. Here’s how:

1. **Open the hood** and prop it securely.  
2. **Locate the battery** (front-left of engine bay).  
3. **Identify the negative (–) terminal**: black cable, marked “–.”  
4. **Loosen the nut** on the negative clamp with a 10 mm wrench—turn counter-clockwise.  
5. **Lift the clamp off** the post and tuck it to the side (insulate it with a rag so it can’t touch the terminal).  
6. Proceed with your install.  
7. When you’re done, **reinstall in reverse**: seat the clamp on the post, tighten the nut snugly, lower the hood.

That’s it—safe install, no sparks, and you’ll avoid throwing fault codes.

## Answer

Mr. White, the CX-5’s 12 V system is low-voltage—so you’re extremely unlikely to get a true “electrical shock.”  The bigger hazards are sparks, short-circuits, and accidentally setting off airbags.  Keep it safe by:

- **Using insulated tools** (or at least non-conductive-coated).  
- **Removing jewelry** (rings, watches) so you don’t bridge terminals.  
- **Wearing gloves** if your hands sweat or you’re worried about minor burns from sparks.  
- **Ensuring ignition OFF** (key out, doors open so the BCM can fully power down).  
- **Disconnecting the negative (–) terminal first** and tucking it away—this removes the chassis as a ground path.  
- **Avoiding the airbag wiring** (work below the dash, steer clear of SRS harnesses).  

Follow the steps I gave, and you’ll avoid sparks, blown fuses, or fault codes—no electrocution risk to speak of.

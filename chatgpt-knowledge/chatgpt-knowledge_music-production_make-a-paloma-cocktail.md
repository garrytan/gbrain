## Answer

In driving, the term "right of way" refers to the rules that govern who gets to proceed first when two or more vehicles come to an intersection, merge, or encounter each other on the road. These rules help prevent accidents by creating a standard for drivers to follow. For instance, at a four-way stop, the first vehicle to arrive has the right of way. If two arrive at the same time, the vehicle on the right goes first. In California, failure to yield the right of way can result in a traffic violation.

## Question

In reference to bartending, what is a Paloma?

## Answer

A Paloma is a popular Mexican cocktail made primarily with grapefruit soda and tequila. The basic recipe includes tequila, grapefruit soda, a splash of lime juice, and a pinch of salt. It's typically served over ice in a highball glass and is known for its refreshing, slightly tart flavor.

## Question

Okay, so assuming I can't find club soda, can you provide a recipe for how to make a Paloma and include the measurements for each ingredient?

## Answer

Certainly, Mr. White. If you can't find grapefruit soda or club soda, you can still make a Paloma using the following ingredients and measurements:

- Two ounces of tequila
- Two ounces of grapefruit juice
- One ounce of lime juice
- One teaspoon of sugar or simple syrup
- A pinch of salt
- Ice

Combine the tequila, grapefruit juice, lime juice, sugar, and salt in a shaker with ice. Shake well and strain into a highball glass filled with ice. Garnish with a lime wedge if desired. This should give you a close approximation of a traditional Paloma.

## Question

To make 5 liters of simple syrup, walk me through it.

## Answer

Here’s how to make **5 liters of simple syrup (1:1 ratio)** — the standard bartender’s recipe.

---

### Ingredients
- **2.5 liters of water**
- **2.5 kilograms (or 2.5 liters) of granulated sugar**

This 1:1 ratio by volume (or near 1:1 by weight) yields about 5 liters of syrup.

---

### Instructions

1. **Heat the water:**
   - Pour **2.5 liters of water** into a large pot.
   - Warm it over medium heat — *not boiling*, just hot enough to dissolve sugar easily (~60–70°C / 140–160°F).

2. **Add sugar:**
   - Slowly stir in **2.5 kg of sugar** while continuing to stir constantly.

3. **Dissolve completely:**
   - Stir until all sugar crystals dissolve and the mixture turns clear (usually takes 5–10 minutes).

4. **Cool:**
   - Remove from heat and allow the syrup to cool to room temperature.

5. **Bottle and store:**
   - Pour into sterilized bottles or jars.
   - Store in the refrigerator — lasts **about 1 month**.
   - Add **1 tablespoon of vodka or Everclear per liter** if you want to extend shelf life to ~3 months.

---

### Optional Variations
- **Rich syrup (2:1 ratio)** — for thicker texture and longer shelf life:  
  Use 3.3 L sugar + 1.7 L water to make 5 L total.
- **Flavor infusions:** add citrus zest, vanilla, herbs, or spices during heating, then strain before bottling.

## Question

How long will it last in the fridge?

## Answer

For **1:1 simple syrup**, here’s the breakdown:

- **Shelf life (refrigerated, sealed, clean bottle):** ~ **1 month**  
- **If fortified with alcohol (≈1 tbsp vodka per liter):** up to **3 months**
- **If kept at room temperature:** only **about 1 week**

---

### Why it Spoils
The 1:1 ratio doesn’t have enough sugar to fully inhibit microbial growth — mold and fermentation can develop, especially if utensils or bottles aren’t sterile.

---

### To Extend Shelf Life
- Use **boiling water** to dissolve sugar and **pour hot** into sterilized bottles.  
- Add **1 tbsp vodka per liter** as a preservative.  
- Store in a **sterile glass bottle** with a tight lid.  
- Label with the **date made** and **discard after 4–6 weeks** if refrigerated.
